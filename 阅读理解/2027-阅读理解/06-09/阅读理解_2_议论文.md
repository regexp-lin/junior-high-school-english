# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle School Students Be Allowed to Wear Earphones on the Way to School?**

In my city, more and more middle school students wear earphones while walking or cycling to school. Some parents like the idea, because their children are quieter in the morning. Others are worried. Should we really allow middle school students to wear earphones on the street? I do not think we should — at least not on both ears.

First, traffic safety is the most serious problem. A student with earphones often cannot hear a car horn, a bicycle bell or a shout from a friend. Experts in our city's traffic department say that pedestrians wearing earphones are much more likely to be hit by electric bikes at crossings. Their eyes may look ahead, but their ears are "switched off". In a busy city street, this is dangerous, especially on rainy mornings when drivers cannot see clearly.

Second, loud music over a long time is bad for our hearing. Many young people turn up the volume to block out street noise. Doctors warn that listening to loud sounds above 85 decibels for more than one hour a day can damage the small hair cells inside the ear, and the damage is usually permanent. Every year, more and more teenagers go to hospital because they cannot hear high sounds clearly. Their parents are always surprised, but the real reason is often those tiny earphones they have been wearing since Grade 5.

Of course, some students will say, "Music helps me relax before a hard school day." That is true. Music can make a long walk feel shorter. But there are safer choices. Students can listen at home before leaving, keep only one ear open on the way, or use a small speaker at a low volume. In this way they still enjoy music, but they do not give up their safety.

In short, earphones are not evil, but full-ear listening while walking in traffic is. Schools should teach students the "one ear open" habit, and parents should set clear volume limits at home. A happy morning song is nice, but it is never as important as a safe arrival at school.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the author's main opinion about middle school students wearing earphones on the street?**

A. Earphones must be totally forbidden.  
B. Students may wear earphones on both ears freely.  
C. Students should not wear earphones on both ears when walking or cycling.  
D. Earphones are useful only in class.

**2. According to paragraph 2, why is it dangerous to wear earphones in traffic?**

A. Because earphones are heavy and hurt the neck.  
B. Because students cannot hear car horns or bicycle bells and may be hit.  
C. Because earphones make students walk too slowly.  
D. Because earphones break easily in the rain.

**3. What do doctors warn about loud listening?**

A. It makes the fingers shake.  
B. It damages the small hair cells inside the ear, often permanently.  
C. It makes the eyes turn red after one hour.  
D. It gives students strong headaches only.

**4. Which is one of the "safer choices" mentioned in the passage?**

A. Throwing the earphones into the bin.  
B. Listening at home before leaving, keeping one ear open on the way, or using a small speaker at a low volume.  
C. Wearing three earphones at the same time.  
D. Only listening during class.

**5. What is the best title for the passage?**

A. How to Buy the Cheapest Earphones.  
B. Morning Music: Safety First on the Way to School.  
C. Why Young People Love Loud Music.  
D. A History of Earphones in China.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第 1 段 "not on both ears"  
【解析】作者反对双耳佩戴式上路，不是完全禁止。A 过度；B/D 与文意相反。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段 "cannot hear a car horn, a bicycle bell..."  
【解析】听不到喇叭铃声，易被撞。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段 "damage the small hair cells inside the ear, and the damage is usually permanent"  
【解析】损伤耳内毛细胞，通常不可恢复。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段 "listen at home before leaving, keep only one ear open..."  
【解析】原文明确列举的三种替代方案。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文  
【解析】文章讨论上学路上听耳机的安全问题，强调安全优先。A/C/D 均不切题。故选 B。
