# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Teens Take Part in Daily Housework?**

① In many Chinese families, middle school students are often told just one thing: "**Just study well; the housework is our business.**" Grandparents cook, parents wash, and children do their homework in the quiet of their rooms. But is this picture really good for young people? Should teens help with **daily housework** — sweeping floors, cooking rice, doing dishes, taking out rubbish — as a regular part of life?

② Some parents believe the answer is no. They point out that school work is already heavy. A Grade 9 student may sit at the desk for ten hours a day, with tests, exams, and extra classes on weekends. Adding housework, they say, will only **steal the little rest time** a child has. "Let them be young," one mother told a newspaper. "Cleaning the toilet can wait until they are 30."

③ However, many teachers and doctors disagree. First, housework helps build **basic life skills**. A student who can never boil an egg or fold a quilt will struggle once he goes to university or works abroad. Second, housework protects the body. Sitting for hours hurts the back and eyes; **standing up to mop the floor** or wash the car gives young people real, simple exercise. Third, research shows that teens who help at home often feel **more independent and more confident** — because they see, every day, that they can make real things happen with their own hands.

④ Housework also quietly teaches **respect**. When a child has to wipe the dining table, he understands why Mum is tired when she asks for silence. When he takes out the rubbish on a cold night, he learns that a clean kitchen does not appear by magic. These small lessons cannot be taught by any book, and they grow into the kind of warm, thoughtful adult that any society needs more of.

⑤ Of course, the amount of housework must be **fair and suitable**. A teen with a big exam next week should not be expected to cook a three-dish dinner. A reasonable plan might be: **15 – 30 minutes a day**, such as washing a few dishes, sweeping one room, or making the bed. Weekends can include a bigger task like a family clean-up or cooking one simple dish. The rule is simple — share the load, but do not drown the child.

⑥ In my opinion, housework is not the enemy of study; in many ways, it is a **quiet partner**. A student who can balance maths questions and a rice cooker grows up stronger, not weaker. Homes are not hotels, and children are not guests. If we want teens to become **warm, capable, and humble adults**, we should give them a broom, a cloth, and a trusting smile — starting today.

**1. What is the main topic of the article?**

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段 "Should teens help with daily housework?"  
【解析】围绕中学生是否该做家务展开。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "steal the little rest time"  
【解析】家长担心挤占休息。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段列出三大好处  
【解析】100分并未被提及。故选 D。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "15 – 30 minutes a day"  
【解析】每日约15–30分钟。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥段 "balance maths questions and a rice cooker"  
【解析】二者应共同培养。故选 C。
