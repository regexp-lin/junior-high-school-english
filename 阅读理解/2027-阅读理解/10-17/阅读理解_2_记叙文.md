> **10-17 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Afternoon Grandma Taught Me to Make Osmanthus Cake**

① Every October, the small **osmanthus tree** in our courtyard fills the whole lane with a sweet, soft smell. When I was little, Grandma used to climb a wooden stool and gently shake the branches so the tiny yellow flowers fell onto a clean cotton cloth. This year the tree bloomed again, but Grandma's legs were no longer as strong, and the cloth was empty for three whole days.

② Last Saturday, Mum asked me quietly, "Xiao Mei, why don't you learn Grandma's **osmanthus cake** this year? She has been waiting." I blinked. I had eaten her cake every autumn of my fourteen years, but I had never tried to make one. I was afraid I would fail in front of her.

③ At half past two, I walked into Grandma's small kitchen with a notebook under my arm. She laughed. "Put that away," she said. "This is not a maths lesson." She placed a big white bowl in front of me, together with four small cups: rice flour, sugar, water, and the tiny yellow flowers we had finally collected this morning.

④ "First rule," she said, "the flowers cannot be washed too long." I looked at her, confused. "If you wash them for five seconds, you keep the smell. If you wash them for five minutes, you keep only the colour." She showed me a quick dip under the tap, then a fast shake on the cloth. The kitchen filled with sweetness, as if autumn itself had stepped inside.

⑤ I mixed the flour and sugar, then slowly poured in the water, stirring with a bamboo stick. At first, my arm moved too fast, and the dough became lumpy. Grandma placed her hand on mine and moved it slowly, slowly, making small circles. "**Cake is a quiet thing**," she whispered. "If you hurry, it forgets its softness." After three minutes, the dough turned smooth, with a faint golden colour from the flowers.

⑥ Next came the **steaming**. Grandma poured the mix into a round bamboo tray, covered it with wet gauze, and placed it over a pot of boiling water. "Twenty-two minutes," she said, looking at the old kitchen clock above the stove. "Not a minute more." While we waited, she told me how her own mother had taught her this cake, on a cold afternoon during the 1970s, in a kitchen so small "one could touch every wall without moving the feet."

⑦ When the time was up, Grandma lifted the lid. White steam rose, carrying that **thick, honey-like sweetness** into the whole house. She pressed the cake gently with one finger; it sprang back at once. "Good," she nodded. "A cake that remembers how to stand up is a cake that remembers its mother." I cut the first square and offered it to her. Her eyes became a little wet, but she smiled.

⑧ That evening, I put three warm cakes in a small blue box and took them to the neighbour, old Mr. Wang, whose wife had passed away last winter. He opened the box, closed his eyes, and said only one sentence: "**This smells like my mother's.**" Walking home under the streetlights, I understood something I had never understood before: **a recipe is not just food. It is a small bridge across time, one that anyone who cooks can stand on.**

---

**注释：** osmanthus 桂花；dough 面糊；gauze 纱布；steaming 蒸；recipe 食谱

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why had the cloth under the osmanthus tree stayed empty for three days this year?**

A. It had rained heavily.  
B. Grandma's legs were no longer strong enough to shake the branches.  
C. The tree did not bloom.  
D. Xiao Mei lost the cloth.

**2. Why did Grandma tell Xiao Mei to put her notebook away?**

A. Grandma could not read.  
B. Because cooking was not a maths lesson — she wanted her granddaughter to feel and remember with her hands, not just copy numbers.  
C. The notebook was dirty.  
D. It was the wrong colour.

**3. What did Grandma mean by saying "Cake is a quiet thing"?**

A. The kitchen must be silent.  
B. The cake should make no sound when eaten.  
C. If you stir in a hurry, the cake becomes lumpy; a soft, smooth dough needs slow, patient movements.  
D. One must not sing in the kitchen.

**4. How did old Mr. Wang react when he opened the box of cakes?**

A. He gave the cakes back.  
B. He laughed loudly.  
C. He closed his eyes and said, "This smells like my mother's."  
D. He called Xiao Mei's parents to complain.

**5. What does the writer want to say in the last paragraph?**

A. Cooking is very difficult for teenagers.  
B. Osmanthus cake is the most famous dish in China.  
C. A recipe is a small bridge across time that carries memory, family and kindness from one generation to another.  
D. Neighbours should always be given food.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "Grandma's legs were no longer as strong, and the cloth was empty for three whole days"  
【解析】奶奶腿脚不便。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第③段 "Put that away ... This is not a maths lesson."  
【解析】奶奶强调做饭要用手感知而非抄笔记。A、C、D 字面化错误。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "my arm moved too fast ... the dough became lumpy ... moved it slowly, slowly"  
【解析】"quiet"喻做事需慢而稳。A、B、D 均字面化。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑧段 "He opened the box, closed his eyes, and said only one sentence: 'This smells like my mother's.'"  
【解析】直引。A、B、D 均无依据。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题／推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段结尾 "a recipe is not just food. It is a small bridge across time ..."  
【解析】食谱象征代际传递与情感。A、B、D 均偏离主旨。故选 C。
