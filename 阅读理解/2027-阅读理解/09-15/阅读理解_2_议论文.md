# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle Schools Assign Homework on Weekends?**

① Every Friday afternoon, the same question runs through almost every middle-school classroom in China: *"How much homework do we have this weekend?"* Some students hope for a light bag, a quiet bike ride, a film with their parents. Others, especially those preparing for Zhongkao, expect two full days at the desk. Teachers, parents and students often hold very different views. **Should middle schools give students homework on weekends at all?** This question is not simple, and it deserves an honest look from more than one side.

② Those who support weekend homework give **three main reasons**. First, learning needs **repetition**. A new grammar point taught on Thursday can be forgotten by Monday unless the student practises it. Second, the weekend offers **longer blocks of time** for deeper work — a whole essay, a full maths paper, a careful reading of an English short story — that cannot fit into a 15-minute break on a school day. Third, the **Zhongkao** is a real exam with real time limits; regular weekend practice, supporters say, helps a student stay "in shape" in the same way that weekend runs keep an athlete ready for race day.

③ However, the other side raises **equally strong concerns**. A recent student survey in our city showed that **over 60 %** of Grade-9 students spend **more than six hours each weekend** on homework alone — not counting extra classes. When a whole weekend becomes another school day, students lose the chance to **sleep enough, play enough and talk to their families enough**. Doctors warn about rising short-sightedness and back pain. Teachers notice more yawns in Monday's first lesson than in any other class of the week. If the cost of weekend homework is tired eyes and empty Sunday nights, **is the learning itself still efficient**?

④ There is also a question of **fairness**. Students from different families face different weekends. Some sit in a warm, quiet study room with a parent who can explain every maths step; others finish their parents' shop at nine and open their English book at ten. Heavy weekend homework **punishes the second group twice** — once for being tired, once for not having extra help. A school that says "we treat every student the same" must ask whether **giving the same thick pile of homework** really produces the same fair result.

⑤ My own view is that the problem is **not homework itself, but the amount and the kind**. A short, focused weekend task — perhaps **one hour of English reading**, **30 minutes of maths review**, and **one creative task** such as a small experiment, a family interview or a book report — can keep a student sharp without stealing the weekend away. What should be clearly **forbidden** is the long, mechanical copying and the "ten-sets-of-old-exam-papers" style of weekend. Homework should leave a student **curious**, not **crushed**.

⑥ Teachers are not the only ones who can solve this. **Parents** can help by protecting at least one half-day of real rest, perhaps a Sunday afternoon in a park, with no phone, no textbook, no worry. **Schools** can help by setting a **clear upper limit** — for example, total weekend homework should not take an average Grade-8 student more than four hours in total. And **we, the students ourselves**, can help by planning: doing the hardest task on Saturday morning, keeping Sunday evening free for a good book and an early sleep.

⑦ In the end, homework is a tool, not a punishment. A wise school gives just enough of it to keep the mind moving, and then steps back and **trusts the weekend itself** to finish the teaching. A child who has slept well, laughed with family, and opened a book of her own choice on Sunday afternoon is not "behind" — she is, quietly, **ready for Monday**.

---

**注释：** repetition 重复练习；short-sightedness 近视；crush 压垮；upper limit 上限

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What does the writer do in Paragraph 1?**

A. He gives his final answer right at the start.  
B. He raises the question for the essay — whether middle schools should give weekend homework — and notes that different groups hold different views.  
C. He only describes students riding bikes on Friday afternoon.  
D. He attacks all teachers who assign any homework.

**2. According to Paragraph 2, which of the following is NOT a reason given by people who support weekend homework?**

A. Repetition helps students remember new knowledge longer.  
B. Weekends give longer blocks of time for deeper tasks.  
C. Weekend practice helps students stay "in shape" for the Zhongkao.  
D. Weekend homework guarantees higher marks in every subject for every student.

**3. What concern does the writer raise in Paragraph 4?**

A. Students with poor eyesight should not do any homework.  
B. Heavy weekend homework is unfair because students from different families have very different home conditions.  
C. Schools should close on weekends completely.  
D. All homework should be done in class.

**4. Which sentence best states the writer's own view?**

A. Weekend homework is always wrong and should be banned.  
B. Students should do as much weekend homework as possible.  
C. The real problem is the amount and kind of homework; short, focused, varied tasks are fine, but long mechanical copying should be forbidden.  
D. Parents should take all homework decisions away from schools.

**5. What is the main message of the last paragraph?**

A. A good weekend itself is part of education; a student who has rested and read well is already ready for Monday.  
B. Homework is always a punishment.  
C. Students should not sleep at weekends.  
D. Schools should give double homework next term.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】段落主旨题  
【定位】第①段 "Should middle schools give students homework on weekends at all? This question is not simple"  
【解析】首段引出议论主题并说明各方分歧。A 错：尚未表态；C、D 皆过度解读。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段列出三点原因  
【解析】该段未承诺"每科高分"。A、B、C 均为原文所述原因。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "students from different families face different weekends ... punishes the second group twice"  
【解析】指家庭条件差异造成不公。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】作者观点题  
【定位】第⑤段 "the problem is not homework itself, but the amount and the kind ... forbidden is the long, mechanical copying"  
【解析】作者主张短而精，反对机械堆量。A、B、D 均偏离原文。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】段落主旨题  
【定位】第⑦段 "trusts the weekend itself to finish the teaching ... is, quietly, ready for Monday"  
【解析】好的周末本身即教育，休息充分的孩子已为周一做好准备。B、C、D 与原文矛盾。故选 A。
