# 阅读理解（记叙文） **My First Train Ride Alone to Visit Auntie**

① On the first Friday of May, my parents made a surprising decision. "You are almost fourteen," my father said over breakfast. "Next weekend, you will take the high-speed train to Wuhan alone to visit Auntie Rose." My spoon stopped in mid-air. Wuhan was three hours away by train. I had never travelled alone that far in my life.

② The next week was full of **small preparations**. My mother printed a one-page "travel card" with important phone numbers, the train times, and Auntie Rose's address. My father showed me how to keep my ID card and ticket in an inside pocket, never in my outside bag. He also made me practise asking politely, "Excuse me, could you help me find carriage 6?" three times at home.

③ Saturday morning came faster than I expected. At the city station, my mother hugged me quickly and stepped back behind the yellow safety line. My heart felt like a small drum. I walked through the gate, found my platform, and entered the right carriage. Seat 12A was by the window. Outside, a kind woman in a blue coat waved goodbye to her own son in the next car. I waved too, because I thought maybe she was waving at me.

④ During the journey, I learned three useful things. First, I discovered that the train moves so smoothly that writing in a notebook is still possible. Second, I learned that the instant noodles served on the train are much saltier than at home. Third — and this surprised me — I found that I could make a short, polite conversation with the grandmother sitting next to me. She was visiting her grandson. We talked about school, pandas, and her hometown near the Yangtze River. When she opened her lunch box, she offered me a soft egg cake. I said, "No, thank you, Auntie," but I smiled warmly, the way my own grandmother taught me.

⑤ When the train arrived, my heart jumped again. What if I could not find Auntie Rose in the big station? I looked around carefully. A loud voice called my name, and there she was — short, smiling, holding a small paper sign with my name in red letters. I ran forward, and she hugged me as tightly as my mother had hugged me that morning, three hours and 800 kilometres before.

⑥ That night in Auntie's flat, while she cut a plate of sweet watermelon, I wrote two messages. To my parents: "I arrived safely. Thank you for trusting me." To myself, inside my diary: "Today I travelled alone. Tomorrow I can do more." I learned that being "grown up" does not happen on a single birthday. It happens in small, real moments — like standing on a bright station platform, and finding that I am no longer afraid.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer's parents send him to Wuhan by train?**

A. Because he had broken a school rule.  
B. Because they wanted him to visit Auntie Rose alone, trusting him to travel by himself.  
C. Because there was no bus to Wuhan.  
D. Because he had to start a new school there.

**2. What did his father teach him to do before the journey?**

A. Keep his ID card and ticket in an inside pocket.  
B. Shout at strangers on the train.  
C. Sleep during the whole journey.  
D. Refuse to talk to anyone.

**3. What is NOT one of the three things the writer learned during the journey?**

A. Writing in a notebook is still possible on a moving train.  
B. Instant noodles on the train are saltier than at home.  
C. He could have a short, polite conversation with a grandmother nearby.  
D. Train windows can be opened freely during the ride.

**4. How did the writer feel when he saw Auntie Rose at the station?**

A. Angry and disappointed.  
B. Relieved and excited — she was holding a paper sign with his name.  
C. Bored and sleepy.  
D. Afraid because he did not know her.

**5. What does the writer MOST want to say through this story?**

A. Train travel is always dangerous for teenagers.  
B. Growing up happens in small, real moments of courage and trust.  
C. Instant noodles are the best food in China.  
D. Grandmothers always offer egg cakes to strangers.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段第2-3句 "Next weekend, you will take the high-speed train to Wuhan alone to visit Auntie Rose."  
【解析】父母让他独自去看望姨妈。A 违反校规、C 无公交、D 换学校都与原文不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第3句 "keep my ID card and ticket in an inside pocket, never in my outside bag."  
【解析】父亲教他把身份证和票放在内袋。B/C/D 均与原文相反或无据。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段三点小发现  
【解析】文中提到 A/B/C 三项，未提火车窗能自由打开，属无中生有。故选 D。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段末 "she hugged me as tightly as my mother had hugged me..."  
【解析】看到举着名字牌的姨妈，"心又一跳"后是轻松与激动。A 生气、C 无聊、D 不认识均不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳/写作意图题  
【定位】第⑥段末 "being 'grown up' does not happen on a single birthday. It happens in small, real moments..."  
【解析】作者想传达成长发生在一次次勇敢与被信任的小时刻里。A/C/D 均为细节过度扩展或错误。故选 B。
