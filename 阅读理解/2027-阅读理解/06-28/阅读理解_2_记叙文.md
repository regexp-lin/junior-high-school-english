# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Night Train and the Stranger's Umbrella**

I was only thirteen when I took the night train alone for the first time. My aunt lived in a small town seven hours away, and my mother had finally agreed that I was old enough to travel by myself. "Just sit quietly, read a book, and do not talk to strangers," she reminded me at the station. I nodded, but the truth was that I was already talking — to myself — about how grown-up I felt.

The train was half empty. My seat was by the window, and across from me sat an old man in a grey jacket. On his lap was a black umbrella, carefully folded. He looked tired, but his eyes were kind. I opened my book and tried to focus, yet every few minutes I peeked at him. He didn't speak. He just looked out of the dark window, as if the fields were showing him a film only he could see.

Around eleven o'clock the train stopped at a small station. The old man picked up his umbrella, nodded to me, and got off. A cold wind rushed in when the door opened. I suddenly felt very alone. The lights in the carriage were low, and I could hear the rain tapping against the window. I lowered my head into my book, but the words made no sense.

Two stops later, a new passenger sat down opposite me — a tall man with a big dark coat and a loud voice. He kept asking me questions. "Where are you going? Alone? Your parents let you?" I answered with short "yes" or "no" and tried to look out of the window. My heart beat faster. When he stood up to put his bag on the shelf, his coat fell open, and I saw a small bottle in his pocket. He laughed too much. I did not like the smell around him.

Just then, the door of our carriage slid open and the **conductor** walked in, checking tickets. Without thinking, I raised my hand and said, "Sir, I think my ticket is wrong, can you help me?" The conductor came over. While he looked at my ticket, he also looked carefully at the tall man. He smiled at me and said, "Your ticket is fine, but it is late. Would you like to move to seat 14B? It is right next to my cabin. I'll keep an eye on you until we arrive."

I nodded very, very quickly.

As I stood up to leave, I saw something on my old seat — the black umbrella of the quiet old man. He must have put it there before he got off. On the handle, a small paper note was tied with a red string. In shaky handwriting it said: "*If it rains when you arrive, use it. Kind strangers ride the same train as unkind ones. Remember, a conductor in uniform is always closer than you think.*"

I held the umbrella tightly all the way to my aunt's town. It did not rain when I arrived, but I took it home and put it in the corner of my bookshelf. Now I am in high school, and the umbrella is still there. Every time I see it, I remember two things: the world has strangers we should fear, and it has strangers we should thank. The hard part is learning to tell them apart — and to speak up loudly when we must.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer feel proud at the beginning of the journey?**

A. Because she won a prize in school.  
B. Because her mother bought her a new book.  
C. Because she was travelling alone for the first time.  
D. Because the train was very fast.

**2. What did the old man in the grey jacket do?**

A. He talked a lot to the writer.  
B. He sat quietly and got off at a small station.  
C. He stole the writer's book.  
D. He gave the writer some food.

**3. Why did the writer feel scared of the tall man with the big coat?**

A. Because his coat was too large.  
B. Because he asked many questions and she noticed a bottle in his pocket, with a smell around him.  
C. Because he didn't speak to her.  
D. Because he wore the same clothes as the conductor.

**4. How did the writer deal with the scary situation?**

A. She jumped off the moving train.  
B. She pretended to sleep.  
C. She asked the conductor for help about her ticket.  
D. She called her mother on her phone.

**5. What does the writer mainly want to tell us?**

A. Night trains are never safe for children.  
B. We should never talk to anyone on a train.  
C. Some strangers help us and some don't; we should learn to tell them apart and speak up.  
D. Umbrellas bring good luck on rainy nights.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"travelling alone for the first time"  
【解析】首次独自乘车让她自豪。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"He didn't speak... got off"  
【解析】老人沉默并下车。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"questions... bottle... smell"  
【解析】问题多+口袋有酒瓶+气味。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"Sir, I think my ticket is wrong..."  
【解析】以查票为由向列车员求助。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】最后一段  
【解析】区分善恶陌生人并勇敢表达。A/B/D 均偏离。故选 C。
