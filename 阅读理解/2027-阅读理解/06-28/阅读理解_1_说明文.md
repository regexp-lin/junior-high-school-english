# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Do We Cry When We Cut an Onion?**

Almost everyone who has helped in the kitchen knows the feeling: you pick up a knife, start cutting an onion, and soon your eyes fill with tears. You are not sad. You are not watching a film. It is just an onion. So why does this common vegetable make even strong adults cry?

The answer is hidden deep inside the onion. When an onion is growing in the field, it stores a special kind of **sulfur** in its cells. This sulfur is kept in small, closed rooms — like tiny safes. As long as the onion is whole, the safes stay shut, and nothing happens. You can hold a whole onion under your nose all day and feel nothing at all.

But when your knife cuts through the skin and into the flesh, you break open thousands of these safes at once. The sulfur quickly mixes with other **chemicals** in the onion and turns into a new gas. This gas is very light and rises into the air the moment it is made. In a few seconds, it reaches your face.

Our eyes are always covered with a thin layer of tears. When the gas meets the tears, it changes again and forms a weak acid. The acid is not strong enough to hurt your eyes, but our eyes are very sensitive. They feel the acid as "danger" and send a quick message to the brain: *something bad is near*. The brain answers in the fastest way it knows — it tells the eyes to produce more tears. The extra tears try to wash the acid away, and that is why our eyes look red and wet.

Is there any way to stop the crying? Yes, and it's simpler than you might think. First, put the onion in the fridge for 20 minutes before cutting. Cold slows down the chemical change, so less gas is made. Second, cut the onion under running water, or keep a bowl of water beside the board. Water catches the gas before it reaches your eyes. Third, use a very sharp knife. A sharp knife cuts the cells cleanly and breaks fewer "safes"; a dull knife crushes more cells and lets out much more gas. Some cooks even wear swimming goggles. It may look strange, but it really works.

So the next time you cry in the kitchen, remember: the onion is not being mean. It is simply trying to protect itself from animals that might want to eat it. Small as it is, the onion has a clever plan — and our tears are the proof.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"small, closed rooms — like tiny safes..."  
【解析】完整时硫被"关"在细胞里。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"break open thousands of these safes..."  
【解析】"safes"被打开→新气体产生。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】因果推理题  
【定位】"send a quick message to the brain: something bad is near"  
【解析】大脑判定"危险"从而分泌眼泪。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题（反向）  
【定位】最后一段所列方法  
【解析】文中未提微波炉切洋葱。A/B/D 均提到。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文  
【解析】解释流泪原因+给建议。A/B/D 均偏离。故选 C。
