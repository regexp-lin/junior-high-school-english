# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Do Whales Jump Out of the Water?**

Every year, along the coast of California and many other places, tourists crowd into small boats to watch one of the most amazing shows of nature: a huge whale leaps out of the ocean, twists its body in the air, and falls back with a giant splash. This action is called "**breaching**". A 30-tonne whale flies almost completely into the air — but why? Scientists have thought about this for many years, and today we have several good answers.

The first reason may be **communication**. Sound travels far in water, but so does the noise of a big splash. When a whale breaches, the splash can be heard more than one kilometre away. Some scientists believe whales use these splashes to send messages to other whales, especially when the ocean is noisy from ships or storms. A group of whales may "talk" in this way during feeding or long journeys.

The second reason may be **cleaning**. Whales swim for weeks in warm water, and tiny creatures, such as **barnacles**, stick to their skin. These small animals can make the whale itchy and slow. When a whale jumps up and slams back on the water, the force of the hit knocks many of these creatures off. Think of it as a giant shower — but one that you take by falling on the water.

A third reason is **hunting**. Some whales, especially humpbacks, work in groups to catch fish. They swim in a circle below a large ball of fish and blow bubbles to keep the fish together. Then one whale rushes up from below, opens its mouth wide, and catches many fish at once. Sometimes, the whale's head breaks the surface with such power that the whole body lifts out of the water. So this kind of "jump" is really the end of a clever fishing trick, not a show for humans.

A fourth reason, more surprising, is **play**. Young whales, like young dogs and young children, enjoy playing. After a big meal, when there is no danger and the sea is calm, a young whale may breach ten or more times in half an hour. It seems to feel joy in the movement itself. Some researchers even believe that breaching helps young whales learn the strength of their own bodies.

Of course, a single whale may breach for many reasons at once, and scientists do not always know which reason is "the real one". What we do know is this: next time you see a video of a jumping whale, remember that this huge action is a mix of **talking, cleaning, hunting and playing**. Under that one great splash, a surprising amount is going on.

**1. What is "breaching"?**

A. A small jump by a whale inside the water.  
B. The action of a whale leaping almost completely out of the water and falling back.  
C. A kind of song that whales sing underwater.  
D. A special name for whale food.

**2. According to paragraph 2, why might whales use breaches to communicate?**

A. Because other whales cannot hear their songs.  
B. Because the splash can be heard more than one kilometre away.

D. Because the splash makes waves for surfers.

A. The whale becomes smaller after jumping.  
B. The salty water makes the skin whiter.

D. They blow bubbles to hide from ships.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义理解题  
【定位】第 1 段 "breaching"  
【解析】鲸几乎整体跃出水面再落下。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段 "splash can be heard more than one kilometre away"  
【解析】水声传播远。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段 "the force of the hit knocks many of these creatures off"  
【解析】撞击力除去寄生物。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段 "blow bubbles to keep the fish together..."  
【解析】吹泡圈围鱼。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】标题归纳题  
【定位】全文  
【解析】多种跃水原因。A/C/D 均片面。故选 B。
