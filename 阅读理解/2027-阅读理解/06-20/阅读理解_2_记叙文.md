# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Speech I Was Afraid to Give**

Last October, our Chinese teacher told the class, "On Friday, each of you will stand in front and give a three-minute speech about 'the person who changed me'." I felt a small cold feeling in my stomach. I could write long essays, I could answer many questions on paper, but speaking in front of 45 classmates — that was my worst fear.

For one week, I wrote my speech. It was about my grandfather, who had taught me to read simple Chinese characters when I was four. Every Sunday afternoon, he would sit with me on a wooden bench and patiently draw the same character, again and again, until I got it right. He had passed away two years before, and I had never spoken about him in public. The words were easy to write at night, but reading them aloud in my bedroom, I still shook.

On Thursday evening, I could not sleep. I kept looking at my speech on the desk and thinking, "What if I forget the words? What if people laugh?" I even thought of asking the teacher to "let me speak next week". But my mother, passing by my door, saw the light on. She sat beside me and said softly, "Your grandpa did not give up when he taught you 'water' for the ninth time. He just smiled and drew it again. Try to remember his smile tomorrow."

Friday morning. My name came fifth on the list. The first four classmates finished easily. Then the teacher said, "Lin Hao, please." My legs felt heavy. I walked to the front. The paper shook in my hand. I looked at 45 faces. I opened my mouth — and nothing came out.

There was one long second of silence. I thought I was going to run out of the room. Then I saw Yuan in the back row, my best friend. He put his thumb up quietly, and he looked at me as if to say, "I believe you." I took a deep breath. I closed my eyes for one moment, and in that moment, I saw my grandfather's smile, exactly as my mother had said.

And I began to speak.

My voice was a little thin in the first minute. But as I described the wooden bench, and the way Grandpa drew the character "water" with a shaking hand, my voice became steadier. Somewhere in the middle, I even looked up and made eye contact with the teacher. When I said, "He is not here today, but every time I finish a difficult homework, I feel that he would smile," a girl in the front row had tears in her eyes. I did too. But the tears did not stop me from speaking.

When I finished, the classroom was quiet for two seconds, and then the clapping began. Mr. Ye, my teacher, nodded slowly and said only, "Thank you, Lin Hao." I walked back to my seat. My hands were still shaking, but inside, something big had changed. For the first time, I realised that fear does not disappear — you just learn to walk beside it.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why was the narrator worried about the speech?**

A. He had not written anything yet.  
B. He was afraid of speaking in front of his classmates.  
C. He did not know who had changed him.  
D. He had lost his paper.

**2. Who was the "person who changed" the narrator?**

A. His Chinese teacher.  
B. His mother.  
C. His grandfather, who had taught him to read characters.  
D. His best friend Yuan.

**3. What advice did the narrator's mother give him?**

A. To give up the speech.  
B. To remember his grandfather's patient smile and try his best.  
C. To change the topic completely.  
D. To read the speech very, very slowly.

**4. What did Yuan do at the moment the narrator froze?**

A. He came to the front and spoke for the narrator.  
B. He shouted the words of the speech.  
C. He put his thumb up quietly and looked at him supportively.  
D. He left the classroom quickly.

**5. What did the narrator finally realise?**

A. Fear always disappears if you wait long enough.  
B. Fear never changes anything in your life.  
C. Fear does not disappear; you just learn to walk beside it.  
D. Fear is the best friend of a good student.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 1 段 "my worst fear"  
【解析】怕当众演讲。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段 "about my grandfather..."  
【解析】演讲主题是爷爷。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段 "Try to remember his smile tomorrow"  
【解析】想起爷爷微笑，尽力而为。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段 "put his thumb up quietly..."  
【解析】默默竖起大拇指给予支持。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】末段  
【解析】恐惧不会消失，要学会与之同行。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。
