# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Chess with Grandpa in the Park**

① My grandpa has been playing Chinese chess since he was a young man. Every Saturday afternoon, he walks to a small pavilion in our neighborhood park, where old friends already wait for him with a wooden board. Last Saturday, I went with him for the first time.

② When we arrived, Uncle Sun and Uncle Zhang greeted us with big smiles. Soon, Grandpa and Uncle Sun started a game. Other old men stood around, watching quietly. I sat next to Grandpa. I only knew the names of the pieces, and I could not tell when he was winning or losing. "Just watch," Grandpa whispered. "The board is like life. You need to see the whole table, not only one piece."

③ The game lasted almost an hour. Several times, Grandpa made a move I did not understand. He put his "horse" in a place where it could easily be taken. But a few moves later, Uncle Sun's face became serious. That "weak" horse was actually a trap. In the end, Grandpa won with a small smile and a nod. The watchers clapped softly, as if they were listening to music.

④ After the game, I asked Grandpa to teach me one simple opening. He was patient. He showed me three safe first moves and explained why each one worked. He also said, "Don't be afraid to lose. In chess, losing teaches you more than winning." I played a quick game with Uncle Zhang and lost, but I laughed instead of feeling sad.

⑤ On our way home, the winter sun was low. Grandpa walked slowly, his hands behind his back. I asked him why he still loved chess after so many years. "A chess board is small," he said, "but friendship sitting around it is big." That night, I wrote this sentence in my notebook. A quiet afternoon in the park had become one of my favourite memories.

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where does Grandpa play chess every Saturday afternoon?**

A. At his own home.  
B. In a small pavilion in the neighborhood park.  
C. At a big chess club downtown.  
D. On a rooftop next to the school.

**2. What did Grandpa say about the chess board?**

A. "The board is like a TV screen."  
B. "The board is like life, you need to see the whole table."  
C. "The board is a secret box for money."  
D. "The board only makes old people tired."

**3. Why did Grandpa put his "horse" in a weak position?**

A. Because he made a careless mistake.  
B. Because he wanted to give the horse to Uncle Sun.  
C. Because it was actually a trap.  
D. Because the horse was broken.

**4. How did the writer feel after losing to Uncle Zhang?**

A. He cried and wanted to leave at once.  
B. He laughed instead of feeling sad.  
C. He got angry and shouted at Uncle Zhang.  
D. He refused to play any more games.

**5. What does Grandpa mean by "the board is small, but friendship around it is big"?**

A. Chess players buy bigger boards every year.  
B. Friendship and warm time matter more than the game itself.  
C. Chess is the most difficult game in the world.  
D. Old people should play alone at home.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "he walks to a small pavilion in our neighborhood park."  
【解析】每周六在小区公园小亭。A、C、D 均不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "The board is like life. You need to see the whole table."  
【解析】爷爷把棋盘比作生活。A、C、D 均不符。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第③段 "That 'weak' horse was actually a trap."  
【解析】那匹马是陷阱。A 非粗心；B、D 均不符。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "I... lost, but I laughed instead of feeling sad."  
【解析】作者输后反而笑了。A、C、D 均不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义/句义猜测题  
【定位】第⑤段末句。  
【解析】比喻：围绕棋盘的友情比棋本身更可贵。A、C、D 均为字面误读。故选 B。

---

**词汇注释（超纲词）**  
- **pavilion** [n.] 亭子  
- **piece** [n.] 棋子  
- **trap** [n.] 陷阱
