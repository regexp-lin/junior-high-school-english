# 阅读理解（说明文） **The Long Journey of Migrating Birds**

① Every autumn, in the cold sky above lakes and rivers, we can see large groups of birds flying together. They are moving to warmer places. Every spring, they come back. This amazing trip is called "migration." Some birds fly only a few hundred kilometres, but others fly more than ten thousand — even across oceans.

② Why do birds make such long and hard trips? The main reason is food. When winter comes to the north, insects hide or die, and seeds become harder to find. Birds would die of hunger if they stayed. By flying south, they can find food and keep warm. In spring, the north wakes up again with new insects and fresh leaves, and the birds return to raise their babies.

③ How do birds find the right way? Scientists have studied this question for many years. They believe birds use several "maps" at the same time. Some birds watch the position of the sun during the day and the stars at night. Others feel the earth's magnetic field, almost like a built-in compass. Young birds often learn from older ones on their first trip, and later they can lead the group by themselves.

④ Migration is not easy. A small bird may lose half of its weight during the trip. Strong winds, storms and tired wings all bring danger. In recent years, new problems have appeared. Tall buildings and bright city lights can confuse young birds, and clean lakes where they stop to rest are getting fewer because of pollution and new roads.

⑤ To help migrating birds, many countries have set up protected wetlands, turned off unnecessary lights during key seasons, and asked people not to catch wild birds. Some schools also join "bird counting days" to record the numbers they see. When we protect the sky and the water, we protect these small travellers. Their amazing journey reminds us that the earth is a shared home for humans and wild lives.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why do birds fly to warmer places in autumn?**

A. Because they like sea water in winter.  
B. Because food becomes hard to find in the cold north.  
C. Because people give them free food only in the south.  
D. Because their feathers fall off every winter.

**2. How do some birds find the right direction during migration?**

A. By calling home on the phone.  
B. By watching the sun, the stars and feeling the magnetic field.  
C. By reading road signs on the ground.  
D. By following airplanes in the sky.

**3. According to Paragraph ④, what is a NEW problem for migrating birds?**

A. Snow on the top of mountains.  
B. Tall buildings and bright city lights.  
C. Too many seeds in the fields.  
D. Too few stars in the sky.

**4. Which of the following is mentioned as a way to help birds?**

A. Planting more trees inside classrooms.  
B. Turning off unnecessary lights during key seasons.  
C. Building higher walls around lakes.  
D. Catching and keeping young wild birds.

**5. What does the writer mainly want to tell us in the last paragraph?**

A. The earth is a shared home for humans and wild lives.  
B. Only scientists care about migrating birds.  
C. Wetlands should be turned into fields.  
D. Children should stop going to the countryside.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "insects hide or die, and seeds become harder to find."  
【解析】冬天北方食物难找，鸟南飞。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Some birds watch the position of the sun during the day and the stars at night. Others feel the earth's magnetic field..."  
【解析】鸟以太阳、星星与地磁导航。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "Tall buildings and bright city lights can confuse young birds."  
【解析】高楼与城市灯光是新问题。A、C、D 均不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "turned off unnecessary lights during key seasons."  
【解析】关闭多余灯光帮助鸟类。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】主旨/写作意图题  
【定位】末段末句 "the earth is a shared home for humans and wild lives."  
【解析】作者强调人类与野生生物共享地球。B、C、D 与内容不符。故选 A。
