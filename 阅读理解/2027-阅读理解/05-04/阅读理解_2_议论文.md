# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Teenagers Learn to Save Their Pocket Money?**

① Every Sunday, millions of Chinese parents give their children a small amount of **pocket money**. Some teenagers put it straight into a snack shop or a mobile game. Others drop every yuan into a piggy bank on their desk. A question that often appears in family chats is simple but important: **should teenagers learn to save their pocket money, or is it better to just spend and enjoy?** I believe saving is a skill every middle school student should learn.

② First, saving teaches a useful life lesson called **"delayed joy"**. When you want a new badminton racket that costs 120 yuan and you only get 15 yuan a week, you quickly learn that waiting is not punishment — it is planning. After eight weeks, the racket is yours, and you enjoy it more because you earned it. This kind of patience is very useful later, in exams, in sports, and in keeping promises.

③ Second, saving gives young people a small but real sense of **control over life**. Many middle school students feel that adults decide everything for them — what to eat, when to sleep, which school to attend. A small savings box is a tiny corner where the student is the boss. He or she chooses what to buy, and when. Having even ten yuan "under your own roof" is a quiet kind of freedom.

④ Third, saving helps us become more careful shoppers. A student who saves starts to ask honest questions in front of a shop window: "Do I really need this?" "Will I still want it next week?" Research from several schools suggests that students who keep a simple weekly **money diary** waste less on sweets and mobile top-ups. They are also more likely to give small gifts to family members, because they can plan ahead.

⑤ Of course, some people argue that childhood should be free from money worries. They say that always saving may make a child sad or **anxious**. This is a fair point, but the answer is not "spend everything now". A balanced rule — such as saving **half**, spending a quarter on small daily joys, and giving a quarter to a family helping fund — brings together kindness, fun and future planning.

⑥ In the end, pocket money is more than a few paper notes. It is a tiny school where teenagers learn about needs, wants, patience and kindness. A student who learns to save at 13 will probably still be saving at 30, when the numbers and the goals are much bigger. So, yes: save a little, spend a little, give a little — and grow up strong.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main question discussed in the passage?**

A. Whether parents should give pocket money at all.  
B. Whether teenagers should learn to save their pocket money.  
C. How to open a bank account before the age of 10.  
D. Why games cost too much money.

**2. What does "delayed joy" in paragraph ② mean?**

A. Happiness that never comes.  
B. Happiness you feel only after waiting and working for something.  
C. Joy from eating sweets very quickly.  
D. Joy that only adults can have.

**3. According to paragraph ④, what do students who keep a money diary tend to do?**

A. Waste more money on sweets and mobile top-ups.  
B. Forget their family members.  
C. Make more careful choices and give small gifts to family.  
D. Refuse to share money with anyone.

**4. What do people who disagree with the writer worry about?**

A. Children may become too generous.  
B. Children may become too good at maths.  
C. Children may become sad or anxious if they always save.  
D. Pocket money may become too big.

**5. What is the writer's FINAL view?**

A. Teenagers should spend all their pocket money at once.  
B. Teenagers should save everything and never buy anything.  
C. Teenagers should save a little, spend a little, and give a little.  
D. Parents should stop giving pocket money.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句 "should teenagers learn to save their pocket money..."  
【解析】作者开篇提出的讨论核心是中学生是否该学会储蓄零花钱。A 是否给零花钱、C 银行账户、D 游戏太贵均不是主要问题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义猜测题  
【定位】第②段第2-3句关于等待 8 周买球拍的例子  
【解析】"delayed joy" 指经过等待和努力后获得的快乐。A "永远不来"与文义相反；C 快速吃糖、D 只限成年人均非文义。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段第3-4句 "waste less on sweets... more likely to give small gifts..."  
【解析】写日常记账的学生消费更谨慎，也更愿意给家人送小礼物。A 正好相反；B 忘掉家人、D 拒绝分享均与原文相反。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段第1-2句 "some people argue... always saving may make a child sad or anxious"  
【解析】反方观点是担心孩子总是储蓄会变得悲伤或焦虑。A/B/D 均与原文不符。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥段末句 "save a little, spend a little, give a little"  
【解析】作者最终主张是"存一点、花一点、给一点"，即平衡。A 全花、B 只存、D 停发零花钱均非作者立场。故选 C。
