# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Winter Science Fair 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Science Department + STEM Club**  
> **2027 Winter Campus Science Fair — "Small Questions, Real Answers"**  
> **Every good scientist started as a student who asked one question twice.**

Our Winter Science Fair is back for its **ninth year**. Between **Monday 29 November and Friday 3 December 2027**, Grade 7, 8 and 9 students will present their small, homemade science projects in the school sports hall. Every student is invited — as an exhibitor, as a volunteer, or simply as a visitor with a notebook and a curious question.

---

## 🧪 Three Kinds of Projects

**Type A — Observation Project (single student or a pair).**  
Choose something **small** that can be watched carefully over 2–3 weeks: how fast a plant reaches for light; how the sound of a bell changes in a cold corridor; how a dumpling cools in different bowls. Record your notes, photos, or short videos.

**Type B — Experiment Project (team of 3–4).**  
Ask a **real question**, make a prediction, test it, repeat the test, and note what went as expected — and what did not. Examples: "Which cloth keeps a cup of water warm longest?", "Does the way we fold a paper plane change its flight time?".

**Type C — Design Project (team of 2–4).**  
Build a **small useful thing** that solves a tiny problem. Examples: a foldable desk-corner lamp from cardboard; a safer bell-rope for the lab; a warm-water reminder clip for old people.

---

## 📝 How to Enter

1. Choose one of the three types.  
2. Fill in the one-page entry form (available outside Room 310, or on the school mini-app → STEM → Fair).  
3. Submit the form to Mr. Lu, the STEM Club teacher, by **Friday 14 November, 18:00**.  
4. Your form should include:
   - Project title (fewer than 15 words).  
   - One sentence about **the question** you are asking.  
   - Names, classes, and roles of your team.  
   - A rough plan for your 2.5 m × 1.0 m display table.

## 🎯 How You Are Judged (Out of 100)

| Area | Weight |
|---|---|
| **Clear question and plan** | 20 |
| **Careful method and honest data** | 25 |
| **Thinking and conclusions** | 25 |
| **Communication at the table** (including a short 3-minute talk) | 20 |
| **Teamwork and kind response to visitors** | 10 |

**Important:** judges **always prefer** a project that answers a small question well over one that tries to answer a big question poorly. "Why does my indoor bamboo grow faster at the west window?" is a better project than "How does the universe work?".

---

## 🛑 Safety and Fair Play

- **No open flames.** Use small LED lights and low-voltage batteries only.  
- **No sharp glass** on table edges; ask the lab office for safer plastic containers.  
- **No animals** brought to the hall (photos and videos are fine).  
- All data must be **your own**. Copying results or pictures from the internet is an instant "no award".  
- Chemistry experiments must have a **teacher's pre-approval signature** by 21 Nov.

---

Sign up at Room 310 by **Thursday 20 November**.

## 🏅 Awards (All Cert. + Small Prizes)

- **Golden Curiosity** (one project per grade, voted by the judges).  
- **Most Honest Mistake** — the team whose "failed" test taught the audience the most.  
- **Kindest Table** — voted by visitors, for the friendliest explanation.  
- **Young Engineer** — best Type-C design project across the three grades.  
- **Teachers' Choice** — a special award for a project that quietly impressed the staff.

> — *Mr. Lu, STEM Club, 7 November 2027*
