# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Lighthouse Keeper Who Taught Me to Wave**

The summer I turned fourteen, our family moved to a small fishing town called Wanhai, by the East Sea. My father had taken a new job at the harbour, my mother was busy setting up our half-empty rooms, and I — I was not happy. I had left my best friends, my school, my weekend football team. For two weeks I spoke to no one. I walked alone to the **lighthouse** every afternoon and sat on the warm rocks until the first star came out.

The lighthouse had been painted white and red, but time had turned it into soft pink and grey. An old man lived there. He had a short white beard and thick eyebrows. He wore a dark-blue coat even when the sun was hot. Everyone in town called him "Uncle Chen". He kept the light running every night and took care of the small garden that grew by the tower door.

On my fourth visit, Uncle Chen waved at me from the top of the tower. I did not know what to do, so I hid behind a rock. The next day, he was at the bottom of the tower, and he waved again, with his whole arm high in the air. I gave him a tiny wave with my finger — the kind you give someone you do not really want to meet. He smiled anyway, as if I had given him a whole bunch of flowers.

One afternoon, a storm came up suddenly. The wind pushed the waves so high they almost touched the top of the rocks. I ran toward the tower for shelter. Uncle Chen opened the iron door before I knocked. Inside, it was warm and smelled of tea. While the storm shook the windows, he boiled water in an old kettle, poured it into two cups, and said, "Sit down, young traveller. Everyone comes to the lighthouse sooner or later."

I did not mean to speak, but somehow the tea made my mouth warm, and my words followed. I told him about my old school, my old friends, my missing football team. He nodded the whole time but said almost nothing. When I finished, he pointed at the tall lamp above us. "Do you know what this lamp does? It does not shout. It does not move. It only stands still and waves at ships in trouble. And yet, many men are alive because of that quiet wave."

I was quiet for a while. Uncle Chen smiled and added, "A shy boy in a new town is not so different. You do not need to run or shout. Just show a small light, wave a small wave, and let others find their way to you."

Summer passed. When September came, I joined a football team at my new school. I waved at classmates in the corridor, even the ones I did not yet know. Some of them waved back; some became friends; some, I still do not know well. But nobody seemed unfriendly any more.

On the night before our family left Wanhai to move again, I climbed the rocks one more time. The lighthouse shone as it had always shone. I raised my hand high in the dark. I am almost sure Uncle Chen, somewhere up in the tower, raised his too. A single wave. That was all. But that summer, a quiet old man, a pink-grey lighthouse, and a shy boy had found their way to each other — just by waving.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why was the writer unhappy at the beginning of the summer?**

A. He had failed an important exam.  
B. He had just moved to a new town and missed his old life and friends.  
C. He did not like the sea.  
D. His father was angry with him.

**2. What was Uncle Chen's daily job?**

A. To sell fish in the market.  
B. To teach football on the beach.  
C. To keep the lighthouse running and take care of its small garden.  
D. To paint the tower red every year.

**3. What did Uncle Chen do when the storm came and the writer arrived?**

A. He closed the iron door and shouted from inside.  
B. He opened the door, made warm tea, and let the writer talk.  
C. He pushed the writer back into the rain.  
D. He called the writer's parents.

**4. What lesson did Uncle Chen teach the writer through the lamp?**

A. You must shout loudly to make new friends.  
B. Only strong people matter in the world.  
C. A quiet light and a small wave can help others find their way to you.  
D. You should never talk to strangers.

**5. What changes happened in September?**

A. The writer joined a football team and began waving at classmates; some became friends.  
B. The writer stopped going to the lighthouse.  
C. The writer stopped studying.  
D. The writer moved back to his old city.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"moved to a small fishing town... left my best friends..."  
【解析】搬家失去朋友，不开心。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"kept the light running... took care of the small garden"  
【解析】守灯塔+打理小花园。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"Uncle Chen opened the iron door... boiled water..."  
【解析】开门，泡茶，倾听。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】"It does not shout... only stands still and waves..."  
【解析】沉静之光+一个小招手就能相遇。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"I joined a football team... waved at classmates..."  
【解析】入队+招手+交朋友。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。
