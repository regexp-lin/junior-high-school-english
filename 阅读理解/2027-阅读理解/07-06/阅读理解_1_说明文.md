# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Do Leaves Change Colour in Autumn?**

Every autumn, the streets of our city put on a show. The green leaves of summer slowly change into yellow, orange, red, and sometimes even a deep purple. In a few weeks, a single tree can look like a painter's palette. It is easy to enjoy this beauty; it is harder to explain it. Why do leaves change colour in autumn? The answer lies inside a tiny kitchen — the **leaf** itself.

Inside every green leaf there are three main colour **pigments**, and each has a job. The first is **chlorophyll**, which is green. Chlorophyll is the star worker of the leaf: it uses sunlight to change water and **carbon dioxide** into the food that keeps the tree alive. Because chlorophyll is so **active** in spring and summer, its green covers up all the other colours, and the whole forest looks green. The second pigment is **carotene** (orange and yellow). The third is **anthocyanin** (red and purple). Both have been inside the leaves all year, but they are quietly hidden behind the strong green.

As autumn arrives, two things change in the environment. Days become **shorter**, and nights become **colder**. The tree, sensing these changes, quietly starts preparing for winter. Making food through chlorophyll uses a lot of energy, and winter sunlight is weak. So the tree stops producing new chlorophyll, and the old chlorophyll begins to break down. Like a curtain slowly lifting, the green fades away. Now the yellow and orange carotene, which was there all along, suddenly becomes visible. This is why **ginkgo** trees turn yellow and **maple** trees first turn orange.

Red leaves tell a more interesting story. Unlike carotene, anthocyanin is not "always there". It is usually made fresh each autumn, when sunny days meet cool nights. During the day, leaves still trap some sugar from weak sunlight. At night, the cold air prevents the sugar from leaving the leaf. The leftover sugar becomes the raw material for new anthocyanin. The more sugar that is trapped, the redder the leaf becomes. That is why maple valleys glow brightest after a cool, clear week.

Weather, then, writes the colour menu of autumn. A summer with many sunny days stores more sugar in the tree. A long autumn with warm, bright days and cool, dry nights helps anthocyanin build up. But a rainy autumn with warm nights will produce dull, brown leaves — the sugar is washed out before anthocyanin can form.

And where does the leaf go at last? A thin layer grows at the base of each stem, cutting off water and food. The leaf, no longer fed, loses its final grip and falls. The tree is not losing something precious; it is quietly locking its doors for winter, so it will not dry out in the cold. Spring will bring new leaves, and new green chlorophyll, and the same quiet cycle will start again.

Next time you walk through a park in October, pick up a red leaf and hold it against the sun. That small piece of colour is a record of last summer's sunlight, last autumn's cold, and a tree's quiet preparation for the winter ahead.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main job of chlorophyll?**

A. To make leaves taste sweet.  
B. To turn sunlight, water and carbon dioxide into food for the tree.  
C. To attract bees into the leaves.  
D. To keep insects away.

**2. Why do yellow and orange colours appear in autumn leaves?**

A. Because the tree paints them.  
B. Because carotene is new and just made in autumn.  
C. Because chlorophyll fades, and the long-hidden carotene becomes visible.  
D. Because the wind blows the green away.

**3. What weather helps leaves become especially red?**

A. Rainy days with warm nights.  
B. Sunny days with cool, dry nights.  
C. Snowy days with windy nights.  
D. Foggy days with hot nights.

**4. Why does the tree drop its leaves in the end?**

A. Because the tree is dying.  
B. Because animals eat them off.  
C. Because a thin layer at the stem cuts off water and food, protecting the tree from drying out in winter.  
D. Because children pull them off during games.

**5. What is the main idea of the passage?**

A. Autumn is the best season to go hiking.  
B. Leaves change colour because of pigments inside them and the changes of weather.  
C. Chlorophyll is the only pigment in leaves.  
D. Tourists love to take photos under trees.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"uses sunlight to change water and carbon dioxide into food"  
【解析】叶绿素光合作用制造养分。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"green fades away... carotene... suddenly becomes visible"  
【解析】绿色褪去，胡萝卜素显现。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"sunny days meet cool nights"  
【解析】晴日+凉夜促生花青素。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"a thin layer grows at the base of each stem"  
【解析】形成脱离层，保护树木。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文  
【解析】色变=色素+天气。A/C/D 均偏离。故选 B。
