# 阅读理解（议论文） **Is It Necessary for Students to Do Housework at Home?**

In many Chinese families, parents do all the housework themselves. They say to their children, "You only need to study. Leave the rest to us." In some families, children have not washed a single bowl by Grade 9. Is this a good idea? I believe it is **not**. Middle school students should do some simple housework at home every week.

First, housework teaches **life skills** that are really useful. Washing a bowl, wiping the floor, folding a shirt — these are small things, but they need real practice. A student who has never folded his own clothes will find it harder in senior high school, and much harder in college, when he has to live in a dormitory. By doing simple housework at home, students quietly build these skills, one by one, without any stress.

Second, housework helps students understand their parents better. After a long school day, a 14-year-old is already tired. But her parents may have worked even longer, and still cooked dinner after coming home. When the student helps wash the dishes for fifteen minutes, she quickly understands how tiring it is to do it every day, for every meal. This understanding turns into respect, and respect turns into better family talk. Many arguments at home are simply because children do not see how much their parents are doing.

Third, housework is also a kind of **rest for the brain**. After four hours of reading and maths problems, the student's head feels full. Going to the kitchen and washing a few plates is a small, physical activity. The hands move; the eyes look at warm water; no words are required. Many studies show that short changes of activity like this actually help the brain to learn more in the next study session. So housework is not a "waste of time" — it may even save time.

Some parents worry that housework will hurt the student's grades. "She has so much homework already," they say. But a 10-minute job once a day, or 30 minutes on the weekend, is not a heavy burden. If it is done at fixed times — for example, washing dishes after dinner, or wiping the floor every Saturday morning — it becomes a small, comfortable habit. The student even feels proud of her role in the family.

Of course, parents should not use housework as a punishment. "You got a bad grade, so you clean the bathroom today!" — this turns housework into something children hate. Instead, housework should be a **shared** family task, as normal as eating or sleeping. When everyone in the family does a little, no one is tired, and the home feels warmer.

So the next time someone says, "Just let your child study," we can gently reply: a child who cannot fold her own shirt is not really ready for the world, no matter how high her test score is.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the author's main opinion?**

A. Parents should do all the housework for middle school students.  
B. Middle school students should do some simple housework at home every week.  
C. Housework is only for adults, never for students.  
D. Schools should teach all housework during class time.

**2. According to paragraph 3, how can housework improve family life?**

A. It forces parents to stop working.  
B. It turns children into servants.  
C. It helps students understand how tiring daily housework is, which builds respect and better talk.  
D. It makes everyone in the family silent.

**3. Why does the author say housework is a kind of "rest for the brain"?**

A. Because housework uses no thinking at all, so students' brains go to sleep.  
B. Because a small physical change of activity can actually help the brain learn more in the next study.  
C. Because housework lasts for many hours, replacing study.  
D. Because housework takes place only on rainy days.

**4. What do some parents worry about, and how does the author answer?**

A. They worry about housework skills being too hard; the author says students can hire help.  
B. They worry about noise; the author says students can use earphones.  
C. They worry that housework hurts grades; the author says a short fixed-time job is not a heavy burden and builds a good habit.  
D. They worry about the cost; the author says housework is expensive.

**5. What does the author say about using housework as a punishment?**

A. It is a good way to keep students quiet.  
B. It is fine once or twice a year.  
C. It is wrong because it makes children hate housework; housework should be a shared family task.  
D. It should replace all tests.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第 1 段末句  
【解析】支持学生每周做简单家务。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段  
【解析】理解父母辛劳并建立尊重。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段  
【解析】短时间切换活动有助下一段学习。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段  
【解析】作者用固定短时间、轻负担回应担忧。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 6 段  
【解析】反对用家务当惩罚，倡议共享家务。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。
