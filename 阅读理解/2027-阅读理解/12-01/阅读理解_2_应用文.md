# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Book-Swap Week 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Library + Green Club**  
> **Book-Swap Week** — *"Old Books, New Owners; New Paper, Old Friends."*  
> **Mon 7 – Fri 11 December 2027, 12:20 – 13:30 & 16:10 – 17:00**  
> **Venue**: Library Floor 1 East Wing

At the end of every term, many of us find a small pile of books we have finished but cannot throw away: a fairy-tale collection from Grade 5, a boxed set of popular-science stories from last winter, a much-loved English graded reader. Meanwhile, a classmate two rows behind you has not yet met **exactly those** books. Book-Swap Week is our small, honest answer: a whole week during which any student at Lihua can **bring up to five** good-quality used books to the library, and **take home up to five** belonging to someone else — for free, in silence, in peace. No stickers, no points, no ranking. Only a quiet new door opened for each book, each reader.

---

## 🕒 Opening Hours

| Time slot | Day | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 12:20 – 13:30 | Mon – Fri | Lunch Swap Hour |
| 16:10 – 17:00 | Mon – Thu | After-school Swap Window |
| 16:10 – 18:00 | Fri | **Final Long Swap** + end-of-week tidy |

- Students bring books in a **cloth bag** (no plastic), hand them to a volunteer at the counter, and receive a small paper stamp for each book accepted.  
- One stamp = one take-home book.  
- A single student may collect at most **5 stamps** across the whole week.

---

## ✅ What Kind of Books Are Welcome

- Children's story books from Grade 1 – 6 (many younger siblings of classmates will be happy!).  
- Novels suitable for middle schoolers: friendship, adventure, historical, light sci-fi.  
- English graded readers (CEFR A1 – B1).  
- Popular-science illustrated books (biology, geography, small physics, light astronomy).  
- Chinese classics published **in school-friendly abridged editions**.  
- Well-kept **workbooks with no written answers** (pencil writing is okay if fully erased).

## ❌ What Cannot Be Swapped

- Any book with **torn covers, missing pages, or strong smells** (ink, mould, cigarette).  
- **Reference books for this year's exam** (please return those to the Grade office).  
- **Teacher keys or answer booklets** of any exam.  
- **Books about adult topics** not suited to middle-school readers.  
- **Books with personal writing** on more than two pages (a small note inside the front cover is fine and sometimes even sweet).

---

## 🧼 Before You Swap — Three Small Gifts to the Next Reader

1. **Wipe the cover** gently with a soft dry cloth.  
2. **Rub out pencil notes** that are only personal reminders (keep the ones that might help the next reader, like small question marks or key words).  
3. **Write a short one-sentence note** on the first blank page if you wish — for example, *"I read this on long bus rides in Grade 7 — the ending is kind."* Your name is optional.

> Many students kept letters that ran in library-swap books from the 2024 trial year; the small notes, unexpectedly, became the most-loved part of the week.

---

## 🔎 During the Swap — Four Small Rules

1. **Whisper, do not shout.** Voices at library-corner level.  
2. **One book at a time in the hand.** No stacking five books on your arm at the counter.  
3. **If two students want the same book**, the one who arrived first keeps it. No swapping for stickers, snacks, or favours.  
4. **Walk to the small "pause corner"** if you want to read a few pages before deciding. Please do not read in the middle of the main aisle.

---

## 📥 Leftover Books — What Happens After Friday

- Any books that have not found a new reader by 18:00 Friday will be cleaned, packed in labelled boxes, and **donated** to the Sunlight Primary School in our partner county — two hours away by train. Photos of the arrival will be shared on the school mini-app.  
- No leftover book is thrown away.  
- A **Grade-9 logistics team** (12 students) will pack and drive to help.

---

## 🌱 Sustainability Notes

This Book-Swap Week is run jointly with the Green Club. Each book that finds a new home instead of a recycling bin saves roughly:

- **2.5 kg of water** (paper-recycling rinses).  
- **1.1 kg of CO₂** (paper-recycling energy).  
- Roughly **the weight of the book in new paper** at a distant mill.

If, across one week, 1,500 books find new readers at Lihua, we save about **3,750 kg of water** and **1,650 kg of CO₂** — roughly the yearly driving footprint of two small family cars.

## 💬 A Small Word from the Library Team

> "At Book-Swap Week, the most precious thing that moves is not the book itself. It is **the small sentence someone wrote in pencil at the front**, the **slightly bent corner of page 47** where a reader paused to think, and **the quiet trust** that the next person, whoever she is, will read it a little more carefully because it was already loved. Please bring your good books. Please write a line. Please carry home the ones that find you."  
> — *Library Team & Green Club, 1 December 2027*
