# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Winter Bird-Watching Week 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Biology Team + Green Club**  
> **Winter Bird-Watching Week** — "**Meet the Quiet Guests of Our Courtyard**"  
> **Mon 6 – Sat 11 December 2027**

Winter may look empty, but our school courtyard is quietly busier than spring. The big plane trees at the north wall, the cedar hedge near the library, the small pond behind Teaching Building B — all of them become a **warm and safe stop** for small birds flying down from the north every December. This year the Biology Team, together with the Green Club, is turning one full school week into the first **Lihua Winter Bird-Watching Week**. No telescopes needed, no scary early mornings; just **eyes, ears, a small card, and three quiet minutes** at the right spot.

---

## 🐦 Who Are Our Winter Guests?

Last winter our teachers quietly kept a small log. The ten most common birds in our courtyard between December and early February were:

| # | Bird (English / 中文) | Where you often see it | Size hint |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Eurasian tree sparrow / 麻雀 | Everywhere, in small groups | Small |
| 2 | Light-vented bulbul / 白头鹎 | Cedar hedge, library wall | Medium |
| 3 | Oriental magpie / 喜鹊 | High branches of plane trees | Large, black-and-white |
| 4 | Azure-winged magpie / 灰喜鹊 | Noisy, often 6–10 together | Medium |
| 5 | Common myna-like starling / 灰椋鸟 | Open lawn, flocks | Medium |

| 7 | Great tit / 大山雀 | Near bird feeders, head black | Small |

A laminated **"Top-10 pocket card"** will be in every classroom by Friday 3 December.

## 🗓 What Happens Each Day

### Mon 6 Dec — Quiet Start (07:30–07:50, before class)

- A small "silent greeting" on the playground; ten quiet minutes to simply **listen**.  
- Three teachers will help point out sounds. No speaking to others during these ten minutes.

### Tue 7 Dec — First Count (Lunch break, 12:20–12:50)

- The first **"Three-Minute Count"** at six fixed spots (map in every classroom).  
- You stand still, count every bird you see or hear in exactly three minutes, mark it on your small card.

### Wed 8 Dec — Bird-Feeder Making (Art lesson + Club time)

- Simple, safe, **plastic-free** bird feeders from cardboard tubes, peanut butter, and wild-bird seed.  
- Feeders will be hung at **five approved spots only** (so that cleaning is possible after the week).

### Thu 9 Dec — Sound Walk (afternoon PE option)

- A 20-minute slow walk along the inner courtyard, guided by the Biology Team.  
- Everyone keeps voice at **library level**.  
- A small recorder at each stop; sounds will be uploaded to a shared courtyard audio map.

### Fri 10 Dec — Second Count + Posters

- Second "Three-Minute Count" at lunch.  
- At 16:10, the small "**Bird Poster Wall**" opens outside Room 208: drawings, photos, short poems, and the first printed **Courtyard Winter Bird List**.

### Sat 11 Dec — Early Morning Extension (optional, 07:00–08:30)

- For families who would like to join.  
- Meet at the north gate; gentle, short, completely optional. Light rain will not cancel it; heavy rain will.

---

## 🚫 Four Firm Rules

1. **Do not chase or scare** any bird. We only **watch and listen**; we never make them fly.  
2. **Do not feed** birds anywhere except at the five approved feeders; a half-eaten bun under a tree can hurt small stomachs.  
3. **Do not climb** trees or the library wall to look into a nest. Nests are private homes.  
4. **Do not play bird-song recordings** from phones to "call" birds; real wild birds can be stressed by fake calls.

---

## 🎒 What to Bring

- **Ears and eyes**. (Most important.)  
- A **pencil** (pens freeze on very cold mornings); the Top-10 pocket card.  
- **Warm, quiet clothes**; shoes without loud soles.  
- **No bright red jackets** during counting if possible (some birds are shy of bright red).  
- One **soft, warm drink** for the Saturday morning, if you join. No food outside — to keep the courtyard clean and to avoid attracting cats.

- **6 feeder-makers** for Wednesday.  
- **4 sound-walk assistants** for Thursday.

**Sign-up** on the school mini-app → Green Club → Bird Week. **Closing 03 December, 17:30.**

## 📝 A Small Reward

Every student who completes **at least four** of the week's activities (the Monday greeting, one count, one feeder, one sound walk or one poster) will receive a small laminated card: *"Quiet Watcher 2027 — Lihua Winter Courtyard"*. The card has a tiny drawing of a Daurian redstart on the back. No grade points are given; this is on purpose. **The reward for watching is the watching itself.**
