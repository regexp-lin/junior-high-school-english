# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle Schools Replace Paper Textbooks with Tablets?**

① On the first day of the new term, I watched a Grade 7 girl drag her school bag along the ground like a small suitcase with wheels. When she finally opened it, I counted **nine thick textbooks**, three workbooks and five pens. Her shoulders were red. In that moment, an old question came to my mind again: should middle schools **replace heavy paper textbooks with tablets**? Some schools have already started; my own school is still thinking about it. After reading reports from several countries and talking with my classmates, I have come to an opinion somewhere **in the middle**: tablets can help, but a full replacement would be a mistake.

② It is easy to list the **good sides** of tablets. First, tablets are **light**. One tablet can hold all the textbooks of all three years of middle school, plus thousands of reading materials. Bags become lighter, shoulders stay straighter, and students can carry home **every subject** they need, not only two or three. Second, tablets are **up to date**. A correction in a history book no longer has to wait for next year's reprint; it can be made overnight. Third, a good learning tablet can **read aloud, play videos, and highlight new words** automatically, which helps students with weaker reading or listening skills. For many students, this kind of **personal** learning is genuinely useful.

③ Yet paper textbooks are not simply "old" or "slow". They have real strengths that a tablet cannot fully copy. Paper is **kind to the eyes**, especially during long study hours. Many eye doctors now report that the number of middle-school students with short-sightedness has gone **up** in the last ten years, and screens are part of the reason. Switching from screens at home to screens at school may turn our eyes into very tired guests on every page.

④ More importantly, paper books invite a **deeper kind of reading**. When I read a printed story, my eyes move slowly, I stop at a sentence I like, and I sometimes **write in the margin**. Research in several countries has found that students often **remember more** from a printed page than from the same text on a screen, especially for difficult material. A tablet is great for quick searches, but a paper book, quietly open on a desk, invites long attention — exactly what a growing brain needs.

⑤ There is also the worry of **distraction**. A tablet is not only a book; it is also a window to games, to short videos, and to messages from friends. Even a well-designed "study mode" can be turned off or worked around. When every student has a tablet during class, teachers have reported that about **one out of four** looks at something else within ten minutes. Of course, students with paper books can daydream too, but at least paper does not send a bright notification in their faces.

⑥ Another small worry is **cost**. A tablet is expensive. When it drops on the floor, repairs are expensive. When it is lost, replacing it is expensive. If a child forgets a paper book, he can borrow one from a classmate. If he forgets a tablet, half of his subjects may disappear with it. Schools in less wealthy areas may find "one tablet per child" a real financial burden, while every child can open a paper book.

⑦ The best answer, I believe, is **not "paper or tablet", but "paper and tablet, each doing what it does best"**. Schools can keep paper textbooks for main subjects — literature, maths, English — where deep reading and careful writing are most needed. Tablets can then act as a **helpful second tool**: for watching short explainer videos, for practising listening, for searching extra examples, or for carrying supplementary reading home. With clear rules — for example, "tablets stay in the bag during paper lessons" — both tools can support learning without fighting for the student's attention.

⑧ The girl with the wheeled school bag deserves a lighter bag, yes. But she also deserves a pair of healthy eyes, a chance to mark her favourite sentences in the margin, and the simple pleasure of closing a real book at the end of a long chapter. If we move too fast, we may lighten her bag but dim her reading. **The wisest classrooms of the future** will hold both a paper book and a tablet — one for depth, one for speed — and will teach students when to use each.

---

**注释：** short-sightedness 近视；margin 页边；distraction 分心；supplementary 补充的；financial burden 经济负担

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion about fully replacing paper textbooks with tablets?**

A. Yes, schools should replace paper textbooks completely.  
B. No, paper textbooks should never be used with tablets.  
C. Tablets can help, but a full replacement of paper textbooks would be a mistake.  
D. Only rich schools should use paper; poorer schools should use tablets.

**2. Which of the following is **NOT** listed as a good side of tablets?**

A. Tablets are very light compared with many paper books.  
B. Tablets can be updated quickly with new information.  
C. Tablets can read aloud and play videos to help learning.  
D. Tablets always cost less than paper books in every school.

**3. According to paragraph 4, what does research in several countries show about reading?**

A. Students always remember much more from screens than from paper.  
B. Students often remember more from printed pages than from the same text on a screen.  
C. Students only remember words they hear, not words they read.  
D. Students learn equally well from paper and screen in every situation.

**4. What does the writer say about the cost of tablets?**

A. Tablets are cheap, easy to repair, and almost free to replace.  
B. Even in poor areas, tablets cost nothing.  
C. Tablets are expensive to buy, to repair, and to replace, which can be hard for less wealthy areas.  
D. Tablets are a small, unimportant cost for every school.

**5. Which statement best matches the writer's final suggestion?**

A. Schools should use only paper books for every subject, every lesson.  
B. Schools should throw away every paper book at once.  
C. Schools should combine paper and tablets wisely, with clear rules for each.  
D. Schools should ban all reading materials from the classroom.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段 "tablets can help, but a full replacement would be a mistake"  
【解析】作者立场折中：平板有用但不应完全取代。A、B 极端；D 曲解。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节判断题（否定型）  
【定位】第②段列举轻便、及时更新、朗读/视频；第⑥段指出价格昂贵  
【解析】文章未将"更便宜"列为平板优点，反而指出其昂贵。A、B、C 均有原文依据。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "students often remember more from a printed page than from the same text on a screen"  
【解析】研究显示纸上阅读记忆更深。A、C、D 均误读。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "A tablet is expensive. ... repairs are expensive. ... replacing it is expensive"  
【解析】购买、维修、替换皆贵，给经济困难地区带来压力。A、B、D 均与原文相悖。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨/写作意图题  
【定位】第⑦—⑧段 "not 'paper or tablet', but 'paper and tablet, each doing what it does best'"  
【解析】作者主张纸书与平板各司其职、组合使用。A、B、D 均极端。故选 C。
