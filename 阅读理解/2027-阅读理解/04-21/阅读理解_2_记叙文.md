# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Saving a Baby Sparrow After the Storm**

① Last Saturday night, our small town was shaken by a sudden **spring storm**. The wind pushed against the windows, the old **camphor tree** outside my grandma's wall bent like a bow, and the rain fell in heavy, white sheets. I sat with Grandma on the sofa, eating warm soybean soup, and listening to the storm quietly talking to our roof. "Storms are difficult for small lives," Grandma said softly, and I did not fully understand her then.

② Early Sunday morning the rain stopped. The sky was clean, the air smelled of wet earth, and the sun climbed up slowly like a sleepy yellow coin. After breakfast I put on my rubber boots and went outside to check the little garden. Leaves and small branches lay everywhere. Near the base of the camphor tree, I saw something strange — a **dark-brown ball** on the wet grass, shaking slightly. I bent down. It was a **baby sparrow**, no bigger than my thumb, with short grey feathers and its eyes still closed.

③ My heart jumped. A gust of wind must have thrown it out of the nest high above. Its tiny body was cold and wet. "Grandma! Grandma, come quickly!" I shouted, softly so as not to scare it. Grandma came out, looked down, and nodded. "Poor thing. Let's not touch it with warm hands yet." She asked me to bring a dry soft cloth, a cardboard box, and some torn newspaper. I also grabbed the **old hand warmer** we used in winter.

④ Slowly Grandma helped the sparrow into the box. We did not put it in direct sun. Instead, we placed the box on the shady side of the veranda and gently laid the half-warm hand warmer, wrapped in cloth, on one side. "Baby birds cannot be too cold or too hot," Grandma said. "We must be like a careful mother." The little sparrow stopped shaking. It opened its beak just once, as if trying to say something we could not hear.

⑤ Then came the hard question: should we keep it at home, or put it back? Grandma looked up at the tall camphor tree. High above, we could see a tidy little **nest** made of twigs. "If its parents are alive," she said, "they will come back looking. Wild birds do best with their own family." We decided to wait. I stayed under the tree, quiet as a stone, and listened. Twenty long minutes later, a small sparrow flew down to the lowest branch, then to the wall, then hopped to the grass. It was a grown sparrow — brown, clear-eyed, alert. It looked at me, then at the box.

⑥ I stepped far back. Grandma used a thin long stick to slowly push the box close to the tree. The mother sparrow flew down, pecked at the baby's head, made a small warm sound, and went back up. For almost an hour she flew down and up, bringing tiny insects to her child. By afternoon, the baby sparrow had dried and gained some strength. Grandma climbed a short ladder and, very carefully, used the soft cloth to lift it back into the nest above.

⑦ That night, as the camphor tree shook gently in a kind breeze, I wrote in my diary: **"Storms are hard for small lives. But small lives are not alone, if small hearts notice them."** I think I finally understood what Grandma had said the night before. I learned that kindness in nature is not loud — it is as quiet as a boot in wet grass, and as light as a baby sparrow in a careful hand.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. When did the storm happen?**

A. Saturday morning.  
B. Saturday night.  
C. Sunday night.  
D. Last Friday afternoon.

**2. What did the writer find under the camphor tree on Sunday morning?**

A. A broken phone.  
B. A lost cat.  
C. A baby sparrow that had fallen out of its nest.  
D. A small fish.

**3. Why did Grandma put the hand warmer wrapped in cloth beside the baby bird?**

A. To heat up the whole kitchen.  
B. To keep the baby bird from being too cold or too hot.  
C. To cook food.  
D. To scare the cats.

**4. Why did they finally decide NOT to keep the bird at home?**

A. Because their house was too small.  
B. Because they were lazy.  
C. Because wild birds do best with their own family, if the parents are alive.  
D. Because the bird was too noisy.

**5. What is the main lesson the writer learned that night?**

A. Storms never come in spring.  
B. Kindness in nature is quiet but powerful; small lives are not alone if we notice them.  
C. Cameras are better than words.  
D. Baby birds make great pets.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "Saturday night"  
【解析】暴雨发生在周六晚上。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "baby sparrow"  
【解析】小麻雀从窝里掉下来。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "cannot be too cold or too hot"  
【解析】保持合适温度。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "wild birds do best with their own family"  
【解析】野鸟应和父母一起。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦段 日记句  
【解析】善意静默而温暖，小生命不孤单。故选 B。
