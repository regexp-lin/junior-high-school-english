# 阅读理解（应用文） **Teen Science Fair 2027 — Call for Projects**

Hello, young scientists!

Our city's biggest student-led science event, the **Teen Science Fair**, is open again! If you are in Grade 7, 8 or 9 in our city, we invite you to turn a question from your daily life into a simple experiment, and share it with hundreds of classmates, teachers, and families.

The slogan this year: **"A question is the first step of science."**

---

### Key Dates

| Stage | Date | What you do |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Online entry opens | Mon, Aug 4, 2027 | Register with a 200-word idea paper |
| Entry closes | Fri, Sept 26, 2027, 23:59 | Final project title cannot be changed after this |
| Home preparation | Oct, 2027 | Carry out your experiment and prepare a small poster |
| City Fair | Sat & Sun, Nov 8 – 9, 2027 | Show your project in City Youth Hall |
| Awards | Sun, Nov 9, 2027, 16:00 | Prizes on the Main Stage |

---

### Topics

You may choose any topic, but every project should answer **one clear question** and test it with a **simple experiment**. Some examples that passed last year:

- Does the shape of a paper plane change how far it flies?  
- Does music help plants grow faster? (Basil vs silence vs pop songs)  
- Can orange peel stop fruit from turning brown?  
- Do phone cases really protect a phone in a 30 cm drop?  
- Why does salt make ice melt faster? (Compared with sugar and sand)

Projects that do **not** fit the fair: political arguments; projects using live animals in painful ways; projects that need expensive lab tools students cannot fully understand.

---

### What Every Project Needs

1. **A clear question** (one sentence).  
2. **A short plan** (what you will change; what you will keep the same; what you will measure).  
3. **At least three tests** of the same experiment (for fair results).  
4. **A simple poster** (A1 size, 60 × 84 cm), including your name, class, question, plan, results (numbers or pictures), and a short conclusion.  
5. **A 3-minute oral explanation** to the judges at your booth.

---

### Rules

1. **Teams:** 1 to 3 students. Students in the same team must be from the same grade.  
2. **Adult help:** parents and teachers may give ideas and help with safety, but the experiment must be done by the students themselves.  
3. **Safety first:** no fire, no dangerous chemicals, and no electricity above 12 V. Protective glasses are provided for experiments that need them.  
4. **Honesty:** write what really happened, even if the result is not the one you hoped for. "Unexpected" results often teach the most.  
5. **Recycle:** please reuse paper boxes, old bottles, and empty jars where possible for your model.

### Prizes

- **"Best Question" Award (3):** ¥500 book voucher each + an afternoon at the Science Museum lab.  
- **"Best Poster" Award (3):** ¥300 voucher each + a printed interview in the City Youth Magazine.  
- **"Clearest Explanation" Award (3):** ¥300 voucher each + a set of simple lab tools.  
- **Everyone at the Fair:** a Teen Science Fair badge and a photo with the judges.

### How to Enter

1. Go to **www.teensciencefair2027.cn** and fill in the online form.  
2. Upload your 200-word idea paper as a PDF (not more than 2 MB).  
3. A confirmation email will be sent within 2 working days.  
4. If your team has any problem with the online form, visit the paper-entry desk at the Science Museum (Room 108) on Wednesdays and Fridays, 16:00 – 18:00.
