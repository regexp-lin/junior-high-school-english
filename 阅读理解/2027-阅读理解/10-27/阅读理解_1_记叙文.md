# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Little Cat That Waited Through My Exam Week**

① The week of our **mid-term exams** began on a grey Monday in late October. My shoulders were already stiff from two weeks of revision. On my way to school that morning, at the corner near the old post office, I noticed something I had never seen before: a **thin, black-and-white kitten**, no bigger than a pencil case, sitting by the iron gate of a closed shop. She looked at me, and did not move.

② I had a maths paper in less than an hour, so I only paused for a second. "Not today," I whispered, and hurried on. But during the whole first exam, the kitten's round eyes came back to my mind between the numbers. When I walked home at noon, she was **still there**, in the exact same spot. I kneeled down and offered her half of my steamed bun. She ate slowly, politely, as if she knew the food was not mine to waste.

③ Tuesday morning — **English exam**. The kitten was waiting again, this time a step closer to the pavement. I brought a small paper box and a folded towel from home. "If you are still here after the test," I said softly, "we will talk." I do not know why I spoke to her like that. I only know that her small, quiet face made my racing heart slow down for thirty seconds, right before I had to step into the exam room.

④ On Wednesday (**Chinese and Physics**) it rained. I asked Mum if she could lend me a big cardboard box. Mum, still in her apron, looked at me for a moment and said, "Only one week. Then we will send her to the animal rescue." I nodded, too grateful to speak. At half past seven, I placed the box against the shop wall, with a soft cloth inside and a saucer of milk. The kitten slipped in at once. "**Thank you**, whispered my tired heart, as if speaking to an old friend.

⑤ Each morning that week, she was waiting. I named her **Mocha**, because her white chest had one small brown spot, like a coffee stain left by a careless writer. On Thursday (**History**), she followed me five steps down the road, then stopped when I stopped. On Friday (**Maths Two and Biology**), she did something I will never forget: she walked around my ankles three times, then sat **neatly on my left foot**, as if to say, "**Stay calm. You have been here before. You can do this.**"

⑥ Friday afternoon, after the last paper, I ran — **ran** — all the way from school. I was not sure what I expected. There she was, curled in the box, sleeping. I sat on the pavement for a long time, the noise of the week slowly leaving my chest. A light autumn wind moved the dry leaves at the corner. I thought about my answers, my mistakes, the questions I had left blank on the physics paper. But strangely, I did not feel crushed. **I felt looked at.**

⑦ On Saturday morning, the animal-rescue woman arrived in a small white van — the same one I had seen in a school event a few weeks earlier. She checked Mocha gently, smiled, and said, "She will be healthy in two weeks. Good food, a warm room, some medicine. Then a kind family." I pressed my face against the van window for a last look. Mocha stood up on her small legs, tilted her head, and blinked slowly. In the cat language, that slow blink, I had read, means "**I trust you**".

⑧ I never saw Mocha again. The rescue later sent a short photo by message: she had been taken home by a retired doctor in the south of the city, and she had a new red collar. For months afterwards, whenever an exam felt too heavy, I would quietly close my eyes and imagine one small weight warm against my shoe — and then the paper seemed a little easier. **A friend need not be human, and a week of exams, it turns out, can also be a week of being looked after.**

---

**注释：** kitten 小猫；revision 复习；apron 围裙；cardboard 硬纸板的；rescue 救助

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where did the writer first see the black-and-white kitten?**

A. In her school classroom.  
B. By the iron gate of a closed shop at the corner near the old post office.  
C. In her mother's kitchen.  
D. At the animal clinic.

**2. On rainy Wednesday, what did the writer's mother finally agree to?**

A. To send the kitten away at once.  
B. To lend a big cardboard box, but only for one week, then send the kitten to the animal rescue.  
C. To keep the kitten forever.  
D. To call the police.

**3. Why did the writer name the kitten "Mocha"?**

A. Because she drank coffee.  
B. Because the kitten was born in a coffee shop.  
C. Because her white chest had one small brown spot, like a coffee stain.  
D. Because "mocha" was her grandmother's name.

**4. What did Mocha do on the morning of Maths Two and Biology exams?**

A. She ran away.  
B. She walked around the writer's ankles three times and sat neatly on her left foot.  
C. She sat on the exam paper.  
D. She jumped into the schoolbag.

**5. What is the main idea of the whole story?**

A. Students should not keep cats.  
B. Rescue workers are the best teachers.  
C. A friend need not be human; during a hard exam week, a small waiting kitten taught the writer that even in pressure, she was being "looked after".  
D. Cats are smarter than students.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "at the corner near the old post office ... by the iron gate of a closed shop"  
【解析】地点直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "Only one week. Then we will send her to the animal rescue."  
【解析】妈妈条件接受。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "her white chest had one small brown spot, like a coffee stain"  
【解析】直引命名来历。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "walked around my ankles three times, then sat neatly on my left foot"  
【解析】动作直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧段结尾句  
【解析】全文情感凝练。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。
