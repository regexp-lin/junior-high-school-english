# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Leaves Change Colour in Autumn**

① Every autumn, trees along streets and in forests slowly change from green to yellow, orange and red. Tourists travel hundreds of miles just to see these beautiful colours. But why do leaves change? The answer lies in something we cannot see — tiny **chemicals** inside each leaf.

② During spring and summer, a leaf is full of a green chemical called **chlorophyll**. This chemical is very important. Under the sunlight, it helps the leaf use water from the root and carbon dioxide from the air to make sugar. The sugar then feeds the whole tree, from its deepest root to its highest branch. Because chlorophyll is so strong and so green, we see the leaf as pure green all summer long.

③ But chlorophyll is not the only colour inside a leaf. Yellow and orange chemicals are there too, hidden behind the strong green. They are like younger students standing behind a taller classmate — present, but not seen. When autumn comes, the days grow shorter and the nights grow colder. The tree slowly closes the small "doors" between the leaf and the branch. Sugar can no longer move away, and the green chlorophyll breaks down day by day. Only then do the yellow and orange chemicals finally step out and show themselves.

④ Red is a more special story. In some trees, such as the **maple**, cold nights and bright days make the trapped sugar change into a new chemical called **anthocyanin**, which is bright red. The more sunny days and cool nights there are, the redder the leaves become. This is why autumn in places like Beijing's Xiangshan or Canada's forests is so famous — the weather there is almost perfect for red.

⑤ Not all leaves fall at the same time. Oak leaves often stay brown on the tree until the next spring. Pines and other "evergreen" trees keep their needles all year, because their narrow leaves lose less water and can survive through a cold winter. In this way, every tree has its own way of facing the cold.

⑥ Falling leaves are not a sad story. When they land on the ground, they return nutrients to the soil, just like a warm blanket protecting the sleeping roots. Next spring, new leaves will use these gifts to grow again. So the next time you walk on a path full of red and yellow leaves, remember that you are walking on a great **circle of life** — small, quiet, but full of wisdom.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why do leaves look green during spring and summer?**

A. Because of the sugar inside them.  
B. Because of a strong green chemical called chlorophyll.  
C. Because of the sunlight reflecting on the tree.  
D. Because of the water on the leaves.

**2. According to Paragraph 3, where do yellow and orange chemicals come from in autumn?**

A. They are carried to the leaf by the wind.  
B. They come from the soil.  
C. They have always been in the leaf, but hidden before.  
D. They are made from the red chemical.

**3. What causes the beautiful red colour in maple leaves?**

A. A lot of rain in autumn.  
B. Cold nights and bright sunny days.  
C. Strong winds at night.  
D. The tree's age.

**4. Why can pine trees keep their needles through winter?**

A. Because they have thick bark.  
B. Because they grow very slowly.  
C. Because their narrow leaves lose less water.  
D. Because they receive more sunlight.

**5. What does the writer compare falling leaves to in Paragraph 6?**

A. A strong cold wind.  
B. A warm blanket protecting the roots.  
C. A sad ending.  
D. A funny joke of nature.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "a leaf is full of a green chemical called chlorophyll"  
【解析】叶绿素使叶子呈绿色。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "hidden behind the strong green"  
【解析】黄、橙色素本就存在，只是被绿色遮盖。A、B、D 均不符。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "cold nights and bright days make the trapped sugar change"  
【解析】冷夜与晴日促使糖转为花青素。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "their narrow leaves lose less water"  
【解析】松针窄小、蒸发少，能越冬。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑥段 "like a warm blanket protecting the sleeping roots"  
【解析】落叶被比作保护根系的温暖毯子。故选 B。
