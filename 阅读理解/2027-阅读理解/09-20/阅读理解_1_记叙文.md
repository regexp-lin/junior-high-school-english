# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Morning I Missed the School Bus**

① On a cold Monday in early December, I did the one thing every student secretly fears: I **missed the school bus**. My alarm had rung at 6:30, as usual. But that night I had stayed up past eleven finishing a history project on the Ming Dynasty, and my brain, in the warm darkness under the blanket, decided one tiny push on the "**stop**" button was not really the same as getting up. When I opened my eyes again, the curtains were already glowing with yellow morning light. The small clock on the wall said **7:42**. The school bus left our corner at **7:35**, every weekday, and never — **never** — waited.

② For three or four seconds I simply stared at the clock, the way someone stares at a broken cup on the kitchen floor. Then something in me started running even before my legs did. I dragged on my uniform with one sock missing, hung my bag over one shoulder, grabbed a piece of cold bread from the kitchen, and shouted "Mum, I'll fix it!" towards the closed bathroom door, though I had no idea how. She could not drive me — she was already preparing for an important morning meeting in her own office. My father was on a business trip in another city.

③ Outside, the sharp December air hit my face like cold water. I ran to the corner, out of breath, just to make sure: no bus, only a thin cat sitting on a low wall. My watch said **7:48**. The first class at school started at **8:15**. By bus from our corner, the trip took twenty-five minutes; by **taxi**, maybe twenty; by **bike**, at least thirty-five. I did not have enough cash for a taxi — my wallet had only fifteen yuan. For a moment I felt my eyes burn, half from cold, half from being scared.

④ I sat on a low stone by the bus stop and took three slow breaths, exactly the way my P.E. teacher had shown us before a race. Then I pulled out my phone. I did not call my mother — she had warned me many times that missing the bus was not an "emergency" I could pass to her. Instead I called **my classmate Xu Liang**, who lived two streets over and whose father often drove him in. "Xu Liang," I said, trying to sound calm, "it's Chen Ming. I missed the bus. Can your dad — any chance?" There was a short pause. Then Xu Liang's voice came back, very clear: "Wait there. We're just leaving. Stay at the old bookstore corner. Two minutes."

⑤ They did not come in two minutes — they came in **three**, a small grey car pulling up to the curb with the window already rolled down. Uncle Xu did not scold me. He only smiled and said, "Seat belt, quickly." Inside the car, Xu Liang gave me half of his own breakfast — a warm egg-and-ham bun — without saying anything. I ate it in silence, partly because my hands were still cold, partly because I did not quite trust my voice yet. The school gate came into view at **8:12**, three minutes before the bell. I jumped out, thanked Uncle Xu twice, and ran inside. My seat in Classroom 3 was as quiet and normal as any other Monday morning. Only the cold bread, still hidden in my bag, reminded me that the morning had nearly gone very differently.

⑥ At lunch, I sat beside Xu Liang and finally said the right "thank you" — the longer kind. He waved it away easily. "It's only a car ride. You would do the same for me." That, I thought, was probably true. But there was still a quieter lesson I had to take home with me: **the real mistake had not happened at 7:42** in front of the clock. The real mistake had happened the night before, the small "five more minutes" I had given myself at eleven. That evening I wrote a short line on the first page of my new notebook: "*Tomorrow morning starts the night before.*" I drew a thin line under it, and closed the notebook.

⑦ I have never missed the school bus again. My alarm is set at 6:25 now, five minutes earlier than before, and I do not touch it twice. But whenever I walk past that low stone by the old bookstore, where I once sat shivering in my thin coat, I remember two things together: the fear of watching a clock turn red in my hand, and the warm, simple kindness of a friend who did not hesitate. **Some lessons arrive late; the best teachers are sometimes our own mistakes — and the quiet help we do not deserve, but receive anyway.**

---

**注释：** alarm 闹钟；emergency 紧急情况；egg-and-ham bun 蛋火腿包；seat belt 安全带

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer miss the school bus that Monday?**

A. Because the bus was late for the first time.  
B. Because he stayed up past eleven the night before finishing a history project, and his brain treated one press on "stop" as the same as getting up.  
C. Because his parents forgot to drive him.  
D. Because there was a heavy snowstorm.

**2. Why did the writer NOT take a taxi?**

A. Because he was afraid of cars.  
B. Because there were no taxis in the area.  
C. Because his wallet only had fifteen yuan, which was not enough.  
D. Because taxis were not allowed to stop at his corner.

**3. What did Xu Liang and his father do when the writer called?**

A. They said no and hung up quickly.  
B. They promised to come pick him up; the grey car arrived in about three minutes; Uncle Xu did not scold him, and Xu Liang shared half of his own egg-and-ham bun.  
C. They called the writer's mother instead.  
D. They sent a taxi to come and get him.

**4. What "quieter lesson" did the writer take from this morning (Paragraph 6)?**

A. That alarm clocks are useless.  
B. That the real mistake was not at 7:42 in front of the clock; it was at eleven the night before, when he gave himself "five more minutes"; tomorrow morning starts the night before.  
C. That students should never do homework at night.  
D. That it is always the parents' fault if a student is late.

**5. Which two things does the writer remember together when he passes the low stone by the old bookstore (Paragraph 7)?**

A. The fear of watching a clock turn red, and the warm, simple kindness of a friend who did not hesitate.  
B. The smell of the bus, and the taste of his breakfast.  
C. The noise of the streets, and the anger of his father.  
D. The cold bread, and the old books on sale.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "stayed up past eleven ... one tiny push on the stop button"  
【解析】熬夜后懒得起床导致迟到。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "my wallet had only fifteen yuan"  
【解析】钱不够打车。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④⑤段 "Uncle Xu did not scold me ... Xu Liang gave me half of his own breakfast"  
【解析】徐亮父子及时来接并分享早餐。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "the real mistake had not happened at 7:42 ... Tomorrow morning starts the night before"  
【解析】真正的错发生在前一晚的偷懒。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "the fear of watching a clock turn red ... the warm, simple kindness of a friend"  
【解析】末段并置"恐惧与友情"。其他均无依据。故选 A。
