# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Empty Seat on Bus 42**

For six months, Bus 42 and I had an agreement. Every school morning at 07:12, the bus arrived at the third telephone pole near my home. I jumped on, paid one **yuan**, and rode in silence to school. I chose the same seat every time: the last one by the window, the sixth row from the front. It was not a special seat. It was only empty because nobody else wanted it — perhaps because the heater under it was broken, or because the window behind it did not close properly, letting in a cold thin wind in winter. But I liked it. From that seat, I could watch the street without anyone bothering me.

At the beginning of March, something changed. An old man with silver hair began to ride Bus 42. He was tall, a little bent, and he held a brown folder to his chest as if it were made of glass. He got on at the stop after mine and always sat in the seat opposite me — the one with the broken heater's twin problem: a door that squeaked every time the bus turned. We never spoke. We only nodded, the way polite strangers nod on a cold morning, and then looked out of our own windows.

One Tuesday, I woke up late. By the time I reached the stop, Bus 42 was already pulling away. I had to run. My backpack was open, and a small pink notebook fell out without me noticing. I caught the bus at the next stop, out of breath. When I sat down in my usual seat, the old man looked at me strangely. Then, without saying anything, he passed the pink notebook across the aisle. His hand was shaking.

"I picked it up from the ground," he said. "It fell from your bag."

I thanked him quickly and hid the notebook in my coat. Inside were my private thoughts — I had been writing in it every night since my grandfather died. I was afraid the old man had read it.

Two stops later, he got off. He left his brown folder on the seat. Everyone said, "Someone left a folder!" but the bus was already moving. I grabbed it and told the driver I would return it.

At school that day, I opened the folder just a little. A yellow card fell out. On it was written:

*"To whoever finds this: I am a retired teacher. I lost my daughter five years ago. Every morning I ride Bus 42 to the park where she used to play. Please do not worry about me. Life is sometimes heavy, but small rides help."*

I stared at the card for a long time. Suddenly, my own pink notebook did not feel so private. My grief was not so lonely. That afternoon, I bought a small box of sweet biscuits and put it inside his folder together with a short note: *"Thank you for the notebook. I am sorry about your daughter. Please have some biscuits on the ride tomorrow. — The girl in the window seat."*

The next morning, when the old man boarded Bus 42, he opened the folder, saw the biscuits, and looked at me. He did not smile with his mouth. He smiled with his eyes — a very quiet, very warm smile. And I learned that a bus seat, even one with a broken heater and a windy window, can carry far more than passengers. It can carry kindness across generations, from one sad heart to another.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer always sit in the last seat by the window?**

A. It was the warmest seat on the bus.  
B. The teacher asked her to sit there.  
C. It was empty because others disliked it, and she could watch the street quietly.  
D. The driver was her uncle.

**2. What did the old man do when the writer's pink notebook fell?**

A. He kept it for himself.  
B. He passed the notebook back to her.  
C. He threw it out of the window.  
D. He gave it to another student.

**3. What did the yellow card inside the brown folder say?**

A. The old man was a singer in a big hall.  
B. He rides Bus 42 every day to buy vegetables.  
C. He was a retired teacher whose daughter had died five years ago, and the bus rides helped him.  
D. He was looking for someone to marry.

**4. Why did the writer feel less lonely after reading the card?**

A. Because the old man bought her a new bag.  
B. Because she realized her grief was not so lonely — other people also carry loss quietly.  
C. Because the bus driver waved at her.  
D. Because the bus was now faster.

**5. What is the best title for the story?**

A. The Broken Heater on Bus 42.  
B. A Kind Smile Across Two Seats.  
C. How to Catch a Bus on Time.  
D. The Girl Who Hated School.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"empty because nobody else wanted it... watch the street without anyone bothering"  
【解析】空座+可安静看街景。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"passed the pink notebook across the aisle"  
【解析】把本子递回她。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"retired teacher... daughter five years ago..."  
【解析】退休教师，女儿五年前过世。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】因果推理题  
【定位】"my grief was not so lonely"  
【解析】悲伤得到共鸣。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】标题归纳题  
【定位】全文主旨  
【解析】跨代温暖传递。A/C/D 均偏离。故选 B。
