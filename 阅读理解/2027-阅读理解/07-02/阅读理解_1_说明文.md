# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Do Volcanoes Sleep for So Long — and Then Wake Up?**

A **volcano** is a mountain with a deep hole leading to hot rock, or **magma**, far under the ground. Some volcanoes explode every few years, throwing out clouds of ash and rivers of red rock. Others seem to do nothing — they stand quiet for hundreds of years. People build towns on their sides, tourists take photos from their tops. Then, one day, a volcano that has "slept" for centuries suddenly wakes up. How is this possible? Why do some volcanoes sleep so long, and what wakes them up?

To understand this, we must first remember what a volcano really is: a **tunnel**. Deep under the Earth's surface, huge plates of rock move slowly against each other. Where two plates meet, the rock can be pushed down, heated, and melted. This melted rock, or magma, is lighter than solid rock, so it slowly rises. If it finds a tunnel, it pushes upward and finally escapes at the top. That escape is what we call an **eruption**.

But rising magma does not happen every day. It depends on two things: **supply** and **pressure**. If only a little magma is being produced down below, the tunnel can stay quiet for a long time. The magma may even cool and harden inside the tunnel, forming a hard plug of rock. As long as this plug stays strong and no new magma pushes against it, the volcano sleeps. From the outside, everything looks normal — grass and trees grow; birds build nests on the rocks.

So why does a sleeping volcano wake up? Scientists point to three common reasons. The first is **new magma from below**. When fresh hot rock arrives, pressure builds inside the tunnel. Bit by bit, the old plug cracks. When the pressure finally wins, the plug is thrown out and hot material shoots into the air. The second reason is **gas**. Magma contains gases like water vapour and carbon dioxide. When magma rises and the pressure above drops, these gases expand quickly, just like the air in a shaken bottle of cola. The gases push the magma up faster and faster. The third reason is **outside events**. A big earthquake near the volcano can shake the plug loose, or a heavy rain can suddenly cool the top of the tunnel, causing unusual stress. Any of these can end the sleep.

This is why scientists watch volcanoes very carefully, even those that look **harmless**. They measure tiny earthquakes, they study the gases around the mountain, and they use satellites to check if the ground is slowly rising. A rise of only a few **centimetres** can mean that magma is filling up inside. If the signs grow stronger, warnings go out and people nearby are asked to leave.

Volcanoes remind us that the Earth is not a quiet stone ball. Under our feet, hot rock is always moving, slowly but never stopping. A volcano's long sleep is not real silence — it is just a pause between two chapters of the same long story. Knowing this, we can admire volcanoes, live near them carefully, and respect the power they hold.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is magma?**

A. A kind of cold water in the mountain.  
B. Melted hot rock deep under the ground.  
C. A type of volcanic grass.  
D. A large glass bottle.

**2. Why does a volcano sleep for a long time?**

A. Because people build towns on it.  
B. Because birds build nests on its rocks.  
C. Because little new magma comes, and a hard plug stops the tunnel.  
D. Because it is afraid of earthquakes.

**3. Which of the following is NOT mentioned as a reason that wakes a volcano?**

A. New magma rising from below.  
B. Gases expanding like air in a shaken cola bottle.  
C. Outside events such as a big earthquake.  
D. The temperature drop of the sea a thousand kilometres away.

**4. Why do scientists watch even "harmless-looking" volcanoes?**

A. To sell photos to tourists.  
B. Because small changes can be warning signs of an eruption.  
C. Because the grass near them is very rare.  
D. Because the volcanoes are beautiful at night.

**5. What is the main idea of this passage?**

A. Volcanoes make the Earth cold.  
B. Volcanoes sleep because they are tired.  
C. Volcanoes sleep and wake according to scientific reasons we can study.  
D. Volcanoes only exist in old stories.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"melted rock, or magma..."  
【解析】地下熔融热岩。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"little magma... hard plug"  
【解析】岩浆少+硬塞封住通道。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（反向）  
【定位】"new magma / gases / outside events"  
【解析】未提千公里外海水温度下降。A/B/C 均提及。故选 D。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】因果推理题  
【定位】"tiny earthquakes... satellites..."  
【解析】微小信号可能预示喷发。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文  
【解析】依据科学原理解释休眠与喷发。A/B/D 均偏离。故选 C。
