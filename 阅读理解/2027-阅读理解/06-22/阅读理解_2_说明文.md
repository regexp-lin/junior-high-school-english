# 阅读理解（说明文） **How Does Our Body Tell Us We Are Thirsty?**

On a hot summer afternoon, you have been running in the playground. Your mouth feels dry, your throat feels sticky, and you really want a cup of cool water. This feeling is called **thirst**. It seems simple, but the system inside your body that creates it is quite clever. How does the body actually tell us that we need to drink?

The story begins in the blood. Our blood is mostly water, but it also carries many small particles, for example salt and sugar. Scientists use the word "**concentration**" to describe how "thick" the blood is. When we lose water — through sweat, through breathing out, or simply by not drinking for many hours — the amount of water in the blood goes down, while the amount of salt stays about the same. So the blood becomes a little "thicker". This small change is the first sign of trouble.

Now here is the clever part. Deep inside the brain, there is a small area called the **hypothalamus**. It is only the size of a peanut, but it is like a tiny control room for many body jobs. Inside this area, there are special cells that measure the concentration of the blood all the time, as you read this page. When the blood becomes too thick, these cells send a fast signal: "We need water!"

The brain then does several things. First, it sends a message to the mouth to produce less saliva, so the mouth feels dry. Second, it tells the skin to sweat a little less, so we lose less water while our body is low. Third, it sends a chemical called **ADH** into the blood; this chemical tells the kidneys to keep more water and to let out less urine. You have probably noticed: on a hot day when you drink very little, your urine becomes dark yellow. That is a clear sign that your kidneys are working hard to save every drop.

But the most important action is at the brain level: the hypothalamus creates the feeling of "thirst" itself. This feeling is your message to yourself: go and drink. Just looking at a glass of water or hearing the sound of a water tap can then make your body react quickly.

There is, however, one problem. The feeling of thirst often arrives **late**. By the time you feel truly thirsty, your body may already have lost 1 – 2 % of its water. For a 50 kg student, this is about 500 mL — two small cups. This is why many doctors say: do not wait until you feel very thirsty. Drink small amounts of water every hour, especially in summer, during sports or during long study sessions.

A healthy body is a river always in need of fresh water. When the river runs low, it sends a quiet message. Our job is simply to listen.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义理解题  
【定位】第 2 段 "how 'thick' the blood is"  
【解析】指血液浓度。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段 "small area called the hypothalamus"  
【解析】下丘脑检测。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段 "ADH... keep more water and to let out less urine"  
【解析】ADH 减少排尿。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段 末句  
【解析】肾脏保水导致尿色加深。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】末段  
【解析】预防性小口饮水。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
