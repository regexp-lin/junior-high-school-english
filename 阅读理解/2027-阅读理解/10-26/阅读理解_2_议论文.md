# 阅读理解（议论文） **Is One Hour of Sports Every School Day Really Necessary?**

① Since the new school-life rules came out, our city has asked every middle school to offer students at least **one full hour of sports every school day** — that is, thirty minutes in the morning and thirty minutes in the afternoon, five days a week. Parents' reactions are split. Some cheer: "At last!" Others shake their heads: "One hour is too much; our children should be reading instead." After thinking for some time, I believe the **one-hour rule is right — and we should make it work well, not water it down.**

② The strongest reason is **health**. Doctors in our country say the **eye-sight problem** among Chinese middle-school students is one of the most serious in the world: more than **70%** of Grade 9 students now wear glasses. Studies from the Shanghai Children's Hospital (2022) and Sichuan University (2024) show clearly that students who spend **at least 60 minutes outdoors** each day are far less likely to become short-sighted. Besides the eyes, one hour of movement helps the heart, the spine, and — very important — **sleep at night**. A tired body finds a warm bed sooner.

③ The second reason is **learning**. It sounds strange, but moving less does not mean learning more. Research from around the world — from Beijing to Finland — has found the same thing: students who have regular P.E. time during the school day often do **better**, not worse, in maths and language. A moving body pumps more blood and oxygen to the brain, and a rested brain remembers **more** in the 40 minutes after sport than in a tired afternoon at the desk. In other words, one hour of sport is not "one hour away from books"; it is "one hour that makes the next book easier to read."

④ The third reason is about **growing up as a whole person**. On the basketball court or the badminton side, students meet problems that no textbook can teach: how to lose politely, how to pass the ball at the right time, how to call a hurt classmate's name without embarrassing him. These are the quiet skills we will need in our families, at our jobs, and in our cities. Take the sport away, and you take away one of the few school places where a student is just a **human among humans**, not a name on a score sheet.

⑤ Of course, people who worry have reasons too. Grade 9 students, facing the Senior High School Entrance Exam in June, often feel every minute matters. Some parents fear that one hour of sport **eats into** review time. Others worry about hot summer days and cold winter days on the playground. A real pain is also the **gap between schools**: some schools have 8 sports teachers and two gyms, while others have 2 teachers and only a dusty yard.

⑥ These concerns, however, do not kill the rule; they only ask that the **hour be spent wisely**. Four small changes can do a lot. First, the hour can be **split**: 25 minutes of fast exercise in the morning, 20 minutes of walking or games after lunch, and 15 minutes of stretching before afternoon classes. Second, on bad-weather days, schools should prepare **indoor options**: skipping rope, balance bar, simple yoga, or table tennis. Third, students who join the school team already do more than one hour a day; they should not be "double-counted", and their homework load should be quietly reduced. Fourth, the government should send **extra sports teachers** and basic equipment to schools with fewer resources, so that "one hour" means the same kind of hour everywhere.

⑦ Some still ask: "What about the few students who hate sport?" The answer is not to force them into basketball. A school can offer **ten kinds** of activity — running, walking, Chinese boxing, table tennis, badminton, dancing, yoga, paper-kite flying, rope-skipping, or even **tai-chi with a grandmother group** in the park next door. The goal is not to train every student into an athlete. It is to make sure every student leaves school at 17:00 with a body that has been **honoured**, not forgotten.

⑧ A school day is about six hours of mind-work. Spending one out of the remaining hours on the body is not a waste; it is the **battery** that keeps the lamp of learning burning. One hour a day, five days a week, for three years — that is the simplest medicine our schools can offer. **Swallow it cheerfully.**

---

**注释：** short-sighted 近视的；oxygen 氧气；score sheet 计分表；double-count 重复计算；equipment 设备

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What does research from the Shanghai Children's Hospital (2022) and Sichuan University (2024) show?**

A. Glasses make students stronger.  
B. Students who spend at least 60 minutes outdoors each day are far less likely to become short-sighted.  
C. Students should study harder at their desks.  
D. Sport is not good for the eyes.

**2. Why, according to Paragraph ③, is one hour of sport NOT "an hour away from books"?**

A. Because sport is easier than study.  
B. Because students who have regular P.E. time often do better, not worse, in maths and language; a moving body pumps more blood and oxygen to the brain.  
C. Because sport replaces homework.  
D. Because sport cancels tests.

**3. What is one real worry that the writer admits in Paragraph ⑤?**

A. Students hate fresh air.  
B. Grade 9 students facing the Senior High Entrance Exam fear that one hour of sport eats into review time; there is also a real gap between schools in teachers and equipment.  
C. Schools will be closed.  
D. No school has a playground.

**4. Which of the following is NOT among the writer's four suggestions in Paragraph ⑥?**

A. Split the hour into several smaller blocks during the day.  
B. Prepare indoor options for bad weather.  
C. Double-count the training of school-team students and quietly reduce their homework load.  
D. Cancel P.E. for Grade 9 in the last month before the exam.

**5. What is the writer's final idea about the one-hour rule?**

A. It is a waste of time that should be removed.  
B. It is only useful for Grade 7 students.  
C. It is a simple, valuable "battery" for learning; schools should spend the hour wisely (many kinds of activity, fair resources, flexible timing) rather than water the rule down.  
D. It should be one hour only in summer.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "at least 60 minutes outdoors ... far less likely to become short-sighted"  
【解析】户外时间与视力保护的因果明示。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "do better, not worse ... more blood and oxygen to the brain"  
【解析】运动反哺学习。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "eats into review time" + "gap between schools"  
【解析】双重担忧直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（否定）  
【定位】第⑥段四项建议  
【解析】A、B、C 皆在建议中，只有"取消 Grade 9 最后一月体育课"文中未提，且违背作者立场。故选 D。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段定调 + 第⑧段比喻 "battery that keeps the lamp of learning burning"  
【解析】作者坚决支持且主张用好。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。
