# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Umbrella I Lent to a Stranger**

① Last autumn, on a grey Tuesday afternoon, the sky outside our school turned from light grey to deep black in less than ten minutes. I was standing at the school gate, holding a **blue folding umbrella** that my grandmother had bought for me the year before. On one side she had sewn a small white cloth square with my name and class in neat black thread: *Chen Xinyi, Class 3, Grade 8*. I loved that umbrella. It was small, but it was mine in a way that few other things were.

② A thin rain began, then thicker, then something like a small waterfall. Students rushed out, some with bright new umbrellas, some with their schoolbags held over their heads, laughing. Near the flower bed by the gate, I saw an **old woman** in a faded grey coat, holding two heavy cloth bags. She had clearly not expected the rain at all. The bags were getting wet, her white hair was getting wet, her thin cotton shoes were already dark from the ground. She looked at the sky, then at the bus stop fifty metres away, and then simply stood there — the way a person stands when they do not know what to do next.

③ I opened my mouth, closed it, opened it again. I could not share the umbrella with her easily — she was a stranger, and the umbrella was small. But the rain was not going to stop. So I walked across the wet stones, lifted the umbrella a little higher, and said, as politely as I could, "Grandma, please take this. I live just around the corner. I can run home." She looked surprised. "Child, no, no, you'll catch a cold." "It's only five minutes for me, Grandma," I said. "Please." I pushed the handle into her free hand before she could refuse again. Then I ran.

④ I was soaked to the skin by the time I reached our small front door. My mother took one look at me, pulled me inside, and laughed the way mothers laugh when they are a little angry and a little proud at the same time. "Where is your umbrella?" she asked. I told her. She was quiet for a moment. Then she handed me a dry towel and said softly, "That was a good umbrella. And a kinder thing to do." She made ginger soup for me that evening. I went to bed thinking, a little sadly, that I would probably never see the umbrella again. The rain outside kept falling all night.

⑤ Two weeks later, on a Saturday morning, someone knocked at our door. Standing outside was the **same old woman**, dry this time, wearing a clean brown coat. In her hand was my blue folding umbrella — dry, clean, and freshly wiped. Beside the umbrella was a small paper bag. "Child," she said, smiling so widely that her eyes almost disappeared, "your grandmother sewed your name inside. I went to your school twice to ask where Chen Xinyi lived. A very kind teacher helped me. I should have brought this back sooner."

⑥ I was too shocked to speak properly. She pressed the paper bag into my hand: inside were **four warm sweet-potato buns**, still soft. "I make these at home," she said simply. "My grandson used to love them. He's in college now, far away. You reminded me of him that day." I looked down at the little white name tag on my umbrella, sewn carefully by my grandmother. It had travelled across a rainy evening, stayed in a stranger's home for two weeks, and now come back — carrying with it a new friendship.

⑦ I did not understand everything that day, but I understood this: **a small kindness can come back in a shape you never expect**. The umbrella is still with me. The name tag has faded a little, but every time I open it in the rain, I think of that grey Tuesday and the old woman's wet white hair. And whenever I see someone standing by a gate, not knowing where to go, I try to remember my mother's words by the door — *"a kinder thing to do"* — and then I act, even if the sky is dark and I am the smaller one.

---

**注释：** folding umbrella 折叠伞；flower bed 花坛；soaked to the skin 湿透；ginger soup 姜汤；sweet-potato bun 红薯包

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer feel especially attached to her blue folding umbrella?**

A. Because it was very expensive.  
B. Because her grandmother had bought it and sewn a small cloth name tag on it.  
C. Because it had a bright colour she chose herself.  
D. Because it was waterproof and could be used as a tent.

**2. What problem was the old woman facing outside the school gate?**

A. She could not remember her own home address.  
B. She was holding two heavy wet bags in a heavy rainstorm and had no umbrella; she seemed not to know what to do next.  
C. She had lost her grandson in the rain.  
D. She was waiting for a school bus that was delayed.

**3. What did the writer's mother do when she saw her soaked daughter come home?**

A. She scolded her harshly and refused to speak to her.  
B. She laughed — a mix of a little angry and a little proud — gave her a dry towel, and later made ginger soup.  
C. She immediately sent her back to find the umbrella.  
D. She called the school to complain.

**4. How did the old woman find the writer's home?**

A. She used a phone number on the umbrella.  
B. The grandmother had sewn a name-and-class tag inside; the old woman went to the school twice and a kind teacher helped her.  
C. She met the writer again on the street by chance.  
D. She posted a notice in the newspaper.

**5. What is the main message of the last paragraph?**

A. People should always keep all their things safely and never lend them.  
B. A small kindness can come back in a shape you never expect; one should still act kindly even when conditions are hard.  
C. Umbrellas last longer than friendships.  
D. Rainy days are the worst days of the year.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "a small white cloth square with my name and class in neat black thread"  
【解析】外婆缝制的名字牌赋予伞特殊意义。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "two heavy cloth bags ... she simply stood there — the way a person stands when they do not know what to do next"  
【解析】老太手提重袋未带伞，不知所措。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "laughed the way mothers laugh when they are a little angry and a little proud ... made ginger soup"  
【解析】妈妈微怒又自豪，给毛巾和姜汤。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "your grandmother sewed your name inside. I went to your school twice ... A very kind teacher helped me"  
【解析】靠伞内名字和老师帮忙上门归还。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨/观点题  
【定位】第⑦段 "a small kindness can come back in a shape you never expect ... I act, even if the sky is dark and I am the smaller one"  
【解析】善举会以意想不到的方式回到身边，应继续行善。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。
