# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Old Bicycle and the New Route**

① My grandfather's old bicycle had been standing quietly in the corner of our garage for almost ten years. Its silver paint had turned grey, the seat was cracked, and one of the bells was missing. Every time I walked past it, I felt a little sorry — it seemed to be waiting for something. Last summer, I finally decided to wake it up.

② It all started on a hot Monday in July. The bus I usually took to Grandma's village had stopped running because the road was being repaired. Mum said, "You can wait until August, or you can find another way." I was already bored after only three days of holiday, so I chose the second answer. That night I found the old bicycle, cleaned the dust off it, and pumped the tyres. The next morning, with a simple map and a water bottle in my bag, I pushed it out of the garage.

③ The first kilometre was easy. The sun was still soft, and the road was flat. I even started to sing. But soon the road turned into a narrow path between rice fields, and then into a long, slow hill. My legs began to burn, and sweat ran into my eyes. I got off the bike and walked, feeling a little silly. An old farmer who was cutting grass saw me and smiled. "Not that way, young man," he said kindly. "If you go left at the big tree, the hill ends after five minutes." I thanked him and turned left.

④ He was right. The hill soon ended, and behind it was a view I had never seen before: a quiet river, a small stone bridge, and rows of yellow sunflowers taller than me. I stopped and took out my water bottle. My heart was still beating fast, but I was smiling. No bus would ever have shown me this place.

⑤ I reached Grandma's village around noon — two hours later than usual, but with a face full of sunshine. Grandma opened the door, saw my red cheeks, and laughed. "You rode that old thing all the way here?" she asked. I nodded proudly. At lunch, she told me the bicycle had once belonged to Grandpa, who rode it to work every day for thirty years. "He used to say," she added softly, "that the long road teaches you things a short road cannot."

⑥ Now I understand what Grandpa meant. The road had taken me longer, but it had given me a kind farmer, a hidden bridge, a field of sunflowers, and a quiet pride I had never felt on a bus. Sometimes the old thing in the corner of the garage is not waiting to be thrown away. It is waiting to take you somewhere new.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer decide to use the old bicycle?**

A. Grandma asked him to sell it.  
B. The usual bus stopped running because of road repairs.  
C. His parents bought him a new bike.  
D. He wanted to race with his friends.

**2. What happened when the writer was going up the long hill?**

A. He gave up and went home.  
B. He met an old farmer who showed him an easier way.  
C. He fell off the bicycle and hurt his leg.  
D. He called his father to pick him up.

**3. What did the writer see after he turned left at the big tree?**

A. A crowded town and a big shopping mall.  
B. A quiet river, a stone bridge and tall sunflowers.  
C. A broken road and some dangerous dogs.  
D. A train station and many tourists.

**4. What did Grandpa often say about long roads, according to Grandma?**

A. Long roads are always dangerous.  
B. Long roads teach you things a short road cannot.  
C. Long roads are only for old people.  
D. Long roads waste too much time.

**5. What does the writer want to tell us through this story?**

A. Old things should be thrown away to make room.  
B. Riding a bicycle is faster than taking a bus.  
C. Sometimes a longer, harder journey can give us surprises and growth.  
D. Farmers always know the way better than maps.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "The bus... had stopped running because the road was being repaired."  
【解析】因修路公交停运，作者改骑自行车。A 项卖车无据；C 项未买新车；D 项非竞赛。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "An old farmer... said kindly, 'Not that way, young man...'"  
【解析】老农指路走捷径。A 项未放弃；C/D 项无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "a quiet river, a small stone bridge, and rows of yellow sunflowers..."  
【解析】转弯后见到河、石桥、向日葵。A/C/D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "the long road teaches you things a short road cannot."  
【解析】爷爷认为长路能教人短路学不到的东西。A/C/D 均属张冠李戴或无中生有。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段全段  
【解析】文章借一次长途骑行，说明更长更难的旅途能带来惊喜与成长。A 与原文反；B 非主旨；D 以偏概全。故选 C。
