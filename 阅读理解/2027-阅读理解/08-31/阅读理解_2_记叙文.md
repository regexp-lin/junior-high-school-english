# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Last Class with Teacher Wang**

① Nobody in Grade 8, Class 3 expected that **Teacher Wang** would retire that summer. She had been our English teacher since Grade 7, and she still walked up and down between the desks with the same **straight back**, the same **quiet voice**, the same **red pen** tucked behind one ear. Then, on the **last Friday afternoon** of the term, just before the final English class, our monitor whispered that Teacher Wang was leaving school for good. We sat down in silence, staring at the empty chalkboard.

② Teacher Wang walked in two minutes late, which was unusual — she was famous for her strict **8:02 rule**. "Good afternoon, everyone," she said, without opening her book. "Today I will not teach grammar. Today I only want to **listen**." She sat down on her chair, crossed her hands on the desk, and looked at us slowly, one row at a time. "If you have one question about English, or about life, or about yourself," she said, "please stand up and ask. I will try to answer every one of them."

③ For a long minute, no one moved. Then **Xiao Chen** stood up first. He was the class's quiet boy, the one who usually slept through vocabulary tests. His voice shook a little. "Teacher Wang, why did you never shout at me, even though I often fell asleep?" The class laughed nervously. Teacher Wang did not laugh. She thought for a moment and then answered: "Because I watched you every day. A boy who sleeps in class but never misses school is not a lazy boy. He is a **tired** boy. Shouting at a tired boy only makes him **more** tired."

④ The room became very quiet. Slowly, **one hand after another** went up. **Lin Ya**, the top student, asked why Teacher Wang always drew a small star at the end of her essays, even when the mark was already high. Teacher Wang smiled. "Because a high mark is for the paper," she said. "A small star is for the **person**."

⑤ **Ma Jia**, who always argued that English was useless, stood up next. She even crossed her arms, just in case. "Teacher, my father says English will not matter in twenty years, because machines can translate anything. So why do we still have to learn it?" Teacher Wang nodded, slowly. "Your father may be partly right," she answered. "Maybe machines will speak better than most of us. But have you ever tried to **thank** someone in a language they have spent their life using? In that moment, the word is not about translation. It is about **respect**. Machines may translate language, but they cannot give respect for you. You must give it yourself."

⑥ I had been holding my own question in my pocket like a folded piece of paper. When my hand finally shot up, I was the last one. "Teacher Wang," I said, "I am not a good student. My marks are always in the middle. When I try harder, I still stay in the middle. Sometimes I feel **useless**. Do you have anything for me?"

⑦ She looked at me for a long time. "Come here," she said gently. I walked to her desk, in front of the whole class. She took the **red pen** from behind her ear and handed it to me. "Keep this pen," she said. "A student who knows she is in the middle and still keeps trying is **not useless** — she is **honest**. Use this pen to mark your own work from now on. Be as kind to yourself as I have tried to be to you." I did not cry in that moment. I only felt something small and warm grow in my chest, the way a heater warms a cold room.

⑧ The bell rang at last. Teacher Wang stood up, bowed to us once — yes, bowed — and said, "Thank you for these two years. You have taught me more than I have taught you." Then she turned, picked up her bag, and walked out of Classroom 3. Nobody spoke for a long time. Xiao Chen wiped his eyes. Ma Jia was looking at her own hands. Lin Ya sat very straight, but her pencil was trembling.

⑨ That summer holiday, I started keeping an English diary. I use a new blue pencil case every term, but the **red pen** always rests on top. Whenever I feel "middle", I take it out, look at it, and write my honest sentence of the day. I still do not have top marks in English. But I have a pen, a memory, and a quiet promise — and, I think, that is more than enough.

---

**注释：** monitor 班长；strict 严格的；translate 翻译；bowed (过去式) 鞠躬

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why was Teacher Wang two minutes late for the last class?**

A. Because she lost her way to the classroom.  
B. The writer does not tell us exactly, but it was unusual for her strict "8:02 rule".  
C. Because she was looking for her red pen.  
D. Because the bell was broken that day.

**2. Why, according to Teacher Wang, did she never shout at Xiao Chen for sleeping in class?**

A. Because she did not notice him at all.  
B. Because she thought sleep was more important than English.  
C. Because she believed he was not lazy but tired, and shouting would only make him more tired.  
D. Because he was her favourite student.

**3. How does Teacher Wang answer Ma Jia's question about machines and translation?**

A. She agrees that English is now completely useless.  
B. She says machines will never translate any language.  
C. She admits Ma Jia's father may be partly right, but points out that machines cannot replace the respect we give when we thank someone in their own language.  
D. She orders Ma Jia to sit down without any answer.

**4. What does Teacher Wang give to the writer at the front of the class?**

A. A new English textbook.  
B. A letter in a white envelope.  
C. Her **red pen**, which had been behind her ear.  
D. A school certificate.

**5. Which is the best meaning of Teacher Wang's words "A student who knows she is in the middle and still keeps trying is not useless — she is honest"?**

A. Average marks are the only thing that matters.  
B. The writer should give up trying because she is in the middle.  
C. Keeping trying despite being an average student is something worthy of respect.  
D. Only top students can be "honest".

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "walked in two minutes late, which was unusual — she was famous for her strict 8:02 rule"  
【解析】文章仅强调这不合她素来严格的习惯，没有给出具体原因。A、C、D 均为捏造。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段末 "He is a tired boy. Shouting at a tired boy only makes him more tired"  
【解析】王老师认为他累而非懒。A、B、D 均与原文相悖。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Your father may be partly right ... Machines may translate language, but they cannot give respect for you"  
【解析】老师部分承认父亲之言，但强调尊重源于自己而非机器。A、B、D 均误读。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "She took the red pen from behind her ear and handed it to me"  
【解析】老师把耳后那支红笔给了"我"。A、B、D 均无依据。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】句意理解/主旨题  
【定位】第⑦段 "A student who knows she is in the middle and still keeps trying is not useless — she is honest"  
【解析】该句肯定了中等但坚持努力的学生的价值——这是一种值得尊敬的"诚实"。A、B、D 均为曲解。故选 C。
