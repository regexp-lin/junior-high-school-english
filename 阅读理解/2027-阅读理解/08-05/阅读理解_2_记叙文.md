# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Letter I Wrote but Never Sent**

① When I was thirteen, I had a quiet quarrel with my best friend Zheng. It was small at first — she said I never listened when she spoke about her new violin lessons; I said she had become boring and "only thought about music". We both went home angry. For the next week, we sat at different tables in the canteen. In the corridor, we walked past each other like two strangers who happened to share the same school uniform.

② My mother noticed that something was wrong. One Saturday afternoon, she sat down beside me and said only one sentence: "If you cannot speak to her yet, why don't you just write it down?" She left a blank sheet of paper on the desk and went to the kitchen. I looked at the paper for a long time. Then, almost without thinking, I picked up a pen.

③ The first line was easy: "*Dear Zheng, I am sorry.*" The second line was harder. I wrote that I did not really mean she was boring; I was only jealous that she had something she truly loved. My grades were dropping, my drawing was ugly, and she had the violin — something her hands and heart clearly belonged to. When I wrote that sentence, I suddenly understood why I was so angry. It was not about her; it was about me.

④ I kept writing. I told her I missed walking home with her after school, arguing about which canteen dish was the worst. I told her I had, a few days before, walked a long way around the building just to avoid meeting her, and that I felt like a small, silly person because of it. By the end of the letter, my eyes felt warm. I signed my name at the bottom, folded the paper, and put it in an envelope.

⑤ The next morning, I carried the envelope to school in my bag. During the whole morning, my heart kept jumping — each time the classroom door opened, I was afraid Zheng would come in, and also afraid she would not. At lunch time, I stood outside her classroom with the envelope in my hand. I could see her back, bent over a book. I stood there for nearly three minutes. Then a teacher walked past, and Zheng turned her head. For a second, our eyes met. Her face went a little red; so did mine.

⑥ I suddenly could not do it. I turned around, went back to my seat, and pushed the envelope deep into my bag. During the afternoon, I told myself I would send it the next day. I did not. A week passed. I did not. A month passed. I did not. The letter stayed in a small blue folder at the bottom of my drawer at home.

⑦ And then something strange happened. Slowly, almost without noticing, Zheng and I began to talk again. It started with a maths homework question. Then with a comment on a football match. One afternoon she invited me to listen to her practise. I sat by her window as she played a simple piece, and for a few minutes we did not need any words at all. We never said "sorry" out loud. But something between us had moved back into the right place.

⑧ The letter, meanwhile, stayed in the drawer. I sometimes thought about burning it or tearing it up, but I never did. A few years later, before moving to a new city for senior high school, I opened the folder again. The paper had turned a little yellow. I read my own words — the apology, the honest jealousy, the silly small walk around the building. I did not feel ashamed. Strangely, I felt grateful.

⑨ I understand now why I kept the letter. I did not send it, but **writing it had already changed me**. In those slow, careful sentences, I had to look at myself honestly, without shouting. I had to admit why I was hurt, and why I was unkind. After that, going back to Zheng was not really a decision any more; it was almost natural. The letter had done its work before the envelope was ever opened.

⑩ Now, whenever I am angry with someone I love, I still write. Sometimes I send the words; more often I do not. But every time, I remember that small blue folder, and my mother's simple sentence on a Saturday afternoon: "*If you cannot speak to her yet, why don't you just write it down?*"

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer quarrel with Zheng at the beginning?**

A. Because Zheng had stolen the writer's violin.  
B. Because the writer felt Zheng did not listen, and Zheng felt the writer was only interested in music.  
C. Because Zheng said the writer never listened, and the writer said Zheng had become "boring" and "only thought about music".  
D. Because they got different marks on a test.

**2. What did the writer's mother suggest on Saturday afternoon?**

A. She told the writer to phone Zheng at once.  
B. She told the writer to forget Zheng forever.  
C. She suggested that if the writer could not speak to Zheng yet, she could write it down.  
D. She told the writer to ask the teacher to help.

**3. What did the writer realise while writing the letter?**

A. That she had always hated Zheng secretly.  
B. That she was not truly angry with Zheng; she was jealous because Zheng had something (the violin) that she truly loved.  
C. That her own violin lessons were more important.  
D. That her mother did not understand her.

**4. Why did the writer **not** give the letter to Zheng in the end?**

A. Because the teacher took the letter away.  
B. Because the writer lost courage at the last minute, turned around and put the letter back in her bag; later they naturally began to talk again.  
C. Because Zheng refused to read any letters.  
D. Because the writer decided never to speak to Zheng again.

**5. What does the writer mainly want to tell us in the last two paragraphs?**

A. Always send every letter immediately.  
B. Writing the letter had already changed the writer and helped her look at herself honestly; the act of writing, not the sending, was the real gift.  
C. Friendship is not important compared with study.  
D. Mothers should not give advice to teenagers.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段  
【解析】原文 "said I never listened... said she had become boring..."。A/B/D 均错/失真。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段  
【解析】原话："If you cannot speak to her yet, why don't you just write it down?"。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段末  
【解析】"I was only jealous..."（她嫉妒朋友有真正热爱的东西）。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤⑥⑦段  
【解析】最后一刻没勇气；后来自然重修于好。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑨段加粗句  
【解析】写下这封信的过程本身已带来改变。A/C/D 均偏。故选 B。
