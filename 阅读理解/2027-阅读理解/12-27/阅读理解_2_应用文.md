# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Student-Union "2028 New-Year Reading List" (Grade-8 Recommended, 8 Books · 4 Chinese + 4 English)**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Grade-8 Student Union Reading Sub-Committee**  
> **Coordinator**: Lin Ruoxi (8-B) · **Teacher advisor**: Mr Qin  
> **List reference**: *NYRL-2028* · **Published on the Student Union board**: **Sunday 27 December 2027**  
> **For**: Any Grade-8 Lihua student who wishes to read more than a textbook this winter break and spring term.  
> **Not for**: exams, marks, homework, competitions, or parent review.

---

① Below is Lihua Middle School's **2028 New-Year Reading List**, put together by six Grade-8 student volunteers after fifteen lunchtime meetings in November and December 2027. It recommends **eight books in total — four in Chinese and four in English (including bilingual editions)** — all chosen **by students, for students**, from the school's Green-Leaf Library and two nearby public libraries. The list is a gentle **suggestion**, not an assignment. No teacher will check if you read any of them. No parent is asked to sign anything. **Any single book, read slowly, is already a small win.** Please do not try to read all eight.

---

## 📚 Selection Rules (written by the student committee)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Students only** choose books; no teachers, no parents, no publishers. |
| 2 | **Age-appropriate**: roughly 60%–70% of words already known to a Grade-8 middle-schooler. |
| 3 | **Not from exam reading lists**; free from any school-test connection. |
| 4 | **At least one of the eight** written by a Chinese author; **at least one** by a woman; **at least one** translated from another language. |
| 5 | **No violence, no adult-only themes, no heavy sad endings**; winter is cold enough. |
| 6 | **Every book must be borrowable** for free from Green-Leaf Library or the Longhua Public Library. |

## 🕒 A Simple Reading Rhythm (optional)

| Reading pattern | Description | Who it suits |
|---|---|---|
| A. **One-Book 30-Day** | Read only one book across the 30-day break, slowly, 15 min / day | Tired students; first-time long-readers |
| B. **Two-Book Rotation** | Read one Chinese + one English at the same time, 20 min each, alternate | Steady students; helps vocabulary both ways |
| C. **Bedtime Chapter** | Read one chapter every night before 22:00 only | Students with small phones near the bed |
| D. **Listen-and-Read** | Read with a free audio book played softly at 0.8× speed | Tired eyes; long-distance train journeys home |

> **Recommended reading time**: **no more than 45 minutes at a stretch**; look 300 m into the distance for 2 minutes between stretches.  
> **Discouraged**: reading more than 2 books at the same time; underlining with four different colours; writing long book reports nobody will read.

## 🫶 Gentle Reading Rules from the Committee

| # | Rule |
|---|------|
| 1 | **A book can be put down.** If you don't enjoy it after 30 pages, close it gently and choose another. |
| 2 | **A book can be reread.** Reading *Charlotte's Web* twice is not "less" than reading two books once. |
| 3 | **A book's speed is personal.** A book that takes 6 hours for one classmate may take 12 hours for another. Both are fine. |
| 4 | **Do not rank your classmates by books finished.** Reading is not a race. |
| 5 | **No book quiz.** If somebody asks you "what was chapter 4 about?", you may answer "I do not remember — I was reading for the feeling." That is a perfectly good answer. |
| 6 | **Private reading stays private.** If a student does not wish to tell classmates what she read this winter, her silence is fully respected. |

## 💬 A Small Word from the Reading Sub-Committee

> "Dear Grade-8 friends, this is a small list, written by six of us for all of you. We argued quietly for fifteen lunch breaks to choose these eight books. There are many more good books on Earth than will fit on one piece of paper, and every year somebody writes another one. If none of these eight fits your mood this winter, walk into a quiet library, pull a book off a low shelf, read the first page under a window, and if your hand does not want to put it down, take it home. That is, perhaps, the only book recommendation rule that truly matters. May your 2028 winter be slow, warm, and full of turning pages.  
> — *Lin Ruoxi, on behalf of the Reading Sub-Committee, 27 December 2027*"
