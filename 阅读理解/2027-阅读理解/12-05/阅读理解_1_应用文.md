# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School End-of-Term Thank-You Wall 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Student Union + Arts Department**  
> **End-of-Term Thank-You Wall 2027** — *"Words Handed Back to the People Who Gave Us a Year."*  
> **Open**: **Mon 21 – Fri 25 December 2027**  
> **Venue**: **Main Hallway (Block A, Ground Floor, between the two staircases)**  
> **Hours**: 07:30 – 17:30 every day, and **Wed 23 Dec until 20:00 (the Thank-You Evening)**.

---

A long wall, one hundred and twenty cards. For the last week of the calendar year, we invite every student, teacher, parent and staff member at Lihua to write **one short, honest, hand-written thank-you** — to a person, a group, or a small everyday kindness that made our school year a little warmer. No gifts, no money, no loud shows. Only words handed back to the people who gave us a year.

---

## 🕒 How the Wall Works

1. **Take a blank card** from the small basket at the right side of the wall (kraft paper, 10 × 15 cm, with a soft pastel back).  
2. **Write one thank-you** in two to six sentences. One card = one thank-you. Write in pen, pencil, or crayon.  
3. **Write the receiver's name or group clearly**, and, **if you wish**, sign your own name. Anonymous cards are completely welcome — **more than half of the wall in 2026 was anonymous**, and that was beautiful.  
4. **Pin the card** onto the wall using the small wooden pegs provided. If the wall is full, use the overflow board to the right.  
5. **Read the others.** Please do not take any card off the wall during the week — even your own.

---

## ✍️ What Makes a Good Thank-You (Suggestions, Not Rules)

- **Be specific.** *"Thank you for always sweeping the corridor before the first bell, Ms Hao"* is stronger than *"Thank you, cleaners."*  
- **Say one concrete moment** if you can: a date, a place, a weather, a small gesture.  
- **Keep it honest.** A small quiet thank-you is a better gift than a loud, fluffy one.  
- **Be kind to yourself, too.** It is okay to thank someone who has gone.  
- It is also okay to write *"Thank you to myself, for staying this long."* — we will put those on the wall too.

---

## 🪧 Six Categories Suggested (You May Invent Your Own)

| Category | Example receiver |
|---|---|
| Teachers | subject teachers, homeroom teachers, PE & music teachers |
| Staff | cleaners, security guards, kitchen staff, bus drivers |
| Classmates | a desk-mate, a study partner, a kind stranger from another class |
| Family | a parent, a grandparent, a sibling |
| A younger/older student | a Grade-1 "little brother", a Grade-9 "big sister" |
| Someone you never met | the electrician who fixed the flickering lamp; the person who planted the cypress tree 30 years ago |

---

## 🕯️ Wednesday Evening: Thank-You Evening (18:30 – 20:00)

Once a year, on Wednesday evening, the hallway is kept open later. Here is what happens:

- **18:30 – 19:00 · Quiet reading of the wall.** Soft music (a single cello), lights dimmed to the reading lamps only.  
- **19:00 – 19:30 · Short shared reading.** Eight volunteers (two from each grade) read aloud one anonymous card each, chosen by the authors themselves earlier that day. **Authors may stay silent in the crowd** — nobody is named.  
- **19:30 – 20:00 · Warm drinks by the south door** (ginger-honey tea, simple biscuits). Students, teachers, parents, and staff all together. No phones on the drink tables, please.

A small light bulb with a warm yellow filament stays on above the wall until 20:00. At 20:00, the lights fade gently. We go home quietly.

---

## 📨 Special Lines for Some Special Readers

- **Staff box**: A small closed wooden box labelled *"For the hands we do not often see."* Cards pinned here will be delivered (**sealed and unread by students**) to cleaners, guards, and kitchen staff on Friday afternoon.  
- **Teacher drawers**: Each teacher has a small labelled drawer in the staff room. Cards addressed to a specific teacher may also be dropped directly into their drawer if preferred.  
- **Class teacher crates**: Each homeroom class will also have a small crate for thank-yous that should reach someone **inside the class**, rather than on the public wall.  
- **Alumni line**: A small separate corner of the wall, for graduates of Lihua who are now in senior high or in the world of work. We will photograph those cards on Friday evening and send copies to alumni, with their permission.

- 2 **Photo archivists** for the Friday-night wall record.

Sign-up via the school mini-app → Student Union → Thank-You Wall. **Closing: Thu 17 December, 17:30.**

---

## 🌿 After the Wall — What Happens to the Cards

- On **Fri 25 Dec, 17:30**, cards are taken down carefully, in the order they were put up.  
- All signed cards are **delivered to the named receivers** over the following Monday and Tuesday.  
- Anonymous cards are collected in a **thick paper envelope** (*"For everyone and for no one in particular"*) and kept in the school archive for one year, then gently pulped for new paper — the same paper used for next year's Thank-You Wall. **Our thanks travel in a quiet circle.**  
- No card is photographed or posted online without the author's written permission.

---

## ❗ Important Notes

- **No names of specific companies or products**, please. This is not an advertisement board.  
- **No criticism**, even of the polite kind. If you have a suggestion for improvement, please write it on the **separate suggestion board** next to the library, not on this wall.  
- **Please do not touch other people's cards**, even to straighten them. A crooked card, hung by a shy first-year, is part of the beauty.

---

## 💬 A Small Word from the Student Union

> "At the end of a long year — a year of exams, new subjects, quiet losses, small wins — we all carry a list of people who made it a little warmer: the aunty who opened the bike-park gate on rainy mornings; the teacher who noticed you were tired; the classmate who lent you a pencil and said nothing about the time you forgot to return it. The Thank-You Wall is, for five short days, a place where those quiet names become visible, and then go home with warm hands. **A school that learns to say 'thank you' aloud once a year** is, in a small way, **a school that has learned something important**."  
> — *Student Union & Arts Department, 5 December 2027*
