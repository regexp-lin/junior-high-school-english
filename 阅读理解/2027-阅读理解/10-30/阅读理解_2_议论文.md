# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle Schools Require Uniforms on All Five School Days?**

① Last week our school parents' meeting saw a polite but sharp debate. One mother, holding a cup of tea, said: "Children should wear uniforms every day. It is fair and simple." Another father answered: "Every day is too much. Let children have at least two 'free-clothes days' a week." The conversation lasted an hour and ended without a clear decision. I was in the back row, listening. The more I listened, the more I believed: **schools should keep the uniform — but not require it every single school day. Three uniform days and two free-clothes days** is a healthier rule.

② Why keep the uniform at all? The strongest reason is **equality**. Students come from very different families. One classmate's winter coat may cost 180 yuan; another's may cost 1,800. A uniform quietly removes this kind of unequal silence from the classroom. It lets the shy student walk in just as freely as the rich student. For this reason alone, taking away uniforms completely — as some voices suggest — would be, in my view, a step backwards.

③ A second reason for uniforms is **focus**. On a normal weekday, a student has to think about homework, tests, club activities, friendships, a thousand details. **Dress** should not be the thousand-and-first. A uniform saves the first ten minutes of every morning. It is a small gift of **quiet time**, which busy students in Grade 8 and Grade 9 often need more than they know.

④ So why not require it **every day**? First, a teenager's sense of **self** is partly built through clothes. Choosing a scarf, folding up a sleeve, pinning a small handmade badge — these are the little ways in which young people practise saying, "This is me." To take away every single chance to express this is to make school feel a little like a factory. Middle school is not a factory. It is the place where **who you are** begins to appear.

⑤ Second, a two-day window for free clothes is good for the whole school culture. Students see their classmates as **whole people**, not just Grade 8(2) Class members. A student who always wears tidy, dull uniforms may suddenly show up on Friday in a red knitted hat made by his grandmother — and his classmates learn that he has a grandmother. Small, warm surprises like this keep the school human.

⑥ Third, there is a **seasonal reason** special to Chinese classrooms. School uniform coats are often cut to one standard size, and in the deep cold of northern winters, they are not always warm enough. Letting students wear their own warm clothes on Mondays and Fridays (the two coldest mornings, when many children come in from long bus rides) simply makes **health** sense.

⑦ Of course, some will ask: "If you open two free-clothes days, won't it lead to fashion shows, strange hairstyles, small jealousy?" It is a real concern. The answer, however, is **to set clear, simple rules**, not to return to seven-day uniforms. For example, our school can ask: no slogans on shirts, no open shoulders, no heavy makeup, no hairstyles that block other students' sight in class. Under such rules, a sixteen-year-old can dress with both **freedom and dignity**. Hundreds of good schools in Shanghai, Hangzhou and Chengdu have used such a rule for years.

⑧ A uniform is a small piece of school life, not its whole soul. Five uniform days turn a school into a **line**; seven free days can turn it into a **market**. But **three and two — uniform and freedom — balance each other**, like the black and white of a chessboard: fair where fairness matters, free where freedom matters. Our school, I believe, is ready for the change. In the end, the best uniforms are not only the ones on our bodies; they are the **good habits**, the **kind manners** and the **clear minds** we all wear — quietly, invisibly, every day.

---

**注释：** uniform 校服；equality 平等；focus 专注；self 自我；jealousy 嫉妒

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion in this passage?**

A. Students should wear uniforms every single school day.  
B. Schools should completely remove uniforms.  
C. Schools should keep uniforms on three days and allow free clothes on two days.  
D. Only rich students should wear uniforms.

**2. According to Paragraph ②, why should uniforms be kept at all?**

A. Because they make students look good.  
B. Because they quietly remove unequal silence between richer and poorer students.  
C. Because they are cheap.  
D. Because they make teachers happy.

**3. In Paragraph ③, what "small gift" does the uniform give students?**

A. Ten extra minutes of sleep.  
B. A quiet ten minutes every morning, by removing the need to choose clothes.  
C. Free breakfast.  
D. A new pair of shoes.

**4. What is the writer's third reason for allowing free-clothes days (Paragraph ⑥)?**

A. To let students show off.  
B. Because standard school coats are not always warm enough in deep winter, so allowing free clothes on cold mornings makes health sense.  
C. Because some students cannot afford uniforms.  
D. Because some students do not like uniforms.

**5. How does the writer answer the worry about "fashion shows and small jealousy" in Paragraph ⑦?**

A. He admits schools should go back to seven uniform days.  
B. He says students should not care about clothes.  
C. He suggests setting clear, simple rules (no slogans, no open shoulders, no heavy makeup, no blocking hairstyles), which many good schools already use.  
D. He says only Grade 9 students should wear free clothes.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "Three uniform days and two free-clothes days is a healthier rule"  
【解析】立论明确。A、B、D 均偏。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "quietly removes this kind of unequal silence"  
【解析】平等作用直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "saves the first ten minutes of every morning ... a small gift of quiet time"  
【解析】专注小礼物直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "not always warm enough ... makes health sense"  
【解析】冬季健康理由。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "set clear, simple rules ... no slogans ... no open shoulders ... no heavy makeup ... no hairstyles that block other students' sight"  
【解析】规则取代禁令。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。
