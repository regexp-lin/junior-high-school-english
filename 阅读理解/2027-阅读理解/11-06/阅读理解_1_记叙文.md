# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The First Dinner I Cooked for My Sick Mother**

① Mum had caught a bad cold at the bus stop on Thursday evening. By Friday morning her forehead was hot, her voice was thin, and she was moving slowly around the flat in her old grey sweater. Dad was away for two days, at a work meeting in Nanjing. When the school bell ended my last class at 16:30, I hurried home with a small, serious thought in my chest: **tonight I would cook dinner for Mum**. I had never cooked a whole meal before. I was almost fourteen.

② I texted Grandma from the bus, because Grandma's recipes always work. She sent a short reply: "*Easy plan. Tomato-and-egg noodles. Steamed pumpkin. Hot ginger water. Take it slow. If the pan smokes, turn the fire smaller.*" I read the message three times, the way I read an exam question before answering. By the time I reached our door, I could already see the kitchen in my mind: the round steel pot, the white plate, the tomato on the windowsill.

③ Mum was on the sofa, wrapped in a thick grey blanket. Her nose was pink; her eyes were a little tired. "You must be hungry," she said, trying to sit up. I gently pushed her shoulder back down. "No," I said, with more bravery than I actually had, "you rest. **Tonight I am the cook.**" She looked at me for a second, surprised. Then a small smile curved on her lips. "Alright, chef," she whispered. "Please do not burn our kitchen."

④ The pumpkin was easier. I washed it, cut it in half — holding the knife **with two hands** as Dad had shown me — and took out the seeds with a spoon. Then I cut it into **six** neat half-moons, put them in the steamer with a splash of water, turned the small flame on, and set the timer for **20 minutes**. I kept telling myself, calmly, "One step, one check. One step, one check." Grandma's voice was in my head like a little radio.

⑤ The tomato-and-egg noodles were harder. I beat three eggs, thinking of Mum's hand which had always done this in one smooth wrist motion. Mine was more like a small, anxious drum. I cut two tomatoes into small red pieces, the juice running onto my cutting board. I added a little oil to the pan, waited for it to move in gentle waves, and poured the egg in. The egg **puffed** up in less than ten seconds — golden, soft, and — for the first time in my life — **cooked by me**. I stood still for two seconds, quietly thanking the pan.

⑥ Then I added the tomatoes, salt, a tiny bit of sugar, and some water. The red and the gold mixed into a warm, shy sauce. In the other pot, noodles were already softening. At 17:58 I carefully lifted the noodles out, poured the sauce on top, sprinkled a few green onion slices, and carried the bowl — with both hands, as if it were made of glass — to the sofa. Mum sat up slowly. She looked at the bowl. Then at me. Then, softly, her eyes became **wet**.

⑦ "It smells just like Grandma's," she said, not yet taking a bite. I shrugged, trying to hide how my heart was beating like a small drum. She took the first bite — the test bite. She closed her eyes for a second, the way grown-ups do when they want to remember something later. Then she smiled: "**A little too much salt. And a lot of love. Very good, chef.**" I laughed. I laughed the tired laugh of a small cook who had not known she could do this. The ginger water came a few minutes later, brown and warm, with a slice of lemon. Mum sipped it slowly, steam rising into her pink nose.

⑧ That night I went to bed with tomato under my fingernails and a new, quiet pride in my chest. Lying in the dark, I understood something I had not put into words before: **love in a family is not only what people give you; it is also what you, small though you are, are finally allowed to give back.** I closed my eyes. In the other room, Mum was sleeping — her first real sleep in two days. Somewhere inside my chest, a tiny pan was still warm, cooking the quiet feeling that I would, one day, know how to take care of the people who had taken care of me. Fourteen is not yet grown up. But on that cold evening in November, **it was grown enough**.

---

**注释：** pumpkin 南瓜；steamer 蒸锅；timer 计时器；beat (eggs) 打（蛋）；fingernail 指甲

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer decide to cook dinner by herself that Friday evening?**

A. Because her mother asked her to.  
B. Because her mother had caught a bad cold (hot forehead, thin voice) and her father was away for two days in Nanjing, so the writer quietly decided to cook dinner for her mum.  
C. Because the fridge was full.  
D. Because she wanted to pass a school cooking test.

**2. Whose recipes did the writer follow for the meal?**

A. Her own.  
B. The P.E. teacher's.  
C. Her grandmother's — tomato-and-egg noodles, steamed pumpkin, and hot ginger water.  
D. A recipe from a cooking TV show.

**3. How did the writer prepare the pumpkin?**

A. She boiled the whole pumpkin without cutting it.  
B. She washed it, cut it in half with two hands, removed the seeds with a spoon, cut it into six neat half-moons, and steamed them for 20 minutes.  
C. She fried the pumpkin seeds.  
D. She wrapped the pumpkin in paper and put it in the oven.

**4. What did Mum say after the first bite of the noodles?**

A. "It is too cold."  
B. "A little too much salt. And a lot of love. Very good, chef."  
C. "I do not like tomatoes."  
D. "You should buy instant noodles next time."

**5. What does the writer learn by the end of the story?**

A. Cooking is not important at all.  
B. Love in a family is only what parents give their children.  
C. Love in a family is not only what people give you, but also what you, small though you are, are finally allowed to give back.  
D. Children should cook every evening.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "Mum had caught a bad cold ... Dad was away for two days, at a work meeting in Nanjing"  
【解析】双重原因直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Easy plan. Tomato-and-egg noodles. Steamed pumpkin. Hot ginger water."  
【解析】奶奶短信直引。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段  
【解析】五步骤直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "A little too much salt. And a lot of love. Very good, chef."  
【解析】妈妈点评直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段 "not only what people give you ... you ... are finally allowed to give back"  
【解析】爱的双向流动。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。
