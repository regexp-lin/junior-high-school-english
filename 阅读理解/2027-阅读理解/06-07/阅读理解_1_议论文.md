# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Students Change Seats in the Classroom Every Month?**

① In many Chinese middle schools, once the teacher decides each student's seat at the start of the new term, that seat does not change for many months. A boy may spend the whole of Grade 7 looking at the same back of the same head; a girl may share her desk with the same classmate from September to June. Recently, some head teachers have started a new idea: **changing seats every month**. Is this a good policy? I believe it is.

② First, regular seat-changing is good for students' **eyes and posture**. In a fixed seat, a student's body always turns slightly in the same direction to see the blackboard. Students in the far right seats, for example, unconsciously twist their necks to the left for many months. Over time, the neck, back and even one shoulder can feel sore. Changing seats every month means the body turns in different directions; the small, silent pressure on the spine is shared more fairly.

③ Second, a new deskmate every month widens our **social world**. A teenager's closest friend is often simply the person who sits nearby. When seats never change, friendships become closed circles; students who are quieter or newer feel left out. After eight or nine seat changes in a school year, each student has sat next to many classmates, shared pens with them, and quietly learned about their lives. The class becomes less like a chessboard and more like a warm neighbourhood.

④ Third, seat-changing teaches a small but important life skill: **adapting to change**. In real life, we will often have to work with people we did not choose — in a new office, a new sports team, a new city. A classroom that changes seats monthly is a soft, low-risk training for this kind of flexibility. Students who quietly adjust to a new deskmate every few weeks grow more patient, more curious and more open.

⑤ Some teachers worry that moving seats will **waste lesson time** or disturb students who have "just got comfortable". These worries are fair, but the problem is small. Seat-changing can be done after school on the last Friday of each month; it costs less than fifteen minutes. Teachers can also keep one "need-based" rule — short-sighted students can stay in the front two rows — so no one is punished by the change.

⑥ A classroom is not only a place where students memorise facts. It is a small, half-shaped society. A rule as simple as monthly seat-changing tells every student: *you belong to the whole class, not only to one corner*. That quiet message, repeated nine times a year, may grow into one of the strongest lessons of middle school life.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Middle schools should never change seats.  
B. Middle schools should change students' seats every month.  
C. Students should choose any seat they want every day.  
D. Only teachers should sit by the window.

**2. According to paragraph ②, what is a problem with never changing seats?**

A. Students become too happy.  
B. The body always turns in the same direction, which causes silent pressure on the spine, neck or shoulders.  
C. Students see too many teachers.  
D. Students cannot see the blackboard.

**3. How does changing seats help the class become "a warm neighbourhood"?**

A. Students buy food for each other.  
B. After many seat changes, each student has sat next to many classmates, shared pens, and learned about their lives.  
C. Students start a business together.  
D. Students make the desks larger.

**4. How does the writer say seat-changing teaches a life skill?**

A. It teaches students to lie.  
B. It teaches flexibility — students learn to adjust to a new deskmate, just as adults often have to work with people they did not choose.  
C. It teaches running fast.  
D. It teaches cooking.

**5. How does the writer answer teachers who worry about wasted time?**

A. Seats should never be changed because of the worry.  
B. Seat-changing can be done after school on the last Friday of the month, taking less than fifteen minutes, with a "need-based" rule for short-sighted students.  
C. Teachers should ignore short-sighted students.  
D. Lessons should be removed entirely.

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末  
【解析】作者主张每月换座。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B

【定位】第②段

【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段  
【解析】多次换座认识更多人。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "a soft, low-risk training for this kind of flexibility."
