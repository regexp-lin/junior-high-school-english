# 阅读理解（记叙文） **A Labour Day Morning at Uncle Ming's Bakery**

① This year, for **Labour Day** on 1 May, our teacher gave us a special small task: spend **one real working morning** with a family member who works with his or her hands. My father works in an office, my mother works in a bank, so I called my **Uncle Ming**, who runs a tiny bakery on Peach Road. "Come at 5 a.m.," he laughed over the phone, "and bring a warm jacket — my shop wakes up very early." I stopped laughing when I heard "5 a.m.", but I said yes.

② At 4:30 a.m. my mother walked me down Peach Road. The streets were dark, and the only warm light came from Uncle Ming's small window. Inside the shop, the air was already full of the sweet smell of **flour, butter, and brown sugar**. Uncle Ming gave me a small white apron, far too big, and tied it twice around my waist. "Rule one," he said, "wash your hands first. Rule two, listen before you touch."

③ For the first hour, I only watched. Uncle Ming took a huge ball of **soft dough** out of a big silver bowl. He pushed it, folded it, turned it, and pushed again. His arms moved smoothly, like an old dance. My own hand reached out once, but he gently pushed it back. "Not yet," he smiled. "Dough is like a child; it only listens to hands that know how to wait." I began to understand that there were many **small rules** I could not see just by buying bread.

④ By 6 a.m., I was allowed to weigh out the small pieces, each about 60 grams, and place them on the floured table. It looked easy. But my pieces were not round, and I was too slow. Uncle Ming did not shout. He stood beside me, gently guided my fingers, and showed me a neat, smooth turn of the wrist. After twenty pieces, I began to feel a small rhythm. The first tray went into the big hot oven at 6:45 a.m.

⑤ At 7:10, the first **milk loaves** came out, glowing golden brown. The smell was so strong that my stomach turned loud. Customers started to come in — the first was an old grandmother who bought four loaves every morning to sell at the small train station. Uncle Ming wrote her name in his notebook and handed the bread with both hands, a little bow. I noticed that each customer was greeted as if they were old friends, even if the bakery owed them nothing.

⑥ By 9 a.m. my arms were a little sore, my white apron was covered in flour, and I had dropped one piece of dough on the floor. But Uncle Ming looked at me with kind eyes. "Not bad for a first Labour Day," he said, and gave me a hot **red-bean bun**, only slightly crooked. "Yours," he laughed. "Everyone's first piece is always crooked. Keep it. Remember it." I sat on a small wooden stool near the window, biting into the warm bun while the sun climbed above the rooftops.

⑦ Walking home at 10, I thought about our classroom homework — "what does labour mean to you?" I used to think the answer was "hard work". Now I think labour is **small hands making small rules, again and again, so that strangers can wake up warm**. On Labour Day this year I did not go to a park or a shopping mall. I went to a tiny bakery, and came home with flour on my shoes and a different kind of respect for the word **"work"**.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer spend her Labour Day morning at Uncle Ming's bakery?**

A. Because her parents forced her.  
B. Because of a school task about spending a real working morning with someone who works with his or her hands.  
C. Because she wanted to earn 500 yuan.  
D. Because her uncle was sick.

**2. What time did the writer arrive at the bakery?**

A. About 4:30 a.m.  
B. About 7 a.m.  
C. About 10 a.m.  
D. About 3 p.m.

**3. Why did Uncle Ming say "Dough is like a child; it only listens to hands that know how to wait"?**

A. Because he hated children.  
B. Because he wanted the writer to understand that good baking needs patience and rules.  
C. Because the dough was dangerous.  
D. Because he wanted to sell his bakery.

**4. How did Uncle Ming treat the first old grandmother customer?**

A. He ignored her.  
B. He shouted at her.  
C. He wrote her name in his notebook and handed bread to her with both hands and a little bow.  
D. He gave her nothing.

**5. What did "labour" mean to the writer by the end of the story?**

A. A way to get rich quickly.  
B. Small hands making small rules again and again so that strangers can wake up warm.  
C. Something that only adults should do.  
D. A holiday only for sleeping.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段  
【解析】老师布置劳动节任务。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "4:30 a.m."  
【解析】凌晨四点半到店。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第③段末  
【解析】强调耐心与规则。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段  
【解析】记名+双手递面包+鞠躬。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦段  
【解析】劳动=让陌生人温暖。故选 B。
