# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Baby Bird We Raised for Ten Days**

① On a windy Sunday morning in May, my sister Lin-Lin and I found a tiny baby bird on the pavement outside our flat. It was almost bald, with only thin grey feathers on its wings, and it was opening and closing its yellow mouth. We looked up — there was a nest high in the old plane tree above, but the wind had been strong all night, and the mother bird was nowhere to be seen. "We can't leave it here," Lin-Lin said, her voice small and worried.

② We took the bird home in a paper box with some soft cloth at the bottom. Mum looked at it, sighed, and said we could keep it until it was strong enough to fly — not a minute longer. "A wild bird is not a pet," she said clearly. "We help it. Then we let it go." Lin-Lin nodded very quickly, trying to hide a big smile. I could see she was already thinking of names.

③ We did not name the bird. Dad said a name makes it harder to say goodbye. Instead, we called it only "Little One". For the first three days, we fed it tiny balls of soft egg every two hours, day and night. Lin-Lin set an alarm even during school, and I woke up at 11 p.m., 1 a.m., 3 a.m., to give it warm water and another little egg ball. My eyes felt heavy at school, but my heart felt light.

④ On the fourth day, Little One opened its eyes more widely, and its feathers became softer and darker. Dad built a simple bigger box with a small perch — a thin branch across the top. Little One tried to stand on the branch. It fell twice, hopped up, fell again. By evening, it could balance for a whole minute. We clapped so loudly that Mum had to remind us the bird needed quiet.

⑤ By day seven, it could fly small circles inside our living room. The first flight was not graceful: it hit the curtain, then the bookshelf, then me on the shoulder, before finally landing on the back of the sofa. But after five or six tries, its wings began to move in clean, certain strokes. The grown-up bird was clearly inside, waiting.

⑥ On day ten, Mum looked at us and said, "It is time." The word hit me harder than I had expected. Lin-Lin went quiet. She held the box close and said, "Just one more day?" Dad shook his head kindly. "If we wait, it will trust us too much. Then the world outside will be dangerous." We walked to the park together.

⑦ At the park, we opened the box under the old plane tree — the same tree where we had found Little One. For a long minute, it did not move. Then, slowly, it stepped onto Lin-Lin's small hand. I saw her fingers shake. "Go," she whispered in a soft voice. "Be careful." Little One turned its head, looked at us, and then, without warning, jumped — a quick, clean flight to a low branch. From there, to a higher branch. Then, it was a grey shape against the bright afternoon sky, and then, nothing.

⑧ We walked home in silence. Halfway, Lin-Lin started to cry a little, and I did not know what to say. Mum put her hand on her head. "Crying is right," she said. "But this is a good cry. You two gave a small life ten days of safety — and then gave it back its real home. That is not losing. That is loving the honest way."

⑨ Now, when we pass the old plane tree, we always look up for a moment. We never know if any of the birds there are Little One. Maybe one, maybe none. But I have learned something quiet and important, thanks to ten warm days and one difficult goodbye: **some of the kindest things we do end with an empty box, not a kept one**. And somewhere, up in the leaves, wings keep moving because two pairs of tired young hands once chose to help and then — harder, braver — chose to let go.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did Mum refuse to give the baby bird a name?**

A. Because it was too small to need a name.  
B. Because Dad said a name would make it harder to say goodbye.  
C. Because Mum did not like pets at all.  
D. Because no one could agree on a good name.

**2. What did the writer and his sister do at night during the first three days?**

A. They both slept without any interruption.  
B. They set alarms and took turns feeding the bird warm water and tiny balls of egg every two hours.  
C. They called a vet every hour.  
D. They put the bird outside every night.

**3. On day seven, how well could the bird fly inside the living room?**

A. It could not fly at all.  
B. Its first flight was clumsy (hitting the curtain, bookshelf and the writer's shoulder), but after several tries its wings moved in clean, certain strokes.  
C. It flew away through an open window.  
D. It only slept all day.

**4. Why did Dad say it was time to let the bird go on day ten?**

A. Because the bird ate too much food.  
B. Because if they waited, the bird would trust humans too much and then find the outside world dangerous.  
C. Because the neighbours complained.  
D. Because Lin-Lin said she was tired.

**5. What does Mum mean by "this is a good cry" in paragraph ⑧?**

A. It is silly to cry about a bird.  
B. Crying is wrong, and Lin-Lin should stop.  
C. It is right to feel sad, because they gave the bird ten days of safety and then returned it to its real home — this is loving honestly.  
D. They should go back and bring the bird home.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Dad said a name makes it harder to say goodbye."  
【解析】不起名以便告别。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "alarm... every two hours, day and night."  
【解析】彻夜轮流喂食。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 全段  
【解析】初试笨拙后渐入佳境。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "it will trust us too much. Then the world outside will be dangerous."  
【解析】过度依赖人类会危险。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段 妈妈的话  
【解析】难过正常，但是爱的完整——帮助再放飞。A/B/D 均偏。故选 C。
