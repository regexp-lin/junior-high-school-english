# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Winter Book-Swap Day 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Library + Reading Club**  
> **Winter Book-Swap Day 2027**  
> **"One old book, one new friend."**

Last summer the Reading Club did a quiet count: our school library received more than **1,400 donated books** from families — but many of them were simply duplicates of what was already on the shelves. At the same time, students quietly told each other: "I really want to read *A Little Prince* in English, but we only have one copy, and it is always out." The committee thought about it, and decided to try something new. On **Saturday 22 November 2027, 09:00 – 12:00**, the library will host our first **Winter Book-Swap Day**. Bring a book. Take a book. Meet the next reader of your old favourite.

---

## 🎯 How It Works

1. **Bring** up to **three** books you no longer need.  
2. **Check them in** at the counter by the main gate. You will receive **one ticket per book**, up to three tickets.  
3. **Use your tickets** to take home up to three different books from the Swap Shelves in the reading hall.  
4. If you do not see a book you want today, you can **keep your ticket** until **Sunday 30 November**, after which all unused tickets are donated (see section "Gansu / Yunnan" below).

---

## 📚 What Kinds of Books?

**Welcome:**
- Graded English readers (level 2–6).  
- Chinese and English fiction for teens (no more than 450 pages per book).  
- Popular science books, especially on astronomy, biology, geography, technology.  
- Good picture-based books for younger siblings (age 5–10).  
- Notebooks are welcome, **but only if they are still blank**.

**Not Welcome:**
- Books with too many notes, highlighter marks, or missing pages.  
- Test-paper books, exam collections, or books whose answer key has been removed.  
- Books in poor condition (torn spine, damp pages, or an unpleasant smell).  
- Books that discuss topics **not suitable for middle-school students**.

Please respect the counter teachers' decision if a book cannot be accepted. They will kindly hand it back with a short note.

---

---

## ✍️ The Reader's Note (Very Important)

Please **stick a small note** inside the front cover of each book you bring, saying:
- your **first name** (or a nickname),  
- **one sentence** about why the book mattered to you,  
- and **one kind wish** for its next reader.

Example:  
> *From Jiahao, 8(3). This book helped me understand my grandfather better. I hope it makes your November Saturday warm.*

The Reading Club believes that a book without a note is only paper. **A note turns it into a quiet letter**.

---

## 🎒 Gansu / Yunnan Bridge

Books that no Lihua student takes by 30 November will be carefully wrapped and sent to our partner schools in **Minxian, Gansu** and **Yongsheng, Yunnan** — together with the postcards already being prepared this month. In this way, **nothing kind will be wasted**. Please do not worry that your book will sit on a shelf forever.

---

## 🛑 Three Simple House Rules

- **No shouting** or running in the reading hall. The library has always been the school's quietest room.  
- **No eating** except at the small water-corner near the cactus plant.  
- **No buying or selling**. This is a swap day. If a book is very popular and two students want it, the student with fewer books taken today has first choice, then we ask them to share.

> "A library is not a place where books live. It is a place where **books move**, quietly, from the hands of one kind reader to the eyes of the next."  
> — *Reading Club, 3 November 2027*

**根据通知内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. On Book-Swap Day, how many books can a student bring and how many can she take home?**

A. Unlimited / unlimited.  
B. Up to three books brought (one ticket per book), and up to three books taken home using the tickets.  
C. Only one book brought, ten books taken.  
D. Ten books brought, one book taken.

**2. Which of the following books is NOT welcome at the Swap Day?**

A. An English graded reader, level 4.  
B. A popular science book about astronomy.  
C. A test-paper book with the answer key removed.  
D. A blank notebook.

**3. What should a student write in "the Reader's Note" inside the front cover of each book?**

A. Her home address and phone number.  
B. Her first name or nickname, one sentence about why the book mattered, and a kind wish for the next reader.  
C. A list of test scores.  
D. A long letter of 500 words.

**4. What happens to books that nobody takes by Sunday 30 November?**

A. They are thrown away.  
B. They are carefully wrapped and sent to the partner village schools in Minxian, Gansu and Yongsheng, Yunnan.  
C. They are sold to a second-hand shop.  
D. They are burnt at the end of the year.

**5. What does the Reading Club mean by "A library is not a place where books live. It is a place where books move..."?**

A. Books should travel on planes and trains every year.  
B. A library must constantly sell new books.  
C. Books reach their real value by passing from one kind reader to the next; the library is a **quiet stream** of care, not a still pool.  
D. Students should not keep their favourite books.
