# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Teachers and Parents Keep a "Quiet Window" After 9 p.m.?**

① A few weeks ago, my mother showed me a screenshot. It was our class group chat at **22:18 on a Tuesday**: three teachers, two parents, one angry voice note, and a list of tomorrow's extra homework. I counted the messages from 21:00 until midnight: **79**. We were all, in a way, working an extra shift — quietly, silently, under the covers. That night I asked my mother a serious question: *"Why does 9 p.m. not really mean 9 p.m. in our school?"* I do not know the full answer. But I do believe one idea: **after 9 p.m., a school group chat should enter a "quiet window" for about 12 hours**, without new assignments or urgent announcements.

② The strongest argument is **sleep**. Doctors across China have said again and again that middle-school students need **9 to 9.5 hours** of sleep each night. In reality, many of my classmates sleep less than seven. A late-night message "Please correct question 18 before tomorrow morning" is, scientifically, a small **alarm clock for the whole family** — parents check, worry, wake the student, and the quiet brain misses one more half-hour of repair time. Over a month, that is ten lost hours. Over a term, a whole night.

③ Second, a "quiet window" respects **teachers** too. A 9 p.m. message from the form teacher to our class often starts another chain: more parents asking "Has my child finished?"; more pictures of half-done homework; more explanations. In the end, the **teacher herself** rarely goes to sleep before eleven. Teachers are also parents, also daughters, also patients of winter coughs. A school that protects students' rest also protects its teachers'. A tired teacher in Grade 7 becomes a less patient teacher by Grade 9.

④ A third reason is the **quality of thinking**. A good message, a fair assignment, a clear note — each needs a **calm mind**. At 22:30, after ten hours of class and meetings, nobody — teacher, parent, student — is at their best. Important messages sent then are often **re-edited, deleted, and re-sent**, which makes the chat even messier. Writing between 9 a.m. and 9 p.m., and waiting until morning for less urgent things, simply produces **better communication**. Old Chinese family wisdom already knew this: *"After dinner, lay down the day's heavy bag."*

⑤ Of course, worried voices exist. Some teachers argue that Grade 9 students are very close to the exam, and one more urgent correction at night may save a mark. Some parents say, "But what if there is a real emergency?" These worries are real, and they deserve real answers, not quick dismissals. A sudden thunderstorm that changes tomorrow's sports event; a child whose temperature has just risen to 39 °C; a rule change from the school district office — these are **genuine emergencies** and should not be held back.

⑥ The answer is not to forbid all messages, but to **change the habit**. Three simple rules can solve 90% of the noise. First, **non-urgent notices** (homework, reminders, study tips) should be posted **before 21:00**, or — if missed — the next morning by 07:30. Second, **genuine emergencies** should use a clear word at the start, such as *"URGENT"* or *"急"*, so parents know when to check and when to rest. Third, teachers can turn on a **scheduled-send** function: write at 23:00 if the idea comes, but **send at 07:30** the next day. Technology already allows this in 2027; only the habit needs updating.

⑦ Some may also say: "But this is how our group chat has always been." Culture is powerful — and tiring. Yet many good schools in Hangzhou, Suzhou and Zhengzhou have quietly switched to a "9 p.m. quiet window" since 2024, and their parent satisfaction scores have **risen**, not fallen. Their teachers report fewer "Sunday-night" messages of panic. Their students sleep, on average, 26 minutes longer. These numbers are small; but **26 minutes of sleep, repeated 200 school nights a year, equals more than 86 hours** — the same as a whole week of extra rest.

⑧ A classroom is not a factory; a family is not a 24-hour help centre. After nine at night, children should close the day and be allowed to be **children** again. Teachers should close their computers and be allowed to be **human** again. Parents should put down their phones and, across the pillow, talk about something small and funny — a joke from the road, a funny face from the dog, a recipe for the weekend. **A "quiet window" is not silence. It is the breathing space that lets our kindness last until morning.** That is the kind of school I would like Lihua to be.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main proposal?**

A. Teachers should send homework messages only at 2 a.m.  
B. After 9 p.m., the school group chat should enter a "quiet window" for about 12 hours, without new assignments or urgent announcements.  
C. Parents should remove the school group chat.  
D. Students should reply to every message instantly.

**2. Why does Paragraph ② argue that late-night messages harm SLEEP?**

A. Because parents sleep in separate rooms.  
B. Because the message acts like a small alarm clock for the whole family — parents check, worry, wake the student — and the student loses important brain-repair time.  
C. Because students prefer talking to chatting.  
D. Because schools are too loud.

**3. Which of the following does the writer mention as a GENUINE emergency in Paragraph ⑤?**

A. A piece of homework the teacher forgot.  
B. A sudden thunderstorm that changes tomorrow's sports event; a child with a fever of 39 °C; a rule change from the school district office.  
C. A classmate posting a funny cartoon.  
D. A new word in the vocabulary list.

**4. Which is NOT one of the writer's three "simple rules" in Paragraph ⑥?**

A. Non-urgent notices should be posted before 21:00 or by the next morning 07:30.  
B. Emergencies should start with a clear "URGENT" word.  
C. Teachers can use a scheduled-send function: write at 23:00 but send at 07:30.  
D. Parents should delete every message at midnight.

**5. What is the meaning of the writer's final sentence in Paragraph ⑧: *"A 'quiet window' is not silence. It is the breathing space that lets our kindness last until morning"*?**

A. Everyone should stay silent after 9 p.m.  
B. Kindness gets tired too; a quiet window gives teachers, parents and students a pause so their kindness survives the next day.  
C. Schools should never communicate.  
D. Parents should shout at children at 9 p.m.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "after 9 p.m., a school group chat should enter a 'quiet window' for about 12 hours"  
【解析】论点清晰。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "small alarm clock for the whole family ... repair time"  
【解析】睡眠破坏机制。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段"genuine emergencies"列举  
【解析】雷雨 / 高烧 / 教育局变更。A、C、D 均为日常。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（否定）  
【定位】第⑥段三条规则  
【解析】A、B、C 明列。D"午夜删除所有消息"文中未提。故选 D。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段结尾比喻  
【解析】善意也需要喘息。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。
