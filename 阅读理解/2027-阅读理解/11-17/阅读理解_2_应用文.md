# 阅读理解（应用文） **Winter Charity Bazaar 2027 — "A Warm Home for Every Small Thing"**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Red Cross Club + Student Union**  
> **Winter Charity Bazaar 2027 — "A Warm Home for Every Small Thing"**  
> **Saturday 13 December 2027, 09:30 – 15:00, School Playground**

Every winter, boxes under our beds and corners of our wardrobes quietly fill with items that we no longer use but are still in very good shape: books, clothes, small toys, stationery, and a thousand small stories. Once a year we open those boxes, carry the items to the school playground, and turn them, gently, into a little bit of help for children we have never met. This year, all money raised will go to the **Mountain-Village School Book-Bag Project** in two partner schools in Gansu Province.

---

## 🛍 Which Items Are Welcome

Please bring items that are clean, complete, and **still loved enough to give away**, including:

- Children's and middle-school **books** (reading, story, exam guide, English picture books).  
- Clean **winter clothes** (coats, sweaters, scarves — gently worn, no stains, no missing buttons).  
- **Stationery** (pencils, pens, erasers, pencil cases, notebooks).  
- **Small toys** (board games, puzzles, plush animals — 25 cm or smaller).  
- **Home corner items** (small lamps, wall posters, framed photos, small bookshelves).  
- **Home-baked goods** (biscuits, small cakes — wrapped, with ingredients list).  
- **Handmade works** by students (cards, paintings, knitted gloves).

---

## 🚫 Which Items We Cannot Accept

- **Food not from your family's kitchen** (shop-bought snacks, opened drinks).  
- **Medicine** of any kind — the bazaar is not allowed to sell it.  
- **Damaged or stained clothing** that a child would not wish to wear.  
- **Real money, watches, gold items** — safer to donate directly through parents.  
- **Live plants and animals**.  
- **Mobile phones and laptops** older than 5 years — we cannot guarantee the data is safely wiped.

---

## 📅 Three Simple Stages

**Stage 1: Item Collection — Mon 1 Dec to Fri 5 Dec, every lunch break.**  
Drop off items at the **Red Cross desk** outside Room 318. Please include a small **name-card slip** inside each bag telling us the item, the class, and a one-line story ("*This book lived on my shelf for two years; the tenth page has my drawing of a dragon*"). Short stories will be posted with each item on bazaar day.

**Stage 2: Sorting and Labelling — Sat 6 Dec, 09:00 – 16:00, Gym 2.**  
36 student volunteers will help sort items into sections (books / clothes / stationery / toys / handmade) and write small price tags. Prices will be kept **low and friendly** — a book for 2–5 yuan, a sweater for 8–15 yuan, a pencil for 1 yuan.

**Stage 3: Bazaar Day — Sat 13 Dec, 09:30 – 15:00, Playground.**  
Every class in every grade may set up a **2 m × 2 m stall** (bring a small cloth table-cover in your class colour). Families and neighbours are warmly invited. Warm ginger water and plain biscuits will be free at the back table.

---

## 💰 Payment & Records

- Payment by the school mini-pay **"Charity Wallet"** or small cash (1, 5, 10 yuan).  
- All sales are recorded in a single shared notebook, counted twice by two volunteers at 15:00.  
- The total amount, signed by two teachers and three student volunteers, will be displayed on the school noticeboard on Monday 15 December.  
- **No item may be given away for free** on bazaar day; everything must pass through the record book. (This keeps the donation transparent and respectful.)

---

## 🤝 Volunteers Needed (40 students)

- **12 collection desk helpers** (30-minute slots, Mon 1 Dec to Fri 5 Dec lunch breaks).  
- **10 sorting helpers** (09:00–16:00 on Sat 6 Dec).  
- **6 mini-pay helpers** on Bazaar Day (scan QR codes, give paper receipts).  
- **6 stall guides** (walk around the playground, help younger visitors find what they need).  
- **4 writers** (write short Chinese-English news for the school newsletter).  
- **2 photographers** (photos will be shared only with permission).

Sign-up form: **outside Room 318 or the school mini-app → Services → Bazaar, by Thu 27 November, 17:30.**

---

## 💛 Three Quiet Rules

1. **Bring only items you still love.** A child at our partner school deserves something loved, not something abandoned.  
2. **Be gentle with the prices.** A bazaar is not a market; the goal is the story behind each item, not the money on top of it.  
3. **No loud music, no hard bargaining, no shouting.** We are, first of all, a school.

> "A bazaar is a short afternoon during which our playground turns into a long soft table, and every item finds, a little faster than usual, a small new home. Between 09:30 and 15:00 on 13 December, we are not the students of Grade 7, 8, or 9. We are simply **neighbours looking after neighbours** — two thousand of us, including the ones in Gansu we have not yet met."  
> — *Red Cross Club & Student Union, 17 November 2027*
