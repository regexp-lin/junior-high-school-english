# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Brown Wallet Left Behind on Bus No. 23**

① On the first Wednesday afternoon after the new term started, I was taking the **No. 23 bus** back home from school. It was almost empty — just me, an old lady with a bag of apples, and an uncle in a dark blue jacket who got off two stops later. At the stop in front of the city library, I was about to stand up, when my eye caught a small **brown leather wallet** on the plastic seat opposite me. It lay half-hidden against the window, looking tired, as if it had been forgotten there for a very long time.

② I hesitated for one second. The bus driver was busy watching the road. Nobody else had seen it. I could have simply got off at my stop and told no one. But I thought of something my mother used to say when I was small: "**If you pick up something that is not yours and keep walking, your hand will remember it. And your heart will too.**" I picked up the wallet carefully. It felt warm and a little heavy.

③ At my stop I did not get off. I walked to the front of the bus, showed the wallet to the driver, and said in a slightly shaking voice, "**I think the uncle in the blue jacket left this.**" The driver, a tall man with grey hair at the sides, looked at the wallet, then at me, and nodded slowly. "You are a smart girl. That passenger got off at the hospital stop. Let us check together." He stopped the bus at the next station, opened the wallet with me — only far enough to see the first card — and we found a **work ID**: "Chen Shuming, No. 2 People's Hospital, Emergency Doctor."

④ The driver smiled. "Let us try his hospital. If you have time, I will drive us there after my run; it is close to my end stop. Are your parents worried?" I called my mother, told her everything, and she said quietly, "Good. Go with Uncle Liu, and call me again when you meet Dr Chen." On the bus, the old lady with apples, who was still sitting there, heard the story. She took two apples out of her bag, pressed them into my hand, and said, "One for you, one for him."

⑤ Forty minutes later, we walked into the **Emergency Department** of the No. 2 People's Hospital. I felt very small, surrounded by **white coats**, beeping machines and quiet, serious voices. Uncle Liu, the bus driver, asked at the front desk. A nurse picked up a phone, and, in less than a minute, a man in a dark blue jacket came **running** down the corridor. It was the same uncle from my bus. His face changed when he saw the brown wallet in my hand.

⑥ "Child!" he said, stopping in front of me. "This — this is not only money. My **hospital card** is here, my **mother's medicine list**, and — my **son's first school photo**." He held the wallet with both hands for a moment. He did not open it yet. He looked at me. "What is your name?" he asked. I said my name in a small voice. "Grade?" "Grade 8, Lihua Middle School." He slowly sat down on a nearby bench and put his head into his hands. "I lost it on the bus this morning," he said at last. "My mother has a heart problem. I was running late. If this wallet had been gone, tonight's medicine — my mother's medicine list — would have taken me days to rewrite." His eyes became red.

⑦ He tried to give me **two hundred yuan** as a thank-you. I shook my head. "No, please," I said. "My mother will be angry if I take money for returning what is yours. Please give it to your mother's medicine instead." He looked at Uncle Liu, who smiled, and then at me. He slowly put the money back. From his pocket he took out a small paper card — the kind doctors use for simple notes — and wrote three short lines: "To Miss Li Xiaoxuan, Grade 8, Lihua Middle School. Thank you. If anyone in your family ever needs help at this hospital, please find me. — Dr Chen." He pressed it gently into my hand.

⑧ That evening, I told Mum everything. She listened quietly, then looked at me for a long time. "Your hand and your heart," she said finally, "now remember something much better than they could have remembered otherwise." I did not fully understand until bedtime. Sitting on my desk was the small apple from the old lady, and beside it was the doctor's note, written on a piece of paper no bigger than my palm. Both of them had travelled, in a way, from that empty afternoon bus. Both of them stayed.

⑨ I still take the No. 23 bus. Sometimes, without really thinking about it, I look at the seats before I get off, just in case someone has left behind something that does not belong to me. My mother's old sentence has become mine now: **if you pick up something that is not yours and walk away, your hand will remember it — so will your heart**. I would rather my hand remember honesty than anything else.

---

**注释：** wallet 钱包；emergency 急诊；corridor 走廊；hesitate 犹豫

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where did the writer first find the brown wallet?**

A. In a seat in the library.  
B. On a plastic seat opposite hers on the No. 23 bus, after most passengers had got off.  
C. In her own school bag.  
D. In the emergency room of the hospital.

**2. What did the writer's mother often say to her about picking up things that were not hers?**

A. "Always keep what you find, because the owner will forget."  
B. "Sell whatever you find and share the money."  
C. "If you pick up something that is not yours and keep walking, your hand — and your heart — will remember it."  
D. "Never touch anything you see on a bus seat."

**3. What did the writer and the bus driver find inside the wallet that helped them find the owner?**

A. A train ticket.  
B. A school report card.  
C. A work ID showing the owner was an emergency doctor at No. 2 People's Hospital.  
D. A bank card only.

**4. Why did Dr Chen feel the wallet was **more than just money**?**

A. Because it was made of expensive gold.  
B. Because it contained his hospital card, his mother's medicine list, and his son's first school photo.  
C. Because it had a very large sum of foreign money inside.  
D. Because it was a gift from the bus driver.

**5. What did the writer refuse to take from Dr Chen, and what did he give her instead?**

A. Refused 200 yuan; received a small handwritten thank-you note promising future help at the hospital.  
B. Refused an apple; received two new apples.  
C. Refused a book; received a bus card.  
D. Refused nothing; took the 200 yuan home.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "my eye caught a small brown leather wallet on the plastic seat opposite me"  
【解析】钱包在对面塑料座位上。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "If you pick up something that is not yours and keep walking, your hand will remember it. And your heart will too."  
【解析】母亲原话。A、B、D 均与原文不符。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段末 "we found a work ID: 'Chen Shuming, No. 2 People's Hospital, Emergency Doctor.'"  
【解析】工作证锁定失主身份。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "my hospital card is here, my mother's medicine list, and my son's first school photo"  
【解析】钱包装着重要证件与珍贵记忆。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "He tried to give me two hundred yuan ... I shook my head ... he took out a small paper card ... 'If anyone in your family ever needs help at this hospital, please find me.'"  
【解析】拒绝了 200 元，收到了医生的感谢便笺。B、C、D 均错。故选 A。
