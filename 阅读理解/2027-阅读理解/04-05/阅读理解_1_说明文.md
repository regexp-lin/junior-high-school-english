# 阅读理解（说明文） **How Migrating Birds Find Their Way**

① Every spring, above the rice fields of South China, you may see **V-shaped lines** of wild geese flying north. Every autumn, the same birds fly all the way south to warmer lakes. They travel thousands of kilometres without maps, without phones, and without any road signs. How do they do it? Scientists have been studying this mystery for more than a century.

② The first helper is the **sun**. During the day, many birds use the sun's position as a natural compass. Because the sun moves across the sky, a bird's brain must also hold a kind of **"body clock"**. If the sun is in the east at 7 a.m., but the bird wants to fly south, it knows to keep the sun on its left. As the day goes on, the bird slightly changes its direction with the sun, just like a walking hand of a clock.

③ At night, however, the sun is gone. That is when **the stars** take over. Young birds study the night sky and slowly learn the pattern around the **North Star**. Because the North Star stays almost still while other stars turn around it, it works like a bright road sign in the dark. Scientists once put young birds in a planetarium and changed the "sky". The birds tried to fly in the direction the fake stars suggested — strong proof that they really use star maps.

④ Even more amazing, birds can feel the **Earth's magnetic field**. Inside their eyes and beaks are tiny cells that seem to react to magnetism. Because of this, birds can "see" an invisible north-south arrow, even on a cloudy night with no sun and no stars. This is why birds can keep flying through thick clouds and long rain without getting fully lost.

⑤ There are also smaller helpers. Some birds use **the shape of mountains, rivers, and coastlines**. A young bird often flies with older birds on its first long trip. It learns the shapes of the land by heart, so next year it can fly the same path alone. Some birds also smell the sea or the forest and use these smells as a **nose map** — each place has its own quiet perfume.

⑥ Sadly, human lights now make the journey harder. **Tall bright buildings** confuse young birds at night, and noisy cities hide the smell of the wild land. In some countries, people now turn off big lights during the main flying seasons. The next time you see a V-shaped line in the sky, you may think of the many clocks, stars, magnets, and memories working quietly inside those small, feathered heads.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段问题 "How do they do it?"  
【解析】全文讨论候鸟如何导航。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "uses the sun's position... 'body clock'"  
【解析】结合太阳与内生体钟。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "stays almost still while other stars turn"  
【解析】北极星不动，像路标。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "feel the Earth's magnetic field"  
【解析】借助地磁场。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "tall bright buildings confuse young birds"  
【解析】人为光污染使鸟迷路。故选 B。
