# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Stuck in the Elevator with a Stranger**

① Our flat is on the **15th floor**. I've taken the elevator a thousand times. It is grey, a little slow, smells faintly of old paper. Nothing interesting ever happens in that small box — until last Wednesday evening, when it suddenly **stopped**.

② I had just come back from cram school, tired and hungry. Between the 8th and the 9th floor, the elevator shook once, the lights flickered, and then everything went still. The red "down" arrow froze. A soft beep came from somewhere inside the ceiling. A voice in my head said, "Not a joke — **really stuck.**" My phone had only 3 % battery; I quickly sent a short message to Mum: "Elevator stopped 8–9 floor. I'm OK."

③ The man standing beside me was a stranger. Maybe in his fifties, carrying two big green bags of spring onions. I did not know our neighbours well. Would he panic? Would he scold me because I was in his way? My heart was hitting my ribs like a small drum.

④ He quietly pushed the **red emergency button** three times. A calm voice answered from the ceiling: "We see you, please wait. A repair team will come within 20 minutes." Then the man turned to me and said, "Don't worry, little friend. **Twenty minutes is not a long time if we make it not long.**" He sat down against the wall, took out a small bottle of water from his bag, and offered me a clean paper cup. "Water is the first thing," he said. "Keep drinking little by little; breathe slowly; it helps more than fear."

⑤ We began to chat. His surname was **Mr. Bai**. He told me he was a retired truck driver who used to drive between Harbin and Guangzhou, sometimes four days on one road. "Once, in the mountains of Guizhou, I was stuck in **a snowstorm for two nights**," he said, smiling. "Compared to that, this elevator is almost a hotel!" I laughed for the first time that evening. The tight rope inside my chest began to loosen.

⑥ I was curious about the spring onions. Mr. Bai said they were for his granddaughter, who would come this weekend and loved onion pancakes. He showed me a photo on his old-fashioned phone — a little girl with two small ponytails holding a cake. "She lives in Shanghai," he said quietly. "I see her only at Spring Festival and once in spring." His eyes were soft but warm.

⑦ In about **17 minutes**, we heard steel tools clang on the outside. The elevator door slowly slid open between two floors. A repairman smiled and said, "Step up carefully, kids!" — though Mr. Bai was clearly no "kid". I helped him carry one bag of onions up to the 10th floor; he refused at first, then laughed and said, "Well, a young hero today, eh?"

⑧ At our door, I told Mum the whole story, quickly, all in one breath. She held my shoulder and said, "See, good people are everywhere, if you are ready to meet them." That night, I wrote just one line in my notebook: "**A small accident opened a small door in the wall of strangers.**" I did not know Mr. Bai's full address. But on Saturday, when the smell of onion pancakes drifted from somewhere on our floor, I smiled — and so, I hoped, did the little girl with two small ponytails.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where was the writer when the elevator suddenly stopped?**

A. Between the 10th and 11th floor.  
B. Between the 8th and 9th floor.  
C. On the 15th floor.  
D. In the lobby on the ground floor.

**2. How did Mr. Bai help the writer calm down in the elevator?**

A. He called the police at once.  
B. He shouted at the repair team through the ceiling.  
C. He pressed the emergency button, offered water, and chatted kindly.  
D. He gave the writer his phone to play games.

**3. What did Mr. Bai do before he retired?**

A. He was a school teacher.  
B. He was a truck driver between Harbin and Guangzhou.  
C. He was a vegetable seller in the market.  
D. He was a factory worker in Shanghai.

**4. Why did Mr. Bai buy two bags of spring onions?**

A. Because he liked to eat them every day.  
B. Because his neighbours asked him to buy some.  
C. Because his granddaughter would come and loved onion pancakes.  
D. Because he wanted to sell them later.

**5. What does the underlined sentence "A small accident opened a small door in the wall of strangers" most likely mean?**

A. The stuck elevator damaged a real door in the building.  
B. The writer and Mr. Bai built a new door together.  
C. An unexpected event helped the writer meet a kind neighbour and feel less distant.  
D. The writer decided to move to another building.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Between the 8th and the 9th floor, the elevator shook"  
【解析】8~9 层之间。A、C、D 不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段"按红色紧急按钮+递水+讲话"  
【解析】多措并举。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "retired truck driver who used to drive between Harbin and Guangzhou"  
【解析】退休卡车司机。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "granddaughter... loved onion pancakes"  
【解析】为孙女做葱油饼。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】词句理解题  
【定位】第⑧段末句 + 全文感悟  
【解析】小意外让作者与陌生邻居拉近距离。A、B、D 均非原意。故选 C。

- **pancake** [n.] 薄饼；煎饼
