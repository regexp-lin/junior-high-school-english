# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Day I Helped a Foreign Visitor Find Her Hotel**

① Last Saturday afternoon, I was on my way to the library with an English homework book under my arm. At the crossing near South Gate Mall, I noticed a tall woman with a big rolling suitcase standing on the pavement. She had short silver hair, red sunglasses pushed up on her head, and a paper map in her hand. She looked up at the road signs, looked down at the map, and then looked up again. She was clearly lost.

② I walked closer but did not say anything at first. I felt a little **shy**. My English teacher had often said, "When you see a foreign visitor, be polite but confident. Your English is not perfect, but a smile is universal." Still, my heart beat quickly. Would she understand my accent? What if I said something wrong? But the lights of the zebra crossing started to flash, and she was turning the wrong way.

③ I took a deep breath. "Excuse me," I said softly, "do you need help?" The woman turned around, surprised, and then smiled widely. "Oh, sweetheart, yes! I am looking for the **Sunshine River Hotel**. Is it near here?" I looked at her map. I recognised the hotel — it was only three blocks away, but the map had tiny old street names and she had been turning towards the wrong direction. I pointed at two tall red buildings at the end of the street. "Not far. I can walk you to the corner. Is that OK?"

④ We walked together for about eight minutes. She told me that her name was Mrs. Carter, she came from Canada, and it was her **first visit** to our city. She had come to attend her son's wedding here on Monday. She praised our spring weather and said, "The cherry blossoms here are kinder than my old school books told me." I asked her carefully about Canadian winter, and she laughed. "It is so cold that my car keys sometimes freeze on my bare fingers!"

⑤ At the last corner, I pointed to the bright red sign of the Sunshine River Hotel. Mrs. Carter pulled a small yellow maple-leaf pin from her bag and tried to give it to me as a thank-you gift. I shook my head. "Please keep it for your grandchildren," I said, remembering something my grandmother had taught me — that helping people should not ask for return. She looked at me softly, took my hand for a moment, and said, "Then I will tell my friends back home about a kind Chinese boy at a red light." I smiled and waved goodbye.

⑥ That night, I sat in my bedroom for a long time. Outside, the city was full of small lights. I had been worried about my English exam for weeks, but suddenly I realised something: I had just had an eight-minute, real English conversation with a kind stranger from Canada, and I had not died of fear. The textbook felt lighter on my desk. I understood that English is not only a subject in school. It is a tiny bridge. And that afternoon, on a simple pavement near South Gate Mall, I had quietly helped to build one.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where did the writer first see Mrs. Carter?**

A. Inside the library.  
B. In a busy supermarket.  
C. At the crossing near South Gate Mall.  
D. At the airport.

**2. Why did the writer feel shy at first?**

A. He had lost his own map.  
B. He worried about his accent and about making mistakes in English.  
C. He was hungry.  
D. He hated foreign visitors.

**3. Why had Mrs. Carter come to the writer's city?**

A. To teach English.  
B. To attend her son's wedding on Monday.  
C. To visit a sick friend in a hospital.  
D. To open a restaurant.

**4. Why did the writer refuse Mrs. Carter's small maple-leaf pin?**

A. He did not like its colour.  
B. He remembered his grandmother's teaching that helping people should not ask for return.  
C. He already had many pins.  
D. He wanted more money instead.

**5. What is the MAIN lesson the writer learned that day?**

A. English is too hard to study.  
B. Helping strangers is always dangerous.  
C. English is not only a school subject — it is a tiny bridge between people, and real courage comes from trying.  
D. Foreign visitors should not come to the city.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段第2句 "At the crossing near South Gate Mall..."  
【解析】初次看到外国游客是在南门商场的路口。A/B/D 均不符。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Would she understand my accent? What if I said something wrong?"  
【解析】作者担心口音和说错话。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "She had come to attend her son's wedding here on Monday."  
【解析】来参加儿子婚礼。A/C/D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "helping people should not ask for return."  
【解析】作者记得奶奶所教。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳/写作意图题  
【定位】第⑥段末 "English is not only a subject in school. It is a tiny bridge."  
【解析】作者领悟到英语是沟通的桥，勇敢尝试最重要。A/B/D 均与原文相反或偏题。故选 C。
