# 阅读理解（说明文） **The Story of Chinese Tea**

① Next time you see a small white cup of tea on a table, remember that it carries a very long story. The Chinese have been drinking tea for more than **4,000 years**. It is said that a long time ago, Emperor Shennong was boiling water under a tree when a few leaves fell into his pot. The water turned gold-brown, and when he tasted it, he found a fresh, bitter-sweet flavour. He smiled and said, "This water wakes up the mind." That is how tea began, at least in the old Chinese tale.

② In China today, there are **six main kinds of tea**: green tea, black tea, white tea, yellow tea, oolong tea and dark tea. Though all are made from the leaves of the same plant (the *Camellia sinensis*), the colour, smell and taste differ because of how the leaves are treated. Green tea is quickly steamed or pan-fired to keep its fresh smell. Black tea is fully **oxidised**, so its taste is stronger and its colour deeper. White tea is only picked and softly dried — the gentlest process of all.

③ Tea also changed the way Chinese people meet. In the ancient south, guests would be welcomed with "three cups of tea": the first bitter, the second sweet, and the third warm and soft. This idea is like a picture of life itself — difficult moments, sweet moments, and peaceful moments, all met with the same patient heart. Even today, in many families, a cup of tea is offered to guests before any words are spoken.

④ Tea travelled far from its home. During the Tang Dynasty, Japanese monks took tea plants back to Japan, where the beautiful tea ceremony later grew. Along the **Silk Road**, tea reached the Middle East, and later it sailed to Europe on tall ships. In London of the eighteenth century, drinking tea with milk and sugar became so popular that a new short break — the famous **"afternoon tea"** — was invented. From West to East, one little leaf had touched hundreds of cultures.

⑤ Tea is also good for health. It contains a small amount of **caffeine** — enough to wake the mind, yet less than coffee. Many studies show that drinking tea regularly may help the heart and clear the mind. However, doctors remind us not to drink tea too strong or too late at night, and never to drink it right before bed, because it may stop us from sleeping well.

⑥ In a busy modern life, a cup of tea still has a quiet power. It gives the hand something warm to hold, the mind something soft to watch (the small steam rising), and the voice something to pause over. To drink tea slowly, with a friend or with oneself, is to slow down the hurried world — a small gift that four thousand years of history have left us in one tiny cup.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. According to the old Chinese tale, how did tea begin?**

A. Farmers planted tea trees on mountains.  
B. Leaves fell into Emperor Shennong's pot and flavoured the water.  
C. Monks brought seeds from Japan.  
D. A soldier found tea leaves in a cave.

**2. What is true about green tea?**

A. It is fully oxidised.  
B. It is softly dried without any heating.  
C. It is quickly steamed or pan-fired to keep its fresh smell.  
D. It is boiled with milk and sugar.

**3. What does the "three cups of tea" idea in Paragraph 3 picture?**

A. Three common illnesses.  
B. Three stages of life — difficult, sweet, and peaceful.  
C. Three famous mountains in China.  
D. Three kinds of Chinese food.

**4. How did tea reach Europe?**

A. It was sent by plane in the 18th century.  
B. It sailed to Europe on tall ships after reaching the Middle East.  
C. Chinese people walked it across the mountains to Paris.  
D. It was sent from Japan to London directly.

**5. What warning do doctors give about drinking tea?**

A. People should drink tea as strong as possible.  
B. Tea should be drunk only at night.  
C. Tea should not be drunk too strong or right before bed.  
D. Children should never drink any tea.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段神农故事  
【解析】传说叶子落入锅中产生茶。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Green tea is quickly steamed or pan-fired"  
【解析】绿茶快速蒸烤保香。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第③段 "difficult moments, sweet moments, and peaceful moments"  
【解析】三盏茶象征人生三段。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "sailed to Europe on tall ships"  
【解析】经中东后乘船抵达欧洲。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "not to drink tea too strong or too late at night"  
【解析】茶不宜浓，也不宜睡前。故选 C。
