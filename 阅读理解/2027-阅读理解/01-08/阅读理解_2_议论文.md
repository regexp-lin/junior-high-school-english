# 阅读理解（议论文）

## **Paper Books or E-books?**

① Reading is important for teenagers, but **how we read** is changing fast. Today, many students read on phones or tablets, while others still love paper books. In the library and at home, you can see both habits side by side. People often ask: which way is better for learning and for life?

② **Paper books** have clear strengths. They are easy on the eyes for long reading. You can turn pages, write notes in the margins, and feel the weight of a real book. Many teachers say paper books help students focus because there are no message alerts or games on the same screen. You also do not need to worry about a low battery when you study for exams.

③ **E-books** are popular for good reasons. They save space and money: one small device can hold hundreds of books. You can search a word quickly, change letter size, and read in low light with a soft screen light. For busy students, carrying a phone may be easier than carrying several heavy books every day. Online stores often sell e-books at lower prices, so more titles are within reach.

④ **However, the “best” choice is not the same for everyone.** Some subjects need deep, slow reading—paper may win. For quick review or travel reading, e-books can be smarter. Your goal and your timetable should guide the choice. What matters most is not the tool, but **the habit of reading every day** and thinking about what you read.

⑤ In my view, we should welcome both forms. Use e-books when you need speed and convenience; use paper books when you want fewer distractions. The real question is not paper **versus** digital, but whether we keep reading with curiosity and care. Good readers learn to match the format to the moment, and that skill is useful for life.

**注释：** tablet 平板电脑；margin 页边空白；alert 提示；versus 与……相对

### 选择题

**1.** According to Paragraph ②, why might paper books help students focus?

A. Because they are always cheaper than e-books.

**2.** According to Paragraph ③, what do e-books help save?

A. The writer thinks paper books are useless in modern life.

D. The writer tells students to stop using phones for reading.

---

### 答案与解析

**1.**  
**【答案】** B  
**【题型】** 细节理解  
**【定位】** 第②段最后一句：“Many teachers say paper books help students focus because there are **no message alerts or games on the same screen**.”  
**【解析】** 纸质书有助专注是因为同一屏幕上没有信息提示和游戏，选 B。A 价格未在②段作为专注原因；C、D 是电子书特点，与②段不符。

**2.**  
**【答案】** A  
**【题型】** 细节理解  
**【定位】** 第③段：“They **save space and money**: one small device can hold hundreds of books.”  
**【解析】** 电子书可节省空间与金钱，选 A。B、C、D 在③段中均未作为电子书的优点提及。

**3.**  
**【答案】** C  
**【题型】** 推理判断  
**【定位】** 第④段：“What matters most is not the tool, but **the habit of reading every day** and thinking about what you read.”  
**【解析】** 作者强调工具不如每日阅读习惯重要，可推断 C。A、B 与④⑤段“两者皆可、因人因情况而异”不符；D 过于绝对，文中未否定用手机阅读。

**4.**  
**【答案】** B  
**【题型】** 主旨大意  
**【定位】** 第①段设问 “which way is better”，②③分述，④⑤总结态度。  
**【解析】** 全文围绕纸质书与电子书对比并给出平衡观点，B 最能概括。A、C、D 或偏离主题或未涵盖全文。

**5.**  
**【答案】** A  
**【题型】** 词义猜测  
**【定位】** 第⑤段：“Use e-books when you need speed and convenience; use paper books when you want fewer **distractions**.” 与②段 “no message alerts or games” 形成呼应。  
**【解析】** 想要更少 distractions 时选纸质书，与电子设备上的干扰相对，distractions 指分散注意力的事物，选 A。B、C、D 与语境无关。
