# 阅读理解（议论文） **Is It Better to Sit in the Front Row of the Classroom?**

Every September, when a new class is formed, there is always a quiet discussion in the first week: **who will get the front row seats**? Some students think the front row is "the best place", because teachers can see them clearly and they can hear every word. Other students say that the back row is more free and less stressful. Which is actually better? I believe the front row is **slightly** better, but only if students use it in the right way.

The first benefit of the front row is **attention**. When your seat is one or two metres from the teacher, your eyes naturally follow the blackboard and the teacher's hand movements. It is simply harder to daydream when the teacher is right in front of you. Research by a team in Shanghai showed that students sitting in the front three rows kept attention for 8 – 10 minutes longer in a 45-minute class than those in the back three rows. This extra attention does not sound like a lot, but in one semester it can mean many more ideas fully understood.

The second benefit is **eye and ear health**. The words on a blackboard are smaller than they look. Sitting at the back row may force some students to half-close their eyes to read, or to lean forward for a long time. Over months, this can lead to headaches and a faster drop in eyesight. A front row seat reduces the effort for both eyes and ears, especially for students who already wear glasses.

But some people argue that the front row is **too stressful**. "The teacher can see your every mistake," they say. In my view, this is not really a problem. Teachers do not stop liking students because of a wrong answer; they usually respect students who try. Of course, a shy student may feel nervous at first, but in three or four weeks this feeling fades. The temporary stress is much smaller than the long-term benefit of paying attention.

There is, however, one real worry: **fairness**. If front row seats go only to "good students", weaker students stay at the back and are even harder to reach. The class becomes a quiet line between "seen" and "not seen". To solve this, our school rotates seats every four weeks, so every student takes a turn in the front. This simple rule is the best of both worlds: front-row benefits are shared, and no student is hidden forever.

So what is my final opinion? The front row is not a magic spot. A lazy student in the front row will still not learn; a careful student in the back can still do well. But if you are willing to sit forward and look up, you will find that the classroom looks a little brighter, a little clearer. And sometimes, that little difference is all you need.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the author's main opinion?**

A. The back row is always the best place.  
B. The front row is slightly better, but only if students use it in the right way.  
C. Where students sit has no effect at all.  
D. Only top students should sit in the front row.

**2. According to paragraph 2, what is one benefit of sitting in the front?**

A. The teacher shouts at other rows, not the front.  
B. Students in the front row do not need to do homework.  
C. Students in the front three rows keep their attention for 8 – 10 minutes longer in a 45-minute class.  
D. Students in the front row eat snacks in class.

**3. Why can the back row be bad for eyes and ears?**

A. Because the lights in the back are too bright.  
B. Because students may half-close their eyes or lean forward for a long time to see the blackboard.  
C. Because the back row is always too warm.  
D. Because classmates at the back speak loudly.

**4. How does the author respond to the worry that the front row is too stressful?**

A. The author says teachers are always angry.  
B. The author says the worry is right and students should avoid the front row.  
C. The author says teachers usually respect students who try, and the stress fades in three or four weeks.  
D. The author says stress is the main goal of school.

**5. How does the school solve the "fairness" problem?**

A. By fixing the same seats for the whole year.  
B. By letting students choose any seat at any time.  
C. By rotating seats every four weeks, so every student takes a turn in the front.  
D. By banning the front row.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第 1 段末句  
【解析】略好但需正确使用。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段研究结果  
【解析】注意力时长多 8–10 分钟。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段  
【解析】眯眼、前倾易致头痛、近视。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段  
【解析】尊重努力学生；紧张感会淡化。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段  
【解析】每 4 周轮换座位。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。
