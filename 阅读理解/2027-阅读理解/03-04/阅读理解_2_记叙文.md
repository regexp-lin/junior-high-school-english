# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Injured Kite in Our Courtyard**

① It was a windy Sunday afternoon last March. My little sister Mimi and I were flying our new blue kite in the courtyard of our building. The wind was strong, and our kite danced up into the grey sky like a small happy fish. Suddenly, we heard a sharp cry, and something **heavy** dropped near the flower bed. It was not our kite. It was a real bird — a small hawk with black-and-white feathers.

② Mimi was scared. "It's bleeding! Is it dying?" she shouted. The bird's left wing was bent in a strange way, and its thin leg was caught in a piece of kite string, probably from another kite flying nearby. I told Mimi to stand back and keep calm, then I used my jacket to cover the bird gently so that it could not hurt itself more by moving.

③ Grandpa Zhao, who was reading a newspaper on a bench, walked over with his cane. "Never touch wild birds with bare hands," he said softly. "Hawks are proud birds. They will try to bite." He asked me to bring a shoebox from home and put some old cloth inside. I ran upstairs two steps at a time, my heart beating fast.

④ When the bird was safe inside the box, Grandpa Zhao called the **city wildlife rescue centre**. A lady named Ms. Lin answered. She said, "Please do not give the bird water or food. Just keep it in a dark, quiet place. We will arrive in twenty minutes." While we waited, Mimi kept whispering to the box, "Please don't be afraid. The doctor is coming."

⑤ Ms. Lin came with two workers and a small blue van. She praised us for using a jacket instead of bare hands, and for not trying to feed the bird. "Many kind people make wild birds worse," she said, "because they don't know the right way." Three weeks later, we received a short message with a photo: the little hawk had a clean wing and a bright eye again, and was going to be set free in the mountains the next Sunday.

⑥ That night, Mimi drew a picture of the kite and the bird together and put it on the fridge. I looked at it for a long time. The wind had blown our plastic kite down from the sky, but it had also brought a small lesson into our home: kindness is not enough by itself; kindness with the **right knowledge** is what really helps.

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What were the writer and Mimi doing when they saw the bird?**

A. Riding their bikes in the park.  
B. Reading in the living room.  
C. Flying a kite in the courtyard.  
D. Shopping at the supermarket.

**2. What was wrong with the bird when it fell?**

A. It had broken its beak on a stone.  
B. It had a bent wing and its leg was caught in kite string.  
C. It was too cold to open its eyes.  
D. It had lost all its feathers.

**3. Why did Grandpa Zhao tell the writer not to touch the bird with bare hands?**

A. Hawks might bite and hurt people.  
B. The bird's feathers were too hot.  
C. Wild birds carry red paint.  
D. The bird was the writer's pet.

**4. What did Ms. Lin ask the children to do while waiting?**

A. Feed the bird some bread.  
B. Give the bird warm water.  
C. Keep the bird in a dark, quiet place.  
D. Take the bird to a hospital themselves.

**5. What lesson does the writer learn from this story?**

A. Plastic kites are bad for wild birds.  
B. Small girls should not play in the courtyard.  
C. Kindness is only useful when it meets the right knowledge.  
D. Wild birds always need to live alone in cages.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段第 1–2 句 "flying our new blue kite in the courtyard"  
【解析】他们正在院子里放风筝。A、B、D 均与原文地点/活动不符。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第 3 句 "left wing was bent... thin leg was caught in a piece of kite string"  
【解析】翅膀弯折+脚被风筝线缠住。A、C、D 与原文不符。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段第 2 句 "Hawks are proud birds. They will try to bite."  
【解析】鹰可能会咬人。B、C、D 均无依据。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段第 3 句 "keep it in a dark, quiet place"  
【解析】Ms. Lin 要求把鸟放在黑暗安静的地方等她们过来。A 项"喂面包"、B 项"喂水"被明确禁止；D 项"自己送医"与"We will arrive"矛盾。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段末句 "kindness is not enough by itself; kindness with the right knowledge is what really helps"  
【解析】作者悟出：好心+正确方法才真正有帮助。A、B、D 偏离主旨或无依据。故选 C。
