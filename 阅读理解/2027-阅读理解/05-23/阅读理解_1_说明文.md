# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Do Some Animals Sleep All Winter?**

① When the cold winds of late autumn begin to blow, many forests go quiet. Bears climb into warm caves, hedgehogs curl up under piles of dry leaves, and frogs sink to the soft mud at the bottom of ponds. They will not come out again until spring. This long, deep winter sleep is called **hibernation**. Why do these animals sleep for such a long time?

② The simplest answer is **food**. In winter, most plants stop growing and many small insects disappear. For a bear that usually eats berries, fish and insects, the empty winter forest is like an empty supermarket. Instead of running around and spending a lot of energy looking for food that does not exist, it is much safer to sleep through the hungry months. When the animal wakes up in March or April, the world is warm and full of food again.

③ To hibernate safely, animals spend the autumn **preparing their bodies**. Bears, for example, eat as much as they can every day. They can gain many kilograms in only a few weeks. This extra fat is not a sign of laziness — it is a kind of natural lunchbox. During the long sleep, the body slowly uses the fat to stay alive.

④ During hibernation, the animal's **body changes** in an amazing way. Its heart beats much more slowly, sometimes only a few times a minute. Its breathing becomes soft and quiet. Its body temperature falls close to the temperature of the cave or the mud around it. These changes help it save energy. A ground squirrel, for example, can use only about one-hundredth of its normal energy during deep sleep.

⑤ Not every "winter sleeper" is the same, however. True hibernators, like ground squirrels and hedgehogs, stay asleep for months without waking up. Bears are what scientists call "light hibernators": their heart and breathing slow down, but on warm days they may open their eyes for a few hours. Frogs and some fish, living in cold water, simply slow down almost to a stop. Each animal has its own style, but the goal is the same: **to pass the cold, food-poor season safely**.

⑥ Hibernation is nature's clever answer to hunger and cold. While humans turn on the heater and open the fridge, wild animals turn down their own bodies and wait. Next time you walk through a quiet winter park, remember that under the snow and inside the old trees, thousands of tiny hearts are beating slowly, patiently, waiting for spring.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文围绕 "Why do these animals sleep for such a long time?"  
【解析】文章解释冬眠原因及身体变化。A/C/D 均非主题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "most plants stop growing and many small insects disappear."  
【解析】冬季食物稀缺。A 价值判断；C/D 无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义猜测题  
【定位】第③段 "This extra fat is not a sign of laziness — it is a kind of natural lunchbox."  
【解析】上文指 extra fat。A/C/D 均不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（反向）  
【定位】第④段心跳减慢、呼吸变轻、体温下降  
【解析】冬眠时身体节省能量，不会增大。A/B/C 均属实。故选 D。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Bears are... 'light hibernators'... on warm days they may open their eyes for a few hours."  
【解析】熊为轻度冬眠者。A/C/D 均错误。故选 B。
