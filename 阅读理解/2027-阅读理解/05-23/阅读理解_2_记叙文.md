# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Day I Painted Our Old Wooden Door**

① Our front door is older than I am. My father says it was made by my grandfather more than thirty years ago, from a single piece of brown wood. Over the years the sun had turned it grey, and the rain had left dark lines like old rivers on its surface. One Sunday morning, when Father opened a big tin of fresh green paint, he turned to me and said, "Xiao Zhou, the old door is tired. Shall we give it a new coat?" I almost shouted "Yes!" before he finished speaking.

② Father placed the tin on a low stool in the yard and handed me a soft brush. "First," he said, "we do not paint. We **listen**." That surprised me. He knocked on the door in three places. In one place, the sound was deep; in another, it was hollow. "Where the wood sings, it is strong," he explained, "and where it sounds empty, the wood is soft and we must be gentle." I tried knocking too. Under my fingers, the old door answered with its own slow voice.

③ Next we cleaned the door with warm water and a cloth. Thirty years of dust came off in brown circles. I worked hard on the bottom corner where, as a child, I had once drawn a little sun with a red pencil. Father smiled. "Do you remember this?" he asked. "Mother was angry for one hour, and laughed for many years." I laughed too, and my face felt warm.

④ At last we were ready to paint. Father showed me how to hold the brush, how to dip it half-way into the tin, how to let extra paint drip back before I touched the wood. My first stroke was thick and ugly. Green drops fell on my shoes. But Father did not shout; he only said, "Slower. One line at a time. The door has waited thirty years; it can wait ten more minutes." Little by little, my lines became straight. A small rectangle of fresh green appeared on the old brown wood. It was like seeing the door take a deep breath.

⑤ By the time the sun was high, the whole door was a soft, shining green. I stepped back and looked at it. The cracks were not all gone, and a few marks from the red sun still showed through, faintly. But to me, the door was more beautiful than any new door in the shop. It carried the past **and** the new paint together.

⑥ That evening, when our neighbour walked by, he stopped and said, "What a lovely colour!" I felt proud, but what I felt most was something quieter. For the first time, I understood that old things do not need to be thrown away. With a little water, a soft brush and a patient afternoon, an old door — and perhaps many other tired things in our lives — can become new again.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Who made the front door, according to the story?**

A. The writer.  
B. Father.  
C. The writer's grandfather.  
D. A shop near their house.

**2. Why did Father knock on the door before painting?**

A. To wake up the neighbours.  
B. To hear which parts of the wood were strong and which were soft, so they could be gentle.  
C. To see if anyone was hiding inside.  
D. To break the door.

**3. What did the writer remember while cleaning the door?**

A. That he had drawn a little sun on the corner when he was small.  
B. That he had broken the door last year.  
C. That he had hidden money behind the door.  
D. That the door was bought in a shop.

**4. What did Father say when the writer's first stroke was thick and ugly?**

A. "Stop now. You are useless."  
B. "Slower. One line at a time. The door has waited thirty years."  
C. "Let me do everything."  
D. "The paint is too expensive."

**5. What lesson did the writer learn from this afternoon?**

A. Old things must always be thrown away.  
B. Old things can become new again with care and patience.  
C. Painting is a waste of time.  
D. Green is the best colour for every door.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "it was made by my grandfather more than thirty years ago..."  
【解析】门由祖父制作。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Where the wood sings, it is strong... where it sounds empty, the wood is soft and we must be gentle."  
【解析】敲门辨别木头强弱以决定用力。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "I had once drawn a little sun with a red pencil."  
【解析】作者回忆儿时涂鸦。B/C/D 均无据。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "Slower. One line at a time. The door has waited thirty years; it can wait ten more minutes."  
【解析】父亲温和鼓励。A/C/D 均与原文相反。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题／主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段 "old things do not need to be thrown away..."  
【解析】旧物可以通过细心和耐心焕然一新。A/C/D 与原文相反。故选 B。
