# 阅读理解（记叙文） **A Bowl of Grandma's Congee on a Cold Morning**

① On the first Monday of November, I woke up with a strange, scratchy feeling in my throat and a head that felt twice its normal size. The window was already painted silver by frost; the wind outside moved the dry leaves at the balcony. I tried to sit up, but a bigger cough sent me back to the pillow. Mum came in and touched my forehead. "**38.6 °C**," she said softly after looking at the thermometer. "No school today. Grandma is coming."

② Grandma lived in the next building, on the sixth floor without a lift. At seventy-five, she climbed those stairs three times a week to buy vegetables, always refusing help. By a quarter to nine that morning, her slow, steady steps were at our door. She came in with a small cloth bag and her old blue apron folded inside. "How is our little patient?" she asked, taking off her thick scarf. Her eyes were tired, but her smile was **steady**, like a lamp that never flickers.

③ The first thing she did was **not** speak to me. She walked into the kitchen, opened the rice container, and measured exactly **half a cup of rice**. Then she took out a small piece of lean pork, a salty duck egg from her bag, and two slices of fresh ginger. "**Century-egg and lean-pork congee**," she said softly, more to herself than to me. "For a head cold, this is a little hospital."

④ I sat on the kitchen chair, wrapped in my warmest blanket, and watched. Grandma washed the rice in three slow changes of water. "The third rinse is for luck," she whispered, in the kind way she had spoken to me since I was three. She turned on the small flame, added the rice, and said, "Now we wait. **Fire too big, heart too small.** Congee will not come to the impatient."

⑤ For forty minutes the kitchen was warm and quiet. She chopped the pork into tiny, even pieces. She sliced the century egg so carefully that each piece showed its black-amber inside, like a little map. While we waited, she asked me small questions: Had I drunk water today? Had the dry wind woken me in the night? Did my English teacher still read poems on Monday mornings? I answered between coughs. Her eyes always turned **fully** to me when I spoke, as if there were nothing else to watch.

⑥ When the rice had softened and the congee had turned milky, she added the pork, the egg, the ginger, and a tiny pinch of salt. A gentle white steam rose and fogged the kitchen window into a soft square. She took a spoon, blew on a little pot-corner, and tasted it herself first. Then she gave me a small cup and said, "Start with a spoonful. Slowly." The congee was light, salty, warm, with a **tiny** sharp wink of ginger that opened my stuffed nose. My forehead began to feel less heavy.

⑦ "Grandma," I said after the second spoonful, "you came up six floors in this cold." She smiled and sat on the other chair. "When your grandfather was ill fifty years ago, I made this same congee in a small pot on a coal fire. He used to say the spoon weighed more than the illness. I believed him." She paused. "A good bowl is not just food. It is a **warm hand** you can hold from the inside." I tried to say "thank you". The cough took it from my mouth. She only shook her head kindly: "**Eat. Words can wait.**"

⑧ That evening my temperature came down to 37.2 °C. The congee stayed in the small rice cooker, with a white plate on top "to stop the soul from flying away", Grandma joked in the old village way. After she had gone home — Mum walking her down the six floors with a flashlight — I stood by the dark kitchen, looking at the covered pot. I realised that in twenty years of cold mornings to come, whenever I felt weak or lost, **I would remember this steam, this slow fire, and the kind voice that said "fire too big, heart too small".** A bowl of her congee, I saw for the first time, was a small, portable grandmother, hidden in a warm cup.

---

**注释：** thermometer 体温计；apron 围裙；congee 粥；ginger 姜；century egg 皮蛋

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did Grandma get to the writer's home that morning?**

A. By taxi.  
B. By climbing the stairs to her own flat, with no climbing needed.  
C. By coming from the next building — where she lived on a sixth floor without a lift — with her small cloth bag and old blue apron.  
D. By bus.

**2. What did Grandma mean by saying "Fire too big, heart too small"?**

A. Her stove was broken.  
B. Cooking congee needs a gentle, patient fire; congee will not come to someone who is in a hurry.  
C. The writer should be braver.  
D. The kitchen was too hot.

**3. What ingredients did Grandma use for the congee?**

A. Rice, carrots, potatoes.  
B. Rice, fish, and tomatoes.  
C. Half a cup of rice, a piece of lean pork, a century egg, two slices of fresh ginger, and a tiny pinch of salt.  
D. Just rice and water.

**4. Why did Grandma first taste the congee herself before giving the writer a cup?**

A. Because she was very hungry.  
B. To check that it was the right taste and the right temperature before giving it to the writer.  
C. Because she did not trust the writer's cooking.  
D. Because she wanted to finish it alone.

**5. What does the writer mean in the last paragraph by "a small, portable grandmother, hidden in a warm cup"?**

A. Grandma will come to live with the writer.  
B. The writer will always keep the pot in her room.  
C. Whenever the writer feels weak or lost in the years to come, the memory of this slow, warm congee will carry Grandma's patient love with her.  
D. Congee is the only food the writer likes.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "lived in the next building, on the sixth floor without a lift ... small cloth bag ... old blue apron"  
【解析】路径与物件直引。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段 "Fire too big, heart too small. Congee will not come to the impatient."  
【解析】比喻慢火慢心。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③⑥段材料清单  
【解析】食材与调味直引。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑥段 "blew on a little pot-corner, and tasted it herself first"  
【解析】家常照顾细节。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段 "in twenty years of cold mornings to come ... I would remember ..."  
【解析】一碗粥承载长久温情。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。
