# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Every Family Hold a Short Weekly Family Meeting?**

① In many homes today, parents and children all live busy lives. Adults leave home early for work, students stay late for classes, and after dinner everyone turns to a different screen. Some educators now suggest a simple habit: **a short weekly family meeting**, around 30 minutes on a chosen evening, where every member sits together and talks. Is this old-fashioned idea really useful for modern families? I strongly believe the answer is yes.

② First, a weekly meeting gives every family member a **fair chance to speak**. In daily life, the loudest voice or the busiest schedule often decides everything. In a meeting, each person has two or three minutes to share: what went well this week, what was hard, what they need from the family. Even a shy younger brother or a tired father can be heard without fighting for attention.

③ Second, it makes **family planning much easier**. Who will pick up the little sister on Thursday? When shall we visit Grandpa? Which Saturday is free for a hiking trip? Without a plan, small things become small fights. A 10-minute calendar check during the meeting can prevent many of these misunderstandings and save weekends from being "accidentally empty" or "accidentally crowded".

④ Third, a regular meeting builds **emotional safety**. Research from several family studies suggests that students who regularly talk with parents about feelings — not only about grades — show less anxiety before big exams and solve small friendship problems faster. Knowing that a safe time is coming every week also helps a teenager hold on during a difficult day: "I can say it on Sunday."

⑤ Some parents worry that their children will turn a meeting into a complaint time, asking only for more phones, later bedtimes, or extra pocket money. This is a real risk. However, a simple meeting rule can help: every complaint must be followed by a **suggestion**. "I want more phone time" can become "I will finish homework by 8 p.m. and then we can agree on 30 minutes of phone time." This turns meetings into problem-solving, not empty argument.

⑥ Of course, a meeting is not a magic tool. It only works if family members listen kindly, keep promises, and do not punish each other for honest words. If these simple ground rules are in place, a short weekly family meeting is one of the easiest and cheapest ways to turn a busy house into a connected home. In my view, every family should at least try it for a month — the benefits may be bigger than anyone expects.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the central question of the passage?**

A. Whether children should do more housework.  
B. Whether every family should hold a short weekly family meeting.  
C. Whether parents should give more pocket money.  
D. Whether teenagers should use less phone time.

**2. According to paragraph ②, why is a weekly meeting helpful in daily life?**

A. It lets each person speak fairly, including the quiet ones.  
B. It allows only parents to speak.  
C. It stops all arguments at home.  
D. It forces children to do homework in silence.

**3. Why does the writer say a weekly meeting builds "emotional safety"?**

A. Because students can earn higher scores automatically.  
B. Because there is always a known time to share feelings.  
C. Because families can buy better food.  
D. Because parents can work longer hours.

**4. What rule does the writer suggest to prevent meetings from becoming only complaining time?**

A. Every complaint must be followed by a suggestion.  
B. Children must stay silent for ten minutes.  
C. Parents must give in on every topic.  
D. All meetings must be held outdoors.

**5. What is the writer's final attitude toward weekly family meetings?**

A. Totally opposed.  
B. Unsure and confused.  
C. Strongly supportive; at least try it for a month.  
D. Only useful for very small families.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句 "Is this old-fashioned idea really useful for modern families?"  
【解析】全文讨论家庭是否应举行每周例会。A/C/D 均非中心议题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第3-4句 "Even a shy younger brother or a tired father can be heard..."  
【解析】每人有发言时间，让安静或疲惫者也能被听到。B 只让家长说、C 停止所有争吵、D 让孩子沉默都是断章取义或无中生有。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段第2句 "students who regularly talk with parents about feelings... show less anxiety"；"a safe time is coming every week"  
【解析】知道固定谈心时间能让人心安。A 成绩提升、C 买更好食物、D 延长工作时间均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段末 "every complaint must be followed by a suggestion"  
【解析】每个抱怨必须附带建议。B/C/D 均与原文不符。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】写作态度题  
【定位】第⑥段末句 "every family should at least try it for a month"  
【解析】作者明确鼓励至少尝试一个月。A 反对、B 不确定、D 仅小家庭有用都不符。故选 C。
