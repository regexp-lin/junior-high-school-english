# 阅读理解（记叙文） **My First Day as a Group Leader**

① Last September, our English teacher, Mr. Wang, put us into five small groups. Each group needed a leader, someone who would collect homework, lead discussions, and help classmates when a word or rule was tricky. When Mr. Wang called my name for Group B, I almost dropped my pencil. Me? I was quiet, slow to speak, and I had never been a leader in anything.

② The first day was a mess. I forgot to remind everyone to bring the homework sheet. Two classmates brought it; three did not. When we started the group task — matching new words with pictures — I was so nervous that I kept looking at the floor. Xiao Dan, our class joker, whispered, "Leader? I think we need a new one." Some of my group laughed. My face turned red, and I felt hot tears coming to my eyes. I hid behind my English book.

③ That evening, I told my grandfather what had happened. He listened quietly on our small sofa, drinking his hot tea. Then he said, "A good leader does not shout the loudest. A good leader sees each person — who is fast, who is shy, who is funny. Tomorrow, try one thing: before the task starts, ask a simple question that only that person can answer." I did not fully understand, but I wrote his sentence at the top of my notebook: *A good leader sees each person.*

④ The next Monday, I tried it. When Xiao Dan, our joker, came in, I said, "Xiao Dan, you tell such funny stories. Can you help us today by making a little joke to warm up the group?" His eyes shone; he nodded. To the quiet girl Mei, I said, "Mei, you are always careful with spelling. Could you check our answers at the end?" She smiled and said, "Sure." One by one, I gave each person a small job that fit him or her.

⑤ Something strange happened. The group began to move like a small, warm team. Xiao Dan made a two-sentence joke and everyone laughed. Mei found three small spelling mistakes that we had missed. Another boy, good at drawing, turned new words into tiny cartoon pictures. By the end of class, our group got the highest mark for discussion. Mr. Wang gave me a quiet smile and said, "Well done, leader."

⑥ After class, Xiao Dan came over and said, "Hey, maybe you are really not so bad as a leader." I laughed and said, "That's because I listened to my grandpa." That night, I added another sentence below the first one in my notebook: *A good leader is not the loudest voice. It is the one who helps every voice to be heard.*

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did the writer feel when Mr. Wang named him as the group leader?**

A. Very proud and ready.  
B. Surprised, because he was quiet and had never led anything.  
C. Bored, because he already had too much homework.  
D. Angry with his classmates.

**2. What happened during the writer's first day as a leader?**

A. His group got the highest mark at once.  
B. He forgot to remind classmates about the homework sheet and felt ashamed.  
C. The teacher asked him to sing a song.  
D. Xiao Dan helped him collect all the papers.

**3. What advice did the writer's grandfather give him?**

A. To shout louder than his classmates.  
B. To complain to the teacher.  
C. To see each person and find what each one can do.  
D. To write fewer English words in his notebook.

**4. How did the writer use Xiao Dan's special ability in the new group task?**

A. He asked Xiao Dan to read the textbook aloud.  
B. He asked Xiao Dan to warm up the group with a little joke.  
C. He asked Xiao Dan to clean the blackboard.  
D. He asked Xiao Dan to sit quietly at the back.

**5. What lesson did the writer finally learn about being a leader?**

A. A good leader gives the loudest orders.  
B. A good leader helps every voice to be heard.  
C. A good leader does all the work alone.  
D. A good leader is always the best student.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末句 "I was quiet, slow to speak, and I had never been a leader"  
【解析】意外+自认从未当过领导者。A、C、D 不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "I forgot to remind everyone... I felt hot tears"  
【解析】忘记提醒作业单+尴尬哽咽。A 项"首日高分"与事实相反；C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "A good leader sees each person... who is fast, who is shy, who is funny"  
【解析】看见每个人各自的特点。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "Xiao Dan... making a little joke to warm up the group"  
【解析】让 Xiao Dan 用小笑话暖场。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段末句 "helps every voice to be heard"  
【解析】作者悟出领导者要帮助每一个声音被听见。A、C、D 均与文意不符。故选 B。
