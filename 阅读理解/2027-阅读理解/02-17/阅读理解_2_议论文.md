# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle School Students Take a Summer Part-time Job?**

① When summer holiday arrives, some students travel, some attend weekend classes, and some stay home to read. In recent years, a new voice has joined the chorus: "Why not take a **small part-time job**?" Helping in a parent's shop, folding clothes in a bookstore, or being a junior volunteer at a summer camp — should middle school students really try these things, or is it better to stay away from work until they grow up?

② Those who are against the idea have serious worries. They say a middle school student is still young. The workplace can be tiring, and sometimes unsafe. If a child gets used to making money, study may become less interesting. One parent said, "My daughter is 14. Her job is to read Shakespeare, not to serve milk tea."

③ These worries are not empty, but they can be answered. No one is suggesting that students take long, hard jobs in factories. A *proper* part-time job for a middle school student means something **short**, **safe** and **interesting** — perhaps four hours on a Saturday morning, standing beside a father in his small bookshop or helping an aunt in her flower shop. Such work can never replace study, but it can teach lessons no classroom alone can offer.

④ First, a small job teaches the real meaning of **money**. A student who earns 80 yuan in a hot afternoon learns to count his money more carefully than a student who only spends it. A bottle of bubble tea no longer seems so cheap. A new pair of sneakers may wait a little longer. These small changes in the mind help students become wiser customers for the rest of their lives.

⑤ Second, a small job builds **real-world skills**. Speaking politely with strangers, checking a list twice, solving a sudden problem without a teacher's help — these are the bricks of a mature mind. One Grade 9 boy told his teacher last September that one summer week in his uncle's small bakery had taught him more about patience than a whole year of maths exercises. His teacher only smiled and said, "Then your uncle is also your teacher."

⑥ Of course, a few fair rules are needed. The work must be **light**, must not be at night, and must never replace study. Parents should stay close by. Schools can offer a simple list of safe places, such as libraries, small museums or community helpers. Under these fair rules, two or three summer mornings behind a small counter may not earn much money, yet they can give a young person something far more valuable — the quiet pride of standing on his own feet for the first time.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What question does this passage discuss?**

A. Whether students should attend summer school.  
B. Whether middle school students should take a part-time summer job.  
C. Whether parents should take summer holidays.  
D. Whether schools should open on Sundays.

**2. Why are some parents against the idea?**

A. They think the workplace is boring.  
B. They think money is too easy to earn.  
C. They worry that work may tire students and make them lose interest in study.  
D. They want children to join the army instead.

**3. What kind of part-time job does the writer support?**

A. A job that pays the most money.  
B. A long full-time job in a factory.  
C. A short, safe and interesting job.  
D. A job that needs night shifts.

**4. According to Paragraph 4, what does a small job teach students about money?**

A. That money is easy to get.  
B. That money should be kept forever.  
C. That earning money needs effort, so money should be spent carefully.  
D. That money is useless for students.

**5. What is the writer's final view?**

A. Students should never take any job.  
B. Every student must take a full-time summer job.  
C. Light summer jobs under fair rules can help students grow.  
D. Parents should send their children far away to work.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句  
【解析】主题是中学生是否应打暑期工。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "workplace can be tiring... study may become less interesting"  
【解析】担心工作让学习兴趣下降。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "short, safe and interesting"  
【解析】短、安全、有趣的兼职。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "learns to count his money more carefully"  
【解析】明白挣钱不易、消费要慎。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "Under these fair rules... stand on his own feet"  
【解析】赞成在合理规则下进行轻度兼职。故选 C。
