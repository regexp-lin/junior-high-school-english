# 阅读理解（应用文） **City Summer Reading Program — Library Invitation**

① Long summer days are just around the corner, and the **Central City Library** is once again opening its doors to young readers. This year's **Summer Reading Program**, now in its eighteenth year, welcomes all middle school students to share one warm, bookish summer.

② **Program Information**

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Dates | July 1 – August 25, 2027 |
| Open Hours | Tue – Sun, 9:00 a.m. – 7:00 p.m. (Closed on Mondays) |
| Place | Central City Library, 58 Garden Road |
| Fee | Free for all Grades 6–9 students with a library card |
| Library Card | Apply at the front desk with your school ID card |

③ **How the Program Works**

- **Read at your own pace.** Take home up to **6** books at a time; return them within 21 days.  
- **Write a short report.** After each book, write 3 to 5 sentences about the part you liked most. A notebook will be given to every new reader.  
- **Collect small stamps.** For each report, the librarian will give you a little golden stamp.

④ **Levels & Small Prizes**

- **5 stamps** — one handmade bookmark.  
- **10 stamps** — a blue "Reading Explorer" pin and a free cup of warm cocoa.  
- **20 stamps** — a ticket to the English Story Night on August 15.  
- **30 stamps** — the proud title "Summer Reading Star" and your name on the library's Wall of Honour.

⑤ **Three Special Activities**

- **Book Chat Sunday** (every Sunday, 3:00 p.m. – 4:00 p.m.): Students sit in small groups and talk about a book of their choice.  
- **Meet the Writer** (July 20, 7:00 p.m.): Local writer Ms. Zhao shares how she wrote her children's novel *River of Stars*.  
- **Volunteer Shelf Helpers** (any Saturday): Help the librarian place books on shelves and earn 2 service hours.

⑥ **Please Note**

- Bring your own water bottle; no drinks are allowed near the shelves.  
- Please **do not** eat snacks inside the reading rooms; a small snack area is provided near the entrance.  
- Phones should be in **silent** mode. Calls should be taken at the quiet room on the first floor.  
- Lost or damaged books should be reported at the front desk; replacing books is free of charge for students who tell the librarian honestly.

⑦ To sign up, please scan the QR code on your school notice board or visit the library before **June 28**. For more help, call **0571-6688-1234** or email **summer@clib.org**. Let a hundred stories keep your summer cool and bright!

**根据材料内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. When does the Summer Reading Program end?**

A. June 28.  
B. July 1.  
C. August 15.  
D. August 25.

**2. How many books can a student take home at one time?**

A. Up to 3 books.  
B. Up to 6 books.  
C. Up to 10 books.  
D. Up to 15 books.

**3. Which prize is given when a student collects 20 stamps?**

A. A handmade bookmark.  
B. A blue "Reading Explorer" pin.  
C. A ticket to the English Story Night.  
D. The title "Summer Reading Star."

**4. What will happen at the "Meet the Writer" event on July 20?**

A. A local writer will share how she wrote a children's novel.  
B. Students will act in a play.  
C. A famous actor will sign T-shirts.  
D. Students will write their own short book.

**5. Which of the following is NOT allowed inside the reading rooms?**

A. Using the phone in silent mode.  
B. Eating snacks.  
C. Reading quietly.  
D. Writing a short report.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "July 1 – August 25, 2027"  
【解析】活动 8 月 25 日结束。故选 D。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "up to 6 books at a time"  
【解析】一次最多 6 本。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "20 stamps — a ticket to the English Story Night"  
【解析】20 枚换门票。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Local writer Ms. Zhao shares how she wrote her children's novel"  
【解析】本地作家分享创作。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "Please do not eat snacks inside the reading rooms"  
【解析】阅览室内不准吃零食。故选 B。
