# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Secret Drawer in Grandpa's Old Desk**

① Every Saturday, I help Grandpa clean his tiny study. His desk is made of **dark brown wood**, heavy as a little mountain, and older than my father. It has four drawers on the right side, and I have opened three of them many times: ink bottles, old stamps, a small black camera. But the fourth drawer, the lowest one, has always been **locked**. Grandpa would smile softly and say, "Another day, Xiao Ye. Another day."

② Last week, Grandpa's back hurt, so I cleaned the study alone for the first time. When I wiped the desk, my sleeve caught on a thin metal key that had been **taped under the top drawer**. I froze. The key was cool in my hand, and the thought of the locked drawer made my heart jump. I knew I should ask first. But the curiosity in me was a loud, stubborn bird. I slowly knelt down and turned the key.

③ The drawer slid open with a tired squeak. Inside, there was no gold or hidden money. There were only three things: a **small pink ribbon**, a yellowed **paper boat**, and a thin **notebook** with edges softened by time. I sat on the wooden floor, confused and a little disappointed. Then I opened the notebook. The first line, in Grandpa's careful handwriting, read: "**Memories are the real treasure of any old man.**" Below that, I found a neat list.

④ The first entry said: "**Pink ribbon — Spring 1978.** Your grandma gave me this the day I left our village for the army. She tied it to my bag so I would not forget my way home." The second entry: "**Paper boat — Summer 1989.** Your mother made this for me the day I got out of hospital. She was only seven. I floated it once across the pond; it did not sink." The third entry stopped me mid-breath. It said: "**Blue hairband — not yet here.** One day, Xiao Ye will give me a gift of his own."

⑤ My face grew hot. I had always thought Grandpa's old study was just a quiet, dusty place. I never knew it was a **small museum** of our family's love. Just then, the study door opened. Grandpa stood in the doorway, slowly holding his back. He did not look angry when he saw the open drawer. He just sighed softly and sat down beside me on the floor.

⑥ "Grandpa, I'm sorry," I whispered. "I used the key without asking." Grandpa picked up the paper boat and turned it over in his hand. "Xiao Ye," he said, "this drawer is not a **secret**. It is a **waiting room**. I was waiting for the right moment to open it with you." He looked at me with soft, wet eyes. "Now I think that right moment is today." I felt a strange mix of shame and warmth, like a small sun rising inside my chest.

⑦ That Sunday, I went to my room and made my own small gift: a **blue paper hairband** from a ribbon Mum had kept in a sewing box. I wrote on the back in careful English letters: "*For Grandpa's drawer, from Xiao Ye, Spring 2027. May we have many more treasures to add.*" When I placed it in his hand, Grandpa did not speak. He just held my fingers tight. I finally understood — **the most valuable things in an old drawer are not things at all. They are the stories only our family can tell.**

---

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why had the writer never looked inside the fourth drawer before?**

A. The drawer had no handle.  
B. The drawer was always locked.  
C. The drawer was too heavy to open.  
D. The drawer was full of dangerous tools.

**2. How did the writer find the key to the locked drawer?**

A. Grandpa gave it to him as a present.  
B. He bought it at a market.  
C. He found it taped under the top drawer while cleaning.  
D. He borrowed it from his mother.

**3. According to Grandpa's notebook, why did the writer's mother make the paper boat?**

A. Because she wanted to sell it at school.  
B. Because Grandpa had just come out of hospital, and she was only seven.  
C. Because it was for a paper boat competition.  
D. Because the village had a festival.

**4. How did Grandpa react when he saw the open drawer?**

A. He was very angry and shouted at the writer.  
B. He took away all the items quietly.  
C. He sighed softly and sat down beside the writer.  
D. He laughed loudly and left the room.

**5. What does the writer finally understand?**

A. Old drawers always hide gold or money.  
B. Family treasures are the stories only our family can tell.  
C. Grandpas should never use old desks.  
D. Keys should be thrown away after use.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "the fourth drawer... has always been locked"  
【解析】第四抽屉一直上锁。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "thin metal key that had been taped under the top drawer"  
【解析】钥匙藏在上抽屉底部。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "Your mother made this for me the day I got out of hospital"  
【解析】妈妈七岁为爷爷出院做的。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "just sighed softly and sat down beside me"  
【解析】轻叹并坐下。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦段末  
【解析】家族故事才是真正珍宝。故选 B。
