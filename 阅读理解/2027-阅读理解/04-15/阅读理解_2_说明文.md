# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Do Sunflowers Turn Towards the Sun?**

① In a summer field, a long line of **yellow sunflowers** stands like a crowd of quiet children, all looking up at the sky in the same direction. In the morning, their bright faces look east. In the afternoon, they seem to have slowly turned to the west. Country people have noticed this for hundreds of years, and even poets have written about "**the flower that walks with the sun**". But what is really happening inside a sunflower, and why does it do this?

② The special movement is called **heliotropism**, from Greek words meaning "turning with the sun". Interestingly, this movement does not happen all the sunflower's life. Only **young sunflowers**, still growing, turn slowly during the day to follow the sun across the sky. Once a sunflower is fully grown, it usually **stops turning** and faces only east, like a frozen clock.

③ So how do young sunflowers do this? The answer is surprisingly clever. Each day, cells on the **east side** of the stem grow faster in the morning, while cells on the **west side** grow faster in the late afternoon. When one side of the stem is slightly longer than the other, the top of the plant **leans towards the shorter side**. As the sun moves from east to west, this difference in growth moves along with it. The whole plant seems to be walking, but really, it is growing unevenly all day.

④ Why would a plant use so much energy to turn? Scientists have found at least two good reasons. First, following the sun means **more sunlight**. More sunlight means more **photosynthesis**, so more food for the plant. Young sunflowers that track the sun can grow up to **20% larger** than young plants that are not allowed to turn.

⑤ The second reason is about **bees and other pollinators**. Once a sunflower becomes an adult and stops moving, it keeps its face pointing **east**. In the morning, the eastern sky warms up faster than the rest of the field. A sunflower facing east becomes warm sooner, so the bees wake up and fly to these warm flowers first. In this way, east-facing sunflowers get more **insect visits**, more pollen delivery, and finally more seeds. A simple choice of direction can decide whether a sunflower produces a full head or only a few seeds.

⑥ So the next time you pass a field of sunflowers, you can whisper to them a small secret: "I know why you turn." Their dance is not just for beauty. It is a careful, living dance of **light, growth, and survival** — a tiny, yellow reminder that even the quietest plants make very smart choices, every single hour of every sunny day.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is "heliotropism"?**

A. A special kind of music.  
B. A plant's movement to follow the sun.  
C. A rare disease in sunflowers.  
D. A farming tool.

**2. Which sunflowers actually turn with the sun during the day?**

A. Only adult sunflowers.  
B. Only young, growing sunflowers.  
C. Only sunflowers at night.  
D. Sunflowers of all ages equally.

**3. How does a young sunflower turn its head during the day?**

A. It has tiny muscles under its petals.  
B. One side of the stem grows faster in the morning, the other in the afternoon.  
C. Bees push the flowers around.  
D. Wind blows the stem in all directions.

**4. Why is following the sun helpful for young sunflowers?**

A. They can speak to other plants.  
B. They scare away birds.  
C. They get more sunlight, more food, and grow about 20% larger.  
D. They become cooler than other plants.

**5. Why do adult sunflowers face mostly east?**

A. Because the wind always blows from the east.  
B. Because the east side of the field is more beautiful.  
C. Because east-facing flowers warm up sooner, attract more bees, and make more seeds.  
D. Because farmers plant them that way.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "heliotropism... turning with the sun"  
【解析】追日现象。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Only young sunflowers... turn slowly"  
【解析】只有幼苗追日。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段生长原理  
【解析】两侧生长速率差。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "more sunlight... 20% larger"  
【解析】光合更强，长得更大。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "warm sooner... more insect visits... more seeds"  
【解析】朝东吸引蜜蜂助授粉。故选 C。
