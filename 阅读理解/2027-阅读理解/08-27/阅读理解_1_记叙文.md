# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Summer I Finally Let Go of the Edge**

① Until the summer before Grade 9, I could not swim. Most of my classmates had learned in primary school, during their bright summer holidays in the **public pool** downtown. But I had always stayed away from deep water. When I was six years old, my cousin had jumped into a small pond, pretending he could not rise again, just to tease me. His joke lasted only ten seconds, but my fear of deep water lasted eight years.

② So every summer, when my friends talked about swimming, I smiled and said, "I prefer books." But deep down, I felt a strange loneliness whenever they came back from the pool, their hair still wet, their faces shining. Last July, my mother looked at me at the dinner table and said softly, "Ming, you will be in senior high next year. Some classmates will invite you to the sea. I do not want you to be the only one who says 'I can't'. This summer, you are going to learn to swim."

③ That Saturday, she drove me to the public pool. Coach Lu, a quiet woman in her forties, was already waiting at the shallow end. "Put on your swim cap," she said kindly. "Today, we will only do three things: hold the wall, blow bubbles, and kick the water. We will not leave the shallow side. I promise."

④ The first ten minutes went well. I held the iron bar at the edge and blew strong bubbles into the water. Then she asked me to let go — just for three seconds — and reach for the bar again. My hand would not obey. Every time I tried, I felt a cold wave of fear climb up my legs. After twenty tries, I began to cry quietly. "Coach, I think I cannot do this. Maybe I am just afraid of water forever."

⑤ Coach Lu did not answer immediately. She pulled herself out of the pool, dried her hands, and sat down on the low edge next to me. "I was also scared of water when I was fifteen," she said. "Not the water itself. The idea that if I let go of the bar, the water would not hold me. But listen, Ming — water holds you all the time. You have to **first believe that**, and only then will you stop trying to hold it yourself."

⑥ She asked me to try something different. "Do not try to swim. Just lie on your back. I will hold your head with one hand. Close your eyes. Let your arms and legs hang softly in the water." I did as she said. My back touched the cool water. For a few seconds, my body still felt stiff — and then, slowly, a strange thing happened. I felt the water **pressing gently up** under my back, under my arms, under my legs. It was not pulling me down. It was holding me.

⑦ "Do you feel it?" she asked quietly. I nodded. A small, warm tear slid from the corner of my eye into the pool water. That little tear, I think, was the last drop of my eight-year-old fear.

⑧ From that afternoon on, things changed. I still could not swim far. But I stopped fighting the water. Each day, step by step, Coach Lu guided me: bubbles, back float, short kicks, then one meter, three meters, five meters. Six weeks later, on a bright Saturday morning, I swam an untidy but true **25 meters** across the shallow end. My mother watched from the side. She said nothing. She only opened her arms wide and waited for me at the other end.

⑨ Now, standing at the beginning of Grade 9, I think often about that summer. The lesson was not really about swimming. It was about **learning to trust something that looks scarier than it is**. Since then, whenever I face a new, frightening thing — a long speech, a hard exam, a new friend — I remember Coach Lu's words: **"Water holds you all the time. You only need to let go, first."**

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why had the writer been afraid of deep water for eight years?**

A. He had almost drowned in the sea when he was a little boy.  
B. His cousin, when the writer was six, had jumped into a pond and pretended he could not rise again, just to tease him.  
C. He had never seen a pool until the age of 14.  
D. His mother had told him deep water was dangerous.

**2. Why did the writer's mother decide that this was the summer he had to learn to swim?**

A. Because she wanted him to become a professional swimmer.  
B. Because he will enter senior high next year and she did not want him to be the only one who says "I can't" when classmates invite him to the sea.  
C. Because the school would test swimming in September.  
D. Because she herself had just won a swimming medal.

**3. What did Coach Lu first promise him at the pool?**

A. That they would only do three simple things and would not leave the shallow side.  
B. That she would throw him into deep water so he learned quickly.  
C. That she would teach him to swim 200 meters in one day.  
D. That her friend would give him a free pool ticket.

**4. What advice from Coach Lu finally helped the writer to relax in the water?**

A. "Hold the bar tightly and never let go."  
B. "Do not try to swim. Lie on your back. Water holds you all the time. You have to first believe that."  
C. "Close your eyes and think of homework."  
D. "Jump into the deepest end; you'll learn in one minute."

**5. What does the writer think is the real lesson of that summer?**

A. That every middle-school student must become a fast swimmer.  
B. That we must learn to trust something that looks scarier than it is, and sometimes we need to "let go, first".  
C. That parents should not push their children into new things.  
D. That swimming is the most important skill in life.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "my cousin had jumped into a small pond, pretending he could not rise again ... my fear of deep water lasted eight years"  
【解析】原文明确描述童年被表哥吓坏致恐水。A、C、D 均无文中依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "you will be in senior high next year ... I do not want you to be the only one who says 'I can't'"  
【解析】母亲的担忧是升入高中后的社交场景。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "we will only do three things ... We will not leave the shallow side"  
【解析】教练承诺仅做基础三件事并留在浅水区。B、C、D 均与原文相反或无依据。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤、⑥段 "Do not try to swim. Just lie on your back ... water holds you all the time"  
【解析】教练的关键建议。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑨段 "learning to trust something that looks scarier than it is ... 'let go, first'"  
【解析】作者在结尾升华主旨。A、C、D 均与立意不符。故选 B。
