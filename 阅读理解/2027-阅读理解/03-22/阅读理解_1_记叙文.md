# 阅读理解（记叙文） **My First Pottery Class**

① Last Saturday was **cold and grey**, the kind of day when you want only warm soup and a thick blanket. Yet our school art teacher, Ms. Tian, had promised a small surprise: **a pottery class** in the old studio behind the gym. I have never been good at handicraft. My cut-out snowflakes always look like broken leaves, and my painted apples always have three strange corners. Still, I signed my name on the list, because Dabao, my best friend, said, "Come on, Lele, one hour of new fun won't kill you."

② When we pushed open the heavy wooden door, a strange smell came to meet us — not bad, just earthy, like a rainy field in spring. Twelve small tables, twelve small lumps of **grey clay**, and twelve pairs of wide eyes. Ms. Tian, wearing a blue apron with **white finger prints** all over it, said with a smile, "Today, each of you will make **one small bowl** — for yourself, for Mum, or for anyone you like."

③ She showed us how to push the clay with our palms, roll it in long soft ropes, and slowly build a round wall. It looked like magic. I tried. My ropes were too thin. They broke. I tried again. This time they were too thick, and my little wall leaned to one side like a **sleeping tower**. Dabao's bowl already had a neat circle and a tiny flower pattern. I wanted to throw my clay back into the box and go home.

④ Ms. Tian walked over, sat down beside me, and did something I did not expect. She did **not** fix my bowl. Instead, she took a pinch of clay, softly pushed my leaning wall back into shape, and said, "See, not straight, not perfect — but it stands. It just needs you to push, slowly, with patience." Then she added in a quiet voice, "Many good things in life are built like this, Lele. Slowly. With patience."

⑤ I started again. This time, I did not think about Dabao's neat flower, or about being the best. I only listened — the clay made soft squeaky sounds under my fingers, like a small animal breathing. Half an hour later, a bowl finally stood in front of me. It was not round, not smooth. One side was thick, one side was thin. The bottom was a little crooked. But it was **mine** — made from my hands, my minutes, my small fight with the clay.

⑥ On the way home, I held the bowl carefully in a paper box. I thought about the moment Mum would see it on the kitchen table. It could hold salted peanuts, or a spoon of sugar, or just a tiny yellow flower from our balcony. I am still not good at handicraft. But now I know something else — a not-perfect thing, made with heart, can still shine. Sometimes, quiet patience is the most beautiful thing we can bring home.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer finally sign up for the pottery class?**

A. Because he was very good at making handicrafts.  
B. Because his best friend Dabao encouraged him to try.  
C. Because the class was a required part of homework.  
D. Because the weather was sunny and warm.

**2. What was the writer's first feeling inside the pottery studio?**

A. The room smelled very bad and he wanted to leave.  
B. The room was empty and boring.  
C. The room had a strange but interesting earthy smell.  
D. The room was too hot to sit down.

**3. What did the writer's first little wall of clay look like?**

A. It was straight and stronger than Dabao's wall.  
B. It was lopsided like a sleeping tower.  
C. It was beautiful with a flower pattern.  
D. It was as perfect as the teacher's example.

**4. Why didn't Ms. Tian fix the writer's bowl for him?**

A. She was too tired to help.  
B. She wanted the writer to practise patience and push the clay himself.  
C. She thought the bowl was already perfect.  
D. She only helped students with neat work.

**5. What does the writer mainly learn from the pottery class?**

A. Making pottery is the best sport for middle-school students.  
B. A not-perfect thing made with heart and patience can still be beautiful.  
C. Only fast hands can make a good bowl.  
D. Teachers should help students fix every small mistake.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末 "Dabao... 'Come on, Lele, one hour of new fun won't kill you.'"  
【解析】好友 Dabao 鼓励作者报名。A 与事实相反；C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "a strange smell... earthy, like a rainy field in spring"  
【解析】陶艺室有泥土气息，奇妙而有趣。A、B、D 与原文不符。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "my little wall leaned to one side like a sleeping tower"  
【解析】作者的墙倾斜。A、C、D 是别人/理想情形。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段 "not straight, not perfect — but it stands... needs you to push, slowly, with patience"  
【解析】老师希望作者亲身体会耐心。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥段 "a not-perfect thing, made with heart, can still shine... quiet patience"  
【解析】不完美的手作也能闪光。A、C、D 与主旨不符。故选 B。
