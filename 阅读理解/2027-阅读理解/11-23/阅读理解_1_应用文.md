# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Author-Visit 2027 — Poet Xu Yuan-zhen Meets Grade 7–9**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Library + Chinese Department**  
> **Author-Visit 2027**: *"The Small World in a Poem"* — with poet **Xu Yuan-zhen**  
> **Fri 11 December 2027, 14:00 – 17:00, Main Hall**

Once a year, Lihua invites a living Chinese writer to spend half a day with our students. We choose writers whose books we can actually read — not too difficult, not famous for fame's sake, but honest. This year we are lucky to welcome the poet **Xu Yuan-zhen** (born 1969), whose small collection *"Quiet River"* was in our school library's "Most-Borrowed Book for Grade 8" list for three years in a row (2024, 2025, 2026). Xu Yuan-zhen rarely gives talks in schools; this will be her **first author visit in our city**.

## 📚 Before You Come — Read One of These, If You Can

To make the afternoon richer, the library has placed **30 extra copies** of each of the following at the "Author-Visit Shelf" near the staircase (free to borrow, one week):

- *Quiet River* — a small 62-page poetry book (recommended for all).  
- *Letters to My Daughter at Fourteen* — 18 short prose letters (recommended for Grade 9).  
- Two **short poems** will be posted in every classroom on Mon 7 December. Please read them at least once before Friday.

If you have no time to read a whole book, **reading two short poems** is enough. Xu Yuan-zhen herself has said: "One short poem that has truly entered a child is worth more than a whole shelf of big books she has only glanced at."

## 🗣 How to Ask a Good Question in the Q&A

Teachers and senior students have gently talked for years about what makes a good author-visit question. Here is our short guide:

1. **Ask about the poem, not the person.**  
   Good: "What did the word *lantern* in that line mean to you?"  
   Less good: "What is your favourite food?"
2. **Be honest, even if the question is small.**  
   Good: "I did not understand line 3 of the poem 'Rain'. Could you help?"  
3. **One question per student, please.** Let more classmates have a turn.  
4. **No autograph requests during the Q&A** — that belongs to the signing session.  
5. If you are **too shy to speak**, you may **write** your question on one of the pink slips at the door; we will read 6 of them aloud.
