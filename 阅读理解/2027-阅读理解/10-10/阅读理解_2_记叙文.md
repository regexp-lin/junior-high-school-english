> **10-10 第2篇**

# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Pair of Glasses I Accidentally Broke**

① My deskmate, Wei, was new to our class this autumn. He was quiet, smart, and very careful with his things. He had one small treasure: a pair of thin round glasses with silver frames. His grandfather, a retired village teacher, had bought them for him in the summer holiday. Wei had told me once, almost shyly, that the old man saved a whole month of pension money for those frames.

② One Friday afternoon, we were putting chairs on top of desks to clean the floor. Our corner of the classroom was crowded. As I lifted the last chair, my elbow caught something on the desk — Wei's glasses. They slipped off the edge, hit the tile floor with a short, clear *tic*, and bounced. When I picked them up, the right lens had a long silver **crack** running from corner to corner. One side arm was bent.

③ My face burned. Wei was at the classroom door, carrying a bucket of water. He had seen nothing yet. For a tiny second, a quiet, dark voice whispered in my head: **"Put them back. Say you don't know. His seat is on the window side — he might think he knocked them off himself."** Nobody was watching. The voice was very convincing.

④ But another voice, smaller and slower, said: **"Think about his grandfather."** I closed my hand around the glasses and walked straight to Wei.

⑤ "Wei," I said. My throat was dry. "I broke your glasses. I'm so sorry." I opened my hand. His eyes widened. I could see him understand in slow stages: first surprise, then a kind of pain, then — and this was hardest to watch — a careful, grown-up quiet. He took the glasses, looked at them, and put them in his school bag. "Okay, Xiaolu," he said. "Thank you for telling me." That was all.

⑥ That night I told my parents. My mother listened without speaking. My father, usually a very quiet man, said only: "How much?" I didn't know. The next morning, Saturday, my father and I walked to the optical shop near Wei's home. The owner looked at the broken pair, told us a new lens would be 180 yuan, and a new frame 220 — a lot for my family, and much more than my month's pocket money.

⑦ We bought both. My father paid for the frame; I would slowly pay back the 180 yuan for the lens from my weekly pocket money — seven weeks of no ice cream, no comic books, no extra rice balls on Friday. On Saturday afternoon, I took the new glasses to Wei's flat and handed him a small envelope. Inside was a note: "**I will give back the ice-cream money my classmates usually pay for. I won't say you broke them.**" He read it and laughed, for the first time in the whole story.

⑧ On Monday morning, Wei wore his new glasses in class. At lunch, he quietly pushed a small orange towards my tray. "I owe you an orange," he said, smiling. "For telling me."

⑨ Since then I have thought many times about that quiet dark voice in my head on Friday. I had never really believed I was a dishonest person until that moment; I had just never been alone in a room with a broken pair of glasses. What I learned was simple but new: **being honest is not mainly a feeling; it is a small decision made quickly, before the easy lie has time to grow.**

---

**注释：** deskmate 同桌；lens 镜片；frame 镜框；optical shop 眼镜店；pocket money 零花钱

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did Wei get his silver-framed glasses?**

A. He bought them himself.  
B. His grandfather, a retired village teacher, bought them with a whole month of pension money during the summer holiday.  
C. They were a prize from a school competition.  
D. His mother gave them as a birthday gift.

**2. How did the writer break the glasses?**

A. She threw them on purpose.  
B. She sat on them during a test.  
C. While putting a chair on a desk, her elbow caught the glasses and they fell on the tile floor.  
D. They fell out of the school bus window.

**3. What was the "quiet dark voice" in the writer's head (Paragraph 3)?**

A. A real person standing behind her.  
B. A thought telling her to hide the broken glasses and let Wei think he had knocked them off himself.  
C. A message on her phone.  
D. Her teacher calling out her name.

**4. How did the writer finally pay for the broken glasses?**

A. Wei's family paid for everything.  
B. The school bought a new pair for free.  
C. Her father paid for the frame; she slowly paid back the 180 yuan for the lens from her weekly pocket money over seven weeks.  
D. The optical shop gave her the glasses for free.

**5. What lesson does the writer most want readers to take from this story?**

A. Never bring glasses to school.  
B. If nobody sees you, you can always lie.  
C. Being honest is not mainly a feeling — it is a small decision made quickly, before the easy lie has time to grow.  
D. Breaking other people's things is always cheap.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "his grandfather, a retired village teacher, had bought them ... the old man saved a whole month of pension money for those frames."  
【解析】爷爷用一个月养老金为他买眼镜。A、C、D 均不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "As I lifted the last chair, my elbow caught something on the desk — Wei's glasses. They slipped off the edge, hit the tile floor"  
【解析】抬椅时胳膊肘碰到眼镜致其坠地。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第③段 "Put them back. Say you don't know. His seat is on the window side — he might think he knocked them off himself."  
【解析】那是让她掩饰错误的内心念头。A、C、D 均非心理描写。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "My father paid for the frame; I would slowly pay back the 180 yuan for the lens ... seven weeks of no ice cream"  
【解析】父亲付镜框，自己用零花钱还镜片钱。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨/写作意图题  
【定位】第⑨段 "being honest is not mainly a feeling; it is a small decision made quickly, before the easy lie has time to grow."  
【解析】作者直接点明主旨。A、B、D 与原文立场相反或荒谬。故选 C。
