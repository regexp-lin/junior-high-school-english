# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Winter Library Read-Aloud Nights 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School Library · Chinese + English + PSHE Departments**  
> **Winter Library Read-Aloud Nights**: *"Voices in a Warm Room"*  
> **Tuesday 15 December – Thursday 17 December 2027, 18:30 – 19:45**

Once a year, for three winter evenings, our library stays open after dinner and turns into a **room of voices**. Students, teachers, and parents who feel like it gather on the wooden floor, dim the lights, and read aloud — short poems, small story pages, favourite paragraphs — to each other. There is no prize, no judge, and no "best reader" voted at the end. The whole idea is simple: to hear how **a loved passage sounds in our own throat**, and to remember that reading, long before it was silent, was first a shared sound.

---

## 📚 The Three Nights — What Each Night Is For

### 🌙 Tue 15 Dec — **Chinese Night**

- Students bring one short Chinese passage (poem, essay, short story paragraph) they love.  
- **Each reader: ≤ 2 minutes.**  
- Grade 9 reads last; between readers, **twenty seconds of silence**, no clapping (we clap only once, together, at the very end).

### 🌍 Wed 16 Dec — **English Night**

- Any reader may bring one short English passage — poem, prose, or dialogue.  
- Teachers may read too; this is not a student-only night.  
- **Each reader: ≤ 2 minutes.**  
- A small rule: read at **half speed** of normal speech, to help every listener follow.

### ❤️ Thu 17 Dec — **"Letter" Night**

- The warmest night of the three. Readers bring **one letter**: a real letter from a grandparent, a short note from a younger sibling, a postcard from an old friend — or a letter written by a famous person, found in a book (e.g. part of a letter by Lu Xun to his son).  
- **Each reader: ≤ 3 minutes.**  
- Before you begin, say one sentence: *"This letter is from ______, written around ______."*  
- No personal information (address, phone number) is read aloud.

---

## 🕯 How the Library Is Arranged

- Lights are dimmed to the small **"reading-corner soft light."**  
- A low wooden stage is placed in front of the south window.  
- The **first three rows** are cushions on the floor (Grade 7–9, about 60 seats); behind them, small chairs (about 40 seats).  
- A small warm-water table stands at the back — help yourselves quietly.  
- The sign at the door reads: **"Phones off. Voices gentle. Ears open."**

---

## 📝 How to Sign Up to Read

- On the school mini-app → Library → Read-Aloud Nights. Opens **Mon 08 December 07:30**; closes **Fri 12 December 17:30**.  
- Each student may read **at most once in the three nights**, so that 60 different readers can take the stage.  
- When you sign up, you also upload a **one-line description** of your passage (e.g. *"Lu Xun, 'A Small Incident'"*) so that teachers can spot overlapping choices in advance.  
- If more than 75 students apply, places are **drawn by lot**. No grade points or test marks are used.

---

## 🎒 What to Bring When You Read

- Your **printed passage** or a handwritten copy — **no reading from phones**, to keep the room screen-free.  
- A **bookmark** in case you lose your line.  
- A **small glass of water** that stays on the floor near your foot, not on the stage.  
- **Comfortable soft shoes.**

---

## 🤫 Four Small Rules of Respect

1. **Be on time.** The door closes at 18:35 for the first reader. Late arrivals wait at the back until the first short break.  
2. **No photos during reading.** A single photo of the Letter Night could turn a safe room into a less safe one. The door volunteers will politely remind anyone.  
3. **No "best-voice" comments** — not during, not after. ("That was beautiful" is fine; "You are the best reader tonight" is not.)  
4. **One warm sentence only** when someone seems nervous. "Take your time, we are here." Nothing else needed.

---

## 🍵 The Small Warm Moments

Between the readers, two small moments return each night:

- At **19:00**, a soft bell rings; everyone closes their eyes for **one minute** and listens to the heating pipe in the wall. (Yes, really; it is a small, honest kind of music.)  
- At **19:35**, one teacher reads a **short piece chosen by the previous night's students** (e.g. one sentence from Tuesday's Chinese night is read in English on Wednesday). The three nights, in this way, quietly hold hands.

---

## 🙋 Volunteer Roles (24 students)

- **6 door volunteers** per night (18:10–18:35).  
- **4 cushion-tidiers** after each night (19:45–20:00).  
- **6 silent timekeepers** (tap a small paper card at the 2-minute mark).  
- **4 warm-water table caretakers**.  
- **4 closing "phone off" monitors** — friendly reminders, never a finger-point.

Sign-up: outside Room 208 or on the school mini-app. **Deadline Wed 10 December, 17:30.**

That small, fair sharing is, in its own quiet way, what literature is for."
