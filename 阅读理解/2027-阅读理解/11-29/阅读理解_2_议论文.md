# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Every Middle-School Student Spend One Full Day a Year in a Real Workplace?**

① On the bulletin board in front of our staff room, a small green notice appeared last Thursday: *"Career Day 2028 — Proposal Meeting, Friday 16:20, Room 202."* The idea is new for us. A nearby city has, since 2023, given every ninth-grade student **one full school day each year** to spend inside a real workplace — a hospital ward, a small café, a fire station, a city library, a printing house, a beekeeper's farm. Students do not work; they **watch, follow, and quietly help** for six to seven hours. Some of our parents are worried; some teachers are cautious; students are mostly excited. After a long discussion on Friday afternoon, I want to argue clearly: **our school should, in 2028, offer one full workplace day a year to every Grade 7, 8, and 9 student — with careful design and safety first**.

② The first argument is that **our idea of work, at age thirteen or fifteen, is often blurry**. A 2025 city poll asked 1,400 Grade 8 students to name "what adults actually do at three common jobs" (nurse, accountant, engineer). More than **55%** could describe only one of the three in specific detail. Most said, honestly, "I see my mum leave for the office, but I do not know exactly what she does there." If our school wants our subject choices in senior high to be more than guesses, we need more than a careers slideshow. We need **one honest day** inside a real workplace. That day is worth twenty slideshows.

③ The second argument is that **a workplace teaches respect in a way no classroom can**. A classmate who spent a voluntary morning in our community library last summer came back with a quiet sentence I will not forget: *"Books arrive, are scanned, are carried, are shelved; my single request of 'Can you help me find this book?' set off about six small steps in the building. I will wait more politely next time."* The workplace, better than any PSHE lesson, teaches how **many invisible hands support one visible moment**. A hospital cleaner, a train driver, a school canteen cook, an electricity-meter checker — a full day near any of them rebuilds a student's map of the city.

④ The third argument is that **it is equal**. Many students, during long holidays, already follow a parent or relative to a workplace — but **only the students whose parents have "showable" jobs do**. A school-led workplace day **levels this**. Every student, regardless of family background, gets the same kind of careful, planned half-day with an adult who is ready to host her. A plan like this is, in one small step, **a kind of practical education equality** that schools rarely have the chance to give.

⑤ Others argue, "One day will not teach anything real." This is partly true and partly wrong. **One day** cannot teach a profession. But one day is surprisingly good at teaching **a few small, honest facts**: what a workplace smells like, how quiet or loud it is, how late adults stay, how many forms and small talks fill a normal afternoon, who says hello to whom. These small facts, collected in only one day and written down carefully, form the foundation on which a student can later decide, in Grade 10 or 11, whether this kind of work is **close to her kind of person**.

⑥ The most serious worry is **safety**. I take this worry very seriously. Our plan must include: (1) **no heavy-machine environments** for Grade 7–9 students; (2) no "one student, one adult" arrangements; always **pairs of students** with at least two adults present; (3) a **short safety session** the week before; (4) a **written plan** sent home to parents for signature; (5) two teachers available by phone on the day. If these five rules cannot be met at a particular workplace, that place **does not join** the programme. Safety is not a soft extra; it is a clear gate.

⑦ Another worry: "Workplaces will feel disturbed; it becomes a burden on busy adults." Our proposed answer is modest: **each workplace hosts at most four students, once a year, for six hours only**. The school's staff prepare everything in advance — clean uniforms, a simple observation notebook, a list of short questions students may ask, and two small "quiet corners" so that the host adult is not accompanied by a student every single minute. Many adults who have been asked by similar programmes in other cities have, in feedback forms, said surprisingly warm things: **"I was pleasantly surprised how little it disturbed us, and how much quiet care the two Grade 9 girls showed while watching us work."**

⑧ There is also a small **return on investment** for the school itself. Students who spend one planned day a year in a workplace report (in 2024 Shanghai data) **stronger motivation** in subjects they had previously thought "useless" — for example, English lessons after a day in a small international hotel; biology and chemistry after a day in a pharmacy; maths after a day in a grocery store's back office. A single day can turn a bored student into a slightly curious one for the next three months. That, by itself, is worth the quiet organisational work.

⑨ On Tuesday, the class representatives will attend the proposal meeting with a one-page summary. We are not asking for a year of internships. **We are asking for one careful day, one year, one workplace; safe, equal, well-planned, kind.** In a school system that spends 90 minutes a week on careers slideshows, one honest day in a real place is not a distraction from our studies — it is one of the few moments in which a textbook briefly **puts on a coat, walks out of the building, and stands next to an adult at her desk**. Let our school be the second city to do this. On the Monday after Career Day 2028, I suspect, every class meeting will begin with sentences beginning, *"I saw..."* — and that small phrase is the beginning of every real education.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main proposal?**

A. Every student should quit school and start work.  
B. In 2028 our school should offer one full workplace day a year to every Grade 7, 8, and 9 student — with careful design and safety first.  
C. Only Grade 9 students should work in factories.  
D. Students should open their own shops.

**2. What did a 2025 city poll of Grade 8 students find?**

A. 90% of students could describe a nurse's job in detail.  
B. More than 55% could describe "what adults actually do" in only one of three common jobs (nurse, accountant, engineer); most said they see their parents leave for the office but do not know exactly what they do — showing that a careers slideshow alone is not enough.  
C. All students had already visited three workplaces.  
D. Students had no interest in any job.

**3. How does the writer answer the worry "One day will not teach anything real"?**

A. By agreeing and cancelling the plan.  
B. By saying one day cannot teach a profession, but it is surprisingly good at teaching small, honest facts — what a workplace smells like, how quiet or loud it is, how late adults stay, how many forms and small talks fill a normal afternoon, who says hello to whom; these small facts form a foundation for later decisions.  
C. By saying work takes years.  
D. By saying the worry is stupid.

**4. Which is NOT one of the five safety rules proposed?**

A. No heavy-machine environments for Grade 7–9 students.  
B. Students work alone with one adult, not in pairs.  
C. A short safety session the week before.  
D. A written plan sent home to parents for signature.

**5. What does the writer mean by "one honest day in a real place" being a moment when "a textbook briefly puts on a coat, walks out of the building, and stands next to an adult at her desk"?**

A. Textbooks should be taken outside every day.  
B. The workplace day is a moment in which classroom knowledge briefly becomes alive and adult — stepping out of the building to stand next to a real grown-up at a real desk — which is when real education quietly starts; the Monday after Career Day will be full of sentences beginning "I saw..."

D. Teachers should be replaced by textbooks.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末立论  
【解析】"一年一天、安全 + 公平 + 计划 + 善意"直引。A、C、D 均偏。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 2025 年调查  
【解析】55% 数据直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段"小而诚实的事实"  
【解析】气味、节奏、小对话直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题（否定）  
【定位】第⑥段五条安全规则  
【解析】第二条规定"必须学生成对 + 至少两位成人在场"；B 相反。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑨段结尾比喻  
【解析】"课本穿衣出门"主旨。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。
