# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Every Middle-School Student Take Part in at Least One Team Sport?**

① Every September, when the new school year begins, a familiar debate returns to our classroom: should **every** middle-school student be required to join at least one team sport — football, basketball, volleyball, relay running, or something similar — for a whole term? Some of my classmates nod straight away; others roll their eyes and say, "I am bad at sports. Please, not me." I believe the answer should be **yes**, but only if schools are wise about **how** they ask us to take part.

② Those who speak against the idea often raise three worries. **First**, they say sports waste study time. Middle-school students already have heavy homework, and a team training of even one hour a week could be used for maths or English. **Second**, some students are simply **not good** at sports. Forcing a shy, short or weaker child to play in front of the whole class, they argue, may **hurt that child's confidence** more than help it. **Third**, there is a real **safety worry** — broken fingers, sprained knees, and small accidents do happen. These are serious points and deserve an honest answer.

③ However, the benefits of team sports reach far beyond scoring goals. **Doctors have said for years** that regular exercise helps growing teenagers **sleep better, see better, and feel less anxious**. In a survey of my own class last year, students who played in the school football club for one term said they could fall asleep "**within ten minutes**" on school nights, while some non-sporty classmates often stayed awake for more than an hour. Better sleep is also better study, whatever subject one takes.

④ Team sports also teach a kind of lesson that maths and English books cannot easily teach. On a pitch, you learn to **pass the ball** instead of keeping it to yourself. You learn to **trust** a slower teammate because she may save the game next time. You learn to **say sorry** when you kick the ball away and to **say well done** when someone else scores. These quiet social skills are exactly what a classroom full of tests rarely measures, yet they are what every workplace and every family will one day need.

⑤ As for the worry about being "**not good at sports**", I think the problem is not the sport itself but the way sport is sometimes taught. If teachers only praise the fastest runner or the best shooter, weaker students do feel small. But if teachers also notice the student who **never gives up**, the one who always **passes the ball first**, or the one who **helps clean the gym** after training, then every child has a way to shine. A well-designed team sport is not a show for the strongest; it is a classroom for **every kind of growth**.

⑥ The concern about study time is also too simple. Research from several Chinese universities has shown that **30–60 minutes of sport two or three times a week** does **not** lower marks; in many schools, it slightly **raises** them, because students return to their desks more awake. Two hours of "extra homework" while tired and sleepy may bring less than one hour of focused work after sport.

⑦ The safety worry is the easiest to answer: safer rules. Schools can use **soft floors**, **well-fitting shoes**, **a warm-up before each training**, and **a clear list** of which actions are safe. With care, small bumps will still happen, but serious injuries can be kept very low. We cannot refuse to cross the street because cars sometimes run red lights; we can, however, **learn to cross it safely**.

⑧ Of course, schools should be thoughtful. Students with **real health problems** — heart conditions, asthma attacks, recent operations — should be offered **alternative** team activities, such as chess teams, reading clubs, or school orchestra ensembles, that still train teamwork without physical risk. The goal is not to force every child onto the football pitch; the goal is to make sure **no child spends three middle-school years alone at a desk**.

⑨ In a world where children talk less face-to-face and scroll through phones more, the **shared breath** on a pitch is almost medicine. Asking every middle-school student to join at least one team activity for one term is not cruel; it is, I believe, **one of the kindest things** a school can do. We learn mathematics so that we will not be cheated. We learn team sport so that we will not be lonely.

**注释：** relay 接力；sprained 扭伤的；anxious 焦虑的；alternative 替代的；ensemble 乐团

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion about every middle-school student joining at least one team sport?**

A. Strongly against it in every case.  
B. Yes, if schools organise it wisely.  
C. Only Grade 9 students should take part.  
D. Only the best athletes should be involved.

**2. Which of the following is **NOT** one of the worries mentioned by people who are against the idea?**

A. Team sports may waste study time.  
B. Students who are not good at sports may lose confidence.  
C. Small sports injuries may happen.  
D. Team sports are too expensive for every school.

**3. According to the writer's class survey, what did students who played football for one term say about sleep?**

A. They usually stayed awake for more than an hour on school nights.  
B. They could fall asleep within about ten minutes on school nights.  
C. They could not sleep at all before matches.  
D. They needed sleeping medicine every night.

**4. What does the writer suggest schools should do for students with real health problems?**

A. Force them to run every morning.  
B. Let them stay home from school.  
C. Offer alternative team activities, such as chess or reading clubs.  
D. Ask them to watch others play without any other activity.

**5. Which sentence best expresses the writer's final view?**

A. "Forcing every child onto a football pitch is the duty of every school."  
B. "Team sports do not help study at all."  
C. "We learn mathematics so that we will not be cheated. We learn team sport so that we will not be lonely."  
D. "Schools should only care about test scores, not about friends."

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段 "I believe the answer should be yes, but only if schools are wise about how they ask us to take part"  
【解析】作者支持，但以学校妥善组织为前提。A、C、D 均与原文相悖或无据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 3 条反对理由依次为：耽误学习、伤自信心、安全隐患  
【解析】文中未提"花费太高"。A、B、C 均对应原文担忧。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "students who played in the school football club ... could fall asleep 'within ten minutes'"  
【解析】踢球的学生十分钟内可入睡。A 为对照组情况；C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑧段 "offered alternative team activities, such as chess teams, reading clubs, or school orchestra ensembles"  
【解析】给予健康有特殊情况者替代性团体活动。A、B、D 均与原文相悖。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨/写作意图题  
【定位】第⑨段结尾 "We learn mathematics so that we will not be cheated. We learn team sport so that we will not be lonely."  
【解析】该句揭示团体运动的真正价值——避免孤独，与文章总论点一致。A、B、D 均与作者立场不符。故选 C。
