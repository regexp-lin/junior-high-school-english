# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle-School Students Learn to Cook?**

In many cities, cooking is slowly disappearing from family life. Delivery apps bring hot meals to our doors in twenty minutes; pre-cooked dishes can be heated in a microwave in three minutes. Some parents even say proudly, "My child has never touched a knife — I want him to focus only on study." But this year, several cities have added one hour of **cooking class** to middle-school schedules, and the topic is being discussed everywhere. In my view, **cooking should be part of every middle-school student's education, and the earlier we learn, the better**.

The first reason is **health**. Teenagers today eat more fast food than any generation before them. According to a 2026 health report of our district, **34%** of Grade 8 students eat three or more delivery meals per week, and nearly one in five are **overweight**. Delivery food tends to be high in salt, oil, and sugar. When students learn to cook simple dishes, they start to understand *what* goes into their bodies. A boy who has chopped tomatoes and beaten eggs five times is less likely to believe that a greasy noodle cup is a "normal" meal. Knowledge changes choice.

The second reason is **independence**. A middle-school student stays at home during the summer holiday for weeks at a time. If his parents are busy at work, he will either stay hungry or rely on junk food. Knowing how to make a bowl of porridge, a plate of stir-fried vegetables, or a simple tomato-and-egg soup can literally keep a young person fed, calm and safe. Such small abilities grow into a bigger feeling: *I can take care of myself*.

People who disagree often say: "Cooking is dangerous. Knives can cut. Oil can burn." Yes, kitchens have small risks. But so do school stairs, PE classes, and science labs. We do not close labs to avoid risk — instead, we teach safety. In the same way, cooking class should begin with rules: how to hold a knife, how to turn off the gas, how to use oven **mitts**, and how to wash hands before touching food. When students practise under a teacher, kitchens become no more dangerous than crossroads where we are taught to look left and right.

A third reason is often forgotten: cooking **teaches patience and gratitude**. A pot of rice does not boil just because we stare at it. Dumplings do not fold themselves. When teenagers help cook Sunday dinner, they see how much time and care go into each plate. Next time Mum or Grandma places food on the table, the sight tastes slightly different. A quiet "thank you" comes more naturally.

In short, cooking is not an old-fashioned job to be pushed aside for "more important" subjects. It is a set of skills that touch our bodies, our minds, and our hearts. If a middle-school student can solve an equation, **translate** a poem, and write a code for a tiny robot, she should also be able to fry an egg. In fact, after a long day of equations and poems, an egg she cooked herself may taste like the best reward of all.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Cooking classes waste students' study time.  
B. Cooking should be part of middle-school education; the earlier the better.  
C. Only girls should learn to cook.  
D. Students should eat only in restaurants.

**2. What did the 2026 health report show?**

A. 3% of Grade 8 students eat three or more delivery meals a week.  
B. 34% of Grade 8 students eat three or more delivery meals a week, and nearly one in five are overweight.  
C. All Grade 8 students are too thin.  
D. Delivery food is always healthy.

**3. Why does the writer compare cooking class with science lab?**

A. To show both are useless.  
B. To show that risks exist in many places, but safety rules solve them.  
C. To show cooking is harder than science.  
D. To show science is more delicious.

**4. According to the writer, what does cooking help students feel?**

A. Fear of knives.  
B. A feeling of "I can take care of myself".  
C. Jealousy of other families.  
D. A wish to quit school.

**5. What does the last paragraph mainly tell us?**

A. Cooking is old-fashioned and should be ignored.  
B. Cooking is an extra skill that touches body, mind and heart.  
C. All students must become chefs.  
D. Eggs are the most important food.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】首段尾句  
【解析】应纳入中学教育，越早越好。A/C/D 均偏离。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"34% of Grade 8 students..."  
【解析】34%+近五分之一超重。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】"We do not close labs... we teach safety"  
【解析】风险普遍存在，安全规则可解决。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"I can take care of myself"  
【解析】自我照顾的信心。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】末段 "touch body, mind, heart"  
【解析】是多方面有益的技能。A/C/D 均偏离。故选 B。
