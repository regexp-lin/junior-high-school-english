# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Birthday Gift I Almost Threw Away**

① On my twelfth birthday, I was expecting something exciting. My best friend had a new tablet; some classmates even had small wireless earphones. When my mother handed me a **small brown paper bag** after dinner, my heart sank a little. The bag was soft, and when I touched it, I could feel something thin and rough inside, like a folded cloth. "From Grandpa," she said quietly. "He sent it last week."

② I opened the paper bag on the sofa. Inside was an **old notebook** with a dark-blue cloth cover. The corners were bent, the cover was a little dirty, and the pages smelled faintly of dust. Stuck on the front, with clear tape, was a small yellow paper: "**For my Xiaolin, from Grandpa. Write down what the world shows you.**" I tried to smile, but I felt a small pain in my chest. A used notebook? Was that all?

③ I hid the notebook in the lowest drawer of my desk, under old exam papers and a pair of broken headphones. I did not tell Grandpa I had received it. Whenever Mum mentioned a phone call, I always said I was busy with homework. Weeks passed, and the brown paper bag grew a soft layer of dust in the corner.

④ Then, one rainy Saturday afternoon in March, the lights went out in our building. Mum lit two small candles and put one on the desk in my room. The Wi-Fi was dead, my tablet was almost out of battery, and I had nothing to do. Out of pure boredom, I pulled open the bottom drawer.

⑤ The brown bag was still there. I took out the old notebook and opened it under the candle. To my surprise, the first pages were **not empty**. They were full of Grandpa's careful, small handwriting. He had written dates from when he was about my age — 1972, 1973. "**March 4, 1972.** My maths teacher said I was slow, so I cried by the river behind the school." "**April 11, 1973.** Today I learnt the name of five new birds. I did not know a country boy could have so many friends in one tree." I turned another page. "**May 20, 1974.** I have decided: when my grandchild is twelve, I will give this notebook. I hope he will write, too."

⑥ I sat very still. The candle shook a little in the cold air. Suddenly the "**old, used notebook**" was not old at all; it was a small, silent bridge between a boy in 1972 and a boy in 2027. I picked up my pencil, turned to the first blank page, and wrote the date of that day. I did not know what to say. In the end, I wrote only one simple line: "**Grandpa, I am twelve today. The power just went out. I think I finally see you.**"

⑦ The next morning, I asked Mum for Grandpa's phone number. We talked for almost an hour. He did not say anything special about the notebook. He only asked about my school, my friends, and whether I still liked to look at the sky. But at the very end he said, in a slow, warm voice: "**Write down the small things, Xiaolin. When you are my age, they will be the big ones.**"

⑧ That evening, I cleaned the cover of the notebook with a soft cloth, put it on the top of my desk, and placed my pencil beside it. For my birthday I had wanted a tablet, a pair of earphones, or a new game. Instead, I got something I could never have bought: a quiet pair of eyes from fifty years ago, looking gently at me, and inviting me to look back.

⑨ Grown-ups often say the best gifts are not always the shiny ones. I used to think it was just something polite they told children who did not get what they wanted. Now, whenever I open my blue notebook, I understand what they really mean.

---

**注释：** faintly 微弱地；out of pure boredom 出于无聊；silent bridge 无声的桥梁

---

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What gift did the writer receive for his twelfth birthday?**

A. A new tablet.  
B. A pair of wireless earphones.  
C. An old notebook with a dark-blue cloth cover.  
D. A new pair of headphones.

**2. How did the writer feel when he first received the gift on his birthday?**

A. Very excited and grateful.  
B. A little disappointed and hurt.  
C. Afraid of his grandfather.  
D. Angry with his mother.

**3. What happened on the rainy Saturday afternoon in March?**

A. The writer bought a new tablet.  
B. The power went out in the writer's building.  
C. The writer's grandfather came to visit him.  
D. The writer's friend gave him new earphones.

**4. According to the diary pages, what did Grandpa do in 1974?**

A. He threw the old notebook away.  
B. He wrote that he would give this notebook to his grandchild when he or she was twelve.  
C. He asked his teacher for a new notebook.  
D. He stopped writing in the notebook.

**5. What does the writer most likely want to tell us through this story?**

A. Old people should not give old things to young people.  
B. Children should only ask for shiny, expensive gifts.  
C. The most meaningful gifts may not look exciting at first, but they carry love and understanding across time.  
D. Candles are more useful than tablets.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Inside was an old notebook with a dark-blue cloth cover"  
【解析】礼物即那本旧笔记本。A、B、D 均为"我"期待或他人拥有之物。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】情感态度题  
【定位】第①段 "my heart sank a little"；第②段 "I felt a small pain in my chest. A used notebook? Was that all?"  
【解析】初收礼物时略感失望与受伤。A 与原文相反；C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "one rainy Saturday afternoon in March, the lights went out in our building"  
【解析】当天楼里停电。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "May 20, 1974. I have decided: when my grandchild is twelve, I will give this notebook"  
【解析】1974 年的日记写明这一决定。A、C、D 均与原文矛盾或无据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧—⑨段 "something I could never have bought" "the best gifts are not always the shiny ones"  
【解析】文章主题即最有意义的礼物往往承载跨越时光的爱与理解。A、B、D 均为片面或错误解读。故选 C。
