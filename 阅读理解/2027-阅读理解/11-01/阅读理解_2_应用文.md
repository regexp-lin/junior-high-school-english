# 阅读理解（应用文） **Young Guides Wanted: School History Room Open Week**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · History & Civics Department + Alumni Office**  
> **2027 School History Room — Open Week for Grade 7 Families**  
> **"Who walked these corridors before us?"**

Our School History Room, on the third floor of the old red building, keeps more than **five hundred objects** from Lihua Middle School's seventy-two years: an old blackboard dusted with real chalk from 1961, a set of pencils used by the first female headteacher, a hand-sewn school flag from 1979, a glass jar of autumn leaves picked by the Class of 1988, and many more. From **Monday 23 November to Saturday 28 November 2027**, we will open the History Room for an **Open Week** and welcome Grade 7 students and their parents inside. Because the room is small and quiet, we need **young student guides**.

---

## 📜 Why We Need Young Guides

An alumni from the Class of 1975 once told us, "*A museum without a young voice is just a cold shelf.*" We agree. A ten-minute tour by a **Grade 8 or Grade 9 student** — someone who has grown up in these same corridors — will make every small object feel close and warm. This year, we would like **16 guides** to work in short shifts across the week.

---

## 🗓 Dates and Shifts

| Day | Opening Hours | Shifts | Language |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon 23 Nov | 16:00 – 17:30 | Two 45-minute tours | Chinese |
| Tue 24 Nov | 16:00 – 17:30 | Two 45-minute tours | Chinese |
| Wed 25 Nov | 16:00 – 17:30 | Two 45-minute tours | Chinese + a **short English section** on the old school badge |
| Thu 26 Nov | 12:45 – 13:30 | One 30-minute tour | Chinese |
| Fri 27 Nov | 16:00 – 17:30 | Two 45-minute tours | Chinese |
| Sat 28 Nov | 09:00 – 11:30 | Three 40-minute tours | **All in English** (guided by the English Club) |

Each guide will work **one or two shifts** in the week. Tea and warm water will be ready behind the third counter; warm coats can be hung on the tall wooden tree by the door.

---

## ✅ Who Can Apply

- Any Grade 8 or Grade 9 student who:
  - Feels calm in front of a small group (about 8 people);  
  - Can keep her voice **gentle, clear, and not too fast**;  
  - Has average or better scores in Chinese; Saturday English-only guides need a teacher's recommendation.
- Students who have a family member who once studied at Lihua are especially welcome — **tell us in the application**, as we may invite you to present the object connected with your family.

---

## 📝 How to Apply

1. Write a short letter (150–200 Chinese characters), telling us:  
   - Which shift you prefer;  
   - Which **one object** from the room you would most like to explain, and why;  
   - One short question you would like to ask the visitors at the end of your tour.  
2. Drop the letter in the **red wooden box** outside the History & Civics teachers' office, **Room 307**, by **Sunday 9 November, 18:00**.  
3. Short practice sessions: **Wed 12 Nov and Sat 15 Nov, 15:00 – 16:30**, in the History Room.

---

## 🎒 What Guides Will Learn

- How to **stand** (feet shoulder-wide, weight on the heel, hands relaxed).  
- How to **lead a group of eight** quietly through narrow spaces.  
- How to **invite questions** from shy visitors without putting them on the spot.  
- How to **close a tour** with one short, warm sentence — not a long speech.  
- Saturday guides will also practise **five simple English phrases** about the objects, such as *"This badge was sewn in 1955 by a retired Chinese teacher called Ms. Wang."*

---

## 🚫 A Few Quiet Rules

- No touching objects with bare hands. White cotton gloves are available by the door.  
- No phones in the room for the visitors (please politely remind them at the start).  
- Guides may use a small printed card, but **no reading long scripts aloud**; we want a real, young voice, not a speaker on a phone.  
- Please arrive **ten minutes early** for each shift. The room, by rule, is kept at exactly 18 °C and 55 % humidity.

---

## 🎁 A Small Thank-You

- Every guide receives a **handwritten thank-you letter** from our Alumni Office and a **one-year free pass** to the school library's alumni section.  
- At the end of the week, all guides are invited to a quiet Saturday-evening tea with two alumni from the Class of 1968 and the Class of 2003, who will share short memories of Lihua and answer our questions.

> "When you stand beside an old object and speak kindly about it, you are not teaching history. You are **borrowing** a quiet minute from the past and giving it to someone who was born long after. Our school is seventy-two years old. We can never hold all of her memories. But we can **pass** a few of them — carefully, warmly — from one young voice to the next."  
> — *Alumni Office, 1 November 2027*

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】Why We Need Young Guides 段 "16 guides to work in short shifts"  
【解析】招募目的明确。A、C、D 均偏。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】Dates and Shifts 表 "Sat 28 Nov ... All in English"  
【解析】周六英语专场。A、B、C 均错。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题（否定）  
【定位】How to Apply 第 1 条三项  
【解析】手机/家址未要求。A、B、D 均在必写项。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】A Few Quiet Rules 两条 "arrive ten minutes early" + "white cotton gloves"  
【解析】双项并列。A、C、D 均错或禁止。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】结尾引言 "borrowing a quiet minute from the past ... pass ... from one young voice to the next"  
【解析】世代传递的主旨凝练。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。
