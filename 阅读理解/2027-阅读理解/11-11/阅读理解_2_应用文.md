# 阅读理解（应用文） **The Lost-and-Found Wall — New Rules and Helpers Wanted**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Student Union + General Affairs Office**  
> **Re-opening of the Lost-and-Found Wall (outside Room 112)**  
> **"A bag, a book, a warm glove, a tiny friend — let them go home."**

Last term our Lost-and-Found Wall quietly filled up with **114 unclaimed items** by June — from pencil cases and keys to a single lonely running shoe. Many items were eventually thrown away because no one knew they were there. From **Monday 17 November 2027**, the Wall will re-open in a **new and warmer form**, outside Room 112. Here is everything you need to know about it.

---

## 📌 The Wall in Three Lines

1. **Picture wall** — every found item gets a numbered photo card, so you can check the wall without touching the items.  
2. **Small shelves** — items sit safely in labelled boxes behind the Picture wall; only the helpers can open them.  
3. **Thank-you pocket** — when you take back your item, please drop a handwritten card into the pocket. We read them aloud at the end of each term.

---

## 🧭 What Goes on the Wall, What Does Not

✅ We keep on the wall:

- **Daily school things**: pencil cases, water bottles, umbrellas, gloves, scarves, key rings, house keys, hair bands, glasses, glasses cases.  
- **Books and notebooks** clearly belonging to a student.  
- **Small decorations** (bookmarks, small badges, handmade cards).  
- **Sports gear** (ropes, wrist bands, small balls).  
- **Low-value electronics** (USB sticks, basic earphones) — **mobile phones and cash** are sent directly to the office, not displayed.

🚫 We do NOT keep on the wall:

- **Food, drinks, medicine**.  
- **Live animals / insects** of any kind.  
- **Mobile phones, cash, bank cards, ID cards** (see § About Phones & Cash below).  
- **Sharp or dangerous objects** — sent straight to the lab safety room.

---

## 🔎 How to Look For Your Item

1. Walk to the Picture wall and read the numbered photo cards. Items are grouped by **colour first**, then by **type**.  
2. If you see a match, fill in the simple sign-out slip: **item number, your name, class, and one small detail about the item** (e.g. "the inside has my English name written in pencil").  
3. Give the slip to a helper at the little desk next to the wall.  
4. The helper opens the correct shelf, checks your detail, and returns the item — usually in under two minutes.

**No detail = no return.** This protects each student's property from mistaken claims.

---

## 📱 About Phones, Cash, ID Cards

Because these are valuable and private, we handle them differently:

- They are **never** put on the Picture wall.  
- They are sent the same hour to the **General Affairs Office** (Room 110).  
- To reclaim, please bring a short note signed by your **form teacher**, which confirms the item is likely yours.  
- Unclaimed phones will be kept for **30 days**; then the phone will be **cleared of personal data** and given to the school's digital reuse project.

## 🙋 Helpers Wanted (24 students)

We need a team of **24 student helpers** for this term — 8 from each grade. Every helper takes **one 20-minute slot** per week. Tasks include:

- Taking a clear photo of each new item, writing a short description card.  
- Running the little desk at the Picture wall during open hours.  
- Carrying unclaimed items between the shelves and the Big-Sweep box.  
- Reading thank-you cards aloud at the end-of-term assembly.

**What you learn:** clear writing, polite speaking, basic photo editing, and a quiet sense of service.

Sign-up form: outside Room 112, or on the school mini-app → Services → Wall.  
**Deadline: Friday 14 November, 17:30.**

---

## 💛 Two Gentle Reminders

- **A kind finder is half a home.** If you pick up something that is not yours, bring it to the wall the same day; even a single glove can make a cold morning different for someone.  
- **A kind owner is the other half.** When you take your item back, a small "thank you" card is a kindness to the whole team — and to the classmate who found it for you.

> "A wall is not a list of lost things. A wall is a list of **small, lucky reunions** — one every few minutes, all week long, for a whole school year."  
> — *Student Union, 11 November 2027*
