# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Old Book I Borrowed by Mistake**

① Last October, our school library was moved into a new building, and for two weeks all the books were packed into large grey boxes. On a rainy Wednesday, I went to the old reading room to look for a dictionary. The shelves were almost empty, and I was about to leave when I noticed a thin brown book lying alone under the counter. It had no cover label and no barcode. Thinking it was one of our library's old books, I wrote my name on a small slip of paper, pushed it under the counter as a "borrow record", and slipped the book into my bag. I told no one, not even Mrs Guo, the librarian, because she was busy with the move.

② That evening I opened the book at home. Inside, in careful old-fashioned handwriting, were short notes about birds — a common sparrow, a kingfisher, a magpie — each with a date, a place, and a single pencil drawing. On the last page, I found a sentence in English that stopped me: "*For my granddaughter Ying, who loves blue birds. — Grandpa Song, March 1998.*" This was not a library book. It was a private notebook, almost thirty years old. My cheeks went hot.

③ The next morning I tried to return the notebook, but the old reading room was already locked — the move was finished. At the new library, I showed Mrs Guo the book. She frowned, turned a few pages, and shook her head. "This is not ours," she said quietly. "But I do remember an old lady who borrowed bird books here a long time ago. Her name was Ying. She used to come every Friday afternoon."

④ "Does she still live nearby?" I asked, my heart beating fast. Mrs Guo thought for a moment. "I am not sure. But she always waited at the bus stop near the small noodle shop on Peach Road. Maybe the shop owners still know her."

⑤ That Saturday, I took the bus to Peach Road. The noodle shop was easy to find — a warm yellow window, steam rising from the kitchen. I showed the notebook to the owner, a short kind woman with flour on her hands. She looked at the name "Ying" and smiled gently. "Auntie Ying moved to the south of the city last winter," she said. "But she left me her new address — she said my noodles were too good to lose." She wrote the address on a small paper napkin.

⑥ The address was forty minutes away by bus. When the door opened, a small grey-haired lady in a soft blue sweater stood there, her eyes quickly going to the book in my hand. Before I could speak, her mouth opened, then closed, and a tear rolled quietly down her face. "Grandpa Song's notebook," she said softly. "I thought I had lost it forever when we moved three years ago."

⑦ She invited me in and poured me a cup of warm tea. For an hour, she turned the pages slowly and told me stories behind each bird: the sparrow that lived under her grandfather's window; the kingfisher she saw on her wedding day; the magpie Grandpa Song had drawn the week before he died. When I stood up to leave, she pressed my hand. "Thank you for the long journey, young man. Most people would have kept a book like this — or thrown it away."

⑧ On my way home, I thought about my first silly mistake: quietly taking something that was not mine, and telling no one. If I had told Mrs Guo that day, none of the rest would have happened — no bus to Peach Road, no noodle shop owner with her kind napkin, no blue sweater at the door, no grandfather's birds returning home after three lost years. Sometimes a wrong first step turns into a long, good road, **if we are brave enough to stop, look, and tell someone the truth**.

⑨ Auntie Ying wrote to me last month. She has started a new notebook — "Birds I See from My Window" — and she asked if I would draw a sparrow for the first page. I am not a great artist, but I am practising. Some promises are worth trying, even with a shaky pencil.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer believe the brown book belonged to the library at first?**

A. Because it had a clear library sticker.  
B. Because it had no cover label and he found it under the library counter during the move.  
C. Because Mrs Guo told him so.  
D. Because another student said it was a library book.

**2. Whose handwriting was inside the notebook, and what was it about?**

A. A scientist writing about robots.  
B. Grandpa Song writing short notes and drawings about birds, with a dedication to his granddaughter Ying.  
C. A famous writer writing poems.  
D. A teacher writing exam questions.

**3. How did the writer finally find Auntie Ying?**

A. He put a notice on the school wall.  
B. He asked Mrs Guo, went to a noodle shop on Peach Road, and got Auntie Ying's new address from the shop owner.  
C. He searched the Internet.  
D. He asked his parents to drive around the city.

**4. How did Auntie Ying feel when she saw the notebook?**

A. Angry, because the writer had taken it by mistake.  
B. Moved to tears, because she thought she had lost it forever.  
C. Bored, because she did not remember it.  
D. Frightened of the young visitor.

**5. What does the writer want to tell readers in paragraph ⑧?**

A. Always keep anything you find; it may be valuable.  
B. A wrong first step can still turn into something good if we are brave enough to stop, look, and tell someone the truth.  
C. Young people should not ride buses alone.  
D. Librarians should always be busy during a move.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "It had no cover label... under the counter."  
【解析】因书无标签又在柜下，误以为是馆藏。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "short notes about birds... 'For my granddaughter Ying... Grandpa Song'"  
【解析】爷爷写给孙女的观鸟笔记。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③–⑤段  
【解析】通过图书管理员线索、面馆老板娘拿到新地址。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "a tear rolled quietly down her face... 'I thought I had lost it forever...'"  
【解析】失而复得，感动落泪。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧段尾句  
【解析】勇于停下来承认错误，能把错误的开头变成美好的旅程。A/C/D 均偏。故选 B。
