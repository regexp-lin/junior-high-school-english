# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Our School Make a Real 25-Minute Noon Nap?**

① If you walk into our school at 12:50 on any winter weekday, you will see a strange scene: half the classroom is already asleep on the desk, the other half is whispering, two students are finishing lunch, and one brave boy is still doing homework. At **13:00** the bell rings, and everyone must stand up and go to the first afternoon class. Most of us feel **worse** than before lunch. Some yawn through geography; some cannot remember the first Chinese poem; one of my friends fell asleep during a listening task last week. The question is simple: **should our school build in a real, quiet 25-minute noon nap, between 12:40 and 13:05?** I believe the answer is yes — and the reasons are stronger than many teachers may think.

② The first reason is **health**. Doctors at the Beijing Children's Hospital and the Fudan University Medical School have long repeated the same finding: middle-school students who take a short noon nap — about **20 to 30 minutes** — show clearly better **afternoon focus**, steadier heart rates, and lower feelings of "heavy-eye" headache by 16:00. A nap longer than 40 minutes, interestingly, is not better; the brain enters deep sleep and wakes up groggy. Twenty-five minutes is, as one doctor said, the "**golden coin**" of the school afternoon.

③ The second reason is **learning**. Science has shown that the 90 minutes right after lunch are the **sleepiest** hours of the whole school day. This is not because of big lunches; it is a natural dip in our body's internal clock, present in almost every human. Asking students to think sharply about a difficult maths problem at 13:05, without any rest, is asking the brain to run a steep hill in tight shoes. After a short nap, the afternoon lessons feel **half a stair easier**. Teachers in Shanghai and Chengdu have reported that since their schools introduced a real nap in 2023, afternoon quiz scores in languages and sciences have risen by about **4% to 7%**.

④ The third reason is **kindness to the quiet students**. In every class there are a few students who, at home, sleep less than their peers — a younger sibling crying at night, a parent working late, a noisy street. Without a school nap, they silently pay a **double price**: less sleep at night, and less rest at noon. A short, respected nap is one of the few quiet gifts that the school can give, equally, to every student, regardless of family conditions.

⑤ Of course, some voices worry. "What if students cannot fall asleep?" "What if a 25-minute nap eats into class time?" "What if some students use the time to chat?" These concerns deserve a real answer, not a polite smile. Yes, some students will not actually sleep. But **lying quietly** in a dim room with the eyes closed still gives the brain about **60% of the rest** of a true nap. And yes, 25 minutes does reduce class time slightly — about 100 minutes a week. Schools in Guangzhou have solved this by shortening the first morning break from 15 to 10 minutes, and by moving some class-meeting time to Friday's double P.E. The arithmetic works out.

⑥ The chat-worry, however, is the most serious, and it has a simple answer: **rules and rhythm**. Lights off. Curtains half-closed. One quiet teacher at the back desk. No phones. No homework out. Small, cheap eye masks available for those who want them. A soft, pre-recorded piece of music — a gentle **erhu** or **rain sound** — playing for the first three minutes, then silence. Many Chinese primary schools have used this system for decades; it works. Middle-schoolers, given a calm environment, are not louder than six-year-olds; they are simply older.

⑦ Some may ask: "Can't students just put their heads on the desk for ten minutes?" The honest answer is: **not very well**. Desk-sleeping blocks blood flow in the arms, wakes the neck in pain, and often dries the mouth against the wooden edge. Over years, many students have reported **shoulder problems** from daily desk naps. A small folded blanket, or even a clean cloth pad on the desk, is much better; a simple reclining chair, used in some new schools, is best of all. A policy can be gentle without being sloppy.

⑧ A school day is a long race, and the noon nap is the single most **human** quiet minute in it. To skip it, in the name of "more class time", is like refusing to drink water during a marathon because "water takes time". Twenty-five minutes of quiet rest does not steal from learning; it **serves** learning. I hope, by next spring, the P.E. clock in every Lihua classroom shows a real noon nap — **12:40 to 13:05, lights low, voices off, eyes closed**. A short rest, taken kindly, is not a break from school. It is a **part** of it.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main proposal?**

A. Students should sleep all afternoon.  
B. The school should build in a real, quiet 25-minute noon nap between 12:40 and 13:05.  
C. Only Grade 9 students should nap.  
D. Students should not eat lunch.

**2. According to Paragraph ②, why should the noon nap NOT be longer than 40 minutes?**

A. Because students will get too hungry.  
B. Because the brain enters deep sleep and wakes up groggy; 25 minutes is the "golden coin" of the school afternoon.  
C. Because classes finish earlier.  
D. Because 40 minutes is against school rules.

**3. Why is the writer's third reason described as "kindness to the quiet students"?**

A. Because some students like being quiet.  
B. Because some students sleep less at home (due to a crying sibling, a late-working parent, or a noisy street); a school nap is a quiet gift shared equally by all students, regardless of family conditions.  
C. Because quiet students get higher marks.  
D. Because quiet students help teachers.

**4. Which TWO things have some schools in Guangzhou used to solve the "class time" problem?**

A. Shortening the first morning break from 15 to 10 minutes, and moving some class-meeting time to Friday's double P.E.  
B. Cutting lunch in half, and stopping P.E.  
C. Starting school at 05:00, and ending at 22:00.  
D. Giving up all tests.

**5. What does the writer mean by saying "a short rest, taken kindly, is not a break from school. It is a part of it"?**

A. Rest and study are separate things.  
B. Learning is hurt by any rest.  
C. A carefully-run 25-minute nap is not time stolen from class; it is a real, necessary part of the school day that serves learning itself.  
D. Students should decide when to rest.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "between 12:40 and 13:05"  
【解析】立论直引。A、C、D 均偏。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "A nap longer than 40 minutes ... groggy ... 'golden coin'"  
【解析】时长科学原理。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "sleep less than their peers ... double price ... regardless of family conditions"  
【解析】平等保护直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "shortening the first morning break from 15 to 10 minutes ... moving some class-meeting time to Friday's double P.E."  
【解析】时间置换双方案。B、C、D 均错或不合理。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧段结尾 "serves learning ... a part of it"  
【解析】休息与学习一体。A、B、D 均偏。故选 C。
