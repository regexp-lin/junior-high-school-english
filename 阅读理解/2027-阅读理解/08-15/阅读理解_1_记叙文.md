# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Night My Hands Would Not Stop Shaking**

① Three years ago, when I was in Grade 6, my mother signed me up for **guzheng** lessons every Saturday morning. At first, I was not happy about it. I only wanted to play basketball with my friends. But slowly, as my fingers began to make simple, clear sounds on the strings, I started to feel a quiet kind of joy that I had never felt before. By the end of Grade 7, my teacher, Ms. Lin, told me something that made my heart jump: she wanted me to play a short piece, *Song of the Evening River*, alone on the school's **Lantern Festival** stage.

② "Alone?" I asked. "In front of the whole school?" Ms. Lin smiled. "Yes. I believe you can do it. You have only two weeks. Please practise every afternoon." That night, I tried to eat my favourite noodles at dinner, but I could not taste anything. Just thinking about the big stage made my stomach feel strange.

③ The next fourteen days passed like a fast river. I practised after school, sometimes until the cleaners had already locked the other classrooms. My right hand played the notes, my left hand pressed the strings for the soft bending sound — I repeated the same few lines over and over until even my dreams had the music in them. Some afternoons I played well, and I felt proud. Some afternoons I made the same mistake twenty times, and I almost cried.

④ The night of the show finally came. The Lantern Festival stage was outside, under hundreds of small red lanterns. There must have been more than eight hundred people — teachers, students, parents — sitting on the ground or standing behind the white chairs. When my name was called, I walked onto the stage with a polite bow. But as I sat down at the guzheng, I realised something terrible: **my hands were shaking so much that I could not feel the strings.**

⑤ I pressed my fingers gently on the first string and tried to start. A small, thin sound came out, weak like a mosquito. I stopped. I tried again — the same weak sound. My ears were full of the noise of my own heart. I could feel all the eyes of the audience turning toward me in silence. For a moment I wanted to run off the stage and never come back.

⑥ Then, from the front row, I heard a very quiet voice say just two words: "Breathe, Mei." It was Ms. Lin. She was smiling gently, her hand slowly moving up and down in the air, as if she were helping me to breathe. I took a deep breath — in, out — and tried once more. This time, my fingers touched the strings and the first full note of *Song of the Evening River* came out, clear and warm.

⑦ Little by little, the music carried me. I forgot the eight hundred faces; I only heard the river, the boat, the evening wind in the song. When the last note faded, there was a strange, quiet moment, and then the whole playground burst into clapping. My mother was wiping her eyes. Ms. Lin was smiling and nodding slowly.

⑧ After that night, I still sometimes feel nervous before a test or a match, but I remember those two words — "**Breathe, Mei**" — and I know that fear can be answered, not by being stronger than it, but by being **calmer** than it.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did the writer feel about her first guzheng lessons?**

A. She was very happy from the first lesson.  
B. She was not happy at first, but slowly began to feel a quiet joy as her fingers made clear sounds.  
C. She refused to touch the instrument for a whole year.  
D. She hated the guzheng and never learned anything.

**2. How long did the writer have to prepare for her solo at the Lantern Festival?**

A. Only one day.  
B. Two weeks.  
C. One month.  
D. A whole summer holiday.

**3. What happened to the writer when she first sat down at the guzheng on the stage?**

A. The strings broke suddenly.  
B. Her hands were shaking so much that she could not feel the strings.  
C. The music was already playing from a loudspeaker.  
D. The audience began to shout loudly at her.

**4. What helped the writer to start playing properly in the end?**

A. Another student came up on stage and played for her.  
B. Ms. Lin's quiet words "Breathe, Mei" and her calm hand movements helping the writer to breathe.  
C. The teachers decided to cancel the show.  
D. Her father loudly told her to hurry up.

**5. What lesson does the writer most likely want to share with readers?**

A. Only brave people should perform on stage.  
B. Fear is best answered not by being stronger than it, but by being calmer than it.  
C. One should stop learning any musical instrument to avoid stage fear.  
D. Teachers should never ask quiet students to play in front of others.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "At first, I was not happy about it ... I started to feel a quiet kind of joy"  
【解析】原文先说"不开心"，后描述逐渐感到"quiet joy"。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "You have only two weeks"  
【解析】原文明确说明有两周时间准备。A、C、D 均与数据不符。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段末 "my hands were shaking so much that I could not feel the strings"  
【解析】原文明确指出双手颤抖严重。A、C、D 均无文中依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "Breathe, Mei ... her hand slowly moving up and down in the air"  
【解析】是林老师两个词和呼吸引导帮作者稳住。A、C、D 均无文中依据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧段末 "fear can be answered ... by being calmer than it"  
【解析】作者在结尾点明主旨：以平静而非更强来回应恐惧。A、C、D 均与作者立场不符。故选 B。
