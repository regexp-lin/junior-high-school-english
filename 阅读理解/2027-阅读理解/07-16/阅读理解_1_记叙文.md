# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Strange Boy Who Cleaned His Own Desk Every Morning**

In September of Grade 8, a new classmate came into 8-3. His name was **Xu Lin**. He was thin, tall, and wore glasses so thick that his eyes seemed to be under water. Everyone in our class noticed one thing about him very quickly: every morning, before the first bell rang, Xu Lin cleaned his desk with a small yellow cloth for exactly three minutes. He wiped the top, the front, the sides, and finally the inside of the drawer. Then he sat down, took a small green book out of his bag, and read silently until class began.

Some students laughed at him behind his back. "Our desks are not dirty," a girl called Tingting said loudly one morning. "Does he think we are pigs?" Others said he was showing off. A boy, Zhang Lei, liked to throw an eraser onto Xu Lin's newly cleaned desk and say, "Oh sorry, I dropped something. Please clean it again!" The whole class would laugh. Xu Lin never said a word. He only picked up the eraser, handed it back, and wiped the small spot where it had landed.

I sat two rows behind him. I did not laugh, but I did not stop the teasing either. Each morning I watched his small, careful routine and wondered what it was really for.

One rainy Tuesday, I arrived at school very early. The classroom was empty — except for Xu Lin. He was not cleaning his own desk. He was standing in front of **Zhang Lei's** desk, which was a mess of old wrappers, a forgotten half-sandwich, and sticky tea stains. Xu Lin had his yellow cloth in his hand. Very carefully, he was wiping the top of the desk, around the rubbish, so that the wood underneath became clean again.

He saw me at the door. His face turned pink. He quickly pushed the cloth into his pocket and turned back to his own seat.

"Why?" I asked. "Zhang Lei laughs at you every day. Why are you cleaning his desk?"

Xu Lin looked down at his own hands. Then he said, very quietly, "Last year, my mother had an illness. She could not move much for three months. She used to clean our house every morning before I woke up, just with a small yellow cloth like this one. Every time I woke up, the house was clean and safe. I did not know how hard it was until she stopped doing it for three months."

He paused, then said, "She is better now. But I promised her — I will clean one small thing every day for the rest of my life, so that someone, somewhere, starts the morning in a clean place. That is all. It has nothing to do with other people."

I did not know what to say. Xu Lin went back to his seat, picked up his small green book, and began to read, as if nothing had happened.

That afternoon, I quietly told two of my best friends. On Wednesday morning, the three of us arrived early too. Without saying anything, we threw away Zhang Lei's rubbish and wiped his desk. On Thursday, four more students joined. By Friday, Zhang Lei's desk was the cleanest in the classroom — even before he sat down.

When Zhang Lei walked in that morning and saw his desk, his face changed colour twice. He looked at Xu Lin, who was reading quietly as usual. He opened his mouth, closed it, and sat down without throwing any erasers. From that day on, he never made fun of Xu Lin again.

At the end of Grade 9, when we wrote short notes for the class yearbook, I wrote one sentence beside Xu Lin's photo: *"He cleaned more than desks."* Many classmates ticked a small heart next to my words. I think they finally understood.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What special thing did Xu Lin do every morning?**

A. He danced in the classroom.  
B. He cleaned his own desk for exactly three minutes with a yellow cloth.  
C. He brought snacks for the whole class.  
D. He slept under his desk.

**2. Why did Zhang Lei throw erasers on Xu Lin's desk?**

A. Because he wanted Xu Lin to pick them up as a favour.  
B. To tease Xu Lin and make him clean the desk again for the class's laughter.  
C. Because his pencil case was broken.  
D. Because he was practising basketball in the classroom.

**3. Why did Xu Lin start his cleaning habit?**

A. Because the teacher paid him 50 yuan every day.  
B. Because his mother had been sick for three months and he made her a promise after she recovered: he would clean one small thing every day.  
C. Because he hated dirty classrooms.  
D. Because his school required it.

**4. Why did the writer and others start cleaning Zhang Lei's desk?**

A. Because the teacher ordered them to.  
B. Because they understood Xu Lin's story and his quiet kindness, and wanted to help.  
C. Because they wanted to win a prize.  
D. Because Zhang Lei paid them to.

**5. What did Zhang Lei do after seeing his clean desk?**

A. He laughed more loudly.  
B. He threw another eraser.  
C. He sat down without teasing and never made fun of Xu Lin again.  
D. He moved to another class.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"cleaned his desk with a small yellow cloth for exactly three minutes"  
【解析】黄布擦桌三分钟。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"Oh sorry, I dropped something. Please clean it again!"  
【解析】故意挑逗让他再擦。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"my mother had an illness... I promised her..."  
【解析】母亲病后许下承诺。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】因果推理题  
【定位】"quietly told two of my best friends..."  
【解析】被徐林的善意感动。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"never made fun of Xu Lin again"  
【解析】不再嘲弄。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

- **routine** [n.] 日常习惯  
- **yearbook** [n.] 纪念册
