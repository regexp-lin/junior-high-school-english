# 阅读理解（记叙文） **My First Visit to the Nursing Home**

① Last Saturday morning, our class went to **Sunshine Nursing Home** near the East Park. Ms. Gao, our head teacher, called it our "first step into the real world". To tell the truth, my heart was a little heavy on the bus. I had never talked with an old stranger before. What if I said something wrong? What if the grandma smelled bad? What if I just froze like a stone and made everyone awkward?

② The nursing home was a clean, bright building with yellow walls and a small garden full of blooming spring flowers. A nurse gave each of us a small paper badge with our name on it. Then she took us into the **reading room**, where eight grandmas and grandpas were waiting on soft sofas. My partner turned out to be **Grandma Wen**, a small, thin woman with silver hair, sitting in a wooden wheelchair by the window. When she looked up and smiled, I saw that her two front teeth were missing. My heart jumped — and softened at the same time.

③ I took out the little list Ms. Gao had given us: **three safe topics** — the weather, the flowers in the garden, and her young days. "It is **so sunny today**, isn't it?" I managed to say. Grandma Wen nodded. "Good for old bones," she said in a quiet, shaking voice. Then, before I could ask the next question, she pointed to my school badge and asked, "Bright Future Middle School? My grandson goes there, too. His name is Lin Hao. Do you know him?" I was surprised — Lin Hao was in Class 7-2, a boy I often played ping-pong with in the small yard.

④ From that moment, our shy little talk turned into a bridge of laughter. Grandma Wen told me that when she was young, she worked as a **primary school teacher** for 40 years. She once taught students by oil lamp in a village where there was no electricity. "I had only three pieces of chalk," she said, "but those kids learned to write their names, one by one." When I asked her favourite subject, she thought for a long moment and answered softly, "Stories. **Every child has a story**, even the quiet ones at the back."

⑤ Then I helped her with a small task: folding paper cranes. My first crane was crooked; hers was neat and small, even though her fingers shook. She gently showed me the fold again and said, "Slowly. Pretty things don't jump into the world. They grow." I could not help thinking of my own homework, my rushed maths answers, my rough handwriting. Grandma Wen didn't know — but she was teaching me, in a way my teachers sometimes can't.

⑥ When it was time to leave, she held my hand and said, "Thank you for listening to an old woman's words." I suddenly did not know how to answer. So I did something I had never done before — I gave her a small hug, careful not to press her thin shoulder. On the bus back to school, I sat quietly at the window. The scary grandma I had imagined before was gone. In her place stood a warm, wise friend, holding in her hands an invisible classroom of quiet lessons about time, about patience, about the **quiet courage of ordinary people**.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did the writer feel on the bus to the nursing home?**

A. Very calm and sure of himself.  
B. Excited and full of jokes.  
C. A little heavy and worried.  
D. Proud because he had been there many times.

**2. What surprised the writer about Grandma Wen at first?**

A. She was holding a bright balloon.  
B. She was dressed like his own mother.  
C. Her two front teeth were missing, yet her smile made the writer's heart soften.  
D. She was wearing a big sun hat.

**3. How did Grandma Wen and the writer discover a connection between them?**

A. They lived in the same small village.  
B. Grandma Wen's grandson was in the writer's school.  
C. They liked the same singer from a TV show.  
D. They both played ping-pong on the school team.

**4. What did Grandma Wen do when she was young?**

A. She worked in a big hospital as a nurse.  
B. She was a singer in a village band.  
C. She was a primary school teacher for 40 years.  
D. She traveled around the country as a painter.

**5. What did the writer learn from this visit?**

A. Ordinary older people can share quiet, valuable lessons.  
B. Folding paper cranes is the easiest art to master.  
C. Grandmas always have the same strict rules as teachers.  
D. Nursing homes are scary places he will never visit again.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "my heart was a little heavy... What if..."  
【解析】紧张担心。A、B、D 与原文不符。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "two front teeth were missing... heart jumped — and softened"  
【解析】没了两颗前牙但微笑让作者心软。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "My grandson goes there, too. His name is Lin Hao"  
【解析】孙子是作者校友。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "worked as a primary school teacher for 40 years"  
【解析】当过 40 年小学教师。A、B、D 与原文不符。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥段 "warm, wise friend... quiet courage of ordinary people"  
【解析】体悟到普通长者智慧与勇气。B 非主旨；C、D 与文意相反。故选 A。
