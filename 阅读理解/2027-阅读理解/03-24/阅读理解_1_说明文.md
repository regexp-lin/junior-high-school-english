# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Onions Make Us Cry**

① In almost every kitchen in China, there is a small quiet moment: Mum or Dad picks up a knife, starts to cut an onion, and suddenly begins to **blink, sniff, and wipe their eyes**. No one is sad. No one is watching a film. It is only a little round vegetable — and yet, it has made cooks cry for thousands of years. Why?

② The answer hides **inside the onion itself**. When the onion is sitting on the table, it looks calm and safe. But inside its thin white layers, two things are stored: first, **sulphur** compounds made in the soil; second, special **enzymes** that help the plant protect itself. As long as the onion is whole, these two things never meet. The moment a knife cuts in, the cells break, and the two parts mix at last. Together, they make a small, invisible gas called **syn-propanethial-S-oxide**. This gas rises quickly into the air — and straight towards our face.

③ So how does this gas lead to tears? When the gas touches the thin, wet surface of our eyes, it slowly turns into a weak acid. Our eyes do not like acid at all. They have a clever **alarm system**: special glands quickly send out extra tears to **wash the acid away** and protect the eyes. That is the reason tears come before we can even say "finished". It is not sadness; it is **self-protection**.

④ If onions really upset our eyes, can we cook without crying? The answer is yes, and there are several simple tricks. First, **chill** the onion in the fridge for about 30 minutes before cutting. A colder onion sends up gas more slowly, giving our eyes more time to blink the gas away. Second, use a **sharp knife**, not a blunt one. A sharp knife cuts cleanly and breaks fewer cells, so less gas is set free. Third, **cut near a running water tap or an open window**; the moving air carries the gas away from your face. Some cooks even wear small swimming goggles — strange-looking, but surprisingly useful.

⑤ So next time you see Dad standing at the kitchen counter with wet eyes, do not rush to ask, "What's wrong?" Smile, hand him a tissue, and remember: behind those tears is simple, silent chemistry — a small reminder that **science is quietly mixed into our dinner**, every evening of the week.

**1. What is the main topic of the article?**

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文围绕"为什么洋葱让人流泪"及"如何避免"  
【解析】核心为成因+对策。A、C、D 非主题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "The moment a knife cuts in, the cells break"  
【解析】刀切入时才相遇。A、B、D 与原文不符。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "weak acid... tears to wash the acid away"  
【解析】气体形成弱酸，泪液冲洗。A、B、D 与原文不符。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节否定题  
【定位】第④段三条技巧：冷藏、锋利、通风  
【解析】"加热洋葱"不在技巧中，且与"冷藏使气体慢"相反。B、C、D 都属文中方法。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨推断题  
【定位】第⑤段末 "science is quietly mixed into our dinner"  
【解析】科学融入日常晚餐。A、C、D 与主旨不符。故选 B。
