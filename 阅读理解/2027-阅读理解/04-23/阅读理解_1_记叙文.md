# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Playing the Guzheng for Grandma at the Nursing Home**

① My grandma has been living in a small **nursing home** on Rose Street for six months. Her legs no longer climb stairs well, and my parents both work far from her old flat. At first I was a little afraid of the place. The halls smelled of soup and soap; old people sat in chairs and slept with their mouths open. When I first went to visit, I did not know what to say. I held her hand, ate a piece of orange, and left in twenty minutes.

② Then came last Saturday. My music teacher, Ms Shen, said our class should prepare a **small concert** for the nursing home on 1 May. "Nothing big," she said gently, "just one song each, on any instrument." I had been learning the **guzheng** (Chinese zither) for three years. My best piece was **《渔舟唱晚》 "Fishing Boats Singing in the Evening"**. I was good at the fast part, but I was also very shy. The idea of standing in front of tens of old strangers made my stomach turn.

③ On Friday night, my mother said, "You do not have to perform if you really do not want to." But then my father added, "Grandma told me on the phone that she has not heard the guzheng for thirty years." I was quiet for a long time that night. I thought about her small soft hands, her thin white hair, and the way she once taught me how to fold a paper crane when I was five. I decided to play.

④ Saturday afternoon came quickly. Six of us from our class arrived at the nursing home with our instruments. The small hall was full — about forty old people in wheelchairs, and five nurses in white coats. I saw Grandma in the front row, wearing her favourite green sweater. My hands were cold. Ms Shen gave me a quick nod, and I sat down behind my guzheng.

⑤ The first ten seconds were hard. My fingers did not want to move. Then I looked up, and Grandma's eyes met mine. She smiled, just a little. Suddenly my hands remembered what to do. The **strings sang**, soft and bright. As the music grew, I saw an old grandfather in the third row slowly move his hand in the air, as if pulling a fishing net. A grandmother next to him closed her eyes and smiled. For a few minutes, the nursing home did not feel like a quiet place for old people — it felt like a warm boat on an evening river.

⑥ When I finished, I was shaking a little. The room clapped, not loudly, but gently, like soft rain. Grandma did not clap. She just held a small pink handkerchief to her eyes, and when I walked down, she whispered into my ear, "**Thirty years. Thank you, my child.**" I understood then that my twenty-minute visits had been too small. Music does not only make the young feel something; it gives the old a way to talk without words.

⑦ Before we left, an old nurse gave me a plate of warm sweet rice balls. My classmate Xiaolu said she would visit her own grandfather more often too. Walking home in the spring wind, I thought: growing older may mean losing many things, but a simple song, played carefully by a grandchild, can bring some of them back. I promised myself that next month I would come again, with a new piece, and a whole evening of time.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer feel a little afraid at first to visit the nursing home?**

A. Because it was too far away.  
B. Because it felt strange and full of sleeping old people.  
C. Because the food was too expensive.  
D. Because nurses did not like visitors.

**2. What instrument did the writer play at the small concert?**

A. The piano.  
B. The violin.  
C. The guzheng (Chinese zither).  
D. The flute.

**3. Why did the writer finally decide to perform?**

A. Because Ms Shen forced her.  
B. Because her father told her Grandma had not heard the guzheng for thirty years.  
C. Because she wanted prize money.  
D. Because she had no homework that day.

**4. During the music, what did the old grandfather in the third row do?**

A. He fell asleep and snored loudly.  
B. He moved his hand in the air, as if pulling a fishing net.  
C. He stood up and started dancing.  
D. He walked out angrily.

**5. What did the writer learn from this experience?**

A. Old people do not enjoy music.  
B. Music, played carefully, can bring back feelings and memories for the elderly.  
C. Nursing homes should become concert halls.  
D. Only piano music is good for old people.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段描述  
【解析】陌生气味+老人午睡，让她害怕。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "guzheng"  
【解析】古筝。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段父亲的话  
【解析】奶奶三十年没听古筝。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段  
【解析】老人仿佛在收渔网。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥–⑦段  
【解析】音乐能唤起记忆与情感。故选 B。
