# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Parents Check Their Children's Phones?**

① Every night in many Chinese homes, the same small war quietly starts: the child is lying in bed with a phone, and the parent walks to the door and says, "Give me your phone. I want to have a look." Some children hand it over; others hide it under the pillow. A question is now heating up on parent forums: **is it right for parents to check their children's phones?**

② Some parents say yes, and the reason is safety. Middle-school students are still learning to tell right from wrong. Dangerous chat groups, strange websites, and even phone scams can appear in one minute. A quick check, they argue, helps parents notice problems early. "I am not spying on her," one mother said. "I am checking the road before my daughter walks on it."

③ However, many teens, and some psychologists, do not agree. At the age of twelve to fifteen, a child is building a **sense of self**. Small secrets — a diary, a small joke with a best friend, a crush on a classmate — are important parts of growing up. When parents read every message, these small private corners disappear. Some students begin to feel that no place in the world really belongs to them. This feeling can quietly grow into anger or distance.

④ A second problem is **trust**. If a mother often opens her daughter's phone, the daughter may learn a clear lesson: *the only safe way is to hide.* She might create a second chat account, or delete messages every evening. In the end, the phone may look "clean", but the real conversation has simply moved to a place where parents cannot see it at all. Checking, in this case, does not protect — it pushes communication into the dark.

⑤ So, which side is right? In my view, neither pure **control** nor full **freedom** is the answer. Parents should sit down with the child and agree together: no phone after 10 p.m., no chat with strangers, no long video apps on school nights. Parents may look at the phone together **with the child**, once a week, to talk about anything strange. This is not spying; this is teamwork.

⑥ A phone is only a small glass rectangle, but the trust between parents and children is long and warm. A wise family uses the phone to **grow** trust, not to **break** it. When we talk about our online life openly, we find that most of our worries are much smaller than we thought.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句 + 全文  
【解析】全文围绕"父母是否应检查孩子的手机"展开。A、C、D 偏题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第 3 句 "Dangerous chat groups, strange websites, and even phone scams... helps parents notice problems early"  
【解析】父母为了及时发现危险（骗局、奇怪网站等）。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段末两句 "no place in the world really belongs to them. This feeling can quietly grow into anger or distance."  
【解析】担心孩子失去私人空间，从而产生愤怒或疏远。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段 "create a second chat account, or delete messages every evening"  
【解析】指青少年会创建隐藏账号或每晚删除信息来躲避检查。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑤段 "agree together... look at the phone together with the child, once a week... This is teamwork."  
【解析】作者建议双方约定规则+偶尔共同查看+以团队合作方式沟通。A、B、D 为极端或偏题。故选 C。
