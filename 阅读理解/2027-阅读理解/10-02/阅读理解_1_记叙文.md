# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The National Day Morning I Went to Work with Dad**

① On the second morning of the 2027 National Day holiday, my father woke me at six. I turned over and pulled the quilt over my ears. "It's a holiday, Dad," I complained. He only smiled and said, "Today you are coming to the bridge with me." My father is a bridge-painter on the new river-crossing in our city. I had never been to his workplace before, and something in his quiet voice told me I should go.

② The bus took us through empty streets. Red flags hung from every lamp post. When we got off, the sky was still grey, but the river was already awake — wide, silver, and very cold. My father's team of six men stood under the huge steel bridge, drinking hot water from a big thermos. They greeted me kindly. "So you are Old Wang's boy," one uncle said, passing me a small paper cup. "Your dad talks about you every lunch break."

③ Then the work began. They were not painting that day; they were **checking** the bridge before the holiday traffic grew heavier. My father clipped a bright orange safety belt around his waist, climbed a narrow ladder, and disappeared under the bridge. I waited above with his tool bag. For almost three hours he tapped, listened, measured, wrote notes. Every small sound — a loose bolt, a thin crack — had to be written down. Below him, the river moved; above him, cars began to cross, *thump-thump, thump-thump*.

④ At ten o'clock he climbed up. His hands were black, his face was red from the wind, and there was a small line of dried paint on his cheek. "How was it?" I asked. "All good," he said, "safe for the holiday." Then he gave me his thermos cup — the last warm water inside — and I suddenly understood: the flag-filled city, the happy families walking to parks, the high-speed trains carrying students home — all of it stood quietly on shoulders like his.

⑤ On the bus back, I did something I had not done for years: I leaned my head against my father's shoulder. He smelt of wind and metal and cold river. He did not speak, but I felt his arm move a little, as if to make more room for me.

---

**注释：** bridge-painter 桥梁涂装/维护工；thermos 保温瓶；safety belt 安全带；bolt 螺栓

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer go with his father that morning?**

A. He wanted to watch the National Day parade on TV.  
B. His father woke him early and quietly asked him to come to the bridge.  
C. His school had organised a field trip.  
D. His mother was ill and needed the father to stay home.

**2. What were the workers doing on the bridge that day?**

A. Painting the whole bridge a new colour.  
B. Checking the bridge carefully before holiday traffic grew heavier.  
C. Building a brand-new bridge from the ground up.  
D. Cleaning the river below the bridge.

**3. What does the underlined word "thump-thump" in Paragraph 3 most probably mean?**

A. The soft sound of rain falling into the river.  
B. The noise made by cars as they crossed the bridge above the father.  
C. The sound of the writer's heart beating from fear.  
D. The ringing of a school bell in the distance.

**4. How did the writer's feelings change during the morning?**

A. From angry, to bored, to angry again.  
B. From proud, to scared, to disappointed.  
C. From sleepy and unwilling, to curious, to quietly thankful and close to his father.  
D. From excited, to afraid, to completely calm.

**5. What does the writer most likely want to tell readers through this story?**

A. Bridge work is dangerous and young people should avoid it.  
B. The happy holidays of ordinary families are quietly supported by workers like his father, and he now respects his father more.  
C. Children should always sleep late during National Day.  
D. Paint on the face is a sign of a good worker.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "he woke me at six ... Today you are coming to the bridge with me."  
【解析】父亲一早叫醒他并让他一起去桥上。A "看阅兵"、C "学校组织"、D "母亲生病"均无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "They were not painting that day; they were checking the bridge before the holiday traffic grew heavier."  
【解析】原文明确说不是涂漆而是节前检查。A 偷换概念（当天不画漆）、C "新建桥梁"无依据、D "清理河流"无中生有。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义猜测题  
【定位】第③段 "cars began to cross, thump-thump, thump-thump"  
【解析】"thump-thump" 描述汽车从父亲头顶桥面驶过发出的沉闷响声。A 雨声、C 心跳、D 铃声均与上下文不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第①段 "I turned over and pulled the quilt over my ears"；第②段 "something ... told me I should go"；第⑤段 "I leaned my head against my father's shoulder"  
【解析】开头贪睡不情愿，后来好奇观察，最后靠在父亲肩上，属感激与亲近。A、B、D 情绪轨迹均与原文矛盾。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】写作意图题  
【定位】第④段 "the flag-filled city, the happy families walking to parks ... all of it stood quietly on shoulders like his"  
【解析】作者借一次陪班经历，表达对普通劳动者的敬意与对父亲的理解。A "避免危险"、C "多睡"、D "脸上的漆"都是表象，非作者意图。故选 B。
