# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Wallet on the Park Bench**

① On the last Sunday of the National Day holiday, my cousin Ming and I went to Green Lake Park to ride bikes. The sky was the soft grey-blue of early autumn, and the wind smelt of sweet osmanthus flowers. We rented two yellow bikes, raced around the lake twice, and then stopped by a quiet bench in the bamboo garden to share a bottle of cold juice.

② Ming sat down first. A second later, he jumped up again. "Something's here," he said, and pulled out a thin **brown wallet** wedged between the wooden boards of the bench. My heart gave a small, surprised jump. Ming opened it carefully. Inside were a student ID card, a bank card, three hundred yuan in cash, a bus pass — and, folded between the cards, **a photo of a small girl holding a birthday cake**.

③ I looked at the ID card. The photo showed a thin university student, about twenty years old, wearing glasses. His name was Zhou Lin, and the address was very far from our park — a university on the other side of the city. "He must have come here with friends, and dropped it when he stood up," Ming whispered. "What do we do?"

④ For a tiny moment, three hundred yuan seemed very big in my head. We could buy that new basketball, and ice cream for a week, and — **no.** I shook my head to push the thought away. "We look for him," I said. "Or we hand it in."

⑤ We first asked the old uncle selling sweet-potato snacks nearby if he had seen a university student with glasses. He had not. We walked to the park office at the west gate. A kind woman in a green uniform took the wallet, put it in a safe drawer, and gave us a small paper receipt with the wallet's details and the time we handed it in. "Well done, boys," she said. "If no one comes today, I will phone his university tomorrow morning."

⑥ As we walked out of the office, Ming looked a little disappointed. "Will we ever know what happens?" he asked. I did not know. We rode home through the falling light, the leaves making a thin, dry sound under the tyres.

⑦ Four days later, after school, I came home to find a white envelope on the kitchen table. Inside was a **handwritten letter** in clear, careful Chinese. Zhou Lin had picked up his wallet from the park office on Tuesday morning. Everything was still there. The photo, he wrote, was of his little niece, Lele, who had lost her mother earlier that year; she lived with him now, and the photo was the only one he carried. "I cannot thank you boys enough," he wrote. "You gave back more than money." In the envelope was also a small hand-made bookmark of two tiny bamboo leaves, and a short note in a child's writing: **"Thank you — from Lele."**

⑧ I put the bookmark in my English textbook that night. My mother smiled and said nothing. Ming and I did not buy the basketball. But every time I open my English book now and see two small green leaves pressed flat between the pages, I understand something I could not fully see in that bamboo garden: **some choices are worth more than they cost.**

---

**注释：** osmanthus 桂花；wallet 钱包；wedged 楔入/卡住；ID card 身份证；bookmark 书签

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where did the two boys find the wallet?**

A. Under a yellow bike.  
B. Wedged between the wooden boards of a bench in the bamboo garden of Green Lake Park.  
C. Beside the sweet-potato snack stall.  
D. In the park office drawer.

**2. What did the boys do after opening the wallet?**

A. They took out the cash and bought ice cream.  
B. They threw the wallet into the lake.  
C. After a short hesitation they decided to look for the owner or hand the wallet in.  
D. They tried to keep the wallet as a souvenir.

**3. Why does the writer say "three hundred yuan seemed very big in my head" in Paragraph 4?**

A. Because he had never seen money before.  
B. Because he briefly felt tempted by the money, but then pushed the thought away.  
C. Because three hundred yuan felt too heavy to carry.  
D. Because he wanted to give it to the park office.

**4. Who finally returned the wallet to Zhou Lin?**

A. The old sweet-potato uncle.  
B. Ming's mother.  
C. The park office, after keeping it in a safe drawer and contacting Zhou Lin's university.  
D. A policeman at the west gate.

**5. What does the writer mean at the end by "some choices are worth more than they cost"?**

A. The boys lost three hundred yuan and gained nothing.  
B. Giving up the money was a small cost, but it brought them a thank-you letter, a hand-made bookmark, and a lasting lesson about what really matters.  
C. Wallets are expensive objects.  
D. Bamboo leaves are more valuable than money.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "pulled out a thin brown wallet wedged between the wooden boards of the bench"  
【解析】钱包卡在竹园长椅木板之间。A、C、D 均为其他地点或后续过程。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段末 "We look for him ... Or we hand it in."  
【解析】短暂犹豫后决定寻主或上交。A、B、D 均与主人公行动相反。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段 "For a tiny moment, three hundred yuan seemed very big in my head ... no. I shook my head to push the thought away."  
【解析】这是内心短暂诱惑，随即自我否定。A、C、D 均误读。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "A kind woman in a green uniform took the wallet ... If no one comes today, I will phone his university tomorrow morning."  
【解析】公园办公室保管并联系大学。A、B、D 均未出现。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨/写作意图题  
【定位】第⑧段 "two small green leaves pressed flat between the pages"，以及整体故事对回报的描写  
【解析】作者通过书签、感谢信、孩子字条，让读者感受精神回报胜过物质。A 完全否定、C、D 字面误读均错。故选 B。
