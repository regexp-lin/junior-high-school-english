# 阅读理解（议论文） **Is Reading a Book Still Better Than Watching a Film?**

① At weekends, many students stand at a small choice: read a 300-page novel, or watch a 120-minute film of the same story? The second often wins. After a long week of school, the soft lights and moving pictures seem so much easier. Yet teachers keep saying, "Please read the book first." So is reading really better than watching, or is this only an old habit?

② Those who love films give strong reasons. A good film can show the world more directly. Costumes, sounds, faces and tears arrive at the viewer in one powerful second. Hong Kong scenes, English country roads, or the cold storms of Siberia — all of these would take pages of writing to describe, but only a few frames of film to feel.

③ However, a book works in a quieter and deeper way. When a novel says, "He walked into the garden," the reader must build that garden inside his own mind — the shape of the trees, the colour of the sky, the exact face of the hero. Every reader creates a slightly different world, and that world belongs only to him. In this sense, reading is not watching; it is **building**.

④ A book also gives us time. A film decides for us: now we laugh, now we cry, now the danger begins. A book lets us stop anywhere. We can close the page at a sad line, look out of the window, and think for five minutes before turning back. This slow thinking builds the quiet muscle of the mind — the muscle needed in math class, in science exams, and in any serious job later in life.

⑤ Films, of course, are not the enemy. A good film may turn a tired reader back to the book, and a book may send a fresh viewer to the cinema. The real problem appears when films **replace** books completely. A student who only watches film versions of *Journey to the West* or *Harry Potter* sees the outside of the stories, but misses the careful language, the small jokes, the long quiet sentences that only ink can carry.

⑥ So the wise choice is not either-or. Read the book first, and let your mind draw the pictures. Then watch the film, and smile at how the director has chosen his colours. Two windows are always better than one, and of these two, the book is still the deeper — the one that truly makes you its co-author, not its visitor.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What choice do many students face at weekends?**

A. Reading a novel or cleaning the yard.  
B. Reading a novel or watching a film of the same story.  
C. Writing a novel or taking a long walk.  
D. Watching a film or playing a new game.

**2. What is the main reason films have their own power, according to Paragraph 2?**

A. Films are much cheaper than books.  
B. Films can show scenes and feelings directly.  
C. Films are only one hour long.  
D. Films are always made in English.

**3. What does the writer mean by "reading is building" in Paragraph 3?**

A. The reader must pay for the book.  
B. The reader must build the story world in his own mind.  
C. The reader must write a new book.  
D. The reader must sell the book later.

**4. What problem does the writer warn about in Paragraph 5?**

A. Films replacing books completely.  
B. Books becoming too short.  
C. Cinemas being too crowded.  
D. Directors choosing bad actors.

**5. What is the writer's final suggestion?**

A. Watch films only, never read books.  
B. Read books only, never watch films.  
C. Read the book first, then watch the film.  
D. Avoid both books and films.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "read a 300-page novel, or watch a 120-minute film"  
【解析】学生面临读书与观影同一故事的选择。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "arrive at the viewer in one powerful second"  
【解析】影片能直接呈现画面与情感。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】句意理解题  
【定位】第③段 "that world belongs only to him"  
【解析】阅读是脑中建构独属世界。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "films replace books completely"  
【解析】作者担心影片完全取代书籍。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "Read the book first... Then watch the film"  
【解析】作者主张先读书再观影。故选 C。
