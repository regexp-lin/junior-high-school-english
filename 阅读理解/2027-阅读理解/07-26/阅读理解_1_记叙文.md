# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Neighbour Who Gave Me a Box of Stamps**

① In our old building lived an old man we children called "Uncle Zhou". He had lived on the sixth floor for more than forty years, and he always wore the same dark blue jacket and a pair of round glasses. He was known in the building for two things: a loud, rough voice, and a small silver key he wore on a string around his neck. None of us had ever seen what that key was for.

② For most of my childhood I was a little afraid of Uncle Zhou. When he came home with bags of vegetables, he never smiled, and if a child kicked a ball too close to him, he would shout "Careful, young man!" in a voice that could shake the windows. My mother told me he had worked for thirty years as a train conductor, and that train conductors "had to be strict". I nodded and kept my distance.

③ That changed the autumn I was twelve. Our Chinese teacher gave us a homework: "Write a report about a hobby of an older person in your building." Most of my classmates chose a gentle grandmother or a cheerful uncle. I stood in front of Uncle Zhou's red door for a full five minutes before I rang the bell. My legs felt heavy, as if I had just climbed the stairs with a bag of rice.

④ When the door opened, Uncle Zhou looked at me over his glasses. "Yes?" he said in that same rough voice. I stumbled through my question. There was a long silence. Then, to my surprise, his face softened. He stepped aside and said, "Come in. But take off your shoes, the floor is clean."

⑤ His flat was small and quiet, with a round table, two old chairs, and a wall of shelves. On the shelves sat dozens of blue and green boxes. Without saying much, he took down a large box, placed it on the table, and unlocked it with the small silver key from his neck. Inside, in neat rows, were hundreds of postage stamps — some bright, some faded, some from countries whose names I had never heard.

⑥ "I started collecting when I was your age," he said, more softly than I had ever heard him. "Every country tells its story on its stamps: its flowers, its heroes, its mountains." He picked up a blue one. "This one is from Iceland. 1969. Look, it is a little whale." He held it up to the light. His eyes were bright, almost young. For more than an hour he showed me stamp after stamp, explaining where each had come from, who had sent it, why he had kept it.

⑦ At the end, he closed the box and looked at me for a long moment. "You came to ask about my hobby," he said. "I want you to have these." He pushed a small yellow tin towards me. Inside were twenty stamps, carefully pressed onto soft paper. "My first stamps. Not the most valuable. But the beginning is always special." I tried to refuse. He raised a hand and smiled — the first real smile I had ever seen on his face. "An old hobby," he said, "needs a young hand to continue it."

⑧ I held that yellow tin all the way home. I wrote the best report of my school year, but that was not the real gift. The real gift was that every evening, when I passed Uncle Zhou's door, he would nod to me, and I would nod back. Sometimes he would knock on our door with a new stamp in his hand. Sometimes my mother sent me up with a bowl of soup. The building felt smaller, the sixth floor felt closer, and the voice I had once been afraid of now sounded simply like the voice of a neighbour.

⑨ Uncle Zhou passed away last spring. His daughter came to see my mother one Sunday afternoon and gave me a large brown envelope. Inside was a short note in his shaking handwriting: *"For the young hand that continues the old hobby."* I opened the envelope. Inside was the full green box — and the small silver key.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why was the writer afraid of Uncle Zhou at first?**

A. Because Uncle Zhou had stolen his toys.  
B. Because Uncle Zhou had a loud, rough voice and seldom smiled.  
C. Because Uncle Zhou was very tall.  
D. Because Uncle Zhou never wore glasses.

**2. What homework did the Chinese teacher give in paragraph ③?**

A. To write a report about a hobby of an older person in the building.  
B. To draw a picture of the old building.  
C. To interview the school head teacher.  
D. To learn a new song.

**3. What was special about the small yellow tin Uncle Zhou gave the writer?**

A. It was made of real gold.  
B. It held Uncle Zhou's first stamps, which were not the most valuable but the beginning of his hobby.  
C. It was filled with money.  
D. It could only be opened with a password.

**4. How did the writer's life change after visiting Uncle Zhou?**

A. He moved to another building.  
B. He and Uncle Zhou began to nod to each other, share stamps and soup, and the building felt warmer.  
C. He never spoke to Uncle Zhou again.  
D. He started collecting coins instead.

**5. What does the writer probably want to show through this story?**

A. Old people are always difficult to understand.  
B. Stamps are the best hobby in the world.  
C. Strict-looking people may hide kindness, and a small honest visit can grow into a lasting friendship.  
D. All neighbours in tall buildings are close friends.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "a loud, rough voice... never smiled..."  
【解析】声音粗、不苟言笑使作者害怕。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Write a report about a hobby of an older person..."  
【解析】采访老邻居的爱好。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "My first stamps. Not the most valuable. But the beginning..."  
【解析】他的第一批邮票，意义重于价值。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑧段 "he would nod to me... a bowl of soup..."  
【解析】彼此点头招呼，互送邮票与汤。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】通篇 + 第⑨段尾  
【解析】文章说明严厉外表下的善意与一次真诚拜访带来的长久情谊。A/B/D 均偏。故选 C。
