# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle-School Students Be Allowed to Use AI Tools for Homework?**

① In 2027, almost every smartphone in a middle-school student's pocket can talk to an **AI assistant**. With a single tap, a student can ask the machine to translate a paragraph, explain a physics formula, correct an English essay, or even write a short composition from a title. The question that used to sound like science fiction has quickly become a real school debate: **should middle-school students be allowed to use AI tools to do their homework?** It is a question that teachers, parents, and students all argue about, and there is no easy answer.

② Supporters of AI in homework point first to **equal access to help**. Not every student has a parent who studied English at university, or an older brother who is strong at maths. For these students, an AI tool can act like a patient, 24-hour tutor: it never laughs at a beginner's question, it never gets tired at midnight, and it explains the same idea five different ways if needed. In this sense, AI can make the **playing field more fair**, giving students without extra-class support a way to close the gap. Supporters also note that AI is now a **basic life skill**, like searching online or writing an email; students who do not learn how to use it well will be left behind after graduation.

③ Those against, however, ask a sharper question: **who is actually learning — the student, or the machine?** When a student asks AI for an answer and simply copies it, the brain does not struggle; and when the brain does not struggle, it does not grow. Teachers in several cities have reported that students who leaned too heavily on AI in Grade 8 did worse on their **in-class** tests than classmates who wrote the same homework the slow way. Homework, the other side argues, is not only about producing a correct answer; it is about **the quiet hours of trying, getting things wrong, and thinking again** — and AI can silently remove exactly those hours.

④ There is also a question of **honesty**. If a teacher asks for a 300-word essay on "my grandmother" and a student pastes AI's polished paragraphs, the mark they receive is not really theirs. Over time, this kind of small, quiet dishonesty can **grow a habit** — the student may start to believe that the easiest way to solve any problem is to ask a machine and pretend the work was personal. Schools that value integrity have reason to worry about this, even when the work itself looks fine on paper.

⑤ My own view is that the sensible path lies somewhere between a **total ban** and a **free-for-all**. AI should be treated in the way many schools treat dictionaries and calculators: **useful tools, allowed in some tasks, forbidden in others**. For example, it is reasonable to let students use AI to **check grammar** in a final English draft they have already written themselves, or to **explain** a maths step they do not understand, or to **suggest** five different possible openings for an essay. It is **not** reasonable to let AI write the essay from scratch and put the student's name on it. The key difference is simple: is AI helping the student **think**, or is it **thinking instead of the student**?

⑥ Schools can make this work in practice. Teachers can **clearly label** each homework as "AI-allowed", "AI-for-checking-only", or "AI-forbidden". Students can be taught to **keep a short note** at the end of their work, such as "*I asked AI for one grammar suggestion in paragraph 2*", so that using AI becomes open and honest rather than secret. Parents, too, can help by asking a new kind of question at the dinner table — not just "Is your homework finished?" but "**What did you actually figure out by yourself today?**"

⑦ AI is not a monster to be hidden, nor a magic box to be worshipped. It is **a powerful new tool**, and like every powerful new tool, it rewards those who learn its proper use and punishes those who misuse it. The future belongs to students who can **use AI without being used by it** — and teaching that balance is perhaps the most important new lesson of our time.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the debate that the writer introduces in Paragraph 1?**

A. Whether AI tools will one day replace all teachers.  
B. Whether middle-school students should be allowed to use AI tools to do their homework.  
C. Whether smartphones should be banned on campus.  
D. Whether parents should help with homework.

**2. According to Paragraph 2, what is one reason some people SUPPORT letting students use AI for homework?**

A. AI always gives the correct answer without any mistakes.  
B. AI can act like a 24-hour patient tutor and make help more equal for students without extra-class support.  
C. AI helps students fall asleep faster.  
D. AI automatically signs the homework for the student.

**3. Why do teachers in several cities worry, according to Paragraph 3?**

A. Because AI tools are too expensive for students to use.  
B. Because students who leaned too heavily on AI did worse on in-class tests than classmates who wrote homework the slow way.  
C. Because AI can never explain maths at all.  
D. Because students started refusing to come to school.

**4. What is the writer's own view (Paragraphs 5–6)?**

A. AI should be totally banned from every school.  
B. AI should be used without any rule at all.  
C. AI should be treated like a dictionary or calculator — allowed as a thinking aid, forbidden when it replaces the student's own thinking; schools can clearly label each homework as AI-allowed, AI-for-checking-only, or AI-forbidden.  
D. Students should ask AI to write every homework to save time.

**5. What does the writer say in the last paragraph?**

A. AI is a monster that must be hidden from children.  
B. AI is a magic box that will solve every problem for us.  
C. AI is a powerful new tool; the future belongs to students who can use AI without being used by it, and teaching that balance is an important new lesson of our time.  
D. Young people should never touch AI until they are adults.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】段落主旨题  
【定位】第①段末 "should middle-school students be allowed to use AI tools to do their homework?"  
【解析】首段提出核心争议。A、C、D 均非主题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "patient, 24-hour tutor ... make the playing field more fair"  
【解析】AI 作为随时可问的辅导员使帮助更公平。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "students who leaned too heavily on AI in Grade 8 did worse on their in-class tests"  
【解析】过度依赖 AI 的学生当堂测验成绩下降。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】作者观点题  
【定位】第⑤⑥段 "like dictionaries and calculators ... clearly label ... AI-allowed ... AI-for-checking-only ... AI-forbidden"  
【解析】作者主张按任务分类允许 AI 作为思考工具。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨/观点题  
【定位】第⑦段 "powerful new tool ... use AI without being used by it"  
【解析】末段强调 AI 是工具，关键是学会平衡使用。A、B、D 均与文意相反。故选 C。
