# 阅读理解（说明文）

**Chinese Tea Culture: A Cup of History**

① Tea is one of the most popular drinks in the world, but its story began in China thousands of years ago. People first used tea as medicine, and later they enjoyed it as a daily drink. Today, tea is still an important part of Chinese life and a window for foreigners to understand Chinese culture.

② There are several main kinds of Chinese tea. **Green tea** is light and fresh, and it is often drunk in spring and summer. **Black tea** has a stronger taste and is common in many families in winter. **Oolong tea** sits between green tea and black tea, with a special smell that many tea lovers like.

③ Making tea is simple, but small steps matter. First, warm the cup with hot water. Next, put a little tea into the cup or pot. Then pour in hot water—usually not boiling water for green tea—and wait for one to three minutes. Finally, smell the tea before you taste it slowly. Good tea is not only about the drink; it is also about calm and care.

④ Tea is more than a drink at the table. In many places, offering tea shows respect to guests. Friends may meet in a quiet tea house to talk instead of rushing in a noisy café. On busy days, a short tea break can help people relax and think clearly.

⑤ In recent years, young Chinese people have found new ways to enjoy tea, such as fruit tea and milk tea in shops. These new styles make tea fashion and fun. Still, the old spirit of tea—sharing, peace, and balance—remains in every cup.

---

**注释：** *Oolong* 乌龙茶（茶类名称）

## 选择题

**1.** According to Paragraph ①, which is TRUE about tea in early China?

A. People drank tea only in winter.  
B. Tea was first used as medicine.  
C. Tea came to China from other countries.  
D. Tea was invented in Europe.

**2.** What can we infer from Paragraph ②?

A. Green tea must be drunk with milk.  
B. Black tea is only for old people.  
C. Oolong tea may be a good choice for people who like a smell between green and black tea.  
D. Chinese people never drink tea in summer.

**3.** What is the main purpose of Paragraph ③?

A. To explain how to make tea step by step.  
B. To sell expensive tea cups.  
C. To warn readers not to drink green tea.  
D. To compare tea with coffee.

**4.** The underlined word “**rush**” in Paragraph ④ is closest in meaning to ______.

A. rest  
B. hurry  
C. smile  
D. wait

**5.** What is the best title for the passage?

A. How to Grow Tea in China  
B. Tea Houses Around the World  
C. Chinese Tea Culture: Past and Today  
D. Why Milk Tea Is Unhealthy  

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
**【答案】** B  
**【题型】** 细节理解  
**【定位】** 第①段第二句 “People first used tea as medicine, and later they enjoyed it as a daily drink.”  
**【解析】** 原文明确说茶最初作药用，后来才成为日常饮品，B 正确。A 与文中未限定季节且与②段绿茶在夏季饮用矛盾；C 与①段“its story began in China”不符；D 未提及欧洲。

---

**2.**  
**【答案】** C  
**【题型】** 推理判断  
**【定位】** 第②段最后一句 “**Oolong tea** sits between green tea and black tea, with a special smell that many tea lovers like.”  
**【解析】** 乌龙茶介于绿茶与红茶之间并有特殊香气，可推断适合喜欢介于两者之间风味的人，C 合理。A 未提牛奶；B “only”过于绝对；D 与②段绿茶常在春夏饮用矛盾。

---

**3.**  
**【答案】** A  
**【题型】** 写作意图  
**【定位】** 第③段整体，尤其是 “First… Next… Then… Finally…” 的步骤描述。  
**【解析】** 本段按顺序介绍温杯、放茶、注水与等待、闻香品尝，目的是说明泡茶步骤，A 正确。B 推销、C 警告不喝绿茶、D 与咖啡对比均非该段中心。

---

**4.**  
**【答案】** B  
**【题型】** 词义猜测  
**【定位】** 第④段 “Friends may meet in a quiet tea house to talk instead of rushing in a noisy café.”  
**【解析】** “instead of” 表对比：茶馆安静交谈 vs 咖啡馆嘈杂中某种行为；与 quiet 对应，rush 表示“匆忙、赶”，与 B “hurry” 最接近。A rest、C smile、D wait 均与语境对比不符。

---

**5.**  
**【答案】** C  
**【题型】** 主旨大意  
**【定位】** 全文：①起源与地位、②种类、③冲泡、④文化意义、⑤年轻人新喝法与传统精神。  
**【解析】** 文章从历史文化到现代变化，总题为“中国茶文化：过去与今天”最贴切，C 正确。A 只涉种植；B 偏重茶馆地点；D 仅片面涉及⑤中的奶茶且未作为全文主旨。
