# 阅读理解（说明文） **How Does a Vacuum Flask Keep Tea Hot for Hours?**

Many Chinese families keep a tall silver bottle on the kitchen table. It is usually called a **vacuum flask**, and it is full of hot water or tea. When you open it in the evening, the tea inside is still warm, even though the outside of the bottle feels cool. How does such a simple bottle do this small magic? The answer lies in three different ways that heat travels.

To understand the flask, we first need to remember how heat moves. Heat travels in three ways: by **conduction** (through direct touch, like a hot spoon in soup), by **convection** (through moving air or water, like warm air rising in a room), and by **radiation** (through invisible waves, like sunlight passing through a window). If a bottle can block all three, the tea inside will stay hot for a long time.

A vacuum flask is really a bottle inside another bottle. The two glass walls are very thin, and between them, almost all the air has been pumped out. This empty space is called a **vacuum**. Because there is no air, heat cannot move by conduction or convection easily — there is almost nothing for the heat to pass through. So the temperature inside the bottle changes only very slowly.

But radiation is still a problem. Heat waves can cross empty space without touching anything, just like sunlight from the sun. To stop radiation, flask makers cover the inside glass wall with a shiny layer, usually silver. This shiny layer reflects the heat waves back into the tea, like a mirror. Only a small amount of heat can escape. This is why a vacuum flask with clear glass and a silver inside can keep water above 70 °C for more than 12 hours.

Of course, a vacuum flask is not perfect. The stopper at the top is made of cork or plastic, and it allows some heat to leak out. Also, the long thin neck of the bottle is a weak point, because it connects the hot inside to the cooler outside. That is why most good flasks have a short neck and a thick stopper. If we often open the flask, heat also escapes in the form of hot steam, so engineers suggest pouring out what you need and closing the flask quickly.

Today, metal vacuum flasks are common in schools and offices. They work in the same way, but they are harder to break than the old glass ones. The next time you drink a cup of tea from your mother's flask, remember: inside that thin silver wall, there is a tiny empty room doing its quiet job of keeping the heat with you.

**1. According to paragraph 2, how many ways does heat travel?**

A. Two.  
B. Three.  
C. Four.

**2. Why is there a vacuum between the two glass walls of the flask?**

A. To make the flask lighter so you can carry it easily.  
B. To make the tea taste better.  
C. Because with no air, heat cannot move by conduction or convection easily.  
D. Because air is not safe for drinks.

**3. How does the flask deal with heat radiation?**

A. It makes the glass thicker outside.

**4. Why is the long thin neck of a flask a weak point?**

A. Because it breaks easily in the hand.  
B. Because it cannot hold the tea inside.  
C. Because it connects the hot inside to the cooler outside, so heat can leak.  
D. Because it is too expensive to make.

**5. What is the main idea of the passage?**

A. Chinese families love tea more than any other drink.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段 "conduction...convection...radiation"  
【解析】三种方式。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段 "no air, heat cannot move by conduction or convection easily"  
【解析】真空阻断对流和传导。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段 "covers the inside glass wall with a shiny layer..."  
【解析】镀银反射辐射。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段 "connects the hot inside to the cooler outside"  
【解析】颈部连通内外，会散热。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文  
【解析】说明保温瓶利用真空和银层减少热量散失。A/C/D 均片面。故选 B。
