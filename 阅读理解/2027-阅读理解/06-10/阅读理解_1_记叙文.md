# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Lost Dog on Jasmine Street**

Last Tuesday, on my way home from school, I heard a soft cry from behind a rubbish bin on Jasmine Street. At first I thought it was a cat. I walked closer, and a small brown dog looked up at me with big, tired eyes. Its left front leg was held up in the air; it did not want to put any weight on it. A thin red rope was still around its neck.

I sat down on the pavement, a little nervous. I had always loved dogs, but I had never touched a strange one alone. The dog did not bark. It just looked at me and made small breathing sounds. There was no name tag, only the red rope, which looked old.

I tried to pick it up, but the dog cried softly, so I stopped. Instead, I took off my jacket and put it gently around the dog like a small soft bed. Then I called my mother. "Mum, there is a hurt dog on Jasmine Street. Its leg may be broken. Can we take it to the animal hospital?"

My mother arrived in twenty minutes. She brought a clean towel and a bottle of water. Together we moved the dog into the back seat of the car very carefully. The animal doctor, Dr. Zhou, looked at the dog's leg and said, "He has been hit by something, maybe a bicycle. The leg is not broken, but it needs rest for a few weeks." Dr. Zhou gave the dog some medicine, a white bandage, and a warm blanket.

That evening, we put the dog in our small garage, on an old cushion. I named him **Xiao Hua** because of the small white spot on his chest. I sat with him for more than an hour, reading my English homework aloud. Strangely, he seemed to like the sound of my voice.

The next morning, my father printed twenty paper notes: "Have you lost a small brown dog with a white spot on Jasmine Street?" We put the notes on lamp posts, in the local shops, and on the school's notice board. On Friday afternoon, an old woman with grey hair and a walking stick came to our door. When she saw Xiao Hua, she cried. His real name, she said, was **Beans**. Beans had run out through a broken gate four days before, and the old woman had been searching every street in the rain.

Before she left, she took my hand and said, "Thank you, child. At my age, every friend matters." I did not know what to answer. I only knew that, for a long time, whenever I walked past Jasmine Street, I would remember that soft cry behind the rubbish bin — and feel a small, warm light in my chest.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where did the narrator first find the small dog?**

A. Inside a garden.  
B. Behind a rubbish bin on Jasmine Street.  
C. In the animal hospital.  
D. On the school playground.

**2. Why didn't the narrator pick up the dog at once?**

A. Because the dog bit his hand.  
B. Because the dog was too heavy.  
C. Because the dog cried softly when he tried, so he stopped.  
D. Because he was afraid of dogs.

**3. What did Dr. Zhou say about the dog's leg?**

A. It was broken and needed an operation.  
B. It was fine, no problem at all.  
C. It was not broken, but it needed rest for a few weeks.  
D. The dog would lose its leg.

**4. How did the narrator and his family look for the dog's owner?**

A. They posted videos online only.  
B. They asked the police to find the owner.  
C. They printed paper notes and put them on lamp posts, shops and the school notice board.  
D. They walked every street in the city.

**5. What is the best title for the passage?**

A. A Day at the Animal Hospital.  
B. The Old Woman with Grey Hair.  
C. The Lost Dog on Jasmine Street.  
D. My First Time at School.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 1 段 "behind a rubbish bin on Jasmine Street"  
【解析】在 Jasmine 街垃圾桶后。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段 "the dog cried softly, so I stopped"  
【解析】小狗哀鸣，不敢抱。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段 "not broken, but it needs rest for a few weeks"  
【解析】未骨折，但要休息数周。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 6 段 "printed twenty paper notes..."  
【解析】贴纸条寻主人。A/B/D 无依据或片面。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】标题归纳题  
【定位】全文  
【解析】围绕街上捡狗展开。A/B/D 均片面。故选 C。
