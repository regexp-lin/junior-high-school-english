# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why We Dream at Night**

① Almost every person dreams, even if he or she cannot remember what the dream is about. Scientists say we have **three to five** dreams each night, and most of them happen in a special stage of sleep called **REM sleep**. During this stage, our eyes move quickly under the eyelids, while our brain becomes almost as active as when we are awake.

② Dreams are not the same every night. Sometimes we fly above a sea of clouds, sometimes we take a difficult exam, and sometimes we simply walk with a family member who has passed away. Where do these strange stories come from? Scientists offer several interesting answers.

③ The first answer is that dreams help our **memory**. During the day, the brain receives a huge amount of information — English words, math formulas, faces of new classmates, and even the smell of breakfast. At night, the brain works like a kind librarian. It reads through these memories, keeps the useful ones, and quietly throws away the rest. Dreams may be the short films that play during this tidying up.

④ The second answer is that dreams help us deal with **strong feelings**. When students feel nervous before an exam, they may dream that they lose their pencil or forget their name. Through such dreams, the brain practises how to face hard situations. In the morning, the bad feeling often becomes smaller, and the real exam feels less frightening.

⑤ A third answer links dreams with **creativity**. Many famous discoveries began in a dream. It is said that the scientist Kekulé found the ring shape of benzene after dreaming of a snake biting its own tail. Artists, writers and even students sometimes wake up with a fresh idea they could not find during the day.

⑥ Of course, not all dreams are good. **Nightmares** — very scary dreams — may appear when we are too tired, too worried, or when we watch frightening films before bed. Doctors suggest three simple ways to sleep better and dream more happily: keep a regular sleep time, avoid heavy food at night, and let the mind rest with a quiet book instead of a bright screen.

⑦ So the next time you wake up from a strange dream, do not be surprised. Your brain is simply doing its night work — cleaning, protecting and sometimes creating — while you lie quietly on your pillow.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. When do most dreams happen?**

A. In the first five minutes of sleep.  
B. During REM sleep.  
C. Just before we fall asleep.  
D. Only in the early morning.

**2. According to Paragraph 3, the brain at night acts like ____.**

A. a noisy classroom  
B. a kind librarian  
C. a strict guard  
D. a busy cook

**3. What is the second reason the writer gives for dreaming?**

A. Dreams make our eyes move quickly.  
B. Dreams help us deal with strong feelings.  
C. Dreams make us hungry in the morning.  
D. Dreams stop us from remembering faces.

**4. What is special about the scientist Kekulé in Paragraph 5?**

A. He often had nightmares about snakes.  
B. He could not sleep for a whole week.  
C. He found an important shape after a dream.  
D. He trained others to remember their dreams.

**5. Which of the following is NOT suggested by the doctors?**

A. Keep a regular sleep time.  
B. Avoid heavy food at night.  
C. Read a quiet book before bed.  
D. Watch a scary film before bed.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "most of them happen in... REM sleep"  
【解析】大多数梦发生在 REM 睡眠阶段。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "the brain works like a kind librarian"  
【解析】大脑被比作善良的图书管理员。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "dreams help us deal with strong feelings"  
【解析】第二个原因是帮助应对情绪。A 是现象非原因，C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Kekulé found the ring shape of benzene after dreaming"  
【解析】他梦后发现苯环结构。A 误读，B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "avoid heavy food at night... instead of a bright screen"  
【解析】医生建议远离恐怖影片，D 为反面。故选 D。
