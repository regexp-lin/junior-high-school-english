# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Parents Give Their Children Pocket Money Every Week?**

In many Chinese families, parents are not sure whether to give their children weekly pocket money. Some say giving money every week will make children lazy or selfish. Others buy anything their children want, whenever they want it, which is not much better. I believe the best way is to give middle school students a **small, fixed weekly pocket money**, and to teach them how to use it.

The first reason is simple: children cannot learn to swim without touching the water. If parents always buy everything, a child never learns the real cost of a pencil, a book or a cup of milk tea. When a 14-year-old spends his own weekly money, however, he quickly notices that "one milk tea a day" takes away half of his money. He starts to think: is that milk tea more important than the novel he also wanted? This small daily choice is one of the earliest lessons in managing money, and no textbook in school can teach it so clearly.

A second reason is responsibility. With weekly pocket money comes a weekly duty: plan before you spend. Parents can help their children write a simple "budget" — perhaps 30% for food or snacks, 30% for books or learning tools, 30% for saving, and 10% for small gifts or donations. Of course the child will not follow this perfectly. But when he sees, at the end of the month, that his saving line is growing, he feels a real pride. This kind of pride cannot be given; it has to be earned.

Some people worry that pocket money may cause students to show off among classmates. This is a fair worry, but the answer is **education**, not "no money at all". Parents should explain that the amount of money is private, and that comparing pocket money with others is as silly as comparing shoe sizes. Schools can also help by teaching simple money habits, such as: do not carry more than you need, do not lend large sums to friends, and keep a small paper book of what you spend.

Finally, I believe weekly pocket money is better than monthly. A month is too long for most middle school students; they spend all the money in the first week and then ask their parents for extra. A weekly plan is shorter, simpler and easier to check. At the same time, it teaches patience — a child who wants an expensive game may have to save for four or five weeks, but the waiting makes the final purchase much more meaningful.

In short, pocket money is not a reward or a trap. It is a small but useful tool. Used wisely, it turns our children into people who can one day handle not only their own wallets, but also the bigger choices in life.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the author's main opinion?**

A. Parents should give their children pocket money only on their birthdays.  
B. Parents should give middle school students a small, fixed weekly pocket money, and teach them how to use it.  
C. Parents should never give pocket money to middle school students.  
D. Parents should let children manage all the family money.

**2. According to paragraph 2, what is one benefit of weekly pocket money?**

A. Children earn extra money at school.  
B. Children learn the real cost of things and make small daily choices.  
C. Children can buy more milk tea than before.  
D. Children spend less time studying.

**3. What is a simple budget example mentioned in the passage?**

A. 50% food, 50% saving.  
B. All money for milk tea.  
C. 30% food or snacks, 30% books or learning tools, 30% saving, 10% small gifts or donations.  
D. 100% saving, 0% spending.

**4. How does the author reply to the worry that pocket money causes showing off?**

A. Parents should give even more money.  
B. The answer is education, not "no money at all".  
C. The children should never meet each other.  
D. All students should get the same amount.

**5. Why does the author prefer weekly to monthly pocket money?**

A. Because weekly money is a larger amount.  
B. Because a weekly plan is shorter, simpler, easier to check, and teaches patience.  
C. Because monthly money makes parents rich.  
D. Because banks only accept weekly deposits.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第 1 段末句  
【解析】支持小额周固定零花钱并教导使用。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段 "the real cost of a pencil..."  
【解析】认识物价并做选择。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段 "30% for food... 30% for books... 30% for saving, and 10%..."  
【解析】30/30/30/10 分配。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段 "the answer is education..."  
【解析】用教育解决攀比。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段  
【解析】周更短、更易跟踪、训练耐心。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
