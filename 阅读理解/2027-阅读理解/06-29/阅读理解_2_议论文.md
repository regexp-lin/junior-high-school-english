# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Students Be Allowed to Choose Their Own Seats in Class?**

Every September, when a new school year starts, one small question quietly worries many of us: *Who will I sit next to this year?* In most middle schools, teachers still decide where each student sits. But more and more students ask: "Why can't we choose our own seats?" In my opinion, **students should be allowed to choose their seats, as long as clear rules are followed**.

Those who disagree often have one main worry: if students choose freely, they will sit with their best friends and talk all day. At first, this sounds reasonable. Yet a careful look tells a different story. In my school, we did a small test last term. In Class 7(3), students chose their own seats for three weeks; in Class 7(4), the teacher decided. At the end of the test, Class 7(3) actually scored a little higher on the monthly English test — and, more importantly, far fewer students said they felt "invisible" in the back corner. **Choice brought responsibility, not chaos.**

The second reason is about comfort. A classroom is not a film cinema, where every seat has the same view. Students are not the same either. Some are short and need to sit near the front; some feel nervous next to the window; some focus better when the sun is behind them. A teacher, no matter how kind, cannot know every small need of forty-five different minds. Letting students choose is a simple but powerful way to **respect these differences**.

Some teachers also worry that weaker students will be "left alone" when friends group up. But there is a third reason that answers this worry: **choosing a seat is a chance to learn social skills**. Teachers can ask students to change seats every two weeks, or to sit with a new partner every Monday. In this way, every student has many chances to meet new classmates. Slowly, children learn how to say "Can I sit here?" and "Yes, welcome," — words that are just as important as any grammar rule.

Of course, full freedom would be a mistake. Rules are needed. I suggest three: *First*, no one may sit alone — everyone must share a desk with someone. *Second*, if any two students' talking affects the class, the teacher may move them the next day. *Third*, eyesight and height must be taken into account; short students always have a spot in the front two rows.

In short, choosing one's seat is not about doing whatever we like. It is about treating students as **responsible people**. When we are trusted, most of us try to deserve that trust. That, I believe, is one of the quietest but most powerful lessons a school can teach.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main idea?**

A. Teachers must always decide seats.  
B. Students should be allowed to choose seats, with clear rules.  
C. Students should sit wherever they want, with no rules.  
D. Only top students can choose seats.

**2. What did the Class 7(3) experiment show?**

A. Grades went down and students felt lost.  
B. Grades went slightly up and fewer students felt "invisible".  
C. The class became very noisy.  
D. Half of the students moved to another school.

**3. Why is comfort an important reason?**

A. Because short students and students with different needs are not all the same.  
B. Because classrooms are cinemas.  
C. Because long desks are uncomfortable.  
D. Because windows are too big.

**4. Why does the writer suggest changing partners every two weeks?**

A. To let students learn social skills and meet new classmates.  
B. To make the classroom cleaner.  
C. To save money on furniture.  
D. To help teachers do less work.

**5. Which rule does the writer suggest?**

A. No rules at all.  
B. One student sits alone at every desk.  
C. If two students' talking affects the class, the teacher may move them the next day.  
D. Only boys can sit in the front.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】首段尾句  
【解析】有规则下允许自选。A/C/D 均偏离。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"scored a little higher... far fewer students said they felt 'invisible'"  
【解析】成绩略升且"隐形感"减少。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"Some are short... not the same"  
【解析】学生身高、性格各异。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"learn social skills... meet new classmates"  
【解析】换座位练习社交。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"if any two students' talking affects the class..."  
【解析】影响课堂→第二天调位。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。
