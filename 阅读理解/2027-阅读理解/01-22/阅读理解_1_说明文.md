# 阅读理解（说明文） **The Spring Festival: More Than a Long Holiday**

① For Chinese people, the most important festival of the year is the Spring Festival, also known as Chinese New Year. It usually falls in late January or early February, when the first new moon of the lunar year rises in the sky. Unlike many other holidays, it is not just one day but a time of about two weeks full of colour, food and family.

② A few days before the festival, families begin "spring cleaning." Every room is swept, windows are wiped, and old newspapers are thrown away. The idea is simple: clean away the dust of the old year and welcome the new. On the doors, red paper strips with good wishes are put up. These are called "chunlian." Red is the colour of luck and happiness.

③ The night before the first day of the new year is called Chu Xi. Families get together for a big dinner, with fish, chicken, dumplings, rice cakes and many other dishes. Fish often appears because its Chinese name sounds like "enough year after year." After dinner, adults give red envelopes with money to children. At midnight, in many places, fireworks and bells welcome the new year.

④ Each day of the festival has its own small customs. On the first day, people visit their parents and grandparents and say warm words like "Gong Xi Fa Cai." On the second day, married daughters often go back to their mothers' homes. Many cities hold temple fairs with dragon dances, lion dances, paper cutting and folk music. Children can try sweet sugar animals or warm soup on cold streets.

⑤ The festival ends on the fifteenth day with the Lantern Festival. People eat sweet rice balls, watch colourful lanterns, and read simple riddles written on them. Although life today is busy and fast, the Spring Festival still reminds us to slow down, come home, thank our family and send good wishes. Old customs can also be warm teachers for young hearts.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. When does the Spring Festival usually begin?**

A. In the middle of July.  
B. When the first new moon of the lunar year appears.  
C. On December 25 every year.  
D. On the first day of October.

**2. Why do families do "spring cleaning" before the festival?**

A. Because their houses will be sold soon.  
B. Because they want to clean away the dust of the old year.  
C. Because cleaning can earn them money.  
D. Because teachers ask every student to do so.

**3. Why does fish often appear at the New Year's Eve dinner?**

A. Because it is the cheapest food in winter.  
B. Because its name sounds like "enough year after year."  
C. Because fish can swim very fast.  
D. Because it is eaten only in southern China.

**4. What happens on the second day of the Spring Festival?**

A. People visit Beijing to climb the Great Wall.  
B. Married daughters often go back to their mothers' homes.  
C. Children return to school to have a math exam.  
D. Farmers start to plant rice in the fields.

**5. What does the writer mainly want readers to feel in the last paragraph?**

A. Old customs waste too much time in modern life.  
B. The festival has nothing to do with young people.  
C. Old customs can also teach and warm young hearts.  
D. Everyone should stop using phones during the festival.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "when the first new moon of the lunar year rises in the sky."  
【解析】春节始于农历新年第一轮新月时。A、C、D 时间均不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "clean away the dust of the old year and welcome the new."  
【解析】扫除是为了扫掉旧岁的尘埃、迎接新年。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "its Chinese name sounds like 'enough year after year.'"  
【解析】鱼的读音谐音"年年有余"。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "On the second day, married daughters often go back to their mothers' homes."  
【解析】大年初二女儿回娘家。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】写作意图/主旨题  
【定位】末段 "Old customs can also be warm teachers for young hearts."  
【解析】作者希望让年轻人感受老习俗的温暖。A 与"老师"意象相反；B、D 均为过度推断。故选 C。
