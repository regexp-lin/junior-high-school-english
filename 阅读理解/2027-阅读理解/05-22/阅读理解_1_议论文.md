# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Cooking Be a Regular Class in Middle School?**

① Last week our school opened a small kitchen on the third floor. Every Class 8 student had a 40-minute lesson there: half of us made tomato-and-egg noodles, the other half cut vegetables for a simple salad. The whole school is now talking about one question: **should cooking be a regular class, like Chinese or math, in middle school?** I believe it should.

② First, cooking teaches an important **life skill** that many teenagers still lack. In many families, parents are afraid the kitchen is "dangerous" for a child, so children are kept far from the stove. As a result, some students can solve a difficult equation but cannot fry an egg. When these students go to high school or university far away from home, they sometimes eat only instant food. A cooking class during middle school fixes this problem before it becomes serious.

③ Second, cooking is a surprisingly strong way to study **science**. When we boil water, we see changes of state. When we add salt into soup, we watch how it dissolves. When we fry garlic in oil, the smell spreads across the room because of moving air particles. A kitchen is really a small laboratory. Many textbook ideas suddenly feel real after a student cooks them.

④ Third, cooking connects us with **culture and family**. China has thousands of local dishes, and each one tells a story: zongzi at the Dragon Boat Festival, tangyuan at Lantern Festival, yuebing at Mid-Autumn. When students learn to make even a simple version of these dishes at school, they begin to understand why grandparents smile when they smell them. Food is memory, and cooking is the key that opens it.

⑤ Some teachers worry that cooking classes will take time away from the **main subjects**, and that the school is already busy enough. I understand this concern. However, only **one lesson every two weeks** is enough. We do not need to turn middle school into a cooking school; we only need a small, steady seed. Safety, of course, must come first: sharp knives and open flames should always be used under a teacher's eye, and students should wear clean aprons and tie back long hair.

⑥ Books teach us how the world thinks; cooking teaches us how the world lives. A young person who can finish a hot dinner for their family, even a simple one, is already on the road to becoming a caring adult. A single small pot can hold more lessons than we imagine. That is why I hope the new kitchen on our third floor will not be a special event, but a gentle, regular part of middle school life.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's opinion about cooking classes?**

A. Cooking classes should be removed from schools.  
B. Cooking should be a regular class in middle school.  
C. Cooking should only be taught to adults.  
D. Cooking is dangerous and useless.

**2. According to paragraph ②, why do many teenagers lack cooking skills?**

A. Because they hate food.  
B. Because parents are afraid the kitchen is dangerous and keep children away from it.  
C. Because cooking is too easy.  
D. Because schools force students to cook at home.

**3. How does cooking help with science, according to paragraph ③?**

A. It shows changes of state, how salt dissolves, and how smells move through air.  
B. It teaches students to ignore science.  
C. It proves that science is wrong.  
D. It changes the colour of books.

**4. Why does cooking connect us with culture and family?**

A. Because every dish costs a lot of money.  
B. Because Chinese dishes like zongzi, tangyuan and yuebing carry stories and memories.  
C. Because cooking is only for old people.  
D. Because families never eat together any more.

**5. How does the writer answer teachers who worry about time?**

A. Cooking should be the only subject.  
B. Only one lesson every two weeks is enough, with safety as the first rule.  
C. Students should skip main subjects.  
D. Schools should close the kitchen again.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末及全文  
【解析】作者支持每两周一节的厨艺课。A/C/D 均与观点相反。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "parents are afraid the kitchen is 'dangerous' for a child, so children are kept far from the stove."  
【解析】家长怕危险，让孩子远离厨房。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "changes of state... watch how it dissolves... smell spreads across the room because of moving air particles."  
【解析】三个例子全都出现在原文。B/C/D 均不符。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "zongzi at the Dragon Boat Festival, tangyuan at Lantern Festival, yuebing at Mid-Autumn."  
【解析】中国传统菜肴承载故事和记忆。A/C/D 均无据或错误。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "only one lesson every two weeks is enough... Safety, of course, must come first..."  
【解析】课程频率很低，并强调安全。A/C/D 与原文相反。故选 B。
