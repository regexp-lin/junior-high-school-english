# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Do Bees Dance?**

① On a warm spring afternoon, if you sit quietly near a flower garden, you may see a honeybee return to its hive and begin to **move in a strange little circle** on the honeycomb. It is not singing. It is not resting. It is actually **talking** to its sisters — through a special dance that scientists call the "**waggle dance**".

② The waggle dance was first described by Austrian scientist **Karl von Frisch** more than 80 years ago. He watched bees for years and noticed something surprising: **a dancing bee tells the others where the food is.** When a bee finds a good flower patch, it flies back home and dances to share the news — the direction and the distance of the flowers.

③ The dance has two simple parts. First, the bee runs in a short straight line while **shaking its body from side to side**. This shaking part is called the **waggle run**. Then it turns and walks back in a half circle, returns to the start, and does the waggle run again. Together, the straight line and the half circle form the shape of the number **"8"** on the honeycomb.

④ How does the bee tell the direction? Here is the clever part. The bee uses the **sun** as its map. If it dances straight up on the wall of the hive, it means "Fly towards the sun, sisters." If it dances 30 degrees to the left of straight up, the flowers are 30 degrees to the left of the sun's direction. A dance pointing straight down means "**Fly away from the sun.**" Even on a cloudy day, bees can still use **polarised light** in the sky, which is too quiet for our eyes to see.

⑤ And the distance? That is in the **length of the waggle part**. A **short waggle** of one second tells other bees that the food is close — maybe only 100 metres away. A **long waggle** of two or three seconds says the flowers are farther — perhaps 1 kilometre or more. In this way, a single dance can carry a **map plus a distance table** on a small wax floor.

⑥ Why is this useful to us? When a farmer notices that the bees in his apple orchard are not dancing, he may worry that something is wrong — perhaps pesticides have made the bees too weak to fly far. So the quiet dance on the honeycomb is not only a message among bees; it is also a **green warning light** for humans. The next time we bite into a sweet honey bun, we can thank the tiny dancers who drew maps with their bodies just to keep their family well fed.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is a "waggle dance"?**

A. A bee's way of finding a mate.  
B. A bee's way of sharing food location with other bees.  
C. A bee's way of fighting with enemies.  
D. A bee's way of going to sleep.

**2. Who first described the waggle dance?**

A. A Chinese scientist.  
B. An American scientist.  
C. An Austrian scientist, Karl von Frisch.  
D. A Japanese farmer.

**3. What shape does the full dance form on the honeycomb?**

A. The letter "O".  
B. The number "8".  
C. A triangle.  
D. A star.

**4. How do bees tell their sisters the direction of food?**

A. By making a different sound.  
B. By using the sun as a map.  
C. By touching each other's wings.  
D. By leaving a smell on leaves.

**5. Why is the waggle dance also useful for humans?**

A. It helps doctors understand the weather.  
B. It makes honey taste sweeter.  
C. It can warn farmers when something is wrong with the bees or the environment.  
D. It teaches children to dance at parties.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "a dancing bee tells the others where the food is"  
【解析】摇摆舞用来告诉同伴食物位置。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Austrian scientist Karl von Frisch"  
【解析】奥地利科学家冯·弗里希首次描述。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "the shape of the number '8'"  
【解析】舞步呈"8"字形。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "uses the sun as its map"  
【解析】以太阳为方向地图。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "green warning light for humans"  
【解析】蜜蜂舞能提示农人环境或农药问题。故选 C。
