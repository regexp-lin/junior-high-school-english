# 阅读理解（议论文） **Paper Books or E-books: Which Is Better for Teenagers?**

① A few years ago, a new student in a middle school class might proudly show his classmates a thick novel in his hand. Today, another student in the same seat may open a thin tablet and swipe through twenty books in one library card. **Paper books** and **e-books** both invite us into the same world of stories, poems, and knowledge. But for a middle school student, which one is really the better friend?

② Supporters of e-books list many strong points. A single tablet can hold **thousands of titles**, from ancient history to the latest young adult novels. E-books often cost **less than paper copies**, and some are even free. They save forest trees, and they are easy to carry on a crowded bus. For students with poor eyesight, the size of the letters can be changed with one finger. In a school where book bags already weigh five kilos, this "weightless library" sounds almost magical.

③ However, many teachers and readers still stand firmly with **paper books**. First, **paper respects the eyes**. A bright screen at night, hour after hour, tires the eyes and makes sleep harder. Second, paper helps the brain **remember better**. Some studies show that when students read long articles on paper, they recall more details than when they read the same text on a screen. Third, paper books invite **slow reading**. There is no notification popping up, no "Next Video" button — just you, the page, and the quiet sound of turning pages.

④ There are also special joys that paper gives and screens cannot. The **smell of a second-hand book** in a street shop, the neat underlines left by a careful reader years ago, the handwritten note from an aunt on the first page — these small things make each paper book a little treasure. A digital file, in contrast, looks the same on every device. It is clean, but it carries no footprints.

⑤ At the same time, we should not pretend that paper books are perfect. They are heavy, easy to lose, and sometimes expensive. When a student has to travel or share one book with a sick grandparent hundreds of kilometres away, an e-book is clearly more practical. So the truth is not that one is good and the other bad; **they are different tools for different jobs**.

⑥ In my opinion, the smart middle school student uses **both**. For a long novel, or for notes that you really want to remember, use paper — let your hand feel every word. For a trip, a quick check, or a huge library at low cost, use a tablet. Most importantly, do not choose a format just because it is "cool" or "old-fashioned". Choose the one that helps you **read more, think harder, and feel a little lighter** after you close the cover.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main question of the article?**

A. Should schools close all old libraries?  
B. Which is better for middle school students — paper books or e-books?  
C. How to write a bestseller at 14.  
D. Why are book bags so heavy?

**2. Which of the following is NOT given as an advantage of e-books?**

A. Holding thousands of titles in one tablet.  
B. Being usually cheaper than paper copies.  
C. Letters that can be resized for weaker eyes.  
D. Giving readers the smell of old paper.

**3. Why do some people still prefer paper books?**

A. Paper books are always colourful.  
B. Paper is easier on the eyes and helps students remember more.  
C. Paper books have hidden games on the back.  
D. Paper books cannot be borrowed from the library.

**4. What special charm does a paper book have, according to paragraph ④?**

A. Notes, underlines, and a smell that tell a small story.  
B. The ability to play videos.  
C. Automatic translation into twenty languages.  
D. A free song when you open the cover.

**5. What is the writer's final suggestion?**

A. Only buy paper books and never touch a tablet.  
B. Use only e-books because trees are important.  
C. Use both, choosing each tool for the right job.  
D. Give up reading and watch more films instead.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末问题  
【解析】全文讨论纸书 vs. 电子书。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段未提及纸的气味  
【解析】纸的气味是纸书特色。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "respects the eyes... remember better"  
【解析】护眼且有助记忆。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "smell... underlines... handwritten note"  
【解析】笔迹与气味是纸书独有。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥段 "the smart middle school student uses both"  
【解析】建议两者结合。故选 C。
