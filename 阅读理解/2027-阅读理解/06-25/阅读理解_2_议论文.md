# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle School Students Have Long Summer Holidays?**

Every summer, Chinese middle school students have about eight weeks of holiday. In some other countries, the summer break is even longer — up to twelve weeks. But in recent years, more and more parents and school experts ask: is such a long holiday really good for students? Some people say long summer holidays should be shortened. I do not agree. I believe long summer holidays are still **necessary**, as long as families plan them well.

First, long summer holidays give students a real rest. During the school year, middle school students wake up at 6:30, finish class at 17:30, do homework till 22:00 and still think about tests on the weekend. This pace is useful but tiring. A short one-week break cannot really refresh them. Only a longer holiday lets the brain and the body slow down, breathe, and come back ready for new challenges. After a restful summer, many students say they enjoy the first month of the new school year more.

Second, the summer is a rare chance for **off-school learning**. During the school year, classes and tests take almost all the time. In summer, a student can finally do the things the school has little time for: read a long novel, start learning the guitar, visit museums in another city, or join a small volunteer project at the community library. These experiences do not show in a test score, but they shape a person's interests, feelings and values for years. A long holiday is the ground where such experiences can grow.

Some people worry that long holidays make students forget what they have learned, especially in maths. There is a little truth here. Without any review, some knowledge does weaken. But the answer is **not** to cut the holiday; it is to add a small, fixed daily study plan — perhaps 60 – 90 minutes a day, not eight hours. With a short but regular review, the knowledge is safe, and the student still has plenty of time for real holiday.

Others say long holidays are "boring" for children whose parents have to work. This is a real worry, but again, the answer is better community and social support, not a shorter holiday. More public libraries open free reading corners in July and August; some parks run cheap sport classes; many cities now offer "summer companion" programmes for children of working families. When families, communities and schools work together, a long holiday can be full of simple, healthy activities — and it costs very little.

Finally, we should remember that not every lesson in life comes from a classroom. Spending long evenings talking with grandparents, helping with simple housework, watching a thunderstorm over the city, or lying on a summer lawn thinking of nothing — all of these are part of growing up. If we cut the summer holiday in half, we may get a few more test points. But we will lose a season that middle school students can **never** have back.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the author's main opinion?**

A. Summer holidays should be shortened because they are too long.  
B. Summer holidays are still necessary, as long as families plan them well.  
C. Summer holidays should be removed completely.  
D. Only top students should have long summer holidays.

**2. According to paragraph 2, why is a long summer holiday important for rest?**

A. Because students finally have time to play video games all day.  
B. Because a short one-week break cannot fully refresh students after a long school year.  
C. Because teachers also want many weeks off.  
D. Because hot weather makes study impossible.

**3. What kind of learning does the author say summer is good for?**

A. Only maths and science drills.  
B. Off-school learning such as reading, a musical instrument, museum visits, or volunteer work.  
C. Only the same subjects as school, but harder.  
D. Learning to drive a car.

**4. How does the author answer the worry that students forget knowledge?**

A. Shorten the holiday so forgetting is less.  
B. Stop all rest during summer.  
C. Add a short, fixed daily study plan of 60 – 90 minutes, without cutting the holiday.  
D. Hire a private teacher for every student.

**5. How does the author answer the worry that long holidays are "boring" for children of working parents?**

A. Such children should just stay home alone.  
B. The answer is better community support, such as free library corners, cheap sport classes and "summer companion" programmes.  
C. Their parents should change jobs.  
D. They should move to another city in July.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第 1 段末句  
【解析】支持长假，但需合理规划。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段  
【解析】一周假期不够恢复。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段  
【解析】非学科性学习。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段  
【解析】每日 60–90 分钟小复习。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段  
【解析】加强社区支持。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
