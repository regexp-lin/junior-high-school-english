# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Pair of Running Shoes My Brother Left Behind**

① My brother Hao is six years older than I am. Three summers ago, he went to university in Xi'an. The day he left, the house suddenly seemed too quiet. Mother cleaned his room slowly; Father sat on the sofa pretending to read a newspaper. At the corner of the shoe rack, I saw a pair of old running shoes — grey, with a bright orange line on each side. Hao had forgotten them.

② I did not care about those shoes at first. They were three sizes too big for me, and the orange line was starting to peel. I put them on the top shelf, behind a basket. For two years, I almost forgot they were there. Then, this spring, our school held a cross-country race. I signed up, although I had never run more than 2 kilometres without stopping. "You must practise," Father said. "Do you have proper running shoes?" I shook my head. Then my eyes went to the top shelf.

③ I climbed up and pulled the old shoes down. To my surprise, my feet had finally grown into them. They were still a little big, but with two pairs of thick socks they fit. I put them on and stepped outside. Every time my foot touched the ground, I thought of Hao — of his long legs, his wild laugh, and the way he used to jog past our window in these same shoes.

④ The next Saturday morning, I began practising. The first day I ran only 800 metres and had to stop, breathing hard. But something about the shoes made me keep trying. On the second week, I reached one kilometre without stopping. On the fourth week, I could run two kilometres slowly, as the sun came up over the quiet neighbourhood. Every evening, I cleaned the shoes with a wet cloth and put them neatly by the door.

⑤ The race was on a bright Saturday. Three hundred students lined up at the playground. When the gun went off, I was not the fastest, but I was not the slowest either. Near the last kilometre, my legs wanted to give up. I looked down at the orange lines — now slightly faded but still there — and I suddenly felt as if my brother was running next to me, tall and easy, just like three years before. I pushed on. I finished in the top thirty.

⑥ That night, I video-called Hao. I held the shoes up to the camera. He laughed, surprised. "So those were still alive!" he said. Then he grew quiet. "Do you know, Xiao Yu, I left them on purpose. I told myself: one day my little sister will need them." I was silent for a long moment. Some gifts, I realised, travel not in boxes, but through **time**. A pair of old running shoes, left in a corner, had been waiting patiently for the day a younger sister would be brave enough to run a race.

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer not use the running shoes for a long time?**

A. The writer did not like orange lines.  
B. The shoes were three sizes too big at first, and the writer put them on the top shelf.  
C. The shoes were broken.  
D. The shoes were hidden by Mother.

**2. Why did the writer finally take down the old shoes?**

A. Because Mother wanted to throw them away.  
B. Because the writer signed up for the school's cross-country race and needed proper running shoes.  
C. Because it was time to go on holiday.  
D. Because the writer wanted to give them to a friend.

**3. What did the writer think of while running in the shoes?**

A. Delicious food at home.  
B. Brother Hao's long legs, his wild laugh and the way he used to jog past the window in these shoes.  
C. The teacher's homework list.  
D. A computer game.

**4. How did the writer feel during the last kilometre of the race?**

A. Bored.  
B. The legs wanted to give up, but the writer felt as if Brother Hao was running next to her and pushed on.  
C. Completely unafraid from the beginning to the end.  
D. Angry with the teacher.

**5. What surprise did the writer learn at the end of the story?**

A. Hao had left the shoes on purpose, thinking that one day his younger sister would need them.  
B. Hao had lost the shoes long ago.  
C. Hao wanted the shoes back immediately.  
D. Hao did not remember the shoes at all.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "They were three sizes too big for me... I put them on the top shelf..."  
【解析】鞋太大被搁置。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段末 "our school held a cross-country race. I signed up..."  
【解析】参加越野赛需要鞋。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "I thought of Hao — of his long legs, his wild laugh..."  
【解析】想起哥哥。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "my legs wanted to give up... I suddenly felt as if my brother was running next to me..."  
【解析】想起哥哥并坚持。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题／推理判断题  
【定位】第⑥段 "I left them on purpose... one day my little sister will need them."  
【解析】哥哥故意留鞋。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

- **cross-country race** [n.] 越野赛  
- **jog** [v.] 慢跑
