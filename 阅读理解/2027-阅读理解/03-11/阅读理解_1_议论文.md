# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Schools Start Classes Later in the Morning?**

① At 6:30 on a cold February morning, Xiao Yu is walking to school in the dark. Her backpack is heavy, her eyes are still sleepy, and she has already eaten her breakfast in three big bites on the bus. Our school, like many in China, begins its first lesson at **7:30 a.m.** This year, a question has been printed in our school newspaper: **should schools start their morning classes a little later, for example at 8:30?**

② Those who say **yes** point first to sleep. Many scientists believe teenagers need **nine hours** of sleep each night, but most middle-school students get only six or seven. A later start, they say, can turn this around. More sleep means a clearer brain, a stronger heart and, in the long run, better marks. The school doctor adds that tired teens are also more likely to catch colds and feel nervous before tests.

③ A later start would also make the morning **safer**. In winter, the sun does not rise until nearly 7:20 in many northern cities. Thousands of students ride bikes or cross busy roads in the half dark. A 2024 report from the Road Safety Centre showed that nearly **40%** of teen road accidents happen between 6:30 and 7:30 a.m. Pushing the start time back could allow most students to travel after sunrise.

④ However, some parents and teachers disagree. Most mothers and fathers leave home before 8:00 to catch their own buses. If school begins at 8:30, who will make breakfast, close the door, and remind the children of their PE shoes? For many families, an early start means a shared family rhythm and less trouble in the morning.

⑤ Others worry about **study hours**. China's middle school day is already packed: Chinese, maths, English, physics, biology, history, PE and art. Moving the start from 7:30 to 8:30 means one less hour in the day, or one more hour pushed into the evening. "My son already sleeps at 10:40 p.m.," one father said. "Later school might push him to 11:40." The evening is not really free.

⑥ In my opinion, a **soft solution** is the best. Schools can keep 7:30 as the first "self-reading" time but make it truly **optional**, with no homework checked and no tests. The real first class can start at 8:00 or 8:15. Students who need more sleep can come later, while those who ride with working parents can arrive early and read quietly. A few more minutes of sleep will not destroy our grades, but they can truly protect our young eyes, our morning roads, and our happy mood for a new school day.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main question discussed in the passage?**

A. Why students must eat a big breakfast.  
B. Whether schools should start classes later in the morning.  
C. How students can cross the road safely.  
D. When winter days become longer in China.

**2. According to Paragraph 2, how many hours of sleep do scientists say teenagers need?**

A. Six hours.  
B. Seven hours.  
C. Eight hours.  
D. Nine hours.

**3. Why does the writer think a later start could make mornings safer?**

A. Parents will drive their children to school.  
B. Most students can travel to school after sunrise.  
C. Students will have time to learn the road rules.  
D. Schools will build new bridges over busy roads.

**4. What do some parents worry about in Paragraph 4?**

A. Their children will be late for the school bus.  
B. Their children may forget to bring PE shoes.  
C. They leave home before 8:00 and cannot take care of the morning routine.  
D. Schools will close their own libraries at 8:30.

**5. What is the writer's final suggestion?**

A. Schools should open at 6:00 a.m. all year.  
B. Schools should keep 7:30 but make it an optional self-reading time, with real classes starting later.  
C. Schools should remove breakfast from the morning.  
D. Students should study only in the evening.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 + 全文  
【解析】全文围绕"学校是否应推迟晨课"展开。A、C、D 偏题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第 2 句 "teenagers need nine hours of sleep each night"  
【解析】科学家建议 9 小时。其余数字均偏少。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段末句 "allow most students to travel after sunrise"  
【解析】推迟后多数学生在天亮后出行。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "leave home before 8:00... who will make breakfast..."  
【解析】家长担心自己上班出门早，无人照料孩子的晨间流程。A、B、D 均无依据。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "keep 7:30 as the first 'self-reading' time but make it truly optional... The real first class can start at 8:00 or 8:15"  
【解析】作者支持"7:30 自读（自愿）+ 8:00/8:15 正式上课"的折中方案。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

- **rhythm** [n.] 节奏
