# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Do Some Animals Sleep All Winter?**

In cold parts of China and other northern countries, winter brings two big problems for animals: there is **little food**, and the weather is **very cold**. Green leaves turn brown, insects disappear, many lakes freeze, and small animals need a lot more energy just to stay warm. Some animals, like the tiger, keep a thick coat and continue to hunt. But a surprising number of animals choose a very different way: they sleep. A deep sleep that lasts for weeks or months is called **hibernation**.

The most famous hibernator is the bear, but in fact many smaller animals are better examples — squirrels, marmots, bats, hedgehogs, and even some frogs. Before winter begins, these animals eat a lot. Some squirrels may eat twice their normal amount of food in autumn. This extra food is stored inside the body as **fat**. When winter arrives, the animal crawls into a safe place — a hole in the ground, a hollow tree, or a cave. Then its body starts a remarkable change.

During hibernation, the animal's **body temperature drops** by a lot. Some marmots' bodies fall from 37 °C to only 4 – 5 °C. The heart also slows down, from maybe 200 beats a minute to only 5 beats a minute. The breathing becomes so slow that an outside observer might not notice it. In this state, the animal uses very little energy — perhaps only one-tenth of its normal daily amount. The fat it stored in autumn now slowly feeds it through the long winter.

But hibernation is not a "deep sleep" in the way humans sleep at night. It is more like a long "**low-power mode**". Every few weeks, the animal's body wakes up for a short time. The heart beats more strongly, the body warms up, and the animal may move a little or even eat a few nuts it stored nearby. Then it goes back into deep rest. Scientists still do not fully understand why these short waking times are needed, but they think the body must check important systems, such as the immune system.

Not every cold-weather animal hibernates. Many birds fly south instead; this is called **migration**. Some insects lay eggs in autumn and die, leaving only the eggs to survive the winter. Fish do not hibernate in a real sense, but some move to deeper water where the temperature is steadier.

Climate change is now affecting hibernation. In some places, winters are shorter and warmer than 30 years ago. Bears sometimes wake up too early because they feel warm, but outside the cave, there is still not enough food. Scientists worry that if warm winters become normal, hibernating animals will have a harder life. Nature's clever winter trick was built for steady cold — not for a climate that keeps changing.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why do some animals hibernate in winter?**

A. Because they love sleeping more than eating.  
B. Because winter brings little food and very cold weather.  
C. Because they are afraid of human hunters.  
D. Because they are born to sleep for half a year.

**2. What does an animal do before winter begins?**

A. It stops eating to clean its body.  
B. It eats a lot and stores extra food as fat inside its body.  
C. It digs a tunnel across the forest.  
D. It hunts bigger animals for meat.

**3. What happens to a marmot's body temperature during hibernation?**

A. It stays about 37 °C, the same as normal.  
B. It rises to about 42 °C.  
C. It drops to only 4 – 5 °C.  
D. It changes from hour to hour.

**4. Why do hibernating animals sometimes wake up for a short time?**

A. Because they feel hungry and hunt a deer.  
B. Because they are about to migrate.  
C. Because the body may need to check important systems, such as the immune system.  
D. Because they hear loud noises from humans.

**5. What problem does climate change cause for hibernation?**

A. Bears and other animals may wake up too early, but find little food outside.  
B. All hibernating animals now sleep twice as long.  
C. Winters have suddenly become much colder.  
D. Animals migrate to the Arctic instead.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 1 段  
【解析】冬季食物少、天气冷。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段  
【解析】大量进食将能量以脂肪形式储存。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段 "from 37 °C to only 4 – 5 °C"  
【解析】体温大幅下降。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段末  
【解析】检查免疫等系统。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 6 段  
【解析】暖冬使动物过早苏醒却无食。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。
