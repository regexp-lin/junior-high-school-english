# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Schools Still Post Public Ranking Lists After Every Exam?**

① After almost every mid-term and final exam at our school, a long white paper used to appear on the wall by the office stairs. On it were printed the names of the top 50 students of the grade, from rank 1 to rank 50, together with their total scores. Students would stop, read, whisper, laugh, or leave quietly. Last term, our new head teacher decided to take the list down. She said, "Let us stop one unfair habit this year." Some parents are not happy; they say ranking lists are a fair, old Chinese tradition. So, **should schools still post public ranking lists after exams?** My opinion is: we should not.

② Supporters of the list usually give three reasons. First, they say that a ranking list **pushes students to work harder**: nobody wants to fall from rank 10 to rank 30, so students study more. Second, a ranking list is **clear and simple** — everyone can see with their own eyes who did well and who did not. Third, they argue that life itself is full of ranking lists — university entrance, job hunting, even games — so middle-school students should get used to them early.

③ These points sound reasonable, but I believe each one has a darker side.

④ First, ranking does push some students to work harder — but often in the **wrong way**. Some classmates say they only care about "beating the person one place above me", not about really understanding the subject. I have seen good students refuse to explain a maths problem to a weaker classmate, because they were afraid of "losing their rank". A list that changes friendship into small, quiet competition is not a healthy list.

⑤ Second, a ranking list may be "clear and simple", but this simplicity hides too much. A student who is 45th in the list may have a painful family situation at home, or a broken arm the week before, or a sleeping problem; a student who is 5th may have had two years of very expensive private tutors. Printing a public list treats all these very different lives as if they were only one number. The "clear" picture is, in fact, often unfair.

⑥ Third, "life has ranking lists" is a half-truth. Yes, life has some selection moments. But most of life is **not** a ranking list: whether you are a kind friend, a patient son, a careful colleague, cannot be printed on a wall. If schools train students to care only about being "rank 1", they are really preparing students for only a small corner of life.

⑦ There is also a quieter harm that is rarely discussed: the harm to those students whose names **never appear** on the list. In a grade of 500, a list of top 50 leaves 90% of the students as silent "others". Many of those 450 students work hard too, improve quietly, help their classmates, and take care of tired parents at home. A public list often tells them, week after week, "Your work is not worth printing." This message, when repeated for three years, can hurt a young mind in ways we cannot easily see.

⑧ To be fair, I am not saying teachers should hide all information. Students and parents deserve to know their own scores, strong subjects and weak ones. Teachers can share detailed **private reports** with each family — this keeps the useful information without the public shame. If a school still wants some "star" moments, it could honour many different things: the **most improved** student, the **most helpful** student in group work, the best team in a science fair, the best reading log, not only the highest total score.

⑨ So, should schools still post public ranking lists? My answer is: no, please take them down. A good school should be a place where children feel safe to try, to fail, and to grow. Replacing the long white paper on the wall with private, honest talks between teachers and each family does not make students lazy — it makes education a little more human.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Schools should print even longer ranking lists, for every subject.  
B. Schools should no longer post public ranking lists after exams.  
C. Only Grade 9 students should see ranking lists.  
D. Students should rank each other every week by themselves.

**2. According to the passage, which is **NOT** one of the reasons given by supporters of ranking lists?**

A. Ranking pushes students to work harder.  
B. A ranking list is clear and simple to read.  
C. Ranking lists help students to make more close friends.  
D. Life itself has many ranking lists, so students should get used to them.

**3. Why does the writer think ranking can push students in the "wrong way"?**

A. Because the paper used for the list is wasted and bad for the environment.  
B. Because some students only care about beating one classmate and even refuse to help weaker friends, turning friendship into small competition.  
C. Because ranking makes students stop eating breakfast.  
D. Because the writing on the list is often wrong.

**4. What "quieter harm" does the writer discuss in paragraph ⑦?**

A. The harm to the top student's handwriting.  
B. The harm done to the 90% of students whose names never appear; the list silently tells them again and again that their work is not worth printing.  
C. The harm to the paper factory workers.  
D. The harm caused by the bell ringing in the corridor.

**5. Which practice would the writer most likely support?**

A. Printing a new ranking list every Monday morning.  
B. Sharing private, detailed reports with each family, and honouring different kinds of students — most improved, most helpful, best team, best reading log.  
C. Cancelling all school exams forever.  
D. Giving the top student all the prizes and ignoring the others.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "we should not" & 第⑨段 "please take them down"  
【解析】作者立场明确。A、C、D 均与立场相反或不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "three reasons"  
【解析】文中列举的三点是督促、清晰、适应人生；并未提及"帮助交到更多好朋友"。A、B、D 均为文中所述。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "only care about 'beating the person one place above me' ... refuse to explain a maths problem"  
【解析】原文直接叙述。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "a list of top 50 leaves 90% of the students as silent 'others' ... 'Your work is not worth printing'"  
【解析】原文明确指出对 90% 未入榜学生的伤害。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段 "Teachers can share detailed private reports ... honour many different things"  
【解析】作者主张私下报告加多元奖项。A 与作者主张相反；C 属过度推断；D 与立场相反。故选 B。
