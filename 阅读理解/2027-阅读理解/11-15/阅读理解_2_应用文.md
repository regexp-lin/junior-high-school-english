# 阅读理解（应用文） **Winter Energy-Saving Week — "One Week, A Thousand Small Switches"**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Student Union + General Affairs Office**  
> **2027 Winter Energy-Saving Week: Mon 24 Nov – Fri 28 Nov 2027**  
> **Theme: "One Week, A Thousand Small Switches."**  
> **Nothing big. Nothing loud. Just a lot of small ones.**

Every winter the school's electricity and hot-water bills rise sharply — partly because we do need more warmth and light, partly because old habits stay with us from October. Last winter, our electricity use in December was **42% higher** than in October. We cannot stop the cold weather; but we can, together, save a thousand small watts. From **Monday 24 November**, Lihua will hold its seventh Winter Energy-Saving Week.

---

## 🔆 Six Quiet Rules for the Week

1. **"Last Out, Lights Off."** The last student leaving a classroom or lab switches off lights, fans, and the projector. A small green sticker is already on every door frame as a reminder.  
2. **"One Window Only."** During the morning break, open one window, not four; cross-winds waste more heat than they refresh.  
3. **"Warm Water, Not Hot."** The corridor hot-water tank will be set to **60 °C** instead of 90 °C. Still safe for tea, less wasteful.  
4. **"Seven-Minute Shower"** in the PE changing room (a small timer is by the door).  
5. **"Stairs for 1–3 Floors."** Use the lift only when going to the 4th floor or higher, or when carrying something heavy. (Teachers and the school nurse may use the lift any time.)  
6. **"Device Sleep at 18:00."** All office computers and classroom screens enter sleep mode automatically. Students who stay late for study should ask the form teacher for a key.

---

## 📚 Four Small Activities

**Activity A — Class Meter Reading (all classes, Mon & Fri).**  
At 07:55 on Monday and 17:00 on Friday, two students from each class read the **small electricity meter** outside their classroom and fill in the form on the door. The class with the **lowest increase** from morning to Friday afternoon will win the "**Green Classroom**" flag for December.

**Activity B — Long-Sleeve Tuesday (Tue 25 Nov).**  
Instead of turning up the heater, wear **one more warm layer** on Tuesday. We save 2 °C worth of electricity across the whole school if 80% of us wear a light sweater or thermal undershirt.

**Activity C — Photo Wall "Yes or No?" (Wed 26 Nov).**  
The Student Union will put up a photo wall outside the library. Each photo shows a small winter moment (a running tap, a lamp left on, a half-open window, a phone charger in an empty plug). Students vote with a small red or green sticker: **"wasteful or wise?"** Voting teaches seeing.

**Activity D — Family Letter (any day this week).**  
Every student writes one short letter to take home on Thursday 27 Nov. The letter may share **one small idea** a family could try that evening — unplugging a phone charger, using a dust-cover on the TV, or lowering the night heater by 1 °C.

---

## 🎒 What Students Should NOT Do

- **Do not** turn off the heater in another classroom that is still being used — leave it to that class.  
- **Do not** cover the electricity meter or climb on a chair to reach it.  
- **Do not** unplug the lab equipment that has the yellow "**Do-Not-Unplug**" label.  
- **Do not** open classroom windows in the early morning before the temperature inside warms up — it wastes more than it helps.  
- **Do not** shame any classmate who forgets a rule. We are building a habit, not a fear.

---

## 🏁 Closing Ceremony (Fri 28 Nov, 16:20, Sports Hall)

- The "Green Classroom" flag will be given to the class with the lowest energy increase.  
- Four student volunteers will read their **family letter** aloud, in both Chinese and English.  
- The PE Department will announce the **total electricity saved** in kWh, and the General Affairs Office will tell us how many **small trees** that amount of electricity could have planted.  
- All students take away a small paper bookmark printed with the six quiet rules — to stick inside the cover of their homework book.

---

- **6 photo-wall helpers** (cut photos, print small red/green stickers, tidy votes).  
- **4 letter writers** (help younger students phrase their family letters in clear Chinese-English).  
- **4 sports-hall set-up helpers** for the closing ceremony.  
- **6 corridor cheerers** who greet classmates at the morning door with "Have you switched off the light?"

Sign-up form: outside Room 112, by **Thu 20 November, 17:30**.
