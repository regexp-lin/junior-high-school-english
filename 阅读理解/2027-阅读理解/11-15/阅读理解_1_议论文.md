# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Every Middle-School Student Carry a Reusable Cup to School?**

① Outside the Room 203 corner shop of our school, a small black bin sits under the window. At 10:00 every morning, when the mid-morning break ends, it is already **three-quarters full** of used paper cups — hot chocolate cups, milk-tea cups, tall coffee cups, small hot-water cups. By the end of a single school week, the head cleaner told us last Friday, this **single bin** holds more than **800 cups**. Multiply that by the 32 weeks of the school year and by the **thirty middle schools** in our city, and the number becomes almost frightening. My view, after watching that bin for many mornings, is that **every middle-school student should carry one reusable cup to school from Grade 7 onwards**, and that our school should quietly stop handing out disposable paper cups at the shop.

② The first reason is **a clear environmental one**. A single paper cup, because it has a thin plastic lining inside, is **very hard to recycle** — most waste plants simply treat it as "mixed rubbish" and send it to landfill or to a burning plant. Each cup takes up to **20 years** to break down in the ground, releasing a small amount of methane as it does. 800 cups a week, from one corner shop alone, means roughly **400 kilograms of waste a year**, most of which does not ever truly disappear. A single reusable cup in a student's bag can replace, over two middle-school years, around **400 such cups**.

③ The second reason is **safer drinking**. The plastic lining on cheap paper cups, when it meets very hot water, can release **small amounts of chemicals** into the drink. Most cups sold to schools meet the national safety standard, and the amount released is below the legal limit; but "below limit" is not "zero". A reusable cup — glass, thick food-grade plastic, or food-grade stainless steel — does not have that lining. In a city where many of us already drink six to eight hot drinks a week at school, a small reduction in chemical exposure, over seven middle-school years, is not a small gift.

④ The third reason is **money in the pocket of a family**. A single hot chocolate in a paper cup costs **5 yuan** at our school. The same hot chocolate, made at home in the morning and carried in a good thermos, costs about **1.5 yuan** per cup. The cost of a good reusable cup is about **60 yuan** and it usually lasts for **more than three years**. A simple sum: ignoring the cup, the family saves roughly **3.5 yuan** a day, about **70 yuan** a school month. For most of our families, that is not nothing. For some of our classmates, it is a lot.

⑤ Some worry, "It is a small burden to wash the cup each evening." Yes, it is a small task — but only a small one. A one-minute rinse with warm water and a little dish soap is enough. Compared with washing our school uniform, tidying our desk, or feeding a family cat, washing a single cup is among the easiest habits a middle-school student can build. Families can even make it a **family habit**: every member rinses their own cup before bed.

⑥ Others say, "Sometimes I forget the cup at home." This is true; habits take time to grow. The school's answer does not need to be a punishment. Instead, the corner shop can keep a small "**emergency paper-cup supply**" for 1 yuan extra per cup, and the extra money can go to the school's small-tree fund. No student would be refused a drink. But the price difference would quietly push most of us to remember the cup next morning.

⑦ A third worry is **cleanliness**. Some students think a reusable cup, carried in a bag all day, is **dirtier than a single-use paper cup**. This is a fair concern. The answer lies in the **quality of the cup**: a sealed lid, a wide mouth (so the bottom can be washed easily), and a soft brush at home. Classroom science labs long ago reported that a well-washed reusable cup carries **fewer bacteria** than the rim of a paper cup that has been touched by many hands in the shop.

⑧ Some people argue, "This is a tiny problem compared with factories and cars." I understand the feeling. But a middle-school student does not yet drive a car. She cannot change a steel mill. She can, however, **say "no, thank you"** to a small paper cup at 09:35 on a Tuesday morning, seventy times a year. Eighty million Chinese middle-school students, together, could keep **32 billion paper cups** out of the ground every year — enough cups, laid end to end, to go **around the Earth more than 100 times**. Small actions, added together, are not small. They are the whole reason our country can change at all.

⑨ This Monday morning I shall take my green bamboo-fibre cup to school. It is not a fancy brand; Mum bought it for 45 yuan from our corner supermarket three years ago. But it holds 350 ml of warm water, it has travelled with me through two winters, and it reminds me, every time I fill it, that I have quietly chosen **one less paper cup in the black bin**. That is not a hero's story. It is simply a student's morning. **A student's morning, multiplied, is what a school's year looks like in the end.**

---

**注释：** reusable 可重复使用的；disposable 一次性的；recycle 回收利用；thermos 保温杯；bacteria 细菌

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Students should drink only water at school.  
B. Every middle-school student should carry one reusable cup to school from Grade 7 onwards; schools should quietly stop handing out disposable paper cups at the shop.  
C. The school should open more paper-cup shops.  
D. Students should never drink hot chocolate.

**2. Why are paper cups at the school shop hard to recycle, according to Paragraph ②?**

A. Because they are too colourful.  
B. Because they are too small.  
C. Because they have a thin plastic lining inside; most waste plants simply treat them as mixed rubbish and send them to landfill or a burning plant — a single paper cup can take up to 20 years to break down, releasing a small amount of methane.  
D. Because students crumple them too much.

**3. How does the writer answer the worry "It is a small burden to wash the cup each evening"?**

A. By saying the cup should be thrown away.  
B. By admitting it is a task, but only a small one — a one-minute rinse with warm water and a little dish soap is enough; compared with washing a uniform, tidying a desk, or feeding a family cat, it is among the easiest habits, and it can become a family habit.  
C. By saying washing is forbidden.  
D. By asking parents to wash the cup.

**4. What does the writer suggest about students who sometimes forget their cups?**

A. The shop should refuse to sell them any drink.  
B. The shop can keep a small emergency paper-cup supply for 1 yuan extra per cup; the extra money goes to the school's small-tree fund; no student is refused a drink, but the price difference quietly pushes most students to remember the cup next day.  
C. They should be fined 10 yuan by the teacher.  
D. They should not drink at school.

**5. What is the main idea of the last two paragraphs?**

A. One student's action is too small to matter.  
B. Small actions, added together, are not small — they are the whole reason a country can change at all; a student's morning, multiplied, is what a school's year looks like in the end.  
C. Only adults can save the environment.  
D. Paper cups are not a real problem.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末立论句  
【解析】核心主张直引。A、C、D 均偏。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "thin plastic lining ... mixed rubbish ... up to 20 years ... methane"  
【解析】回收困难原因直引。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段  
【解析】反驳思路直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "1 yuan extra ... small-tree fund"  
【解析】温和推力机制直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧、⑨段结语  
【解析】行为累积伦理。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。
