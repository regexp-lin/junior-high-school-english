# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Stray Dog in the Rain**

① The rain started falling at the end of school. I stood under the red iron gate, holding my bag over my head, wishing Mom would come earlier than usual. At that moment, I saw a small brown dog pressed against the stone wall. Its thin body shook with cold, and one back leg was lifted off the ground. A few older boys were laughing and throwing small stones at it. I felt something hot rise inside my chest.

② I ran over, waved my arms and shouted, "Stop it!" The boys stared at me for a second and then walked away, grumbling. The little dog pulled itself deeper into the corner, trying to disappear into the wet stones. Its eyes were wet — not from the rain, I thought, but from a deeper fear. I slowly opened my bag, took out half of my cold bread, and placed it gently on the ground.

③ At first, the dog did not move. Its nose worked carefully through the rainy air. After a long minute, it took a tiny step forward and ate the bread in two bites. I waited until it finished, and then I softly said, "Little brown. You're safe now." My voice sounded strange to myself, slow and warm, like my mother's voice in winter.

④ Just then, Mom arrived under a big black umbrella. She was going to scold me for being wet from head to toe, but one look at the dog stopped her. Mom bent down, touched the dog's head, and said quietly, "Her leg is hurt. She needs a doctor." She took off her pink scarf and wrapped the little body. Together, we carried her to a small animal clinic down the street. The doctor said the leg was not broken, only twisted, and gave us medicine and a warm bag.

⑤ That night, we made a small bed out of an old box and a soft towel. "We'll find her owner," Mom said. "If no one comes in two weeks, she stays with us." For a week, we put up posters on every corner, but nobody came. On the eighth day, Mom simply smiled, bought a new red dog-bowl and said, "Welcome home, Little Brown."

⑥ Two years later, Little Brown has grown into a lively dog with a shiny coat. Every rainy afternoon, she waits for me at the red iron gate, her tail moving like a small flag. I often wonder: did I rescue her that rainy day, or did she also rescue the kinder part of me? Some answers, perhaps, do not need to be said aloud.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did the writer feel when he/she saw the boys throwing stones?**

A. Calm and patient.  
B. Angry and wanted to stop them.  
C. Afraid and wanted to run away.  
D. Bored and wanted to watch.

**2. What did the writer do first after the boys left?**

A. Called the police on a phone.  
B. Took the dog home at once.  
C. Gave the dog half of his/her bread.  
D. Laughed at the dog.

**3. Why did Mom take off her pink scarf?**

A. She felt too hot in the rain.  
B. She wanted to clean the stones.  
C. She wrapped the injured dog with it.  
D. She wanted to give it to the boys.

**4. What did the family decide after eight days of no owner?**

A. They would give Little Brown to the school.  
B. They would keep her and welcome her home.  
C. They would sell her to the clinic.  
D. They would leave her in a park.

**5. What does the writer think about Little Brown now?**

A. She is only a burden for the family.  
B. She may have rescued the kinder part of the writer.  
C. She is the noisiest dog in the street.  
D. She is not as lovely as other dogs.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第①段 "I felt something hot rise inside my chest"  
【解析】作者心生怒火。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "placed it gently on the ground"  
【解析】先分一半面包给狗。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "took off her pink scarf and wrapped the little body"  
【解析】用围巾裹住受伤的小狗。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Welcome home, Little Brown."  
【解析】一家决定收养。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段 "she also rescue the kinder part of me"  
【解析】作者觉得小狗也拯救了自己善良的一面。故选 B。
