# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Teenagers Keep a Nature Journal?**

① Many middle school students today record their daily lives with photos and short videos. One tap, and a colourful memory is saved on the phone. Yet there is an older and much quieter habit that I think every teenager should try at least once — **keeping a nature journal**: a simple paper notebook where you write, draw and note down what you see in the natural world each week. Is this old idea still useful in 2027? I believe it is more useful than ever.

② First, a nature journal teaches us to **slow down and look closely**. To draw a willow leaf, you must sit for five quiet minutes and notice its shape, colour, veins and tiny insect marks. To describe the sound of cicadas, you must listen carefully for a few seconds. This slow looking is now rare. Busy days and fast screens make our eyes jump every few seconds. A journal invites them to rest on one small thing and really see it.

③ Second, a nature journal builds a strong bond with our **local environment**. A girl in Chengdu might draw the same tree by her front gate every month. After one year, her notebook tells a quiet story: pale buds in March, full green leaves in June, golden colours in October, bare branches in January. She no longer sees "a tree" — she sees **her** tree. People protect what they know, and a journal is a very friendly way to start knowing.

④ Third, a nature journal makes us better **thinkers and writers**. Teachers of both science and Chinese often say that students who regularly keep nature notes ask sharper questions and use richer, more accurate words in their essays. "The wind is strong" becomes "The wind moves the willow leaves into narrow silver lines." This is not empty decoration. It is the real power of language, trained by real observation.

⑤ Some students worry that they cannot draw well, or that they have no time. These worries are easy to answer. A nature journal is not an art show — stick figures, simple sentences, rough shapes and dates are perfectly fine. Ten **minutes a week** is already enough, maybe during the weekend on a bench in the schoolyard, in a park, or even on a rainy balcony watching drops and sparrows.

⑥ A phone camera can capture a moment; a nature journal captures an eye, a mind and a question, all together. At a time when many young people feel tired of their screens, a paper notebook with a few bird feathers pressed inside may be one of the cheapest and most healing presents we can give ourselves. I strongly believe every teenager should start one — even a small one — and see what silent wonders his or her notebook begins to collect.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main question?**

A. Whether students should use more social media.  
B. Whether teenagers should keep a nature journal.  
C. Whether parents should ban drawing in school.  
D. Whether teachers should give less homework.

**2. Why does drawing a willow leaf help students according to paragraph ②?**

A. Because they will earn extra money for drawing.  
B. Because it forces them to slow down and look closely at the leaf.  
C. Because leaves bring good luck.  
D. Because drawing a leaf is required in every exam.

**3. What story does the girl's notebook tell after one year?**

A. The same tree through different seasons: buds, green leaves, golden colours, bare branches.  
B. The story of twelve different countries.  
C. The names of all her classmates.  
D. A list of her favourite snacks.

**4. What do teachers say about students who keep nature notes?**

A. They become bored with reading.  
B. They ask sharper questions and use richer, more accurate words.  
C. They stop talking to their friends.  
D. They write essays with no feelings.

**5. What does the writer reply to students who say "I cannot draw well"?**

A. They should take expensive drawing classes first.  
B. A nature journal is not an art show — stick figures, simple sentences and rough shapes are fine.  
C. They should write in English only.  
D. They should give up the idea.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "Is this old idea still useful in 2027? I believe it is more useful than ever."  
【解析】核心讨论青少年是否应坚持自然笔记。A/C/D 均非本文问题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "sit for five quiet minutes and notice its shape, colour, veins..."  
【解析】画叶子迫使人慢下来细看。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "pale buds in March, full green leaves in June, golden colours in October, bare branches in January."  
【解析】笔记记录同一棵树在四季的变化。B/C/D 均不符。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "students who regularly keep nature notes ask sharper questions and use richer, more accurate words..."  
【解析】常写自然笔记的学生提问更敏锐、用词更丰富。A/C/D 均与原文相反或无据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "A nature journal is not an art show — stick figures, simple sentences..."  
【解析】作者回答：自然笔记不是画展，简笔也可。A/C/D 均不符。故选 B。
