# 阅读理解（议论文） **Are Online Lessons as Good as Classroom Lessons?**

① In recent years, online lessons have become a normal part of school life. Students can sit at home in their pyjamas and join a math class by a few simple clicks. Some parents and students praise this new way of learning. Others, however, believe that a computer screen can never replace a real classroom. So which side has more reason to stand on?

② Those who love online lessons often point to **convenience**. On rainy days, they do not need to leave the house. Students in small villages can listen to the best teachers in big cities. If they miss a point, they can simply replay the recording. One Grade 8 boy from Guizhou said last year, "Online English class is my window to a wider world."

③ These are real advantages, but the classroom offers something a screen cannot easily give: **real people and real feelings**. In a classroom, when a shy student gives a wrong answer, the teacher sees his red face and adds a soft smile. Classmates laugh, cheer and help each other during small group work. Such warm moments live in the air between people, not inside a camera lens.

④ A classroom also teaches students to **listen with their whole body**. Sitting in a quiet room for 40 minutes, back straight, eyes on the blackboard, is itself a kind of practice. At home, a student may check a phone every three minutes, lean lazily on a sofa, or even eat snacks behind the screen. After half a year of such habits, his attention becomes short and his body restless — problems that may stay long after the online course ends.

⑤ Research supports these worries. In 2024, a study from Beijing Normal University followed 500 Grade 8 students for a full year. Those who took most of their lessons online scored **12 percent lower** on their final English reading test than those who studied in a regular classroom. The study also found that fully online students had **fewer close friends** and said they felt lonely more often.

⑥ Of course, online lessons do have their place. Short extra classes, make-up lessons for sick days, and live talks with foreign students are all bright gifts of the internet. Still, when we ask whether online can fully **replace** the classroom, the honest answer is "not yet." A good school is not only a building where knowledge is poured in — it is a living garden where minds, eyes and hands grow together.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main question in this passage?**

A. Whether students should use more computers at home.  
B. Whether online lessons are as good as classroom lessons.  
C. Whether schools should be closed in winter.  
D. Whether parents should learn English with their children.

**2. According to Paragraph 2, one advantage of online lessons is ____.**

A. cheaper textbooks  
B. convenience and access to good teachers  
C. more homework each day  
D. longer summer holidays

**3. What does Paragraph 3 mainly say about a real classroom?**

A. It has more comfortable chairs.  
B. It offers real people and real feelings that a screen cannot give.  
C. It has better internet than home.  
D. It is closer to the students' houses.

**4. What did the 2024 Beijing Normal University study find?**

A. Online students scored 12 percent lower on a final English reading test.  
B. Online students had more close friends than others.  
C. Online students could sleep 20 minutes longer.  
D. Classroom students could not focus at all.

**5. What is the writer's final opinion?**

A. Online lessons should completely replace classroom lessons.  
B. Online lessons should be banned by every school.  
C. Online lessons are useful, but cannot fully replace the classroom.  
D. Online lessons should be used only in summer holidays.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段最后一句  
【解析】围绕"网课是否能媲美课堂"展开。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "convenience... best teachers in big cities"  
【解析】方便且能接触名师。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】段落主旨题  
【定位】第③段 "real people and real feelings"  
【解析】真实的人与情感是课堂优势。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "scored 12 percent lower on their final English reading test"  
【解析】网课生成绩低 12%。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "not yet... cannot fully replace"  
【解析】作者承认网课有用但不能完全替代。故选 C。
