# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Letter Between Page 78 and 79**

① Our school library sits on the second floor of the old west building. It is a quiet place with brown wooden shelves and a soft yellow lamp at each table. Most students go there for maths textbooks or popular novels. I go there for something else: I love old, slightly dusty books that nobody has opened for many years. Last spring, I found something I will never forget inside one of them.

② It was a rainy Wednesday afternoon. After my English class, I went up to the "**Donated Books**" shelf near the window. These are books given to the library by old students many years ago, and some of them are older than my mother. I picked out a small green book called *Stories of the Small Town*. The cover was soft with age, and the pages smelled a little like tea leaves.

③ I carried the book to a quiet corner and opened it. Between page 78 and page 79, a small, folded piece of paper slowly fell out onto my lap. At first I thought it was a bookmark, but when I picked it up, I saw handwriting in blue ink. It was a short letter, written in careful Chinese:

> "Dear future reader,  
> If you find this letter, please write back. I am Lin Xi, Grade 8. Today my father has moved our family to another city, and I am sad to leave this school. I hope somebody in the future will still remember this little town.  
> — June 20, 1998"

④ I read it three times. Then I turned the paper over. On the back there was a small line with a tiny school address and a phone number. The paper was almost thirty years old. I wondered, with a strange feeling in my heart: had anyone ever answered this letter? Where was Lin Xi now? Was she still alive?

⑤ I went home and told my grandmother. Her eyes grew bright. "Lin Xi... that name is familiar," she said slowly. After searching in an old red notebook on her shelf, she suddenly laughed softly. "Look — your Auntie Mei lived two doors away from a girl named Lin Xi when they were young. She now works in Hangzhou, as a doctor."

⑥ My father helped me send a short email through an old classmates' group. Two weeks later, a gentle reply came from Dr. Lin. She wrote, "I cannot believe it. I was only thirteen when I wrote that letter. I kept hoping, but no one ever found it. Thank you for writing back — thirty years late, but right on time."

⑦ Last month Dr. Lin came back to our town. She visited the library, stood by the same window, and held the little green book in her hands for a long time. When she saw the old shelf and the yellow lamp, she whispered, "The room is the same, only I have grown older." Then she turned to me and smiled, "You know, I wrote that letter without hoping very much. It is strange — sometimes the things we do with small hope travel furthest in time."

⑧ That night, at my own desk, I folded a new letter, slipped it into another old library book, and wrote on top: "*Dear future reader*…". I did not know who will find it. I only knew one thing now: **some quiet words, written on a rainy afternoon, can wait thirty years for a patient reply**.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where did the writer find the old letter?**

A. In the school rubbish bin.  
B. Inside an old donated book on the library's "Donated Books" shelf, between pages 78 and 79.  
C. In his father's bookshop downtown.  
D. Under his classroom desk.

**2. Who wrote the old letter, and when?**

A. A teacher, on June 20, 2017.  
B. A Grade 8 girl named Lin Xi, on June 20, 1998.  
C. The librarian, only two weeks before the writer found it.  
D. The writer's grandmother, many years ago.

**3. How did the writer finally find out who Lin Xi was?**

A. He saw her photo in a newspaper.  
B. He asked his grandmother, who remembered the name; Auntie Mei had lived near Lin Xi.  
C. The police helped him to look for her.  
D. He used a special computer program at school.

**4. What did Dr. Lin say when she came back to the library?**

A. "This place is completely different from what I remember."  
B. "The room is the same, only I have grown older."  
C. "Please give me back the book; it belongs to me."  
D. "I don't remember writing any letter."

**5. What is the main idea that the writer wants to share at the end of the story?**

A. Students should stop reading old books.  
B. Some quiet words, written with small hope, can wait thirty years and still find a patient reply.  
C. Everyone should keep a diary only on rainy days.  
D. Old libraries are useless now that we have the internet.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Donated Books shelf" & 第③段 "Between page 78 and page 79, a small, folded piece of paper slowly fell out"  
【解析】原文清晰说明信在"捐赠图书"架的某本旧书 78、79 页间。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段信内容 "I am Lin Xi, Grade 8 ... June 20, 1998"  
【解析】信末直接署名和日期。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "I went home and told my grandmother ... Auntie Mei lived two doors away from a girl named Lin Xi"  
【解析】是通过奶奶及姨母线索查到。A、C、D 均无文中依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "The room is the same, only I have grown older"  
【解析】原文直接引用。A、C、D 均与原文相反或不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧段末 "some quiet words, written on a rainy afternoon, can wait thirty years for a patient reply"  
【解析】作者结尾升华：带着小小期望写下的话可以穿越时间被回应。A、C、D 均与作者立场不符。故选 B。
