# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Teenagers Buy Fewer New Clothes Each Year?**

① A quick look at any popular short-video app will show you one common scene: a young girl or boy opening a delivery box, pulling out a new jacket, a new pair of shoes, a new hat, and then smiling brightly for the camera. This is called **"haul video" culture**. In many middle school circles, "What new clothes did you get this week?" has slowly become a normal greeting. But should teenagers really buy a new piece of clothing every week? I think it is time to slow down.

② First, many teenagers do not realise how much **water and energy** a simple T-shirt costs. Scientists say that producing one cotton T-shirt uses about 2,700 litres of water — as much as a person drinks in two and a half years. Jeans cost even more. If every student in China buys a few "fast fashion" T-shirts each season, the pressure on rivers, lakes and electricity plants becomes very real, even if we cannot see it from our shopping bag.

③ Second, buying too often can also hurt our own minds. A student who keeps comparing clothes with classmates may slowly lose **the joy of one good outfit**. Happiness becomes a short flash at the moment of opening the box, then quickly fades and must be refilled by a new order. Many young people say they feel "not enough" after five minutes of trying on a new dress — an empty feeling that no delivery can really fix.

④ Third, we easily forget that our **old clothes can still have many stories**. A school hoodie carries the memory of a class photo; a sweater from Grandma carries her warm hands; a pair of trainers remembers a winning basketball game. Throwing away clothes that are still good — just because they are "from last year" — is a very fast way to lose small, quiet treasures in our drawers.

⑤ Of course, I do not mean that teenagers should never buy new clothes. Bodies grow, shoes wear out, and weather changes. A smarter idea is the **"plus-one, minus-one" rule**: when you buy one new piece of clothing, pass on an old piece that still fits to a younger cousin, a classmate in need, or a second-hand shop. In this way, shopping becomes a kind of circle, not a stream of waste.

⑥ In the end, clothes are not only soft pieces of cloth. They tell other people who we are, and — more importantly — they tell us how we feel about ourselves. A teenager who buys fewer but more careful pieces builds a quieter, stronger sense of style. That kind of confidence is one thing no delivery box can carry. Let us open fewer boxes, enjoy the clothes we already have, and spend our pocket money on things that truly grow us up.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What does "haul video" culture describe?**

A. Videos of students doing homework together.  
B. Videos of young people opening delivery boxes of new clothes and showing them off.  
C. Videos of teachers buying books online.  
D. Videos about how to fix old clothes.

**2. Why does the writer talk about 2,700 litres of water in paragraph ②?**

A. To show that everyone should drink more water.  
B. To show how much water is needed to make just one cotton T-shirt.  
C. To advertise a bottle of water.  
D. To complain that tap water is too expensive.

**3. According to paragraph ③, what may buying too often do to teenagers' minds?**

A. Make them feel proud of every outfit forever.  
B. Slowly reduce the joy of one good outfit and create an "empty" feeling.  
C. Make them better at maths.  
D. Help them sleep longer.

**4. What is the "plus-one, minus-one" rule?**

A. Buy two new pieces and throw away one.  
B. When you buy one new piece, pass on one old but still usable piece to someone in need.  
C. Buy only shoes and throw away all tops.  
D. Never buy clothes online.

**5. What is the writer's MAIN view?**

A. Teenagers should never buy any new clothes.  
B. Teenagers should buy fewer but more thoughtful clothes, and pass on what they no longer need.  
C. Schools should give every student free clothes.  
D. Delivery boxes should be made bigger.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义猜测题  
【定位】第①段 "opening a delivery box, pulling out a new jacket, a new pair of shoes..."  
【解析】由上下文可知"haul video"为晒快递/晒新衣类视频。A/C/D 均与原文场景不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "producing one cotton T-shirt uses about 2,700 litres of water..."  
【解析】用数字说明一件棉 T 恤的水耗。A/C/D 均属偏题。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "the joy of one good outfit... an empty feeling..."  
【解析】频繁买会让人对一件衣服的满足感减弱并产生"空虚感"。A/C/D 与原文不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "when you buy one new piece of clothing, pass on an old piece that still fits..."  
【解析】买一件、送出一件仍可穿的旧衣。A/C/D 均不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳/态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "open fewer boxes, enjoy the clothes we already have..."  
【解析】作者主张少而精、并将旧衣传递出去。A 完全禁止、C 学校发衣、D 快递盒变大均偏题。故选 B。
