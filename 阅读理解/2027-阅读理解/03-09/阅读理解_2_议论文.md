# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle-School Students Wear Makeup to School?**

① Walk out of any popular short-video app in the evening, and you will see many teen faces with careful eyeliner, bright lip colour and "glass skin". Some girls — and a growing number of boys — are bringing this look to the classroom. In a recent school survey of 800 Grade 7–9 students, **almost one in five** said they sometimes put on light makeup before school. This has started a warm debate: should middle-school students wear makeup to school?

② Supporters say it is just a small freedom. Makeup, like hairstyles or running shoes, can help a teen feel fresh and confident. "When I look better, I focus better in class," one Grade 8 girl wrote. Beauty products today are much lighter and safer than before; a thin lip balm or a drop of sunscreen hardly counts as makeup. A school that forbids everything, they say, treats students like small children who cannot choose anything by themselves.

③ Other people, however, are worried. First, **study time is limited**. When a student spends twenty minutes in front of a mirror in the morning, she often cuts breakfast or sleep. A tired face after lunch can rub off half of the makeup, and then she worries about her look more than about her math problems. Second, many teens start with a small lipstick but soon buy more and more. Costs rise, and some students borrow money from parents or even classmates to keep up with trends.

④ Skin itself is another worry. Dermatologists have pointed out that teenage skin is **still changing** and is easily hurt by strong products. Long-term heavy makeup in middle-school years has been linked to more pimples, red spots and dry areas. Simple sunscreen is fine; thick foundation every school day is not. A bright face at fifteen, doctors warn, is not worth a tired face at twenty-five.

⑤ There is also a fairness question. If makeup becomes normal in the classroom, students who choose not to wear any may feel left out, as if they have lost a quiet competition. Teachers have noticed that such "beauty pressure" can quietly hurt friendships and even grades, especially for girls who are already shy.

⑥ In my view, schools should **not** ban every drop of beauty product — sunscreen, lip balm and clean hair are all welcome. But heavy eye makeup, long fake lashes and bright blush are better kept for weekends or special family dinners. School is a place to train the mind and the heart, not the face. When we are young, **a clean smile is already the best "filter"** we will ever wear.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What does the school survey in Paragraph 1 tell us?**

A. Almost half of the students wear heavy makeup.  
B. About one in five students sometimes wears light makeup to school.  
C. No boys in the school are interested in makeup.  
D. Students spend all their pocket money on lipstick.

**2. According to the supporters in Paragraph 2, how can makeup help teens?**

A. It can make them better at maths.  
B. It can replace breakfast.  
C. It can help them feel more confident.  
D. It can win scholarships for them.

**3. What is the second worry mentioned in Paragraph 3?**

A. Students may lose their sunscreen at school.  
B. Students may keep buying more products and spend more money.  
C. Students may stop taking photos.  
D. Students may forget the colour of their lip balm.

**4. What does the writer mean by "a bright face at fifteen is not worth a tired face at twenty-five"?**

A. Old people should not wear makeup.  
B. Heavy makeup now may harm skin in the future.  
C. Makeup makes students look older than they are.  
D. Young skin is always dry and needs foundation.

**5. What is the writer's final view?**

A. Middle-school students must wear makeup every day.  
B. All makeup should be banned in and out of school.  
C. Light care products are OK, but heavy makeup should be kept for special times.  
D. Students should only wear makeup during exams.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末两句 "almost one in five said they sometimes put on light makeup"  
【解析】调查显示约五分之一学生偶尔会上淡妆。A 项"近半数"夸大；C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第 2–3 句 "help a teen feel fresh and confident... I focus better in class"  
【解析】支持者认为化妆让人更自信。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段末句 "many teens start with a small lipstick but soon buy more and more. Costs rise..."  
【解析】第二个担忧是花费不断攀升。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义/语义理解题  
【定位】第④段末 "A bright face at fifteen... is not worth a tired face at twenty-five"  
【解析】意思是现在浓妆伤皮肤，日后可能出现疲惫肤况。A、C、D 均非句意。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "not ban every drop... heavy eye makeup... better kept for weekends"  
【解析】作者主张轻度护肤可以，重妆留给周末。A、B、D 均与原文不符。故选 C。
