# 阅读理解（议论文） **Is It Healthy for Teenagers to Have One "Best Friend for Everything"?**

① In many middle school classrooms, a quiet but real question can be found in every little friendship circle: **"Who is your best friend?"** Most of us have one name ready, and we are sometimes very proud of it. We sit together at lunch, walk home together, send messages before sleep, and share secrets that we never tell our parents. Yet in recent years, some teachers and psychologists have started to ask: is having **one single "best friend for everything"** really healthy for a teenager? I believe the answer is: not always.

② First, we should remember that our feelings, interests and problems are many. One friend is very unlikely to understand everything. A basketball partner may not love the poems you write in your notebook. A quiet reading friend may not enjoy the loud music you like. If we try to force one person to be our **whole world**, that person will feel tired, and we ourselves may feel that we are "too much". A healthier life has several **caring circles**, not one crowded corner.

③ Second, putting all our feelings into one friendship creates a heavy kind of fear. When two best friends always do everything together, a small disagreement can suddenly feel like the end of the world. If that single friend falls ill, moves to another city, or simply sits next to someone else one day, we may feel lost, even betrayed. A broader friend group softens these natural bumps. Losing ten minutes with one friend does not feel like losing the whole sky.

④ Third, having several friends helps us **grow into different versions of ourselves**. With one friend we joke; with another we study; with a third we volunteer; with a fourth we quietly talk about worries. Each friendship is a little mirror. By looking into more than one mirror, we slowly see a fuller picture of who we are — funny, hard-working, kind, curious, sometimes shy and sometimes brave. A single mirror, no matter how bright, can only reflect one angle.

⑤ Some students may feel that I am arguing against the idea of having a close friend. I am not. A very close friend is a precious gift, like a soft warm coat. What I am asking teenagers to think about is simply this: do not let that coat become the whole wardrobe. It is good to invite more people into your regular life — classmates from different classes, cousins, English-corner friends, weekend club members. Different friends will **share**, not compete for, your heart.

⑥ In short, a teenager's heart is not a small box with room for only one thing. It is a long shelf with space for many different kinds of friendships, each with its own shape and colour. Treasure your very close friend, but please also leave a few pages of your life for others. In the end, kindness spread widely usually returns to us more warmly than love stored narrowly in one single hand.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main question?**

A. Whether teenagers should have no friends at all.  
B. Whether having one single "best friend for everything" is really healthy for a teenager.  
C. Whether best friends should live in the same city.  
D. Whether all teenagers should stop playing basketball.

**2. According to paragraph ②, why is one friend unlikely to be enough?**

A. Because one friend always lies.  
B. Because our feelings, interests and problems are many; one person cannot cover them all.  
C. Because one friend usually lives too far away.  
D. Because good friends always speak different languages.

**3. Why does the writer compare friendships to "mirrors"?**

A. Mirrors are cheap and easy to find.  
B. Different friendships help us see different sides of ourselves and grow.  
C. Mirrors are beautiful to look at.  
D. Mirrors can replace all teachers.

**4. What does the writer say about having a very close friend?**

A. It is a bad idea that should be avoided.  
B. It is a precious gift — but we should not let it become our whole wardrobe of friendship.  
C. It only belongs to primary school children.  
D. It must be kept a secret.

**5. What is the writer's final advice?**

A. Stop talking to your very close friend.  
B. Put all love into one single friendship.  
C. Treasure your very close friend, but also leave a few pages of your life for other friends.  
D. Replace friendships with homework.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "is having one single 'best friend for everything' really healthy..."  
【解析】核心问题即是否应仅有一位全能挚友。A/C/D 均非中心。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第1-2句 "our feelings, interests and problems are many. One friend is very unlikely to understand everything."  
【解析】一个人难以承担所有角色。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段 "Each friendship is a little mirror... see a fuller picture of who we are."  
【解析】不同朋友反映不同自我。A/C/D 均非比喻意义。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "A very close friend is a precious gift... do not let that coat become the whole wardrobe."  
【解析】珍贵但不能独占整个"衣柜"。A/C/D 均不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥段末 "Treasure your very close friend, but please also leave a few pages of your life for others."  
【解析】作者建议珍惜挚友同时留空间给他人。A/B/D 均与作者观点相反。故选 C。
