# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Summer I Learned to Swim**

When I was in Grade 7, I was the only student in my class who could not swim. In the PE class by the school pool, my classmates jumped into the water and laughed like happy fish. I stood at the side, holding a towel, telling myself the same lie: "Next year. Next year I'll learn." The truth was simpler. I was afraid.

That summer, my cousin Xiao Ping came to our town. She was one year younger than me, but she swam like a dolphin. On her first morning, she looked at me, then at the pool, and said kindly, "Let's try, just together. You don't have to swim far. Just stay with me."

The first lesson did not go well. I jumped in with a rubber float, but when she gently pulled the float away, I panicked. I hit the water with my arms, I swallowed a mouthful of it, and I shouted. The lifeguard, a tall young man, ran to the edge. Xiao Ping caught my hand and said in a calm voice, "Breathe slowly. Look at me. You are not going down, you are floating." I was shaking, but somehow I stood up and my feet touched the bottom. The water was only up to my chest.

That evening, I did not want to try again. I sat on the bed and refused to go to the pool the next day. Xiao Ping did not argue. She sat beside me and said, "Remember when I was scared of the big dog at Grandma's house? You stood in front of me for a whole afternoon. I was only brave because you were there." I was surprised. I had almost forgotten that afternoon. "If you go tomorrow," she added, "I promise I will not take the float away again — until you tell me to."

Over the next two weeks, we went to the pool every morning. I held the float, and Xiao Ping swam slowly beside me. When I felt calmer, I asked her to take the float away for ten seconds, then twenty. Each small success made me a little braver. On the last day of July, I swam 15 metres without a float, all the way from one side of the pool to the other. My arms were tired, my eyes were red, but I was smiling like a fool.

That night, I wrote in my notebook: "The water is not my enemy. My fear was." Now, three years later, I still cannot win a swimming match. But I do not stand at the side of the pool any more. And whenever a younger student tells me, "I'm too scared," I sit down and say, "Let's try, just together."

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why couldn't the narrator swim before Grade 7?**

A. Because he had no free time at all.  
B. Because there was no pool near his home.  
C. Because he was afraid of water.  
D. Because swimming was not allowed in his school.

**2. What happened during the first lesson?**

A. The narrator swam across the whole pool.  
B. The narrator panicked and stood up with his feet touching the bottom.  
C. Xiao Ping pulled him out of the water.  
D. The lifeguard got into the water to help.

**3. Why did the narrator agree to continue the lessons?**

A. Because his mother forced him.  
B. Because Xiao Ping reminded him that he once protected her from a big dog, and she promised not to take the float away suddenly.  
C. Because the school gave a big prize for swimming.  
D. Because the weather became too hot.

**4. How far did the narrator swim on the last day of July?**

A. 5 metres.  
B. 10 metres.  
C. 15 metres, from one side of the pool to the other, without a float.  
D. 50 metres.

**5. What lesson did the narrator learn from this experience?**

A. You cannot beat your fear; you just have to avoid it.  
B. The real enemy is often your own fear, not the water.  
C. Only tall people can swim well.  
D. Swimming is useless in daily life.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 1 段 "The truth was simpler. I was afraid."  
【解析】作者承认怕水。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段 结尾  
【解析】惊慌后脚碰到池底站起来。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段  
【解析】堂妹讲起过去相互保护并做承诺。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段 "I swam 15 metres without a float"  
【解析】无浮板游 15 米。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】倒数第 1 段 "The water is not my enemy. My fear was."  
【解析】真正敌人是恐惧。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

- **lifeguard** [n.] 救生员  
- **success** [n.] 成功
