# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Two Rough Hands I Once Felt Ashamed Of**

① My father is a **builder**. For as long as I can remember, he has left home at six each morning, carrying a small old bag of metal tools, and returned at seven in the evening, his jeans and shirt covered in grey dust. His hands are not the hands of a teacher or a doctor. They are dark, wide, with short, thick fingers. The skin is rough, full of small cuts, some old, some new. When he touches my head, it feels like a warm, dry piece of stone.

② When I was in Grade 7, a new girl called Anna came into our class. Her father was a university professor. One afternoon, at the school gate, my father came to pick me up on his old blue scooter. He had just finished work; his clothes were dusty, and his hands were still grey from cement. Anna was standing near the gate. When she saw my father wave to me, she turned her eyes away politely, as if she had seen nothing at all.

③ That night, I was quiet at the dinner table. I pushed a piece of fish around my bowl. I did not want to admit it, but in that quiet moment at the gate, I had felt **ashamed** of my father's dusty clothes and rough hands. Ashamed of him, and — worse — ashamed of **myself** for feeling this way.

④ The next Saturday morning, my father said, "Ming, come with me today. I want you to see where I work." I did not want to go, but his voice left no room for "no". We took the bus to a large building site across the river.

⑤ The site was noisy and dusty. Tall iron frames rose up into the grey sky. I saw my father put on a yellow hard hat and introduce me to his team: Uncle Li, Uncle Zhou, Aunt Mei. Their faces were brown from the sun. Their hands all looked like my father's. They smiled at me, offered me warm rice porridge from a thermos, and called me "little engineer".

⑥ My father then took me up two floors of a half-finished apartment building. He showed me a small, neat **wall** he had built that week. "Touch this, Ming," he said. I pressed my hand on the grey wall. It was smooth and cool, straight as a maths line. "Your mother will buy an apartment one day," he said. "Maybe not from me, but from someone like me. Inside every apartment you see in our city, there is a wall built by somebody's father." He looked at his own hands. "These hands are not beautiful. But they build a city."

⑦ I said nothing. I suddenly felt a heat behind my eyes. That afternoon I watched him work for four hours. I saw how carefully he placed each brick, how he checked his line with a small plumb, how Uncle Li passed him tools without a word because they had worked together for fifteen years. The rough, grey, "ugly" hand of my father was, in fact, the hand of a quiet master.

⑧ On the way home, I walked beside him. I took his hand in mine. It felt like warm stone, as always. But for the first time in my life, I felt that my father's rough hand was one of the most beautiful things I had ever held.

⑨ Later that week, our English teacher asked us to write a short piece called "*The Person I Am Proud Of*". Many students wrote about their doctor uncle, their pilot mother, or a famous athlete. I wrote four simple words at the top of my paper: "**My father's hands.**"

---

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's father's job?**

A. A university professor.  
B. A builder.  
C. A pilot.  
D. A doctor.

**2. Why did the writer feel ashamed at the school gate?**

A. Because his father did not come to pick him up.  
B. Because his father's clothes were dusty and his hands looked rough when Anna was nearby.  
C. Because his father shouted at him in front of classmates.  
D. Because his father drove a big expensive car.

**3. Why did the father take the writer to the building site?**

A. Because he wanted the writer to start working at the site too.  
B. Because he wanted the writer to see his work and the team he worked with.  
C. Because he was bored at home that Saturday.  
D. Because the writer wanted to become a professor.

**4. What did the father say when he showed the writer the smooth grey wall?**

A. "This wall is worth more than any professor's office."  
B. "These hands are not beautiful. But they build a city."  
C. "Everybody should quit school and start building."  
D. "This wall will fall down in a year."

**5. What does the writer most likely want readers to feel at the end of the story?**

A. That building sites are dangerous for all children.  
B. That the work of quiet hard-working people — even when their hands look rough — can be a beautiful and proud thing, once we really look at it.  
C. That no one should be proud of their parents.  
D. That builders should retrain to become professors.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "My father is a builder"  
【解析】原文首句已点明。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②③段 "his clothes were dusty, and his hands were still grey from cement ... I had felt ashamed"  
【解析】原文明确因衣着与手在同学面前感到羞愧。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "I want you to see where I work" & 第⑤段介绍同事  
【解析】父亲想让作者看到自己工作的地方与团队。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段末 "These hands are not beautiful. But they build a city"  
【解析】父亲的原话直接引用。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧段 "one of the most beautiful things I had ever held" & 第⑨段作文标题 "My father's hands"  
【解析】作者希望读者体会到平凡劳动者工作的尊严与美。A、C、D 均与作者立场不符。故选 B。
