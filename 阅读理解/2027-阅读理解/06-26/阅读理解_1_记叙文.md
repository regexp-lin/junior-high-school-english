# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Scholarship Letter That Almost Went to the Wrong Girl**

Our school only gave three scholarships every year, each 2,000 yuan. In Grade 9, I applied for one. I wrote my essay at night, checked it many times, and handed it in on the last day before the deadline. Then I tried to forget about it. My mother's factory pay had been cut that spring, and we needed every yuan.

Two weeks later, the results were printed on the school notice board. My eyes found the list quickly. The three names were not mine. I stood still. I did not cry in front of classmates, but my throat felt tight. I walked to the bathroom, washed my face with cold water, and told myself: "Maybe next time." There would, of course, not be a next time before the high school entrance exam.

The next morning, something strange happened. Our homeroom teacher, Ms. Fan, asked me to stay after class. Her face looked a little worried. "Ling, about the scholarship… I need to check something with you. Did you fill in 'Zhang Ling' or 'Zhang Jing' on the form?"

"Zhang Ling," I said, surprised. My full name is Zhang Ling. Zhang Jing was a different girl in another class.

Ms. Fan sighed. "I think there was a typing mistake by the office. One of the three winners is 'Zhang Jing' — but your essay and her essay look very similar to the panel. I checked last night. Your essay is the one that won. The letter went to the wrong girl."

I was shocked. But before I could feel happy, Ms. Fan added quietly, "Zhang Jing has already received the money. She has told her mother, who was very proud. This happened three days ago."

I said nothing for a long time.

Ms. Fan waited. Then she asked gently, "What do you want to do? You can ask the school to take the money back and give it to you. That would be fair. Or I can ask the committee to prepare a second 2,000 yuan — but that would take a long time, and it may not happen before the end of the term."

I thought of my mother's tired face, of the small "owed" list by our rice jar. Two thousand yuan was real money. But I also thought of Zhang Jing, a shy girl I had only spoken to once. Her mother had been proud for three days.

"Ms. Fan," I said slowly, "could you please go with me to the office, and we tell them quietly? I want the real winner to be correct on paper. But I don't want Zhang Jing or her mother to be hurt. Can we ask the school to keep her as a winner too, and give me any extra help that might be possible? If not, let it be. I will just try harder for next year's… something."

Ms. Fan looked at me for a long moment. Then she took my hand and said, "I'll try my best."

That afternoon, the headmaster talked with Ms. Fan. The school, in the end, decided to give a **fourth** scholarship that year, with money from the parents' committee. My name was added to the board. I ran home with the paper, and my mother, for the first time in months, did not look so tired.

Later, I became quiet friends with Zhang Jing. We never spoke about that week. But I know a little more about something important: being fair to myself, and being kind to others, are often not two different things.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the narrator apply for the scholarship?**

A. Because her parents forced her to.  
B. Because her mother's factory pay had been cut, so the family needed the money.  
C. Because the headmaster chose her to apply.  
D. Because she wanted to buy a new bike.

**2. What did the narrator do right after seeing she had not won?**

A. She shouted at her classmates.  
B. She asked the teacher to change the list.  
C. She washed her face with cold water and tried to accept it.  
D. She stopped coming to school for a week.

**3. What mistake did the office make?**

A. They printed the wrong year on the letter.  
B. They sent the letter to "Zhang Jing" instead of "Zhang Ling" because the two essays looked similar and the names were close.  
C. They lost the narrator's essay.  
D. They forgot to give any scholarship that year.

**4. Why did the narrator choose a gentle way to handle the mistake?**

A. Because she was afraid of the headmaster.  
B. Because she thought Zhang Jing might be angry with her.  
C. Because Zhang Jing and her mother had already been proud for three days, and she did not want to hurt them.  
D. Because she had a lot of money at home.

**5. How did the story finally end?**

A. The school took back the money from Zhang Jing and gave it to the narrator.  
B. The school decided to give a fourth scholarship, with money from the parents' committee, so both girls could keep it.  
C. The narrator got no money at all.  
D. Zhang Jing refused to speak to the narrator forever.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 1 段 "my mother's factory pay had been cut..."  
【解析】家中急需钱。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段  
【解析】去洗脸、自我安慰。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段  
【解析】误将奖学金发给张菁。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第 9 段  
【解析】不愿伤害已欢喜三日的母女。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】倒数第 2 段  
【解析】校设第四份奖学金，两人共享荣誉。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
