# 阅读理解（说明文） **How Does a Microwave Oven Heat Food So Fast?**

On a busy Monday evening, many families warm up leftover rice in a strange white box, and after only two minutes, the rice is steaming hot. The box is a **microwave oven**. It looks simple, yet it heats food much faster than a traditional gas or electric cooker. How does it do this? The answer lies in one very small thing: the water molecule.

To begin with, we need to remember that almost all our food contains water. Rice, meat, vegetables, and even bread all have tiny drops of water inside them. A **water molecule** is a small particle with two hydrogen "arms" and one oxygen "head". Very importantly, this molecule has two ends: one slightly positive and one slightly negative. In short, a water molecule is a bit like a tiny magnet.

A microwave oven is really a small machine that creates short invisible waves, called **microwaves**. These waves are a kind of energy; they are much weaker than the waves in an X-ray machine, and they cannot pass through the metal wall of the oven. But they can easily pass through plastic plates, glass bowls, and of course, into food.

Now, the key trick. The microwaves create an electric field that changes direction very, very quickly — about 2.45 billion times every second. Each water molecule tries to line up with this field, so it keeps flipping back and forth. It spins like a tiny toy. This fast movement is another name for heat: when molecules move fast, the thing becomes warm. In other words, the microwave oven does not heat the food directly; it makes the water inside the food move faster, and the food becomes hot from the inside.

This is why a microwave oven is so fast. In a traditional oven, heat comes from the outside and slowly reaches the middle. In a microwave, however, almost all parts of the food are heated at almost the same time. A small bowl of rice can become hot in 60 – 90 seconds.

But microwave ovens have weak points too. First, they often heat food unevenly. Some parts may be very hot, while others are cool, because the waves bounce inside the oven and create "hot spots". This is why we stir the food after heating and let it stand for a few seconds. Second, microwaves do not brown food very well. Bread becomes soft and limp, not crunchy, because there is no hot surface to push away water and create a brown crust.

Safety is also important. Never put a metal plate or a foil wrapper into a microwave oven. Metal reflects the waves and can cause sparks or even a small fire. Plastic that is not "microwave-safe" may melt into the food. Empty operation is also dangerous, because the waves have nothing to absorb and may damage the oven itself. When used with care, however, the microwave is still one of the fastest, most energy-saving kitchen tools ever invented.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What special feature of a water molecule is most important for microwave heating?**

A. Its colour.  
B. Its smell.  
C. Its weight.  
D. It has one slightly positive end and one slightly negative end.

**2. About how many times per second does the electric field change direction inside a microwave oven?**

A. 2.45 billion times.  
B. 100 times.  
C. Once per minute.  
D. Only once in total.

**3. Why does a microwave oven heat food faster than a traditional oven?**

A. Because it uses hotter metal plates.  
B. Because it heats almost all parts of the food at almost the same time, instead of from the outside slowly.  
C. Because it blows hot wind onto the food.  
D. Because it uses steam from boiling water.

**4. Why does bread become soft (not crunchy) in a microwave oven?**

A. Because the bread is old.  
B. Because microwaves do not brown food well; there is no hot surface to push away water and make a brown crust.  
C. Because the bread contains no water.  
D. Because the oven is too cold.

**5. Which action is NOT safe in a microwave oven?**

A. Put a "microwave-safe" plastic plate with food inside.  
B. Put a small glass bowl of rice with a little water inside.  
C. Put a metal plate with foil inside.  
D. Stir the food and let it stand for a few seconds after heating.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段 "one slightly positive and one slightly negative"  
【解析】极性分子。A/B/C 均错。故选 D。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段 "about 2.45 billion times every second"  
【解析】电场方向变化频率。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段  
【解析】微波使食物内部同步受热。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 6 段  
【解析】无表面焦化过程。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题（反向）  
【定位】第 7 段 "Never put a metal plate or a foil wrapper..."  
【解析】金属易产生火花，危险。A/B/D 均安全。故选 C。
