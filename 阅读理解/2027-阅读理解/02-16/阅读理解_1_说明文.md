# 阅读理解（说明文） **The Giant Panda: A Bamboo-eating Mystery**

① With its round black eyes, white cheeks and lazy walk, the **giant panda** is perhaps the most loved animal in the world. China has treated pandas as a national symbol for years, sending them to zoos in many countries as "friendship messengers." Yet one strange question has puzzled scientists for a long time: **why does this big, heavy animal choose to eat only bamboo — a plant that is thin, hard and very difficult to digest?**

② Scientists now believe the answer lies in history. Millions of years ago, the ancestors of the panda were **meat-eaters**, more like bears or wolves. Around **two million years ago**, the climate in Southwest China changed, and many small animals became difficult to find. Bamboo, however, grew everywhere — in cool mountains, wet valleys and low hills. Slowly, pandas learned to use this easy food, and over time their bodies changed to match it.

③ One amazing change is the panda's hand. A normal bear's paw cannot hold a thin bamboo stick, but a panda has a special "sixth finger" — actually a small bone that works like a thumb. With this tiny helper, a panda can hold a bamboo stick firmly, much like a child holding a pencil. Without this small bone, eating bamboo would be almost impossible.

④ However, pandas still keep the **stomach of a meat-eater**. A cow has four parts in its stomach and can slowly break down plants. A panda has only one short stomach, which cannot get much energy out of bamboo. As a result, a panda must eat **10 to 40 kilograms** of bamboo every day — almost the weight of a school student — just to stay alive. That is why we often see them eating quietly for twelve hours a day.

⑤ Bamboo also has another problem. Every 40 to 60 years, a whole bamboo forest will flower and then die together, leaving an entire mountain area without food. For centuries, pandas had to walk across valleys to find a new forest. Today, roads and farms have cut many of these natural paths. This is why scientists have built many **panda corridors** — long strips of green forest that connect one protected area to another, helping the pandas to travel safely.

⑥ Thanks to careful protection, the number of wild pandas has risen from about 1,000 in the 1980s to more than 1,800 today. The panda is no longer marked "endangered," but is still listed as "vulnerable." This lovely story reminds us that every species alive today is the result of a long, careful conversation with nature — and that a small kindness from humans can help that conversation continue.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What mystery has puzzled scientists for a long time?**

A. Why pandas have black and white fur.  
B. Why pandas are found only in mountains.  
C. Why pandas eat only bamboo.  
D. Why pandas sleep twelve hours a day.

**2. According to Paragraph 2, what were pandas long ago?**

A. Meat-eaters like bears or wolves.  
B. Small flying animals.  
C. Plant-eaters only.  
D. Sea creatures.

**3. What is the "sixth finger" of a panda?**

A. A small bone that works like a thumb.  
B. A long sharp claw.  
C. A soft piece of skin.  
D. An extra eye near the paw.

**4. Why must a panda eat so much bamboo every day?**

A. Because bamboo is very heavy.  
B. Because its stomach gets little energy out of bamboo.  
C. Because other pandas will steal its food.  
D. Because bamboo is the only food in China.

**5. What are "panda corridors"?**

A. Long wooden roads built for trucks.  
B. Strips of green forest connecting protected areas.  
C. New farms for pandas to work on.  
D. Tall stone walls around cities.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末句  
【解析】为何只吃竹子。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "ancestors of the panda were meat-eaters"  
【解析】古代熊猫为食肉动物。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "a small bone that works like a thumb"  
【解析】第六指是像拇指的小骨头。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "cannot get much energy out of bamboo"  
【解析】胃获取能量低，需大量进食。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "long strips of green forest that connect one protected area to another"  
【解析】绿色森林走廊连通保护区。故选 B。
