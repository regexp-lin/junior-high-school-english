# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Cooking Lesson from Grandma**

① Every year during the summer holiday, my parents sent me to my grandmother's small house in the countryside for two weeks. Grandma lived alone, in a one-storey house with a tiny yard and an old apricot tree by the gate. She was thin, almost eighty, and wore a blue apron whenever she stepped out of bed. I loved her — but, to be honest, I also felt a little bored. The village was small, the internet was slow, and her TV only had three channels.

② The summer I turned fourteen, something changed. My mother, before putting me on the bus, said something odd: "This time, don't just eat Grandma's cooking. Learn something. You are old enough." I nodded politely, but I did not plan to learn anything. On the first morning, when Grandma asked if I wanted to help with breakfast, I said, "I'll just watch, Grandma. You go ahead."

③ She smiled. "No watching today," she said gently but clearly. "Today you wash the rice."

④ I did not expect that. I was holding a comic book. I put it down slowly and went to the kitchen. Grandma showed me the rice, poured a handful into a small bowl, and said, "Water, hands, rub. Three times. Then clean water." My first try was a disaster — half the rice fell into the sink. My second try was better, but the water came out too dirty. On the third try, I noticed something: if I moved my hands slowly, in small circles, more dirt came out and less rice fell away. Grandma nodded. "You see," she said, "even rice has a way."

⑤ After that morning, Grandma added a small cooking task to every day. Day 2: break an egg without dropping any shell into the bowl. Day 3: cut a cucumber into thin, equal pieces. Day 4: watch the soup — "Not every bubble is the same," Grandma said. "The big ones mean too much fire." Day 5: make a simple tomato-egg dish from start to end, with Grandma only standing nearby, saying almost nothing.

⑥ On day seven I failed badly. I burnt the oil and the eggs came out black. I was angry with myself and almost threw the pan into the sink. Grandma stopped me with a soft hand. "Xiao Ling," she said, "our mistakes teach us more than our good dishes. Look at the oil; smell it. Next time, you will know." She opened the window, made a fresh attempt with me, and this time the eggs came out yellow and perfect. She ate a big slice and said, "You see? Even the burnt pan was a teacher."

⑦ On the last morning of my visit, Grandma did something I did not expect. She gave me a small red notebook. Inside, in her careful old handwriting, were twelve recipes — **twelve dishes of our family**. The list began with plain rice (day 1) and ended with "winter dumplings for family reunion". Next to each dish, she had added a little note: *"For this dish, measure with the heart, not only with the spoon."* *"If the soup is too salty, add a little sugar, and learn."* *"Do not cook when you are angry. Food will remember."*

⑧ I read the notebook on the bus home, and then again at my own desk. That autumn, when my mum was tired, I made a simple tomato-egg dish for dinner. My father's eyes went wide — not because it was the best dish he had ever eaten, but because his own daughter had made it. My mother quietly wiped her eyes. I remembered what Grandma had said: "Food remembers."

⑨ Grandma passed away last winter, quietly, at the same old house. We buried her under the apricot tree in the yard. I took her small red notebook home. I do not cook every day, because homework is heavy in Grade 9. But on Sunday evenings, I open the notebook, pick one dish, and cook it slowly. When the smell of hot oil meets the kitchen air, I feel she is standing behind me again — thin, smiling, her soft voice saying, "Not every bubble is the same."

---

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What was the writer's attitude to visiting Grandma before she turned fourteen?**

A. She was very excited and never felt bored.  
B. She loved Grandma but also felt a little bored because of the quiet village and slow internet.  
C. She refused to go at all.  
D. She went only to use the TV.

**2. What was Grandma's first cooking lesson on day 1?**

A. How to break an egg without dropping any shell.  
B. How to cut a cucumber into thin, equal pieces.  
C. How to wash the rice — using small slow circles with her hands.  
D. How to make winter dumplings.

**3. What did Grandma tell the writer when she almost threw the burnt pan into the sink?**

A. "Our mistakes teach us more than our good dishes. Look at the oil; smell it."  
B. "Go to your room and stop cooking."  
C. "You must buy a new pan."  
D. "Call your mother immediately."

**4. Which of the following is one of the notes Grandma wrote in the red notebook?**

A. "Cooking is only for adults."  
B. "For this dish, measure with the heart, not only with the spoon."  
C. "A good cook always uses a machine."  
D. "Food should never be hot."

**5. What does the writer mean at the end of the passage by "Not every bubble is the same"?**

A. Only big bubbles are useful.  
B. The writer cannot cook at all now.  
C. Grandma's quiet, careful attention to small details stays with her, even after Grandma has passed away.  
D. Bubbles are boring and should be ignored.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末句  
【解析】爱外婆但因乡下安静、网速慢感到无聊。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段  
【解析】第一课是用小圈手法洗米。A/B 为之后的日子；D 为最后一道菜谱。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段  
【解析】失败也教会人。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段  
【解析】以心为量。A/C/D 均与笔记内容不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑨段末句  
【解析】外婆留下的细致与温度长存心中。A/B/D 均偏。故选 C。
