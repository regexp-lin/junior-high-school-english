# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Shoes I Never Asked For**

For the whole winter of Grade 7, I wanted a pair of new basketball shoes. Not any pair — the blue-and-white ones that Lei Feng wore in our team. In our small town, everyone knew the shop on Pine Street that sold them. The price was 298 yuan — almost a month of my father's lunch money. I never said a word about it at home. My father worked at a small fruit stall; my mother cleaned offices at night. We were not poor, but we were careful.

At Spring Festival, Grandma came to visit. She gave me a red envelope. Inside was 300 yuan — more than she usually gave, and almost exactly the price of the shoes. I counted the notes twice and tried to act calm. Mum caught my eye. I think she guessed what I was thinking. She smiled quietly but did not say a word.

The next morning I ran to the shop. The blue-and-white shoes were still on the shelf, bright as the sky. I took them to the counter, but before the shopkeeper wrapped them, I stopped. My eyes happened to fall on a newspaper on his desk. In the corner was a small notice: "**City Children's Home — need warm winter shoes for 48 children. Size 31 – 37. Please help.**"

I stood there for a long moment. Then I asked the shopkeeper, "How much are the plain dark-blue canvas shoes at the back?"

"Forty-eight yuan," he said, surprised.

I did not buy the bright blue-and-white ones that day. I walked home with the red envelope still in my pocket and a plan growing in my head.

I did not tell anyone at first. For two weeks I used my own small savings and skipped three bubble-tea afternoons. I wrote a notice on a big piece of paper and put it in the window of Dad's fruit stall: "*Warm-Shoes Plan: ten yuan buys one winter shoe for one child at the Children's Home. Bring a receipt from any shop and I will thank you in person.*"

The first day, only one old woman stopped by. She gave twenty yuan. The second day, a middle-school boy I did not know brought fifty yuan. By the end of the week, we had collected 680 yuan. My father silently added 100 yuan of his own. Mum added 60. Together with my red envelope, we had enough for 48 pairs of canvas shoes — exactly. The shopkeeper gave us a small discount when he heard what we were doing.

On a cold Saturday morning, Dad, Mum, and I delivered the shoes to the Children's Home. The director, a thin woman in a grey sweater, opened the boxes and touched each pair with both hands, as if they were soft animals. Forty-eight children lined up. Some took the shoes shyly; some held them for a long time before putting them on. A boy about my age looked down at his feet, then looked up at me. He did not say anything, but his face — I think I will remember his face for a very long time.

On the way home, I did not feel sorry about the blue-and-white shoes at all. Dad put one arm around my shoulder and said quietly, "You know, I grew up without winter shoes. I used to hope someone would give me a pair." He looked at his old boots and smiled. "Tonight, son, you gave that pair to me, too — in a way."

Spring came. A boy from the Children's Home wrote me a short letter. He drew a small pair of dark-blue shoes at the bottom, with a small white star above them. He wrote, in careful characters: "*Elder brother, my feet are no longer cold. Thank you.*"

I still do not own the blue-and-white shoes. I think I may never buy them. Somehow, they look less important now. When I stand up in gym class, even in my old pair, I feel taller than I used to. Some shoes, I learned, do not need to be on our feet to lift us up.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer want the blue-and-white shoes so much?**

A. They were the cheapest shoes in town.  
B. Every member of his basketball team, including Lei Feng, wore them; they were his dream shoes.  
C. His mother forced him to buy them.  
D. They were free of charge.

**2. Why did the writer change his plan at the shop?**

A. Because the shopkeeper refused to sell them to him.  
B. Because he saw a newspaper notice asking for warm winter shoes for 48 children at the Children's Home.  
C. Because he suddenly had no money.  
D. Because the shoes were already sold out.

**3. How did the writer collect more money?**

A. He borrowed money from the bank.  
B. He used his own savings, skipped bubble-tea, put a notice in his father's fruit stall, and got help from strangers, parents, and the shopkeeper's discount.  
C. He sold his old books.  
D. He went to the city library.

**4. How did the writer feel when he saw the boy at the Children's Home put on the shoes?**

A. He felt sorry for not buying the blue-and-white shoes.  
B. He felt the boy's face was something he would remember for a very long time.  
C. He felt bored and tired.  
D. He felt angry with his parents.

**5. What does the writer mean by "some shoes do not need to be on our feet to lift us up"?**

A. Only expensive shoes can make people tall.  
B. Helping others can give us strength and dignity, even when we still wear our old shoes.  
C. A gym class always makes students feel tall.  
D. Blue-and-white shoes are not good for basketball.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"Lei Feng wore... dream shoes"  
【解析】队员都穿，是他的梦想鞋。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"City Children's Home... need warm winter shoes..."  
【解析】看到募鞋启事改变主意。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"small savings... notice... strangers... discount..."  
【解析】自存+宣传+众人+折扣。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"remember his face for a very long time"  
【解析】那个男孩的脸他会记很久。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】"feel taller than I used to"  
【解析】善行让人心气高。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
