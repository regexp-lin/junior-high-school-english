# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Does the Moon Change Shape Every Night?**

① On the night of the **Mid-Autumn Festival**, families across China look up and see a large, perfectly round **full moon**. Two weeks later, the same Moon looks like a thin silver "**C**". Two weeks after that, it almost disappears from the sky. Has the Moon suddenly become smaller, or broken into pieces? Of course not. The Moon is always the same size; it is **our view of it** that keeps changing. So why, then, does the Moon appear in so many different shapes across the month?

② The first important fact is that the Moon **does not make its own light**. It only **reflects sunlight**, like a huge grey mirror floating in space. This means that at any moment, **one half** of the Moon is always bright (facing the Sun), and **the other half** is always dark (turned away from the Sun). The Moon's shape, from our view, depends entirely on **how much of the bright half we can see from Earth**.

③ The second important fact is that the Moon **travels around the Earth**, completing one circle about every **29.5 days**. As the Moon moves, the angle between the Sun, the Moon and the Earth keeps changing. When the Moon is on the **same side** of the Earth as the Sun, the bright half faces the Sun and the dark half faces us — we see almost nothing, and we call this a **"new moon"**. When the Moon is on the **opposite side**, the Sun lights up the whole side facing us, and we see the full bright disc — a **"full moon"**.

④ Between these two extremes, the Moon passes through several familiar stages, called **phases**. A few days after the new moon, a thin bright line appears on the right edge of the Moon — the **crescent moon**, shaped like a narrow "C" (in the Northern Hemisphere the bright side is usually on the right). About a week later, we see **half** of the bright side — this is the **first quarter** moon, which looks like the letter **D**. A few days more, and most of the bright side is visible — the **gibbous moon**, nearly full. After the full moon, the whole process **reverses**: gibbous, then third quarter (like a backwards D), then waning crescent, then new moon again. The whole cycle takes **about one month** — in fact, the word "month" comes from "Moon".

⑤ Many people confuse the **phases of the Moon** with **eclipses**. They are not the same. **Phases** happen every month, quietly, because of the Moon's normal journey. An **eclipse** happens only a few times a year, and only when the Sun, Earth and Moon line up in an almost perfect straight line. During a **lunar eclipse**, the Earth's shadow falls across the Moon and turns it reddish for a short time; during a **solar eclipse**, the Moon passes in front of the Sun and casts a shadow on the Earth. Eclipses are **rare and dramatic**; phases are regular and gentle.

⑥ A practical tip for young moon watchers: if you see a **"D-shaped" moon**, the Moon is **growing** and will be full in about a week; if you see a **backwards-D shape** (a "C" that opens to the right), the Moon is **shrinking** and will soon disappear. In English there are two neat words for this: a **waxing** moon is getting bigger, a **waning** moon is getting smaller. Once you learn these two words, even a single quick look at the sky will tell you which part of the month it is.

⑦ So, the next time a small child asks "Where did the rest of the Moon go?" you can smile and say: nowhere. The Moon is still whole, still about **3 474 km** across, still **384 000 km** from us, still turning quietly around the Earth as it has for more than four billion years. What changes each night is only the **light** and our **angle of view** — a small reminder that in astronomy, as in life, what we see and what is truly there are not always the same thing.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段 "why does the Moon appear in so many different shapes across the month?"  
【解析】全文解释月相变化。A、C、D 与主题不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "does not make its own light ... reflects sunlight ... one half is always bright"  
【解析】月不自发光，仅反射阳光。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "completing one circle about every 29.5 days"  
【解析】一个月相周期约 29.5 天。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Phases happen every month ... eclipse ... only when ... almost perfect straight line"  
【解析】月相常见有规律，食则罕见需三点共线。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题
