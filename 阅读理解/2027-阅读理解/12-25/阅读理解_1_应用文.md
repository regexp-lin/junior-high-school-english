# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School 2028 Winter-Break Study-Plan Template (Optional, Gentle Version)**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Student Affairs Office + Grade 8 Homeroom Team**  
> **2028 Winter-Break Study-Plan Template — *Optional, Gentle, Student-Owned***  
> **Break dates**: **Saturday 17 January 2028 — Sunday 15 February 2028 (30 days)**  
> **Return to school**: **Monday 16 February 2028, 07:40**  
> **Form reference**: *8-Zone Daily Card* · *14:00 Review Window* · *3-Rest Rule*

---

① Below is Lihua Middle School's **2028 Winter-Break Study-Plan Template**. Please read slowly. **This plan is a suggestion, not a homework**. It is designed by four Grade-8 homeroom teachers (Mr Qin, Ms Lin, Ms Zhao, Mr Gu) together with ten Grade-8 student volunteers, after two school-union meetings in December. **No student is required to use any part of this plan.** No parent is required to sign it. No teacher will collect it after the break. It exists only to help any student who wishes, gently and privately, to keep a small daily rhythm during the 30-day winter break — and to make sure that rest and family time come first.

---

## 🗓️ The 30-Day Big Shape (suggested, fully adjustable)

| Week | Dates | Rough daily "study feeling" | Must-do family / rest items |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | 17 – 23 Jan | **Soft landing** · 1.5–2 hours / day only | At least 2 full days with no study at all |
| Week 2 | 24 – 30 Jan | **Steady middle** · 2–2.5 hours / day | Chinese New Year Eve (26 Jan) = 0 study |
| Week 3 | 31 Jan – 6 Feb | **Steady middle** · 2–2.5 hours / day | Lantern Festival-week family evening free |
| Week 4 | 7 – 13 Feb | **Gentle taper-up** · 2.5–3 hours / day | At least 1 full day completely screen-free |
| Last 2 days | 14 – 15 Feb | **Soft return** · 1.5 hours / day only | Early sleep both nights, before 22:00 |

- **Study does not include shop-tutoring classes.** If you attend any outside class, count half its minutes toward the daily total.  
- **Maximum any single day**: **3 hours of self-study total**, no matter what week. Any more is not admired here; it is gently discouraged.  
- **Minimum any single day**: **0 minutes is allowed**. A rest day is a real day.

---

## 📋 The 8-Zone Daily Card (the main working tool)

Each suggested study day uses one A5 paper card divided into **8 small zones**. Each zone is **about 15 minutes**. Students colour in a small dot when each zone is done.

| Zone | Suggested content (15 min each) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1. English — *speak* | Read aloud one textbook paragraph slowly, twice | Phone may record your voice; **delete after** |
| 2. English — *write* | Copy 8 new words into the **vocabulary ring-book**, 1 simple sentence each | No English dictionary app — use paper dictionary if possible |
| 3. Chinese — *read* | Read 1,500 characters of any book you chose | Not a textbook; a real book you like |
| 4. Chinese — *write* | Write 6 lines about "today" in a small notebook | Nobody reads this but you |
| 5. Maths — *one question* | One single "slow" maths question, worked out on paper | **Stop when done**; do not chain 5 questions |
| 6. Physics / Chemistry — *re-read* | Re-read 1 textbook page of the term just ended, quietly | Underline 1 sentence you now understand better |
| 7. **Break zone** | **Stand up, drink warm water, look outside 90 seconds** | **Must do**; not optional |
| 8. **Free zone** | Anything — drawing, piano, mahjong with Grandpa, folding laundry, walking the dog | **Not study**; rest is the point |

- **Daily minimum used zones**: **zero**. A student may skip the whole card any day.  
- **Daily maximum used zones**: **8 zones = 2 hours**. Do not squeeze a 9th zone.  
- **Zone 7 "Break zone" is required** on any day that uses more than 2 study zones. Even in holiday, small breaks protect small eyes.

---

## 🕑 The 14:00 Review Window (optional but kindly suggested)

> For any student who wishes to keep one **small steady anchor**, the school suggests using **14:00 – 14:45** (45 minutes) every non-festival day as a **quiet private review window**.

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 14:00 – 14:05 | Drink warm water, sit down, set a paper timer for 40 minutes |
| 14:05 – 14:35 | Do **one subject only** — not three, not two — one subject |
| 14:35 – 14:45 | Write 3 lines in notebook: *"Today I understood … / still not sure about … / nice small feeling …"* |
| After 14:45 | **Close the book fully.** No "just one more". Window is closed. |

- **Music off** during this window; room light above 300 lux if possible.  
- **No phone** on the desk; place it in another room.  
- **If you miss the window one day, do not make it up** the next day. Miss it gently and move on.

---

## 🧘 The 3-Rest Rule (non-negotiable)

Even the most serious student at Lihua Middle School must keep **three rest guarantees** during the 30-day winter break.

| # | Rest guarantee | Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Big Family Days — no study** | 25 Jan Xiaonian, 26 Jan CNY Eve, 27 Jan Day 1, 11 Feb Lantern Festival = 4 full no-study days |
| 2 | **1 Full Screen-Free Day per Week** | One chosen day (or rolling) each week with no phone, no computer, no TV: 4 full screen-free days in the break |
| 3 | **Sleep Before 22:30, 22:00 on the final two nights** | 14 and 15 Feb must be early nights; body clock returns to school rhythm |

A winter break without these three rests, **even with excellent study performance**, is considered by the school a **tired break**, not a **good break**.

---

## 📵 Phone, Screen, and Eye Rules (gentle suggestions)

| Item | Suggestion |
|---|---|
| Daily screen (non-study) | **≤ 90 minutes** |
| Study-related screen | Only for dictionary check or teacher group notice — **not for videos** |
| Phone next to pillow | **Strongly discouraged**; put 2 m from bed |
| Outdoor light | **At least 40 min per day**, even in cold weather (coat + scarf) |
| Chinese-New-Year red packets on phone | Please pause after receiving; avoid late-night scrolling on 26–27 Jan |

Six hours of study a day will not make your child better than three hours of study a day — it will only make her tired. Please let your child sleep late sometimes, play mahjong with Grandpa, help wash the New Year dumplings, and walk slowly in a park for 40 minutes. A well-rested Grade-8 student in February usually learns more in one school week than a tired one learns in three. Thank you for trusting this small, quiet plan. — *Mr Qin on behalf of the Grade-8 Homeroom Team, 25 December 2027*"

## ❓ Frequently Gently-Asked Questions

**Q1. Will the homeroom teacher collect this plan after the break?**  
A1. **No.** Nothing is collected. No one scores it. No one will read your private card.

**Q2. Will a student without this plan be punished on 16 February?**  
A2. **No.** This plan is fully optional. Some students do wonderfully with no plan at all.

**Q3. May a student share the card daily with a parent?**  
A3. **Yes, if the student wishes.** But parents are asked **not to demand** to see it; this is a student-owned tool.

**Q4. May a student borrow extra 8-Zone A5 cards?**  
A4. **Yes**, from the Student Affairs Office on 15 January between 12:30 and 13:00. Bring your student card; no sign-up needed.

**Q5. What if the plan makes me feel more tired, not less?**  
A5. **Stop using it immediately.** Any plan that makes rest smaller is the wrong plan. The school believes rest is the root of learning, not its enemy.
