# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Every Middle School in China Hold One "Parent-as-Teacher Day" Each Term?**

① Last Thursday morning, in our small 8-B classroom at Lihua Middle School, something almost never seen in a normal Chinese middle school happened. From **08:10 to 08:55** — one single 45-minute lesson — the person standing at the chalkboard was **not Mr Qin, our English homeroom teacher**; it was **Mr Shen, the father of my classmate Shen Kexin**. Mr Shen is a **train-signal engineer** on the Beijing–Shanghai high-speed railway. He wore his ordinary blue work jacket. He did not prepare a PowerPoint slide. He brought, in a cloth bag, **three small broken signal boxes** from his workplace, **one folded circuit drawing** on yellow paper, and **one real engineer's notebook** filled with his own handwriting from the year 2019. For 45 minutes, he explained — in plain Chinese, with almost no "professional words" — how a **high-speed train decides, in one-tenth of a second, whether to brake at a yellow signal**. At the end of the lesson, our class clapped for almost a full minute. That same evening, three Grade-9 homeroom teachers wrote in the school group, *"Can we do this once every term?"* I believe the answer should be **yes**. **This is why every middle school in China should hold at least one "Parent-as-Teacher Day" (家长讲师日) each term — one 45-minute lesson, in which an ordinary working parent, not a celebrity and not a school principal, quietly teaches a class.**

② **The first reason is simple: textbooks are small and working lives are huge.** Our Grade-8 textbooks, though well written, can only include a small picture of the adult working world. A physics chapter has two pages about friction on a wheel; it cannot also tell you how a real train-signal engineer calculates the exact distance needed to stop 400 tonnes of steel on a rainy night. A geography chapter mentions city rubbish, but it cannot describe how a sanitation worker (Shi Yifan's father, who will teach us in March) decides, at 04:30 in the morning, which of three districts her truck should start in. A "Parent-as-Teacher Day" gently **opens a small window** from the textbook into the real working street outside — not in a noisy career fair with posters and free candy, but in **one quiet 45-minute lesson**, in the same classroom where we do English Monday to Friday.

③ **The second reason is about respect.** In many Chinese middle schools today, students admire mostly **famous professions**: doctor, professor, software engineer, airline pilot. Noble jobs, all. But our parents, of course, do **every kind** of work: bus-drivers, kitchen cooks, veterinary nurses, village postal workers, accounting clerks, supermarket managers, rural teachers, hospital cleaners. **Every one of these jobs quietly holds a small city up.** When a **real bus-driver** of twenty-two years stands at our chalkboard for 45 minutes and explains, in simple words, how she reads seventeen mirrors to keep eleven children safe between two schools each morning, a Grade-8 student learns something her textbook will **never** be able to print: that the word *ordinary* is, in fact, the most important word in the Chinese working language. That respect, once grown, **does not disappear**.

④ **The third reason is about family closeness, which often quietly grows in Grade 8.** Some critics ask, *"Will children feel embarrassed to have their own parent teaching?"* My classmate Shen Kexin answered this for us. In the 25 minutes before Mr Shen walked in, Kexin sat at her desk with her head slightly down. By minute 12 of the lesson, when Mr Shen said gently, *"I still make a small mistake on the signal colour roughly twice a year, and then I must call a colleague to check — I am not perfect in this job and I never was,"* **I saw Kexin look up and smile at her father for perhaps the first time in a full term.** That quiet moment was worth, I think, more than a whole year of "family-relationship lectures" from a loudspeaker. A child who sees her parent respected at her own classroom chalkboard **comes home softer**. The family, quietly, gains a little warmth in December.

⑤ **Some readers may worry that it takes too much teacher-preparation time.** In fact, it does not. Mr Qin spent **twelve minutes** two weeks ago writing a small one-page invitation letter to Mr Shen, **eight minutes** phoning the safety office to confirm the factory samples were harmless, and **five minutes** on Thursday morning introducing Mr Shen. That is **25 minutes** of teacher time, once a term, for a 45-minute lesson that no textbook chapter could match. A **Parent-as-Teacher Day** is quite possibly the highest value a homeroom teacher in China can buy with 25 minutes of her time.

⑥ **Some readers worry that working parents will not have time or confidence.** Quite true: not every parent can come, and not every parent wishes to. That is why a "Parent-as-Teacher Day" must be **voluntary, gentle, and rotating** — any parent may say *no* with no reason given, no child is ever named or embarrassed, and the school simply waits until another family is free. Across a full three-year junior-high, if each class hosts one such lesson per term, that is **six lessons from six ordinary working parents** per student by graduation. Six small windows. Six ordinary working lives. Six quiet pieces of respect. **That is, I believe, enough to gently change how a Grade-9 student, in her future, looks at every bus-driver, every kitchen cook, every train-signal engineer she will ever meet.**

⑦ In summary, one 45-minute "Parent-as-Teacher Day" each term is not a big school reform. It is a **small quiet low-cost hour**. But like our Silent-Reading Morning and our Monthly Family-Story Evening, its quiet smallness is its strength. **Open one classroom. Invite one ordinary parent. Turn off the slide projector. Listen for 45 minutes.** That, I believe, may be one of the warmest, most honest lessons any Chinese middle school student can receive — and it is a lesson **every middle school in China can afford to give, starting this spring term.**

**注释：** signal engineer 信号工程师；circuit 电路；sanitation worker 环卫工人；veterinary nurse 宠物护理员；rotating 轮流的
