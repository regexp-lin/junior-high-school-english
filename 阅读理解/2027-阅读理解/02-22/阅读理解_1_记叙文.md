# 阅读理解（记叙文） **My First Solo Subway Ride**

① When I turned thirteen last April, my parents finally agreed to let me go to my weekly art class by subway **on my own**. For most city kids, taking Line 2 from School Road to Central Museum is a very normal trip. But for me — a girl whose parents had walked her to every corner since kindergarten — it felt like climbing a small mountain.

② On the first Saturday, Mom prepared a little paper card. On it she had written three stations, my class address, her phone number and a short sentence: *"If something goes wrong, stay calm and ask a station worker for help."* She slipped the card into my coat pocket and said, "Keep it next to your heart." Dad patted my head. "You'll be fine," he said simply, but I noticed his eyes shone a little.

③ The subway hall was full of moving legs. Cold wind from the tunnel blew up my sleeves. I bought my own ticket with a trembling finger, walked through the gate, and followed the moving crowd to the platform. When the silver train arrived with a sharp *whoosh*, my heart jumped into my throat. I pressed my back against the wall for two seconds, then stepped inside.

④ I sat down and counted each station quietly in my head. "One — Youth Park. Two — West Road. Three — ..." Between West Road and Central Museum, a kind grandmother sat next to me. She looked at my paper card, smiled, and said, "You're doing great, little one. I'll get off at the same station. Just follow me." Her gentle voice calmed my heart at once, like a warm cup of milk.

⑤ At Central Museum station, I got off with the grandmother and said a shy "thank you." She patted my shoulder and walked toward the north exit, disappearing into the crowd. I took out the card again, read it once more, and walked toward the south exit — exactly as Mom had told me. Ten minutes later, I saw the red gate of my art class. My teacher, Ms. Huang, looked at me, smiled widely, and said, "You arrived **all by yourself** today." I nodded, trying not to show how proud I felt.

⑥ Riding home that afternoon, the subway felt less frightening. The same metal train now seemed a little like an old friend. I realised that growing up is not a sudden jump, but a series of small subway rides: each one a little farther, each one a little braver. That night, I wrote on Mom's paper card: *"Thank you. I'm keeping this for the next trip."* I still have it today, folded neatly inside my English textbook.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why was the writer nervous about the subway ride?**

A. Because she had never been to school.  
B. Because she had always been walked everywhere by her parents.  
C. Because she was afraid of loud music.  
D. Because she could not read any station name.

**2. What did Mom write on the little paper card?**

A. Only her own name and phone number.  
B. Three stations, the class address, her phone number and a short sentence.  
C. A list of things to buy at the supermarket.  
D. A long poem in English.

**3. Who helped the writer during the ride?**

A. A young boy with a red cap.  
B. A kind grandmother sitting next to her.  
C. A tall man in a black suit.  
D. A loud policeman on the platform.

**4. What did Ms. Huang say to the writer?**

A. "You arrived all by yourself today."  
B. "You are always late these days."  
C. "Let's cancel today's class."  
D. "I prepared a gift for you."

**5. What does the writer learn from the trip?**

A. Subway trains are very dangerous.  
B. Growing up is a series of small brave steps, not a sudden jump.  
C. Paper cards are useless.  
D. Parents should never let children go out alone.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "whose parents had walked her to every corner"  
【解析】父母一直陪伴，独自出行让她紧张。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段卡片内容  
【解析】含三个站、地址、号码和一句叮嘱。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "a kind grandmother sat next to me"  
【解析】老奶奶帮助了她。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "You arrived all by yourself today"  
【解析】美术老师的原话。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段 "series of small subway rides: each one a little braver"  
【解析】成长是一次次小勇敢的旅程。故选 B。
