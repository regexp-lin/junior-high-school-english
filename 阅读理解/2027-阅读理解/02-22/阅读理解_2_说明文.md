# 阅读理解（说明文） **The Amazing Chinese Abacus**

① In many old Chinese shops, one wooden tool used to sit quietly on the counter — a rectangular frame filled with rows of round beads. When the shopkeeper's fingers flew over the beads with a soft *clack*, money was counted, prices were added, and customers walked away with a smile. This clever wooden tool is the **Chinese abacus** (算盘), a simple-looking friend that helped Chinese people calculate for more than 700 years.

② The abacus most of us know today took its shape during the **Ming Dynasty**. Its wooden frame holds a line of thin rods. On each rod, two beads sit above a middle bar and five beads sit below. Each lower bead stands for "one," and each upper bead stands for "five." By quickly moving beads up or down, a trained user can add, subtract, multiply and even divide very large numbers. Some masters can run the abacus faster than many people on a smartphone today.

③ Before the abacus, Chinese people used a different tool called **counting rods** — little wooden sticks placed on a board. Counting rods were useful, but they took up a lot of space and were easy to lose. A small abacus, on the other hand, could be hung on a shop wall or carried under an arm. This made the abacus quickly popular with farmers, merchants and even the imperial court.

④ The abacus is not only a tool; it is also a **trainer of the brain**. Children who learn abacus at a young age often develop stronger concentration and better number sense. In a study of 600 primary school children in Shanghai in 2024, those who had learned abacus for one year scored about **15 percent higher** on mental math tests than those who had not. Even more interesting, many of them could "see" a small abacus inside their mind and use it during exams.

⑤ The abacus also travelled abroad. During the Ming and Qing Dynasties, it reached Japan, Korea, Vietnam and beyond. In Japan, people slightly changed the design — one bead above and four below — and used it for school and business for centuries. Today, many world-class abacus competitions are still held every year in Asia, and some of the fastest competitors are children as young as ten.

⑥ Of course, modern computers and phones do most of our counting now. Yet the abacus is far from forgotten. In 2013, it was added to the **UNESCO list of the World Intangible Cultural Heritage**, standing proudly beside Chinese calligraphy and paper-cutting. The next time your finger slides gently on a smooth screen, perhaps it is worth remembering that, long before the first electronic chip, ten round wooden beads already taught the world how to count.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. During which dynasty did the abacus take the shape we know today?**

A. Tang Dynasty.  
B. Song Dynasty.  
C. Ming Dynasty.  
D. Qing Dynasty.

**2. On each rod of a Chinese abacus, how many beads are above the middle bar?**

A. Two.  
B. Five.  
C. Seven.  
D. Ten.

**3. Why did the abacus become popular more quickly than the counting rods?**

A. Because it was made of silver.  
B. Because it was small and easy to carry.  
C. Because it could write Chinese characters.  
D. Because it cost no money.

**4. What did the 2024 Shanghai study find?**

A. Abacus students became weaker in English.  
B. Abacus students scored about 15 percent higher on mental math tests.  
C. Abacus students could not use a pen.  
D. Abacus students always got full marks.

**5. What happened to the abacus in 2013?**

A. It was banned in shops.  
B. It was added to the UNESCO list of World Intangible Cultural Heritage.  
C. It was only used for children's toys.  
D. It was completely replaced by calculators.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "during the Ming Dynasty"  
【解析】明代形成定型。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "two beads sit above a middle bar"  
【解析】上两珠下五珠。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "could be hung on a shop wall or carried under an arm"  
【解析】便携小巧。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "scored about 15 percent higher on mental math tests"  
【解析】心算成绩高出 15%。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "UNESCO list of the World Intangible Cultural Heritage"  
【解析】2013 年入选世界非遗。故选 B。
