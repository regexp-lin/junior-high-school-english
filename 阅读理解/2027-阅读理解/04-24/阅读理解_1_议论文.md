# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Every Middle School Student Learn to Swim?**

① Summer is coming, and with it, sad news appears in newspapers almost every week: a teenager has drowned in a river, a pond, or a swimming pool. According to the National Water Safety Report in 2026, about **1,200 children** under 16 died of drowning in China last year, and most of them **could not swim**. This makes many parents, teachers, and doctors ask a serious question: **should every middle school student in China be required to learn swimming as a basic life skill?**

② Those who say **yes** have strong reasons. First, swimming is a **life-saving skill**. If a child falls into a pond or steps into deep river water, even ten seconds of floating on his back can be the difference between life and death. Second, swimming is good for **health**. It uses almost every muscle, builds strong lungs, and is gentler on joints than running or football. Third, it teaches **courage and calm**: a student who has learned to breathe slowly in cold water often becomes braver in other parts of life, too.

③ However, other people worry about the **cost** and the **pressure**. Some small rural towns have no public pool at all; in these places, "required swimming" may be unfair. Buying a pool or renting one every week is expensive, and many parents already pay a lot for tutoring classes. Adding one more skill means **less free time** for tired students, and in some families, **more worry about money**. Simply telling every school to teach swimming does not make a safe pool suddenly appear.

④ There is also the worry that making swimming a **hard requirement** may turn a healthy sport into a **stressful test**. Some students are naturally afraid of water. If they are told, "You cannot pass Grade 9 without swimming 25 metres," they may feel trapped. A child who is pushed too hard may learn to hate swimming, and that is the opposite of what we want.

⑤ In my view, swimming should be **strongly encouraged**, but rolled out step by step. Cities with pools and well-trained coaches can start "water safety weeks" for all Grade 7 students. Schools without pools can team up with nearby hotels, sports centres, or community clubs to share pool time. The government can offer free training places for families who cannot pay, and rural schools can first teach **dry-land safety lessons**: how to shout for help, how to throw a rope, how to use a long branch to save a friend without falling in yourself.

⑥ A world where every 13-year-old can float for two minutes is a safer world. But we must move **with care**, not with fear. Swimming is not a magic solution, and it must not become another heavy bag on a child's shoulders. With patient planning, cooperation, and kindness, we can slowly help every Chinese middle school student become **friends with the water**, not afraid of it.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why do many people ask the question in the article?**

A. Because swimming pools are too expensive to build.  
B. Because many teenagers have drowned, most of whom could not swim.  
C. Because students do not like P.E. classes.  
D. Because swimming is very old-fashioned.

**2. Which is NOT a benefit of swimming mentioned in the article?**

A. It is a life-saving skill.  
B. It is good for the muscles and lungs.  
C. It teaches calm and courage.  
D. It makes students jump higher in basketball.

**3. What worry is raised in paragraph ③?**

A. Some towns have no pool, and lessons can be expensive.  
B. Swimming ruins school uniforms.  
C. Teachers hate water.  
D. Every child can already swim.

**4. What solution does paragraph ⑤ offer for schools without pools?**

A. Closing these schools.  
B. Teaming up with hotels, sports centres, or community clubs, and first teaching dry-land safety lessons.  
C. Building a pool inside every classroom.  
D. Sending students to another country.

**5. What is the writer's final attitude?**

A. Swimming should never be taught in school.  
B. Every student must pass a hard swim test or fail the grade.  
C. Swimming should be strongly encouraged but rolled out step by step with care.  
D. Only boys should learn swimming.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段数据与描述  
【解析】许多少年因不会游泳而溺水。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段好处清单  
【解析】并未提到"让篮球跳得高"。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段担忧  
【解析】乡镇无泳池、费用高。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段解决方案  
【解析】合作共享泳池 + 陆地安全课。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑤–⑥段  
【解析】鼓励但稳步推进。故选 C。
