# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Bicycle Early Class with Dad**

① I gave up my first bike when I was eleven. After the third fall, which left a long scratch down my knee and a longer scratch down my pride, I pushed the bicycle into the corner of the storeroom. My parents did not argue with me. My dad only said, "When you are ready, it will still be there." **Three years passed.** The bicycle stayed, slowly covered by dust and old boxes, like a small forgotten idea.

② In October, I failed the ten-minute cycling test on our Grade 8 sports class. The P.E. teacher, Ms. He, did not scold anyone; she only said kindly, "Some skills you must carry into Grade 9. Think about it." That night, at dinner, Dad noticed my silence. He said, quietly, "The first bike is still in the storeroom, you know. And your old dad, believe it or not, used to be a paper-boy on a big black bicycle in the early nineties." I looked at him. He looked back with that small, patient half-smile I had seen since I was three.

③ We started the following Saturday, at **06:45**, in an almost empty park near our flat. The leaves were a thin, wet red; the sky was a soft grey. Dad had **cleaned the bike the night before** — tyres pumped, chain oiled, the small yellow bell on the left polished. He had also brought a second bicycle for himself, borrowed from our neighbour, Mr. Wu. "We will ride together," he said. "I do not want to just stand and watch."

④ The first 15 minutes were mostly falling. Not big falls — small, confused ones, the kind where the front wheel turns suddenly and both feet hit the ground with a small thump. Each time, my heart shrank a little. Each time, Dad did not rush over. He only waited, and then said calmly, "**Look where you want to go, not at the front wheel.**" He must have repeated that sentence thirty times that morning. It slowly crept into my body — more than into my head.

⑤ On the third Saturday, I managed twelve metres without falling. On the fifth, thirty. On the seventh, the whole long path around the lake, twice. Dad rode behind me, a few metres back, never too close, never too far. Sometimes he whistled an old folk song I did not know; sometimes he said nothing for ten minutes; sometimes he called out, "Shoulder relaxed, Xiaotao. **Relax the shoulder, and the wheel relaxes too.**" I did not fully understand what he meant then. I understand it now.

⑥ On an early November morning — a bright, cold Saturday — we stopped at the top of a small hill by the park pond. Dad turned his bicycle around, looked at me seriously, and said, "Today, no hands from me if you fall. Only you and the bike." I nodded. I pushed off. The cold air bit my face; the front wheel wobbled for half a second; then it straightened, and I felt, for the first time in three years, that small, steady **glide** — the world becoming smooth under my wheels.

⑦ Halfway down the hill, I passed an old man feeding sparrows near a stone bench. He looked up and smiled at me, the way old men smile at running children. In my rear mirror, I saw Dad following slowly, his hand off the handlebar, clapping three small, quiet claps as if to himself. I pretended not to see. But my eyes burned a little, and this time it had nothing to do with the cold wind.

⑧ That evening I wrote in my journal, "*On 06 November 2027, I was no longer afraid of my own bicycle.*" It took me three weeks to add the next sentence. It took me much longer to really understand it: "**Some things we give up when we are eleven are not lost. They are only waiting for us to grow kind enough to try again, with someone who does not rush us.**" The bike still waits by the door every Saturday. My dad still waits with me. And slowly, slowly, the ten-metre boy who gave up in fear is becoming the fourteen-year-old girl who knows that **falling three times is not the same as not knowing how to ride**.

---

**注释：** scratch 擦伤；pride 自尊；paper-boy 送报男孩；wobble 摇晃；glide 滑行

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer give up her first bike at the age of eleven?**

A. Because the bike was broken beyond repair.  
B. Because after the third fall, which left a long scratch down her knee and a longer scratch down her pride, she pushed the bike into the storeroom corner.  
C. Because her parents refused to keep it.  
D. Because she moved to another city.

**2. What did Dad do the night before their first Saturday practice?**

A. He sold the old bike and bought a new one.  
B. He cleaned the bike — pumped the tyres, oiled the chain, polished the small yellow bell — and borrowed a second bicycle from the neighbour so he could ride with her.  
C. He told her to practise by herself.  
D. He bought her new shoes.

**3. Which sentence did Dad repeat most often during the early mornings?**

A. "Do not cry."  
B. "Hold on tight."  
C. "Look where you want to go, not at the front wheel."  
D. "Ride as fast as you can."

**4. On the November morning at the top of the small hill, what "test" did Dad give his daughter?**

A. He raced her down the hill.  
B. He said that if she fell, he would not use his hand to catch her; only she and the bike.  
C. He blindfolded her.  
D. He told her to ride for an hour without a break.

**5. What is the deeper lesson the writer puts in her journal?**

A. Bikes are dangerous and should be avoided.  
B. Some things we give up at eleven are not lost; they are waiting for us to grow kind enough to try again, with someone who does not rush us.  
C. Only fathers can teach children to ride bikes.  
D. Falling three times means you cannot ride.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "After the third fall ... scratch down my pride ... pushed the bicycle into the corner of the storeroom"  
【解析】放弃原因直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "cleaned the bike the night before ... borrowed from our neighbour, Mr. Wu"  
【解析】备车细节直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "Look where you want to go, not at the front wheel."  
【解析】高频口令直引。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "no hands from me if you fall. Only you and the bike"  
【解析】独立测试直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段日记引言  
【解析】耐心重启的人生启示。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。
