# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Paper Textbooks Be Replaced by Digital Ones?**

① When the new term started, Mr. Zhao, our geography teacher, showed us a small black tablet. "Next year," he said, "our school may try to put **all your textbooks** into this machine. No more heavy paper books in the bag." In the classroom, someone clapped; someone sighed. The question is not as simple as it first looks: **should paper textbooks be replaced by digital ones?**

② People who **agree** with the change talk about three clear points. First, weight. A Grade 8 schoolbag can easily weigh **more than 6 kg**. Many students' shoulders hurt by Friday afternoon. A single 400-gram tablet, in theory, can hold **every textbook, dictionary, and workbook**. Second, digital books can **grow and move**. If a mistake is found in a paper book, it takes a whole new printing to fix. On a tablet, the fix may arrive in one night, together with animated maps, short videos of volcanoes, and voices of native English speakers. Third, paper books cost a lot of **trees**. A big school of 2,000 students may need 8 tons of paper each year. Digital books can cut this number down and help the blue-green earth breathe.

③ Others **prefer paper** and give strong reasons. First, reading on a screen hurts the eyes. More than **60 %** of Chinese middle-school students already wear glasses; adding another screen might push this number even higher. Second, paper feels different. When you hold a paper book, you can touch the cover, smell the ink, and mark the margin with a yellow pen. These small actions help many students remember words longer. Third, machines may fail. A low battery, a broken screen, or a software error can pause a whole class. In a village school far from a shop, such "small" problems can become big ones.

④ In my view, we do not need to choose "all paper" or "all digital"; the wise way is to **mix them carefully**. Chinese, English, and maths — subjects that need deep reading and quiet thinking — are better in paper for now. Science, geography, and history — subjects with many changing maps, photos, and videos — may be stronger on a tablet. The school can also keep **"paper-only hours"** every day, for example, the first hour in the morning, to give eyes a rest.

⑤ Technology is a good servant, but not always a good master. A textbook, paper or digital, is only a door. What matters is the eager mind that walks through it. As long as students still **ask real questions** and still **enjoy learning**, the door can be old or new — both ways lead to the same bright room.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main question the writer discusses?**

A. Whether paper textbooks should be replaced by digital ones.  
B. How to reduce the weight of school bags.  
C. Why students' eyes are becoming worse in China.  
D. How teachers should buy tablets for class.

**2. Which of the following is NOT a reason given by supporters of digital textbooks?**

A. A single tablet can hold many books.  
B. Digital books can be updated quickly with rich media.  
C. Paper books may waste many trees.  
D. Digital books never go wrong, even when the battery is low.

**3. According to paragraph ③, why do some students like paper books?**

A. Paper books are always more colourful than tablets.  
B. Paper books help many students remember words better.  
C. Paper books are cheaper to print than tablets.  
D. Paper books can play English audio.

**4. What is the writer's own opinion about the new plan?**

A. Every paper book must be replaced by a tablet as soon as possible.  
B. No digital book should ever enter the classroom.  
C. Paper and digital should be mixed wisely, and "paper-only hours" can be kept.  
D. Schools should stop using both paper and digital books.

**5. What does the last paragraph mainly tell us?**

A. Old doors are always better than new ones.  
B. Technology is a tool; the eager mind matters most.  
C. Students must buy both tablets and paper books.  
D. Teachers should learn computers first.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句 "should paper textbooks be replaced by digital ones?"  
【解析】核心议题为纸质教材是否被替换为电子教材。B、C、D 非主题。故选 A。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节否定题  
【定位】第②段三点理由：重量、更新、保护树木  
【解析】"永不出问题"为捏造，且与第③段描述电子设备也会失灵矛盾。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "these small actions help many students remember words longer"  
【解析】翻书、闻纸、画笔记有助记忆。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第④段 "mix them carefully... paper-only hours"  
【解析】作者主张按学科混合使用并保留"纸质时段"。A、B、D 与作者观点不符。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨推断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Technology is a good servant... the eager mind that walks through it"  
【解析】技术是工具，真正重要的是好学的头脑。A、C、D 与主旨不符。故选 B。

**词汇注释（超纲词）**  
- **digital** [adj.] 数字的；电子的  
- **margin** [n.] 书页空白处  
- **servant** [n.] 仆人（此处比喻工具）
