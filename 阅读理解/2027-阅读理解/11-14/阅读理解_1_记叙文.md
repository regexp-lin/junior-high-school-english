# 阅读理解（记叙文） **My First High-Speed Train Ride to Visit Grandpa**

① Last Saturday was the **first time in my life** I took a train on my own. I am fourteen. The train was the **07:42 G-class high-speed train** from our city to the small city of Jinhua, where my grandpa lives. For weeks I had been asking Mum to let me go. Grandpa's seventy-second birthday was on Sunday, and our family could not all travel together — Dad had a weekend shift, Mum had to take my younger brother to a swimming competition. "If we all cannot go," I had said very seriously, "I will go by myself." Mum looked at me, counted my age twice in her head, and finally, on Thursday evening, she said: "Fine. One person. One train. One night. You come back on Sunday afternoon. And you call me at every station."

② I could not sleep well on Friday night. I packed my small red backpack six times. A toothbrush, a small towel, a clean shirt, my senior-high entrance review book (just in case), a packet of Grandpa's favourite sugar-free sweets from the local shop, and a **thick yellow envelope** containing my return ticket, 300 yuan in cash, my ID card, and Mum's phone number written carefully three times on three different pieces of paper. Mum had said, "Hide one copy in your shoe. Never put everything in the same pocket." I smiled, but I did it.

③ At 06:50 on Saturday morning, the station was brighter than I expected. Morning light spilled through the tall glass roof. The floor was full of small suitcases and quiet families. I queued at the security check, passed it slowly, and reached Platform 3. I had never felt so **aware of myself** — of my own steps, of my own breath, of my own two feet standing on the shining floor next to strangers. At 07:30, a silver train slid in along the track like a long arrow. My heart moved forwards and backwards at the same time.

④ I found Carriage 7, Seat 12A — a window seat. I put my backpack under the seat, sent Mum a photo of the seat number, and took out my review book. For the first fifteen minutes I could not read one line. The train moved; the old houses and yellow trees of our city moved faster than I could follow; and in the glass my own small face looked older than usual. A young woman across the aisle smiled at me and asked gently, "First time alone?" I nodded. She said, "You'll be fine. I did the same at your age. Breathe slowly."

⑤ The train reached Jinhua South at **09:18**. I was supposed to take **Exit B** and look for a tall yellow sign that read "集合区 Meeting Point". I walked past Exit A by mistake, doubled back, asked a station helper, and finally found the yellow sign. And there, under it, was **Grandpa**: thin, smiling, holding a small paper bag. I almost ran — but I did not. I walked steadily, the way a fourteen-year-old walks when she has just handled two hours on a train by herself. Grandpa's eyes were a little wet. He said only, "Xiaoqi, you have grown." Then he handed me the small paper bag. Inside was a soft **warm steamed bun** — my favourite since I was four.

⑥ The whole weekend felt **bigger** than weekends usually do. I helped Grandpa water the three pots of winter greens on his little balcony. We walked to the small market, and I, not he, carried the vegetables back. On Sunday morning I cooked him a simple breakfast of egg-and-onion noodles — a recipe I had practised twice at home. He ate quietly, put down his chopsticks, and said, "When I was fourteen, I had never travelled by myself. The world is different now. **That is, mostly, a good thing.**"

⑦ On Sunday afternoon, he walked me back to Jinhua South. At the yellow sign he took out two small things from his inside coat pocket: a red envelope (not money, but a **handwritten letter** for me to read on the train), and a **small old compass** he had used when he was a young engineer. "Not for getting lost," he said. "For remembering that, when the world is new, a small friend in your pocket is enough." I hugged him then. I am not usually a hugger. The train north left at 15:40.

⑧ On the G-class train home, I opened his letter carefully. It was not long. It ended with one sentence in his small, familiar blue handwriting:

> *"A first journey alone is not about reaching the station. It is about discovering that the person standing at the station is yourself — slightly older than yesterday, and slightly braver than you thought."*

I put the compass in my left pocket, the letter in my right, and watched the yellow fields go past the window. Outside, the sun was beginning to sink. Inside, something small and steady had, I believe, **risen in me**.

**注释：** G-class high-speed train 高铁G字头列车；platform 站台；carriage 车厢；compass 指南针；steady 平稳的

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer travel to Jinhua alone?**

A. Because she wanted to escape from school.  
B. Because Grandpa's 72nd birthday was on Sunday and the family could not all go — Dad had a weekend shift and Mum had to take her younger brother to a swimming competition — so the writer went alone.  
C. Because she was sent to a new school.  
D. Because her parents could not afford three tickets.

**2. Why did Mum ask the writer to put one copy of the phone number in her shoe?**

A. Because shoes are bigger than pockets.  
B. Because Mum wanted to hide money.  
C. Because if you hide one copy in your shoe, you do not put everything in the same pocket — a safety idea when travelling alone.  
D. Because shoes smell strongly.

**3. How did the writer feel during the first fifteen minutes on the train?**

A. Sleepy and quiet.  
B. She could not read one line; the train moved, old houses and yellow trees moved faster than she could follow; her own face in the glass looked older than usual — she was aware and slightly nervous.  
C. Hungry and cold.  
D. Angry at her mother.

**4. What did Grandpa give the writer at Jinhua South?**

A. Only a small warm steamed bun.  
B. Only a red envelope with money.  
C. A small warm steamed bun at the station on arrival; and before she left, a handwritten letter and an old engineer's compass — "not for getting lost", but as "a small friend in your pocket when the world is new".  
D. A mobile phone and keys.

**5. What is the main message of Grandpa's letter in the last paragraph?**

A. A first journey is all about the station.  
B. A first journey alone is not about reaching the station; it is about discovering that the person standing at the station is yourself — slightly older than yesterday and slightly braver than you thought.  
C. Students should not travel alone.  
D. A compass is the best travel tool.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "Dad had a weekend shift ... swimming competition"  
【解析】背景直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第②段妈妈叮嘱 "Never put everything in the same pocket"  
【解析】安全意识。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "could not read one line ... own small face looked older"  
【解析】首次独行的敏感体验直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段到站礼物 + 第⑦段分别礼物  
【解析】两处礼物综合直引。A、B、D 均不全。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段信末引句  
【解析】独行的精神收获。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。
