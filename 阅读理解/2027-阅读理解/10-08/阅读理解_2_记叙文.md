# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Tin Box Under My Father's Desk**

① I had lived in the same flat for fourteen years, and I thought I knew every corner of it. One rainy Thursday evening, however, I knocked a pencil under my father's old wooden desk, and when I bent down to pick it up, my fingers touched something cold, square and forgotten — a **small tin box** pushed far back against the wall.

② It was the size of a school lunch box, dark green with a little rust on one corner. I pulled it out. My father was still at work; my mother was in the kitchen with the radio on. I hesitated. Opening a box that did not belong to you was, as my grandmother would say, not polite. But the box was not locked. Curiosity is a soft, quiet pressure. After a long minute, I slowly lifted the lid.

③ Inside there was no money, no secret letter, no strange treasure. Instead, the box held **six things**: a child's first tiny red hat; a school badge that said *Grade One, Wind Bell Primary School*; my first baby tooth, wrapped in white paper; a small crayon drawing of a house with a tree beside it, signed "Xiaomu, 6"; a photograph of me at maybe age two, being carried on my father's shoulders in a field of yellow rape flowers; and a single folded sheet of newspaper, thin and yellow with age.

④ I opened the newspaper last. It was a page from fourteen years ago, dated the day I was born. The weather forecast said: "Cloudy morning, sunny afternoon, slight wind from the southeast." At the top my father had written, in a steady pen: **"Little Xiaomu came at 4:11 in the afternoon. Seven pounds. Healthy. All well."**

⑤ My eyes suddenly felt hot. For fourteen years, this box had been sitting three metres away from where I did my homework. My father — who rarely says "I love you", who usually asks me only about my grades — had quietly built, year by year, a tiny museum of my life. He had kept my first tooth. He had kept the drawing I did at six and signed with two characters I could barely shape. He had kept a newspaper just because a baby he had never even met yet was coming.

⑥ I heard the front door open. My father was home. I put everything back as I had found it, closed the lid carefully, and pushed the box into its corner. In the living room he shook the rain off his coat, looked up at me, and said only, "You look tired. Did you finish the maths?" "Almost done, Dad," I said.

⑦ I went back to my desk and wrote until nine. For the first time that year, I did not mind that my father did not say sweet words. Somewhere, thirty centimetres below our feet, was a little green box full of them — kept quiet, for fourteen years, the way he knew how.

⑧ That night, before turning off the light, I pulled one of my old drawings out of my schoolbag — a rough sketch of our flat I had done in art class. I wrote on the back, in careful small letters: **"For Dad, from Xiaomu, 14."** Then I slipped it under the green tin's lid. I did not say a word. I thought he might find it, one rainy Thursday evening, many years from now.

---

**注释：** tin box 铁盒；rust 锈；crayon 蜡笔；rape flower 油菜花；weather forecast 天气预报

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did the writer discover the small green tin box?**

A. Her father gave it to her as a birthday present.  
B. She knocked a pencil under her father's desk and felt the box against the wall while picking up the pencil.  
C. She received it in the post from her grandmother.  
D. She bought it at a second-hand market.

**2. Which of the following was **NOT** in the tin box?**

A. A child's tiny red hat.  
B. A crayon drawing signed "Xiaomu, 6".  
C. Three hundred yuan in cash.  
D. A yellow newspaper page from the day the writer was born.

**3. What had the writer's father written on the newspaper?**

A. A letter to the writer's grandmother.  
B. "Little Xiaomu came at 4:11 in the afternoon. Seven pounds. Healthy. All well."  
C. A list of daily chores.  
D. A shopping list for the weekend.

**4. Why did the writer's eyes "feel hot" (Paragraph 5)?**

A. Because she was angry with her father for keeping secrets.  
B. Because she suddenly realised that her father, who rarely says loving words, had quietly kept a small museum of her whole life for fourteen years.  
C. Because the light in the room was too strong.  
D. Because she had cut her hand on the box.

**5. What does the writer's own small drawing in the tin box at the end most likely mean?**

A. She wants to throw away the box.  
B. She has stopped loving her father.  
C. She silently answers her father's long years of quiet love with a small gift of her own, hoping he will find it one day.  
D. She wants to sell her drawings.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "I knocked a pencil under my father's old wooden desk ... my fingers touched something cold, square and forgotten — a small tin box"  
【解析】拾笔时无意发现。A、C、D 均未出现。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段列出"six things"，其中无现金  
【解析】红帽、徽章、乳牙、蜡笔画、合影、报纸，皆在。现金未提。A、B、D 均在其中。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段原句 "Little Xiaomu came at 4:11 in the afternoon. Seven pounds. Healthy. All well."  
【解析】父亲在报纸上写下出生当日记录。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "my father ... who rarely says 'I love you' ... had quietly built, year by year, a tiny museum of my life."  
【解析】感动于父亲沉默却绵长的爱。A、C、D 完全误读。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】写作意图/推理题  
【定位】第⑧段 "I slipped it under the green tin's lid. I did not say a word."  
【解析】以沉默回应沉默的爱，是全文情感升华。A、B、D 与文本相反或荒谬。故选 C。
