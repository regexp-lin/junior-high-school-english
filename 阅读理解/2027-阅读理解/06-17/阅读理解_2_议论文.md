# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Schools Teach More Cooking Classes?**

At our school, students learn maths, Chinese, English, science and P.E. But one basic life skill is almost missing: cooking. Some schools now have a short cooking class once a month. Is this enough? I do not think so. I believe every middle school should teach more cooking classes, at least **once a week**.

First, cooking is a basic life skill, just like swimming or riding a bicycle. In the past, many children learned cooking from their parents at home. Today, however, parents are often busy. Food comes from delivery apps or school canteens. More and more young people in their twenties say they cannot even boil a simple bowl of noodles or cook an egg. A 10-minute cooking class cannot teach skills that really work. A full weekly class, with real pots and knives, can.

Second, cooking helps students learn about healthy food. In a class, students can compare a plate of home-made vegetables with a box of instant noodles. They can check how much salt, sugar and oil is in both. This kind of learning is far more useful than remembering numbers from a textbook. When a 14-year-old has cut a bunch of cabbage and washed her own rice, she starts to pay more attention to what she puts in her body. Over time, this builds a healthier habit than any poster on the wall.

Third, cooking is a quiet way to teach science and maths. How much water do you need for 200 grams of rice? Why does oil jump when water touches it? Why does bread become brown in the oven? Every cooking step hides a small science lesson. A student who helps wash vegetables, cut them into equal pieces and put them in boiling water is also practising measurement, patience and timing. These are the same skills she uses in a chemistry lab or on a maths test.

Of course, some people worry that cooking classes are "a waste of study time". They think students should focus on main subjects and leave cooking for later. I understand this worry, but I believe a balance is possible. A weekly cooking class only takes 45 – 60 minutes; the total school year still has hundreds of hours for maths and science. And a tired student may even concentrate better in the next maths class after 40 minutes of happy, hands-on work.

In a country so rich in food and culture, it is strange that many of our students cannot cook the simplest dishes of their own region. If we want our young people to grow up strong, healthy and independent, we should bring a pan, an apron and a kind teacher into their classrooms every week.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the author's main opinion?**

A. Cooking is too difficult for middle school students.  
B. Every middle school should teach more cooking classes, at least once a week.  
C. Only some famous schools should teach cooking.  
D. Cooking should replace maths and Chinese.

**2. What reason does the author give in paragraph 2?**

A. Cooking tastes good, so everyone loves it.  
B. Many young people cannot do basic cooking at home because parents are busy and food comes from apps or canteens.  
C. Schools have too much free time.  
D. Cooking can make students richer.

**3. What does paragraph 3 mainly say?**

A. Cooking is dangerous for young children.  
B. Home-made food always costs more than instant food.  
C. Cooking helps students learn about healthy food and build better eating habits.  
D. Students should eat only raw vegetables.

**4. According to paragraph 4, how does cooking help with science and maths?**

A. It replaces all science classes.  
B. It teaches students to hate numbers.  
C. Cooking steps hide small science lessons and practise skills like measurement, patience and timing.  
D. It lets students skip the chemistry lab.

**5. How does the author respond to the worry that cooking classes waste study time?**

A. The author agrees and wants fewer cooking classes.  
B. The author says a 45 – 60-minute weekly class is a small part of the year, and students may even concentrate better after hands-on work.  
C. The author says students should only cook in the evening.  
D. The author says cooking should be done alone at home.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第 1 段末句  
【解析】作者主张至少每周一节烹饪课。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段  
【解析】家长忙+外卖普及，年轻人缺乏基础烹饪能力。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】段落大意题  
【定位】第 3 段  
【解析】烹饪帮助认识健康饮食。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段  
【解析】烹饪过程隐含科学并训练测量、耐心、时序。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段  
【解析】每周仅 45–60 分钟，占比小；且动手后可能提升专注。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
