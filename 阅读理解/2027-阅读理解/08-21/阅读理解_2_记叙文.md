# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Afternoon the Blue Vase Fell**

① When I was in Grade 7, we lived in a small flat on the fourth floor. On the low wooden table in our living room stood a tall **blue-and-white vase**. It was older than I was. My mother had bought it before I was born, on the first trip she took to Jingdezhen with my father. There was always a thin line of sunlight crossing its round belly in the afternoon. My mother used to say, "That vase is the quiet soul of our home."

② One Saturday afternoon in late April, my mother went out to visit a sick friend, and my father was downstairs buying vegetables. I was alone in the flat, bouncing a small basketball in the living room, even though my mother had told me not to. I said to myself, "Just ten times, just by the sofa. Nothing will happen." I bounced the ball once, twice, three times — then the fourth bounce went too high. The ball jumped away, hit the table, and the tall blue vase slowly **tipped over**. I saw it fall as if in slow motion. When it landed on the wooden floor, it broke into **seven or eight pieces**, some small, some sharp.

③ For a full minute I just stood there, holding the ball tightly. My heart was beating so hard that I could hear it in my ears. I wanted to pick the pieces up, but my hands were shaking. Then I heard slow footsteps in the corridor. It was my father with his bag of vegetables. He opened the door, looked at the floor, looked at me, and did not speak for a long time.

④ "I… I'm sorry, Dad," I said. My voice sounded small and strange to my own ears. "I was just bouncing the ball. I didn't mean to."

⑤ My father put his vegetables on the kitchen table. Then he walked slowly into the living room, knelt down on the wooden floor, and began to gather the broken pieces **one by one**, with great care. He did not shout. He did not cry. He just said softly, "Go and bring me a small box from your desk."

⑥ I brought him a cardboard box. My father placed every piece of the vase gently inside it. Then he looked up at me.

> "Your mother will be sad tonight," he said quietly. "But listen to me carefully. I will not hide what happened from her. And you — you are going to tell her yourself. A broken vase can only be glued; a broken honesty is much harder to repair."

⑦ That evening, when my mother came home, I walked to her before she had even taken off her coat. "Mum, I have to tell you something," I said. My voice was shaking. I told her everything — the basketball, the ten bounces that became four, the blue vase falling, and the cardboard box in the living room. I waited for her to be very angry. But my mother only sat down on the sofa, held the box on her lap, and was quiet for a long time.

⑧ Finally, she looked at me with tired, kind eyes and said, "Thank you for telling me the truth. The vase is only a thing. We can live without it. But a son who can come to his mother and say, 'I did this, I'm sorry,' — that is a much more valuable thing." Then she let me cry quietly into her shoulder.

⑨ A few weeks later, my father used a special glue to put the pieces of the vase back together. It never looked perfect again; you could always see the fine silver lines of the glue. But my mother placed it back on the same low wooden table, and every afternoon the thin line of sunlight still crossed its round belly. My father looked at me with a small smile and said, "See? **Some broken things, when we are honest about them, become the most beautiful things in the house.**"

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What did the writer do in the living room on that Saturday afternoon?**

A. The writer read quietly on the sofa.  
B. The writer bounced a small basketball near the sofa, against his mother's rule.  
C. The writer helped his father wash the vegetables.  
D. The writer painted the vase with a new colour.

**2. What happened when the fourth bounce of the ball went too high?**

A. The ball hit the TV screen.  
B. The ball jumped onto the table and the blue vase tipped over, breaking into seven or eight pieces.  
C. The ball disappeared into the kitchen.  
D. Nothing happened at all.

**3. How did the writer's father react after he came home and saw the broken vase?**

A. He shouted loudly at the writer.  
B. He did not speak for a long time, then quietly asked for a small box and carefully gathered the pieces; he said the writer had to tell the mother himself.  
C. He hid the pieces and told the writer to keep it a secret.  
D. He threw the pieces into the rubbish bin without a word.

**4. What did the writer's mother say when she finally heard the truth?**

A. "I will never forgive you for this."  
B. "The vase is only a thing, but a son who can tell his mother the truth is a much more valuable thing."  
C. "You must buy me a new vase tomorrow."  
D. "I don't care about you any more."

**5. Which sentence best expresses the main lesson of the story?**

A. Children should never play at home.  
B. Some broken things, when we are honest about them, become the most beautiful things in the house.  
C. Old vases should always be kept in a locked room.  
D. A good parent should never talk about broken things with children.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "bouncing a small basketball in the living room, even though my mother had told me not to"  
【解析】原文明确说明在违反妈妈规定地拍球。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "The ball jumped away, hit the table ... broke into seven or eight pieces"  
【解析】原文直接描述过程。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③、⑤、⑥段  
【解析】父亲不大声责骂，让儿子自己告知母亲，符合 B。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑧段 "The vase is only a thing. We can live without it ... that is a much more valuable thing"  
【解析】原文直接引用妈妈的话。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑨段末 "Some broken things, when we are honest about them, become the most beautiful things in the house"  
【解析】全文以花瓶事件为载体，强调诚实让"破碎"升华。A、C、D 均与主旨不符。故选 B。
