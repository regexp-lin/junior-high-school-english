# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Does the Moon Keep Changing Its Shape?**

① On a quiet summer evening, if you look at the sky two or three nights in a row, you will find that the moon is never the same shape. Sometimes it is a thin, clean silver line, like a fingernail; sometimes it is half of a cake; sometimes it is full and round, like a big plate. The Chinese traditional calendar even uses these changes to mark each month. **Why does the moon change its shape so regularly?** The secret is not really in the moon itself.

② The moon is a rocky, grey ball, very much like a smaller brother of the Earth. It does not shine with its own light. Like us, it borrows light from the sun. One half of the moon is always lit up by sunlight; the other half is always in the dark. From space, the moon would actually look like a round ball that is half-bright and half-black, the same way every night.

③ So why, from Earth, does it seem to grow and shrink? The answer lies in **where we stand**. As the moon travels in a slow circle around the Earth, the bright half of the moon faces us at different angles. Imagine yourself holding a ball that is painted white on one side and black on the other. As you turn around, a friend sitting on the sofa sees more white, then more black, and back again. The moon does the same thing in the sky.

④ When the moon is between the sun and the Earth, its bright half faces the sun, and the dark half faces us. The moon is now almost invisible. This phase is called **new moon**. A few days later, the moon has moved along its circle, and we begin to see a thin silver curve on one side — the **crescent**. After a week, we see a half circle (first quarter). After two weeks, the moon is now on the opposite side of the Earth from the sun, and the whole bright half faces us — the **full moon**. Then the shape slowly shrinks again, back to new moon. The whole cycle takes about **29.5 days**.

⑤ Because the moon's path is slightly tilted, we do not usually see an **eclipse** every month. Only when the sun, the Earth and the moon all line up almost perfectly does the moon's shadow fall on the Earth (a solar eclipse) or the Earth's shadow fall on the moon (a lunar eclipse). These are special moments worth a night of sky-watching.

⑥ The "changing" moon is thus a kind of big, slow clock in the sky. It is not really changing; we are only seeing different parts of its bright side. For thousands of years, farmers have planted crops by it, sailors have sailed by it, and poets have written by it. Next time a full moon rises at the end of a summer day, look up and remember: you are watching a quiet, steady ball of rock, simply turning its brightest face towards you for one night.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the passage mainly about?**

A. Why the moon is cold.  
B. Why the moon seems to change its shape in the sky.  
C. How to land on the moon.  
D. How poems about the moon are written.

**2. Does the moon make its own light, according to the passage?**

A. Yes, it glows on its own.  
B. No, it borrows light from the sun; one half is always lit up, and the other half is always dark.  
C. Yes, its light comes from Chinese lanterns.  
D. No, its light comes from the Earth.

**3. Why does the moon look like different shapes from Earth?**

A. Because the moon is really shrinking.  
B. As the moon travels around the Earth, its bright half faces us at different angles, so we see different portions of it.  
C. Because clouds change the moon's shape.  
D. Because the Earth is painted white on one side.

**4. Which shape do we see when the moon is on the opposite side of the Earth from the sun?**

A. New moon.  
B. Crescent.  
C. First quarter.  
D. Full moon.

**5. Why don't we see an eclipse every month, according to paragraph ⑤?**

A. Because the moon's path is slightly tilted, so the sun, the Earth and the moon do not line up perfectly every month.  
B. Because the moon refuses.  
C. Because the Earth moves too slowly.  
D. Because the sun goes to sleep.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文  
【解析】月相变化。A/C/D 均非主题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "It does not shine with its own light... borrows light from the sun."  
【解析】借光反射。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段  
【解析】视角变化。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "the whole bright half faces us — the full moon."  
【解析】满月。A/B/C 均不符。故选 D。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "the moon's path is slightly tilted..."  
【解析】轨道倾斜。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。
