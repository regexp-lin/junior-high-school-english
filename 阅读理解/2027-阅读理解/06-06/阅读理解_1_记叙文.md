# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Stranger Who Fixed My Bicycle Chain**

① Last Wednesday, I was riding home from school along the new park road. The sky was turning grey, and the wind smelled of rain. About three kilometres from home, my bicycle suddenly made a strange noise, and my feet started to spin freely in the air. I looked down. The chain had slipped off. At the same moment, cold drops began to fall on my hair.

② I tried to put the chain back myself. I had seen my father do it three times before, but it turned out to be much harder than it looked. My hands soon became black with oil. The chain kept slipping out of my fingers. The drops of rain grew into a real shower. My school bag, my T-shirt, even my socks started to feel heavy. I thought, "I'll just have to walk. It will take an hour, but at least I will get home."

③ Just then, a man on a silver electric scooter stopped beside me. He was about my father's age, wearing a blue raincoat. "Little friend, do you need help?" he said. He had to shout a little because of the wind. Before I could answer, he had already taken off his raincoat and thrown it over both me and my bicycle. "Hold the handle for me," he said. I held it tightly. He knelt on the wet ground, lifted the chain with two fingers, and clicked it back on the gear in only a few seconds. It seemed easy when he did it.

④ "Try now," he said. I squeezed the brakes and pushed the pedals once. The wheel turned smoothly. My heart jumped. "Thank you, thank you!" I said, half to him and half to the rain. His hands, I noticed, were now black with oil and red from the cold. I felt a small, painful feeling in my chest. "Your raincoat is wet, Uncle. Please take it back." He smiled and put it on again. Drops ran down the back of his head. "No problem," he said. "Ride carefully. The road after the bridge is slippery." Then he climbed onto his scooter and went the other way, disappearing into the grey rain.

⑤ I did not know him. I did not even ask his name. He did not ask mine. He did not wait for a thank-you gift or a photo. He simply helped a wet middle school student fix a bicycle chain, and then left. As I rode home, the rain felt lighter, although it was still the same rain.

⑥ That evening, at dinner, I told my parents the story. Mother asked me to draw a picture of the man's blue raincoat so we would "remember him in our hearts". Father only said, "Some day, another child will need help on a rainy road. It may be you holding the raincoat then." I nodded. From that Wednesday on, whenever I see a stranger standing beside a broken bicycle in the street, I stop — even if I am a little late. Kindness, I have learned, is a chain that someone once put back on for me.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What problem did the writer meet on the way home?**

A. He lost his wallet.  
B. His bicycle chain slipped off, and it began to rain.  
C. He could not find the road home.  
D. The bus was full.

**2. Why could the writer not put the chain back by himself?**

A. He did not have enough money.  
B. It turned out to be much harder than it looked; his hands became black with oil and the chain kept slipping.  
C. A teacher forbade him.  
D. He had forgotten how to ride a bicycle.

**3. What did the stranger on the silver electric scooter do first?**

A. He took off his raincoat and threw it over the writer and the bicycle.  
B. He called the writer's mother.  
C. He gave the writer money.  
D. He took the writer home.

**4. How did the writer feel when he noticed the stranger's hands were black with oil and red from the cold?**

A. Angry.  
B. A small, painful feeling in the chest.  
C. Sleepy.  
D. Hungry.

**5. What lesson did the writer learn from the event?**

A. Never ride a bicycle again.  
B. Kindness is a chain that someone once put back on for him, and he should now stop for others who need help.  
C. Rain is always dangerous.  
D. All strangers are untrustworthy.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末  
【解析】链条掉了＋下雨。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段  
【解析】更难、油污、滑手。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "he had already taken off his raincoat and thrown it over both me and my bicycle."  
【解析】先用雨衣遮雨。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "I felt a small, painful feeling in my chest."  
【解析】心里感触。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题／推理判断题  
【定位】第⑥段  
【解析】善意会传递。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
