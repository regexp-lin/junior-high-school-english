# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Is Sea Water Salty?**

If you have ever swum in the sea and accidentally got a mouthful of water, you probably made a face and remembered one thing very clearly: the sea is **salty**. Very salty. About thirty-five grams of salt float in every kilogram of sea water. Yet the water of lakes and rivers, which also comes from clouds and rain, is almost fresh. Where does all this sea salt come from? And why does it stay?

The answer begins on the land. When rain falls on mountains, hills, and soil, it is slightly **acidic** — not strongly, but enough to slowly attack rocks. As the rain runs across the land, it picks up tiny pieces of **mineral**, including sodium, potassium, and, most importantly, **chlorine**. These minerals do not stay high in the mountains. They travel with the water, first into small streams, then into rivers, then finally into the sea.

A single river delivers only a small amount of salt each day. But rivers have been doing this job for **millions** of years. Over such a long time, even a tiny stream becomes like a slow but patient baker, adding spoon after spoon of salt to a great pot. The sea, big as it is, has no escape from this: almost every river in the world ends in the sea.

Now, here is the important second step. Unlike the salt, the **water** does not stay forever. The sun heats the sea surface every day and lifts water molecules into the air as **vapour**. The vapour becomes clouds, the clouds travel, and eventually rain falls — often back onto land, far from the sea. But while the water can escape into the air, the salt cannot. Salt is too heavy and too stable. It must stay behind. Year by year, the sea loses fresh water to clouds and keeps salt from rivers. The result is simple: a stable, salty sea.

Some students ask, "Why do lakes not get as salty as the sea, then?" Good question. Most lakes have rivers flowing in **and** flowing out. The "out" river carries the salt away to somewhere else, keeping the lake fresh. But lakes without an "out" river — for example, the Dead Sea or China's Qinghai Lake — keep their salt too, and they are also very salty. This proves the idea: salt stays where water cannot escape except through the sky.

Interestingly, the sea's saltiness is also important for us. Salt makes sea water heavier than fresh water, helping ships float more easily. It slightly lowers the freezing point, which is why sea water does not freeze easily in winter. It supports sea animals whose bodies have adapted to salty water over long periods of time. And it even affects world climate: salty, cold water sinks deep, creating huge ocean currents that carry heat around the planet.

So, the simple taste we call "salty" is in fact the story of rain, rocks, rivers, and many, many years of patience. Next time you stand on the beach, take a closer look at the waves. They are not just water. They are a giant history book, written in salt.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. About how much salt is there in one kilogram of sea water?**

A. Almost nothing.  
B. About 3.5 grams.  
C. About 35 grams.  
D. About 350 grams.

**2. Where does the sea's salt mainly come from?**

A. From ships and boats.  
B. From rain directly falling into the sea.  
C. From rivers, which wash tiny minerals out of rocks and soil.  
D. From salt-making factories near the coast.

**3. Why does the sea stay salty when water leaves it?**

A. Because salt is too heavy to rise into the air; it stays while water escapes as vapour.  
B. Because fish eat the fresh water and spit out the salt.  
C. Because the moon pulls fresh water away from the sea.  
D. Because the sun adds more salt to the water.

**4. Why are most lakes NOT very salty?**

A. Because lakes are deeper than the sea.  
B. Because lakes have both an "in" river and an "out" river, so salt is carried away.  
C. Because lakes are colder than the sea.  
D. Because lakes are protected by mountains.

**5. Which of the following is NOT mentioned as a benefit of sea salt?**

A. Ships float more easily.  
B. Sea water does not freeze easily in winter.  
C. Salty currents help carry heat around the world.  
D. Salt makes sea water taste sweet.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"thirty-five grams of salt float in every kilogram"  
【解析】约 35 克。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"rain... picks up... minerals... travel with the water..."  
【解析】河水冲出矿物再带入海。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】因果推理题  
【定位】"Salt is too heavy and too stable. It must stay behind"  
【解析】盐太重，水以蒸气逃走。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"Most lakes have rivers flowing in and flowing out"  
【解析】有出口河，盐被带走。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（反向）  
【定位】"benefits..."  
【解析】文中从未说海水变甜。A/B/C 均提及。故选 D。
