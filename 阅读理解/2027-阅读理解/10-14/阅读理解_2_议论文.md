# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Students Be Allowed to Choose Their Own Deskmate Each Term?**

① At the start of every term, our form teacher hands out a new seating chart. Some students cheer, some frown, and a few ask the old question at the back of the classroom: "**Can we choose our own deskmate this time?**" The question sounds small. In fact, it quietly asks something much bigger — **how much say should students have in their daily school life?**

② Those who say **yes** usually give three reasons. First, **we know our classmates best**. We know who talks too much in class, who is patient enough to explain a difficult math problem, and who shares the same hobby. A well-matched deskmate can become a study partner, an honest critic, and sometimes a friend for life. Second, **being trusted makes us act more responsibly**. When we are given the right to choose, we feel the weight of the choice — if the result is poor, we cannot simply blame the teacher. Third, **a small right is a good practice for bigger rights** later in life: at university, at work, and in the community, adults must make their own decisions and live with them. If school never lets us try, we grow up with eyes but no experience.

③ However, many teachers and parents disagree, and their worries are not unreasonable. They often point out three risks. One, **students tend to pick the friendliest face, not the best partner**. A shy, thoughtful classmate may be left alone simply because no one approaches her, while the loudest table may gather four chatterboxes who fail every dictation together. Two, **deskmate-picking can turn into a small, painful drama**: some students are chosen first and some last, and the "last" ones are quietly hurt. Three, **a fixed group of friends becomes a closed circle**. A student who only sits with her best friend from primary school may never learn to work with strangers — the exact skill she will need later.

④ These worries are real, yet they do not mean "**no**". They mean "**not without rules**". A better plan is **guided choice**, not full freedom. For example: every student writes down **three classmates** she would be happy to sit with, and the teacher makes the final chart based on the lists, with two extra rules — **no one is left out** and **no pair sits together for more than two terms in a row**. In this way, students feel heard; teachers still protect the quiet children; and every half-year, the class refreshes itself.

⑤ School is not only a place for textbooks. It is also the first place where we practise being **fair**, being **brave enough to speak**, and being **kind enough to include others**. A seating chart is a tiny piece of paper, but the hand that writes it can teach all three lessons at once. Letting students help choose their deskmate — wisely, with clear rules — may be one of the smallest and most **useful** freedoms a classroom can offer.

---

**注释：** deskmate 同桌；seating chart 座位表；chatterbox 话多的人；guided choice 有引导的选择

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What does the writer think the "small question" in Paragraph ① is really about?**

A. Who cleans the classroom.  
B. How much say students should have in their daily school life.  
C. Which textbook to buy.  
D. How to pass the math exam.

**2. According to Paragraph ②, which of the following is NOT a reason for letting students choose their own deskmate?**

A. Students know their classmates best.  
B. Being trusted makes students more responsible.  
C. A small right is a practice for bigger rights later in life.  
D. Students always pick their closest friend.

**3. Which risk is mentioned in Paragraph ③?**

A. Quiet students may be left alone, while loud students gather in one table.  
B. The classroom will be too quiet.  
C. Students will refuse to come to school.  
D. Teachers will have no work to do.

**4. What is the writer's own suggestion in Paragraph ④?**

A. Let every student sit alone.  
B. Use a fully free choice with no rules.  
C. Use guided choice: each student writes three names, and the teacher makes the final chart with rules to protect everyone.  
D. Keep the same deskmate forever.

**5. What is the writer's main idea in the whole passage?**

A. Seating is a small, boring question.  
B. Students should never be trusted.  
C. Teachers should always decide alone.  
D. Letting students help choose their deskmate — wisely, with clear rules — is a useful small freedom that trains fairness, courage and kindness.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第①段 "how much say should students have in their daily school life?"  
【解析】作者明示小问题其实是大问题。A、C、D 均与座位无关。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（否定）  
【定位】第②段列出三点理由  
【解析】A/B/C 文中均有。D "always pick their closest friend" 是反方担忧，不是赞成理由。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "a shy, thoughtful classmate may be left alone ... while the loudest table may gather four chatterboxes"  
【解析】该风险明示。B、C、D 无依据。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "guided choice ... three classmates ... teacher makes the final chart ... no one is left out ... no pair for more than two terms"  
【解析】"有引导的选择"即核心建议。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Letting students help choose their deskmate — wisely, with clear rules — may be one of the smallest and most useful freedoms a classroom can offer."  
【解析】结尾主题句即主旨，兼含公平/勇气/善良三课。A 贬损、B/C 极端。故选 D。
