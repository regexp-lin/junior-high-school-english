# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Every Middle School Student Learn to Swim?**

① Every summer, sad news comes from rivers and lakes across the country: a few teenagers drown during a hot afternoon with friends. The families are heart-broken. Once again, a familiar question is asked in newspapers and on the radio: **should swimming be a required skill for every middle school student?**

② Some parents disagree. They point to busy timetables already full of Chinese, maths, English, physics and sports. "My daughter sleeps at 11 p.m. every night," one mother said. "Where is the time for swimming lessons?" Others worry about cost. In small cities, swimming pools may charge 40 yuan each time, and school pools are still rare.

③ These worries are real, but they should not stop us. Swimming is not just a hobby — it is a **life-saving skill**, like knowing how to cross the road or call 120. A good swimmer can stay afloat long enough to wait for help or carry a younger sister to safer water. According to the Ministry of Education's 2024 report, cities that added basic swimming lessons in primary and middle schools saw **drowning cases fall by nearly 40%** within three summers.

④ Beyond safety, swimming is quiet exercise for the whole body. Legs kick, arms pull, back stretches, breath deepens — and because the water holds you up, the knees and back feel no hard shock. Doctors often say swimming is "kind to the body but strong on the heart". For students who sit at a desk most of the day, even two swims a week can ease back pain and improve sleep.

⑤ Of course, not every family has an Olympic pool nearby. But a useful swimming lesson does not need to be fancy. A small community pool, a short safety course during summer holidays, or one class a month inside the school's PE period can already teach students to **float, breathe and reach the edge**. These basic skills, together with the strict rule **"never swim alone"**, are usually enough to turn fear into confidence.

⑥ In my view, learning to swim should be as normal as learning to ride a bike. A piece of paper cannot protect a child on a hot July afternoon, but a pair of **strong arms and steady breath** can. Schools, families and communities should work together so that by the time a student leaves middle school, he or she can proudly say: **"If I fall into the water, I know what to do."**

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What problem is mentioned in Paragraph 1?**

A. Too much homework in summer.  
B. Teenagers drowning in rivers and lakes every summer.  
C. Expensive school uniforms.  
D. Heavy traffic around schools.

**2. What are the main worries of some parents, according to Paragraph 2?**

A. Busy timetables and high cost.  
B. Fear of cold water.  
C. Weak teachers.  
D. Too many school trips.

**3. What did the 2024 Ministry of Education report show?**

A. Drowning cases rose in most cities.  
B. Cities that added swimming lessons saw drowning cases fall by about 40% in three summers.  
C. Swimming is bad for young students.  
D. All schools must close their pools.

**4. Why does the writer think swimming is good exercise?**

A. It needs special clothes.  
B. It is gentle on the joints but strong for the heart.  
C. It makes students lose weight in one week.  
D. It is only for winter.

**5. What is the writer's final view?**

A. Learning to swim should be as normal as learning to ride a bike.  
B. Swimming is a luxury only rich families can afford.  
C. Schools should remove PE class.  
D. Students should only swim alone.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段  
【解析】暑期溺水事故。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段  
【解析】时间紧＋费用高。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段  
【解析】三年溺水降 40%。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "kind to the body but strong on the heart"  
【解析】对关节温和但益心脏。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段首句  
【解析】像学骑车一样普及。故选 A。
