# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Saturday I Taught Grandma Her New Phone**

① Last October, my father bought my grandmother a new smart phone for her 70th birthday. Grandma is kind and strong, but she had never touched a phone with no buttons before. Her old phone had only nine big number keys, and it was enough — she used it only to call my mother or my uncle. The new phone was thin, shiny, and silent, and Grandma looked at it as if it were a small animal that might bite her.

② "I do not need it," she said on the first evening. "I am too old. My hands are slow." But my father smiled and said, "Mum, you will like it. Ming will teach you on Saturday." My father turned to me. I was a bit surprised. "Me?" I asked. "You learned in four days," he said. "That is far more than any phone shop teacher."

③ That Saturday morning, I sat on a small stool next to Grandma at the dining table. The new phone lay between us like a quiet black mirror. "OK, Grandma," I said, "let's start from the beginning." I pressed the round button, and the screen lit up.

④ She jumped a little. "Oh! Why is it so bright?" I laughed. "Do not worry, it is only light." I showed her how to touch the green phone icon, how to find my father's name in the list, and how to press it with one finger — not two, not the whole hand. Her finger shook on the screen. The first three times nothing happened. The fourth time, the phone started to ring at my father's desk upstairs. My father came down, his phone glowing in his hand, and said, "Well done, Mum!" Grandma clapped quietly. Her eyes shone like a child's.

⑤ But teaching her was not always easy. In the afternoon, I tried to show her how to scroll down a long list. She pushed the screen with too much force, and the list jumped wildly from top to bottom. She got upset. "This phone is too clever for an old woman," she said, and she almost stood up and walked away. I remembered then that Grandma, thirty years ago, had taught my father how to ride a bicycle in a small muddy village — and she did not give up on him. How could I give up on her now?

⑥ I took a deep breath and smiled. "Grandma, please sit. Let me try again in a different way. Imagine the list is a soft cat sleeping on the sofa. If you push too hard, it will jump up and run away. If you push it gently..." I moved her finger slowly down the screen, and the list scrolled smoothly. "...the cat stays and walks where you want." For the first time that afternoon, Grandma laughed. "Oh, your cat is better than your phone," she said.

⑦ By evening, Grandma could call, send a short voice message, and even use the camera to take a slightly crooked picture of our old fish tank. She saved the picture, put her hand on my shoulder, and said quietly, "Your grandfather used to carry me across the rice fields when it rained. Today, you carried me across this strange little river. Thank you, little boat."

⑧ That night, I thought about her words for a long time. I had always seen Grandma as someone who teaches me — how to make dumplings, how to grow green onions in an old pot, how to fold a paper bird. For the first time, I had taught her something back. It was a strange but beautiful feeling. I realised then that love in a family is not only parents teaching children. It also grows, like a quiet tree, when **children patiently teach the adults who first taught them**.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did Grandma have a hard time with the new phone at first?**

A. She had lost her glasses.  
B. She had never touched a phone without buttons before; her old phone had only nine big number keys.  
C. She did not like her father.  
D. The phone was actually broken.

**2. Who decided that the writer should teach Grandma the new phone?**

A. The writer's mother.  
B. The writer's father, who said the writer had learned phones very fast.  
C. The phone shop teacher.  
D. Grandma herself, without asking anyone.

**3. When Grandma pushed the screen too hard, what did the writer do?**

A. He shouted at her and left the room.  
B. He compared the list to a sleeping cat on the sofa and guided her finger gently; they both laughed.  
C. He took the phone away from her forever.  
D. He called his father to teach her instead.

**4. What did Grandma do at the end of the day?**

A. She threw the new phone into the rubbish bin.  
B. She called her son, sent a voice message, and took a slightly crooked photo of the fish tank; then she thanked the writer.  
C. She broke the new phone by mistake.  
D. She asked her father to buy another phone.

**5. What is the main idea the writer wants to share at the end of the story?**

A. Old people cannot learn to use smart phones.  
B. Love in a family is not only parents teaching children; it also grows when children patiently teach the adults who first taught them.  
C. Smart phones should be kept away from grandparents.  
D. The writer is now too tired to help anyone again.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "she had never touched a phone with no buttons before. Her old phone had only nine big number keys"  
【解析】原文明确旧机有大按键，新机无按键，造成陌生。A、C、D 均无文中依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "My father turned to me ... You learned in four days"  
【解析】父亲把教学任务交给"我"。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "Imagine the list is a soft cat sleeping on the sofa ... For the first time that afternoon, Grandma laughed"  
【解析】作者用"睡猫"的比喻温和教导。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "Grandma could call, send a short voice message, and even use the camera to take a slightly crooked picture"  
【解析】原文直接描述三项成就。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧段末 "love in a family ... also grows ... when children patiently teach the adults who first taught them"  
【解析】作者点明反哺的亲情主题。A、C、D 均与作者立场不符。故选 B。
