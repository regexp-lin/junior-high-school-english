# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle School Students Sleep at Least Nine Hours a Night?**

① According to a 2026 report from the China Youth Sleep Centre, the average Chinese middle school student sleeps only **7 hours** each school night — almost two hours less than doctors advise. Health experts now say clearly: **teenagers aged 12 to 15 should sleep at least 9 hours a night**. This idea sounds simple and caring. Yet in many classrooms, parents' chat groups, and school meetings, the discussion is anything but calm.

② Those who **support** the 9-hour rule stand on strong ground. First, our brains and bodies grow mostly while we sleep. Growth hormone, new brain connections, and the healing of tiny tired muscles — all happen at night. A Grade 8 student who sleeps poorly for a week may notice it in her maths quiz score, her quick temper, or even her height. Second, good sleep **strengthens the memory**. Many school experiments show that students who sleep 9 hours remember **up to 40 % more** new English words than those who sleep 6 hours and drink strong tea. Third, 9 hours of sleep protects **mental health**. Anxiety and sadness are more common in teenagers who stay up late. A calm morning after enough rest is, in some ways, the best morning medicine.

③ Still, others point to **real difficulties**. Let us count: a Grade 8 student often finishes school at 17:00, has dinner by 18:30, starts homework around 19:00, and needs about 3 hours for math, English, and physics. Add 30 minutes of reading and 15 minutes of shower, and the clock says 22:45. If the student wakes up at 6:30, that is less than 8 hours — still short of the 9-hour goal. Some critics argue that the rule is a **kind dream, not a realistic plan**, unless schools and families change many other things. Others worry that when students are told "you must sleep 9 hours", they may feel even more **anxious** about time they cannot find.

④ So what is a better way? I believe the answer is not "only sleep" or "only study", but **smarter design**. Schools can start classes at **8:10** instead of 7:40; teachers can carefully choose fewer but richer homework tasks; after-school classes can try to finish before 21:00. At home, parents can offer a **no-screen half hour** before bedtime and take turns to walk the dog so students have more time to rest. A student, too, can plan the day better — fifteen minutes lost to short videos is fifteen minutes less sleep.

⑤ Sleep is not a "waste of time". It is the **quiet factory** that builds tomorrow's mind. A student who wakes up bright is more than a sleepy body with open eyes — he or she is ready to listen, to ask, and to be kind. Nine hours may not always be possible; **"at least eight, closer to nine whenever we can"** is a goal worth chasing. A well-rested teenager, in the end, learns more in five hours than a tired one in ten.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main question of the article?**

A. How many hours of homework students should do each day.  
B. Whether middle school students should sleep at least nine hours a night.  
C. Why many parents come home late every night.  
D. How schools choose their new teachers.

**2. Which is NOT a reason given by those who support the 9-hour rule?**

A. Growth and brain connections happen mostly during sleep.  
B. Enough sleep improves memory of new words.  
C. Enough sleep helps protect teenagers' mental health.  
D. Enough sleep makes teenagers taller than their parents in two weeks.

**3. Why do some people think 9 hours of sleep is "a kind dream, not a realistic plan"?**

A. Teenagers don't like sleeping early.  
B. The current school and homework schedule leaves too little time at night.  
C. Doctors in China do not agree with the rule.  
D. Schools only care about exam scores.

**4. Which of the following is NOT a suggestion the writer makes?**

A. Schools can start classes later, for example at 8:10.  
B. Teachers can design fewer but richer homework tasks.  
C. Parents and students can avoid screens for 30 minutes before bed.  
D. Students should stay up and study until 1 a.m. every night.

**5. What is the writer's main idea in the last paragraph?**

A. Sleep is a waste of time that should be reduced.  
B. Nine hours is an absolute rule no one can break.  
C. Good sleep is the quiet factory of tomorrow's mind; we should chase enough sleep as much as possible.  
D. Parents alone can solve the problem of sleep.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "teenagers... should sleep at least 9 hours a night"  
【解析】核心议题为是否应每晚至少睡 9 小时。A、C、D 非主题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节否定题  
【定位】第②段三点理由：生长、记忆、心理  
【解析】"两周长高"未在文中，且不合科学。A、B、C 属文中理由。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段计算出时间紧张  
【解析】现行学习作息压缩睡眠。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节否定题  
【定位】第④段三条建议：晚些上课、精选作业、睡前离屏  
【解析】"凌晨 1 点熬夜"与建议相反。A、B、C 均属建议。故选 D。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨推断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "quiet factory that builds tomorrow's mind"  
【解析】强调充足睡眠的重要。A、B、D 与文意不符。故选 C。
