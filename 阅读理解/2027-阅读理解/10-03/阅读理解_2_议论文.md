# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle-School Students Learn to Cook Simple Meals at Home?**

① A few weeks ago, a new short video went viral on our school chat group. In it, a 14-year-old boy stood in front of a microwave, staring at a bowl of rice. He pressed one button, then another, then a third. Finally he looked at the camera and whispered, "I give up." The class laughed, but my mother did not. "This," she said, "is something your school is not teaching you, and we are not teaching you either." Her words set me thinking: **should middle-school students learn to cook simple meals at home — or is it enough to just focus on studying?**

② I strongly believe that cooking a few simple dishes should be a **basic life skill** for every middle-school student. It is not about becoming a chef; it is about becoming a person who can feed himself when his parents are not around.

③ First, cooking builds **real independence**. A student who can boil an egg, fry vegetables, wash rice and steam dumplings will not go hungry during a sick day, a summer exchange trip or even the first week of high-school dormitory life. Parents cannot follow their children forever; the sooner the kitchen stops being a scary room, the freer the child is. Independence is not only about sleeping alone or paying a bus card; food is, quite literally, where independence begins.

④ Second, cooking teaches **patience and planning** — two qualities that homework alone does not. To make a bowl of tomato-and-egg noodles, you must wash vegetables *before* the water boils, not during; you must cut onions small enough to cook through, not so big that they stay raw. You must turn off the gas on time. In other words, a simple meal is a small project. Children who have done a hundred such small projects by the age of fifteen tend to be calmer when a school project has a deadline.

⑤ Third, cooking **connects family members**. In many busy Chinese homes, children and parents now eat at different times, look at different screens, and speak fewer and fewer sentences at the table. But ask a mother to teach her son to make dumplings this Sunday afternoon, and two hours of real conversation quietly appear — about meat, about grandma's village, about last week's test. A simple dish is a reason to sit together. That, I believe, matters more than any dish itself.

⑥ Of course, **people who disagree have a point**. Middle-school life is full: classes, clubs, homework, sports, sleep. Adding cooking, they say, means adding one more tired duty. But cooking **once a week** — a single dish on Saturday lunch — is hardly a burden. And if that dish replaces a bowl of instant noodles, it may actually make the student healthier.

⑦ Another worry is **safety**. Knives cut, gas burns, hot oil splashes. This worry is serious, and it is exactly **why students should start young and start with parents nearby** — not the opposite. A 14-year-old who has never touched a stove will still, one day, touch a stove alone. Better that the first time happens with a calm adult beside him.

⑧ Some also say that **cooking is not useful in exams**, and nothing that is not on an exam paper deserves a student's time. I think this view is short-sighted. Education is not only preparation for exams; it is preparation for **life after exams**. A student who can read a physics formula but cannot cook rice has only half finished his education.

⑨ In conclusion, I believe every middle-school student should learn, step by step, to cook at least **five simple dishes** before graduation — perhaps a bowl of noodles, a plate of fried vegetables, tomato-and-egg, a simple soup, and a basic rice dish. Schools can help through a few weekend "family-kitchen" tasks; families can help through patience. The goal is not a perfect cook, but a young person who, on any quiet evening, can turn an empty kitchen into a warm dinner — for himself, and one day, for someone else.

---

**注释：** microwave 微波炉；dormitory 宿舍；deadline 截止日期；splash 溅；short-sighted 目光短浅的

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main view in this essay?**

A. Middle-school students should never touch a kitchen.  
B. Middle-school students should learn to cook simple meals at home, because it is a basic life skill.  
C. Cooking is more important than studying.  
D. Only boys, not girls, need to learn cooking.

**2. In Paragraph 3, the writer mainly argues that cooking can**

A. make a student a famous chef.  
B. help a student become independent, especially when parents are not around.  
C. save the family a lot of money.  
D. replace school lessons completely.

**3. Why does the writer say a simple meal is like "a small project" (Paragraph 4)?**

A. Because it costs a lot of money.

C. Because it is graded by a teacher.  
D. Because it must be finished within ten seconds.

**4. How does the writer reply to the worry that cooking is not safe for children (Paragraph 7)?**

A. He fully agrees and says children should avoid kitchens forever.  
B. He argues that exactly because kitchens can be dangerous, children should start young with parents beside them, so that their first time alone is not their first time cooking.  
C. He says safety is not important at all.  
D. He says only restaurant cooks need to worry about safety.

**5. Which of the following would the writer most likely support?**

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨/观点题  
【定位】第②段 "cooking a few simple dishes should be a basic life skill for every middle-school student."  
【解析】作者核心观点是把简单做饭视为基本生活技能。A "从不进厨房"、C "烹饪比学习重要"过度、D 性别歧视均不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "cooking builds real independence ... not go hungry during a sick day ... first week of high-school dormitory life"  
【解析】第③段主题即"独立"。A "名厨"夸大、C "省钱"非本段论点、D "取代学校课程"无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义猜测题/细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "you must wash vegetables before the water boils ... cut onions small enough ... turn off the gas on time ... a simple meal is a small project."  
【解析】作者将"步骤有序 + 计时"比作小工程以训练耐心与计划。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑦段 "this is exactly why students should start young and start with parents nearby — not the opposite"  
【解析】作者反驳：正因厨房有风险，才应在父母陪同下及早练习。A 放弃立场、C 否定安全、D 张冠李戴均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑨段 "learn, step by step, to cook at least five simple dishes before graduation"  
【解析】作者主张毕业前会 5 道简单菜。A "开餐馆"、B "禁止"、D "考 20 道复杂菜"都与作者主张不符。故选 C。
