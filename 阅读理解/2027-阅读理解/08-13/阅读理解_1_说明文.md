# 阅读理解（说明文） **How Do Bees Turn Flowers into Honey?**

① On a bright morning in early summer, you may notice a small golden-brown insect moving busily from flower to flower in the school garden. That is a honey bee. Each jar of honey on our breakfast table is the result of the hard work of thousands of these tiny creatures. But how exactly does a sweet-smelling flower become the golden liquid we spread on our bread? The whole process is far more interesting than most people imagine.

② The first step begins in the flower itself. Inside most flowers, there is a sweet liquid called **nectar**, which the plant produces to attract insects. When a worker bee lands on a flower, it pushes its long, thin tongue deep into the heart of the flower and sucks up the nectar. The nectar is then stored in a special "honey stomach" inside the bee's body — this is different from the stomach the bee uses for its own food. One tiny bee must usually visit **about 100 flowers** just to fill this honey stomach once.

③ The second step happens on the way back to the **hive**. While flying home, the bee mixes the nectar with a natural chemical from its body called an **enzyme**. This enzyme slowly starts to break the sugars in the nectar into simpler forms. Even before the bee reaches its hive, the nectar is already beginning to change.

④ The third step is the most surprising. When the worker bee arrives at the hive, it does not keep the nectar for itself. Instead, it passes the liquid, mouth to mouth, to another bee inside. That bee then passes it again to a third bee, and the chain may continue for quite some time. Each time the nectar is moved, more enzyme is added, and a little more water evaporates. The liquid becomes slowly thicker and sweeter.

⑤ The final step takes place inside the **honeycomb** — the beautiful wall of six-sided rooms that bees build out of wax. The thickened nectar is placed in these small rooms. Then, hundreds of bees gather around the comb and move their wings very fast. Their tiny wings act like electric fans, pushing warm air over the liquid. In this way, even more water dries out. Once the honey is thick enough, the bees cover each small room with a thin wax lid, keeping the honey fresh for a long time.

⑥ Honey is more than just food for humans. It is the main winter food for the whole bee colony. So next time you taste a spoonful of honey, remember that it may have taken one small bee the work of its whole short life — **about six weeks** — to produce only half a teaspoonful. That is a small, sweet reminder that simple things around us are often the result of patient teamwork.

---

**注释：** nectar 花蜜；hive 蜂巢；enzyme 酶；honeycomb 蜂房；evaporate 蒸发

---

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What does a worker bee do with the nectar when it is still inside the flower?**

A. It swallows the nectar into its ordinary food stomach.  
B. It sucks up the nectar with its long thin tongue and stores it in a special "honey stomach".  
C. It throws the nectar directly onto the honeycomb wall.  
D. It mixes the nectar with water from a river nearby.

**2. About how many flowers must one bee visit to fill its honey stomach once?**

A. About 10.  
B. About 50.  
C. About 100.  
D. About 1,000.

**3. When does the enzyme from the bee first begin to change the nectar?**

A. Only after the nectar arrives in the honeycomb.  
B. During the flight back to the hive, the bee mixes the enzyme with the nectar.  
C. After the bees have covered the honey with a wax lid.  
D. Only after humans take the honey out of the jar.

**4. Why do the bees move their wings very fast around the honeycomb?**

A. To make a loud sound that keeps visitors away.  
B. To cool the young bees inside the hive.  
C. To push warm air across the liquid so that more water dries out, turning the nectar into thick honey.  
D. To help themselves fall asleep faster at night.

**5. What does the writer most likely want readers to feel after reading the passage?**

A. That honey should never be eaten by humans.  
B. That bees are dangerous insects we should try to kill.  
C. That a simple thing like honey is the result of long, patient teamwork by many small creatures.  
D. That people should start keeping bees in their own bedrooms.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第2句 "it pushes its long, thin tongue deep into the heart of the flower and sucks up the nectar"  
【解析】原文明确提到工蜂用长而细的舌头吸取花蜜，并储存于特殊的"蜜胃"中。A 项"普通食物胃"与原文"different from the stomach the bee uses for its own food"相矛盾；C 项将后续步骤的内容前置，属于张冠李戴；D 项"河水"属无中生有。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段末句 "about 100 flowers"  
【解析】原文明确指出蜜蜂通常需要访问约 100 朵花才能装满一次"蜜胃"。A、B、D 均与原文数据不符。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "While flying home, the bee mixes the nectar with ... enzyme"  
【解析】原文指出回巢途中蜜蜂就开始把花蜜和酶混合，且"Even before the bee reaches its hive, the nectar is already beginning to change"。A 项将时间推迟，与原文矛盾；C、D 项均属无中生有。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "act like electric fans, pushing warm air over the liquid ... more water dries out"  
【解析】由"像电风扇那样吹动暖风，使更多水分蒸发"可以一步推出目的是把花蜜进一步变稠变为蜂蜜。A、B、D 在原文中均无依据，属无中生有。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】写作意图题  
【定位】第⑥段 "a small, sweet reminder that simple things around us are often the result of patient teamwork"  
【解析】作者在结尾点题，意在提醒读者：身边看似简单的事物背后是众多小生命耐心协作的结果。A、B、D 均与作者立场明显不符。故选 C。
