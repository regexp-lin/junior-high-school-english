# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Stranger Under the Blue Umbrella**

① It was a Wednesday afternoon in late May, and I had just finished my last class at school. When I walked out of the gate, the sky suddenly turned dark grey. Before I could even take out my phone, heavy rain began to fall, thick as a wall of water. I had forgotten to bring my umbrella that morning, and my thin school bag was of no help at all. I stood by the gate, watching my classmates ride or drive away one by one, feeling quite lonely and a bit sorry for myself.

② After about ten minutes, the rain was still falling hard. My shoes were already wet, and I could feel the cold water slowly climbing up my socks. I decided to run to the bus stop at the next corner, hoping a bus would come soon. As I was about to step into the rain, a quiet voice behind me said, "Little friend, do you need to share my umbrella?"

③ I turned around. An old man in a worn green coat was standing there, holding a large blue umbrella high above his head. His hair was already grey, and his shoes looked old and a little broken, but his smile was warm and kind. Without thinking too much, I nodded quickly and ran under the umbrella with him. "Where are you going?" he asked gently. "The bus stop at the corner," I said. "Good. I walk that way too," he answered.

④ As we walked together, he told me he was a retired teacher from the same school. He used to teach history many years ago, before I was even born. He spoke slowly, and his voice was soft, almost lost in the sound of the rain. I noticed that he kept moving the umbrella toward my side, so that his own shoulder was already half wet. When I tried to push it back, he just smiled and said, "It's only a little rain. I'm old enough; I don't mind."

⑤ When we finally reached the bus stop, the rain had not stopped. My bus was not there yet, and the old man said he still needed to go further down the street to visit his friend. Before he left, he reached into his small bag and took out a folded, smaller umbrella. "Here," he said, putting it into my hand. "I keep this one for rainy days like today. Take it. You can give it back the next time you see me at school, or just pass it on to anyone who needs it more."

⑥ I wanted to say many things, but the words felt too small for that moment. I only managed a quiet "Thank you, sir." He nodded, turned, and walked into the rain, his blue umbrella slowly moving down the grey street until I could not see it any more.

⑦ That night at home, I placed his folded umbrella carefully on my desk. I did not know his name. I did not know if I would ever see him again. But I knew one thing clearly: the world had just taught me something that no textbook could teach me — **true kindness often wears old shoes and holds the umbrella toward someone else**.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer stand by the school gate after class?**

A. The writer was waiting for the mother to come by car.  
B. The writer had forgotten to bring an umbrella and the rain was very heavy.  
C. The writer was looking for a lost school bag.  
D. The writer was punished by the teacher and could not leave.

**2. What did the old man say when he first spoke to the writer?**

A. "Please get out of my way."  
B. "Little friend, do you need to share my umbrella?"  
C. "Go home right now, it is dangerous."  
D. "Do you know where the bus stop is?"

**3. Why did the old man's own shoulder get wet on the way to the bus stop?**

A. Because his umbrella was broken and full of holes.  
B. Because he kept moving the umbrella more toward the writer's side so the writer would stay dry.  
C. Because the wind suddenly blew the umbrella away.  
D. Because he was carrying a heavy bag on his shoulder.

**4. What did the old man give the writer before he left?**

A. A history book.  
B. A warm coat.  
C. A small folded umbrella that the old man kept for rainy days.  
D. A bus ticket and some money.

**5. What does the writer most likely want readers to learn from this story?**

A. Old people are usually stronger than young people in the rain.  
B. True kindness does not need fancy clothes; it often appears in small, thoughtful acts from ordinary people.  
C. Middle-school students should always refuse help from strangers.  
D. Everyone should buy an expensive umbrella for rainy days.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末 "I had forgotten to bring my umbrella that morning"  
【解析】原文明确说明作者忘带伞，且"heavy rain began to fall"。A、C、D 在原文中均无依据，属无中生有或张冠李戴。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段末 "Little friend, do you need to share my umbrella?"  
【解析】原文直接引用了老人的原话。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "he kept moving the umbrella toward my side, so that his own shoulder was already half wet"  
【解析】原文明确说明老人把伞往作者一侧倾斜，所以自己肩膀湿了。A 项"伞破了"原文无提及；C 项"风吹走伞"不符；D 项"背包"虽曾提及但不是肩膀湿的原因，属张冠李戴。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "he reached into his small bag and took out a folded, smaller umbrella"  
【解析】原文明确说明老人送了一把折叠小伞。A、B、D 均无文中依据。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑦段末 "true kindness often wears old shoes and holds the umbrella toward someone else"  
【解析】作者在结尾点明：真正的善意常常来自穿着旧鞋、把伞向别人倾斜的普通人。A 项属过度推断；C 项与作者态度相反；D 项与文章立意无关。故选 B。
