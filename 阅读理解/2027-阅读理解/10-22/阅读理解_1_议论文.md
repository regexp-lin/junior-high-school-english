# 阅读理解（议论文） **Are Comic Books Really a Waste of Time for Middle-School Students?**

① When a student is caught with a **comic book** under the desk during class, most teachers will shake their heads and say, "Put that away and open your real book." In the eyes of many adults, comics are still the **guilty snack** of reading — sweet, easy, and a little shameful. But are they really a waste of time? After reading Chinese, Japanese and French comics for three years, I believe the honest answer is **no, not if they are chosen wisely**.

② First, comics are **stepping stones**, not traps. For students who struggle with long walls of black print, the pictures in comics give their eyes a place to rest. A reader who finishes one comic today may, six months later, feel brave enough to open a full novel. Many bookshop managers say the same thing: **most teenagers who love reading novels in Grade 9 started by reading comics in Grade 6.** In other words, comics are not the enemy of deep reading; often, they are the **door**.

③ Second, comics teach a **visual language** that the modern world keeps asking us to use. A good comic page is a small lesson in layout, time, silence and sound. When we watch a short film, read a news infographic, or even scroll through a science-museum poster, we unconsciously use skills first learned in comics: "What does this big panel mean? Why is this word handwritten, not typed? Why is the character silent here?" Students who grow up with thoughtful comics become, slowly, **better readers of modern information**.

④ Third, many comics carry **real stories with real weight**. Think of *Maus*, a comic about the Holocaust; *Persepolis*, a comic about growing up in Iran; or the many Chinese comics about the Long March, country schools, or migrant workers. These are not fairy tales for small children; they are serious history, told in a style that reaches the heart quickly. A student who cries at the last page of *Persepolis* has not wasted her afternoon.

⑤ Of course, I do not say **every** comic is good. Some comics are full of cheap violence or empty jokes, and three hours spent on them is three hours the teenager will not get back. That is why **choosing** matters. A useful rule is simple: **a comic that makes you a better person is worth reading twice; a comic that only makes you laugh and forget is worth reading once; a comic that makes you smaller, meaner, or more tired is not worth reading at all.**

⑥ Some parents worry that reading comics damages the eyes more than reading normal books. In truth, any close reading for hours is hard on the eyes. The same rule applies to novels, textbooks, and screens: **rest every 20 minutes, and read in bright light.** The problem is the hour, not the comic.

⑦ A middle-school student has perhaps **three hundred weekends** left before university. If every single page of reading must be a "serious classic", we will grow into young adults who see reading as a duty, not a joy. A wisely chosen comic, read for pleasure beside a fruit plate, may build the quiet habit that keeps a real reader alive for life. **Stories told in pictures are still stories; and stories, in any form, are never a waste of time.**

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion about comic books for middle-school students?**

A. All comics are a waste of time.  
B. Comics are harmful to the eyes, so they should be banned.  
C. Wisely chosen comics are not a waste of time; they can be stepping stones to deeper reading.  
D. Students should only read comics in class.

**2. What do many bookshop managers say, according to Paragraph ②?**

A. Teenagers who love novels in Grade 9 often started by reading comics in Grade 6.  
B. Comics are replacing textbooks.  
C. Comics are more expensive than novels.  
D. No teenager reads novels today.

**3. What "modern skill" does the writer say comics help to train?**

A. Typing fast on a phone.  
B. Reading visual information — layout, timing, silence — which is also needed for films, infographics, and posters.  
C. Drawing cartoons for money.  
D. Writing essays in English.

**4. What simple rule does the writer give for choosing comics in Paragraph ⑤?**

A. Buy only the most expensive ones.  
B. A comic that makes you a better person is worth reading twice; a comic that only makes you laugh and forget is worth reading once; a comic that makes you smaller, meaner, or more tired is not worth reading at all.  
C. Read every comic twice.  
D. Only read foreign comics.

**5. What does the writer suggest about the "eye problem"?**

A. Comics are much worse for eyes than novels.  
B. The real problem is the long hours of close reading, not the type of book; rest every 20 minutes and read in bright light applies to all reading.  
C. Students should never read at home.  
D. Only screens hurt eyes.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段立论 "not if they are chosen wisely" + 第⑦段总结  
【解析】有条件肯定漫画价值。A、B、D 均偏离或极端。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "most teenagers who love reading novels in Grade 9 started by reading comics in Grade 6"  
【解析】直引。B、C、D 均错。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "lesson in layout, time, silence and sound ... short film ... infographic ... poster"  
【解析】读图时代所需技能。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段三级标准直引  
【解析】"两次 / 一次 / 不值得"三层分别对应。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑥段 "The problem is the hour, not the comic."  
【解析】问题在时长而非书型。A、C、D 均错或极端。故选 B。
