# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Night I Cooked a Full Dinner for My Parents**

① My parents had been busy all April. My father, a **hospital nurse**, often came home with dark circles under his eyes. My mother, a kind middle-school teacher, stayed up late marking Grade 9 essays. By the third week, even their smiles looked tired. One Wednesday afternoon, I stood in the bright kitchen and had a strange, serious idea: **tonight, I would make the whole dinner.**

② I was only fourteen, and I could cook just three simple things: boiled eggs, fried rice, and sliced cucumber with soy sauce. But I had saved **80 yuan** of pocket money. With my best friend Lele, I wrote a small menu on a piece of notebook paper: **tomato egg noodles, stir-fried potato strips, a plate of cold cucumber, and a pot of corn and pork-rib soup**. I did not tell my parents. Lele walked me to the **old market** behind our school at 3 p.m.

③ The market was loud, full of shouts and shining wet tomatoes. A grandmother selling pork ribs smiled when I looked shy. "First time, girl?" she asked. I nodded. She gave me the freshest ribs and taught me how to press them gently with my finger to check if they were cold enough. An old uncle who sold corn picked out three sweet cobs and threw in a free spring onion. "For a good little chef," he said. With two heavy bags, my back warm, I went home.

④ Cooking was much harder than eating. I burnt the potato strips a little on the first try, because I did not know that cold oil into a cold pan meant a slow heat. I called Lele, and she said, "Hot oil, low heat, and keep turning." I tried again and it worked. The soup, however, refused to become tasty. I had forgotten to add ginger. The tomatoes and eggs, in contrast, were easy — the **soft red liquid** looked almost like my mother's on a Sunday.

⑤ By the time I heard the lift door at 7:15 p.m., the small kitchen was full of steam and I had a spot of oil on my sleeve. I had also broken one bowl, one spoon, and I think a little of my courage. My father opened the door first, held his key in the air, and stopped. He did not say anything. My mother came behind him, put down her bag, and quietly sat down at the table, as if she was afraid the dishes would fly away if she moved too fast.

⑥ The soup was too salty. The potato strips were crisp but slightly dark. Only the tomato egg noodles were really good. My father took a sip of soup, paused, and then said with a serious voice, "Mm. Hot." My mother laughed, and tears came into her eyes. She tried the cucumber. "Sweet," she said. "Very sweet." They ate slowly. They praised each dish. My father even washed his bowl again to finish every last drop of soup, salty or not.

⑦ After dinner, they did not let me wash up. My mother wiped the table and whispered, "The dishes taste like **our family**." That night I wrote in my diary: "**Being loved is not only being fed. Sometimes it is being cooked for — in both directions.**" Since that evening, I have cooked one small dinner every week. My potato strips have become much better. The soup still needs ginger, but our small kitchen has never been warmer.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer decide to cook for her parents?**

A. Because she wanted to win a cooking prize.  
B. Because her parents had been busy and tired all April.  
C. Because she hated her mother's food.  
D. Because she was punished at school.

**2. How did the writer go shopping for the ingredients?**

A. She went alone at midnight.  
B. Her mother sent her by taxi.  
C. Her best friend Lele walked with her to the old market.  
D. A neighbour carried her on a motorbike.

**3. What mistake did the writer make when frying the potato strips for the first time?**

A. She put ice in the pan.  
B. She used cold oil in a cold pan and did not heat it properly.  
C. She forgot to peel the potatoes.  
D. She used sugar by mistake.

**4. What was the problem with the soup?**

A. It was too salty.  
B. It was too sweet.  
C. It was too cold.  
D. It had sand inside.

**5. What did the writer learn from that evening?**

A. Cooking is boring and a waste of time.  
B. Being loved sometimes means being cooked for in both directions.  
C. Her parents cannot cook.  
D. Shops are better than markets.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段  
【解析】父母忙累，女儿想为他们做饭。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段末  
【解析】好友乐乐陪她去市场。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段  
【解析】凉油凉锅、慢热。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "too salty"  
【解析】汤太咸。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦段日记句  
【解析】被爱也可是"被煮饭"。故选 B。
