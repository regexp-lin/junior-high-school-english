# 阅读理解（议论文） **Are Weekend Outdoor Trips More Valuable Than Extra Tutoring?**

① On any Saturday morning in my neighbourhood, two kinds of students get up early. The first group carries thick books and walks into tutoring centres; the second group puts on hiking shoes and goes with their parents to hills, parks or small rivers. Which weekend is more valuable? Some parents believe more hours of study always mean better results. I respectfully disagree. I believe that, for most middle school students, **a regular outdoor trip on the weekend can be more valuable than an extra tutoring class.**

② First, the body needs **movement**. During the school week, we sit for seven or eight hours every day. Our eyes stare at the same distance; our back and neck slowly become stiff. A weekend tutoring class only adds two or three more sitting hours. A hike, even a short one, stretches our legs, clears our lungs and makes us tired in the **good** way — so that we sleep better on Sunday night.

③ Second, the brain needs **space**. Research in many schools has shown that students remember new ideas better when they have time for rest and free play between study sessions. A long walk in a park is not "wasted time". While our feet keep moving, our thoughts slowly sort themselves out. Some students even say that math problems which seemed impossible on Friday become clear after a Sunday afternoon by the river.

④ Third, an outdoor trip teaches lessons that books **cannot**. When we help to cook lunch on a small camping stove, we learn about fire and safety. When we help to read a map in a forest, we learn directions and distance. When we talk to an old farmer about his fields, we understand why fresh vegetables cost what they cost. None of these lessons can be bought from a tutoring centre.

⑤ I understand that some students do need extra help, especially before an important exam. For them, a short study session on Saturday morning can be useful. But even these students should **protect** their Sunday afternoon for the outdoors. One trip a week is not a luxury; it is a kind of medicine for both body and mind. Parents who replace every outdoor trip with a tutoring class may save money on tickets, but they pay more in stress and unhappy children.

⑥ The truth, I think, is that study and nature are not enemies. The real enemy of a growing teenager is a weekend that is **only** a desk, a chair and a screen. Let us keep the map, the map the textbook cannot draw: a path under tall trees, a rice field in the distance, a quiet bench by the lake. Such a map, step by step, teaches the most important lesson — **how to stay a healthy, curious, kind human being**.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main opinion of the writer?**

A. Extra tutoring classes are always better than outdoor trips.  
B. A regular outdoor trip on the weekend can be more valuable than an extra tutoring class.  
C. Middle school students should never study on weekends.  
D. Parents should not care about their children's health.

**2. What problem does the writer describe in paragraph ②?**

A. Students do not read enough.  
B. Students sit for many hours during school, and an extra tutoring class only adds more sitting hours.  
C. Parents are too busy.  
D. Schools start too late.

**3. According to paragraph ③, why is a long walk NOT a waste of time?**

A. It makes students forget all homework.  
B. It gives the brain space, helps memory and lets ideas sort themselves out.  
C. It replaces school lessons completely.  
D. It makes students run away from home.

**4. Which lesson is NOT mentioned as something an outdoor trip can teach?**

A. Fire and safety from cooking on a small camping stove.  
B. Directions and distance from reading a map.  
C. Why fresh vegetables cost what they cost, from talking to a farmer.  
D. How to write a perfect exam paper in ten minutes.

**5. What does the writer say about outdoor trips and tutoring in the end?**

A. They are enemies and students must choose only one.  
B. Study and nature are not enemies; the real enemy is a weekend of only desk, chair and screen.  
C. Students should study every minute of the weekend.  
D. Outdoor trips are useful only for primary school children.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末及全文  
【解析】作者支持周末规律的户外活动。A/C/D 均与原文相反。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "we sit for seven or eight hours every day... only adds two or three more sitting hours."  
【解析】久坐问题被加剧。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "students remember new ideas better when they have time for rest... thoughts slowly sort themselves out."  
【解析】大脑需要空间。A/C/D 均错误。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（反向）  
【定位】第④段列举三例  
【解析】A/B/C 均列出；D 未提及。故选 D。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题／主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段 "study and nature are not enemies... The real enemy... is a weekend that is only a desk, a chair and a screen."  
【解析】学习与自然不是敌人。A/C/D 与原文相反。故选 B。
