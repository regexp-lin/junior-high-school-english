# 阅读理解（应用文） **Postcards to a Distant Teacher: Winter 2027 Pen-Friend Project**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · English Department + Youth Red Cross Club**  
> **2027 Winter "Postcards to a Distant Teacher" Project**  
> **To our partner village schools in Gansu and Yunnan — we have not forgotten you.**

For five years, our school has been partners with two small village middle schools — one in **Minxian**, Gansu, and one in **Yongsheng**, Yunnan. In winter, their classrooms are **cold and quiet**; most of their young teachers are graduates who travel a long way from home. This winter, we want to send them a small, warm gift that needs **no electricity** and **no box** — a **handwritten postcard** from each of us.

---

## 🎯 Our Goal

- **2,000 postcards** in total (one from every student, one from every teacher).  
- **40 small "letter packs"** for the English Teachers' Office of each partner school (20 × 2).  
- A **short video reply** filmed by the two partner schools in January, shown at our Spring Opening Assembly.

---

## ✍️ What to Write On Your Postcard

1. A **greeting** in English and in Chinese (or in your dialect), like "*Hello, dear teacher. I am Li Yue from Class 8(2), Lihua Middle School*."  
2. **One thing you have been learning recently** that you are proud of: a new English word, a poem you have memorised, a maths puzzle you have solved, a recipe you have tried.  
3. **One small question** for the teacher — about their school, their village, the tallest tree they see from the window, or what they eat on a cold morning.  
4. **One wish** for their class this winter: for example, "*May your reading corner stay warm and full of stories*."  
5. (Optional) A **tiny sketch** in the corner — a pear, a bird, a pair of shoes. Pictures cross borders faster than words.

**Language:** English is welcome but not required. Half English, half Chinese is also fine. Please **write by hand**, with a pen that does not easily rub off.

---

## 🚫 Things to Avoid

- No real surnames of classmates or teachers without their permission.  
- **No pity words** like "poor children" or "poor school". Our partner schools are our **friends**, not a charity case.  
- **No money, no phone numbers, no home address** on the card. Our English Department will mail them together, safely.  
- No political slogans, no advertisements, no jokes about food, clothes, or dialect.

---

---

## 🖊 Materials Provided

- **Two blank postcards per student** — simple card, school logo in the corner.  
- A **return address label** printed with our school name.  
- A **small card of useful phrases** in English (short, polite sentences you can copy).  
- A **pencil sharpener** and free black pens on the library help-desk during lunchtime, 5 – 13 November.

---

## 🙋 Who Can Help More?

- Students who speak **Gansu or Yunnan dialects** are warmly invited to write one extra card in that dialect on the back.  
- Students from the Art Club may design **the front of 20 "class-set postcards"** for Grade 7 partner classes.  
- Students who are **shy** about handwriting may simply draw a picture on the front and copy our teacher's short English text on the back. No pressure, no judgement.

---

## 💬 From a Former Student

> "Two years ago, I wrote a short postcard to a maths teacher called Ms. Zhang in Minxian. Her reply came in the spring, on a piece of yellow paper torn from a notebook. She said she had put my card above her desk, and every Monday morning she read it again. I still keep her reply in my pencil case. **Big schools send you homework; small schools sometimes send you courage.**"  
> — *Chen Yue, Class of 2026*

*Please begin your postcard this week. Even a short one is a piece of warmth. The winter is long in the mountains.*
