# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle-School Students Have a Fixed Monthly Pocket Money?**

① My classmate Xiaoman receives exactly **100 yuan every month** from her parents. Another classmate, Wenbo, has **no fixed money at all**: he simply asks when he needs something. A third friend, Yumeng, gets **50 yuan a week** only if her room stays tidy. Three different habits, three different families. Which way is best? After listening to parents, teachers and my own grandfather, I believe the answer is clear: **a fixed, modest monthly pocket money** — tied to no strict performance test — is the healthiest choice for most middle-school students.

② The first reason is **learning to manage money**. Money, like reading, is a skill: the earlier we try it on our own, the better. A fixed amount teaches three useful habits in one small package. First, **budgeting** — if your 100 yuan this month is gone on the 20th, you will eat a lot of home porridge before the 1st. Second, **waiting** — if a new pen costs 60 yuan and you only have 40, you learn to **delay joy**, one of the most important skills of adult life. Third, **thinking in limits** — every pocket, every bank card, every salary in the world has a border. Practising inside a small border today keeps us calm inside a larger one tomorrow.

③ The second reason is **dignity and privacy**. A student who must ask for every yuan — bus card, birthday gift for Dad, a bowl of noodles after evening class — slowly feels like a young beggar in her own home. A student with a fixed monthly amount can quietly take care of small, normal needs. Parents do not need to know that their daughter is buying a blue pen to replace a broken one, or that their son is saving for a small photo frame of his late grandmother. Small money, carried with dignity, is one of the first real signs of **growing up**.

④ The third reason is **the rest of our family's money**. A fixed amount gives parents peace, too. They know, month after month, how much the child side of the family will spend. There are no surprise 80-yuan afternoons, no long evening arguments over a single coin. With a simple plan, both sides can **stop guessing** and start trusting.

⑤ Of course, worried voices exist. Some teachers say that children **spend wildly** if they hold their own money; others point out that **rich pocket money** creates unfair pressure among classmates, as "he has 300 a month, she only has 50". Both worries are real. The answer, however, is **not to stop the idea**, but to **shape it**. Parents can start small — 30 yuan a month in Grade 7 — and slowly raise the amount in Grade 8 and Grade 9. Schools can, at the form-teacher's meeting, gently suggest a **recommended range** (for example, 30–120 yuan a month) so that families stay in a healthy neighbourhood.

⑥ A special word about **"money for marks"**: a payment that only comes after a 95-point test paper. I strongly disagree with this. Tying pocket money to a mark turns learning into a business. The student who already scores 100 is **paid twice** (once by the joy of knowing, once by cash); the student who struggles is **punished** both by the mark and by empty pockets. A family is not a marketplace. Pocket money should come as a trust, not a reward for something that is already its own reward.

⑦ Some also ask: "Why not pay children for housework?" A light form of this may be fine — for example, a small bonus if a student builds a new habit, such as washing all the family shoes every Saturday for three months. But **daily helping** — setting the table, wiping the floor — should stay **free**. We live together, so we clean together. Turning every duty into a price tag makes a kitchen feel like a restaurant.

⑧ One hundred yuan a month does not make a rich child, and no pocket money at all does not make a poor one. But a fixed, modest sum — quiet, reliable, not tied to tests — gives a middle-school student three gifts at once: **money skills, dignity, and trust**. Three small keys that, many years from now, will quietly open larger doors.

**注释：** pocket money 零花钱；budget 预算；dignity 尊严；recommended 推荐的；bonus 奖金

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Students should get no pocket money.  
B. A fixed, modest monthly pocket money (not tied to marks) is the healthiest choice for most middle-school students.  
C. Students should decide their own salary from their parents.  
D. Pocket money should change every week.

**2. In Paragraph ②, which of the following is NOT one of the three skills a fixed pocket money teaches?**

A. Budgeting.  
B. Waiting and delaying joy.  
C. Thinking inside a limit.  
D. Always asking parents for help before buying anything.

**3. Why does the writer think "dignity and privacy" are important in Paragraph ③?**

A. Because students like to keep secrets.  
B. Because a student who must ask for every yuan feels like a young beggar in her own home, while one with a small fixed amount can quietly handle normal needs.  
C. Because parents don't care about their children.  
D. Because money is more important than family.

**4. Why does the writer disagree with "money for marks"?**

A. Because it makes learning too easy.  
B. Because it turns learning into a business; students who already do well are paid twice, while struggling students are punished both by low marks and by empty pockets.  
C. Because 95 points is too high a standard.  
D. Because money is not interesting to students.

**5. What does the writer say about paying for housework in Paragraph ⑦?**

A. All housework should be paid.  
B. Daily helping (setting the table, wiping the floor) should stay free, because we live together so we clean together; but a light bonus for a new long-term habit may be fine.  
C. Students should refuse to do housework without pay.  
D. Only parents should clean.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段 "a fixed, modest monthly pocket money — tied to no strict performance test — is the healthiest choice"  
【解析】立论清晰。A、C、D 均极端或偏离。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（否定）  
【定位】第②段三项 budgeting / waiting / thinking in limits  
【解析】A、B、C 均在文中。D 与"学会独立"相反。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "young beggar ... quietly take care of small, normal needs"  
【解析】尊严价值直引。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑥段 "paid twice ... punished both by the mark and by empty pockets"  
【解析】分数换钱的不公。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "daily helping ... should stay free ... small bonus ... new habit"  
【解析】免费 + 少量奖励，双重规则。A、C、D 均错或偏激。故选 B。
