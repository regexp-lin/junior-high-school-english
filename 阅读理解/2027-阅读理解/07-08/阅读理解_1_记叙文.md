# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Goat That Came to Math Class**

On a cool autumn morning, our math teacher, Mr. Zhang, walked into Classroom 8-2 carrying a thick folder, a piece of chalk, and — this is the part that surprised everyone — a small brown goat on a red rope. The goat wore a tiny yellow bell around its neck. Our classroom went completely silent, and then, all at once, forty-five students began to laugh.

"Good morning, class. Meet **Hu Shi** — the goat of 8-2," Mr. Zhang said, with the **straightest** face I have ever seen. "Hu Shi is our guest today. Please treat him kindly. Any student who disturbs him will do three extra math problems for homework."

The laugh cooled into a curious quiet. Mr. Zhang tied the goat to a chair leg and began the lesson as if goats in classrooms were normal. He drew a big circle on the blackboard and asked Liu Ming to find its area. Liu Ming, who loved mocking others, stood up with a cheeky smile, waiting for a chance to make jokes. He pointed at the goat and said, "Mr. Zhang, can Hu Shi help me?"

"Of course," Mr. Zhang said calmly. "He might know more than you think."

Hu Shi looked up. He did not move. He just chewed something slowly and blinked twice. The class giggled but went back to work. Step by step, the problem was solved. Nobody had time to poke fun at the goat, because Mr. Zhang kept drawing new circles, triangles, and strange star shapes on the board.

Around 9:15, something unexpected happened. Someone at the back started a tiny sound with their desk — *tap tap tap*. The boy next to them joined in, and soon the whole back row was tapping together, trying to get Hu Shi to dance. The goat grew nervous. Its head turned. Its bell rang. Its eyes widened. For the first time, the goat looked like he might cry in his own quiet animal way.

Mr. Zhang put down his chalk. He walked slowly to the back of the room.

"Nobody is doing math problems at the moment," he said, "because Hu Shi is scared."

Those seven words changed the room. The tapping stopped. Heads went down. Red faces grew a little redder.

"Today I did not bring Hu Shi to teach you math," Mr. Zhang went on. "I brought him to teach you **respect**. A goat cannot speak back. He cannot defend himself. He cannot explain that your noise frightens him. Remember this feeling when your little brother says something strange, when the new student cannot speak our **dialect**, when Grandma tells the same story for the fifth time. The weaker the voice, the bigger our responsibility to listen."

For a long minute, nobody moved. Then Liu Ming, the boy who had wanted to mock the goat, stood up and walked quietly to Hu Shi. He patted the goat's brown back, gently, the way you touch a sleeping baby. He said, "I'm sorry." Other students came up, one by one, to pet the goat. The bell on his neck rang softly, like a small tune.

At the end of the class, Mr. Zhang untied Hu Shi and said, "This goat belongs to my cousin's farm. He goes back today. But every time I draw a circle on the board, remember the lesson he left here."

That afternoon, when I wrote my math homework at home, I wrote a little message on the top of the page: **Respect is the first answer of every problem.** I believe Mr. Zhang would give me full marks for that — even though it was not part of the question.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What did Mr. Zhang bring into the classroom on that morning?**

A. A big dog.  
B. A folder, a piece of chalk, and a small brown goat.  
C. A large white rabbit.  
D. A basket of apples.

**2. How did the class first react when they saw the goat?**

A. They ran out of the classroom at once.  
B. They stayed completely silent, then suddenly began to laugh.  
C. They started crying.  
D. They asked to leave school early.

**3. Why did the goat get nervous at around 9:15?**

A. Because Mr. Zhang shouted at it.  
B. Because some students at the back tapped their desks to make it dance.  
C. Because the sun became too hot.  
D. Because another animal came into the room.

**4. What lesson did Mr. Zhang actually want to teach?**

A. How to find the area of a circle.  
B. How to raise goats at home.  
C. Respect — especially for those whose voices are weaker.  
D. How to enter a classroom quietly.

**5. What did Liu Ming do after the teacher's serious words?**

A. He kept laughing at the goat.  
B. He walked to the goat, patted it gently and said sorry.  
C. He left the classroom.  
D. He refused to do homework.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】首段 "carrying a thick folder, a piece of chalk, and... a small brown goat"  
【解析】文件夹+粉笔+小羊。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"our classroom went completely silent, and then... began to laugh"  
【解析】先静后笑。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"tap tap tap... get Hu Shi to dance"  
【解析】学生敲桌子吓到了羊。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】"I brought him to teach you respect..."  
【解析】尊重弱者才是真正的课。A/B/D 均偏离。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"walked quietly to Hu Shi... said, 'I'm sorry.'"  
【解析】主动走过去道歉。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
