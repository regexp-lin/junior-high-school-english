# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Every Student Learn a Musical Instrument?**

① Walk into any music shop on a Saturday, and you will see lines of parents with children in their arms: some test pianos, some try violins, some clutch bright trumpets as if they are already stars. In some Chinese schools, even more is happening: principals are starting to discuss a new question: **should every student, without exception, be required to learn at least one musical instrument?** Let us see both sides.

② People who support the idea point to **brain benefits**. Many studies show that regular music practice can improve memory, focus, and even maths thinking. When a student learns to match fingers with notes, eyes with sheet music, and ears with timing, many parts of the brain work together. Some scientists describe music lessons as a "**full-body workout for the mind**". In a world with more tests and heavier homework, such training may be more valuable than it first seems.

③ Supporters also mention **feelings and friendships**. A guitar in a camp circle, a quiet piano at a school ceremony, a loud drum at a sports day — music can bring people together in ways words cannot. Students who feel nervous in big groups often find it easier to **make friends through music**, especially in school orchestras and bands. Music also gives teens a healthy way to release feelings after a hard day, instead of scrolling through their phones late into the night.

④ However, other teachers and parents warn that **required lessons can become a burden**. Instruments are not cheap. A good violin costs thousands of yuan; a new piano can cost ten times more. Some families live far from any music teacher, and online lessons are not always useful for small hands. If a school forces every child to learn an instrument, some students may practise with tears, not joy — and that often kills any real love for music.

⑤ There is also the question of **time**. A middle school student already has maths, English, PE, science, and art in the same short week. Adding at least 30 minutes of daily practice is not a small thing. If the practice is forced, the student may simply move through the motions without feeling anything. And if music feels like another exam, it will lose the very charm that makes it worth keeping in the first place.

⑥ In my view, a **better middle way** is possible. Schools can offer a wide range of **free instruments** — such as ocarinas, recorders, harmonicas, or simple keyboards — so that every student can try music in a low-cost, low-pressure way. Students who truly fall in love with a particular instrument can then choose it themselves, with family support. In this way, music becomes a **gift**, not a **chain**. And a gift, once freely accepted, is far more likely to stay with us for the rest of our lives.

**1. What is the writer mainly discussing?**

B. Let every student choose a free, simple instrument to try first.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末问题  
【解析】全文讨论是否强制学乐器。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "improve memory, focus, and even maths thinking"  
【解析】提升记忆、专注与数学思维。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段（价格、师资、情感）  
【解析】没有说危害耳朵。故选 D。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "offer a wide range of free instruments"  
【解析】先提供便宜乐器让学生尝试。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】句意理解题  
【定位】第⑥段末  
【解析】礼物自由而链锁强迫。故选 B。
