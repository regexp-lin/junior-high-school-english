# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School — "Young Maker" Robotics & AI Club 2027 Autumn Recruitment**

---

> **Lihua Middle School — Science & Technology Department**
> **Club Recruitment Notice**
> Released: **24th September 2027**

**Hello, young makers,**

After six years of growth, our school's oldest science club — the **"Young Maker" Robotics & AI Club** — is opening its doors for the **2027 autumn recruitment**. Last year, our team won a **gold prize** at the city-level Robotics Contest and built a small smart-watering system for the school garden. This year, we hope to add **45 new members** (15 from each grade, Grade 7 to Grade 9).

---

## 1. Club Activities

**Weekly meetings:** every **Friday 16:00 – 17:30**, in the **Maker Space (Science Building, 3F)**.

**Four main project teams:**

| Team | What You Will Do | Good For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Hardware Team** | Build and repair small robots, 3D-print parts, wire sensors | Students who like making things with their hands |
| **Code Team** | Write programs in **Python / Scratch**, train small AI models | Students who enjoy logic and computers |
| **AI + Daily Life Team** | Plan small AI projects that help daily life (e.g. smart rubbish bin, pet feeder) | Students who love thinking about real problems |
| **Reporter Team** | Write English reports, shoot short videos for the club | Students who like writing and media |

---

## 2. Main Events of the Year

| Month | Event |
| --- | --- |
| 10 Oct | **Opening day** — Maker Space, 16:00 |
| 15 Nov | **"Mini Robot Fair"** on open-air stage |
| 20 Dec | **Inter-class AI quiz** in Hall A |
| 8 Mar | **City Robotics Contest** (selected members) |
| 15 Apr | **Smart Garden Project** launch |
| 25 May | **Year-end showcase** + club dinner |

> **Note:** Big city contests may require travel on Saturdays; parent permission will be needed.

---

## 3. Who May Apply

- Current Grade 7–9 students of Lihua Middle School.
- **Science** and **maths** scores of **75 or above** (Grade 7: 70 or above).
- **Passion** for science and a **willingness** to attend all Friday meetings unless sick.
- Prior programming experience is **not** required; you will be trained.

---

## 4. How to Apply

Choose **one** of the two ways below:

**Path A — the Short Application**
1. Fill in the **yellow application form** from Mr Su's office (Room 307), Science Building.
2. Write a **150-word English introduction** on "**one idea I would like to build**".
3. Submit paper form before **Monday 5th October, 17:00**.

**Path B — the Maker Challenge (optional, higher chance of being chosen)**
- Bring a **small home project** to the interview — any device, drawing, or written plan counts.
- Past examples: a torch made from a soda can, a paper-cup speaker, a simple English-word-card app.

All applicants must attend a **30-minute interview** on **Saturday 10th October, 9:00 – 12:00**, in the Maker Space. The final name list will be posted on **Wednesday 14th October** on the school website and the Science Building notice board.

---

## 5. Club Rules

1. All equipment in the Maker Space must be **returned to its box** at the end of each meeting.
2. **No food or coloured drinks** near computers or soldering stations.
3. **Long hair must be tied back** when using the 3D printer or soldering iron.
4. Members who miss three Friday meetings **without a good reason** will be asked to step back so that another student can join.
5. **Online ideas are welcome**; **copying another student's code** is not.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How many new members does the "Young Maker" club plan to recruit in autumn 2027?**

A. 15 members, all from Grade 7.  
B. 45 members in total, 15 from each of Grades 7, 8 and 9.  
C. 100 members from any grade.  
D. Only 5 members.

**2. A student loves writing in English and would like to make short videos for the club. Which team should she choose?**

A. Hardware Team.  
B. Code Team.  
C. AI + Daily Life Team.  
D. Reporter Team.

**3. A Grade-8 student has a science score of 72, a maths score of 80, and wants to apply. Will this student be ACCEPTED to apply?**

A. Yes — the minimum is 70 for Grade 8.  
B. No — Grade 8 students must have science and maths scores of 75 or above.  
C. Yes — scores do not matter at all.  
D. No — Grade 8 students are not allowed to apply.

**4. Which of the following is TRUE about the application process?**

A. All applicants must skip the interview and submit only the form.  
B. Path A requires a 150-word English introduction on "one idea I would like to build", with the paper form due before Monday 5th October, 17:00; all applicants must still attend a 30-minute interview on Saturday 10th October in the Maker Space.  
C. The final name list will only be shared by phone call, never online.  
D. Programming experience is required to apply.

**5. Which of the following actions would BREAK the club rules?**

A. Tying long hair back before using the 3D printer.  
B. Returning all equipment to its box at the end of a meeting.  
C. Copying another student's code and submitting it as your own.  
D. Eating dinner at home before the Friday meeting.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】正文 "add 45 new members (15 from each grade, Grade 7 to Grade 9)"  
【解析】各年级招 15 人，共 45 人。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 1 部分 Reporter Team "Write English reports, shoot short videos"  
【解析】记者组负责英语报道与短视频。A、B、C 均不匹配。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节判断题  
【定位】第 3 部分 "Science and maths scores of 75 or above (Grade 7: 70 or above)"  
【解析】8 年级需两科均 75 及以上。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 部分 Path A、面试安排  
【解析】B 选项完整吻合原文流程。A、C、D 均与原文相悖。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节判断题  
【定位】第 5 部分第 5 条 "copying another student's code is not"  
【解析】抄袭同学代码明令禁止。A、B、D 均符合规则。故选 C。
