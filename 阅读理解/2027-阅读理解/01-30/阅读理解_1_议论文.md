# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Schools Add More P.E. Classes?**

① In many Chinese middle schools, students have only two or three physical education (P.E.) classes every week. Recently, some doctors and teachers have suggested that schools should add more P.E. classes, maybe one every school day. Is this a good idea?

② Those who agree have strong health reasons. According to a recent city report, the number of students with back pain and poor eyesight has gone up quickly in the last five years. More time sitting at the desk means less time moving. A daily P.E. class helps students run, jump, stretch their backs, and rest their eyes from screens. A stronger body often means a stronger mind in math and English lessons.

③ Supporters also say that P.E. is more than exercise. It is a lesson about teamwork, rules and fair play. On the football field, students learn to pass the ball, cheer for friends and shake hands with the other team. These soft skills will help them in clubs, in workplaces and in family life. A daily class allows more space for such learning.

④ However, some parents worry about the extra time. They say students already have heavy homework and little time for sleep. If one more P.E. class replaces a language or math class, students may fall behind in key exam subjects. A few parents even worry about the safety of daily running, especially in hot or polluted weather.

⑤ In my view, adding more P.E. is a good idea, but the plan must be careful. Schools can keep the total length of a school day the same, and add a 30-minute "active period" instead of another full class. They can also choose safe, simple activities such as rope jumping or quick table tennis inside the classroom on bad-air days. A healthy student can fight tiredness, learn better, and enjoy more of life. That is a win for everyone.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How many P.E. classes do most middle-school students have every week now?**

A. None.  
B. One.  
C. Two or three.  
D. More than five.

**2. According to Paragraph ②, what is one health problem that has become worse?**

A. Poor eyesight and back pain.  
B. A serious fear of water.  
C. The love of spicy food.  
D. Children growing too tall.

**3. What does the writer say P.E. can teach besides exercise?**

A. How to speak three foreign languages.  
B. How to cook for a big family.  
C. Teamwork, rules and fair play.  
D. How to fix a computer.

**4. Why do some parents worry about more P.E. classes?**

A. They think P.E. teachers are too young.  
B. They fear students will fall behind in key exam subjects.  
C. They believe P.E. is boring for students.  
D. They think the school day is too short.

**5. What does the writer finally suggest?**

A. Adding a full new P.E. class every day without changing anything.  
B. Replacing English and math classes with sports.  
C. Adding a 30-minute "active period" instead of another full class.  
D. Stopping all P.E. classes for a whole year.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "students have only two or three physical education (P.E.) classes every week."  
【解析】每周 2-3 节。A、B、D 均不符。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "the number of students with back pain and poor eyesight has gone up."  
【解析】背痛与视力差都增多。B、C、D 均无依据。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "a lesson about teamwork, rules and fair play."  
【解析】体育还教团队合作与规则。A、B、D 均无依据。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "students may fall behind in key exam subjects."  
【解析】家长担心影响主科学习。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "add a 30-minute 'active period' instead of another full class."  
【解析】作者建议设置 30 分钟活动时段。A 与作者态度不符；B、D 与原文相反。故选 C。
