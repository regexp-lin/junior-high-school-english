# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle Schools Start Classes Later in the Morning?**

① At 6:30 on a cold December morning, my alarm clock rings for the third time. The sky is still black, my eyes will not open, and I know — from three winters of practice — that the next forty minutes will be a tiny, quiet war between me and my bed. Then I get up, grab bread, and run for a bus at 7:10 to make it to the first lesson at 7:50. Across our country, millions of middle-school students live almost the same morning. Recently more schools, parents, and doctors have begun to ask: **why not start the school day later?** In my view, **moving the first lesson from 7:50 to 8:30 would do far more good than harm, and middle schools should seriously consider it.**

② The first reason is **biology**. Teenagers are not simply "being lazy" when they cannot wake up at six. Sleep scientists have shown that between roughly ages 12 and 17, the body's natural sleep rhythm shifts later. Most teens feel truly sleepy only after 22:30 and are not really awake before 07:30. The American Academy of Sleep Medicine has said for years that middle- and high-school start times before 8:30 "fight against the teenage body". You cannot argue with a clock that is ticking inside you.

③ The second reason is **enough sleep**. Middle-school students need around **9 hours** of sleep a night to grow well and think clearly. But most of us get 7 or 7.5. A 2026 survey of 12,000 students in three Chinese cities found that only **17%** reached 9 hours on a school night. With a later school start, students could either go to bed at the same time and sleep longer, or — even if they still slept 8 hours — begin the day when the body is really ready. Both paths help memory, mood, and physical growth.

④ The third reason is **safety**. Many middle-school students ride bikes or walk across busy roads before the sun rises in winter. Tired riders see less, react more slowly, and cross roads they "think" are empty. Reports from several cities show that morning traffic accidents involving students rise sharply between 7:00 and 7:30. When schools start at 8:30, most students travel in daylight. A few extra minutes of sunshine can, in a very real sense, save lives.

⑤ Of course, many adults worry. "If school starts later," they say, "it will finish later — and students will have even less free time." This is a reasonable worry, but it can be solved. Schools can trim five minutes from lunch, ten minutes from class breaks, or adjust the evening self-study hour. In a 2025 pilot in Shenzhen, 15 schools moved to an 8:30 start without pushing the end time later than 16:30 for Grades 7 and 8. In three months, students' daily sleep increased by an average of **38 minutes**, absence because of illness dropped by 15%, and — to many parents' surprise — exam scores did not fall.

⑥ Another worry is about **working parents**. "We must leave home by 7:30," some parents say. "Who will watch the children before school?" This is a real concern, especially for families without grandparents nearby. The answer, however, is not to keep the bell at 7:50. Schools can open a **quiet morning room** from 7:20, where students read, review, or simply eat breakfast, with one teacher on duty. The official lesson can still start at 8:30. Students gain flexibility without losing safety.

⑦ Some may also ask, "Isn't waking up early a lesson in itself? Life will force us up early one day." This sounds wise, but it misses a key point. The question is not whether young people should ever wake up early. The question is whether **years of forcing 6:30 wake-ups** on teenage bodies that are not ready helps them — or simply teaches them to live with a quiet, steady tiredness. Real discipline comes from rested minds, not sleep-starved ones.

⑧ A later start is a small change on a clock, but a big change in a life. Forty extra minutes of sleep, a safer morning trip, clearer lessons, kinder moods at home — none of these are luxuries. They are needs. For the sake of our students, and the teachers who teach them, it is time to stop asking teenage bodies to fight their own clocks. Move the first bell to 8:30, and watch a quiet, healthy revolution begin.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Middle schools should start classes even earlier, at 6:30.  
B. Middle schools should move the first lesson from 7:50 to 8:30.  
C. All schools should be closed in winter.  
D. Students should never go to school in the dark.

**2. Why do teenagers often have trouble waking up at 6:30, according to paragraph ②?**

A. Because they are simply lazy.  
B. Because their natural sleep rhythm shifts later during ages 12–17; they feel sleepy only after 22:30 and are really awake only after 07:30.  
C. Because their parents keep them up all night.  
D. Because 6:30 a.m. does not exist in winter.

**3. What did the 2026 survey of 12,000 students in three Chinese cities show?**

A. All middle-school students got 10 hours of sleep.  
B. Only 17% of students reached 9 hours of sleep on a school night.  
C. Sleep is not important for students.  
D. Teenagers sleep more than adults.

**4. How does the writer answer the worry that "students will get home later"?**

A. Schools can trim a few minutes from lunch and breaks; a 2025 Shenzhen pilot showed 8:30 start did not push end time past 16:30 for Grades 7–8.  
B. Students should simply accept going home at 22:00.  
C. The end time does not matter at all.  
D. School weekends can be cancelled.

**5. What does the writer say about "working parents must leave home by 7:30"?**

A. Schools cannot do anything for those families.  
B. Schools can open a quiet morning room from 7:20 for students, with one teacher on duty, while lessons still start at 8:30.  
C. All parents should change jobs.  
D. Grandparents are the only answer.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】首段末句  
【解析】主张把早课改到 8:30。A/C/D 均偏。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "body's natural sleep rhythm shifts later..."  
【解析】生物钟后移。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "only 17% reached 9 hours..."  
【解析】仅 17% 达到 9 小时睡眠。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "trim five minutes from lunch... Shenzhen..."  
【解析】压缩午餐课间、深圳试点未延迟放学。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "quiet morning room from 7:20..."  
【解析】提早开"安静晨间室"。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
