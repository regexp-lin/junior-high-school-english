# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Cats Sleep So Much**

① Walk into any Chinese family with a cat, and you will see the same gentle picture: the cat is **sleeping** — on the sofa, on the laundry basket, on the window sill in the afternoon sun. If you watch carefully, it looks like the cat's main job on the earth is to close its eyes. On average, a house cat sleeps **12 to 16 hours a day**; kittens and older cats may even sleep **20 hours** out of 24. Why does such a clever little animal rest so much?

② The first reason hides in cats' **long history as hunters**. Long before they slept on our sofas, their wild grandparents hunted rats, small birds, and insects in the grass. Hunting is hard work: a lion-like sprint, a careful jump, sharp claws — all in less than one minute. After each hunt, the body needs time to **repair tired muscles and refill energy**. Sleep is the cat's ancient battery charger. Even in a warm flat, with a bowl of dry food ready at any time, the cat's body still follows that old plan: hunt in short bursts, rest for a long time.

③ The second reason is a **special kind of sleep** cats enjoy. When you watch a sleeping cat, you may see two faces. In **light sleep**, its ears still turn a little, its tail twitches, and the slightest sound will make it jump up. In **deep sleep**, its whole body is soft and warm, and its eyes move quickly under the lids — a sign of dreaming. Light sleep helps the cat save energy without losing its safety; deep sleep helps the brain and body recover. About **three-quarters** of a cat's sleeping time is light sleep, which is why cats can look like they are "half sleeping, half listening" for most of the day.

④ The third reason is simply **food and temperature**. Cats are mostly meat eaters. A tiny mouse does not give them much energy, so even small cats save energy by moving less. Heat also plays a part: cats love warm corners, and a warm cat is more likely to doze. In winter, they curl up near a heater; in summer, they sleep on the cold bathroom floor. A smart cat always follows the temperature that feels best.

⑤ So, is this long sleep good for them? **Yes, when it is normal.** But owners should watch for sudden changes. If a cat suddenly sleeps much more, refuses food for more than a day, or hides in a strange corner, it may be feeling sick; a vet visit is wise. On the other hand, a cat that never settles down, keeps running and crying at night, may need more play or more company.

⑥ Next time your cat closes its eyes on your homework book, don't be too quick to push it away. A cat's long, soft sleep is a quiet story of **millions of years of hunting, listening, and resting**. Its sleepy face, in a way, connects your warm flat with a wild field a thousand years ago.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How many hours a day does a normal house cat usually sleep?**

A. 2 – 4 hours.  
B. 6 – 8 hours.  
C. 12 – 16 hours.  
D. 22 – 24 hours.

**2. Why do cats need so much sleep according to paragraph ②?**

A. Because they are too lazy by nature.  
B. Because hunting takes huge energy in short bursts, so rest is needed.  
C. Because cats hate sunlight.  
D. Because cat food has too much sugar.

**3. What is the difference between "light sleep" and "deep sleep" in cats?**

A. In light sleep, the cat's ears still move; in deep sleep, eyes move under the lids and the body is soft.  
B. Deep sleep is shorter than light sleep by a few seconds only.  
C. Cats only have light sleep and no deep sleep.  
D. Light sleep happens only in the morning.

**4. When should a cat owner worry about a cat's sleep?**

A. When the cat sleeps 12 hours in one day.  
B. When the cat enjoys a warm corner near the heater.  
C. When the cat suddenly sleeps much more, eats nothing, and hides in a strange place.  
D. When the cat dreams during a nap.

**5. What is the main purpose of the last paragraph?**

A. To ask owners to push cats off homework books.  
B. To remind us that a cat's sleep links today's home with an old wild past.  
C. To warn that cats are dangerous animals.  
D. To advise keeping cats awake as much as possible.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "12 to 16 hours a day"  
【解析】家猫每日睡 12–16 小时。A、B、D 不符。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "hunting is hard work... repair tired muscles"  
【解析】狩猎耗能需长睡恢复。A、C、D 不符。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段描述  
【解析】浅睡耳动，深睡眼快动、身软。B、C、D 与原文不符。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "sleeps much more, refuses food for more than a day, hides in a strange corner"  
【解析】异常表现需看兽医。A、B、D 属正常情况。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨推断题  
【定位】第⑥段 "millions of years of hunting, listening, and resting"  
【解析】猫的长眠连结家与远古。A、C、D 与主旨不符。故选 B。
