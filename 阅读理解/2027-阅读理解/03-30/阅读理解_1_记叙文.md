# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Waking Up Early for the Sunrise**

① I have always been a "night child". At eleven o'clock my eyes are still bright, but at seven o'clock in the morning, my pillow seems to have **tiny glue** all over it. So when Mum said, "Let's climb **Cloud Hill** this Sunday to see the sunrise," I only smiled — and quietly hoped she would forget.

② She did not forget. On Saturday night, at 22:00 sharp, she set the alarm to **4:30 a.m.** "It takes two hours to drive and walk up," she said softly. "If the sun sees us late, it won't wait." I sighed. I love Mum, but at that moment, I loved my blanket even more.

③ The alarm rang like a small cruel bell. Through half-open eyes, I put on two jackets, a hat, and even thick socks — spring mornings in our city are colder than people think. By 5:10 we were on the quiet highway. The streets looked almost empty; only a few delivery drivers in yellow jackets were rushing past. Mum opened a thermos of hot milk and a small tinfoil packet of rice balls. "**Breakfast of sunrise climbers**," she said, smiling. The milk was so warm that my half-sleeping brain clicked on, little by little.

④ We parked at the foot of Cloud Hill at **5:40**. A thin white mist floated between the pine trees. We climbed up a stone path together, listening to our own breath. At first I complained — my legs were heavy, my face cold, and **I could not see anything special** in the grey sky. Mum did not push me to walk faster. She just said, "The best moments don't shout. They whisper. You need to walk to them."

⑤ At about **6:15**, we reached the old **pavilion at the top**. Already twenty or thirty people stood in a careful row along the wooden railing — old grandfathers with tea flasks, a young couple with their dog, even three boys my age with a tripod and a small camera. We stood at the east side and waited.

⑥ Then it began. First, the sky turned **light grey**; next, the far line of hills became a thin, bright **orange thread**. Suddenly, a small red arc peeked above the tree tops. In only one minute, the whole round sun was there — big, warm, quiet. The cold mist started to shine, as if someone had lit a tiny lantern inside every drop. I did not say anything. Nobody did. A long silence fell over the pavilion, more gentle than any song.

⑦ When we walked down, I was tired, but a new, strange happiness sat inside my chest. Mum glanced at me. "Worth it?" she asked. I nodded hard. She smiled and added, "The world has **another face** between 4 a.m. and 7 a.m. Most people never meet her. Today you did."

⑧ That afternoon, I slept for two long hours. But in my dream, I kept seeing the red arc over the pine trees, and I kept hearing Mum's quiet voice about the other face of the world. Since that Sunday, I still go to bed a little late sometimes. Yet now, every once in a while, I set my own alarm for 4:30, put on two jackets, and walk toward the early, quiet sun.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did the writer usually feel about early mornings before this trip?**

A. He loved to get up before sunrise every day.  
B. Morning was the easiest time of his day.  
C. Waking up in the morning was very hard for him.  
D. He liked to read books before breakfast.

**2. What did Mum bring for the early breakfast?**

A. Coffee and bread.  
B. Hot milk and rice balls.  
C. Orange juice and cookies.  
D. Green tea and dumplings.

**3. What did Mum mean by "The best moments don't shout. They whisper"?**

A. People should always speak in a quiet voice.  
B. Beautiful moments are often soft and need patience to be found.  
C. The mountain was too noisy for travelers.  
D. Mum was angry at the writer for complaining.

**4. What happened at about 6:15 at the pavilion?**

A. The writer and his mum finally reached the top.  
B. The sun had already fully risen.  
C. They began to climb the mountain.  
D. The sky became dark again.

**5. What did the writer mainly learn from this sunrise trip?**

A. It is better to sleep all day and skip sunrise.  
B. The world has another quiet face in early morning, worth the tired legs.  
C. Cameras are the best way to keep memories.  
D. Mum was wrong to wake him up so early.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "I have always been a 'night child'... at seven o'clock... tiny glue"  
【解析】自认是夜猫子，早起困难。A、B、D 与原文相反。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "a thermos of hot milk and a small tinfoil packet of rice balls"  
【解析】热牛奶+饭团。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词句理解题  
【定位】第④段 Mum 的话 + 后文日出对照  
【解析】美好时刻需耐心。A、C、D 均非妈妈语义。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "At about 6:15, we reached the old pavilion at the top"  
【解析】6:15 到达山顶亭。B、C、D 时间点不符。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦、⑧段 "The world has another face... walk toward the early, quiet sun"  
【解析】世界还有另一面，值得付出努力。A、C、D 与主旨不符。故选 B。
