# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Do We Get Goosebumps?**

① On a cold winter morning, you step out of a warm room, and a strong wind blows on your bare arms. Suddenly, hundreds of tiny bumps rise on your skin like small hills. Or perhaps you are listening to a beautiful piece of music, and all at once, the back of your neck feels strange and slightly cold. These little bumps have a very old name — **goosebumps**, because they look like the rough skin of a goose with the feathers pulled off.

② Goosebumps are not caused by a wish or a dream. They are the work of tiny **muscles** below the skin. Every **hair** on your body sits in a small hole, and next to each hole is a tiny muscle called the *arrector pili*. When something cold or exciting happens, a quick message runs from your brain to this little muscle. It pulls hard, the hair stands up, and a small round bump appears on your skin. Multiply this by millions of hairs, and — there you go — a whole field of goosebumps.

③ But why does the body do this? Science offers two interesting answers. The first comes from **our animal ancestors**. Long ago, humans had thick fur, much like cats and dogs today. When the air turned cold, the fur would rise up and trap a layer of warm air near the skin, keeping the body warmer. Today our fur is gone, but this ancient system still runs inside us. The muscles pull, but the hair is too thin to keep much heat. Goosebumps on a human are almost a souvenir from an old world.

④ The second reason is connected with **strong feelings**. When ancient people felt danger, their fur would rise, making them look bigger and scarier to enemies. The same system starts when we are afraid today — that is why a scary film or a sudden noise in a dark corridor can give us goosebumps. Interestingly, strong positive feelings, such as a moving song or a moment of deep surprise, also switch on the same tiny muscles. In both cases, the brain is saying, "Something important is happening. Pay attention."

⑤ Goosebumps also leave clues about our health. Doctors sometimes ask patients to notice when their skin feels cold or bumpy. Frequent goosebumps without clear reason may hint at a fever on the way, or that a person has not had enough warm clothes. On the good side, some scientists in 2022 found that the tiny muscles near each hair may also help **new hair grow** more quickly. Far from being useless, those small bumps might be quiet helpers.

⑥ So next time a wind blows or a song moves you to tears, look at your arms. Those tiny hills are a small message from a body that remembers its long journey — through ice, through fire, through caves and cities — all the way to this very second. Even the smallest things on our skin carry the story of humanity's long walk.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why are goosebumps called "goosebumps"?**

A. Because they only appear on geese.  
B. Because they look like the rough skin of a goose with the feathers pulled off.  
C. Because geese first told humans about them.  
D. Because they taste a little like goose meat.

**2. What causes goosebumps on our skin?**

A. Tiny muscles pulling the hairs up.  
B. Cold water under the skin.  
C. Thick fur growing quickly.  
D. The sun warming the body.

**3. Why did ancient humans find goosebumps useful?**

A. They could grow new fingers.  
B. Their thick fur rose and trapped warm air near the skin.  
C. They could walk on water.  
D. Their skin could change colour.

**4. Besides fear, what kind of feelings can also give us goosebumps?**

A. Only feelings of hunger.  
B. Strong positive feelings, such as a moving song.  
C. Feeling sleepy after lunch.  
D. Only feelings of anger.

**5. What did some scientists discover in 2022?**

A. Goosebumps are a dangerous disease.  
B. The tiny muscles near each hair may help new hair grow more quickly.  
C. Humans no longer get goosebumps in winter.  
D. Only children can have goosebumps.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "like the rough skin of a goose"  
【解析】因其形似拔毛后的鹅皮而得名。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "tiny muscles... It pulls hard, the hair stands up"  
【解析】立毛肌收缩使汗毛竖起。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "trap a layer of warm air near the skin"  
【解析】古人厚毛竖起可保暖。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "strong positive feelings, such as a moving song"  
【解析】强烈正向情感也会诱发。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "help new hair grow more quickly"  
【解析】2022 年研究发现其或助毛发生长。故选 B。
