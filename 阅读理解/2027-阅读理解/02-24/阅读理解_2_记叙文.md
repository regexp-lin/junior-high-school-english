# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Saving the Little Swallow**

① It was the first warm afternoon of spring. I had just come back from school and was sitting at the kitchen table, eating an apple, when I heard a soft, frightened cry from the yard — **"cheep… cheep…"**. At first I thought it was only the wind, but the sound came again, weaker this time.

② I walked outside. Under the old plum tree lay a tiny **black-and-white swallow**, no bigger than my palm. One of its small wings was open at a strange angle, and its chest was moving up and down very quickly. It must have fallen from the high nest under the roof. Orange Cat from next door was already watching it from the wall, tail swinging slowly.

③ My heart beat fast. "Stay calm," I told myself. I ran back inside, got a shoebox and lined it with a soft old towel. Then I picked up the baby bird as gently as I could, the way my biology teacher had shown us. Its little feet held my finger tightly. I closed the lid halfway so it could still breathe but Orange Cat could not see.

④ Dad came home at six. He looked at the wing carefully and shook his head. "I'm not a bird doctor," he said, "but I know someone who is." He called **Aunt Wei**, a young woman who volunteers at the city's **Wildlife Rescue Centre**. She told us exactly what to do: keep the bird warm, do not give it water through its beak, and bring it in the next morning.

⑤ That night I hardly slept. I checked the shoebox three times with a small flashlight. The swallow was still breathing. Early the next day, Dad and I took a taxi to the rescue centre. Aunt Wei cleaned the wing, gave the bird a tiny drop of medicine, and put it into a warm room with other young birds.

⑥ Two weeks later, on a bright Saturday, Aunt Wei sent me a short video. The swallow — now strong and quick — flew in a big circle above a green field and disappeared into the blue sky. I watched the video again and again, and a quiet feeling filled my chest. I had not saved the world that day. But I had saved **one small life**, and for that one life, my one afternoon had made all the difference.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where did the writer first hear the strange cry?**

A. In the classroom.  
B. At the kitchen table.  
C. At the rescue centre.  
D. On the school bus.

**2. What had probably happened to the little swallow?**

A. It had fought with Orange Cat.  
B. It had fallen from a high nest.  
C. It had flown from another country.  
D. It had been hit by a car.

**3. What did the writer do to keep the bird safe from the cat?**

A. Carried it to school.  
B. Put it in a shoebox with a towel and closed the lid halfway.  
C. Tied its feet with a rope.  
D. Asked a neighbour to watch it all night.

**4. What advice did Aunt Wei give on the phone?**

A. Feed the bird lots of water at once.  
B. Keep the bird warm and take it to the centre the next morning.  
C. Let the bird fly as soon as possible.  
D. Put the bird back under the tree.

**5. What did the writer mainly learn from this experience?**

A. Even one small action can make a real difference to a life.  
B. Birds are dangerous pets.  
C. School is more important than animals.  
D. Taxis are faster than bikes.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段  
【解析】在厨房餐桌听到叫声。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第②段 "fallen from the high nest"  
【解析】小燕子从高处鸟巢跌落。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "shoebox ... lid halfway"  
【解析】用鞋盒毛巾，盖半盖避免猫看见。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段魏阿姨建议  
【解析】保持温暖，次日送中心。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段末句  
【解析】一次小行动也能改变一个生命。故选 A。
