# 阅读理解（议论文） **Are Short Videos Really a Useful Way to Learn?**

① Open any phone in any middle-school classroom, and you will find the same small icon on the home screen — the bright square that leads to short videos, where a clip lasts 15, 30, or 60 seconds. Many students now use these videos not only for fun but also to "learn" English grammar, maths tricks, history facts, and cooking steps. Teachers, however, are quietly divided. Are short videos really a useful way to learn, or are they a beautiful cage that keeps our minds jumping from one thing to another? In my view, **short videos can be a helpful learning tool — but only when we use them carefully, like a small side dish, not a main meal.**

② First, there is no doubt that short videos have real **strengths**. A good 60-second clip can show how a heart pumps blood, how to fold a pair of trousers, or how to use a new English word, all in a way that a chapter of dry text cannot. Pictures, sound, and motion work together. For visual learners especially, a single clear animation may save a whole evening of confused reading. Short videos also bring ideas from far away: a biology teacher in Iceland, a museum curator in Egypt, a Chinese calligraphy master in a small town can each be in our pocket in a few taps.

③ Second, short videos offer **quick answers**. When a student wants to know what "photosynthesis" means in English, a 30-second animation can give both a clear picture and a correct pronunciation faster than most textbooks. During homework, when we are stuck on a hard maths idea at 9 p.m., a short video can sometimes unblock us in one or two minutes, and we can return to the problem with new confidence. These are real gains, not to be denied.

④ However, short videos have serious **weaknesses**, too, and these must be faced honestly. The most common problem is **shallow learning**. A 60-second clip gives the top layer of an idea, not the deep roots. If we only watch the clip and never practise — never write the sentence, solve the problem, or explain the rule in our own words — the idea quickly fades. Research from Beijing Normal University in 2025 found that students who only watched grammar videos remembered **25% less** after one week than students who read a short text and then did practice questions. Video feels like learning, but feelings can mislead us.

⑤ A second weakness is the **jumping habit**. Short videos are built to keep us watching one after another. The average user switches clips every 30 seconds. Once our brain gets used to this rhythm, it becomes harder to sit with one paragraph, one chapter, one hour-long lesson without feeling bored. Several class teachers have reported that students who watch many short videos in the evening find it noticeably harder to focus on a long reading task the next day. Study, in the end, is not a thirty-second trick; it is a slow, patient conversation with a subject.

⑥ A third worry is **quality**. Some short videos are made by thoughtful teachers. Others are made by people who are simply trying to sell something or attract clicks. A confident voice and a cool background music do not equal correct knowledge. Students, especially young ones, often find it hard to tell the difference. One wrong rule heard in a short clip can stay in the head for years.

⑦ Does this mean we should delete the apps and forget them? No. The answer is not to refuse the new tool, but to **use it with rules**. I suggest three simple ones. **Rule one: video first, then book.** After watching, always read a short article on the same topic to check what the video left out. **Rule two: watch, then write.** For every ten minutes of study video, spend at least ten minutes writing notes in our own words or solving a related question. **Rule three: clock the app.** Set a daily limit of 20 to 30 minutes total, and put the app in a folder called "Tools", not on the home screen.

⑧ In short, short videos are powerful — sometimes too powerful. Used wisely, they are a small, bright window that lets more light into our study room. Used carelessly, they are a soft pillow where our attention falls asleep. The difference is not in the phone. The difference is in the careful, grown-up hands that hold it.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main view on using short videos for learning?**

A. Short videos should never be used by students.  
B. Short videos can be a helpful learning tool, but must be used carefully, like a small side dish, not a main meal.  
C. Short videos should completely replace books and lessons.  
D. Only teachers, not students, should watch short videos.

**2. According to the passage, what are the real strengths of short videos?**

A. They make students taller and healthier.  
B. They use pictures, sound and motion to explain ideas, and can bring experts from far away into our pocket.  
C. They do our homework for us.  
D. They are always 100% correct.

**3. What did the 2025 Beijing Normal University study find?**

A. Students who only watched grammar videos remembered 25% less after one week than students who also did practice questions.  
B. Students who only read textbooks forgot everything.  
C. Video learning is always better than reading.  
D. Short videos cured students' forgetfulness.

**4. Which of the following is NOT one of the writer's "three simple rules" for using short videos?**

A. Watch a video first, then read a short article on the same topic.  
B. For every ten minutes of study video, spend at least ten minutes writing notes or solving a related question.  
C. Set a daily limit of 20 to 30 minutes and put the app in a folder called "Tools".  
D. Watch as many short videos as possible before every exam, with no limit.

**5. What does the writer mean by "the difference is not in the phone" in the last paragraph?**

A. Any phone can be useful if it is expensive.  
B. The real question is how we — with care and self-control — use the tool, not the tool itself.  
C. Phones are always bad, and only books are good.  
D. Students should buy new phones to learn better.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】首段末句 "short videos can be a helpful learning tool — but only when we use them carefully..."  
【解析】作为"小配菜"使用。A/C/D 均偏。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Pictures, sound, and motion work together..."  
【解析】图像声音动作并用，跨国专家触手可及。A/C/D 均荒谬。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "students who only watched grammar videos remembered 25% less..."  
【解析】只看视频一周后遗忘多 25%。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 三条规则  
【解析】规则为先视频后书、看后动笔、控时放入工具夹。D 与之相反。故选 D。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段末句  
【解析】区别在于是否理性使用工具，而非工具本身。A/C/D 均偏。故选 B。
