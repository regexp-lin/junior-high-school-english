# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Morning I Was the Flag-Raiser**

① My English teacher, Ms. Guo, stopped me at the classroom door one Friday afternoon last October. "Linxi," she said quietly, "your class has been chosen to raise the flag on Monday morning. **You** will be the flag-raiser." I stared at her for two full seconds, unable to feel my own feet. I had been in two flag-raising teams before, but never in the front row. Never the one whose hand would actually pull the rope.

② The weekend passed in a fog of worry. On Saturday night, I stood in front of my bedroom mirror and practised lifting my right hand in a straight line, five centimetres below my chin, exactly as the P.E. teacher had shown us. My little brother watched from the door and whispered, "You are going to drop it, sister." Mum sent him away with a spoon. "Nobody drops a flag," she said, with a calm I did not believe.

③ On Sunday night I wrote the words of the national anthem on a small piece of paper and slept with it under my pillow. I did not need the paper — I had sung the anthem every Monday since Grade 1 — but the paper made me feel **less alone** in the dark room. At around two o'clock I woke up, sure I had forgotten my white gloves. I got out of bed, found them folded on my desk, and went back to sleep, smiling at myself for being silly.

④ Monday morning. 07:25. The playground was full of **one thousand and four hundred** pairs of eyes, all facing east. The sky was a colourless pale grey, a little cold. My two team-mates and I stood behind the tall pole, flag folded like a big red envelope in my partner Xiaowen's arms. My hand was on the rope. The rope was **colder** than I had expected. Music started — three slow drum beats, then the first note of the anthem.

⑤ I pulled. Too fast. The flag rose too quickly in the first second and almost reached the end before the second line of the song. My heart fell into my shoes. Xiaowen, behind me, whispered four English words from Ms. Guo's morning class: "**Slow hands, slow heart.**" I heard her. I steadied my wrist. For the last seven lines of the anthem, I moved the rope in tiny, even pulls, like a fisherman lifting a net full of small fish. When the flag reached the top, the last note was also just ending. The playground was silent for half a second — and then a small wind came from the east and **opened** the red square against the sky.

⑥ We stepped back. The headteacher gave her Monday speech; I did not hear a single word. I was watching the flag move. The cold in my fingers slowly turned warm. Behind the microphone, Ms. Guo caught my eye from the staff row and, for a second, raised her eyebrow — the tiny, silent **"well done"** that teachers give when they do not want others to hear.

⑦ In the classroom afterwards, my partner Xiaowen passed me a small note. It said: "*At the beginning, too fast. But the end was a poem.*" She had drawn a little flag beside the words. I folded the note into four and slipped it into my English book. When our form teacher asked me to describe the morning in five words, I said: "**Scary, cold, slow, warm, and long.**" Everyone laughed, but I meant every word, in order.

⑧ That night I took down the pink sticker on my desk that had said "Do not make mistakes" since the start of Grade 8. I replaced it with a small piece of paper that said, in my neatest English, "**Slow hands, slow heart.**" It is still there. Every time I face a hard moment — a hard question on a test, a hard conversation with Mum — I read those four words. And I remember that on one ordinary Monday in October, in front of one thousand four hundred pairs of eyes, I pulled a red flag up a cold pole, made **one** mistake, and still, somehow, **ended like a poem**.

---

**注释：** flag-raiser 升旗手；anthem 国歌；pole 旗杆；headteacher 校长；eyebrow 眉毛

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did the writer feel when Ms. Guo first told her she would be the flag-raiser?**

A. Proud and ready.  
B. Surprised and unable to feel her own feet.  
C. Bored and sleepy.  
D. Angry at her brother.

**2. Why did the writer sleep with the words of the anthem under her pillow on Sunday night?**

A. Because she had not memorised them yet.  
B. Because the paper made her feel less alone in the dark room.  
C. Because her mum asked her to.  
D. Because she thought the paper would bring her luck in a test.

**3. What mistake did the writer make when the music started on Monday morning?**

A. She pulled the rope too slowly.  
B. She dropped the flag.  
C. She pulled too fast, and the flag almost reached the end before the second line of the anthem.  
D. She forgot the melody.

**4. What four English words from Ms. Guo's class helped the writer steady herself?**

A. Do not make mistakes.  
B. Slow hands, slow heart.  
C. Lift your chin up.  
D. Look at the flag.

**5. What does the story mainly tell us?**

A. Flag-raising is an easy, boring duty.  
B. Always practise in front of a mirror.  
C. Even when we start a hard moment with a mistake, calm hands and a calm heart can still let us "end like a poem".  
D. The writer is better than her classmates.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第①段 "stared at her for two full seconds, unable to feel my own feet"  
【解析】震惊失控。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "the paper made me feel less alone in the dark room"  
【解析】心理作用直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Too fast ... almost reached the end before the second line"  
【解析】节奏过快。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Slow hands, slow heart."  
【解析】同伴提示直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧段结尾 "ended like a poem"  
【解析】以"慢手慢心"收束成长。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。
