# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Old Bicycle I Almost Sold**

① The bicycle had been leaning against our balcony wall since I was in Grade 5. It was an ordinary **dark-green** bike with a slightly rusty chain, two worn-out handle grips, and a small **silver bell** that still said "ding" in a clear, thin voice. My father had bought it second-hand the summer I learnt to ride. For almost two years it went with me everywhere — to the street market, to the small lake behind the school, to the noodle shop on Saturdays. Then, in Grade 7, a new electric bike came into our home, and the old green bicycle slowly became **a quiet shadow** on the balcony.

② On the first Saturday of September this year, I decided to sell it. Mum had been saying the balcony looked crowded. I had seen online that a second-hand app could take photos in thirty seconds. I opened the app on my phone, pointed the camera at the old bike, and tapped the "**post**" button. I wrote just four words: "**old bike, cheap, working**." Within an hour, a man messaged me: "Will come at 3 p.m."

③ At lunchtime, Dad came out onto the balcony holding two apples. He stopped when he saw me wiping the dust off the bike. "You are selling it?" he asked, in that careful voice he uses when he is trying to **not** say too much. I nodded. "It just sits here. Nobody uses it." He stood there for a moment, looked at the old green frame, and quietly put the apples on the small table. "Clean it well, then," he said. "Give it one last clean."

④ I was a little surprised — he usually cared about electricity bills, not old bicycles. But I wiped the wheels, cleaned the chain with an old cloth, and pumped some air into the tyres. As I worked, Dad came out from the living room with a small box. Inside were **two things**: a tiny spanner, and a thin notebook with a brown cover. "Remember these?" he asked. I looked at him blankly.

⑤ He opened the notebook. On the first page, in my own childish handwriting from Grade 5, there was a shaky line: "**Day 1 — fell down 4 times. Knees hurt. Hate this bike.**" On the next page: "Day 6 — I rode three metres without holding on! Dad was crying." I looked up at him. He only smiled and turned to the last entry, dated a summer Sunday in Grade 6: "Day 34 — Today I took Dad to the lake. He sat behind me. He said he was safe. I was so proud I wanted to shout." My eyes began to sting a little, and I did not know why.

⑥ "I wrote those entries **with** you," Dad said softly. "You dictated, I wrote. You were too small to hold a pen and the spanner at the same time." He turned the spanner in his hand. "This is the one we used to fix the back brake every month. It still fits." Then he said something I had not expected: "You can still sell the bike. It is yours. I only wanted you to know — **this bike is not just metal**."

⑦ At 2:55 p.m., the buyer's message came: "I am downstairs." I stood on the balcony with the bike. I looked at the silver bell, and gave it a gentle flick. "**Ding.**" The same thin, clear voice. In my memory, a Grade-5 girl with a small helmet wobbled along the lake path, laughing too loudly. I thought of her. I thought of the father running behind her, arms out, afraid and proud.

⑧ I opened the app and wrote a short message to the buyer. "I am very sorry. I have changed my mind. Please choose another bike. Thank you for your time." I pressed send before my courage could run away. Then I walked down to the storeroom, found a larger cover, and gently pulled it over the old green bike. I did not throw it away. I did not sell it. I did not even ride it — not yet.

⑨ That evening, Dad did not say a single word about the bike. He only asked me to pass the soy sauce at dinner. But as we ate, I noticed his eyes moving to the balcony door every now and then, very quickly, as if checking that the green shadow was still there. And it was. It is. Some things, I have learnt, cost almost nothing and are worth more than any money can buy.

---

**注释：** rusty 生锈的；spanner 扳手；dictate 口述；flick 轻弹；wobble 摇晃

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer first decide to sell the old bicycle?**

A. The bicycle was badly broken and useless.  
B. The writer wanted to buy a new pair of shoes.  
C. The bike had been leaning unused on the balcony, and Mum said the balcony looked crowded.  
D. Dad asked him or her to sell it.

**2. What was Dad's reaction when he first saw the writer cleaning the bike to sell?**

A. He was very angry and shouted at the writer.  
B. He spoke softly, told the writer to "give it one last clean", and brought out a small box of old things.  
C. He laughed loudly and left the balcony.  
D. He called the police.

**3. According to the old brown notebook, what did the writer do on "Day 34"?**

A. Fell off the bike and hurt a knee.  
B. Rode the bike alone to the street market.  
C. Took Dad to the lake, with Dad sitting behind, and felt very proud.  
D. Sold the bike for the first time.

**4. After looking at the notebook and the silver bell, what did the writer finally decide?**

A. To sell the bike for a higher price.  
B. To throw the bike away that evening.  
C. To cancel the sale, keep the bike, and gently cover it in the storeroom.  
D. To give the bike to a complete stranger online.

**5. Which sentence best expresses the main lesson of this story?**

A. "Old things are always better than new things."  
B. "Some things cost almost nothing and are worth more than any money can buy."  
C. "Parents should never keep old bicycles."  
D. "Only new bicycles are safe to ride."

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末 "became a quiet shadow on the balcony"；第②段 "Mum had been saying the balcony looked crowded"  
【解析】因长期闲置、阳台拥挤而决定出售。A、B、D 均无依据。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "'Clean it well, then,' he said"；第④段 "Dad came out from the living room with a small box"  
【解析】父亲语气克制，让"我"好好清理，并拿出一只小盒子。A、C、D 均与原文相悖。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Day 34 — Today I took Dad to the lake. He sat behind me. He said he was safe. I was so proud"  
【解析】Day 34 的内容为带爸爸去湖边并感到自豪。A 为 Day 1；B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑧段 "I have changed my mind. Please choose another bike"；"pulled it over the old green bike. I did not throw it away. I did not sell it"  
【解析】取消出售并将车罩好留存。A、B、D 均与原文不符。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨/写作意图题  
【定位】第⑨段末 "Some things ... cost almost nothing and are worth more than any money can buy"  
【解析】该句即全文主旨：有些事物无关价格，却难以用金钱衡量。A、C、D 均为表面误读。故选 B。
