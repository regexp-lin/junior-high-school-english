# 阅读理解（应用文） **City Teen Pen-Pal Programme 2027 — Write, Send, Connect**

---

## **About the Programme**

The **City Teen Pen-Pal Programme** connects middle-school students (Grade 7–9) in our city with teenagers from twelve other cities around the world. Every **paper letter** you write will be placed in a blue envelope, crossed with a bright stamp, and sent by real post — not email. Our aim is simple: in an age of fast texts and short videos, we want teenagers to practise the slower, warmer habit of **writing by hand to someone far away**.

This is the 5th year of the programme. In 2026, more than 1,800 students joined us from our city alone.

---

## **Who Can Join?**

- **Age:** Grade 7–9 students from any school in the city.  
- **Language:** You must be able to read and write simple English. (CEFR A2 and above is enough — don't worry about perfect grammar.)  
- **Attitude:** Willing to write **at least 4 letters a year** and reply within **three weeks** after receiving a letter from your pen-pal.

*Grade 6 students may join as "Junior Pen-Pals" with a parent's help.*

---

## **What You Get**

- A pen-pal matched by age, hobby, and English level.  
- **A "Starter Pack"** posted to your home in late September:  
  * 6 blue airmail envelopes  
  * 12 pieces of light, lined writing paper  
  * 2 gel pens  
  * 1 small booklet, "*How to Write a Friendly Letter in English*"  
- Free **monthly online workshops** (Saturday 10:00–11:00) on letter-writing skills, travel stories, and friendship across cultures.  
- A certificate from the City Education Bureau after completing **4 letters**.  
- The chance to be published in our end-of-year booklet *Dear Friend Far Away*.

---

## **Rules & Tips**

1. Every letter must be **between 120 and 250 words**. Long enough to share a real story, short enough to stay readable.  
2. **Share safely.** Give your first name and school only. Do not share your full address, phone number, or ID on the first three letters. Home addresses will be exchanged later, through the Programme Office.  
3. **Please be kind.** If you disagree with your pen-pal about food, football, or pop stars, that is fine — but stay polite.  
4. **Keep to the deadline.** If you cannot reply in three weeks, please email us so your pen-pal does not feel forgotten.  
5. No money, gifts of high value, or private social-media accounts in the first term. Small flat items — a postcard, a bookmark, a drawing — are welcome.
