# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle-School Students Do One Hour of Sports Every Day?**

① Walk past any middle school during a study evening, and you will see the same picture: rows of heads bent over books, eyes a little red, shoulders a little tight. In recent years, our country has clearly said that middle-school students should do **at least one hour of sports every school day**. Some parents and students, however, quietly ask: is that too much? In my view, **one hour of daily sports is not a waste of time — it is one of the smartest investments a middle-school student can make**.

② The first reason is the **body**. A young person's bones, heart and lungs grow strongest when they are used regularly. A 2024 school-health report in our city compared 1,000 Grade 8 students: those who did sports for at least 50 minutes every day grew, on average, **2 cm taller** over the year than those who did less than 20 minutes. They also fell ill about 40% less often. These numbers are not small. Growing bodies need movement as much as they need sleep, rice or water.

③ The second reason is **the brain**. It sounds surprising, but running on a playground makes the brain work better at a desk. When we exercise, the heart sends more blood — and more oxygen — to the brain. Memory, focus, and mood all improve. A well-known experiment from a middle school in Beijing asked one class to run lightly for fifteen minutes before an English vocabulary test. The class scored an average of **7 marks higher** than a parallel class that simply sat and reviewed. Sports are not the enemy of study; they are a quiet helper.

④ Still, some people worry: "One hour a day is too long. It takes time away from subjects." This worry is fair, but only if we think of learning as a cup that can be filled only with writing and reading. Real learning also happens through movement — a child who can jump higher trusts his own body more, and a team member who runs a good pass learns teamwork no maths problem can teach. The one hour does **not** come from our study time. It can come from the ride-and-walk part of the day, the short break between classes, and the time we usually spend on phones in the evening. Research shows that students who do sports every day sleep better and finish homework **faster**, not slower.

⑤ Another worry is about **injuries**. Some parents worry that a ball to the face or a fall on the track is too dangerous. Safety is important, and schools must take it seriously: warm-up first, clear rules, soft mats, and a teacher always nearby. But the answer to danger is not to sit still. A careful run is far safer than long hours of no movement, which quietly damage the young body and often bring back pain, poor eyes, and even anxiety.

⑥ Some say that "sports" means the same boring thing — laps around a track. But one hour can be many things: basketball today, a jump-rope game tomorrow, cycling with Dad on Saturday, table tennis with Grandma after dinner. Students should be allowed to **choose**, and schools should offer a menu, not a single dish. When we enjoy the activity, one hour feels short.

⑦ A final, quieter benefit is **mood**. Many middle-school students carry stress we adults forget. Sports give the body a way to let the stress out. A tired, honest tiredness after a game is very different from the tight, heavy tiredness after too much sitting. Students who move regularly often say they feel calmer in exams, kinder to friends, and happier when they go home.

⑧ One hour a day is small: it is less than a single episode of a TV show, less than the time we spend on a phone, less even than the time we spend waiting for a school bus in a week. But that one hour, given back to the body every day, grows into stronger health, sharper minds, and steadier moods. It is, in short, **the cheapest, kindest gift we can give our future selves**.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion in this passage?**

A. Middle-school students should not do any sports.  
B. One hour of daily sports is a smart investment for middle-school students.  
C. Sports should replace all school subjects.  
D. Only boys should do sports every day.

**2. What did the 2024 school-health report in the writer's city find?**

A. Students who did more sports fell ill more often.  
B. Students who did at least 50 minutes of daily sports grew about 2 cm taller on average over a year and fell ill 40% less often than those who did less than 20 minutes.  
C. All students grew the same amount regardless of sports.  
D. Daily sports caused weaker bones.

**3. How does exercise help with study, according to the passage?**

A. It lets students skip homework.  
B. It sends more blood and oxygen to the brain and improves memory, focus and mood.  
C. It allows students to cheat in tests.  
D. It makes teachers give higher marks for no reason.

**4. How does the writer answer the worry that "sports take time away from subjects"?**

A. By saying students must give up all homework.  
B. By saying the one hour can come from commuting, breaks and phone time, and that daily sports help students sleep better and finish homework faster.  
C. By saying only weekend sports are useful.  
D. By saying sports should last three hours, not one.

**5. Why does the writer mention basketball, jump-rope, cycling and table tennis in paragraph ⑥?**

A. To show that "sports" can mean many enjoyable activities, and schools should offer a menu of choices.  
B. To sell basketball shoes.  
C. To prove that table tennis is the only safe sport.  
D. To say that students should only play with adults.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】首段尾句 "one hour of daily sports is not a waste of time..."  
【解析】明确支持每天一小时运动。A/C/D 均偏。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "those who did sports for at least 50 minutes every day grew... 2 cm taller... fell ill 40% less often."  
【解析】身高+2cm、患病少 40%。A/C/D 均与原文反。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "more blood — and more oxygen — to the brain. Memory, focus, and mood all improve."  
【解析】运动给大脑供氧，改善记忆专注情绪。A/C/D 均荒谬。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段 "It can come from the ride-and-walk part of the day... sleep better and finish homework faster..."  
【解析】运动时间来自通勤、课间、手机时间，并能提升学习效率。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑥段 "schools should offer a menu, not a single dish."  
【解析】说明运动可多样、学校应提供选择。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。
