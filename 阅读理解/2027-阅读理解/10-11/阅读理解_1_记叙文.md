# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Man Who Stood in the Rain with an Umbrella**

① On a heavy Wednesday in early October, I went home later than usual. Our class had stayed to clean the classroom for the new-term open day. By the time I walked out of the school gate at half past six, the rain had already begun — thin at first, then thick, then blowing sideways in a sharp autumn wind.

② My umbrella was at home. So was my mother, who had the flu that week and could not come to pick me up. My plan was simple: run to the bus stop two streets away, catch the 7 o'clock bus, and hope my schoolbag did not turn into a sponge on the way.

③ Near the little flower shop at the corner, I passed a **blind man** waiting with his white stick at the zebra crossing. I had seen him a few times before; he often came home from the massage clinic around this hour. Today he was getting very wet. The rain was running off his grey jacket in small silver lines, and he was standing still — I guessed he could not hear the traffic clearly through the noise of the rain.

④ I almost ran past. The bus was the bus; the rain was the rain. But at the last step, I stopped. I had no umbrella, but I had two hands, and I had eyes.

⑤ "Uncle," I said, a bit louder than I meant to. "The light is red. I'll tell you when it turns green." He turned his face slightly towards my voice and smiled without showing his teeth. "Thank you, young friend. Today the sound is not very clear."

⑥ We waited. I stepped a little ahead of him, so the wind hit me first. My hair was already dripping down my collar. Thirty seconds. Forty. Then the light changed. "Green now," I said, and we walked across the long zebra crossing together, my sleeve very lightly touching his wet jacket so he could feel the line of my motion.

⑦ On the other side, he said thank you again and turned towards his lane. I turned in the other direction, towards the bus stop — and suddenly a dry, warm thing was held over my head. An umbrella. A **large black umbrella**. Behind me, a tall man in a delivery uniform was holding it.

⑧ "I saw what you did at the crossing," he said quietly. His face was half hidden by the wind. "My grandfather was blind. Here — your bus stop is two streets away, right? Let me walk with you." He had a big heavy insulated bag of food on his other shoulder; he was clearly late for his own delivery.

⑨ I tried to refuse, but he only laughed and said, "Hurry, hurry, the rain is getting bigger." At the bus stop he handed me a **second** umbrella, a small folded one from his delivery bag. "Give it back some other day," he said, "my shop is near the hospital — Green Light Dumplings, anyone knows us." Before I could even read his name badge, he was already running off into the grey, the big black umbrella bouncing on his shoulder.

⑩ That night I sat at home with my small red thermos of ginger tea, and I thought about the rain. For five minutes I had helped a blind man cross a road. For another fifteen minutes, a total stranger had walked with me through the storm — only because he had seen what I did. Kindness, I suddenly understood, is not a one-time gift; it is **a small signal that other people, quietly, also know how to send back**.

---

**注释：** zebra crossing 斑马线；massage clinic 按摩店；sleeve 袖子；insulated bag 保温袋；thermos 保温瓶

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why was the writer walking home alone in the rain?**

A. Because she was angry with her parents.  
B. Because her class had stayed late to clean the classroom, her umbrella was at home, and her mother (who had the flu) could not come to pick her up.  
C. Because the school bus had broken down.  
D. Because she wanted a nice walk.

**2. What did the writer do for the blind man at the zebra crossing?**

A. She called the police.  
B. She stood a little ahead of him, told him when the light turned green, and walked across with her sleeve lightly touching his jacket so he could feel her direction.  
C. She ran past him to catch the bus.  
D. She gave him her schoolbag.

**3. Why did the delivery man offer the writer his big black umbrella?**

A. Because he wanted to sell her food.  
B. Because he was bored and wanted to chat.  
C. Because he had seen her help the blind man at the crossing, and his own grandfather had been blind.  
D. Because the writer had paid him in advance.

**4. What did the delivery man give the writer **in addition to** walking her to the bus stop?**

A. A small box of hot dumplings.  
B. A plastic raincoat.  
C. A second, small folded umbrella from his delivery bag, so she could take it home.  
D. Ten yuan for the bus.

**5. What does the last paragraph mainly want to tell the reader?**

A. Kindness costs a lot of money.  
B. Helping others is useless in the rain.  
C. Kindness is not a one-time gift — when you send a small kind signal, other people quietly know how to send one back.  
D. Only rich people should help blind strangers.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①②段 "our class had stayed to clean the classroom ... my umbrella was at home. So was my mother, who had the flu"  
【解析】清洁延迟 + 自己没伞 + 母亲生病不便接。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "I stepped a little ahead of him ... 'Green now,' I said ... my sleeve very lightly touching his wet jacket"  
【解析】三步具体行动。A、C、D 均与原文相反或无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑧段 "I saw what you did at the crossing ... My grandfather was blind."  
【解析】快递员目睹其善举，自家亦有盲人亲人。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑨段 "he handed me a second umbrella, a small folded one from his delivery bag."  
【解析】多给一把折叠伞让她带走。A、B、D 均未出现。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨/写作意图题  
【定位】第⑩段 "Kindness ... is a small signal that other people, quietly, also know how to send back."  
【解析】善举具有回响性。A、B、D 与原文立场相反或荒谬。故选 C。
