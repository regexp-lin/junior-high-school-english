# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Day the Old Piano Came Home**

Grandma's living room had always felt empty to me, though I could never say why. There was a sofa, a small table, two wooden chairs, and a picture of my grandfather on the wall. Only when the old piano came home did I finally understand: a whole world had been missing, and no one talked about it.

The piano arrived on a rainy Saturday in March. Two men pushed it slowly through the front door, their shoes leaving small dark prints on the floor. Grandma stood by the window, pressing one hand against the glass. Her other hand, I noticed for the first time, was shaking.

"Where did it come from, Grandma?" I asked.

"A kind neighbour from years ago," she said, her voice so soft that I almost missed it. "The piano used to be mine. When your grandfather got sick, I sold it to pay for his doctor. The neighbour, Mr. Lu, kept it in his room for thirty years. Now he is moving to the countryside, and he wants me to have it back."

Thirty years. The number did not fit into my head.

The workers lifted the wooden lid. The keys inside were yellow, like old teeth, but each one was still there. Grandma walked over, very slowly, as if she were afraid the piano might disappear. She sat down on the little bench. Her fingers hung in the air for a long time, then fell gently onto the keys.

The sound that filled the room was not beautiful at first. The strings needed tuning, and her fingers had forgotten the quick dance of long ago. But after a few notes, something strange happened. Her face changed. The lines around her eyes softened. She began to hum, then to smile. A tune appeared — simple, slow, a little sad — and I realised I had heard it before, not from Grandma, but from a music box she kept hidden in her drawer.

"This was your grandfather's favourite," she told me, without taking her eyes off the keys. "I played it the night we met."

Afterwards, we did not speak for a while. The rain stopped. A thin line of sunlight crossed the piano, making the yellow keys shine like gold. I brought her a cup of warm water. She patted my hand and said, "Sit. You will learn this song, too."

Every Sunday after that, I went to Grandma's house. I learnt slowly, one hand first, then both. My fingers were stiff and clumsy; hers, once again, were quick and sure. Sometimes I played wrong notes on purpose just to hear her laugh. The living room, which had seemed empty for as long as I could remember, was suddenly full — of music, of stories, and of a grandfather I had never been old enough to know.

A year later, Mr. Lu came to visit from the countryside. He listened to Grandma play, then looked at me and said, "Child, the piano was always hers. I was only keeping it warm." Those words stayed with me. Some things, I think, never really leave us. They wait — in a neighbour's room, in a drawer, in an old song — until the right day comes to bring them home.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why had Grandma sold the piano many years ago?**

A. Because she didn't like music any more.  
B. Because her house was too small.  
C. To pay for Grandfather's doctor when he was sick.  
D. Because Mr. Lu asked to buy it.

**2. How long had Mr. Lu kept the piano?**

A. Three years.  
B. Thirteen years.  
C. Thirty years.  
D. Three hundred years.

**3. What did Grandma do right after she sat down at the piano?**

A. She played a fast and happy song at once.  
B. Her fingers hung in the air, then fell gently onto the keys.  
C. She closed the piano and walked away.  
D. She asked the writer to play for her.

**4. Why does the writer say the living room was "suddenly full"?**

A. Because Mr. Lu moved in.  
B. Because new furniture arrived.  
C. Because music, stories, and memories of Grandfather filled the room again.  
D. Because many neighbours came to visit every Sunday.

**5. What can we learn from Mr. Lu's words "I was only keeping it warm"?**

A. Mr. Lu was not the real owner; he was just keeping the piano safe for Grandma.  
B. The piano was cold in his countryside house.  
C. Mr. Lu wanted to sell the piano again.  
D. Mr. Lu didn't like the piano.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"sold it to pay for his doctor"  
【解析】为丈夫治病卖琴。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"kept it in his room for thirty years"  
【解析】三十年。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"Her fingers hung in the air for a long time, then fell gently onto the keys"  
【解析】手指悬空然后轻落琴键。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】因果推理题  
【定位】"full — of music, of stories, and of a grandfather..."  
【解析】充满音乐、故事与对爷爷的回忆。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】"The piano was always hers..."  
【解析】卢先生只是替奶奶保管。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。
