# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Day I Lost My Voice Before the English Contest**

① For three weeks, I had been practising for the **City Middle School English Speech Contest**. My topic was "A Letter to the Earth in 2050". I practised in front of a mirror, in the lift, and even while washing my hair. Mum said I talked about the Earth in my sleep. The contest was on Friday afternoon. Everything was ready — except, on Friday **morning**, I woke up and found that **my voice was gone**.

② At first I thought I had not woken up fully. I opened my mouth to say "Good morning", but only a tiny, scratchy sound came out, like a thin wind through a paper. Mum put a hand on my forehead and said, "No fever, but you went running in the cold rain on Wednesday, remember? And you shouted at that football match last night." I nodded, horrified. **How could I give a speech with no voice?**

③ I sat on the sofa, silent. Mum made honey lemon tea. Dad came over with a folded newspaper and wrote on it: "**Hospital first. Panic later.**" At the clinic, Dr. Wang smiled kindly and said my **vocal cords** had some soft swelling. "Voice rest, warm water, no shouting. You may speak softly by this afternoon — no promises." Inside me, I felt a heavy stone slowly settle in my chest.

④ At school, my English teacher, Ms. Li, listened to my broken whispers. She did not scold me. Instead, she said, "We have two choices, Jiayu. One: I tell the judges you are sick, and you give up. Two: we try our best — and if you cannot speak, we explain on the stage." I wanted to say "I will try", but my voice cracked halfway. Tears jumped to my eyes. Ms. Li handed me a packet of tissues and a warm flask of water.

⑤ During lunch break, my best friend Mei and I walked to a quiet corner of the garden. She held my hand and said, "**Your speech is already in your heart. Your voice is just the last mile.**" She reminded me to breathe from the belly, not the throat. Then she did something funny: she pretended to be the judges, made a grumpy face, and whispered "mooo" like a small cow. I laughed so hard that a real, clear word came out: "Thank you!" I was shocked. My voice, though thin, had returned.

⑥ At 3:30 p.m., my name was called. I walked onto the bright stage with shaky legs. My first line — "Dear Earth, you are our only home" — was not loud, but it was steady. In the middle, my voice almost left me again. I took a deep breath, looked at the back wall, and tried to push my words softly, **not from my throat, but from my stomach**. The judges leaned forward to listen. I could see kindness in their eyes.

⑦ I did not win the first prize. I got third place, with a wooden-edged certificate and a warm note from a judge: "*Your voice was quiet, but your message was loud.*" On the bus home, I stared out at the spring streets and thought: the Earth, and I, had something in common. Even a small, tired voice can carry a big message, as long as it is real. I will remember **that nervous, whispered morning** for a long, long time.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer lose his voice on the morning of the contest?**

A. He caught a very bad flu.  
B. He ran in the cold rain and shouted at a football match.  
C. He ate too much ice cream.  
D. He sang loudly all night.

**2. What did the doctor tell the writer to do?**

A. Shout more to warm up the throat.  
B. Take pills for three weeks.  
C. Rest the voice, drink warm water, and not shout.  
D. Stop going to school for a month.

**3. What two choices did Ms. Li give the writer?**

A. To lose the contest quietly, or to shout at the judges.  
B. To give up and tell the judges he was sick, or to try his best.  
C. To leave school, or to change his speech topic.  
D. To cry on the stage, or to sing a song.

**4. What did Mei do to help the writer?**

A. She wrote a new speech for him.  
B. She gave him a toy cow.  
C. She held his hand, gave breathing tips, and made him laugh.  
D. She called his mother to bring him home.

**5. What did the writer learn from the experience?**

A. Only a loud voice can win a contest.  
B. Even a small, tired voice can carry a big, real message.  
C. It is best to give up when you feel sick.  
D. Judges only like funny jokes on stage.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "running in the cold rain... shouted at that football match"  
【解析】冷雨中奔跑加喊加油。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Voice rest, warm water, no shouting"  
【解析】医嘱三条。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 Ms. Li 的话  
【解析】要么放弃，要么尽力。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "held my hand... breathe from the belly... whispered 'mooo'"  
【解析】拉手+呼吸+逗笑。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦段 "small, tired voice can carry a big message"  
【解析】真心之声同样有力。故选 B。
