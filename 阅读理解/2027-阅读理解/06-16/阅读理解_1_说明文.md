# 阅读理解（说明文） **How Did Humans Measure Time Before Clocks?**

When we want to know the time today, we simply look at a phone or a wall clock. But for most of human history, people had no such tools. They still needed to know when to plant crops, when to meet friends, or how long it took to boil a pot of rice. So how did they do it? The story of time goes back thousands of years, and almost every tool used the same idea: **find something in nature that changes in a regular way**.

The earliest tool was the **sundial**. People put a straight stick on flat ground. As the sun crossed the sky, the stick's shadow moved slowly. By marking 12 or 24 lines around the stick, they could tell which part of the day it was. Sundials were simple and almost free, but they had one clear problem: they did not work at night or on cloudy days. In rainy areas, a sundial was useful for only half the month.

To solve this, some early scientists invented the **water clock**. The idea was clever. A large pot was filled with water, and a small hole was made at the bottom. As water dripped out at an even speed, the water level fell. Marks on the side of the pot showed how many "hours" had passed. Some water clocks, built in ancient China and Egypt, could tell time even at night. However, water clocks had problems too. Cold weather could turn the water to ice, and the speed of water falling changed a little with the water level.

Later, Europeans used **sand glasses**, also called hourglasses. A thin neck of glass connected two bulbs of glass. Fine sand moved slowly from the top bulb to the bottom. When all the sand had fallen, a fixed amount of time had passed — often 30 minutes or one hour. Sand glasses were safe on ships, because water might spill, but sand stayed in the bulb. Sailors used small sand glasses to plan their work on long voyages.

The biggest change came in the 14th century, when Europeans built the first **mechanical clocks** with gears and weights. These clocks were installed in the towers of cities. People then looked up at the tower and heard the bells. Over the next centuries, the clocks became smaller and smaller. In the 17th century, the **pendulum clock** made time much more accurate, and in the 20th century, the **quartz watch** made accurate time cheap enough for everyone.

Today, our phones are checked every second by atomic clocks in big science centres. These modern clocks are billions of times more accurate than the stick and the shadow. And yet, the simple idea is still the same: find something in nature that changes in a regular way, and use it to count the moments.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What was the main problem of the sundial?**

A. It was very expensive.  
B. It did not work at night or on cloudy days.  
C. It was too small to see.  
D. It moved when a strong wind blew.

**2. How did a water clock tell the time?**

A. By heating water and watching the steam.  
B. By shouting the time every hour.  
C. By letting water drip out at an even speed and watching marks on the side of the pot.  
D. By weighing the pot every minute.

**3. Why were sand glasses safe on ships?**

A. Because sand is very heavy.  
B. Because sand did not spill the way water might, so the tool stayed usable.  
C. Because sand could stop a fire.  
D. Because the sailors enjoyed counting grains.

**4. What important change happened in the 14th century?**

A. People stopped using clocks completely.  
B. Europeans built the first mechanical clocks with gears and weights in city towers.  
C. Chinese engineers built the first water clocks.  
D. Phones replaced all clocks.

**5. What is the main idea of the passage?**

A. Humans have always used complicated machines to tell the time.  
B. Only the sundial gives real time.  
C. Through history, humans have used many tools to tell time, but the idea is the same: find something in nature that changes regularly.  
D. Modern clocks are worse than old ones.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段 "did not work at night or on cloudy days"  
【解析】日晷夜晚和阴天无效。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段 "water dripped out at an even speed..."  
【解析】匀速滴水看刻度。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段 "water might spill, but sand stayed in the bulb"  
【解析】沙不会溢出。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段 "in the 14th century..."  
【解析】机械钟出现。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文  
【解析】历史多种计时工具，核心思想一致。A/B/D 均偏题。故选 C。
