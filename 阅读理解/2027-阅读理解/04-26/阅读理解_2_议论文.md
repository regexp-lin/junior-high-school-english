# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Children Be Paid for Housework?**

① In many Chinese homes, this spring, a small but warm argument is happening at the dinner table: **should children be paid for doing housework?** Some parents believe that offering a little money for washing dishes, sweeping the floor, or walking the dog is a fair and useful deal. Others worry that money inside the family may push out something more important — love. Let us take a careful look at both sides.

② People who support **paying** say it is a good way to teach **money sense**. A 13-year-old who has earned 20 yuan by cleaning the kitchen for five evenings may think twice before spending it on bubble tea. He may also learn simple ideas like "budget", "saving", and "fair wage". In a world full of online shopping and quick payments, such lessons are, in fact, very hard for schools alone to teach.

③ Paying can also make housework feel **fairer**. In many Chinese families, parents come home tired after long work hours, and children often help nothing. A small amount of pocket money in return for clear tasks may remind children that running a home is **real work**, and not something that happens by magic. Over time, a paid child may learn to see how much his mother does every day — silently, without payment.

④ On the other hand, many teachers and grandparents do not like the idea. They say a family is not a **company**. If every small kindness has a price, love slowly becomes a list of **receipts**. A child who has always been paid for sweeping the floor may one day ask, "Why should I help Grandma carry her shopping — she does not pay me." That, they argue, is a dangerous road.

⑤ There is also the worry that paying may turn kind children into **greedy ones**. Once a "market" exists in the family, prices may **rise**: "Ten yuan for dishes is too little now." Meanwhile, jobs that cannot be easily paid — like listening patiently to a tired father — may fall in value. Not all help can be measured by a small paper note or a WeChat transfer, and perhaps the best help never should be.

⑥ My own view tries to take a middle path. **Basic chores** — making one's own bed, putting away one's clothes, washing one's own bowl — should simply be part of being a family member, with no payment at all. But **extra, heavy, or skilled tasks** — such as cleaning the whole kitchen, washing the car, or helping Grandma with a two-hour shopping trip — can be connected with **small rewards**, like pocket money, book coupons, or family cinema tickets. The key is that the list is **written together**, and reviewed every season.

⑦ In this way, children learn two things at once: **love does not need money**, and **work does deserve thanks**. A healthy family is not a silent factory, nor a cold market. It is a place where dishes are washed together, and where, sometimes, a small reward is a whispered way of saying, "I see you. Thank you."

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main question of the article?**

A. Should children be paid for doing housework?  
B. How to wash dishes quickly.  
C. Should parents do all the housework?  
D. Should kids live alone at 12?

**2. According to paragraph ②, one benefit of paying children is that it helps them:**

A. Become famous on the Internet.  
B. Learn basic money skills like saving and budgeting.  
C. Eat more candy every week.  
D. Get better grades in P.E.

**3. What do people against payment worry about?**

A. The family can become like a company, and love becomes a list of receipts.  
B. Children will become too tall.  
C. Parents will lose their jobs.  
D. Schools will close.

**4. According to the writer, which task should NOT be paid?**

A. Cleaning the whole kitchen.  
B. Washing the family car.  
C. Making your own bed and washing your own bowl.  
D. Helping Grandma with a two-hour shopping trip.

**5. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Pay children for every little thing.  
B. Never pay children for any housework.  
C. Do not pay for basic chores, but give small rewards for extra, heavy, or skilled tasks, with a family list.  
D. Only pay boys, not girls.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末问题  
【解析】讨论是否付钱给孩子做家务。故选 A。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "money sense"  
【解析】学会基本理财观念。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "love slowly becomes a list of receipts"  
【解析】爱变成账单。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 基本家务  
【解析】铺床洗自己碗不应付酬。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥段  
【解析】中间路线，细节分类。故选 C。
