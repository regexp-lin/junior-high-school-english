# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Misunderstanding with My Best Friend**

① Lili has been my best friend since Grade 3. We sat at the same desk for three whole years. We knew which page of the maths book the other one secretly hated; we knew each other's lunchbox smells from ten steps away. In early October this year, for the first time in our lives, we stopped speaking for six silent days. It began with something very small.

② On a Tuesday evening, our teacher posted a group photo of our class on the class WeChat group. At the bottom of the photo was a small box of comments. A boy called Kun wrote: "Lili looks tired today." Before I could think, I typed: "Yes, she has been crying over that English test. Not surprising." Send.

③ My message was not mean. Lili had, in fact, cried quietly on Monday evening over her 72 in English. She had told me, in whispers, at the back of the classroom. I had meant "not surprising" as a soft joke — a friendly, teasing thing. But the moment my message sat inside that group of 52 classmates and 2 teachers, it looked cold, public, **like a betrayal**.

④ Within ten minutes, a private message came from Lili. "You told **everyone**?" Three seconds later she left our two-person chat. She had blocked me.

⑤ For the next six school days, we did not look at each other. I tried to send her a message three times. Each time her profile picture stayed grey. In class I stared at the back of her head and could not hear the lesson. On Sunday afternoon, my mother found me lying on my bed with my face pressed into the pillow. "Write it out first," she said, "before you say anything."

⑥ I wrote, by hand, a two-page letter. On the first page I explained what I had meant by "not surprising". On the second page I stopped explaining. I simply said: **"I was careless. Your number was 72, not mine. Your tears were yours, not mine. I had no right to put them into a public group. I am sorry."** I did not ask her to forgive me. I just put the letter in her maths textbook on Monday morning before she arrived.

⑦ At lunch she did not come to the canteen. At afternoon break, she walked past my desk and, without stopping, dropped a small folded paper on it. My heart stopped. I unfolded it. Inside, in her small careful writing: **"Thank you for the letter. I am still sad. But I am not angry any more. Can we walk home together tomorrow? 3:45, back gate."**

⑧ On Tuesday at 3:45 I was at the back gate ten minutes early. She arrived exactly on time, in her usual dark blue coat. We walked home slowly. For a long block we said nothing. Then she said: "I cried again on Saturday. I thought I had lost a friend because of one sentence." I said: "I was afraid I had, too." We both laughed, a small short laugh like something starting to warm up again.

⑨ That night in my diary I wrote a sentence I will not forget: **"In a good friendship, a careless sentence is a wound — and an honest apology, without excuses, is the only medicine that works."**

⑩ I still keep the small folded paper inside my pencil case. When I see it, I remember two things: how easy it is to hurt a friend in thirty seconds, and how much work and honesty it takes to walk that friend home again.

---

**注释：** betrayal 背叛；block 拉黑（聊天）；canteen 食堂；forgive 原谅；apology 道歉

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How long had the writer and Lili been best friends before the misunderstanding?**

A. Only a few weeks.  
B. Since Grade 3; they had even sat at the same desk for three years.  
C. Since they were babies.  
D. Only during this October.

**2. What exactly upset Lili?**

A. The writer had borrowed her pencil case without asking.  
B. The writer had told the teacher about her English mark.  
C. The writer publicly typed "she has been crying over that English test. Not surprising." in the class WeChat group of 52 classmates and 2 teachers.  
D. The writer had laughed at her in class.

**3. What did the writer's mother advise her to do?**

A. Wait for Lili to come first.  
B. Write it out by hand before saying anything.  
C. Send a long voice message.  
D. Forget the whole thing.

**4. How did Lili first reply after reading the handwritten letter?**

A. She shouted at the writer in class.  
B. She returned the letter with corrections in red pen.  
C. At the afternoon break, she dropped a small folded paper on the writer's desk saying thanks, admitting she was still sad but not angry, and asking to walk home together the next day at 3:45.  
D. She phoned the writer's parents.

**5. What main lesson did the writer learn from this experience?**

A. Never make friends online.  
B. It is easy to hurt a friend in thirty seconds, and only an honest apology without excuses can slowly heal that wound.  
C. Never write letters to anyone.  
D. Good friends never have misunderstandings.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "Lili has been my best friend since Grade 3. We sat at the same desk for three whole years."  
【解析】原文明确时长与同桌事实。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②③段 "she has been crying over that English test. Not surprising ... that group of 52 classmates and 2 teachers"  
【解析】真正伤害来自公开说出她的脆弱。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Write it out first, before you say anything."  
【解析】母亲建议先动笔整理。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "she walked past my desk and ... dropped a small folded paper ... 'Thank you for the letter. I am still sad. But I am not angry any more. Can we walk home together tomorrow? 3:45, back gate.'"  
【解析】一纸条件 + 时间地点明确。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨/写作意图题  
【定位】第⑨段 "a careless sentence is a wound — and an honest apology, without excuses, is the only medicine that works."  
【解析】作者明点主旨。A、C、D 均与文旨无关或相反。故选 B。
