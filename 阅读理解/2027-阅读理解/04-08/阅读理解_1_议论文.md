# 阅读理解（议论文） **Are Short Videos Eating Up Our Free Time?**

① Open any bus on a Monday morning, and you will see the same quiet picture: rows of people, from 13 to 73, staring at little glowing screens. Thumbs swipe up, up, up. A 15-second song, a cooking trick, a funny dog, a dance challenge — and then another one, and another. **Short videos** have become the tap our daily time is leaking from. The question is: is this new habit helping us, or quietly eating up our free time?

② Supporters say short videos are **fun and useful**. In a single afternoon, a student can learn to tie a tie, fix a squeaky door, or sing a new English song — all in tiny, easy clips. For lonely elderly people, short videos are like a small friend in the pocket, showing them farming in Yunnan, dance classes in Beijing, and flowers from all over the world. Some teachers even use short clips to show science experiments that would be too hard or too dangerous to do in a normal classroom.

③ Yet many experts and parents worry. First, the apps are designed to **hold attention as long as possible**. They use smart programs that choose the "perfect" next video for each user, making it hard to stop. Many students plan to watch "just five minutes" before homework, but two hours later, they are still on the sofa, with maths books still closed. Over time, this habit can make studying feel dull, because study cannot reward the brain every ten seconds the way videos can.

④ Second, short videos often show **fast and shallow** content. A deep science paper can be cut into a 20-second clip, but so can wrong or half-true information. Some videos may even push young people towards risky tricks, from dangerous diets to scary dares. When the mind gets used to very short, very bright messages, it may become harder to read long books, write long essays, or listen to a parent's careful advice.

⑤ Third, sitting still for hours with a phone is bad for our **body and eyes**. Doctors report more and more cases of back pain and short sight among teenagers. The shoulders curve in, the neck bends down, and the eyes stay glued to one bright spot of blue light. Short videos make the hours fly, but the body pays the bill later.

⑥ Should we give up short videos? Probably not. A better answer is to **become the master, not the servant**. Set a clear daily limit, such as 30 minutes. Turn off the auto-play feature. Watch with a purpose: learn a skill, practise a language, or relax for a short while, then put the phone away. Keep some "phone-free zones", like the dinner table and the last 30 minutes before sleep. Free time is precious; we should enjoy it, not lose it without noticing.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the article mainly about?**

A. How to make a popular short video.  
B. Whether short videos are helpful or harmful to our free time.  
C. The history of mobile phones.  
D. How to open a bus company.

**2. Which is NOT given as a benefit of short videos?**

A. They can teach small daily skills.  
B. They can keep lonely elderly people company.  
C. They can show science experiments safely.  
D. They can always give completely correct information.

**3. Why is it hard for students to stop watching short videos?**

A. The phone makes a warning sound every minute.  
B. Smart programs choose the "perfect" next video to keep users watching.  
C. Teachers ask them to keep watching.  
D. Parents pay them to watch more.

**4. According to paragraph ⑤, what health problems are caused by too much short-video watching?**

A. Stomach ache and fever.  
B. Hearing loss and tooth pain.  
C. Back pain and short sight.  
D. Skin problems.

**5. What does the writer finally suggest?**

A. We should completely give up short videos.  
B. We should become the master and set rules, not let videos control our time.  
C. We should watch as many videos as possible every day.  
D. We should forbid all apps on students' phones.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末问题  
【解析】全文围绕短视频对空闲时间的影响。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段列出益处 / 第④段提及错误信息  
【解析】文章指出短视频也会传播错误信息。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "smart programs that choose the 'perfect' next video"  
【解析】智能算法使人难停。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "back pain and short sight"  
【解析】背痛与近视。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥段 "become the master, not the servant"  
【解析】做自己的主人。故选 B。
