# 阅读理解（应用文） **Spring Pen-pal Programme: "East Meets West"**

---

**Jade Dragon Middle School × Green Hill School (New Zealand)**  
*A quiet, slow friendship — one letter at a time.*

---

**1. Programme Idea**

Our English Department is pairing **60 Chinese students** (Grade 7–9) with **60 students** of the same age at Green Hill School, Wellington, New Zealand. Over three months, each pair will **exchange three letters** to learn about each other's daily life, school, hobbies, and cities. All letters will be written in English.

---

**2. Main Dates**

| Letter | Theme | Deadline |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Letter 1 | "My school day and my hometown" | 30 April |
| Letter 2 | "A small story about my family or a friend" | 31 May |
| Letter 3 | "A traditional festival and one thing I want to ask" | 30 June |

*The Kiwi students will reply within three weeks after each letter.*

---

**3. Writing Rules**

1. Each letter should be **200–350 words**, handwritten or typed.  
2. Use **polite greetings** (e.g. "Dear Lily,") and **a short closing** (e.g. "Yours, Ming").  
3. You may **include one photo** (A6 size) with each letter: your classroom, pet, or favourite snack.  
4. No personal phone numbers, home addresses, or bank details.  
5. Every letter will be **read by a teacher** for safety before it is sent.

---

**4. What You Can Get**

- A real international friend who writes back in real pen and ink  
- A **Pen-pal Passport** — a small book to store your letters and stamps  
- At the end of the programme, a short **"Friendship Gala"** with video calls  
- An **"East Meets West" certificate** for your student file

---

**5. How to Join**

- Step 1: Ask your English teacher for the **Pen-pal Application Form**.  
- Step 2: Write a **short self-introduction (about 100 words)** on the form: your hobbies, your favourite book, your favourite food, and one question you want to ask a Kiwi friend.  
- Step 3: Hand the form to Teacher Green in **Office 205** before **Thursday, 15 April, 5:00 p.m.**  
- Step 4: You will be paired with a Kiwi student in three days; we try to match hobbies and grades.

---

**6. Kind Tips**

- If your pen-pal writes slowly, please wait patiently. **Slow letters are often warm letters.**  
- Try **not** to copy from translation apps word by word. Write what YOU really want to say.  
- If a letter never arrives, tell Teacher Green. Backup copies are kept in the office.  
- Please **do not sell or exchange** the letters online. They are private.

---
