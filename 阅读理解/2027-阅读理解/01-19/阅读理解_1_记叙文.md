# 阅读理解（记叙文） **My First Time on Ice**

① Last winter, my cousin Jack invited me to go to the new ice rink in our town. He was already a confident skater, but I had never tried before. On the bus, I kept looking at my watch. My hands were a little cold, not from the weather, but from nervousness.

② At the rink, I changed into heavy skating shoes. They felt strange, as if someone had tied two metal pencils to my feet. Jack helped me stand up, but as soon as I let go of his hand, my legs began to slide in two different directions. Crash! I fell down on my back within three seconds. Several young skaters passed by and smiled kindly, which made me feel warm and silly at the same time.

③ Jack did not laugh. He held out his hand and said, "Everyone falls the first time. Let's go slowly." He taught me to bend my knees a little and keep my feet close together. I tried again, and again, and again. I fell five more times in the first twenty minutes. My knees hurt, my gloves were wet, but I refused to go back to the bench.

④ After about an hour, something magical happened. I pushed off with my right foot and slid forward for a few seconds without falling. Jack clapped loudly. "You did it!" he shouted. I tried to stop and fell again, but this time I laughed instead of cried. Bit by bit, I learned to slow down, to turn in a wide circle, and even to wave at Jack while moving.

⑤ On our way home, the sky was full of stars. I thought about the bruises on my knees and the sound of the ice under my shoes. I realized something important: being afraid of falling cannot stop us from standing up again. The ice taught me that mistakes are just part of learning something new.

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did the writer feel on the bus to the ice rink?**

A. Excited and full of energy.  
B. Nervous although it was not very cold.  
C. Sleepy because it was late at night.  
D. Angry with his cousin Jack.

**2. Why did the writer's legs slide in two directions at first?**

A. Because the ice was too warm.  
B. Because he was wearing the wrong shoes.  
C. Because he did not yet know how to balance.  
D. Because Jack pushed him on purpose.

**3. What did Jack do when the writer fell the first time?**

A. He laughed loudly at him.  
B. He held out his hand and taught him how to stand.  
C. He called the skate teacher for help.  
D. He left the rink in anger.

**4. How did the writer finally feel when he slid without falling?**

A. Tired and sad.  
B. Successful and proud.  
C. Scared and quiet.  
D. Bored and cold.

**5. What lesson did the writer learn from his first time on ice?**

A. Falling is always a sign of bad luck.  
B. Only adults can learn to skate.  
C. Being afraid of falling cannot stop us from standing up again.  
D. Mistakes should be hidden from our friends.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末句 "My hands were a little cold, not from the weather, but from nervousness."  
【解析】作者紧张导致手凉，并非天冷。A 过度情感解读；C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第②段 "as soon as I let go of his hand, my legs began to slide in two different directions."  
【解析】刚放开哥哥的手即滑倒，说明他还不会保持平衡。A 与冰场事实不符；B 鞋子正是滑冰鞋；D 与原文相反。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "He held out his hand and said, 'Everyone falls the first time. Let's go slowly.' He taught me to bend my knees..."  
【解析】Jack 伸手并教他正确姿势。A 与"did not laugh"矛盾；C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "'You did it!' he shouted. I tried to stop and fell again, but this time I laughed..."  
【解析】成功滑行时作者感到成功并开心。A、C、D 情绪不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨/写作意图题  
【定位】末段 "being afraid of falling cannot stop us from standing up again."  
【解析】作者总结：不能因怕摔而放弃站起来。A、B、D 均与原文立意相反。故选 C。
