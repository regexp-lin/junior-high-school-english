# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Winter Solstice Calligraphy Workshop 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Chinese Department + Art Department**  
> **Winter Solstice Calligraphy Workshop 2027** — *"One Brush, One Word, One Warm Hour."*  
> **Date**: **Tuesday, 22 December 2027** (Winter Solstice, 冬至)  
> **Time**: **15:30 – 17:10** (100 minutes, after last afternoon class)  
> **Venue**: **Art Room 301 + Corridor A (3F)**  
> **Cost**: **Free** (all paper, ink, brushes, and couplets provided)

---

① On the shortest day of the year, **Tuesday 22 December 2027**, Lihua Middle School opens its calligraphy room for a small, warm, hundred-minute winter-solstice afternoon. The Chinese Department and Art Department invite **all Grades 7–9 students** — especially those who have never picked up a brush before — to come to Art Room 301, sit at a long wooden table with warm yellow light, and write **one single careful character** on a piece of red or white paper. You may take your own paper home, give it to a family member, or leave it on our **"Warm Word Wall"** for the whole school to see until 31 December. No skill is needed. No photo is posted.

## 🧑‍🏫 Student Captain Roles (12 captains)

- **4 Grinding captains**: help younger students grind the ink stone; no ink too thick, no ink too watery; **~2 minutes** of grinding per cup.  
- **4 Table-side captains**: help with brush grip (索笔式 or 五指执笔式), paper alignment, posture.  
- **2 Wall captains**: gently hang finished papers on the Warm Word Wall with wooden clothes-pegs, straight, 3 cm apart.  
- **2 Clean-up captains**: lead brush-washing at 17:05; teach proper brush-drying (pointed downward on rack).

All captains have practised calligraphy for **at least two years**. They will answer any small question quietly; **please do not be shy**.

---

## 🪄 Mr Tao's Four Small Rules

1. **Sit straight; feet flat on the floor.** *"Calligraphy starts at the knees, not the wrist."*  
2. **Breathe out, then write.** The exhale steadies the forearm; the stroke is slower and cleaner.  
3. **The first stroke is the hardest.** Do not scratch a light test line first; commit.  
4. **A shaky line is a true line.** Do not throw away a paper because the brush wobbled; the trembling of a young hand is part of the character.

---

## 🎒 What to Wear / Bring

- **A long-sleeved shirt** that is **not too loose** — loose sleeves drag in the ink.  
- **Hair tied back** if long.  
- **Bring one clean cloth bag** from home to carry the finished paper (the papers must lie flat for 20 minutes before folding).  
- **No sweet drinks** allowed in Art Room 301; plain water only, in a sealed bottle.

---

## 🍜 Small Kindness Table (in Corridor A)

- **Free hot ginger-honey tea** for every participating student (one small 100 ml cup).  
- **One small red-bean dumpling** (tangyuan) per student — a small nod to the winter-solstice tradition that tangyuan means "reunion of the family around a round warm thing."  
- **No photography** around the Kindness Table, please. Quiet drinking; quiet thinking.

---

## ❗ Important Quiet Reminders

- **This is NOT a competition.** Nobody's character is ranked. Nobody's character is photographed.  
- **The Warm Word Wall** is anonymous. You may sign with **only your year and class** (e.g. *7B*) at the lower-right corner, no name. Students who prefer to stay completely anonymous may leave the corner blank.  
- **Finished papers** stay on the wall until 31 December. After that, students may take their papers home; unclaimed papers are folded gently and donated to the reading corner of Sunshine Elderly Home.  
- **Absent homeroom teachers** will be sent a short written notice if their students attend.  
- **If you finish early**, please sit quietly at your table and watch a neighbour write; do **not** talk.

## 💬 A Small Word from Ms Qin and Mr Tao

> "The winter solstice is the shortest day of the year, and for thousands of years, our ancestors marked this short day by gathering, writing, eating warm food, and going to bed early. On the evening of 22 December, while most of us will simply walk home and sit under a warm electric light, we invite you first, for **one hundred quiet minutes**, to stand in front of a small white page with a small black brush — and to write **one careful word of your own** for the winter to come. It does not have to be beautiful. It only has to be yours. **A single character, written slowly by a fourteen-year-old on the shortest afternoon of the year, carries a kind of silence that no screen in the world can quite replace.** Come. The paper is waiting."  
> — *Chinese Department & Art Department, 21 December 2027*
