# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Does Our Breath Turn White on Cold Days?**

① Every child in northern China learns this little winter trick long before school. On the first truly cold morning, we step outside, blow out a long breath towards a friend, and there it is — a small, soft **white cloud** in the air that disappears in two seconds. We laugh, we breathe out again, and we chase the cloud with our finger. But when my cousin Zhou Chen asked me last week, "Why is my breath white only in winter?", I suddenly realised I did not really know. The answer, it turns out, is a small classic of physics and is very easy to understand.

② First, a simple fact: our breath is **always wet**. Inside our lungs, the air is kept at about **37 °C** and is full of water. When we breathe out through the mouth and nose, the air leaves our body carrying a large amount of **invisible water vapour**. This is true in July as in January. In summer, you cannot see your breath because the water vapour stays invisible in the warm outside air. So the question is really: **what changes in winter?**

③ The answer lies in **temperature**. Warm air can hold a lot of water vapour; cold air can hold much less. Think of air as a large soft pocket. In summer, the outdoor pocket is wide open and can hold many small drops of water, completely unseen. In winter, the outdoor pocket becomes **small** and tight — the cold air simply **runs out of room** for the extra water coming out of your mouth.

④ When our warm, wet breath meets the very cold outside air, the water vapour **suddenly condenses** into **tiny water droplets**. Each droplet is about **0.01 millimetre** wide — too small to fall as rain, but big enough to reflect sunlight. Millions of these tiny droplets together form what our eyes see as a soft, white cloud for one or two seconds. Then the droplets spread out and evaporate back into the drier air, and the cloud is gone.

⑤ The "white" colour is worth a word. Water droplets alone are clear. But when light hits **millions of tiny droplets** from every direction, it gets scattered equally at all wavelengths — red, yellow, green, blue. When all wavelengths scatter together, our eyes see the mixture as **white**. This is the same reason clouds in the sky look white, snowflakes look white, and a cup of hot milk looks pale in a sunbeam. A thousand tiny mirrors of light, working together, become white.

⑥ How cold does the outside air need to be before we can see our breath? Generally, you need the outside temperature to be about **5 °C or lower**, and low humidity helps. In a dry, cold desert the same breath makes a longer cloud; in a warm, wet bathroom the cloud disappears at once. That is why, in Chongqing or Guangzhou, breath-clouds are rare even in January, while in Harbin or Tibet they are everyday winter toys.

⑦ The same simple rule explains many winter scenes. Why does a glass of iced orange juice grow a coat of tiny water drops on the outside? The cold glass cools the invisible water vapour in the kitchen air; the vapour condenses onto the glass, just as your breath condenses in cold air. Why does the window of the Tuesday-morning bus "fog up"? Forty warm, wet pairs of lungs are quietly exhaling; the cold glass turns their breath into a thin film of water droplets. Even the **foggy mirror** after a hot shower is the same physics in a smaller frame. "**Warm water, meet cold surface.**" That is the whole lesson.

⑧ My cousin Zhou Chen listened to all of this with a serious face. At the end he said, "So my breath is really a **tiny cloud factory**." I smiled. He is not wrong. The next time you walk to school on a cold morning, blow out a long breath and watch the small quiet cloud in front of your nose. You are not just breathing; you are running a **tiny weather system** — a warm wet lung, a cold sky, and the invisible water of your own body, briefly turned visible, briefly yours, and then — like most beautiful things in life — gently **gone**.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the key fact about human breath in Paragraph ②?**

A. Our breath is always dry.  
B. Our breath is always wet; it leaves the body at about 37 °C, full of invisible water vapour, both in summer and in winter.  
C. Our breath has no water in summer.  
D. Our breath is cold when it leaves the nose.

**2. Why can cold air NOT hold as much water as warm air, according to Paragraph ③?**

A. Because cold air is a solid.  
B. Because the cold air's "pocket" is small and tight; it runs out of room for the extra water coming out of our mouth.  
C. Because water hates the cold.  
D. Because our mouths are smaller in winter.

**3. What happens the moment warm, wet breath meets very cold outside air?**

A. It freezes instantly into ice.  
B. The water vapour suddenly condenses into tiny water droplets, about 0.01 mm wide, which together look like a soft white cloud for one or two seconds.  
C. It becomes completely invisible.  
D. It turns into oxygen.

**4. Why does a "breath cloud" appear white in colour?**

A. Because it contains white salt.  
B. Because when light hits millions of tiny droplets from every direction and scatters equally at all wavelengths, our eyes see the mixture as white.  
C. Because breath has a white smell.  
D. Because clouds always come in pairs.

**5. Which of the following is NOT mentioned in the passage as an example of the same "warm meets cold" physics?**

A. A glass of iced orange juice growing tiny water drops on the outside.  
B. The fogged-up window on a winter-morning bus.  
C. The foggy mirror after a hot shower.  
D. A piece of metal becoming magnetic in the cold.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "about 37 °C ... invisible water vapour ... true in July as in January"  
【解析】基础事实直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段"pocket becomes small and tight ... runs out of room"  
【解析】温度与气体含水量的比喻直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "suddenly condenses ... tiny water droplets ... 0.01 millimetre"  
【解析】凝结过程直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "scattered equally at all wavelengths ... mixture as white"  
【解析】白色成因。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（否定）  
【定位】第⑦段三例 "iced orange juice / bus window / bathroom mirror"  
【解析】A、B、C 均在文中；D 与磁性无关。故选 D。
