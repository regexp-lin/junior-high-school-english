# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Small Boy at the Bus Stop**

① It was a wet Thursday evening at 18:10. I had just finished my volunteer hour at the community library, and I was standing alone at Bus Stop No. 47, waiting for the last bus home. The November rain fell in a thin, patient way. Only two people shared the shelter with me: an old man reading a newspaper, and a **small boy**, maybe **six or seven years old**, in a bright yellow raincoat and a pair of muddy sports shoes. He was holding a blue schoolbag that looked too big for his small shoulders.

② He kept looking left, then right, then down at the pavement. Every time a bus came, he took one small step forward, looked carefully at the number, and then stepped back. After the fourth bus left without him, I saw that his **lower lip was trembling**. I had seen that face before — the face of a child trying very hard not to cry in public.

③ I walked a few steps closer but did not stand too close. "Hi," I said softly. "Are you waiting for someone?" He looked up at me. His eyes were wet, but not yet leaking. "I should take the 47 bus home," he whispered, "but none of them say 'Sunshine Street' inside the window. I don't know… **if I got on wrong, my mother would not know where I went.**" He said this like a small, careful adult.

④ I knelt down so we were eye-to-eye and smiled as gently as I could. "My name is Jiaying," I said. "I go to Lihua Middle School. I am in Grade 8. Can you tell me your name and the name of your school?" He paused. "Zhou Chen," he said finally. "First-year student, Sunshine Primary School, Class 1(4). My mother's phone is 138… 138…" He stopped. "I forget the rest." He looked down. The patient rain outside the shelter did not care.

⑤ I reached into my coat pocket, took out my phone, and showed him a picture of my younger cousin — roughly his age — because a familiar small face always calms a worried one. "Look," I said. "He is in Grade 1 too. Every Thursday he comes home a little later." Zhou Chen's mouth made the smallest smile. Then I said, calmly, "I will help. But first, I need you to breathe with me. In… two… three. Out… two… three." We did it three times. His trembling lip slowly stopped.

⑥ Together we walked to the small shop at the corner of Bus Stop No. 47. The aunty who runs the shop knew me from the library. I asked her if Zhou Chen could **use her shop phone**. She waved her hand kindly: "Of course. Call Mama." Zhou Chen dialled two numbers he remembered, stopped, shook his head, and dialled again. The second call connected. A small, anxious voice filled the shop. I heard, through the line: "**Baby! Baby, are you safe?**" He nodded seven times before he remembered to say "yes" out loud.

⑦ Twenty minutes later, a woman in a grey raincoat ran into the shop. Zhou Chen did not say a word. He simply pushed his small face into his mother's coat. The aunty and I looked at each other and smiled. The mother turned to me, breathing fast: "He took the wrong bus from school. He got off here to find the right one, and… and…" Her voice broke. I shook my head, "He did it right," I said. "He stayed at the stop. He asked. He is a brave boy." The mother pressed her face against the top of her son's small head for a long second.

⑧ On the last bus home that evening, I sat by the window and watched the orange street lamps go past, each one a warm round dot in the rain. I was not a hero. I only had three small pieces of luck: a pocket that had a phone, a library where I had learned the word *"breathe"*, and an aunty who let a child use her shop phone for free. Still, I realised something: **in a large city full of fast cars and busy adults, a middle-school student can sometimes be the smallest, kindest adult a small child will meet on a rainy evening.** When I was halfway home, Zhou Chen sent me a picture through his mother's phone: a yellow raincoat with a red heart drawn on the back, next to one word in a child's big letters — *"friend"*.

---

**注释：** shelter 雨棚；tremble 颤抖；kneel 跪下；dial 拨号；raincoat 雨衣

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did the writer first notice that the small boy was in trouble?**

A. He spoke loudly about his school.  
B. He was kept looking left, then right, then down, and after the fourth bus left without him, his lower lip was trembling.  
C. He fell asleep on the bench.  
D. He lost his schoolbag.

**2. Why did Zhou Chen NOT get on any of the buses that came first?**

A. Because he had no money.  
B. Because none of the buses said "Sunshine Street" inside the window, and he was afraid that if he got on wrong, his mother would not know where he went.  
C. Because the buses were too full.  
D. Because it was raining too hard.

**3. Why did the writer show Zhou Chen a picture of her younger cousin?**

A. Because she had nothing else in her pocket.  
B. Because she wanted to test his memory.  
C. Because a familiar small face always calms a worried one.  
D. Because she wanted him to remember her cousin's name.

**4. How did Zhou Chen finally reach his mother?**

A. He shouted in the shelter.  
B. The writer ran two miles to find his mother.  
C. The writer and Zhou Chen walked to the corner shop, and the kind aunty let him use the shop phone for free.  
D. A police officer called his mother.

**5. What is the main idea of the last paragraph?**

A. Only adults can help lost children.  
B. In a large city full of fast cars and busy adults, a middle-school student can sometimes be the smallest, kindest adult a small child will meet on a rainy evening.  
C. Students should not talk to children.  
D. The writer is a great hero of the city.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "four buses ... lower lip was trembling"  
【解析】观察细节直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "none of them say 'Sunshine Street' ... if I got on wrong, my mother would not know"  
【解析】小心的安全意识直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "a familiar small face always calms a worried one"  
【解析】安抚策略直引。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "walked to the small shop ... use her shop phone ... waved her hand kindly"  
【解析】路径直引。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧段  
【解析】少年也能成为最温柔的"小大人"。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。
