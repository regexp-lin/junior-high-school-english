# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Teenagers Try One Screen-Free Day Each Week?**

① If you walk into a middle school classroom in the middle of an exam week, you will see most students glued to a screen — reviewing notes on a phone, chatting with friends on a study group, or watching a short video between two questions. On weekends, the screen time can grow to six hours or more. Some doctors and teachers now suggest a small but bold idea: **one screen-free day each week** for teenagers, usually a weekend day. Is this old-sounding rule useful today? I believe it is.

② First, a screen-free day lets the **eyes rest**. Every school day already asks young eyes to focus on books, boards and screens for about 10 hours. Without a break, students may feel headaches, dry eyes and slower reading speed. One whole day away from phones, tablets and televisions acts like a quiet holiday for the eyes. Students who try it often say their eyes feel lighter and brighter the next Monday.

③ Second, a screen-free day gives the **brain room to be bored** — and boredom is surprisingly useful. When we are bored, the brain begins to wander, make connections, and ask creative questions. Many writers and scientists say their best ideas come during long walks, slow breakfasts, or rainy afternoons by a window. Every tap we make in a busy app robs a few of those quiet moments. A screen-free day gives them back.

④ Third, such a day opens space for **real face-to-face relationships**. Families rediscover simple activities: cooking together, playing Chinese chess, going for a walk, visiting grandparents. Friends call each other and actually meet, instead of only sending a heart sticker through an app. These real-life meetings, small as they look, carry the heaviest happiness.

⑤ Of course, screens are not our enemy. Our textbooks are now on tablets; teachers send notices online; students search for interesting articles on the internet. A week without any screen would be unrealistic, and even unfair, for a modern teenager. That is why I do not ask for a "no-screen life". I only ask for **one day** — a gentle pause, not a war against technology.

⑥ Making that day work is easier with simple rules. Choose a fixed day (say, Saturday). Tell your close friends in advance so no one is worried when you do not reply. Place your phone in a drawer from morning to evening. Plan one indoor activity and one outdoor activity. Keep a short paper diary of how your body and mind felt. After four Saturdays, read the diary again. I believe most teenagers will find that the screen-free day is not something they lose, but something they quietly receive.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main suggestion?**

A. Teenagers should never use any screens.  
B. Teenagers should try one screen-free day each week.  
C. Teenagers should only watch short videos.  
D. Teenagers should use screens for 12 hours a day.

**2. Why does the writer think a screen-free day lets eyes rest?**

A. Because screens are always very cold.  
B. Because school days already ask young eyes to focus on books, boards and screens for many hours.  
C. Because eyes hate colour.  
D. Because eyes only like the dark.

**3. According to paragraph ③, why is some boredom actually useful?**

A. It helps students copy answers from classmates.  
B. The brain wanders, makes connections and asks creative questions.  
C. It stops the heart from working.  
D. It makes teenagers grow taller.

**4. How does the writer answer people who think a "no-screen life" is unrealistic?**

A. He insists on a whole week without screens.  
B. He only asks for one day — a gentle pause, not a war against technology.  
C. He says screens should be destroyed.  
D. He says parents should hide all devices forever.

**5. Which is NOT a piece of simple advice given in the last paragraph?**

A. Choose a fixed day.  
B. Tell close friends in advance.  
C. Place your phone in a drawer from morning to evening.  
D. Never leave home during the screen-free day.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "one screen-free day each week for teenagers..."  
【解析】作者主张每周一天无屏幕。A/C/D 均不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "school days already ask young eyes to focus on books, boards and screens for about 10 hours."  
【解析】学校已让眼睛长时间工作。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "the brain begins to wander, make connections, and ask creative questions."  
【解析】无聊让大脑产生创造性联想。A/C/D 均与原文相反。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "I only ask for one day — a gentle pause, not a war against technology."  
【解析】作者只要求一天的温和停顿。A/C/D 均与原文相反。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段建议：选固定日、提前告诉朋友、手机入抽屉、室内外活动各一、写日记  
【解析】未提"不可出门"，属无中生有。A/B/C 均为文中建议。故选 D。
