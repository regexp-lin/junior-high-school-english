# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle School Students Do at Least One Hour of Housework Every Week?**

① In my school's new parent-teacher meeting, one small question caused a long and lively discussion: **should middle school students spend at least one hour a week on housework — washing dishes, sweeping floors, folding clothes or preparing simple meals?** Some parents said "yes"; others said "no, my child is too busy." I believe the answer should be a clear "yes".

② First, housework is a kind of **body exercise**. An hour of sweeping a floor, wiping tables and washing bowls is equal to a gentle walk in the park. Our hearts beat a little faster, our arms move in different directions, and our eyes look away from screens. For a middle school student who already sits for eight hours at a desk, this kind of moving is quietly healthy.

③ Second, housework teaches us **how effort and result are linked**. In homework, the result is often a grade, and we may wait two weeks to see the mark. In housework, the result is **here and now**: a dirty plate becomes shiny; a messy bed becomes flat; an empty bucket of water turns into clean floor. This direct link is an important life lesson. It reminds us that, in most of life, reward comes **after** work, not before.

④ Third, housework gently reshapes our sense of **family**. In many homes, mothers still do about 70 per cent of housework. Children grow up thinking that a clean kitchen just "happens". When a teenager mops a floor on a rainy Sunday, he or she begins to understand the invisible labour that has been supporting the family for years. A small "thank you, Mum" from someone who has just done the dishes himself carries much more meaning than one from someone who has never touched a tap.

⑤ Some parents worry that housework will **steal study time**. At first this sounds reasonable, but numbers tell a different story. One hour of housework per week is less than **15 minutes per day** on average — less than the time many students already spend on short videos. A ten-minute tidy-up between two study sessions also gives the brain a useful break. Research shows that such physical pauses can raise later concentration in math or English.

⑥ Housework is not about saving parents a little work. It is about helping children grow into kind, responsible adults who know how to run their own small homes one day. I therefore hope that every middle school will quietly encourage its students — and that every parent will resist the temptation to do "everything for the child". One broom in a teenager's hand does more teaching than many chapters of a textbook.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main opinion of the writer?**

A. Middle school students should not do any housework.  
B. Middle school students should do at least one hour of housework every week.  
C. Housework is only for adults.  
D. Parents should do all housework alone.

**2. How, according to paragraph ②, is housework similar to a gentle walk in the park?**

A. Both are done outside.  
B. Both make the heart beat a little faster, move the arms and give the eyes a break from screens.  
C. Both are free of all physical effort.  
D. Both must be done with music.

**3. Why does the writer say housework teaches us about "effort and result"?**

A. Because housework results come much later than exam results.  
B. Because the result — a shiny plate, a flat bed, a clean floor — appears right after the work is done.  
C. Because housework is always easy.  
D. Because housework is always painful.

**4. How does housework change a teenager's sense of family, according to paragraph ④?**

A. It makes teenagers angry with their mothers.  
B. It helps teenagers see the invisible labour that has been supporting the family for years, and makes their "thank you" more meaningful.  
C. It stops teenagers from talking to their mothers.  
D. It causes family members to move out.

**5. How does the writer answer parents who worry about "stealing study time"?**

A. One hour a week is less than 15 minutes a day, and short physical pauses can raise later concentration.  
B. Studies are not important at all.  
C. Children should study 24 hours a day.  
D. Parents should hire other people to help their children study.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末及全文  
【解析】作者主张每周至少一小时。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段  
【解析】三点共同：心跳、手臂活动、屏幕休息。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "the result is here and now..."  
【解析】即时可见结果。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "he or she begins to understand the invisible labour..."  
【解析】看见隐形付出。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段  
【解析】时间开销少且短暂休息有助专注。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。
