# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle Schools Replace Paper Textbooks with Tablets?**

① Every September, our school gives each student about **twelve paper textbooks**, weighing more than five kilograms in total. By October, our school bags already look tired, and so do our shoulders. Every year, someone asks the same question: "**Can we just use a tablet instead?**" The idea sounds modern, clean, and light. But is a tablet really ready to fully **replace** paper textbooks in middle school? I believe the answer, for now, is **not yet** — even though tablets should have a larger place in our learning.

② Those who support the change list real advantages. First, **weight**. A tablet of 500 grams can hold every textbook from Grade 7 to Grade 9, plus hundreds of extra books. Second, **updates**. A mistake on a paper page stays there for five years; a mistake on a tablet page can be corrected overnight. Third, **multi-media**. A geography lesson about rivers becomes more alive with a short video, and an English lesson with real-voice recordings is worth more than a silent black-and-white page. Fourth, **paper saving**. Our country prints about **630 million** school textbooks every year; if half became digital, many trees would stay standing.

③ All these arguments sound strong. But each of them, on a closer look, has a shadow. First, studies from several universities (Peking University, Tsinghua, and others between 2021 and 2024) found that students who **read long texts on a tablet** usually remember **less** and feel **more tired** than students reading the same text on paper. The eye, they say, enjoys a matt paper page for **long reading**, and a bright screen for **quick checking** — two different jobs. Second, a tablet **can be corrected quickly**, but it can also be **damaged**, **stolen**, or **broken** quickly. A dropped tablet during a school running day may cost 2,000 yuan; a dropped textbook costs a few yuan to replace. Third, **multi-media is not always helpful**: if every English text comes with a colourful animation, students may gradually lose the patience to sit with a long quiet page. Fourth, paper-saving is a real good, but tablets have their own cost to the earth — rare metals, large amounts of electricity, short hardware lives of 3–4 years.

④ There are also two worries most supporters forget. First, **eye health**. Our school already warns that many students stay on screens for four to six hours a day outside class. If textbooks, too, move to screens, our eyes may add another five hours of screen time a day during school, reaching dangerous levels before Grade 9. Second, **fair access**. Paper textbooks cost the same for everyone and never run out of battery. A tablet, however, needs a home with strong Wi-Fi, reliable electricity, and parents who can afford a replacement when it breaks. A 100% tablet school could quietly widen the gap between classmates from different families.

⑤ What, then, is a better plan? A **mixed system** makes more sense. Keep paper textbooks for main subjects — Chinese, Maths, English, History — where long reading, slow thinking, and handwritten notes are most useful. Move some extra materials — extra listening, video experiments, English audio, and quick dictionaries — onto **a shared classroom tablet** used on fixed days, not every day. This way we gain the speed and sound of the screen, without losing the depth and rest of the page.

⑥ Paper textbooks are not perfect; heavy bags are a real problem, and schools should find lighter, smarter editions. But the old page still gives us something the new screen cannot: a **quiet, battery-free, patient** partner who lets the reader stop, think, underline with a pencil, and fall asleep on top of it on a Sunday afternoon. For now, that partner is still worth keeping.

---

**注释：** tablet 平板电脑；update 更新；matt 亚光的；rare metal 稀有金属；fair access 公平获取

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Paper textbooks should be banned.  
B. Tablets should be given to every student tomorrow.  
C. Tablets are not yet ready to fully replace paper textbooks in middle school, but they should have a larger place in learning.  
D. Schools should stop teaching from any book.

**2. According to Paragraph ②, which is one real advantage of tablets over paper textbooks?**

A. Tablets never break.  
B. A 500-gram tablet can hold all textbooks from Grade 7 to Grade 9 plus hundreds of extra books.  
C. Tablets are cheaper than one pencil.  
D. Tablets look prettier.

**3. What did university studies between 2021 and 2024 find?**

A. Students prefer tablets for every kind of reading.  
B. Students who read long texts on tablets usually remember less and feel more tired than those reading the same text on paper.  
C. Tablets make students' eyes stronger.  
D. Paper is becoming unsafe.

**4. Why does the writer worry about "fair access"?**

A. Paper is too cheap.  
B. Tablets need Wi-Fi, electricity and families who can afford replacements, so a 100% tablet school could widen the gap between students from different families.  
C. Schools are too rich.  
D. Tablets should be free.

**5. What "mixed system" does the writer suggest in Paragraph ⑤?**

A. Paper for every subject, no tablets at all.  
B. Tablets for every subject, no paper at all.  
C. Keep paper textbooks for main subjects (Chinese, Maths, English, History), and use a shared classroom tablet for extra listening, video experiments, audio, and quick dictionaries on fixed days.  
D. Give each student a tablet only at home.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段立论 "not yet ... tablets should have a larger place"  
【解析】作者有条件反对全面替代。A、B、D 均极端或偏离。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "500-gram tablet can hold every textbook ... plus hundreds of extra books"  
【解析】容量直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "remember less and feel more tired than students reading the same text on paper"  
【解析】直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "Wi-Fi ... reliable electricity ... parents who can afford a replacement ... widen the gap"  
【解析】不公平源于家庭差异。A、C、D 偏离。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段混合方案详述  
【解析】主科用纸、辅助材料用共享平板。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。
