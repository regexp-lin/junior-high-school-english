# 阅读理解（说明文） **The Journey of a Pencil: From a Forest in Yunnan to Your Desk**

① You may think a **pencil** is one of the simplest things in the world. Look more carefully, and you will find that the short yellow stick on your desk has travelled **thousands of kilometres** and passed through more than ten pairs of hands before it could help you write a single letter.

② A good pencil starts in a **forest**. Most school pencils in China are made from the wood of a soft, straight tree called **Yunnan cedar** (云南红杉) or a similar kind grown in Sichuan. These trees are cut down only when they are **twenty to thirty years old**, not young ones, because younger wood is too soft to hold a point. The trunks are then sent to a timber yard, cut into short blocks of about 18 centimetres, and **left to dry for three to four months**. If the wood is used when it is still wet, the finished pencil will bend and crack in your hand.

③ The next stop is a **pencil factory**, usually in Hebei or Zhejiang. Here, dried wood blocks are sliced into thin boards called "**slats**", each as wide as a pencil and as long as your finger. Eight narrow grooves are cut across each slat, like tiny railway tracks. Meanwhile, in another workshop, the **lead** of the pencil is being made — though, in fact, a pencil lead has no lead (the metal) at all. It is a mix of powdered **graphite** (石墨) from mines in Heilongjiang and fine **clay**, pressed into long thin strings, then baked in a hot oven. The more graphite, the softer and darker the line; the more clay, the harder and lighter.

④ The two halves now meet. Each lead string is laid into a groove of one slat, covered with a second slat carrying glue, and pressed together so tightly that, from the outside, the wood looks like one piece. A spinning knife then shapes the whole block into eight long pencils at once — six-sided or round, depending on the model. The pencil is not yet finished; it still looks pale and rough.

⑤ Next comes **paint**. The pencils are dipped in yellow or dark green lacquer (漆) many times. Between each dip, they travel slowly on a conveyor belt for about an hour to dry. Then the **brand name**, **hardness level** (such as 2B, HB, 4H), and a small decoration are pressed on with a hot stamp. At the far end of the line, a small machine pushes a pink **rubber** into a ring of metal and fixes it to one end of each pencil. At the other end, another machine uses a fast-spinning blade to **sharpen** the point.

⑥ Before leaving the factory, pencils take one last test. A quality worker drops a sample **from about one metre high**; if the lead inside has cracked during production, it will break and the pencil is rejected. Ten pencils from every batch are also tested on paper to check that the **darkness** (how dark the line is) is even. Only after these tests are the pencils packed into small cardboard boxes, each box holding ten or twelve, and sent by truck to bookstores and supermarkets across the country.

⑦ One pencil can draw a line about **55 kilometres long** before it is used up — roughly the distance from our school gate to the next town. It can pass through the hands of a forester in Yunnan, a timber worker, a factory worker in Hebei, a truck driver, a shop assistant, and finally you, a Grade 8 student. **The simple pencil in your pencil case is not simple at all.** It is a small carrier of many jobs, many hours, and many careful hands — ready to write whatever thought your mind sends down the page.

**注释：** cedar 雪松；timber 木材；slat 薄木片；graphite 石墨；lacquer 漆；hardness 硬度

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. According to Paragraph ②, why are very young trees NOT used for pencils?**

A. They are too rare.  
B. The wood is too soft to hold a sharpened point.  
C. They smell bad.  
D. They are too expensive.

**2. What does a pencil's "lead" really contain?**

A. The metal lead.  
B. A mix of powdered graphite and fine clay, pressed and baked.  
C. Dried ink.  
D. Black paint only.

**3. What decides whether the pencil's line is darker or lighter?**

A. The colour of the outside paint.  
B. The length of the pencil.  
C. The mix of graphite and clay: more graphite means softer and darker; more clay means harder and lighter.  
D. The kind of tree used.

**4. What is done to make sure only good pencils leave the factory?**

A. Nothing; all pencils are packed directly.  
B. A sample is dropped from about one metre high to check that the lead inside has not cracked; ten pencils from every batch are also tested on paper for even darkness.  
C. Every single pencil is tested by being broken in half.  
D. Only the teacher checks them.

**5. What is the main message of the passage?**

A. A pencil is a very simple, small object.  
B. Pencils should be replaced by pens.  
C. A pencil may look simple, but it is the result of many kinds of work, long travel, and careful hands — it can draw a line about 55 km long before it is used up.  
D. Chinese pencils are the best in the world.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "younger wood is too soft to hold a point"  
【解析】直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "a mix of powdered graphite ... and fine clay ... baked"  
【解析】直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "more graphite, softer and darker ... more clay, harder and lighter"  
【解析】比例决定深浅。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "dropped ... about one metre high ... Ten pencils from every batch are also tested on paper"  
【解析】两项测试。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑦段 "simple pencil ... is not simple at all ... small carrier of many jobs ... 55 km long"  
【解析】综合概括。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。
