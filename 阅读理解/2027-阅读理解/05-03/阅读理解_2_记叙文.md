# 阅读理解（记叙文） **A Windy Afternoon with Grandpa's Old Kite**

① It was the first Saturday of May, and the wind outside our window would not keep still. It shook the windows, pushed the curtains, and sang a soft whistle under the door. Grandpa looked at me with bright eyes and said, "Perfect day. Get your shoes." From a dusty corner of his cupboard, he brought out a very old bamboo kite, shaped like a blue butterfly. "This one is older than your mother," he said proudly.

② We walked to the small green hill behind our building. On the way, Grandpa taught me three rules: never run with your eyes on the kite only; never let other kite lines touch yours; and never pull too hard at the beginning. I nodded, but in my heart I just wanted the butterfly to fly high, right now.

③ At the hill, Grandpa held the line, and I held the kite. "Run!" he shouted. I ran, and when I felt a strong pull, I let go. The blue butterfly jumped, fell, jumped again, and at last climbed into the sky. I **shouted for joy**, ran backwards, and almost stepped on a small dog. Grandpa laughed so much he had to sit down on the grass.

④ The wind grew stronger. The kite rose higher and higher, until it was a tiny blue dot against the white clouds. Then, without warning, a sudden hard wind pulled the line to one side. The line crossed a tall tree near the hill, and the kite got **stuck** in the top branches. My heart sank. "I'm sorry, Grandpa!" I said. "I pulled too hard."

⑤ Grandpa did not shout. He just walked slowly to the tree. "A kite is a teacher," he said quietly. "It only listens if you listen to the wind first." With a long bamboo stick, he reached up and shook the branches. It took almost twenty minutes. Leaves fell on our heads. Finally, the blue butterfly floated down into my hands. One wing had a small tear, but it was still alive.

⑥ On the way home, Grandpa put his warm hand on my shoulder. "Next Saturday, same hill, same kite," he said. "With a little patience, even an old butterfly can fly again." I held the kite close to my coat and smiled. I understood that the wind, the kite, the tree, and Grandpa were all teaching me the same thing: **listen first, then act**. And that afternoon, with a torn wing and a tired grandfather, suddenly felt like one of the happiest days of my spring.

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What did Grandpa take out of his cupboard?**

A. A new plastic rocket.  
B. An old bamboo kite shaped like a blue butterfly.  
C. A pair of running shoes.  
D. A small toy dog.

**2. Which rule did Grandpa NOT teach the writer on the way to the hill?**

A. Never run with your eyes on the kite only.  
B. Never let other kite lines touch yours.  
C. Never pull too hard at the beginning.  
D. Always shout loudly while running.

**3. Why did the kite get stuck in the tree?**

A. A bird pulled it away.  
B. A sudden hard wind pulled the line to one side.  
C. The writer cut the line on purpose.  
D. Grandpa let go of the line.

**4. What did Grandpa mean by "A kite is a teacher"?**

A. A kite can read books to children.  
B. We should listen to the wind and act with patience.  
C. A kite is better than a real teacher at school.  
D. Kites only fly in classrooms.

**5. How did the writer most probably feel at the end of the story?**

A. Angry at Grandpa.  
B. Afraid of the wind.  
C. Thankful and happy, with a new understanding.  
D. Bored and tired of kites.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末句 "he brought out a very old bamboo kite, shaped like a blue butterfly."  
【解析】原文明确：爷爷拿出的是一只蓝色蝴蝶形状的旧竹风筝。A 塑料火箭、C 跑鞋、D 玩具狗均与原文不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第2句 "never run with your eyes on the kite only; never let other kite lines touch yours; and never pull too hard at the beginning."  
【解析】爷爷只讲了三条规则，A/B/C 都在其中，D "跑动时大声喊叫"原文未提，属无中生有。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段第2-3句 "a sudden hard wind pulled the line to one side. The line crossed a tall tree..."  
【解析】突如其来的强风把线吹向一边，线缠上高树，风筝被卡住。A 有只鸟、C 故意剪线、D 爷爷松手均属无中生有。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段第2句 "A kite only listens if you listen to the wind first." 结合第⑥段"listen first, then act"  
【解析】爷爷借风筝说道理：先倾听风再行动，强调耐心与顺势，推断核心意思是"要听风而动，保持耐心"。A 把风筝当读书者、C 与学校老师比较、D 限于教室均不符语境。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】写作意图/情感态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "suddenly felt like one of the happiest days of my spring"  
【解析】文末"最开心的一天之一"与对规则的领悟可知作者心情是感激、开心并有所领悟。A 生气、B 害怕、D 厌倦均与"happiest"相反。故选 C。

**词汇注释（超纲词）**  
- **bamboo** [n.] 竹（子）  
- **whistle** [n./v.] 口哨声；吹口哨  
- **branch** [n.] 树枝  
- **patience** [n.] 耐心
