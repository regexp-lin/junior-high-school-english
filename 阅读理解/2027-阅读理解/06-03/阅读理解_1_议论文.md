# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Classrooms Be Decorated by Students Themselves?**

① In many Chinese middle schools, classroom walls look almost the same: white paint, one dark-green slogan, a calendar, a fire-safety poster. At the start of our new term, however, some schools have begun a small, quiet experiment: the **students themselves** design and decorate most of the back wall of the classroom, under the guidance of the head teacher. Is this a good idea? I strongly believe it is.

② First, a student-decorated classroom becomes **our classroom**. When we paint a group tree with names in the shape of leaves, add a corner for "Favourite Book of the Month", or hang our own sketches, the room stops being just a cold public space. It becomes a warm map of who we are this year. Students who feel that the room is theirs take better care of it — fewer broken chairs, fewer writings on desks.

③ Second, decorating together is a **lesson in cooperation** that no textbook can teach. Not everyone likes the same colour; not everyone agrees that a poster about football is more important than one about books. To finish one wall, students must listen to each other, give up some ideas, and vote for the best plan. These are small skills, but they are the same skills adults use every day in offices and hospitals.

④ Third, the process brings **quiet surprises**. The noisy boy who never speaks up in English lessons turns out to draw beautiful cartoons. The shy girl with small glasses writes the best poems for the wall. When students decorate the classroom, each of them gets a short chance to stand on a stage they did not expect. Teachers can also discover hidden talents that a normal test would never show.

⑤ Some people worry that student decorations will make the classroom **look messy** or **distract** students from study. This worry is fair, but it can be managed. A good rule is "**three zones, one quiet week**": the back wall belongs to students, the side walls stay plain and calm, and every term begins with one quiet week of no new posters. In this way, the classroom keeps a balance between self-expression and focus.

⑥ A classroom is not only a place where lessons happen. It is a place where young lives grow for 220 days every year. Giving students the chance to decorate even a small part of that place is a small gift of **respect**. In return, they will often give back more care, more creativity and more attention. A few hand-made posters may be the most valuable lesson on the wall.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's opinion?**

A. Classrooms should look exactly the same everywhere.  
B. Students themselves should design and decorate part of the classroom, under a teacher's guidance.  
C. Classrooms should have no decorations at all.  
D. Only teachers should decorate classrooms.

**2. According to paragraph ②, what happens when students decorate their own classroom?**

A. They leave the classroom empty.  
B. The room becomes a warm map of who they are that year, and students take better care of it.  
C. They start to break more chairs.  
D. They fight every day.

**3. Why is decorating together called "a lesson in cooperation"?**

A. Students must accept only the teacher's plan without any discussion.  
B. Students must listen to each other, give up some ideas and vote for the best plan.  
C. Students must paint the same colour as their parents choose.  
D. Students must finish the wall alone.

**4. What "quiet surprises" does the writer talk about in paragraph ④?**

A. Some students are found to have hidden talents, such as drawing cartoons or writing poems.  
B. Teachers leave their jobs.  
C. The lights suddenly go out.  
D. Parents come to clean the wall.

**5. What rule does the writer suggest for balance between decoration and focus?**

A. No decoration at all.  
B. "Three zones, one quiet week" — back wall for students, side walls plain, and one quiet week at the start of each term without new posters.  
C. Decorate the whole classroom every week.  
D. Only boys may decorate.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末  
【解析】支持学生参与班级装饰。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "It becomes a warm map of who we are this year..."  
【解析】温暖地图。A/C/D 均与原文相反。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "listen to each other, give up some ideas, and vote for the best plan."  
【解析】合作需要倾听、妥协、投票。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "The noisy boy who never speaks up in English lessons turns out to draw beautiful cartoons..."  
【解析】隐藏才华。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "three zones, one quiet week"  
【解析】三区一静周。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
