# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Winter Mental Wellness Week 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Psychology Office + Form Teachers + Student Union**  
> **Winter Mental Wellness Week** — *"A Warm Week Before the Cold Exams"*  
> **Mon 13 – Fri 17 December 2027**

The last two weeks before final exams are **the heaviest two weeks of the term**, not only in homework but also in small, quiet worries. This winter, one week before exam day, our school is holding the first **Winter Mental Wellness Week**. The aim is simple: to **make it easy** for every student to talk, rest, slow down, and notice how she feels. No tests, no scores, no points. Just a few small warm doors opened each day.

---

## 🕒 The Day Rhythm

| Daily | Time | Activity | Where |
|---|---|---|---|
| Every morning | 08:00 – 08:05 | **"Three-Minute Quiet Start"** — lights dim, one slow breath exercise | Form classroom |
| Every lunch | 12:25 – 13:00 | **Warm Drink Corner** (free ginger tea & warm water) | Library Floor 1 lobby |
| Every afternoon | 15:50 – 16:20 | **"Step-and-Talk Walk"** — voluntary 30-min circle around the courtyard | Main gate |
| Every evening | 17:00 – 17:45 | **"Quiet Listening Room"** — one-on-one booking with a counsellor | Room 110 |

---

## 📅 The Special Day-by-Day Theme

### Mon 13 — **"I Name It"** Day

- Students take **one small yellow card** at the classroom door.  
- Write **one feeling** you have felt this week (e.g. *"nervous"*, *"tired"*, *"okay, but a bit lonely"*, *"excited"*).  
- No name on the card. Drop it into the wooden box near the stairs.  
- At 16:00, the card wall opens in the corridor of Building B. You will see that **you are not alone**.

### Tue 14 — **"I Rest"** Day

- A short "Rest Hour" is added to the timetable, **16:00 – 17:00**: no new homework given today after lunch.  
- Students may nap on the green cushions in the library reading corner (maximum 30 min per student).  
- The school mini-app pauses all non-urgent homework messages in that hour.

### Wed 15 — **"I Move"** Day

- **Step-and-Talk Walk** is doubled to 45 minutes (15:30 – 16:15).  
- Students walk in pairs or small groups of three; speak softly about **anything except exams**.  
- A warm lemon tea is waiting at the library door afterwards.

### Thu 16 — **"I Write"** Day

- During the final 10 minutes of each subject class, the teacher offers a **soft writing prompt** — for example: *"Write one small worry. Tear up the paper before you go home."*  
- No writing is collected, read or marked. The act of writing is the whole point.

### Fri 17 — **"I Give"** Day

- At 15:00, students sit in class circles and each person, in turn, gives **one small honest thank-you** to another classmate (e.g. *"Thank you for lending me your eraser on Monday"*).  
- This takes about 20 minutes. No thank-you must be big or dramatic. A small one is best.

---

## 🚪 Private Support All Week

- **Quiet Listening Room (Room 110)** — book a 25-minute one-to-one session with Ms Wang (our school counsellor) on the school mini-app. Sessions are fully private.  
- **Form-Teacher Door-Open Hour** — every form teacher will be in the form classroom 17:00 – 17:30 with the door **literally open**. Come with one question, one worry, or silence.  
- **Anonymous "Green Letter" box** — near Room 110. Write a letter, seal it, drop it in. Ms Wang replies within two school days on the anonymous answer-board outside Room 110.

---

## 🚫 Three Things We Will NOT Do This Week

1. **No public ranking, comparison or "best-student" wall** in the classrooms or corridors.  
2. **No extra practice papers** assigned by any subject teacher during the Rest Hour on Tuesday.  
3. **No "cheer-up" slogans** that require students to say they are happy. If today is not a happy day, the week has no rule asking you to pretend.

---

## 📝 Small Rules of Kindness

- If a classmate is having a hard week, **ask before you hug**. A warm question is often kinder than a sudden touch.  
- If you see a classmate crying quietly, **do not take a photo**; pass a paper tissue and sit nearby quietly, or find a teacher.  
- Phones may not be used to photograph the yellow feeling cards. The wall is safe because it is **anonymous**. A single photo can end that.
