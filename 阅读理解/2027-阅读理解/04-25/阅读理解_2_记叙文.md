# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Learning to Ride a Unicycle**

① Last spring, my neighbour **Uncle Zhao** pushed open the door of our shared courtyard with a shining silver **unicycle** on his shoulder. "A student moved out and gave me this," he said, smiling. "Do you want to try, Wen?" I looked at the single black wheel and one little seat and almost laughed. How could anyone balance on that? But I nodded, because Uncle Zhao was a kind old gym teacher, and I was a curious thirteen-year-old boy.

② The first afternoon was terrible. I tried to sit on the seat and push off the wall, but after three seconds my legs flew into the air and my body fell hard onto the stone. My left knee had a long red scratch. "Three rules," Uncle Zhao said, laughing softly. "One, small falls do not count. Two, always wear your knee pads. Three, eyes **not** on the wheel, but on a tree far away." I did not understand rule three. How could I balance if I did not look at the wheel?

③ For the next two weeks, I practised every evening after my homework. Uncle Zhao stood beside me with a bamboo stick — not to hit me, but for me to **hold on** when my body shook. The first week I could only move two or three metres before falling. The second week, I could move ten metres if I held the stick. Friends from my class laughed when they passed by. "Wen, why not a normal bike?" one boy joked. I did not answer. Every fall, my teeth closed tighter.

④ On the eighth evening, something strange happened. I was trying again, holding Uncle Zhao's stick, when a small brown dog ran past us, chasing a paper ball. I looked up to follow the dog with my eyes, and for a long moment I **forgot** about the wheel under me. When I looked down again, I was already **five metres past** Uncle Zhao. He was standing still, his hand in the air, his mouth open. "That," he said slowly, "is rule three."

⑤ I practised harder. After another week I could ride around the whole courtyard, only touching the wall twice. My mother came out to watch. Even **Old Grandpa Sun**, who usually slept on the stone bench, woke up and clapped his thin hands. "Our Wen is flying," he said. "Tomorrow will you take me to buy vegetables with that thing?" Everyone laughed.

⑥ Before National Day, my school held a **Children's Festival**. Ms Liu, our class teacher, asked if anyone wanted to show a special skill. Two months before, I would never have raised my hand. But that day I did. At the show, I rode the unicycle slowly onto the stage, turned twice, and **did not fall**. I heard the children laughing and clapping; I could see my best friend Xiao Ming taking a video. But in my heart, the loudest voice was not from them. It was from the evening eight weeks ago, when I had first looked up at a passing dog.

⑦ The unicycle now rests in a corner of our courtyard, sometimes dusty, sometimes shining. Uncle Zhao has moved to a new city. But whenever I meet something new and hard — a long maths problem, a difficult English paragraph, a new dance step — I remember **rule three**: keep your eyes on something far away, not on the wheel. The small, shaking part of me is often still there, but the rest of me has learnt to **ride over it**.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Who gave the writer the unicycle?**

A. His grandfather.  
B. His best friend Xiao Ming.  
C. His neighbour, Uncle Zhao.  
D. His English teacher.

**2. What was "rule three"?**

A. Never wear knee pads.  
B. Always stare at the wheel.  
C. Look at a point far away, not at the wheel.  
D. Count falls out loud.

**3. What finally helped the writer balance for five metres?**

A. Uncle Zhao's loud shouting.  
B. A small brown dog that ran past and made the writer look up.  
C. A new seat cushion.  
D. A new kind of bike.

**4. How did the writer feel at the Children's Festival show?**

A. Sad and angry.  
B. Proud inside, remembering the evening with the dog.  
C. Too tired to speak.  
D. Afraid of the crowd and wanted to run away.

**5. What is the main lesson the writer took away?**

A. Unicycles are more fun than bikes.  
B. To master hard things, look at a far goal and do not keep staring at the small shaking part.  
C. Dogs should not be in courtyards.  
D. Older teachers are always right.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 Uncle Zhao  
【解析】邻居赵叔叔。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "eyes not on the wheel, but on a tree far away"  
【解析】看远处而非轮子。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 小狗  
【解析】小狗让他抬眼忘了轮子。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑥段内心描述  
【解析】心里自豪，回想那晚。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦段  
【解析】看远方，而非死盯困难处。故选 B。
