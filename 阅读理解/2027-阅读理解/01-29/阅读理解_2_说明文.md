# 阅读理解（说明文） **Rainbows: Why the Sky Can Wear a Colourful Belt**

① After a summer rain, sometimes we can see a beautiful colourful arch across the sky. It is a rainbow. Many young children think a rainbow is "drawn" in the sky by a kind god. In fact, a rainbow is made by three simple things: the sun, tiny water drops, and our own eyes.

② To see a rainbow, the sun must be behind us, and the small drops of water in the air must be in front of us. This is why we often see rainbows when the sun comes out after a rain shower, when we spray water in the garden, or even near a waterfall. Without water in the air, there can be no rainbow; without the sun, there can be no rainbow either.

③ The colours come from the sun. Sunlight looks white, but it is actually a mix of many different colours. When sunlight enters a small water drop, the drop bends the light. Different colours bend in slightly different ways, so they separate. Then the light bounces off the back of the drop and goes back out, reaching our eyes as a soft band of colour. Each drop sends only one colour to our eyes, but millions of drops work together to show the whole rainbow.

④ A rainbow usually has seven colours: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo and violet. Red is on the outside, and violet is on the inside of the arch. Sometimes a second, weaker rainbow appears above the main one. In the second rainbow, the colours are in the opposite order, with violet on the outside. Scientists call it a "double rainbow."

⑤ A rainbow is not a real object in the sky. You cannot fly a plane to the end of it or touch it with your hand. Still, rainbows remind us that wonderful things can come from simple ones — just sun, water and the angle of our eyes. The next time the rain stops and the sun smiles, look up; the sky may be wearing its colourful belt.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. According to the passage, what three things make a rainbow?**

A. Clouds, snow and wind.  
B. The sun, tiny water drops and our own eyes.  
C. Stars, moon and rain.  
D. Thunder, lightning and trees.

**2. Why can we see a rainbow near a waterfall?**

A. Because the water there is very warm.  
B. Because there are many small water drops in the air.  
C. Because the waterfall is close to the sea.  
D. Because fish in the waterfall shine like light.

**3. Why does sunlight show different colours inside a water drop?**

A. Because the drop makes the light stop completely.  
B. Because different colours bend in slightly different ways.  
C. Because the drop adds new colours to the light.  
D. Because the drop removes all white from the light.

**4. In a "double rainbow," what is on the OUTSIDE of the second rainbow?**

A. Red.  
B. Green.  
C. Violet.  
D. Yellow.

**5. What does the writer mainly want readers to feel in the last paragraph?**

A. Rainbows are real objects we can touch.  
B. Wonderful things can come from simple ones.  
C. Only scientists can enjoy rainbows.  
D. People should avoid going outside after rain.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末句 "the sun, tiny water drops, and our own eyes."  
【解析】三者形成彩虹。其他选项不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "even near a waterfall."  
【解析】瀑布附近空气中有大量小水滴。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Different colours bend in slightly different ways, so they separate."  
【解析】颜色因折射角度不同而分离。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "In the second rainbow, the colours are in the opposite order, with violet on the outside."  
【解析】副虹外侧为紫色。A、B、D 均不符。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨/写作意图题  
【定位】末段 "wonderful things can come from simple ones..."  
【解析】简单的事物也能带来美好。A 与"not a real object"相反；C 片面；D 无依据。故选 B。
