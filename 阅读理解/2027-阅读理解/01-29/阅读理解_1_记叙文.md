# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Grandma's Paper Scissors**

① Every winter holiday, I go back to my grandma's village. Her small house is old, but the walls are always covered with red paper cuttings: dancing children, running horses, blooming flowers and fat fish. My friends in the city may think these pictures belong to a museum, but in Grandma's home, they are part of daily life.

② Grandma has been a paper-cutting artist for more than fifty years. When I was five, she tried to teach me, but I could not even hold a pair of scissors well. This year, I finally asked her to teach me again. She laughed and said, "A fourteen-year-old pair of hands can do much more than a five-year-old pair." Then she showed me how to fold the red paper and how to move the small scissors slowly.

③ My first cut looked like a broken umbrella. I felt disappointed. Grandma did not say "good" or "bad." She only pointed to the sunlight on the window and said, "The sun does not light up a tree in one second. It warms the tree little by little." I tried again and again. After six or seven attempts, a simple paper flower appeared in my hand. It was not perfect, but it was mine.

④ That afternoon, Grandma told me the old stories behind many designs. A fat fish stands for "plenty of food." A carp jumping over a dragon gate means success in study. Two children holding hands mean friendship and good luck. Each small paper cutting carries a quiet wish. I realized that paper cutting is not only about pictures, but also about how people send warm thoughts to each other.

⑤ Before I went back to the city, Grandma gave me a special red envelope. Inside was not money, but a beautiful paper cutting: a girl holding a pen, with a small open book beside her. "For my student," she said. I now keep it on my desk. Every time I see it, I think of the warm winter afternoon, the smell of paper, and Grandma's slow, patient voice.

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where does the writer visit every winter holiday?**

A. A big city museum.  
B. Her grandma's village home.  
C. An art gallery in the capital.  
D. A school camp in the mountains.

**2. Why did Grandma start teaching the writer paper cutting again this year?**

A. Because the writer was going to take an exam.  
B. Because the writer was old enough to handle scissors well.  
C. Because Grandma was too tired to cut alone.  
D. Because it was a school project.

**3. What did Grandma say about the sun?**

A. The sun never warms the trees.  
B. The sun warms the tree little by little.  
C. The sun only shines in summer.  
D. The sun is too hot for the trees.

**4. What does a "carp jumping over a dragon gate" mean in the paper cutting?**

A. Plenty of food for the family.  
B. Friendship and good luck.  
C. Success in study.  
D. A quiet and peaceful life.

**5. What is the main message of the passage?**

A. Paper cutting carries warm wishes and patient learning.  
B. Children should stay in the village forever.  
C. Paper cutting can only be done by old people.  
D. Red paper is cheaper than other colours.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "Every winter holiday, I go back to my grandma's village."  
【解析】每个寒假都回奶奶村里。其他选项与原文不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第②段 "A fourteen-year-old pair of hands can do much more than a five-year-old pair."  
【解析】奶奶认为作者已长大，手更灵巧。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "It warms the tree little by little."  
【解析】阳光一点一点温暖大树。A、C、D 均不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "A carp jumping over a dragon gate means success in study."  
【解析】鲤鱼跳龙门寓意学业有成。A 为鱼代表；B 为两个孩子牵手；D 无依据。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文通过剪纸学习表达耐心与美好祝愿。  
【解析】A 概括文章主题。B、C、D 均片面或错误。故选 A。
