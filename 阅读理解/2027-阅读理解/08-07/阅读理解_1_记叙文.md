# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Rainy Evening at the Small Shop**

① It was a Tuesday evening in late October, and the rain had already been falling for three hours when I walked into Mr. Wang's small shop at the corner of Willow Road. I was twelve, and my mother had sent me out for a bottle of soy sauce and a packet of salt. Normally, I would hurry back, but the water outside the shop door was now almost as high as my shoes, and I had foolishly come without an umbrella. So I stood just inside the door, shaking water out of my hair and waiting.

② The shop was warm and smelled of dried fruit, soap, and old paper. Mr. Wang, a small, thin man about seventy years old, sat behind the counter on his wooden stool, reading a newspaper under a weak yellow lamp. He looked up, saw my wet head and my sad face, and said gently, "It will stop. It always stops. Come in, have a seat."

③ He pointed to an old plastic chair near the counter. I sat down and held my little paper bag on my lap. At that moment, the bell above the door rang, and an old woman came in. She was carrying a huge black umbrella, and she was soaked from the knees down. "Mr. Wang," she said, her voice shaking a little, "I am sorry, but I forgot my purse at home. I really need some medicine from the cabinet — just the small bottle, number three. My husband's heart is not good, and the rain is too heavy for me to go back."

④ I expected Mr. Wang to say no, or to ask her to come back tomorrow. Instead, he stood up at once, opened a small cabinet behind the counter, and took down a brown bottle. "Grandma Liu," he said, "take it. Come and pay whenever you can." He put the bottle in her hand and a second, smaller packet of biscuits on top of it. "Eat a little on the way. Your hands are shaking."

⑤ She tried to say thank you, but her lips were trembling too much. She only nodded, bowed her head twice, and hurried out, holding the bottle carefully under her coat.

⑥ I was amazed. When the bell rang quiet again, I looked at Mr. Wang and said, "Uncle, what if she forgets? What if she never pays?" I was not a rude child; I just could not understand. My own weekly pocket money was ten yuan, and the bottle had a price of more than thirty.

⑦ Mr. Wang smiled and sat back on his stool. "Little friend," he said, "this shop has been open for forty-one years. In those years, three customers have truly forgotten me. Three, in forty-one years. Most people remember. And even if she forgets, I will sleep well tonight. If I do not give the medicine, maybe tonight I will not sleep at all. Do you understand?"

⑧ I did not, completely. I said, "But it's a lot of money." He laughed, a small, soft laugh. "Money comes back in many ways. If I treat neighbours like family, they tell other neighbours, and the shop stays alive. If I treat them like thieves, the shop closes, and I sit alone at home, watching TV and feeling angry. I am seventy, and I prefer to have people around me."

⑨ The rain slowly grew thinner. I stood up, thanked him, and walked out into the quiet wet street. At home, I gave my mother the soy sauce and the salt, and then I stood at the window, thinking. I had learned maths and English that year, but nobody in school had taught me the lesson I learned that evening: **sometimes the best business is a little trust**.

⑩ Three weeks later, I passed the shop again. Through the window I saw Grandma Liu at the counter, handing Mr. Wang a red envelope. She was smiling and talking, waving a finger in the air. Mr. Wang was laughing. He looked up, saw me through the glass, and waved. I waved back — not to say hello, really, but to say thank you for something he did not know he had taught me.

⑪ Many years have passed. Mr. Wang's shop has closed; a coffee house stands there now. But every time I am about to judge someone too quickly — in school, at work, on the street — I still hear his soft laugh and his gentle sentence: "*Most people remember*." And, most days, he is right.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer stay in Mr. Wang's shop that evening?**

A. Because his mother had forgotten to pay the bill.  
B. Because he had no umbrella, and the rain outside was very heavy.  
C. Because Mr. Wang asked him to work in the shop.  
D. Because he wanted to read the newspaper with Mr. Wang.

**2. Why did the old woman come into the shop?**

A. She came to buy some fresh fruit.  
B. She came to complain about the shop.  
C. She needed medicine for her husband but had forgotten her purse at home.  
D. She came to shelter from the rain only.

**3. What did Mr. Wang do when the old woman said she had no money?**

A. He asked her to come back the next day.  
B. He gave her the medicine and a packet of biscuits, and told her to pay whenever she could.  
C. He gave her the medicine but kept her umbrella as a deposit.  
D. He refused her and asked her to leave.

**4. According to Mr. Wang, why is it a good business to trust neighbours?**

A. Because the government pays him for trusting people.  
B. Because most people remember and pay; trust keeps the shop full and his heart calm.  
C. Because his medicines have no cost for him.  
D. Because the police watch every customer.

**5. What is the main lesson the writer learned that evening?**

A. Rainy evenings are the best time to do business.  
B. Sometimes the best business, and the best way to live, is a little trust in other people.  
C. Old people should always be given free gifts.  
D. Small shops will always be richer than big ones.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末  
【解析】没带伞+雨大。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段  
【解析】给丈夫买药但忘带钱包。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段  
【解析】给药+饼干并说"随时来付"。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑦⑧段  
【解析】大多数人记得；信任让店活、心安。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑨段末句 & 第⑪段  
【解析】最好的生意/活法是对他人多一份信任。A/C/D 均偏。故选 B。
