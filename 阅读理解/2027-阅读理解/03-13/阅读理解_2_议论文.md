# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Students Choose Their Own Seats in Class?**

① Every new term, the same small moment happens in millions of Chinese classrooms: the teacher walks in with a seating plan, and students quietly take the seats chosen for them. Some boys wish they could sit beside a best friend. Some tall girls ask to sit in the front so they can see the blackboard better. A question is now growing louder in parent-teacher meetings: **should middle-school students be allowed to choose their own seats?**

② Those who say yes talk first about **comfort**. A student who sits beside a trusted friend usually feels calm, and a calm student learns more easily. Teachers often say the brain turns on only when the heart feels safe. Choosing a seat, supporters argue, is a small way to let students feel **at home** in the classroom, not like passengers who must sit where the bus driver says.

③ A second reason is **responsibility**. Middle-school students are growing fast. If a fourteen-year-old cannot even decide where to sit for a lesson, when will she learn to decide bigger things, like how to plan a weekend, or how to reply to a new friend online? Free seat choice, supporters say, is a small practice for the much bigger choices that wait outside the school gate.

④ However, teachers remind us of another side. If everyone chooses freely, popular students become the centre of a big circle, while shy, quiet or new students often find an **empty lonely corner**. A class, they say, is not a café. A classroom should help every student meet **different** people, not only the ones they already like. Sitting beside someone new, even for two weeks, can start a surprising new friendship.

⑤ Parents add a practical worry. Close friends sitting together often talk about last night's TV show, or pass little notes. Homework quality can quickly drop, and the teacher must spend more energy on quiet-please reminders than on lessons. "My son's marks fell ten points when he sat with his best friend," one mother said in a WeChat group. "His marks rose again when they were moved apart."

⑥ So, what is a fair answer? In my view, neither pure **free choice** nor full **teacher control** works best. A healthy way is this: the teacher decides about half of the seats (to balance eyes, heights and learning needs), and lets students choose the other half, **changing every month**. Once a term, the teacher and the class can talk together about what has worked. A classroom built by **both sides** feels like a home, and also trains us to live with new faces — which is what we will have to do all our lives.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句问题 + 全文  
【解析】全文讨论"中学生是否可以自选座位"。其他三项偏题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "feels calm... a calm student learns more easily"  
【解析】支持者先从"舒适"角度出发。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "popular students become the centre of a big circle, while shy, quiet or new students often find an empty lonely corner"  
【解析】担心受欢迎的学生聚拢，内向新学生被边缘化。B、C、D 无依据。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "My son's marks fell ten points when he sat with his best friend..."  
【解析】该故事说明好友坐一起可能导致分心、成绩下滑。B、C、D 均偏题。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "teacher decides about half of the seats... lets students choose the other half, changing every month"  
【解析】作者主张师生共同决定、每月调整。A、B、D 均为极端或无依据。故选 C。
