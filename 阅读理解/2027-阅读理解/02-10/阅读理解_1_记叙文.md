# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Planting a Tree with Dad**

① Dad and I have never talked much. He is a quiet man who drives a long-distance truck, and he is often away for weeks. When he comes home, he smells of dust and gasoline, sits on the small stool, and watches TV without many words. I love him, of course, but I often feel there is a thick glass wall between us.

② Last Tree Planting Day, Dad suddenly stood up from the dinner table and said, "Ming, let's plant a little pear tree in the backyard tomorrow." I was surprised. My father had never asked me to do anything together. Mom winked at me behind his back. "Go, go," she whispered. "Your dad has been planning this for a month."

③ The next morning was windy. Dad carried a spade and a small pear tree wrapped in wet newspaper. In the corner of the yard, he showed me how to dig. At first, my spade kept sliding on the stones, and I dropped soil on his old grey shoes. I waited for him to get angry, but he only smiled a little. "Hold the spade like you hold a friend's hand," he said softly. "Not too tight, not too loose."

④ While we worked, Dad began to talk. He told me that he had planted his first tree with *his* father forty years ago, next to an old village well. That little tree was now so tall that it gave shade to the whole yard. "Every time I feel tired on the road," he said, "I think of that tree, and I know someone is still waiting for me at home." I listened quietly, the wind pulling at my jacket.

⑤ When the hole was deep enough, Dad let me place the young tree inside. He held the thin trunk steady while I filled the soil with my bare hands. My fingers turned cold, and a few tears came from nowhere. I did not know why. Perhaps it was the wind, perhaps it was those simple sentences from Dad that I had never heard before. We finally watered the tree together, poured slowly, the way one pours medicine for a sick grandmother.

⑥ Tonight, I am writing this journal under the desk lamp. Through the window, I can see the small pear tree standing bravely in the moonlight. Dad has already gone back on the road. Yet the glass wall between us feels thinner now. A tree is growing in our yard — and somewhere, I feel, a father and a son are finally growing toward each other, one small root at a time.

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why does the writer feel distant from his father at first?**

A. His father often shouts at him.  
B. His father is often away and speaks very little.  
C. His father does not allow him to watch TV.  
D. His father does not love him.

**2. What did Dad want to do on Tree Planting Day?**

A. Take the family to a famous forest.  
B. Plant a pear tree with his son in the yard.  
C. Cut down an old dead tree.  
D. Sell a small tree to a neighbor.

**3. What did Dad mean by "Hold the spade like you hold a friend's hand"?**

A. Hold the spade with a gentle but steady grip.  
B. Drop the spade on the ground.  
C. Only hold the spade with one finger.  
D. Keep the spade in a box.

**4. Why, according to Paragraph 4, did Dad's old tree mean so much to him?**

A. Because it gave a lot of pears every year.  
B. Because it reminded him of home and the one who waits for him.  
C. Because he planned to sell it one day.  
D. Because it was the only tree in the village.

**5. What is the main idea of Paragraph 6?**

A. The writer is angry that Dad has left again.  
B. The writer has learned to plant many trees.  
C. The glass wall between father and son is becoming thinner.  
D. The family will move to another city soon.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "often away for weeks... without many words"  
【解析】父亲常年外出、话少，所以作者觉得远。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "let's plant a little pear tree in the backyard"  
【解析】父亲邀请儿子一起种梨树。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】句意理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Not too tight, not too loose."  
【解析】比喻握铁锹要松紧适中。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "I know someone is still waiting for me at home"  
【解析】老树让他想起家和等待他的人。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段 "the glass wall between us feels thinner"  
【解析】父子间的隔膜正在变薄。故选 C。
