# 阅读理解（说明文） **Chinese Paper-Cutting: Stories in Red Paper**

① Every Spring Festival, many Chinese families paste bright red paper pictures on their windows, doors and walls. These tiny artworks, often full of flowers, fish and happy children, are called **paper-cuttings**. What looks like a simple decoration is in fact one of the oldest and most lovely folk arts in the world.

② Paper-cutting began in China more than **1,500 years ago**, not long after paper itself was invented. At that time, paper was still expensive, so cutting pretty patterns was a way to make a small piece of paper bring a big joy. Farmers, grandmothers and even children would cut with small scissors or tiny knives. In many northern villages, a girl was said to be ready for marriage only if she could cut a beautiful flower.

③ The shapes carry deep meanings. A **fish** (鱼 *yú*) sounds like the word for "plenty," so fish shapes wish the family a year of plenty. **Two magpies** on a plum branch mean "happy news is coming." Even small geese, lotus flowers and children holding fruit each have their own stories. In a single red square, a grandmother might cut a whole paragraph of warm wishes without saying a word.

④ The tools are surprisingly simple. Most artists use a small pair of sharp scissors, a thin knife, and red paper soft enough to fold. The paper is folded many times, and then the artist cuts through all the layers at once. When the paper is opened, a flower with four, six or even twelve "petals" suddenly appears. Some famous masters can cut fifty identical small figures — dancing girls or jumping lions — from a single folded sheet.

⑤ Today, paper-cutting is no longer only a family craft. It travels around the world as a gift of Chinese culture. In 2009, **UNESCO** listed Chinese paper-cutting as part of the *World Intangible Cultural Heritage*. Young artists are now mixing the old red paper with modern ideas — pandas holding coffee cups, tall buildings rising from a mountain river — so that the tradition becomes fresh again for a new generation.

⑥ Next time you enter a Chinese home during the New Year, pause for a moment by a window. Behind each bright red flower is a pair of careful hands, a small pair of scissors, and a long history that once travelled from a simple village to the whole world. A tiny piece of paper, in the end, can hold a very big heart.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. When did Chinese paper-cutting begin?**

A. About 500 years ago.  
B. About 1,000 years ago.  
C. More than 1,500 years ago.  
D. Only 200 years ago.

**2. According to Paragraph 3, a fish shape in paper-cutting often means ____.**

A. a long life  
B. a year of plenty  
C. a great journey  
D. a strong army

**3. Why can an artist cut many identical figures at one time?**

A. Because the paper is very thick.  
B. Because the paper is folded many times before cutting.  
C. Because the scissors are very big.  
D. Because the paper is made of cloth.

**4. What happened to Chinese paper-cutting in 2009?**

A. It was banned from shops.  
B. It was listed by UNESCO as World Intangible Cultural Heritage.  
C. It was exported only to Europe.  
D. It was used to decorate airplanes.

**5. What is the writer's attitude toward Chinese paper-cutting?**

A. It is too old to be useful.  
B. It is a small but valuable art full of culture.  
C. It should stay only in villages.  
D. It should be replaced by modern computer art.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "more than 1,500 years ago"  
【解析】剪纸起源于 1500 多年前。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "fish... 'plenty,' so fish shapes wish the family a year of plenty"  
【解析】鱼寓意"年年有余"。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "The paper is folded many times, and then the artist cuts through all the layers at once."  
【解析】多次折叠一次剪出多个图案。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "UNESCO listed Chinese paper-cutting as part of the World Intangible Cultural Heritage"  
【解析】2009 年入选世界非遗。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "A tiny piece of paper... can hold a very big heart"  
【解析】作者赞美剪纸"小纸却藏大情"。故选 B。
