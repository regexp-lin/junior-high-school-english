# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Painting the Old Courtyard Wall with Dad**

① Grandpa's old courtyard in our hometown has a long, low wall. It is made of pale bricks, painted creamy yellow many years ago. Over time, rain and sun had left it **dirty, cracked, and very tired**. On the last day of our Qingming Festival visit, Dad suddenly said, "Xiaofei, let's give this wall a new face. You and me, half a day — deal?" I was not excited, but I was not against it either. Anything sounded better than sitting inside and listening to the clock.

② Right after lunch, we walked to the small hardware shop at the top of our lane. Dad chose two big cans of **white paint** with a soft shine, a thick roller, two brushes, a long wooden stick, and an old green **drop cloth** to protect the ground. I carried the lighter bags; Dad carried the cans. On the way back, an old neighbour, Uncle Wu, saw us and smiled widely. "Father and son painting together — that is the best medicine for a spring afternoon."

③ Before we began, Dad did something I did not expect. He did not pick up the brush first. Instead, he handed me a scraper and said, "Clean comes before colour. If we paint on dust, the new coat won't live long." For the next 40 minutes, we pushed off loose paint, brushed away the sand, and wiped the wall with a damp cloth. My arms began to ache, and I started to worry — **we have not even started the painting.** Dad saw my face and said, "Xiaofei, many people only watch the bright part of work. The truly hard part is before that. Remember today."

④ Finally, paint time came. Dad taught me how to **dip the roller lightly**, roll gently from top to bottom, and overlap each line a little. My first stripes were **zebra-like** — some parts very white, other parts grey. I felt embarrassed. Dad only laughed, "A wall is kind; it forgives the first ten strokes." I tried again. This time the roller seemed to understand me; the white slowly stretched out like a clean morning snow.

⑤ At about three o'clock, the sun came out from behind a cloud. Our half-painted wall turned **bright as a new page**. Dad and I stepped back to look. He ruffled my hair and said, "See? We didn't just paint bricks; we painted a bit of spring onto Grandpa's wall." I noticed our hands — covered in white spots, dried paint under the nails, a small line of colour on my cheek. But I felt a warm, steady kind of proud, different from getting a 100 on a test.

⑥ The last part was the corners. They were harder than I thought. I had to hold a small brush and be very careful. I even painted a small white star quietly, near the bottom corner, under Dad's nose. "Shhh," I whispered to the wall, "this is our little secret, okay?" When we finally finished, the sky had turned a soft pink. Grandma came out with warm milk tea. She looked at the wall, smiled, and said, "Now the courtyard has a younger face than me."

⑦ That night, I rode back to our city flat with three things I did not have in the morning: white spots on my jeans that Mum never managed to wash off; a mild pain in my right shoulder; and a new idea. **"Clean comes before colour"** — Dad's simple line stayed in my head. I realized it was not only about walls. My essays, my maths, my friendships — all of them might look better when I first took time to fix the dusty base, before adding any bright colour.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did Dad want to paint the courtyard wall?**

A. Grandma ordered Dad to paint it right away.  
B. The wall had become dirty and cracked over years.  
C. The school asked students to paint a wall.  
D. A friend paid Dad to paint the wall.

**2. What did Dad and the writer do BEFORE they painted the wall?**

A. They drank tea in the courtyard.  
B. They bought flowers at the shop.  
C. They scraped off loose paint and cleaned the wall.  
D. They called Grandpa for advice.

**3. What did the writer's first paint stripes look like?**

A. Perfectly white and neat.  
B. Like a zebra, with some bright and some grey parts.  
C. Bright yellow instead of white.  
D. Hidden by a thick dust.

**4. What small secret did the writer add on the wall?**

A. He wrote his full name in big letters.  
B. He drew a red heart near the gate.  
C. He painted a small white star in a corner.  
D. He stuck a cartoon sticker on the wall.

**5. What is the main lesson the writer learned?**

A. Painting a wall is harder than writing essays.  
B. A father's help is only useful once.  
C. Clean the base before adding bright colour — a lesson for walls and for life.  
D. Old houses should always be left as they are.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "dirty, cracked, and very tired"  
【解析】墙因多年风雨显破旧。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "a scraper... clean comes before colour"  
【解析】先清理旧漆和灰尘。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "zebra-like — some parts very white, other parts grey"  
【解析】条纹像斑马，不均匀。A、C、D 不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "painted a small white star quietly, near the bottom corner"  
【解析】画了白色小星。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦段 "'Clean comes before colour'... essays, maths, friendships"  
【解析】生活同样先打好基础。A、B、D 与主旨不符。故选 C。

- **drop cloth** [n.] 防护布  
- **stripe** [n.] 条纹
