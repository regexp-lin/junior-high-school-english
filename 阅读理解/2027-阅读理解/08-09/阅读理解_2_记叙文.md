# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Day I Took the Wrong Bus**

① On a grey Saturday morning last spring, I took the wrong bus — and found a friend I did not know I needed. I was fourteen then, and I was going to the city library, which I could reach by Bus 17 from our neighbourhood. But I was sleepy, and I stepped onto Bus 71 by mistake. The two numbers look very similar in our city — thick, black, on a white board.

② I did not notice my mistake for a long time. I sat by the window, opened my novel, and read for almost half an hour. When I finally looked up, none of the streets outside were familiar. The tall glass buildings were gone. We were passing small shops, old houses, tree-lined lanes, and a long river I had never seen before. I stood up in a panic, walked to the driver, and asked, "Uncle, is this Bus 17?"

③ The driver shook his head. "17? Young man, you are on Bus 71. We are almost at the end of the line, East Lake village." I looked at my phone: the map showed me a small dot, thirty-two kilometres from home. I had less than fifteen yuan in my pocket and almost no signal for online pay.

④ "What should I do?" I said, my voice too small. The driver smiled. "Get off at the terminus, cross the bridge, and take Bus 71 back. Same ticket." His words were kind, but at that moment, my head was still full of library books I would not reach, and of my mother — who was at home, thinking that I was reading peacefully in a quiet building. I sat back in my seat, embarrassed and tired.

⑤ Beside me was an old man with a big wooden walking stick. He had white hair, a brown jacket, and clear, calm eyes. He had heard my conversation with the driver. "Young man," he said gently, "don't worry. Everyone takes a wrong bus once in their life. Mine was in 1978." He laughed quietly. "Come, since you are here, look at this village — it is one of the oldest in our city."

⑥ We got off at the terminus together. The village was smaller than I expected: two main streets, some yellow mud walls, clean stone steps going down to the river. The old man pointed. "My grandfather was born in that house. The wall on the left is over one hundred years old." He told me the names of three trees. He told me why the village was built on that bend of the river. He even showed me a stone with an old date carved on it.

⑦ Then he opened a small paper bag and gave me half of his steamed bread and a boiled egg. "Young man, food is important when you are lost." I was going to say no, but my stomach was loud, and he had already pressed the food into my hand.

⑧ While we ate, he asked me why I was going to the library. I told him: I was preparing a short speech on "a quiet place in our city", for an English class the next week. He nodded slowly. "I know a quiet place," he said. He led me behind the small village school, where an old, huge tree stood, its branches spreading over a clean stone table. "I have come here," he said, "since I was eight. I have sat under this tree with my father, my brother, my wife, and — later — alone. Every kind of thinking I have ever done happened here. It is the most quiet place I know."

⑨ For almost an hour, we sat under that tree. He told me stories about his village: the flood of 1954, the school of 1967, the day his son first rode a bike. I did not take out a pen; I just listened. The air smelled of rain and leaves. Somewhere, a chicken was clucking. I had never felt less worried.

⑩ Later, he walked me back to the bus stop and waved as Bus 71 pulled away. I rode home slowly. At home, my mother was, of course, confused when I said I had been to East Lake village. "For the library?" she asked. "Well," I said, "I found a better one."

⑪ The next Monday, I gave my English speech. I did not speak about the city library. I spoke about a tree behind a small village school, and about an old man who turned my wrong bus into a right memory. My English teacher said it was my best speech of the year. I knew why. I had not written it. I had lived it.

⑫ Since that day, I have sometimes thought: getting on the wrong bus, if we are open, may be a quiet way of meeting the right people. Not every mistake is a mistake. Some are, in their strange way, invitations.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What mistake did the writer make that Saturday morning?**

A. He bought the wrong book at the library.  
B. He took Bus 71 instead of Bus 17 by accident, because the numbers looked similar.  
C. He forgot to pay for his bus ticket.  
D. He got off the bus at the wrong stop on purpose.

**2. When the writer realised he was on the wrong bus, how did the driver help him?**

A. The driver drove him back to the city for free.  
B. The driver told him to get off at the terminus, cross the bridge, and take Bus 71 back, using the same ticket.  
C. The driver called the writer's mother.  
D. The driver was angry and asked him to leave at once.

**3. What did the old man do when he saw the writer was worried?**

A. He ignored the writer.  
B. He told the writer not to worry, shared his food, and showed him around the village, including a quiet old tree.  
C. He lent the writer money and walked away.  
D. He called the police.

**4. Why did the writer later say his English speech was his "best speech of the year"?**

A. Because his mother helped him write every sentence.  
B. Because he had actually lived the story — the village, the old man, the tree — and did not only read about it.  
C. Because his teacher paid him money.  
D. Because he spoke Chinese in the English class.

**5. What is the main lesson of the story?**

A. Students should always take Bus 71 instead of Bus 17.  
B. Not every mistake is really a mistake; sometimes taking a "wrong" path leads us to meet the right people and find unexpected good things.  
C. Old men know more than young men about buses.  
D. City libraries are not worth visiting.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末  
【解析】两路车号相似，错上了71路。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段  
【解析】终点下车过桥回乘71路，同票有效。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤–⑨段  
【解析】安慰、分食、介绍村庄与老树。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑪段末  
【解析】"I had lived it."——亲身经历。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑫段  
【解析】错乘/走错路有时是遇见的邀请。A/C/D 均偏。故选 B。
