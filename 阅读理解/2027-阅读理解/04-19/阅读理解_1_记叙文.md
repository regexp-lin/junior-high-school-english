# 阅读理解（记叙文） **A Strange Encounter at the Night Market**

① On Saturday evening, my cousin Kai and I went to the **Old Street Night Market**. The sky was soft purple. The **red paper lanterns** swung gently in the spring wind. The air smelled of grilled squid, sweet potato, and hot milk tea. I had finally finished my maths exam, so Mum gave me 50 yuan and said, "Have fun, but be home by 9 o'clock." We were full of plans: try two snacks, buy one small gift, and take three photos.

② Twenty minutes into the market, we met a strange scene at a quiet corner. A **thin old man**, wearing a grey cotton coat and carrying a heavy cloth bag, was standing still in the middle of the crowd, **looking lost**. He seemed to be searching for someone. He kept saying, "**Xiao Lan? Xiao Lan, are you there?**" People walked past him without slowing down. Kai pulled my sleeve. "That is strange," he whispered. "Shall we help?"

③ I hesitated. I did not know the old man. I had read stories about city tricks. But his eyes were watery, and he was shaking a little. I stepped forward and spoke as politely as I could. "Grandfather, are you okay? Can we help you?" He looked up. His eyes were surprised, then soft. "Good child, I… I think I cannot find my way back. My wife, Xiao Lan, went to buy **tofu skewers**, and I turned around, and now the streets all look the same." He showed us an old grey flip-phone that he had **turned off by mistake**.

④ Kai helped him turn the phone on. I bought a bottle of warm water from the nearest stall and gave it to the old man. While we waited for his phone to boot, I asked him a few simple questions: "Do you remember the name of the place where you met your wife tonight?" He paused. "Yes — the **big red archway** by the stone lion." "Good!" I said. "That is only 200 metres from here." Kai offered his arm. "We can walk you there slowly, if you want."

⑤ On the way, the old man told us small things. His name was Grandpa Shi. He was 79. He and his wife were from our city, married for 55 years. Every spring Saturday, they visited this night market together because they had met here in 1972. "She still makes me carry her bag," he smiled, "and I still carry it with pride." Kai and I looked at each other. Neither of us spoke, but our eyes shone. There was something grand and quiet in his words.

⑥ When we reached the red archway, a small, tidy old lady was standing with a worried face, a paper tray of **tofu skewers** in her hand. "You stupid man!" she cried, half scolding, half laughing. "I have walked this market three times!" She then turned to us and bowed gently. "Thank you, children. Who taught you to be so kind?" Kai, still holding a photo of a mooncake he had just bought, did not know what to say. I smiled. "Our parents. And our grandparents."

⑦ When we finally started walking back, Kai checked his phone. 8:47 p.m. We had spent all our time helping, and we had not bought a single gift or tried a single snack. But my pockets, heavier with 30 unspent yuan and two warm stories, felt fuller than before. Sometimes, I realized, **the best thing you take home from a night market is not a bag of food, but the small good deed you would not have tried a year ago.** That night I understood that kindness, like the red lanterns, quietly lit up the whole street.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How much money did Mum give the writer for the night market?**

A. 30 yuan.  
B. 50 yuan.  
C. 80 yuan.  
D. 100 yuan.

**2. What was wrong with the old man when the writer first saw him?**

A. He was shouting angrily at the crowd.  
B. He was lost and calling for his wife "Xiao Lan".  
C. He was selling tofu skewers.  
D. He was fighting with another old man.

**3. What did the writer and Kai do FIRST to help the old man?**

A. They called the police.  
B. They gave him their last 30 yuan.  
C. They turned on his phone, gave him water, and asked simple questions.  
D. They ran to the market office.

**4. Where did the old man and his wife first meet each other long ago?**

A. In a bookshop in another city.  
B. At the big red archway in this night market in 1972.  
C. In the local train station.  
D. At a cooking class.

**5. What does the writer learn from this experience?**

A. Night markets are full of thieves.  
B. Old people should not go out at night.  
C. A small good deed can become the best thing you take home from a night market.  
D. Students should never speak to strangers.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "50 yuan"  
【解析】妈妈给了50元。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "looking lost... Xiao Lan?"  
【解析】老人迷路找妻子。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段动作描述  
【解析】开手机、递水、询问。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "they had met here in 1972... big red archway"  
【解析】1972年在红牌楼相识。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦段末  
【解析】善举比零食更珍贵。故选 C。
