# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Old Bookseller Near the Bus Stop**

① Every day on the way to school, I pass a small second-hand bookshop by the number 9 bus stop. The shop has no neon sign — only a plain wooden board with three Chinese characters painted in old black ink. Inside, an old man with white hair sits behind a low counter. Between us there has never been more than a simple "Good morning." But for three whole years, I thought he was nothing more than a quiet shadow behind a pile of books.

② Last Wednesday, everything changed. I had just failed my monthly English test. I failed by a lot — only 61 out of 100. Walking past the bookshop, I felt too tired to go straight home. I pushed open the old wooden door and sat in a corner beside a pile of English dictionaries, trying not to cry in front of a stranger.

③ The old man did not say anything at first. After ten minutes, he quietly placed a small glass of warm water on the table near me. Then he handed me an old thin book. Its cover said *A Little Dictionary of Brave Words*. "This is not for sale," he smiled. "But you can read a page if you like." I opened it at random. The first word I saw was "**persevere**", with an old handwritten note in the margin: "_to try again, with a tired but open heart_".

④ I looked up. The old man was wiping the dust off another shelf. "Grandpa," I said shyly, "why do you love books so much?" He sat on a small wooden stool and told me his story. Thirty years ago, he had been a middle school Chinese teacher. When he retired, he decided to open a small bookshop, not to sell, but to "keep books company". He had lost his wife twelve years ago, and his only son now worked in a different city. "Books do not leave you," he said gently. "They wait, all their lives, for a reader who needs them."

⑤ He did not mention my red face or my school bag full of marked papers. Instead, he pointed at a page in a second book, open on his counter. It was the story of a Russian writer who failed nine times before publishing one famous novel. Somewhere between that page and the small warm glass of water, the heavy lump in my chest began to melt a little. When I stood up to leave, he wrapped *A Little Dictionary of Brave Words* in brown paper. "Please read slowly," he said. "Bring it back when you no longer need it."

⑥ That evening, I placed the small book on my desk, beside my English test. I studied the word "persevere" and the twelve words that came after it. I did not score 100 on the next test — not even close. But I did score 79. On a Saturday afternoon, I returned the book to the old man, added two fresh oranges, and said, "Thank you, Grandpa." His quiet eyes filled with warm light. Now I always smile at him, and he always smiles back. The number 9 bus stop, which used to be only a bus stop, has become a small, bright corner of my city.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer push open the old bookshop door last Wednesday?**

A. He wanted to sell some of his old books.  
B. He had just failed his monthly English test and felt too tired to go home.  
C. He was meeting his teacher there.  
D. The shop was on fire.

**2. What did the old man put on the table for the writer?**

A. A bowl of rice.  
B. A small glass of warm water.  
C. A new school uniform.  
D. A box of sweets.

**3. Why did the old man open the bookshop after he retired?**

A. To make a lot of money.  
B. To "keep books company" after his wife passed away and his son moved to a different city.  
C. To train new Chinese teachers.  
D. To sell tea with books.

**4. What did the writer learn from the old man's stories and the dictionary?**

A. That books are a waste of time.  
B. That trying again with an open heart (persevere) is important, and that failure is not the end.  
C. That every student can become famous.  
D. That the old man would pay for his school fees.

**5. How did the writer feel about the bookshop by the end of the story?**

A. It was annoying and in the way.  
B. It was only a bus stop, nothing more.  
C. It had become a small, bright corner of his city — a place of comfort and growth.  
D. It was the most expensive shop in town.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "I had just failed my monthly English test... too tired to go straight home."  
【解析】作者刚英语不及格，太疲惫想歇一歇。A/C/D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "placed a small glass of warm water on the table..."  
【解析】老人端来一小杯温水。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "not to sell, but to 'keep books company'... lost his wife twelve years ago..."  
【解析】退休后开书店是为了"陪伴书"，也缘于亲人离开。A/C/D 均不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断/主旨题  
【定位】第③⑤段 "_to try again, with a tired but open heart_" 以及俄罗斯作家失败九次的故事  
【解析】作者领悟到坚持与再尝试的意义。A/C/D 均与原文相反或无据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】写作意图/情感态度题  
【定位】第⑥段末 "has become a small, bright corner of my city."  
【解析】作者最终感到那里是城市里温暖的小角落。A/B/D 均不符。故选 C。

**词汇注释（超纲词）**  
- **persevere** [v.] 坚持不懈  
- **retire** [v.] 退休  
- **margin** [n.] 页边；边空
