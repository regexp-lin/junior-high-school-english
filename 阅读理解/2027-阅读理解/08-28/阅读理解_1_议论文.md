# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle Schools Still Ask All Students to Learn Classical Poems by Heart?**

① Every middle-school student in our country knows the feeling: your Chinese teacher stands at the door of the classroom, a list in her hand, and you have 20 seconds to **recite** a Tang poem in front of her before you may enter. Some students love it; others quietly hate it. In a recent school survey, **nearly half** of the Grade 8 students said: "Why should we learn so many old poems by heart? In 2027, we have AI; it can give us the meaning in one second." So, **should middle schools still ask every student to learn classical poems by heart?** I believe the answer is yes — though the way we teach them must be thoughtful.

② Those who disagree have understandable reasons. First, they say learning old poems takes up a lot of evening time that could be used for Maths, English or sleep. Second, old Chinese characters and grammar are hard; many students memorise sounds but do not really understand the poem. Third, they argue that the ideas of old poems belong to the lives of farmers and officials a thousand years ago, and may not speak to a city teenager's life today.

③ These points are fair, but I do not think they close the case.

④ First, learning a poem by heart is not just about memory — it is about **the sound of the language**. Classical Chinese poems were made to be **said aloud**. They have a clear rhythm, beautiful repeats, and short but powerful lines. When we recite them — "床前明月光" — the sound of the words enters our body. Over time, this slowly teaches us how to hear Chinese at a higher level, just as learning songs teaches us to hear music. A student who can recite twenty poems writes better in his own words too.

⑤ Second, although some old characters are hard, **understanding grows with time**. When a Grade 7 student first recites 《静夜思》, he may only feel "it rhymes nicely". When he reads it again at 25, alone in a cold city far from home, he may suddenly feel tears in his eyes: **this is exactly how I feel tonight**. A poem learned early is like a seed planted in the mind; the real flower may open ten years later. AI can give us a translation, but AI cannot give us the **feeling** we have when a childhood poem comes back to us on a lonely night.

⑥ Third, the worry that "old poems are too far from modern life" is partly a misunderstanding. Yes, farmers and officials are not our daily world. But the real **subjects** of Tang and Song poems — missing a friend, the moon over a home village, a rainy day by a window, the quiet sadness of getting older — are still the deepest things in human life. A middle-school student today feels all of these, too. The language is old; the heart is not.

⑦ There is also a wider reason. **Classical poems are a common language** in Chinese culture. When we meet a stranger who has read the same poems, there is an invisible "hello" between us, even if we say nothing. At the Chinese New Year table, a grandmother who remembers 《元日》 can look at her grand-daughter and smile; the girl can smile back and finish the next line. That shared memory is a quiet treasure that no short video can replace.

⑧ Of course, I am not saying every teacher should only **test** students by counting how many lines they recite. The worst way to teach poetry is to turn it into a race: fifty poems in one term, with no time to feel any of them. Schools could try three changes: **recite fewer but go deeper**, **connect each poem to a real moment in life**, and **allow some poems to be chosen by students**. A poem loved by the student is always better than a poem forced down the throat.

⑨ So, should every middle-school student still learn classical poems by heart? Yes, but with patience, with real feeling, and with room to grow. A hundred good poems carried quietly in the mind is one of the most beautiful gifts a school can give a young person — a small private library that no phone can take away.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main view?**

A. Middle schools should stop asking students to learn classical poems.  
B. Middle schools should still ask every student to learn classical poems by heart, but the teaching method needs to be thoughtful.  
C. Only Grade 9 students should study classical poems.  
D. Classical poems are only for future language teachers.

**2. According to the passage, which is one of the reasons given by people who **disagree**?**

A. Classical poems are too short to be useful.  
B. Many students memorise the sounds without really understanding the old characters and grammar.  
C. Classical poems cost too much money to buy.  
D. Most poems are not allowed in schools.

**3. How does the writer answer the worry that "the ideas of old poems belong to people from 1,000 years ago"?**

A. By saying we should stop reading any old book.  
B. By saying the deep subjects of old poems — missing a friend, the moon over home, quiet sadness — are still the deepest things in human life today.  
C. By saying modern life has no feelings.  
D. By saying old poems are only for tourists.

**4. What extra benefit of classical poems is described in paragraph ⑦?**

A. They help students win money at contests.  
B. They are a common cultural language; shared poems make an invisible "hello" between people, and join family memories.  
C. They are often sold abroad as souvenirs.  
D. They help students get better at sports.

**5. Which of the following changes does the writer **NOT** suggest for schools?**

A. Recite fewer poems but go deeper into each one.  
B. Connect each poem to a real moment in life.  
C. Allow some poems to be chosen by the students.  
D. Turn poetry into a fast race of fifty poems in one term.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "yes — though the way we teach them must be thoughtful"  
【解析】作者既肯定背诵古诗，也强调教法需用心。A、C、D 均与立场不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "many students memorise sounds but do not really understand the poem"  
【解析】原文直接列出此理由。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "The real subjects ... are still the deepest things in human life"  
【解析】作者指出主题(思乡、友情、孤独)并未过时。A、C、D 均与作者立场不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "Classical poems are a common language ... an invisible 'hello' ... That shared memory is a quiet treasure"  
【解析】古诗作为共同文化语言连结人们。A、C、D 均无文中依据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑧段 "The worst way to teach poetry is to turn it into a race: fifty poems in one term"  
【解析】作者明确反对把诗歌变成竞速。A、B、C 为作者建议。故选 D。
