# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Year-End Volunteer Thank-You Ceremony 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Student Union + Student Affairs Office**  
> **Year-End Volunteer Thank-You Ceremony 2027** — *"A Small Room, A Warm Cup, A Quiet Thank You."*  
> **Date**: **Wednesday, 30 December 2027**  
> **Time**: **16:00 – 17:10** (70 minutes, after last afternoon class)  
> **Venue**: **Main Hall (1F), Lihua Middle School**  
> **No tickets. No entrance check.** All Lihua students, staff, and one parent per student are warmly welcome.

---

① On the last Wednesday of 2027, Lihua Middle School will hold its annual **Volunteer Thank-You Ceremony** — not a prize ceremony, not a ranking event, but a small quiet hour in which the school family pauses to say "thank you" to the **238 student volunteers** who gave, in small quiet pieces, **about 4,760 hours** of kind time during the 2027 school year. This year, volunteers helped with Reading Partners, the Library Book-Swap Fair, the Handicraft Fair walks to Sunshine Elderly Home, Morning-Light Run captains, Cross-Grade Reading pairs, Bus-Stop Guides on rainy mornings, Winter Solstice Workshop captains, Silent-Reading Morning library-basket carriers, and more. **Every volunteer, without exception, is thanked.** No student is named as "best" or "most". Every volunteer receives exactly the same small paper envelope, containing exactly the same three small things.

---

## 🕒 Ceremony Schedule

| Time | Activity | Where |
|---|---|---|
| 15:45 – 16:00 | Guests arrive; warm lemon tea and ginger biscuits at the entrance | Main Hall foyer |
| 16:00 – 16:05 | Opening words by Principal Gao | Main Hall stage |
| 16:05 – 16:15 | **"Our 2027 in Numbers"** (short silent slide show; no names) | Main Hall screen |
| 16:15 – 16:40 | **Thank-You Walk**: each of the 238 volunteers walks quietly across the stage, shakes one hand, receives the thank-you envelope, and returns to her seat (target pace: **6 students per minute**) | Main Hall stage |
| 16:40 – 16:55 | **Three short volunteer reflections** (2 min each): one Grade 7, one Grade 8, one Grade 9; draw by lot in advance | Main Hall stage |
| 16:55 – 17:05 | **Closing words** by Head of Student Affairs Ms Qin | Main Hall stage |
| 17:05 – 17:10 | Quiet exit; free hot tangyuan at the foyer kindness table | Main Hall foyer |

---

## ✉️ What Is in the Thank-You Envelope (every volunteer gets the same three things)

| Item | Content | Note |
|---|---|---|
| 1. A small **handwritten thank-you card** | Written by Principal Gao, Ms Qin, or one of the nine homeroom teachers — 3–4 lines, no template | Each card is personal; not the same text as any other |
| 2. A **"Kind Hands" enamel pin** | 1.8 cm diameter, soft green, a single line drawing of two small hands | Worn on the uniform collar if the student wishes; wholly optional |
| 3. A small **sealed envelope marked "Open on 1 February 2028"** | Contains a one-year reminder — a small thought to read quietly 34 days later | Private; not to be opened early; not to be shared with classmates |

**No money, no voucher, no gift card is given.** The school believes that thanks given in paper and words — not in yuan — carry more weight.

---

## 🎤 The Three Short Volunteer Reflections (draw-by-lot)

| Grade | Topic (chosen freely by the student) | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Grade 7 | "One small thing I did not know I would learn this year" | 2 minutes |
| Grade 8 | "Someone who quietly taught me something, without meaning to" | 2 minutes |
| Grade 9 | "A small thing I now know about being a volunteer" | 2 minutes |

- Speakers are **randomly drawn** from the 238 volunteers on **Monday 28 December** by a simple paper-lot method. Any drawn student who does not wish to speak may **decline without reason**; a second lot is drawn.  
- Each speaker writes her own short words; **no teacher reviews or edits** them.  
- **No names of other students** are mentioned in the reflections, out of privacy.  
- **Applause is welcomed but not required.** A single quiet nod from the audience is considered equally warm.

---

## 🪑 Seating (simple, no VIP)

- **Seats are unassigned.** First-come, first-seat.  
- **Volunteers sit among their classmates**, not in a special front row.  
- **Parents and siblings** sit in the back four rows to leave closer rows for students.  
- **Disabled seating and wheelchair spaces** are in the second row, east side; please see Ms Song at the door.  
- **Phones off or silent**; **no photo taken of any student's face** unless she nods yes personally. A Journalism Club photographer will take only wide-angle shots of the audience and stage.

---

## 🫖 Warm Kindness Table (16:55 – 17:10)

- **Free hot tangyuan** (one bowl of 4 small sweet rice balls with ginger broth) for every guest.  
- **Free warm lemon tea** (100 ml paper cup).  
- **A small box** at the side: any student who wishes may drop in one **anonymous folded paper** ("thank you to a volunteer I never told"); the box is read silently by Ms Qin on the first Monday of January, and the slips are then destroyed — they are not posted, not photographed, not archived.  
- **No food or drink** may be carried into the Main Hall seating area; kindness table is in the foyer only.

---

## 🫶 Volunteer Privacy Rules (important)

- **No student's name or hour-count is read aloud** from the stage.  
- **No leaderboard** of "most hours" is made.  
- **"Our 2027 in Numbers"** slide show shows only **group totals**, never individual hours.  
- **Any student who does not wish to walk across the stage** may quietly take her envelope from Ms Qin afterwards at the corridor door between 17:00 and 17:20. There is no penalty; the school respects shy students fully.  
- **Any student who wishes not to be thanked at all** may write a small paper note to Ms Qin by 29 December; her envelope will be kept for her in a drawer indefinitely, untouched, until she requests otherwise.

---

## 📅 After the Ceremony (quietly, for the next school year)

- **Volunteer sign-up for Spring Semester 2028** will open on **Monday 8 February 2028**.  
- **No 2027 volunteer is guaranteed automatic continuation**; every student re-chooses each semester.  
- **No 2027 hour count is carried forward** as an official "credit"; the hours were their own reward.  
- **A small volunteer-coordinator meeting** for returning students will be held **16:00 – 16:45, Tuesday 9 February 2028**, Room 204.

---

## ❗ Important Quiet Reminders

- **This ceremony is not a competition.** The word **"best"** will not be used on stage.  
- **The ceremony is optional for volunteers.** Attendance is warmly encouraged but fully private.  
- **No applause meters, no music, no fireworks.** A quiet hour is the entire point.  
- **Leaving early** is allowed without signing anything; simply walk quietly out the rear door.  
- **If you are sick** (fever, flu, cough), **please rest at home**; a special small envelope delivery will be arranged by Ms Qin for you within 3 school days.

---

## 💬 A Small Word from Ms Qin

> "In 2027, **238 Lihua students** — about **29% of our whole school** — did some small voluntary kindness for other people on our campus or in our community, usually quietly, usually without being noticed, usually for **somebody who did not know her name**. Somebody walked a book to Sunshine Elderly Home. Somebody sat with a Grade-7 student for 40 minutes every Friday. Somebody taught her grandmother how to send a voice message. Somebody carried a library basket up three flights. Somebody knelt down to a six-year-old at the compound gate and said, *'Are you lost? Come, I will walk with you.'* **We do not know, and we do not need to know, which of you did which.** We only know that 238 of you did something, and that 4,760 hours of quiet kindness happened in a school building. On 30 December, for seventy minutes in a warm hall, we simply want to pause and say, to all of you together, **thank you.** That is all. No big words. No ranks. Just this small quiet hour. Come, if you can. You are the reason the school feels warm in December."  
> — *Ms Qin, Head of Student Affairs, 23 December 2027*
