# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School 2028 New-Year Wish Board — A Small Quiet Wall for Everyone**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Student Union + Student Affairs Office**  
> **2028 New-Year Wish Board** — *"A Small Quiet Wall for Everyone."*  
> **Reference**: WB-2028 · **Published**: Friday 31 December 2027  
> **Open dates**: **Friday 31 December 2027 (13:40) — Monday 17 January 2028 (08:00)**  
> **Venue**: **Central corridor wall, 1F Building A** (between the Main Hall and the Green-Leaf Library)

---

① The **2028 New-Year Wish Board** is a small quiet wall that Lihua Middle School opens to every student, teacher, canteen worker, night guard, cleaner, librarian, and parent, for the **17 days** between the last afternoon of 2027 and the first morning of the winter exam. It is not a competition, not a social-media event, and not a ranking of who writes the loudest wish. It is a **single 12-metre white wall**, lit gently, on which anybody in the Lihua family may pin a small **8 × 8 cm handwritten wish card**, anonymous or signed, in Chinese or in simple English, for 2028 — their own wish, or a wish they would quietly like to send to someone else. **Every wish is welcomed. No wish is ranked. No wish is removed until 17 January.**

---

## 🗓️ Opening and Closing Times

| Date | Time | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Fri 31 Dec 2027** | 13:40 (after lunch) – 18:00 | Board opens; first cards pinned |
| **Sat 1 Jan – Fri 14 Jan 2028** | 07:30 – 18:30 daily | Open during all school hours |
| **Sat – Sun 15 – 16 Jan** | 10:00 – 16:00 | Weekend reduced hours |
| **Mon 17 Jan 2028** | 07:30 – 08:00 | **Final viewing** before winter exam begins |
| **Mon 17 Jan 2028, 17:00** | — | **All cards gently removed and archived** |

- **Pinning** is self-service: take a card from the Wish Table at the left end of the wall, write, pin it with one of the small wooden pins provided.  
- **Reading** is free and quiet: walk slowly along the wall, read softly, do not lean in or photograph.  
- **No music** plays near the board; the corridor stays peaceful.

---

## 🖊️ The Small Rules (printed on every wish card)

| # | Rule |
|---|---|
| 1 | **One card per person per day**, up to **three cards total** across the 17-day window. |
| 2 | **Maximum 50 Chinese characters or 40 English words** per card; shorter is warmly welcomed. |
| 3 | **Kind tone only.** No ranking of classmates, no criticism of any student or teacher, no shop-promotion wishes. |
| 4 | **No names of classmates, teachers, or staff** written on any card — if a wish is to someone, use "*to a quiet friend in 8-B*" or "*to the kind cleaner on the south stairs*". |
| 5 | **Anonymous or signed is up to you.** Signed cards use **first name only** (no surname). |
| 6 | **Cards may be written in Chinese or English** (or both); spelling errors will never be corrected on the board. |
| 7 | **No phone photographs** of other people's cards; a small sign on each side of the board reminds all visitors. |
| 8 | **No removing or moving** somebody else's card is allowed; each card stays where its writer pinned it. |

---

## ✨ Six Small Sub-Zones on the Wall (by gentle theme)

| Zone | Suggested wishes | Small chalk label |
|---|---|---|
| **1. For Myself** | Private small wishes for personal growth, study, kindness to self | *"My quiet 2028."* |
| **2. For a Classmate** | Wishes to any classmate (name not written) | *"Somebody near me."* |
| **3. For a Teacher or Staff Member** | Wishes to any Lihua adult (name not written) | *"Thank-you corner."* |
| **4. For My Family** | Wishes for parents, grandparents, siblings | *"Home and warmth."* |
| **5. For a Stranger** | Wishes for someone the writer does not know but thinks of | *"Kindness to nobody named."* |
| **6. For the City / Country / World** | Larger peaceful wishes for 2028 | *"Beyond our wall."* |

> Students may pin in any zone they wish. The zones are **gentle suggestions, not boundaries**. A card in the "wrong" zone is still welcome; no staff member will move it.

## 🧠 The Monday 17 January Gentle Archive Ceremony

- At exactly **17:00 on Monday 17 January 2028**, just after the last winter-exam paper of the day ends, **Mr Qin and Ms Wu will quietly remove every card together** from the wall.  
- **No photograph is taken.** No newspaper report. No loudspeaker ceremony.  
- Every card is **placed into a brown paper archive box** (reference **WB-2028-Archive**).  
- The archive box is stored in the Green-Leaf Library's **"2028 Winter Memory Drawer"** on the second shelf.  
- **Any student** may **quietly** open the drawer during library hours **any time in 2028** and sit on the reading-corner cushion to reread the wishes. The drawer is open to students only.  
- On **Friday 20 December 2028**, before the winter break, **the whole archive box is respectfully burned** in a small quiet ceremony in the school back garden, attended by three student representatives and Ms Qin. No loud speech; no media.  
- **Why burning?** Because these are small quiet wishes, not permanent public documents. They were given one peaceful year to live; after that, their warmth remains, even if the paper does not.

---

## 🕊️ Privacy Rules (non-negotiable)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| 1 | **No card is signed with a surname**; even signed cards use first name only. |
| 2 | **No staff member** is allowed to identify the handwriting of any card, on or off the board. |
| 3 | **No parent** may remove a card for any reason; cards belong to the school wall, not to any family. |
| 4 | **No class discussion** or homework assignment may be built on the wish board; the board is not a teaching material. |
| 5 | **No media of any kind** — school website, WeChat group, short video, local newspaper — may photograph or share the content of any card. |
| 6 | **A student who recognises her own card** should not point to it, talk about it publicly, or claim authorship; private authorship is part of the board's gift. |

---

## 🏮 A Small Ritual Suggestion (fully optional)

> If a student wishes, after pinning her card, she may quietly:
>
> 1. Stand two steps back from the wall for 30 seconds.  
> 2. Read three other people's wishes, slowly, without commenting.  
> 3. Place her right hand lightly over her heart, wish each of the three silently good luck in 2028.  
> 4. Walk away without turning back to look at her own card again.  
>
> This small ritual is **not required**; it is only a small quiet way of letting go of the wish with a gentle respect. Any student who finds the ritual unnecessary may simply walk away after pinning. Both paths are equal.

---

## ❓ Frequently Gently-Asked Questions

**Q1. What if I do not have anything to wish for?**  
A1. Then please do not pin. The board is warmly optional. A blank corner of wall is a perfectly valid wish.

**Q2. What if I make a mistake on my card?**  
A2. Take another card quietly. The old one may be placed, folded small, in the mistakes basket at the Wish Table; it will be archived too, gently.

**Q3. What if somebody ruins or tears a card on the wall?**  
A3. Please tell Mr Qin quietly. A replacement blank card will be pinned in the same spot with the note *"card respected, rewritten 2028"*, and no student will be publicly blamed.

**Q4. Can primary-school siblings of Lihua students pin a card?**  
A4. Yes — any small family member accompanied by a Lihua student is warmly welcome to pin a card during the opening hours.

**Q5. Can teachers also pin cards?**  
A5. Yes. Teachers are welcome to write in Zone 1 (for themselves), Zone 3, Zone 5 or Zone 6. The Wish Board is a Lihua family wall, not only a student wall.
