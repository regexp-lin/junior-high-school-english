# 阅读理解（记叙文） **When the Lights Went Out**

① It was a Tuesday evening, and the whole neighborhood was living in its usual quiet way. Mom was cutting vegetables for dinner, Dad was reading a newspaper on the sofa, and I was sitting at my small desk, fighting with a difficult math problem. Suddenly, everything changed in one second. The lights, the TV, the refrigerator — all of them went dark at the same time. A power cut had hit our whole building.

② At first, I was a little annoyed. I had already spent twenty minutes on the problem, and now my screen was black. I opened the window and looked out. The whole street was dark too; only the moon and a few small stars lit up the world. In the sudden silence, I could hear children laughing and a neighbor calling for her cat. It was strangely beautiful, like a short pause in a busy song.

③ Mom lit two small candles in the kitchen. Their soft orange light danced on the tiles. "Tonight we'll have a candle dinner," she smiled. Dad brought out an old chess board from a drawer I had never noticed before. "When I was your age," he told me, "my father and I used to play chess every time the power went out in the village." I had never thought my busy, serious father had such a childhood.

④ Dinner that night was very simple — rice, egg soup, and a small plate of cabbage — yet it somehow tasted different. Without the noise of the TV, we actually heard each other's words. Mom told us a funny story about a neighbor's talking parrot. Dad explained why the Moon was so bright that night. I surprised myself by telling them about a small girl in my class who had just lost her pet hamster. I had not told anyone about her before.

⑤ After dinner, Dad and I played three games of chess by candlelight. I lost all three, but I did not mind. In the shadows, I could see Dad's face clearly — the tired eyes, the small kind lines around his mouth. He looked younger, as if the darkness had taken twenty years off his face. Once, when I made a silly move, he laughed so hard that the candle shook. I could not remember the last time I had heard him laugh like that.

⑥ Around 10 p.m., the lights came back with a soft *click*. The refrigerator hummed, the screen on the wall woke up, and the small magic disappeared. But that night, I wrote one line in my notebook: *"Sometimes, to see the people you love most clearly, you need the lights to go out for a while."*

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What was the writer doing when the lights went out?**

A. Watching TV with Dad.  
B. Cooking dinner with Mom.  
C. Trying to solve a difficult math problem.  
D. Listening to music on the sofa.

**2. How did the writer feel at first about the power cut?**

A. Extremely happy.  
B. A little annoyed.  
C. Afraid and shaking.  
D. Curious and excited.

**3. What new thing did the writer learn about Dad that night?**

A. He used to play chess with his own father during power cuts.  
B. He was afraid of the dark.  
C. He had once been a cook.  
D. He had lost a hamster in his childhood.

**4. Why did dinner taste "different" to the writer that night?**

A. Mom used a new kind of salt.  
B. Without the noise of the TV, the family really heard each other.  
C. They ate in complete silence.  
D. Dad cooked the food himself.

**5. What is the main message of the last paragraph?**

A. Electricity makes life boring.  
B. The writer will ask Dad to break the power again.  
C. Sometimes darkness helps us see the people we love clearly.  
D. Chess is the best family game.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "fighting with a difficult math problem"  
【解析】作者正在做数学题。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "At first, I was a little annoyed."  
【解析】起初有些烦。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "my father and I used to play chess every time the power went out"  
【解析】得知父亲儿时常与祖父下棋。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段 "Without the noise of the TV, we actually heard each other's words."  
【解析】没有电视声，家人真正倾听彼此。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段 "need the lights to go out for a while"  
【解析】偶尔停电让我们看清所爱之人。故选 C。
