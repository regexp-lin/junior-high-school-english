# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle School Students Keep a Handwritten Diary?**

① In the age of smartphones, writing a diary with pen and paper may sound as old-fashioned as **sending letters by pigeon**. A chat app is faster; a photo says more in one tap than a page of words. Yet many teachers, especially of Chinese and English, still ask students, "Have you written your diary this week?" Is keeping a **handwritten diary** really worth the time, or has it become a tired old habit?

② Those who agree say a diary is the cheapest and kindest **listener** a teenager can have. Middle school is a time full of small storms: a tough test, a small fight with a friend, a secret worry before a speech. Speaking these thoughts aloud can be hard. A diary asks no questions, judges no one, and waits quietly until the writer is ready to think.

③ A handwritten diary also **slows down the brain** in a good way. When we type, our fingers move faster than our ideas. When we write by hand, every word takes two seconds longer, and those two seconds give the brain time to choose better words, remember small details, and notice feelings we might otherwise miss. Some studies in 2023 even showed that students who wrote daily by hand could remember new English words more easily than those who only typed.

④ On the other side, some classmates believe a paper diary is simply too slow. Modern life moves fast, and a digital note can be saved, searched, and opened on any device in seconds. Also, a handwritten notebook can be lost or read by others, which makes some students worry about privacy. These are real problems, and they should not be ignored.

⑤ However, these worries have simple answers. A small, cheap notebook costs less than a milk tea, and a little lock or a secret drawer can keep it safe. As for speed — writing a diary is not a race. Its value is exactly that it is slow, personal and **hand-made**, like a small piece of art every night. Even five minutes is enough.

⑥ In my view, a handwritten diary is not an old habit but a small daily **gift to your future self**. Ten years from now, you will not remember every chat message, but the page you wrote on a rainy Wednesday in Grade 8 will still hold your voice, clear and warm. Close the phone for ten minutes. Open a blank page. Write — and meet yourself there.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第①段  
【解析】将其比作"信鸽送信"。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段  
【解析】廉价而有耐心的倾听者。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段末句  
【解析】手写更利于记英语单词。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段  
【解析】速度慢与隐私问题。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段  
【解析】手写日记是送给未来自己的礼物。故选 A。
