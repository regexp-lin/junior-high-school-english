# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Teens Share Their Photos on Social Media?**

① Walk into any middle school classroom during the ten-minute break and you will see students holding phones, tapping screens and laughing at short videos or photos. Many of these pictures come from friends, but a growing number are posted by the students themselves. Selfies at the school gate, photos of test scores, a new haircut, or a group picture after PE class — they all go onto apps in one or two seconds. But should teenagers share so many of their photos online?

② Some people say "yes", and they have reasons. Posting photos helps students **stay in touch** with friends who live far away. It also encourages them to try new things: learning a dance, cooking a dish, or visiting a small museum becomes more interesting if they can show the photos later. A photo on a class group, they say, is just a modern way of saying "Look what I did today!"

③ However, many parents and teachers worry. Once a photo is online, it is **almost impossible to take back**. A silly face from Grade 6 might be found by a university three years later. Worse, some photos give away private information: a school badge, a house number, or the family's car licence plate. In 2025, the China Children's Centre reported that **more than 40%** of teen online-safety problems began with a photo that was "just for fun".

④ Another worry is how photos change the way teens feel about themselves. Pictures on apps are often edited: faces look thinner, skin looks whiter, eyes look bigger. When students compare their own look with these "perfect" versions every day, some start to feel sad about their real face. A healthy teenage year should be full of football, books and friends — not full of filters.

⑤ In my view, teens should not stop using social media, but they should become **smarter sharers**. Before posting, a student can ask three quick questions: *Is this really private? Would I be fine if my teacher saw it? Am I doing it only to get "likes"?* If any answer feels uncomfortable, it is better to keep the photo for family and close friends only. A clever sharer keeps both the fun and the safety of growing up.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer mainly writing about?**

A. How to take better photos with a phone.  
B. Whether teenagers should share many photos online.  
C. Why middle schools should ban all phones.  
D. How to make friends on the Internet.

**2. According to Paragraph 2, what is ONE good thing about sharing photos?**

A. It helps students win prizes at school.  
B. It makes students learn faster in class.  
C. It helps them keep in touch with far-away friends.  
D. It makes their eyes look bigger.

**3. Why is it dangerous to put photos of school badges or car licence plates online?**

A. These things are expensive to replace.  
B. They might share private information with strangers.  
C. Teachers often ask students to hide them.  
D. The colours are hard to see on small phones.

**4. What does the writer think about edited photos?**

A. They are funnier than real photos.  
B. They are the best way to record teenage life.  
C. They can make teens feel bad about their real look.  
D. They should be shared with teachers first.

**5. What is the writer's final suggestion?**

A. Teens should stop using social media completely.  
B. Teens should delete photos after one week.  
C. Teens should think carefully before posting photos.  
D. Teens should only follow their favourite stars.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段最后一句标题性提问 + 全文讨论  
【解析】文章围绕"青少年是否应该在网上分享太多照片"展开讨论，正反两方观点并最终给出建议。A 项只是表面内容；C 项"全面禁止手机"未涉及；D 项"交网友"偏离主题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第 2 句 "Posting photos helps students stay in touch with friends who live far away."  
【解析】明确说分享照片有助于与远方朋友保持联系。A、B、D 均文中未提或张冠李戴。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段第 3–4 句 "give away private information: a school badge, a house number, or the family's car licence plate"  
【解析】此类照片会泄露个人隐私信息。A 项"昂贵"、C 项"老师要求遮挡"、D 项"颜色难看"均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段末两句 "some start to feel sad about their real face... not full of filters"  
【解析】作者认为每日与"完美"修图作比较会让青少年对真实外貌产生负面情绪。A、B、D 均与文意相悖。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑤段 "smarter sharers... three quick questions... think carefully"  
【解析】作者不主张完全不用社交媒体，而是建议发照片前认真思考。A 项过度推断；B、D 均无依据。故选 C。
