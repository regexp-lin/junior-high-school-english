# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Teaching Grandma to Use a Smartphone**

① When Mum gave Grandma a new smartphone on her **seventy-second birthday**, the phone sat on the kitchen table for a whole week, untouched, still in its plastic film. Grandma looked at it the way one might look at a small, sleeping animal — curious, polite, a little afraid. "Too many buttons for an old brain," she finally said on Sunday morning. "Xiao Mu, come. You teach me. Slowly."

② I nodded and sat beside her. I was fifteen. For my whole life, Grandma had taught me — how to tie shoelaces, how to fold dumplings, how to wait quietly at the tram stop without whining. Today, for the first time, the roles had **turned around**. I felt shy and proud at the same time.

③ The first lesson was the **on button**. Grandma pressed it so lightly that nothing happened. "Press more firmly, like pressing a ripe pear," I said. She laughed and pressed again. The screen lit up blue, and her face — I will never forget — **lit up too**. "So it is alive," she whispered.

④ The second lesson was not easy. I tried to teach her the WeChat app. I spoke slowly, used simple Chinese words, drew arrows on a paper next to us. But Grandma kept **tapping too hard**. Sometimes her finger jumped to the wrong icon. Sometimes the whole screen spun around. I had to stop myself from saying, "No, Grandma, **not** that one." Instead, I remembered how patient she had been when I could not pronounce the letter "R" at six years old, and I said, "It is fine. We can go back. Everything here can be **undone**."

⑤ Three weeks passed. Grandma learned to answer video calls, to tap an orange button to turn a voice message into words, and to scan the green code at the small vegetable shop near our house. The shop owner, Auntie Zhao, saw her pay with the phone for the first time and clapped her hands. "Ayi, you are modern now!" Grandma smiled shyly. "I am still the same old pot," she said. "Only the handle is new."

⑥ One Thursday evening in mid-October, something unexpected happened. Grandma's own mother, my great-grandmother, was **95 years old** and lived alone in a small town three hundred kilometres away. Usually, we called her by the old home telephone, but that week her phone was broken, and she had no way to reach us. A neighbour set up a simple tablet for her and sent us a video-call number. Grandma, hands shaking a little, **made the first call herself**. The screen flickered, and there, in a small square, was Great-grandma, dressed neatly in a dark-blue coat, her white hair combed carefully. Two mothers and daughters, **separated by seventy-two years and three hundred kilometres**, looked at each other through a piece of glass. Great-grandma said only six words: "My child, **you look well today**." Grandma's eyes filled, and so did mine.

⑦ When we closed the app, Grandma sat quietly for a long time. Then she turned to me and said, "**I was wrong to be afraid of it.** The phone is only a small road. The place where I arrived — that is where I wanted to go all along." She patted my hand. "Thank you for walking with me."

⑧ Later that night, I watched Grandma wash the dishes with her new habit of humming — something she had not done for years. I understood, for the first time, that teaching is not only a gift we give to children. Sometimes, the best lesson is **a granddaughter sitting down beside a grandmother, with no clock in the room, saying "take your time."** A small phone had carried Grandma across a bridge she had stopped believing she could cross.

---

**注释：** smartphone 智能手机；app 应用程序；undone 撤销；scan 扫码；flicker (屏幕) 闪烁

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the phone sit on the kitchen table for a whole week at first?**

A. The phone was broken.  
B. Grandma thought there were too many buttons for her old brain and was a little afraid.  
C. Mum did not let Grandma use it.  
D. Grandma did not like the colour.

**2. What did Grandma say when she saw the screen light up for the first time?**

A. "It is scary."  
B. "So it is alive."  
C. "Too bright."  
D. "Take it away."

**3. When Grandma kept tapping too hard and making mistakes, what did Xiao Mu remember?**

A. How strict her grandmother had been.  
B. How patient Grandma had been when she could not pronounce the letter "R" at six years old — so she said kindly, "It is fine. Everything here can be undone."  
C. She remembered her own English test.  
D. She remembered her mother's anger.

**4. Why did Grandma's first video call with Great-grandma feel so special?**

A. They had never talked before.  
B. Great-grandma was going to visit.  
C. Grandma made the call herself; two mothers and daughters, separated by 72 years and 300 kilometres, could see each other again because Grandma had learned how.  
D. The phone played a happy song.

**5. What does Xiao Mu finally understand at the end of the story?**

A. Phones are dangerous for old people.  
B. Teaching is only something adults do for children.  
C. Teaching can flow in both directions; the best lesson was a granddaughter sitting patiently with her grandmother, and the phone became a small road that took Grandma across a bridge she had stopped believing she could cross.  
D. Grandma should buy a new phone next year.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "Too many buttons for an old brain"  
【解析】直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "So it is alive"  
【解析】直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "how patient she had been when I could not pronounce the letter 'R' ... Everything here can be undone"  
【解析】回忆童年被耐心对待。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "made the first call herself ... separated by seventy-two years and three hundred kilometres"  
【解析】亲自打通加重了代际意义。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧段 "teaching is not only a gift we give to children ... a small road"  
【解析】作者结尾点题。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。
