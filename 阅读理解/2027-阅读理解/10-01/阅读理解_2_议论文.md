# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle-School Students Be Required to Do Community Volunteer Work?**

① Last month our class began a new discussion after a short **report on the morning news**: in several cities, middle schools are now **requiring every student** to do at least **twenty hours of community volunteer work** each year — cleaning a park, helping at a local library, visiting a nursing home, sorting recycling in the neighbourhood, or supporting a traffic-safety day outside a primary school. Some families welcomed the idea; others were angry, saying that children already have too little time. **Should middle-school volunteer work be a real school requirement, or should it stay completely voluntary?** I believe that a small, reasonable **requirement is a good thing** — as long as schools plan it carefully.

② The first argument for **requiring** it is simple: **volunteer work is part of education itself**, not something extra. Textbooks can teach us what society is; only **doing** can teach us how it feels. A student who spends two Saturdays sorting books in a community library sees, in person, that reading rooms do not keep themselves tidy — real people do. A student who helps an elderly neighbour for an afternoon understands, not in theory but in her own tired feet, why **respect for the elderly** is more than a slogan on a wall. These lessons cannot be learned from an exam paper.

③ Secondly, a clear requirement **makes volunteering fair**. Today, without rules, the students who do community service are usually the ones whose families **already have the time, money or connections** to arrange it — through friends, church groups or paid summer camps. Students from busy working families, or from quieter personalities, often never get a chance. When the school arranges activities for **everyone**, the child of a doctor and the child of a street-cleaner stand side by side in the same park with the same broom. This kind of equal starting line is, in itself, valuable education.

④ A third reason is **the effect on our communities**. Many public libraries, nursing homes, traffic-safety teams and animal shelters are badly understaffed on weekends. Even a small team of students — well-prepared and politely behaved — can make a real difference: a nursing home can run a Saturday-afternoon reading hour, a library can stay open an extra two hours, a primary-school crossing can have four pairs of watchful eyes instead of two. **Our towns are quietly better** when students show up.

⑤ Of course, **those who disagree have reasonable worries**. The most common one is **time**: middle-school students already sleep late and wake early, juggling maths, English, PE, projects and tests. Forcing twenty more hours into a packed year, critics say, will only bring **exhaustion and resentment**. They are right that we must be careful. But twenty hours a year is **roughly twenty-five minutes a week** — less than one TV episode. If schools break it into a few weekend mornings and one school-holiday afternoon, almost every student can manage.

⑥ A second worry is that **required volunteering is not real volunteering**. If the school forces us to go, the argument goes, the "kindness" is not kindness at all — we are just ticking a box. There is something to this worry. But kindness is not only a feeling; it is also a **habit**. Most children do not **want** to brush their teeth at first either; after a few months of being gently required, brushing becomes natural. In the same way, being guided to help others for a few Saturdays can turn something awkward into something the student **chooses** on his own years later.

⑦ A third worry is about **safety and quality**. Sending children to random places can be risky, and badly organised volunteering — "move this pile of rocks, then go home" — teaches nothing. This, however, is not an argument against requirements; it is an argument for **good planning**. Schools should sign formal agreements with partner organisations, provide basic training, arrange safe transportation, and ask students to write a short reflection afterwards. With these steps, community service becomes a real learning programme, not a Sunday-afternoon accident.

⑧ There is also a deeper reason, one easy to forget on busy school mornings: a country is built not only by scientists, engineers and CEOs, but also by **ordinary people who are willing to help a stranger**. A society where every young person, rich or poor, has at least once **swept a park, read to an old person, or guided a small child across the street** is a society with a stronger **inner ground**. In this sense, a modest school requirement is **not a burden**; it is the country quietly teaching its next generation how to stand on its own feet and lend a hand.

⑨ My conclusion is therefore balanced but clear. Middle schools should **require** a small amount of community volunteer work — around **twenty hours a year**, divided into short, safe, well-planned activities. Students who, for serious reasons of health, family or distance, cannot take part in a given activity should be offered **alternatives**. The point is not to force every student to love every task; it is to make sure that, by the time we graduate, **every one of us has looked up from the textbook at least a few times**, and helped the real world we will soon step into.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main view in this essay?**

A. Middle-school students should never do community volunteer work.  
B. Middle-school students should do at least a thousand hours of volunteer work every year.  
C. Middle schools should require a small, reasonable amount (about twenty hours a year) of carefully planned community volunteer work.  
D. Only rich students should be allowed to volunteer.

**2. In Paragraph 3, what advantage of a school requirement does the writer describe?**

A. It lets only the best students volunteer.  
B. It makes volunteering fair: without rules, usually only students from families with time, money or connections get the chance; when the school arranges it for everyone, all students start on an equal line.  
C. It earns the school more money.  
D. It frees teachers from all work.

**3. How does the writer reply to the worry that twenty more hours a year will exhaust students (Paragraph 5)?**

A. He says exhaustion is a good thing.  
B. He calculates that twenty hours a year is roughly twenty-five minutes a week — less than one TV episode — and says that with a few weekend mornings and one holiday afternoon almost every student can manage.  
C. He says students should sleep less.  
D. He says homework should be cancelled instead.

**4. What is the writer's answer to the worry that "required volunteering is not real volunteering" (Paragraph 6)?**

A. He fully agrees and gives up his position.  
B. He points out that kindness is not only a feeling but also a habit — like brushing teeth, being gently required for a few Saturdays can later turn into something students choose on their own.  
C. He says kindness is useless.  
D. He says all school rules are wrong.

**5. In the last two paragraphs, what is the writer's overall conclusion?**

A. Volunteering is a waste of time.  
B. Middle schools should require about twenty hours of well-planned, safe volunteer work a year, with alternatives for students in real difficulty, so that by graduation every student has at least a few times lifted his or her eyes from the textbook and helped the real world.  
C. Only parents should decide, not schools.  
D. Only high-school seniors should volunteer.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨/观点题  
【定位】第①段 "small, reasonable requirement is a good thing" & 第⑨段 "require ... around twenty hours a year"  
【解析】作者支持学校安排合理、少量的志愿要求。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "makes volunteering fair ... child of a doctor and the child of a street-cleaner ... same broom"  
【解析】强制要求可保公平。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "twenty hours a year is roughly twenty-five minutes a week ... less than one TV episode"  
【解析】作者算账说明负担不重。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "kindness is not only a feeling; it is also a habit ... turn something awkward into something the student chooses on his own"  
【解析】善良亦可习惯养成。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨/观点题  
【定位】第⑨段 "around twenty hours a year ... alternatives ... every one of us has looked up from the textbook at least a few times"  
【解析】末段总结核心立场。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。
