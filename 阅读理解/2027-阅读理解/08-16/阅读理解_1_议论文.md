# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle-School Students Have Cooking Classes at School?**

① Last term, our school started a new weekly class called **"Life Kitchen"**, a 45-minute cooking class for Grade 7 and Grade 8. In the beginning, many parents were confused. "Is this really a school subject?" they asked. "Shouldn't students spend that time on Maths or English?" After one full term, the same parents are now asking the opposite question: "Why didn't schools teach us this when we were young?" I believe that **cooking classes belong in middle school**, and they should not be seen as "wasting time".

② Some people still doubt the idea. They say that middle-school life is already full — six to eight subjects, homework, exams, weekend clubs — and adding cooking will only make students more tired. Others worry about safety: knives are sharp, the gas is hot, and young hands are not always careful. A few parents also say that cooking is something "every child will learn later anyway", so there is no need to take class time for it.

③ These worries are understandable, but most of them have answers.

④ First, about the "already full" argument: **learning basic cooking is, in fact, a kind of rest** for busy students. In our Life Kitchen class, I have seen classmates who look tired all morning suddenly become lively when they cut carrots or stir an egg in a hot pan. Short, active, hands-on lessons are very different from reading-heavy subjects. Many students told our teacher that they sleep better on cooking days. So cooking is not extra pressure; it is a healthy change of mood.

⑤ Second, about **safety**: yes, knives and fire can be dangerous, but the question is not whether to meet these tools, but **when**. Children who never touch a real knife before the age of 18 can actually be more careless when they finally do. In the Life Kitchen class, every student is taught the safest way to hold a knife and how to stand away from hot oil. I would rather learn this with a careful teacher now than alone in a small shared flat at 19.

⑥ Third, about "children will learn it later": **many adults never do**. My uncle is thirty-five and can still only boil water and cook instant noodles. He eats take-away almost every night, which is expensive and not good for his health. If he had learned three simple home dishes when he was thirteen, his life today would be quite different.

⑦ Beyond answering the worries, there are clear **positive** reasons to have cooking at school. It teaches students about **science** (why onions make us cry, why meat needs cooking), about **maths** (weighing, measuring, timing), and about **culture** (why we eat dumplings, why spring rolls are golden). It also teaches something harder to measure: **how to care for others**. Cooking a small dish for the whole family, and watching them smile, is one of the quietest but deepest lessons in love.

⑧ Of course, I am not saying every student should become a chef. The goal is not to train professionals, but to help every young person leave middle school with **three or four simple, healthy dishes** they can cook, and with a basic understanding of the food they eat every day.

⑨ So, should cooking be part of middle school? My answer is clear: yes. A school should prepare young people not only for college exams, but also for daily life. And daily life starts, three times a day, at the kitchen table.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main view?**

A. Cooking is not important for middle-school students.  
B. Cooking classes should be part of middle school and should not be seen as wasting time.  
C. Only Grade 9 students should learn cooking.  
D. Students should stop learning Maths and English in order to cook.

**2. How does the writer answer the worry that middle-school life is "already full"?**

A. By saying that students should study less of every other subject.  
B. By saying that hands-on cooking is actually a healthy change of mood and helps students rest, not extra pressure.  
C. By saying that students should do cooking only during summer holidays.  
D. By saying that parents should cancel all weekend clubs.

**3. Why does the writer think it is safer to learn about knives and fire at school?**

A. Because teachers can replace dangerous knives with plastic toys.  
B. Because with a careful teacher, students meet the tools now and learn the right way, instead of meeting them alone for the first time as young adults.  
C. Because students should never cook at home, only at school.  
D. Because hospitals are nearby.

**4. What does the writer want to show by talking about the 35-year-old uncle?**

A. That adults are always better at cooking than children.  
B. That some adults never really learn to cook by themselves, so an early school class could really change a person's daily life.  
C. That take-away food is always cheaper than home cooking.  
D. That cooking is only useful for professional chefs.

**5. What is NOT mentioned as a benefit of Life Kitchen classes?**

A. It teaches basic science behind food.  
B. It teaches weighing, measuring, and timing.  
C. It trains every student to become a restaurant chef.  
D. It teaches students how to care for others through cooking for the family.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "cooking classes belong in middle school"  
【解析】作者立场明确：烹饪课应进入中学课表。A、C、D 均与立场相反或不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "learning basic cooking is, in fact, a kind of rest ... a healthy change of mood"  
【解析】作者回应"学业满"时强调烹饪是动手、是心情切换。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "the question is not whether to meet these tools, but when ... I would rather learn this with a careful teacher now"  
【解析】在有老师指导的情况下早些学比 18 岁后独自面对更安全。A、C、D 均属无中生有或与原文矛盾。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑥段 "My uncle is thirty-five and can still only boil water ... If he had learned three simple home dishes when he was thirteen, his life today would be quite different"  
【解析】用叔叔的例子说明"长大自然会学"并不总是成立，早学影响深远。A 项与原文相反；C 项与作者立场矛盾；D 项以偏概全。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段好处列举 & 第⑧段 "The goal is not to train professionals"  
【解析】作者明确指出不是要培养职业厨师。A、B、D 均为文中列出的好处，C 与第⑧段相反。故选 C。
