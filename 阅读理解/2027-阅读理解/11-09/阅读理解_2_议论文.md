# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle-School Students Take Small Paid Jobs During the Long Holidays?**

① A small neighbourhood bookshop near my home put up a sign last weekend: "**Weekend helper wanted — Grade 8 or Grade 9 student, 3 hours a day, 30 yuan per day**". Within one morning, the sign had been taken down and the helper chosen. The news in our class was loud: some parents called the idea "a wonderful lesson", others called it "too early, too tiring". On the school online group, more than sixty students asked for opinions. My own view is this: **short, safe, and adult-supervised paid work during long holidays is, on balance, a good thing for middle-school students** — as long as three clear lines are kept.

② The first argument for is **understanding money**. Most of us have lived for fourteen years on family money only. Thirty yuan a day sounds small; yet earning thirty yuan by **carrying boxes, sorting books, and standing for three hours** is a very different thirty yuan from a red-envelope at Spring Festival. After two weekends, the student understands, in her body, why her parents are sometimes tired at the dinner table. A **hundred dinner-table lectures** cannot teach this lesson; three quiet Saturdays at the bookshop can.

③ The second argument is **real-world skills**. In a classroom, we talk about people; in a small shop, we talk to them. We learn how to greet a customer, how to say "I am sorry, we are out of this book today", how to count change slowly and clearly, how to clean a shelf without knocking over the display, and — above all — how to **keep a small promise to a stranger** ("I will hold this book for you until 17:00"). These are useful skills that will serve us in university, in future offices, and in any future team we join.

④ A third argument is **character**. A short job teaches the body what the mind already suspects: **good work is mostly quiet and repeated**. Most of the day, the helper is not doing something exciting; she is just standing kindly, replacing a torn price sticker, wiping the door handle, arranging ten same-coloured notebooks in a row. The middle-school student who has felt this quiet rhythm — and still come back the next morning — learns something very important about what real life asks of grown-ups.

⑤ Of course, clear worries exist. First is **safety**. A thirteen- or fourteen-year-old body is not yet a worker's body; lifting heavy boxes, long hours on the feet, late evenings alone in an empty shop — all these can hurt the back, the knees, or the sleep. Second is **schoolwork**. Grade 9 students, in the year before the Senior High Entrance Exam, need their long holiday for rest and planning. Third is **fair pay**. A small paid job can easily turn into a **cheap long job** if no one watches carefully.

⑥ These worries, however, do not kill the idea; they only ask us to **draw three clear lines**. Line one: **no more than 3 hours a day, no more than 5 days a week, no work after 19:00**. Line two: the work must be **physically safe** — no knives, no chemicals, no heavy loads above 5 kg, no climbing ladders. Line three: **parents and the school must both know about it**, in writing. A simple signed note from parents and a short notice in the form-teacher group is enough. Under these three lines, a holiday job stays a small training, not a real job.

⑦ What kind of work, then, is suitable? Good examples include **helping in a small family shop**, assisting at a community library, watering and counting plants at a neighbourhood nursery, writing small posters for a local café, folding towels in a family laundry, or translating short menus into English for a small restaurant. Less suitable work includes anything involving **motor vehicles**, kitchen open fire, alcohol sales, or video-live-streaming for profit — these should wait until at least sixteen.

⑧ Some may ask: "Why not just read more books?" Reading is precious. But a middle-school student reads **twelve years at school**. Two small paid Saturdays in a holiday will not replace reading. They will **add a second window** to the same room. Through the first window (books) we see the grown-up world as an idea; through the second (small honest work) we taste it, just for a few hours, on our own feet. When we return to our classroom in March, the desk feels a little steadier — not because the desk has changed, but because **the person sitting at it has**.

**注释：** helper 帮手；customer 顾客；character 品格；safety 安全；signed note 签字便条

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Students should take full-time jobs during holidays.  
B. Middle-school students should never do paid work until university.  
C. Short, safe, adult-supervised paid work during long holidays is, on balance, a good thing for middle-school students, as long as three clear lines are kept.  
D. Students should only work for their relatives.

**2. Why does Paragraph ② say thirty yuan from work is "very different" from a red-envelope?**

A. Because the money is taxed differently.  
B. Because earning it by carrying boxes, sorting books, and standing for three hours teaches, in the body, why parents are sometimes tired — something a hundred dinner-table lectures cannot teach.  
C. Because the red-envelope money is always less.  
D. Because money from a shop does not count as real money.

**3. Which of the following is NOT included in the writer's "three clear lines" in Paragraph ⑥?**

A. No more than 3 hours a day, no more than 5 days a week, no work after 19:00.  
B. The work must be physically safe: no knives, no chemicals, no heavy loads above 5 kg, no ladder climbing.  
C. Parents and the school must both know about the job in writing.  
D. The student must earn at least 100 yuan a day.

**4. Which of the following works is the writer NOT recommending for middle-school students?**

A. Helping in a small family shop.  
B. Watering and counting plants at a neighbourhood nursery.  
C. Writing small posters for a local café.  
D. Video-live-streaming for profit.

**5. What is the meaning of the "second window" in the last paragraph?**

A. The classroom will have more windows.  
B. Reading books shows the grown-up world as an idea; small honest work during holidays lets the student taste it on her own feet — a second way of seeing the same world, which reading alone cannot give.  
C. Students should buy a new window for their home.  
D. The window is actually a book cover.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "on balance, a good thing ... three clear lines"  
【解析】立论明确。A、B、D 均偏。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第②段 "carrying boxes ... standing for three hours ... hundred dinner-table lectures"  
【解析】体感认知 vs 口头教育。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（否定）  
【定位】第⑥段三条底线  
【解析】A、B、C 均在文中；D"至少 100 元"不是作者立场。故选 D。

**4.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（否定）  
【定位】第⑦段"Less suitable work ... video-live-streaming for profit"  
【解析】A、B、C 均为好例。故选 D。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段比喻 "first window (books) ... second (small honest work)"  
【解析】两扇窗互补。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。
