# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Lost Key on the Garden Wall**

① Last Sunday afternoon, my mother asked me to walk our neighbour Mrs. Li's small garden while she went to the hospital to see a friend. Mrs. Li, who is seventy-two, has a tiny back garden full of tomato plants and roses. She handed me a silver key on a **red string** and said, "Please lock the back gate when you leave. And please don't lose this key — it's the only one."

② The garden was warm and quiet. I watered the plants, pulled a few small weeds, and even picked up a thin yellow leaf that a wind had blown in. I felt proud, like a small gardener. At 4 p.m., I closed the back gate and turned the key carefully. Then I put the red string over my neck, patted my chest, and walked home.

③ Halfway down the street, I stopped like a stone. My neck felt light — too light. The red string was gone. **My heart jumped into my throat.** I ran back to the gate, searching the ground, the grass, and even the inside of my shoes. Nothing. The sky was getting grey. I imagined Mrs. Li coming home at night, standing outside her gate, unable to get in.

④ For a few minutes I felt like crying. Then I told myself, "Crying does not find keys." I started walking backwards along my path, eyes on every stone and every corner. At the small garden wall near the park, I saw a tiny flash of red in a crack between two bricks. It was the string, caught on a rough piece of stone! I had leaned against the wall ten minutes earlier to tie my shoes.

⑤ I pulled the string out slowly — and the key was still there. I almost shouted with joy. When I returned to Mrs. Li's flat that evening, I told her the whole story, including the losing part. She laughed, patted my shoulder, and said, "A key on a wall is nothing. A boy who comes back to find it — that is a real gardener." She gave me a warm tomato from her shelf and taught me how to make the string tighter.

⑥ That night, before sleep, I tied the red string into a small **knot** around the key and placed it back in Mrs. Li's hand. I learned two things in one afternoon: first, a small piece of string can carry a very large responsibility; and second, honesty is much lighter to carry than a lost key.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer visit Mrs. Li's garden?**

A. To meet his own friends.  
B. To water the plants while Mrs. Li was away.  
C. To buy tomatoes for dinner.  
D. To play football with other children.

**2. What happened halfway down the street?**

A. The writer saw a thief.  
B. The writer realised the key and string were gone from his neck.  
C. Mrs. Li called him by phone.  
D. It began to snow.

**3. Where did the writer finally find the red string?**

A. Inside his shoe.  
B. In a tomato plant.  
C. In a crack between two bricks on a garden wall.  
D. At the hospital.

**4. How did Mrs. Li react when she heard the whole story?**

A. She was angry and shouted at him.  
B. She laughed and said he was a real gardener.  
C. She asked him to pay for the key.  
D. She refused to see him again.

**5. What is the main lesson the writer learned?**

A. Gardening is very easy.  
B. Honesty and careful responsibility matter more than the key itself.  
C. Keys should never leave their locks.  
D. Tomatoes can open any gate.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段第1-2句 "my mother asked me to walk our neighbour Mrs. Li's small garden..."  
【解析】原文说作者替邻居照看小菜园。A 见朋友、C 买西红柿、D 踢球均与原文不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段第2-3句 "My neck felt light... The red string was gone."  
【解析】走到半路发现钥匙和红绳不在脖子上。A 小偷、C 接电话、D 下雪均无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段末句 "in a crack between two bricks"  
【解析】红绳卡在园墙两块砖的缝隙里。A/B/D 与原文不符。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段第3句 "She laughed, patted my shoulder, and said, 'A key on a wall is nothing. A boy who comes back to find it — that is a real gardener.'"  
【解析】Mrs. Li 笑着称赞他是真正的园丁。A 生气、C 要求赔钱、D 不再相见都与原文相反。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥段末句 "honesty is much lighter to carry than a lost key"  
【解析】文末表明作者领悟到责任与诚实的意义。A 说园艺很容易、C 钥匙不能离锁、D 番茄万能都属无稽。故选 B。
