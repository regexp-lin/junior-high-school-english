# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Silver Coin My Grandfather Gave Me**

① When I was ten, my grandfather called me into his small bedroom. He opened a wooden drawer and took out a little red cloth bag. Inside was a silver coin, round, grey-white, and a little heavier than a one-yuan coin. "Hu Hu," he said, "this was given to me by my own father when I was ten. Now I am too old, and your father thinks the time has come." He placed the cool coin in my hand. "Keep it. But remember — a coin is nothing, if you do not know the story behind it."

② That evening, Grandfather told me the story. It was 1942. His village in Anhui was poor, and a bad flood had just washed away the rice fields. A kind old teacher from the next village walked three days to our village to teach the children for free. He had no place to stay, so Grandfather's father let the teacher share the small wooden bed. Before the teacher left, he gave my great-grandfather this silver coin, saying, "I have nothing else. Please use it only for the children."

③ "My father never spent the coin," Grandfather said. "He passed it to me when I was ten. I worked very hard at school, and the coin was always at the bottom of my pocket, cool against my leg. On difficult days, I would touch it and think of the teacher I never saw." I looked at the coin. In the lamp light, I could see tiny scratches on its surface, like very old handwriting.

④ The next Monday at school, our English test was very hard. Three minutes before the bell, I still had not finished. My heart began to jump. I reached into my pocket for a tissue, and my fingers touched something round and cool — the silver coin. For one second, I could see my grandfather's wooden drawer, the kind old teacher walking three days through the mud, and the coin lying at the bottom of a boy's pocket in 1942. My hand suddenly stopped shaking. I turned back to the paper and finished the last two questions with a steady pen.

⑤ At home that evening, I told Grandfather what had happened. He smiled, the same small smile I had seen so many times. "So," he said quietly, "the coin is still working. It does not buy anything in the shops. But it gives courage, and that may be the most useful thing it can do." He patted my shoulder.

⑥ I have now kept the coin for three years. Sometimes I take it out and look at the tiny scratches, trying to read them like a letter. On the surface, a silver coin is only a piece of metal. To me, however, it is a bridge — from a 1942 village, through my great-grandfather's hands and my grandfather's pocket, all the way to a busy middle school classroom of today. One day, I hope, I will give it to my own child, and the story will grow **a little longer**.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How old was the writer when his grandfather gave him the silver coin?**

A. Seven.  
B. Ten.  
C. Twelve.  
D. Fifteen.

**2. Why did the old teacher give the silver coin to the writer's great-grandfather?**

A. To pay for food every day.  
B. Because the family let him share their small wooden bed, and the teacher wanted to thank them with the only thing he had, asking that it be used for the children.  
C. Because the great-grandfather sold the coin to him.  
D. Because the teacher lost it by accident.

**3. What did the writer feel in the English test when he touched the coin?**

A. He wanted to buy something in a shop.  
B. He imagined the kind old teacher walking three days through the mud, and his hand suddenly stopped shaking.  
C. He wanted to leave the classroom.  
D. He was afraid of the silver.

**4. What does Grandfather mean by "the coin is still working"?**

A. The coin can buy new things in modern shops.  
B. The coin still gives courage, even though it does not buy anything.  
C. The coin is still hot from the sun.  
D. The coin can open a bank door.

**5. What does the coin mean to the writer now?**

A. A piece of metal without any meaning.  
B. A bridge that connects a 1942 village, his great-grandfather, his grandfather and his own life.  
C. A toy for small children.  
D. A secret that should never be told.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "When I was ten..."  
【解析】十岁时得到硬币。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Grandfather's father let the teacher share the small wooden bed... I have nothing else. Please use it only for the children."  
【解析】感谢借宿并希望用于孩子。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "I could see... the kind old teacher walking three days through the mud... My hand suddenly stopped shaking."  
【解析】回忆故事给作者勇气。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义猜测题／细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "It does not buy anything in the shops. But it gives courage..."  
【解析】硬币仍给人勇气。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题／主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段 "it is a bridge — from a 1942 village..."  
【解析】连接三代的精神之桥。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
