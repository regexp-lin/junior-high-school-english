# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Do Deserts Get Very Cold at Night?**

① When we think of a desert, a picture usually appears in our mind: yellow sand, strong sunlight and a thermometer showing 40°C. This picture is correct — but only for the **daytime**. The true surprise of many deserts, like the Gobi in north China or the Sahara in north Africa, is that after sunset the air cools so quickly that by midnight it can feel like winter. Travellers who wear only a T-shirt at noon sometimes need a thick jacket before bed. Why does a desert change its temperature so strongly in just a few hours?

② The first reason is **water**. Water has a special ability: it heats up slowly during the day and gives its heat back slowly at night. Big lakes, forests and oceans hold a lot of water, so cities by the sea, such as Qingdao or Sanya, have mild nights. A desert, however, is very dry. The sand, the rocks and the thin air carry little water. Without this natural "battery" of warmth, the heat of the day escapes into the sky almost as soon as the sun goes down.

③ The second reason is the **sky**. In a desert, clouds are rare and the air is unusually clean and dry. During the day, this clear sky lets strong sunlight hit the sand without any blocking layer. At night, the same clear sky means that heat flies straight up into space with nothing to stop it. Scientists call this heat loss by radiation. On a cloudy night, even in winter, cities often feel warmer because clouds act like a soft blanket. A desert has no such blanket.

④ The third reason is **sand** itself. Sand is a surprisingly poor keeper of heat. The top layer gets very hot within an hour of sunrise, but below a few centimetres, the sand stays much cooler. When the sun sets, the hot top layer quickly gives up its heat and becomes cold, while the cool layer below cannot send much warmth upward. Because sand does not hold heat deep inside, it cannot "warm" the desert through the night.

⑤ These three reasons together explain an amazing number: in parts of the Sahara, a difference of more than **30°C** can appear between 2 p.m. and 4 a.m. Travellers who cross the desert must prepare for both extremes — a light hat and sunscreen for midday, a warm coat and a hot drink for midnight.

⑥ The cold desert night is not just a strange fact. It teaches us something about our planet. Places rich in water and clouds — like the river valleys where most people live — quietly save us from daily temperature "shocks". Without that natural gift, our own cities would also feel burning by day and freezing by night. The desert, in its silent way, reminds us how important **water** is for the safe, gentle weather we enjoy.

**1. What is the main question the passage tries to answer?**

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文围绕 "Why does a desert change its temperature so strongly..."  
【解析】主问题为沙漠夜晚变冷。A/C/D 均非主题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "it heats up slowly during the day and gives its heat back slowly at night."  
【解析】水充当热量缓冲。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "clouds act like a soft blanket."  
【解析】云像一层被子。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "top layer quickly gives up its heat... cannot 'warm' the desert through the night."  
【解析】沙子不深存热量。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题／推理判断题  
【定位】第⑥段 "how important water is for the safe, gentle weather we enjoy."  
【解析】水保护城市免受温差冲击。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
