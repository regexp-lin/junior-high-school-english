As the year closes, screen-time at home rises, rooms are darker, and revision hours are long. The school infirmary's November check-up showed that, of **1,216 students examined**, **721 (59%)** had **eye dryness** and **188 (15%)** had **new or worsening myopia** since June. Vision-Health Week is our calm response. One short week, one small set of daily habits, one small gift to a pair of eyes you will need for the next seventy years.

## 👀 The "20 · 20 · 20" Rule We Are Trying This Week

Every **20 minutes** of close work (reading, writing, screen, homework), look at something at least **20 feet away (about 6 metres)** for **at least 20 seconds**.

- This simple rule, originally suggested by American optometrists in the 2010s, is **now backed by 11 large studies**, the latest in 2024 (*The Lancet Eye Health*), which found **22% less end-of-day eye fatigue** in teenagers who used it for four weeks.  
- Each classroom will have **one small green sticker** on its back wall at exactly 6 metres from the blackboard — look there every 20 minutes.  
- A **gentle bell** will sound once every 20 minutes during study periods to remind us, just above whisper volume.

## 🏥 Free Check-Up Station (Infirmary, Room 1-103)

- **Open**: 14 – 18 Dec, 16:10 – 17:30 (Mon–Fri).  
- **What is checked**: distance vision, near vision, **axial length** (the key number for myopia), eye-pressure, tear-film test.  
- **Takes about 8 minutes per student.**  
- Booking via school mini-app → Health → Vision. **Up to 30 slots per day.**  
- Results are printed as a small card. **Numbers are sent only to you and (if you tick "yes") to your parents** — never to the class.  
- **Red flag numbers** (e.g. fast axial-length growth) will be followed up with a short letter to guardians and a suggested visit to the district eye hospital.

## 🪟 Classroom Adjustments for the Week

- Lamps in all classrooms checked by the electrician on **Sun 13 Dec**; any flicker will be fixed.  
- Blackboards cleaned fully at the end of each day — **no "ghost chalk" in the morning glare**.  
- Curtains **half-open on sunny mornings**, closed on very bright afternoons between 14:00 – 15:00 if glare reaches the second half of the room.  
- **Desk-lamp recommendation**: warm white, ≥ 500 lux, **left side** of the book if right-handed, **right side** if left-handed.  
- **Paper, not only screen**: Teachers, where possible, will print a paper version of slides this week to reduce total screen time by about **40 minutes a day per student**.

---

## ✅ Five Small Home Rules (Suggested, Not Enforced)

1. Keep your **reading distance** at about **35 cm** (roughly the length of your forearm).  
2. Have **one overhead light on** when reading at night, not only the desk lamp — two light sources reduce contrast strain.  
3. When a screen is used for homework, try **20-min bursts with 20-sec far-looks**.  
4. Before sleep, **close your eyes for 90 seconds**, palms gently covering them — the so-called "palming" rest.  
5. On weekends, try to spend **at least 1 hour outdoors** — outdoor natural light slows myopia growth by **around 30%** in studies of children aged 10–14.

## ❗ Important Notes

- The bell sound is **low-volume** and will not ring during tests or exams.  
- Students wearing **glasses** or **contact lenses** may join all activities; no need to remove them.  
- Students who already have a doctor's treatment plan (e.g. **orthokeratology lenses / low-dose atropine**) should **follow their own doctor's instructions first**; Vision Week is a complement, not a replacement.  
- **This is not a screen-ban week.** Tablets used for homework are fine. We are only asking for small pauses.

The kindness is yours to give."
