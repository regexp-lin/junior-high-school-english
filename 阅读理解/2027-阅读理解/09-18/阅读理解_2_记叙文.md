# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Silent New Girl at Desk 17**

① She came into our class on a quiet Wednesday afternoon in mid-September. Her name was **An Qi**, our teacher said, and she had just moved to our city from a small town in the south. An Qi was tall and very thin, with her hair tied back in a simple ponytail. She wore a school uniform that was **one size too big**, and when Mr. Lin asked her to say a short hello, she only gave a tiny nod and looked at the floor. Her seat was **Desk 17** — right next to mine.

② For the first two weeks she almost never spoke. In class she answered only when asked, and always in a voice so quiet that classmates in the last row had to **lean forward**. During the ten-minute break she sat at Desk 17 and carefully wrote in a **small green notebook**. Some of my classmates whispered behind her back: "Maybe she is shy." "Maybe she thinks she is too good for us." "Maybe she just doesn't like us." I did not join the whispering, but I did not help her either. I was, to be honest, a little afraid of the silence that sat around her desk like a **quiet wall**.

③ Then one rainy Thursday, after the first period of the day, I noticed something on the floor by her seat. It was a small, folded **paper crane**, made from a page of her homework. I picked it up and, without really thinking, put it gently back on her desk. She looked up sharply. For a moment I thought she was angry. Then she opened the drawer, took out **another paper crane**, this one a soft orange, and pushed it quietly across the wooden desk towards me. She did not smile, but her eyes looked different — a little less far away.

④ That afternoon was P.E. class. Our teacher decided we would play **three-against-three basketball**. An Qi had never played before. She stood on the edge of the court, holding her sleeves and watching. I waved at her. "Come," I said, "you can be on our team — we won't laugh." Our team lost seven rounds out of eight, mostly because An Qi kept passing the ball wrongly. But with each mistake, she laughed — **a real, short laugh** — the first sound from her we had heard in weeks. After class, walking back to the classroom, she suddenly said, in a tiny clear voice, "My father had a very bad accident in July. That is why we moved. I did not want people to ask about it."

⑤ I stopped walking. I did not know what to say. So I only asked quietly, "Is he a little better now?" She nodded. "A little." Then, just as quietly, "The doctor says he will walk again next year." I did not tell her it would be fine. I did not tell her not to worry. I only said, "I'm glad." Later that evening I told my mother the whole story. My mother did something surprising: she took out a small piece of paper and **folded a paper crane with me**. "Give this to her tomorrow," she said. "Some children find it easier to start with a small thing than a big word."

⑥ The next morning I put the paper crane on Desk 17 before An Qi arrived. When she came in, she looked at it, looked at me, and — for the first time — **smiled a full smile**. At break, she took out her small green notebook and asked me something that surprised me: "Can you help me practise talking to people?" I was not sure I knew how, but I said yes. We started small: saying "good morning" to the canteen lady; asking a senior for directions; reading aloud for one minute each day from an English book. The green notebook slowly filled up with short lines like "*Today I said thank you three times*" and "*Today I laughed twice in class*".

⑦ By **early October**, our classmates noticed the change. An Qi began to answer questions in her normal voice. She joined the class choir. She still sat at Desk 17, and I still sat beside her. Sometimes, in the middle of a noisy afternoon, she would lift her head from the desk and say, quite clearly, "Lin Yue, thank you for that crane." I would look at her and say nothing special — only nod the way one nods at an old friend. Life had not become perfect for her; her father was still in hospital. But the silence around Desk 17 had softened, and I had quietly learned the first real lesson of that autumn: **kindness does not need a loud voice; sometimes it is only a small paper crane, a short wave on a basketball court, or a willingness to simply wait.**

---

**注释：** ponytail 马尾；paper crane 纸鹤；three-against-three 三对三；canteen 食堂；choir 合唱团

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did An Qi behave during her first two weeks in the class?**

A. She talked loudly and tried to make many new friends quickly.  
B. She hardly spoke, answered only when asked, and quietly wrote in a small green notebook during breaks.  
C. She refused to come to school at all.  
D. She cried every class.

**2. What small "first bridge" was built between the writer and An Qi on the rainy Thursday?**

A. The writer gave An Qi a new schoolbag.  
B. The writer picked up a folded paper crane from the floor and put it back on her desk; An Qi pushed a second orange crane back across the desk.  
C. They shouted at each other in class.  
D. They exchanged math homework.

**3. Why had An Qi and her family moved to the new city?**

A. Because her father wanted a bigger house.  
B. Because her father had a very bad accident in July, and the move was connected to that.  
C. Because she won a scholarship.  
D. Because she hated her old school.

**4. What did the writer's mother do to help An Qi?**

A. She phoned An Qi every night.  
B. She folded a paper crane together with the writer and suggested giving it to An Qi, saying "some children find it easier to start with a small thing than a big word".  
C. She visited An Qi's father in hospital.  
D. She bought An Qi a new uniform.

**5. What is the main message of the last paragraph?**

A. Being loud is the best way to help new classmates.  
B. Kindness does not need a loud voice — sometimes it is a small paper crane, a short wave in P.E., or simply being willing to wait for someone to speak.  
C. Every silent student is sad and should be forced to talk.  
D. Friendship is only possible when everyone shares the same hobby.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "almost never spoke ... always in a voice so quiet ... wrote in a small green notebook"  
【解析】头两周安静少言，课间在绿色笔记本上写字。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "a small, folded paper crane ... I picked it up ... pushed a second orange crane"  
【解析】纸鹤的拾起与互递成为第一次默默交流。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "My father had a very bad accident in July. That is why we moved"  
【解析】父亲 7 月出事故，由此搬家。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "folded a paper crane with me ... 'easier to start with a small thing than a big word'"  
【解析】妈妈一起折纸鹤并给出建议。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨/观点题  
【定位】第⑦段 "kindness does not need a loud voice ... a small paper crane, a short wave ... willingness to simply wait"  
【解析】末段升华：善意无需喧哗，耐心与小事即可。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。
