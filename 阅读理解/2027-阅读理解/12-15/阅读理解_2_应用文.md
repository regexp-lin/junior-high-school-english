# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Year-End Book-Swap Fair 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Library + Chinese Department**  
> **Year-End Book-Swap Fair 2027** — *"Let Your Good Books Keep Walking This Winter."*  
> **Date**: **Friday, 26 December 2027**  
> **Time**: **13:00 – 17:00** (four hours, one afternoon)  
> **Venue**: **Lihua Central Library (2F) + Sunlight Corridor (3F)**  
> **Cost**: **Zero money. Only books.**

---

① Every student in our school owns, on average, **27 books**. Every student, in her three middle-school years, reads maybe **9 of them** a second time. The rest sit on a small shelf, quiet and loyal, growing a small layer of winter dust. On the last Friday of term, we invite you to give those books a **second living year**. The Year-End Book-Swap Fair uses no money at all: you bring a book you have finished; you take a book somebody else has finished. One for one. **812 students, one small library, hundreds of books walking in quiet circles.** Nothing is bought. Nothing is sold. Books only walk.

---

## 📚 How the Swap Works (the only rule you must remember)

- **One-for-one**: for every book you bring in, you may take one book away. No exceptions.  
- **The book you bring** must be: in readable condition (no torn pages, no bad smell, no marks that make reading impossible), written in Chinese or English, appropriate for Grades 7–9 (no inappropriate adult content).  
- **The book you take** must be one from today's swap tables. Library books are **not** part of the swap.  
- **You may bring up to 5 books per student per afternoon** (and therefore take up to 5 away).

---

## 🏷️ What to Do When You Arrive

| Step | Where | What |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Book-Check Desk (2F entrance) | Hand in your books. A student librarian checks each book for condition and genre, and gives you one **green token per accepted book**. |
| 2 | Token Window (2F) | Walk with your green tokens to the Swap Tables. |
| 3 | Swap Tables (2F + 3F) | Browse. Pick a book you want. Hand in a token. Keep the book. |
| 4 | Exit Desk (2F exit) | A second student librarian writes your name and your new book's title in the small Swap Ledger. This is kept for one year in case of mistakes. |

---

## 🗂️ The Six Swap-Table Zones

| Zone | Genre | Where |
|---|---|---|
| **Zone A** | Chinese literature (fiction, essays, poetry) | 2F, east window |
| **Zone B** | English graded readers (Levels 2–4) | 2F, centre |
| **Zone C** | Science & popular knowledge (astronomy, biology, history) | 2F, west window |
| **Zone D** | History & biography (Chinese and world) | 3F Sunlight Corridor, north |
| **Zone E** | Picture & comic books (for rest-reading, not exchange bonus) | 3F Sunlight Corridor, south |
| **Zone F** | Old textbooks & workbooks (Grades 7–8, reference only) | 3F staircase corner |

- You may cross zones freely.  
- One token works in any zone.  
- **If a book has no taker by 16:30**, it moves to the "**Share-for-Free Shelf**" near the exit, where any student may take it without a token.

---

## 🌱 Care of Each Book

Before the swap, please do three small, kind things to the book you bring:

1. **Erase your pencil notes** — the next reader deserves a clean first look.  
2. **Write a small kind line** on the back inside cover, e.g. *"This book made me laugh on the bus home. I hope it makes you smile too. — A friend in Grade 7, Dec 2027."*  
3. **Put a clean sticky note** on the cover if any page has a tiny tear; do not hide small damage.

These three small acts turn the swap into something quietly human: every book carries, to its next home, a small whispered hello from a stranger who loved it first.

## 📖 Book-Recommendation Corner (14:30 – 15:00)

Three Grade-9 students, chosen by draw, each speak for three minutes on one book that changed them this year. This year's speakers and books:

| Speaker | Grade | Book | Book language |
|---|---|---|---|
| Liang Yichen | 9A | *The Little Prince* (《小王子》) | Chinese |
| Zhao Xinyue | 9C | *Charlotte's Web* | English |
| Sun Jialuo | 9D | *活着* (*To Live*, abridged school edition) | Chinese |

Students are welcome to listen, ask one question each, and then continue swapping. **No applause signs, no filming, no scoring.**

---

## 🚫 Quiet Forbidden Items (please do not bring)

- Textbooks currently in use this term (please keep for your own study).  
- Any book with torn cover or missing pages.  
- Any book with strong smell, wet marks, or yellow food stains.  
- Any book marked *"私人日记"* (private diary) or similar personal writing.  
- Any adult novel or any comic with violent / inappropriate content for middle school.

Books that do not pass the Book-Check Desk are **gently returned**; the student may still browse the swap tables but cannot take a book without a token.

---

## 🫖 Winter Kindness Table (3F Sunlight Corridor)

- **Free warm ginger tea** for all swappers (small paper cup, 100 ml, no sugar).  
- **Free hand-warmer cards** for any student who is visibly cold.  
- A small **"Rest-Only Chair"** area: if you do not want to swap, just come to read for 20 minutes.  
- **No phones** please on the Kindness Table; reading and quiet only.

---

## ❗ Important Gentle Reminders

- **The fair is totally voluntary.** No student is required to come. No class is brought together.  
- **Nobody is ranked by the number of books she brings or takes.** A student who brings one and takes one is as welcome as a student who brings five.  
- **A book that finds no taker is not a "bad" book.** It may simply be waiting for another year. The Share-for-Free Shelf takes care of it.  
- **You may give a book to a specific friend.** Write *"for Xia Lin, 7B"* on a sticky note on the cover; the Book-Check Desk will keep it and hand it to her when she arrives.  
- **Phones** may be used only to take **one** photo of your new book and nothing else; no video, no short-video posting.

---

## 💬 A Small Word from the Library

> "A book is, perhaps, the only object in this school that is richer for being second-hand. Every small pencil mark, every carefully folded page-corner, every kind line written in the back cover, is a small quiet trace of a reader who walked this way before. On 26 December, for four quiet hours, we invite **812 students** and **one small library** to let our loved books keep walking this winter. One for one. **No money. Only books. Only kindness.** Let us show that a school, at the end of a hard term, still knows how to share."  
> — *Head Librarian Ms Yu & the Chinese Department, 15 December 2027*
