# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Helping a Lost Tourist Find the Panda Base**

① On the first Saturday after the Spring Festival, I went to **Chengdu East Railway Station** with my father. He had to pick up some documents, and I brought my English notebook to kill the waiting time. Little did I know that a short afternoon would turn into my first real **"English class outside school"**.

② I was sitting by the window, practising vocabulary, when a tall, tired-looking man in a grey coat stopped beside me. He held a map with a big red circle on it and asked nervously, "Excuse me... do you speak English?" I froze for two seconds. Then I remembered what my teacher Ms. Chen always said: "A little English is better than none." I nodded and smiled.

③ The man was from **New Zealand**. He had just arrived by high-speed rail and wanted to visit the **Giant Panda Base**, but he could not understand the ticket machine, and his phone had no Chinese signal. He pointed at the red circle on his paper map and asked, "Am I close?" I looked carefully. The circle was not even on the right map — it showed an old bus stop that had been moved in 2024.

④ I took a deep breath. "Please follow me," I said in slow English. I walked him to the **underground passage** and pointed at a clear blue sign: **Metro Line 3 → Panda Avenue Station**. Then I wrote on the back of his map in neat letters: *"Metro Line 3, 6 stops, then Bus 198, 3 stops, Panda Base."* I used my own phone to scan and pay for his metro ticket, and he promised to send the money back by the hotel WeChat later.

⑤ Before he walked down the stairs, he stopped and shook my hand. "Young man," he said, "I have travelled to many countries, but today you are the friendliest person I have met in China." His eyes were bright. I felt my face grow warm, but my heart was full of a quiet, sunny feeling.

⑥ That evening at dinner, Dad asked why I was smiling at my rice bowl. I told him the whole story. He laughed and said, "Your English did not get a high score today, son, but it earned something better: a new friend in a faraway country." I opened my notebook before bed and wrote one line in English: **"Small help, bigger world."** Outside the window, the city lights shone quietly, and I thought — maybe this is the real reason we learn English after all.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where did the writer meet the lost tourist?**

A. At Chengdu Airport.  
B. At Chengdu East Railway Station.  
C. At the Giant Panda Base.  
D. At a hotel near the school.

**2. Why did the tourist stop to ask the writer for help?**

A. He was hungry.  
B. He could not understand the ticket machine and his phone had no Chinese signal.  
C. He had lost his wallet.  
D. He wanted to practise Chinese.

**3. What did the writer do to help the tourist?**

A. Gave him 500 yuan.  
B. Carried his suitcase to the hotel.  
C. Wrote clear directions and scanned a metro ticket for him.  
D. Called the police.

**4. What did the tourist say before leaving?**

A. "You are the friendliest person I have met in China today."  
B. "You need more English lessons."  
C. "Goodbye forever."  
D. "I will never come back to China."

**5. What is the main lesson the writer learned?**

A. English is only useful for passing exams.  
B. Small help can connect you to a bigger world.  
C. Tourists always ask too many questions.  
D. High-speed rail is more important than English.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段  
【解析】成都东站。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段  
【解析】看不懂售票机且手机无信号。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段  
【解析】写清方向并替他扫码买地铁票。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段  
【解析】夸中国最友好的人。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段末句 "Small help, bigger world"  
【解析】小帮助连接大世界。故选 B。
