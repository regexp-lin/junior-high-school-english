# 阅读理解（说明文） **How Bees Find Flowers**

① On a warm spring afternoon, you may notice a bee flying straight from one flower to another, never wasting a second. Its short trip from the hive to a flower and back may look simple, but behind it lies a small miracle of nature. How can a tiny bee, with a brain no larger than a grain of rice, know exactly where the sweetest flowers are?

② First, bees use **colour**. Their eyes can see many colours that humans can see, as well as **ultra-violet light** that we cannot. Many flowers have special ultra-violet patterns, which act like bright arrows pointing to the centre of the flower, where the nectar is. From a few metres away, a bee may read these arrows as easily as we read a road sign.

③ Second, bees use **smell**. The antennae on their heads can pick up the soft smell of a flower from surprisingly far away. Some bees can even tell the difference between two very close flowers — for example, a rose that opened this morning and one that opened yesterday. The fresher flower often gives more sugar, so the bee chooses it first.

④ But a bee does not search all by itself. Back in the hive, it shares information with its sisters through a clever **"waggle dance."** When a worker bee finds a rich garden, it flies home and dances in a figure-eight on the wall of the hive. The angle of the dance tells the direction of the flowers to the sun. The length of the dance tells the distance. In this way, other bees fly out along a perfect map — all drawn in the air by a single pair of wings.

⑤ Scientists also believe that bees use the **sun** as a compass and even remember good flowers for **several days**. If clouds cover the sun, the bee can still tell its direction by the pattern of light in the sky. Some bees even notice tiny electric fields around flowers and use this signal to check whether another bee has just taken the nectar.

⑥ When we eat honey on our bread in the morning, perhaps we should say a quiet "thank you" to these small hard-working creatures. Each golden spoonful is the result of colour, smell, dance, sun and a great deal of patient flight. The next time a bee flies across your window, look again — it is carrying the wisdom of a whole kingdom on its wings.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main idea of Paragraph 2?**

A. Bees have very weak eyes.  
B. Bees use colour and ultra-violet patterns to find flowers.  
C. Bees can only see black and white.  
D. Human eyes are better than bee eyes.

**2. According to Paragraph 3, what can some bees do?**

A. Tell the difference between two very close flowers.  
B. Count the petals of a flower.  
C. Make flowers bloom earlier.  
D. Change the colour of flowers.

**3. What does the "waggle dance" tell other bees?**

A. Only the colour of the flowers.  
B. Only the smell of the flowers.  
C. The direction and distance of the flowers.  
D. How many days the flowers will last.

**4. What can bees do when clouds cover the sun?**

A. They go home and sleep.  
B. They still tell direction by the pattern of light.  
C. They lose their way completely.  
D. They start to fight each other.

**5. What is the writer's main purpose in this passage?**

A. To teach us how to make honey.  
B. To complain about insects in the city.  
C. To show us how clever and hard-working bees are.  
D. To warn us that bees are dangerous.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】段落主旨题  
【定位】第②段 "bees use colour... ultra-violet patterns... like bright arrows"  
【解析】第二段介绍颜色与紫外线图案帮蜜蜂定位。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Some bees can even tell the difference between two very close flowers"  
【解析】某些蜜蜂能分辨两朵相近花朵。B、C、D 均无依据。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "The angle... tells the direction... The length... tells the distance."  
【解析】摆尾舞传递方向与距离。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "tell its direction by the pattern of light in the sky"  
【解析】云遮日时蜜蜂靠天空光线辨向。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段 "the wisdom of a whole kingdom on its wings"  
【解析】全文意在展示蜜蜂的智慧与勤劳。A、B、D 均偏题。故选 C。
