# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Old Man at the Silent Bus Stop**

① It was a cold Tuesday in late November, and the sky had that grey, heavy look that means snow is near. I had just finished my piano lesson and was walking to the bus stop near Nanhu Park, a thick scarf pulled up to my nose. The stop is usually busy after school, but that afternoon it was strangely quiet. Only one other person was there — a small, thin old man in a brown coat, sitting very still on the metal bench.

② I glanced at the board. My bus, the 17, was due in twelve minutes. I took out my phone and looked down. When I looked up again, I noticed that the old man had not moved at all. He was staring at the road, but his eyes were not following any car. I suddenly felt something was wrong, but I was a little shy, so I did not speak.

③ Five minutes passed. Then, slowly, the old man opened his mouth and whispered something. I could not hear him clearly. "Sorry?" I said, stepping closer. He looked up at me, and his eyes were wet. "I think… I think I can't remember where I live," he said in a small voice. My heart gave a soft, surprised jump.

④ His coat had no name tag inside. His pockets held only a handkerchief, half a packet of biscuits, and a small green button. No phone, no wallet, no key. He said his name was Mr Li. He remembered that he had walked out after lunch to buy soy sauce. He remembered his daughter's face. But when I asked where his home was, he closed his eyes and shook his head. "It was near a tall white tree," he said. "That is all."

⑤ For a moment I did not know what to do. Then I remembered a poster on the wall of our classroom — the one about helping lost elderly people with memory problems. **Step 1: stay calm. Step 2: keep the person warm. Step 3: call the community service line.** I took off my scarf and put it around him. He looked surprised, as if no one had given him anything for a long time, and he said, "Thank you, little sister." I smiled, although my own neck began to feel the cold.

⑥ I dialled 12349, the community help number. A calm woman asked a few short questions and told me to stay where I was. Fifteen minutes later, a small white car arrived, and a young officer in a bright jacket stepped out. He spoke to Mr Li gently, looked at the biscuits and the button, and smiled. "I think I know this uncle," he said. "We had a report from his daughter an hour ago — she couldn't find him."

⑦ Before Mr Li got into the car, he stopped, turned, and pressed the small green button from his pocket into my hand. "My wife sewed this on my coat forty years ago," he said softly. "She would want you to have it." I tried to say no, but he had already gone. I stood at the stop, holding the button, my eyes suddenly hot.

⑧ I missed three buses that afternoon. When I finally got home, the sky was dark and a few snowflakes had begun to fall. My mother asked why I was late, and I showed her the small green button on my palm. She did not say anything for a moment. Then she put the button into a little glass box on the shelf — next to my first lost tooth. "Some things," she said, "are too important to keep in a pocket."

---

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer feel that something was wrong with the old man at first?**

A. He was shouting loudly at the road.  
B. He had not moved at all for a long time, and his eyes were not following any car.  
C. He was holding a dangerous knife.  
D. He tried to steal the writer's phone.

**2. What did the writer find in the old man's pockets?**

A. A phone, a wallet and a key.  
B. A handkerchief, half a packet of biscuits and a small green button.  
C. A full sandwich and two letters.  
D. Money and a driver's licence.

**3. Which steps did the writer follow from the classroom poster?**

A. Stay calm, keep the person warm, and call the community service line.  
B. Run away and tell no one.  
C. Put the person on a bus alone.  
D. Give the person a lot of money.

**4. Why did Mr Li give the writer the small green button?**

A. Because he had too many buttons at home.  
B. Because the button was made of real gold.  
C. Because his wife had sewn the button on his coat many years ago, and he wanted to thank the writer.  
D. Because the officer asked him to do so.

**5. What does the writer's mother mean by saying "Some things are too important to keep in a pocket"?**

A. Pockets are too small for big things.  
B. The button itself is not important at all.  
C. The meaning of the small green button — kindness, memory, a stranger's trust — is worth keeping carefully.  
D. The writer should throw the button away.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "the old man had not moved at all... his eyes were not following any car."  
【解析】老人一动不动、目光呆滞。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "a handkerchief, half a packet of biscuits, and a small green button."  
【解析】口袋里仅有手帕、饼干、绿纽扣。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Step 1: stay calm. Step 2: keep the person warm. Step 3: call the community service line."  
【解析】冷静、保暖、打电话。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "My wife sewed this on my coat forty years ago..."  
【解析】纽扣由已故的妻子缝上，具特殊意义。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段 "put the button into a little glass box... next to my first lost tooth..."  
【解析】母亲把纽扣珍藏，象征善意与信任。A/B/D 均偏。故选 C。
