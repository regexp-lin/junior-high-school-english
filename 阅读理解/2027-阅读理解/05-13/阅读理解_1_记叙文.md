# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Umbrella My Neighbour Lent Me**

① The sky looked clear on Monday morning, so I left for school with only my backpack. I did not even glance at the weather forecast. By the time my afternoon classes ended, however, heavy grey clouds had gathered over the city. The first raindrops fell just as I stepped out of the school gate, and the rain grew thicker with every minute.

② I stood under the thin roof of the gatehouse with many other classmates. Some had clever yellow raincoats; others had bright folding umbrellas. I had nothing. I watched my friends run off one by one, and I began to feel a cold, lonely worry. My mother was still at work, my father was travelling for a training course, and my phone had only five percent of battery left.

③ Then I heard a soft voice from behind. "Excuse me, young man, are you waiting for someone?" I turned and saw Mrs. Wu, our downstairs neighbour, a thin lady of about seventy who walked her two tiny brown dogs in our neighbourhood every evening. I had only ever said "good morning" to her, nothing more. She was carrying two shopping bags and a long dark-green umbrella.

④ Before I could answer, she handed the umbrella to me. "Take mine. I am going home by a covered bus stop. You are going the other way, aren't you?" I tried to say, "No, no, I'm fine, I can wait," but she simply smiled and placed the handle gently into my hand. "Please. An empty umbrella is useless. A lent umbrella is a friend."

⑤ I walked home under her umbrella while she walked to the bus stop with her two shopping bags and wet hair. For the whole seven-minute walk, I kept looking back, ready to run after her if the rain became too strong. When I arrived home safely, I felt warm, but also a little **guilty**. The next day after school, I went to the supermarket with my own money, bought two small umbrellas — one blue, one pink — and put them into a clean paper bag.

⑥ After dinner, I knocked on Mrs. Wu's door. Her dogs barked happily. She opened the door with surprise. I handed her the bag and said, "One is for you to keep, one is for the next young man who forgets to check the weather." She laughed loudly, and then hugged me very lightly, like a grandmother. That small green umbrella is still in our shoe cupboard, waiting for the next rainy day — and, I hope, waiting for me to pass the kindness forward.

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why didn't the writer bring an umbrella in the morning?**

A. His mother forgot to buy one.  
B. The sky looked clear, and he did not check the weather forecast.  
C. He lost it at school the day before.  
D. His father did not allow it.

**2. Why did the writer feel worried under the gatehouse?**

A. His teacher was angry with him.  
B. His mother was at work, his father was away, and his phone was almost dead.  
C. He had lost his backpack.  
D. He saw a lion on the street.

**3. Who gave the writer the umbrella, and what was special about her?**

A. A classmate he sees every day.  
B. Mrs. Wu, the downstairs neighbour he had only greeted briefly before.  
C. A new teacher from Beijing.  
D. A tourist from Shanghai.

**4. What did Mrs. Wu mean by saying, "A lent umbrella is a friend"?**

A. Umbrellas do not like being alone.  
B. Lending an umbrella to someone is an act of friendship, more useful than keeping it unused.  
C. Umbrellas are only friends in summer.  
D. All umbrellas must be green.

**5. Why did the writer finally buy two umbrellas the next day?**

A. He wanted to give them back to his school.  
B. He wanted to return kindness to Mrs. Wu and also prepare to help the next person in need.  
C. He wanted to sell them online.  
D. He wanted to give them to his dogs.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段第1-2句 "The sky looked clear... I did not even glance at the weather forecast."  
【解析】早上天晴他未看天气预报。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段末句 "My mother was still at work, my father was travelling... my phone had only five percent of battery left."  
【解析】父母不在、手机快没电让他焦急。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Mrs. Wu, our downstairs neighbour... I had only ever said 'good morning' to her, nothing more."  
【解析】把伞借给他的是楼下邻居、平日只打过招呼的吴阿姨。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段末 "An empty umbrella is useless. A lent umbrella is a friend."  
【解析】借出去的伞才真正有意义，代表帮助与友情。A/C/D 均为字面化理解，不是意涵。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解/主旨题  
【定位】第⑥段 "One is for you to keep, one is for the next young man..."  
【解析】作者既回报邻居也准备帮下一个忘带伞的人。A/C/D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**词汇注释（超纲词）**  
- **weather forecast** [n. phr.] 天气预报  
- **gatehouse** [n.] 门房（此处指校门口带顶的门楼）  
- **guilty** [adj.] 内疚的
