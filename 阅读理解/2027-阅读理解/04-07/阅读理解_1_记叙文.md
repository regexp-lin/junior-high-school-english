# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Teaching Grandma How to Use a Smartphone**

① Last Sunday, Mum handed me a shiny new smartphone and said, "This is for Grandma. Your job, Xinyi, is to **teach her how to use it**." I groaned. Grandma is 71, forgets her own slippers under the sofa, and still likes to listen to the radio at breakfast. **How do you teach a smartphone to a radio-person?** But Mum only smiled and pushed me gently towards Grandma's room.

② Grandma was sitting by the window, knitting a blue scarf for my brother. She looked at the shiny phone as if it were a strange little fish. "I do not need this," she said quickly. "The buttons are too small. My fingers are too slow. And what for? I already have a good pair of ears and a good pair of eyes." I sat down beside her, took a deep breath, and said, "Grandma, let's try just one thing today — only **one**."

③ We started with video calls to Aunt Yue in Shanghai. I opened the app, pressed the green camera button, and suddenly Aunt Yue's face appeared on the screen, laughing. Grandma's eyes grew as big as two moons. "**It is really her!**" she whispered, touching the screen softly, as if afraid to break it. Aunt Yue said, "Mum, I can see your new haircut. You look ten years younger!" Grandma blushed and covered her face with her hands, like a young girl.

④ After the call, Grandma looked at the phone in a new way. She asked me to show her how to take photos. Her first picture was a close-up of my nostrils, because she did not know which side the camera faced. We laughed so hard that Dad came in to check. Her second picture was her blue scarf, half-finished. "**I will send this to your aunt**," she said proudly. It took her five minutes to press "Send", but she did it herself.

⑤ The next day, Grandma wanted to learn **voice messages**. She held the phone too far from her lips and whispered, "Hello, is it working?" I moved the phone a little closer and said, "Try again, Grandma, like you are telling me a secret." She smiled and said, "Xinyi, do not forget to drink warm water. Grandma loves you." When her voice played back through the speaker, she stared at the phone with tears in her eyes. "The little box remembers my words," she said.

⑥ By the weekend, Grandma could do three things on her own: video-call Aunt Yue, take photos of her cooking, and send a voice message to me at school. Sometimes she still called the home button the "big eye", and sometimes she thought the flashlight was a broken mode. But she was no longer afraid. She would say, "Phones are not scary. They are just **very fast pen and paper**."

⑦ That evening, Grandma asked me a question I had not expected. "Xinyi, when you were little, how many times did I teach you how to hold chopsticks? How many times did I tie your shoe before you could do it yourself?" I thought quietly. "Many, many times," I said. Grandma nodded. "Now it is your turn." I felt something warm move in my chest. A smartphone is small, but **the patience we give our grandparents is not**. And that, I realized, was the best lesson of all.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did Mum give Xinyi the task of teaching Grandma?**

A. Mum was too busy cleaning the house.  
B. Xinyi had the newest phone in the family.  
C. Grandma got a new smartphone and needed to learn how to use it.  
D. Grandma asked for an English lesson.

**2. How did Grandma react when she FIRST saw the phone?**

A. She was very excited and wanted to use it right away.  
B. She said she did not need it because she already had good ears and eyes.  
C. She threw it into the sofa.  
D. She broke the screen by accident.

**3. What was Grandma's FIRST photo?**

A. A photo of the blue scarf.  
B. A photo of Aunt Yue.  
C. A close-up of Xinyi's nostrils.  
D. A photo of the kitchen.

**4. Which sentence about Grandma's learning is TRUE?**

A. She learned how to play video games online.  
B. She could video-call, take photos, and send voice messages by the weekend.  
C. She sent her first voice message in English.  
D. She refused to use the phone after a week.

**5. What did Xinyi finally realize at the end of the story?**

A. Teaching grandparents takes patience, and it is our turn to give it.  
B. Smartphones will soon replace grandparents.  
C. Only young people can use technology well.  
D. Video calls are too expensive for families.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "This is for Grandma. Your job... is to teach her"  
【解析】奶奶有新手机需教。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "I do not need this... a good pair of ears and a good pair of eyes"  
【解析】奶奶最初拒绝。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "close-up of my nostrils"  
【解析】首张是鼻孔特写。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段总结  
【解析】奶奶学会三项技能。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦段 "Now it is your turn"  
【解析】耐心互相回馈是重点。故选 A。
