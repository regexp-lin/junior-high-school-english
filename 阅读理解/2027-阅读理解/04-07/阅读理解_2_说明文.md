# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Is the Ocean Salty?**

① If you ever have the chance to stand on a sea beach in Hainan or Qingdao and taste a drop of sea water, you will find it is **salty and slightly bitter**. Rivers and lakes, on the other hand, are usually fresh. They feed the same planet, yet their tastes are very different. Why is the ocean salty, but most rivers are not? The answer is older than any human city.

② The main reason is simple: **rivers carry salt to the sea, but salt cannot easily leave the sea.** On land, rain falls on rocks and hills. As water runs through the soil and stones, it slowly **washes out tiny amounts of salts and minerals**. This water then flows into rivers, and the rivers flow into the sea. So every day, all over the Earth, rivers act like a long line of delivery trucks, bringing a thin layer of salt to the ocean.

③ The ocean receives the salt, but then the **sun heats the surface** of the sea. Pure water turns into clouds, leaves the sea, and becomes rain again. But the salt is left behind, because salt cannot evaporate the way water does. Over hundreds of millions of years, the amount of salt in the sea has grown into what we taste today — about **35 grams of salt in every litre** of sea water.

④ There is a second helper under the sea: **undersea volcanoes and hot springs**. Deep beneath the waves, hot water often shoots out from cracks on the ocean floor. This water carries minerals such as sodium, calcium, and chloride. Over time, these minerals add to the salty soup we call the sea. A small part of the salt in the ocean has travelled not from rivers, but from the deep kitchen of the Earth itself.

⑤ Still, the sea is not equally salty everywhere. Near the **mouth of a big river**, such as the Yangtze or the Amazon, the sea is less salty, because fresh river water keeps washing in. In **hot and dry places**, such as the Red Sea, the sun is stronger, and less fresh water arrives from rivers, so the water is saltier. The Dead Sea, between Israel and Jordan, is so salty that swimmers **float on top** without trying.

⑥ Why should we care? First, this salty ocean is a **huge climate machine**. It moves heat around the Earth and helps create rain, wind, and storms. Second, many sea creatures have grown to fit this salty home; too much change, caused by pollution or melting ice, can hurt them. So the next time you taste salt on your lips after a sea swim, remember that you are tasting a quiet story of rain, rock, river, and fire — a story as long as the Earth itself.

**1. What is the main idea of the article?**

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末问题  
【解析】全文解释海水为什么咸。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "washes out tiny amounts of salts and minerals"  
【解析】雨水从岩石冲出盐分，河流带入海。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "salt cannot evaporate"  
【解析】水可蒸发，盐不能。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "hot and dry places... stronger sun... less fresh water"  
【解析】炎热干燥且河流少，使其更咸。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "huge climate machine... many sea creatures"  
【解析】气候调节与生态。故选 A。
