# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Old Letter in Grandma's Drawer**

It was a rainy Saturday afternoon when I found the letter. I was helping Grandma tidy her small bedroom, and she asked me to open the bottom drawer of the wooden desk. "There are some old things inside," she said with a small smile. "Take your time."

The drawer was full of photos, a thin blue notebook, and two or three yellow envelopes. I picked up the top envelope. It was stamped 1978. My heart started to beat a little faster. I had never seen a letter that was older than my mother.

"Can I read it?" I asked.

Grandma sat down beside me. She looked at the letter quietly for a moment, then said, "Yes, Xiao Jun. It was written by your great-grandfather."

I carefully took out the paper. It was thin, soft, and the ink had turned brown. The handwriting was small but neat. Most of the words were easy to read, although some were hard because of the old style. In the letter, my great-grandfather wrote to his son (my grandpa) who was working in a far city. He told him that the plum tree in the yard was in flower, that the family dog was a little old now, and that "no matter how busy life is, always write home once a month, because your mother counts the days."

I looked up. Grandma's eyes were wet. She did not cry loudly; tears just quietly went down her face. She took my hand and said, "Your grandpa kept this letter in his bag for forty years. When he got very sick last winter, he told me, 'Keep it safe. One day, show it to the children.'"

I did not say anything for a while. The rain was still falling outside. I suddenly felt that this small paper was heavy — heavier than my school bag, heavier than any book. Inside it was not only words, but a father's worry, a mother's waiting, and a promise that crossed forty years.

That night, before I went to bed, I sent a short message to my own father, who was on a business trip: "Dad, are you eating well? Please come home soon." I had never sent such a message before. Usually I only sent things like "homework finished".

My father answered quickly: "Yes, son. Thank you. I will be home on Friday."

I put down the phone, looked out of the window, and thought about the plum tree that my great-grandfather had once planted. Although I had never seen it, I felt that it was still, in some way, growing in me.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where did the narrator find the old letter?**

A. In a box under the bed.  
B. On the top of a bookshelf.  
C. In the bottom drawer of a wooden desk in Grandma's room.  
D. In an old school bag.

**2. Who wrote the letter?**

A. The narrator's grandpa.  
B. The narrator's father.  
C. The narrator's great-grandfather.  
D. Grandma.

**3. What did the great-grandfather ask his son to do in the letter?**

A. Send more money home every month.  
B. Come home immediately and stop working.  
C. Write home once a month, because his mother counted the days.  
D. Sell the old family dog.

**4. Why did Grandma's eyes become wet?**

A. Because the rain came inside through the window.  
B. Because she felt cold.  
C. Because the letter brought back deep family memories.  
D. Because she was angry with the narrator.

**5. What did the narrator do that night?**

A. Threw the letter away.  
B. Sent his father a warm, caring message for the first time.  
C. Went to plant a new plum tree in the yard.  
D. Called the school and asked for a holiday.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 1 段 "open the bottom drawer of the wooden desk"  
【解析】奶奶卧室木桌底抽屉。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段 "It was written by your great-grandfather."  
【解析】曾祖父所写。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段 "always write home once a month, because your mother counts the days"  
【解析】每月写一次家书。A/B/D 无依据。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第 6 段 情感描写  
【解析】家人旧事触动情感。A/B/D 均牵强。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】倒数第 3 段 "sent a short message to my own father..."  
【解析】主动给父亲发温暖问候信息。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

- **envelope** [n.] 信封  
- **ink** [n.] 墨水  
- **plum** [n.] 李子，梅子
