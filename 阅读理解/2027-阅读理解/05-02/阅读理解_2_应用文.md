# 阅读理解（应用文） **Summer First-Aid Basics Leaflet — City Red Cross**

> **City Red Cross**  
> **SUMMER FIRST-AID BASICS – FREE LEAFLET FOR STUDENTS**  
> *"Five minutes of knowledge can save a life."*

---

Dear students and families,

Summer is coming. Heat, water, and the kitchen are three common places where small accidents happen. This short leaflet gives you **five clear and simple first-aid steps** for common problems. Please keep it in your schoolbag. In an emergency, **always call 120 first** if the injury is serious.

---

### 1. Small Cuts and Bleeding

1. **Wash your hands** with clean water and soap if possible.  
2. **Press** the cut gently with a clean cloth for 5 minutes.  
3. **Clean** the wound with clean running water (not alcohol, not soy sauce, not toothpaste).  
4. **Cover** it with a clean bandage.  
5. If the cut is **deep** or the bleeding does not stop, call **120**.

### 2. Minor Burns (for example, from a hot pan)

- Run **cool water** (not ice water) over the burn for **at least 10 minutes**.  
- Do NOT break **blisters**, and do NOT put **butter**, **egg white**, or **toothpaste** on the burn.  
- Cover with a clean soft cloth and go to the clinic if it is larger than a coin.

### 3. Nosebleed

- Sit down and lean **slightly forward**, not backward.  
- Gently **pinch the soft part** of the nose (not the bony part) for 10 minutes.  
- Breathe through the mouth.  
- If bleeding does not stop after 15 minutes, go to the hospital.

### 4. Heatstroke (on very hot days)

Signs: hot dry skin, headache, tiredness, confusion.

- Move the person to a **cool, shaded place**.  
- Remove heavy clothing.  
- Wet the skin with cool water; use a fan if possible.  
- Give small sips of water if the person is awake.  
- Call **120** if the person is confused, unconscious, or has a very high body temperature.

### 5. Choking on Food

For an **adult or older child** who cannot speak or breathe:

1. Stand behind the person.  
2. Make one hand into a fist, place it a little above the belly button.  
3. Hold the fist with your other hand.  
4. Give **five quick upward pushes** into the belly.  
5. Repeat until the food comes out, or call **120** at once.

*Do not use this method on very young babies under 1 year. Use soft back-slaps and chest-pushes, and call 120 immediately.*

---

### Important Reminders

| DO                                        | DO NOT                                      |
|-------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Stay calm, speak softly                   | Run and shout unnecessarily                 |
| Call 120 early for serious injuries       | Wait too long before calling a doctor       |
| Use clean cloth and water                 | Use soy sauce, toothpaste, or old folk tricks |
| Help the person lie in a comfortable position | Move someone with a possible neck injury |

### Free Training

The **City Red Cross** offers a **free 2-hour first-aid class** for students aged 12–16 every Saturday in May and June.

- **Place:** Red Cross Training Hall, 5 Peace Road  
- **Time:** 10:00 a.m. – 12:00 p.m.  
- **Sign up:** online at www.citrycross.cn or by phone 010-6699-8866  
- **Age limits:** 12–16 (with an adult if under 13)  
- **What you'll learn:** bandaging, recovery position, CPR basics, and child choking

Every student who finishes the class will receive a small **First-Aid Card** with the steps above and a **free hand-size first-aid kit**.

**Stay safe. Help others safely. Have a bright summer.**

**1. When a student has a small cut, what should he do first?**

A. Put toothpaste on it.  
B. Wash his hands with clean water and soap if possible, then press with a clean cloth.  
C. Call the headmaster.  
D. Jump into a cold pond.

**2. Which of the following is NOT allowed on a minor burn?**

A. Running cool water for at least 10 minutes.  
B. Covering with a clean soft cloth.  
C. Putting butter, egg white, or toothpaste on the burn.  
D. Going to the clinic if the burn is larger than a coin.

**3. What is the correct body position for a nosebleed?**

A. Lying flat with head back.  
B. Sitting down and leaning slightly forward, pinching the soft part of the nose.  
C. Jumping and waving arms.  
D. Running to open the window.

**4. Which of the following is a sign of heatstroke?**

A. Very cold wet skin and strong appetite.  
B. Hot dry skin, headache, tiredness, and confusion.  
C. Loud laughter only.  
D. Deep sleep with no headache.

**5. Who can join the free first-aid class?**

A. Only adults over 30.  
B. Students aged 12–16 (with an adult if under 13).  
C. Only children aged 5.  
D. Only nurses.
