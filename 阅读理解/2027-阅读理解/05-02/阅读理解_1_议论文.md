# 阅读理解（议论文） **Is It a Good Idea for Middle School Students to Keep a Pet?**

① On a bright May afternoon, I pass our school gate and see at least three "pet stickers" on the back of parents' cars: a small dog, a fat orange cat, a green-eyed rabbit. In recent years, **pet keeping** has become very popular in Chinese middle-school families. Many students today ask their parents, "Can I have a kitten?" Some parents agree with a smile, others worry about noise, dust, and study time. The real question is: **is it a good idea for middle school students to keep a pet?**

② Those who strongly say **yes** list several real benefits. First, pets are good for **emotions**. After a hard exam, a small wagging tail at the door can melt a bad mood in a second. Studies in several cities have found that students with a pet at home often feel **less lonely** and less anxious before tests. Second, taking care of an animal teaches **responsibility**: feeding on time, cleaning the cage, going to the vet when needed. These are small adult lessons inside a warm daily life.

③ Third, pets may help **health**. Walking a dog twice a day is a mild form of exercise. Children who live with pets from an early age, some research suggests, may have **stronger immune systems** and **fewer allergies**. Also, caring for an animal can teach empathy — feeling another creature's hunger or thirst, and learning not to ignore small living things in the world around us.

④ However, it is not a simple "yes". Keeping a pet well is also a **long promise**. A dog may live 12 years, a cat 15 years, a tortoise more than 50. A middle school student in Grade 7 today may already be in university when her rabbit is still alive. If parents cannot or will not help, the animal may end up **abandoned**, which is both cruel and dangerous. City streets in many places have too many stray cats already.

⑤ There is also a question of **time and space**. A middle school student with six subjects, daily homework, and a test every two weeks does not have endless free hours. A large dog needs long daily walks; a small bird needs quiet and clean air; a goldfish needs a clean tank. In a small city flat, with neighbours above and below, not every pet fits every family. Allergies in family members and safety for very young brothers and sisters must also be considered.

⑥ My own view is that middle school students can keep a pet, but **only with clear family rules**. Before the pet arrives, parents and children should talk about **five questions**: (1) who will feed it every day; (2) who will clean after it; (3) who will pay for food and the vet; (4) what to do during long holidays; (5) what to do if the child cannot continue the care. If these questions have honest answers, a pet can become a quiet friend, a life teacher, and a warm part of family growth.

⑦ A pet is not a toy, and it is not a piece of furniture. It is a small, breathing life with its own fears and joys. Loving it means more than posting a cute video online. It means choosing to be its human forever, through homework weeks, school trips, and long summer heat. With thought, with family support, and with patience, a middle school pet can be one of the kindest decisions a young person makes.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What main question does the article discuss?**

A. How to choose a cat over a dog.  
B. Whether middle school students should keep a pet.  
C. Why cats are better than rabbits.  
D. How to train a parrot.

**2. Which is NOT a benefit of keeping a pet mentioned in paragraphs ②–③?**

A. Pets help emotions and may reduce loneliness.  
B. Pets teach responsibility.  
C. Pets may help some children have stronger immune systems and fewer allergies.  
D. Pets can write students' homework for them.

**3. Why does the writer call keeping a pet a "long promise"?**

A. Because pets have short lives.  
B. Because pets may live many years and the child must still care for them as an adult.  
C. Because pets cost nothing to feed.  
D. Because pets do not need owners.

**4. Which question is NOT on the writer's "five-question list" before a family gets a pet?**

A. Who will feed it every day.  
B. Who will pay for food and the vet.  
C. Who will decide its favourite TV show.  
D. What to do during long holidays.

**5. What is the writer's FINAL view?**

A. No middle school student should keep any pet.  
B. Every middle school student must have a pet.  
C. A middle school pet can be a kind choice, if the family has clear rules and strong support.  
D. Only parents, not students, should keep pets.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末问题  
【解析】讨论中学生是否该养宠物。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②–③段  
【解析】文中未提"宠物会写作业"。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "a rabbit... 15 years... 50 years"  
【解析】宠物寿命很长。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段五问  
【解析】"决定其电视节目"不在列表中。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥–⑦段  
【解析】有清晰规则与家庭支持即可。故选 C。
