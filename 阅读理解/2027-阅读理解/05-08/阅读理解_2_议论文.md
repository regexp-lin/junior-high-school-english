# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Every School Teach Students Basic First-Aid?**

① Last winter, news reports told the story of a 14-year-old boy who saved his grandfather's life with a simple first-aid skill he had learned in a school lesson only three months earlier. Stories like this are becoming more common, yet many Chinese schools still do not offer regular first-aid classes. Some people ask an important question: **should every middle school teach basic first-aid as a required part of the curriculum?** I think the answer is clearly yes.

② First, emergencies happen close to home. A small child can choke on a piece of fruit during a birthday party. An old neighbour can slip on wet tiles in a bathroom. A classmate can fall off a bike and start bleeding. Hospitals and ambulances are wonderful, but they are not always near. In the first few minutes after an accident, the people who are there — often ordinary students — are the first and most important rescuers.

③ Second, basic first-aid skills are **not hard to learn**. In many countries, students aged 12 and above already learn how to call emergency numbers, how to help a choking person, how to press a bleeding wound, and how to place an unconscious friend in a safe side position. These skills can be taught in about ten hours spread over one term, using simple role-plays. Such training can save lives without turning the curriculum upside down.

④ Third, learning first-aid helps young people grow stronger in character. When a student practises calling 120 clearly or asking a partner, "Are you OK?", he or she builds calm and confidence. These are skills that travel far beyond medicine — to leading a team, to standing on a stage, to helping a frightened child cross a busy road. In short, **first-aid builds life-aid**.

⑤ Some teachers worry that first-aid lessons may take too much time away from maths, Chinese and English. This fear is understandable, but maybe exaggerated. A well-designed programme can use physical education periods and class-meeting hours. Schools can also invite the **Red Cross** or local hospitals to help once a month. Students will not lose their grades; in fact, many teachers in pilot schools report higher concentration after a hands-on first-aid week.

⑥ Of course, first-aid lessons are not a free ticket to dangerous behaviour. They are not about turning students into doctors. They are about giving ordinary young people the chance to do **ordinary good** in an unusual moment. I strongly believe that every middle school in our city should add first-aid to its weekly timetable. A lesson of one hour today may become a life saved tomorrow.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main question the writer discusses?**

A. Whether hospitals are near every school.  
B. Whether every middle school should teach basic first-aid.  
C. Whether students can become professional doctors.  
D. Whether physical education is more important than maths.

**2. Why does the writer say students are often the "first and most important rescuers"?**

A. Because students are stronger than adults.  
B. Because hospitals are always closed on weekends.  
C. Because in the first few minutes after an accident, the people nearby — often students — act first.  
D. Because ambulances are forbidden in cities.

**3. How does the writer answer the worry that first-aid takes too much time from key subjects?**

A. He refuses to answer the question.  
B. He suggests using PE periods and class-meeting hours, with help from Red Cross or hospitals.  
C. He agrees that all maths lessons should be cut.  
D. He asks students to study first-aid at home only.

**4. What does "first-aid builds life-aid" MOST likely mean?**

A. First-aid training turns every student into a paid helper.  
B. First-aid skills also build calm, confidence and useful habits for life.  
C. First-aid means throwing away all old lessons.  
D. First-aid can replace sleep and food.

**5. What is the writer's final view?**

A. Only a few schools should try first-aid lessons.  
B. First-aid lessons are dangerous for students.  
C. Every middle school in the city should add first-aid to its weekly timetable.  
D. First-aid lessons should replace all PE lessons.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句 "should every middle school teach basic first-aid..."  
【解析】全文围绕是否应将急救列入必修课展开。A/C/D 均非核心问题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段末句 "the people who are there — often ordinary students — are the first and most important rescuers."  
【解析】事故发生头几分钟最先救援的往往是身边的学生。A 更强壮、B 医院周末关门、D 禁救护车都属无中生有。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段第3-4句 "use physical education periods and class-meeting hours... invite the Red Cross or local hospitals..."  
【解析】用体育课/班会、并邀请红十字会或医院协助。A 不答、C 取消数学、D 在家学均不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义猜测题  
【定位】第④段上下文 "calm and confidence... leading a team, standing on a stage..."  
【解析】"life-aid"在此指对整个人生有益的技能。A 付费助手、C 丢掉旧课、D 替代睡眠吃饭均不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳/态度题  
【定位】第⑥段末句 "every middle school in our city should add first-aid to its weekly timetable."  
【解析】作者明确主张全市每所中学都应将急救纳入每周课表。A 仅少数学校、B 有危险、D 取代体育均非作者观点。故选 C。
