# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Morning I Left My Schoolbag on the Bus**

① It was the first Monday after the long National Day holiday. I had spent seven perfect days reading, eating mooncakes and sleeping late. My bag that morning was heavier than usual — two novels borrowed from the library, a new pencil case from my aunt, and a thin blue folder that held **every piece of homework from the holiday**. Maths problems. An English essay. A book report. Three weeks of work, all in one folder.

② On the 7:20 bus, I sat at the window, listening to music and half-dreaming of the beach we had visited. Two stops before school, a crowd pushed in. An old lady with a heavy shopping bag stood right in front of me. I stood up, gave her my seat, and moved towards the door. At the school stop I jumped off quickly because I was already a little late.

③ Halfway across the square in front of the school gate, my hand suddenly felt light. Too light. I froze. I had left my schoolbag on the bus. Everything was in it: homework, library books, my phone, my keys. For a second the whole square seemed to turn silver and cold.

④ I ran back to the bus stop, but of course the bus was already far down the road. My heart began to beat in my ears. The first bell rang. I should have cried, but a small, clear voice in my head said, **"Think first, cry later."**

⑤ I remembered the number on the side of the bus: **Route 28**. I remembered the driver's black beard. I knew the bus would turn around at the Riverside terminal, about forty minutes away, and come back. I walked quickly to the security uncle at the school gate. "Uncle Zhao," I said, "I need help. I left my schoolbag on a Route 28 bus five minutes ago." He looked at my face, put down his newspaper, and picked up the phone.

⑥ Uncle Zhao called the **bus company's lost-and-found line**. A kind woman asked for the bus number, the stop where I got off, the exact time, and a short description of the bag. She said she would send a message to every Route 28 driver. Then Uncle Zhao walked me to my classroom and explained everything to my teacher, Ms. Lin. She nodded and said, "Sit down. Your classmates can lend you books for the first two lessons."

⑦ I could not really focus in Chinese class. Every five minutes my eyes went to the classroom window. Then, during the break before the third lesson, Uncle Zhao knocked quietly on the classroom door. In one hand, he was holding **my blue folder**. In the other, my whole schoolbag.

⑧ "The driver found it under the seat," he said, smiling under his grey moustache. "He brought it to the next stop and passed it to an inspector, and the inspector was just on his way to another school nearby. One phone call, three people. And here it is." The class cheered. I cheered loudest. My homework was safe, my keys were safe, even my library books were safe.

⑨ That night, I wrote a short thank-you card for the driver and dropped it at the bus company with my mother. I do not even know his name. But I learned something important that morning: **panic helps nobody**; a clear memory, a polite voice and kind strangers can rescue a whole folder of holiday homework — and maybe a little of a twelve-year-old's faith in her own city, too.

---

**注释：** folder 文件夹；terminal 终点站；security uncle 保安；lost-and-found 失物招领；inspector 巡查员

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why was the writer's bag especially heavy that morning?**

A. Because she had put her winter coat inside.  
B. Because it held two library books, a new pencil case and a folder with all her holiday homework.  
C. Because she was carrying her sister's bag for her.  
D. Because she had put a heavy water bottle inside.

**2. How did the writer realise she had left her bag behind?**

A. Her teacher phoned her.  
B. A classmate told her at the gate.  
C. Walking across the school square, she suddenly noticed her hand felt too light.  
D. The bus driver ran after her.

**3. What did "Think first, cry later" in Paragraph 4 most probably help the writer to do?**

A. Forget about the bag and go to class.  
B. Calm down and try to solve the problem before getting upset.  
C. Cry for a long time before acting.  
D. Run after the bus until she caught it.

**4. Who first called the bus company to report the lost bag?**

A. The writer herself.  
B. The writer's mother.  
C. Uncle Zhao, the school security guard.  
D. Ms. Lin, the Chinese teacher.

**5. What lesson did the writer most want to share at the end of the story?**

A. Never give up a seat to an old lady on the bus.  
B. Panic does not help; keeping a clear head, asking politely and trusting kind people can solve a big problem.  
C. Holiday homework is not important.  
D. You should always phone your parents first in an emergency.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "two novels borrowed from the library, a new pencil case from my aunt, and a thin blue folder that held every piece of homework from the holiday"  
【解析】原文明列三样东西造成背包特别重。A "冬衣"、C "妹妹的包"、D "水瓶"均无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "my hand suddenly felt light. Too light. I froze. I had left my schoolbag on the bus."  
【解析】自己过广场时手感到空才意识到。A 老师打电话、B 同学告知、D 司机追来均未发生。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段 "a small, clear voice in my head said, 'Think first, cry later.'" 接着第⑤段"remembered the number ... walked quickly to the security uncle"  
【解析】这句话让她先冷静思考并求助，而非先慌张大哭。A "放弃"、C "先哭"、D "追公交"均不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "Uncle Zhao called the bus company's lost-and-found line."  
【解析】是保安赵叔叔先打的失物热线。A 自己、B 母亲、D 老师都错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨/写作意图题  
【定位】第⑨段 "panic helps nobody; a clear memory, a polite voice and kind strangers can rescue a whole folder"  
【解析】作者提炼经验：冷静 + 求助 + 善良的陌生人。A、C、D 均偏离主旨或荒谬。故选 B。
