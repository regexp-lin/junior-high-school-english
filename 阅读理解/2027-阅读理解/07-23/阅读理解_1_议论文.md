# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle Schools Teach Simple Cooking in Class?**

① At the end of a long school day, what is the first question that greets most of us at home? For me, it is almost always the same three words from my mum: "What's for dinner?" She is asking herself, not me — because she knows, as I know, that I cannot really answer. I can name twenty English tenses, solve three kinds of triangles, and draw the map of Africa, but I cannot cook a single proper meal for my family. In my view, this is a problem. **Simple cooking should be a regular lesson in every middle school, at least one hour a week.**

② The first reason is **real-life ability**. We say we are preparing students for the future, but a future adult who cannot boil an egg or wash rice is not really prepared. Today, many families eat more take-out food than ever. It is convenient, but often more oily, more salty, and more expensive than a home-cooked dish. A student who can cook three or four safe and tasty meals — tomato-egg noodles, steamed fish, a simple soup — gains a quiet freedom: the freedom to feed himself on a sick day, to thank his parents on their busy day, to share a small meal with friends during an overnight trip.

③ The second reason is **health**. Recent reports show that middle-school students in big cities eat too much salt and sugar, and not enough vegetables. A 2026 health survey of 5,000 teenagers in our province found that only **1 in 4 students** could name five common vegetables they had cooked themselves. When students learn to cook, they meet food with their own hands: they see how much oil goes into a "crispy" chicken leg, how much sugar is hidden in a bowl of instant noodles. Knowing is the first step to choosing better.

④ The third reason is **family and culture**. Chinese food is not just food; it is memory. Grandma's dumplings on New Year's Eve, Mum's rice cake at Mid-Autumn, a neighbour's mooncake — each dish carries a story. When schools teach a simple steamed bun or a local soup, they also teach history, geography, and gratitude. A school cooking class is a short bridge between the classroom and the kitchen table at home.

⑤ "But we already have so much homework," some classmates will say. "Where is the time for cooking?" This worry is fair, yet it confuses **time spent** with **time wasted**. One hour a week — the same length as a single long video we might watch for fun — can be enough to learn one new safe skill. Schools can put the class on a fixed afternoon, so that homework is not touched. In fact, studies of schools in Japan and parts of Shandong have found that cooking class often **improves** students' mood and focus in the next lesson, not the opposite.

⑥ Others may ask, "Is it safe?" Cooking uses knives, hot oil, and fire. These are real risks, but they are not reasons to refuse. They are reasons to **teach**. When we avoid the skill, the same student will face the same knife at 20 or 30, but with no training at all. A well-designed class teaches safety first: how to hold a knife, how to turn off gas, how to use a small first-aid kit, how to help a classmate with a tiny burn. Safety knowledge is itself a life lesson that no textbook chapter can replace.

⑦ Finally, a cooking class teaches **team work and respect**. In a group of four, someone washes, someone chops, someone watches the stove, someone sets the table. If one person is slow, the dish is cold. Each student quickly learns that dinner — like any shared project — depends on quiet, honest team work. And after eating what you made together, it is very hard not to clean the kitchen, and very hard not to say thank you.

⑧ We do not cook every day in order to become chefs. We cook in order to become kinder and more capable people — people who can care for their own bodies, their families, and the old, wonderful food traditions behind them. One hour a week is a small ask for such a big gift. It is time for our middle schools to bring the kitchen into the classroom.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Middle schools should replace all subjects with cooking.  
B. Middle schools should teach simple cooking for at least one hour a week.  
C. Students should never eat take-out food.  
D. Only girls should learn to cook at school.

**2. What did the 2026 health survey show about teenagers in the writer's province?**

A. All students cook well.  
B. Only one in four students could name five common vegetables they had cooked themselves.  
C. Students eat more vegetables than adults.  
D. Students ate no salt and no sugar at all.

**3. How does the writer answer the worry that "we already have too much homework"?**

A. Students should do cooking instead of all homework.  
B. One hour a week is only as long as a video; schools can fix the class on a certain afternoon, and cooking may even improve mood and focus.  
C. Homework is not important, so cooking class should take its place.  
D. Students should cook four hours every day.

**4. Why does the writer think cooking is also connected to family and culture?**

A. Because Chinese food carries stories and memory — dumplings at New Year, mooncakes at Mid-Autumn, family dishes with history.  
B. Because food tastes the same everywhere.  
C. Because cooking has no connection with any festival.  
D. Because only restaurants know traditional dishes.

**5. What does the writer say about safety in a cooking class?**

A. Cooking is too dangerous, so we should never teach it.  
B. Knives, hot oil and fire are risks, but they are reasons to **teach** safety, not to refuse the skill.  
C. Students should use no knives, no heat and no fire at all.  
D. Safety is only the job of the kitchen at home.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】首段尾句 "Simple cooking should be a regular lesson..."  
【解析】每周至少一小时烹饪课。A/C/D 均偏。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "only 1 in 4 students could name five common vegetables..."  
【解析】仅四分之一能说出五种自己做过的蔬菜。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "One hour a week... cooking class often improves students' mood and focus..."  
【解析】每周一小时不占作业，还能提升状态。A/C/D 均偏。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "Chinese food is not just food; it is memory..."  
【解析】中餐承载家族与节日记忆。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "They are reasons to teach."  
【解析】危险正是学习安全的理由。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
