# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Uniform Warm-Winter Drive 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Student Union + Red Cross Club**  
> **Uniform Warm-Winter Drive 2027** — *"A Uniform You Grew Out of Can Keep Another Child Warm."*  
> **Collection Period**: **Mon 14 Dec – Fri 18 Dec 2027**  
> **Venue**: **Main Hallway, Block A, Ground Floor (next to the Thank-You Wall) + Class-level collection boxes**

---

Every December, many students grow out of last year's uniforms. Sweaters that no longer close at the chest, jackets whose sleeves are two-finger-widths short, winter trousers that suddenly stop at the ankle. These clothes are not broken; they are simply too small for us now. They are, however, still **warm, clean, and strong** — and in the mountain primary school at **Guishan County (500 km north of our city)**, where our volunteer teachers have been partnered with for three years, there are children whose families cannot easily buy a new winter uniform. This December, we invite the whole Lihua community to bring any uniforms in good condition and donate them **gently, anonymously, and without pressure** to the Warm-Winter Drive.

---

## 🕒 Collection Hours

| Time slot | Day | Place |
|---|---|---|
| 07:30 – 07:55 | Mon – Fri | Class collection boxes |
| 12:00 – 13:15 | Mon – Fri | Main Hallway (Red Cross Club desk) |
| 16:10 – 17:30 | Mon – Thu | Main Hallway (Red Cross Club desk) |
| 16:10 – 18:00 | Fri | **Final drop-off window** |

- **Anyone** can donate: students, teachers, staff, parents, even grandparents.  
- **All drop-offs are anonymous** unless the donor chooses otherwise.  
- **One small kraft-paper card** is available at every desk so donors may, if they wish, write **one warm sentence** to slip inside a pocket of the donated clothes. Example: *"This jacket kept me warm in Grade 7. I hope it keeps you warm too."*

---

## ✅ What We Can Accept

- **Winter uniforms**: jackets, trousers, thick sweaters, long-sleeve shirts, thermal underlayers, school-issued hats, scarves, gloves.  
- **Normal winter shoes** (cleaned, dry, laces tied in pairs).  
- **Unused thermal socks** (in sealed original packaging only).  
- **Light-weight down / padded coats** in good condition (no cigarette smell, no mould, no tears).  
- **Clothing must be clean and dry**. Please wash at home and fold neatly in a cloth or paper bag.

## ❌ What We CANNOT Accept

- **Underwear** (new or used), **used socks**, **used thermal base-layers** (hygiene).  
- **Clothes with tears longer than 5 cm, strong smells, mould, or visible stains**.  
- **Summer clothes** (this is a winter drive).  
- **Formal wear, dresses, high heels, fashion items** that are not suitable for primary-school children in a cold mountain village.  
- **Cash or vouchers**. This is not a money drive. If you wish to donate money, the school recommends the official county education charity.

---

## 📏 Size Guide (Please read before packing)

| Size | Approximate age | Current Lihua student equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| XXS / 110–120 cm | 5–7 years | Very small primary-school pupils |
| XS / 120–130 cm | 7–9 years | Small |
| S / 130–140 cm | 9–11 years | Medium primary |
| M / 140–150 cm | 11–13 years | Large primary / small Grade 7 |
| L / 150–160 cm | 13–15 years | Our Grade 7–8 size |
| XL+ | 15+ years | Grade 9 and above |

- **Sizes XXS – M** are most needed this year.  
- **L and XL** are welcome, but the county has reported **less demand** at those sizes (their senior grades have the support of the county budget).

---

## 🌡️ Four Small Preparation Steps at Home

1. **Wash** with normal detergent. If the label allows, run an extra rinse — detergent residue can irritate children's skin.  
2. **Dry completely** (indoor drying in heating season takes 24–36 h).  
3. **Check pockets**. Return any student ID cards, pens, or pocket money to the owner.  
4. **Fold neatly** and pack in a **cloth bag** (not a plastic one if possible). Label with a small note: **size, gender (optional), season**.

---

## 📦 What Happens After Drop-Off

1. **Sorting**: Red Cross Club volunteers check each item for cleanliness and size on **Sat 19 Dec, 09:00 – 17:00**. Items failing the check are returned (anonymously) to the "Repair Corner" next Monday, where the school sewing-machine club fixes small issues in three weeks.  
2. **Packing**: Accepted items are packed by size into labelled boxes.  
3. **Delivery**: Boxes are shipped to Guishan County Central Primary School by a partner logistics company **free of charge** (they have supported this drive for 3 years).  
4. **Arrival photos**: With permission of the partner school, photos of the boxes arriving (with children's **faces blurred**) will be posted on the school mini-app on the **first week of January 2028**.

## 🤝 Last Year's Numbers (2026 Drive)

- **1,138 items** collected across 5 days.  
- **89%** passed the sort; **11%** went to the Repair Corner; **all** eventually arrived.  
- **62 pocket-messages** were slipped into clothes pockets; **44 short thank-you notes** came back by post in February 2027.  
- **Total shipping distance**: 502 km. **Total weight**: 214 kg.

## ❗ Important Quiet Rules

- **No photographs of donors at the drop-off desk**. Donation is a private act.  
- **No class-level ranking** of how many items each class collected. We do not compete in kindness.  
- **No public thanks to specific donors**. A general sentence of thanks will be written on the wall at the end of the drive.  
- **Donors who bring family clothes** should make sure the owner (mother, older brother) has agreed.  
- **Teachers will also donate** — in 2026, Mr Shen donated his old PE windbreaker.

## 💬 A Small Word from the School Red Cross Club

> "A uniform that a fifteen-year-old no longer fits is, in a different pair of hands, still warm, still strong, and still beautiful. Somewhere five hundred kilometres north, a nine-year-old will, in February, stand slightly taller inside it, perhaps noticing the small paper card in the pocket that says: *'This jacket kept me warm in Grade 7. I hope it keeps you warm too.'* That small piece of kept warmth is the real thing we are trying to pass on this December. **Thank you for your gently folded clothes. Thank you for your quiet hands.**"  
> — *Red Cross Club, 9 December 2027*
