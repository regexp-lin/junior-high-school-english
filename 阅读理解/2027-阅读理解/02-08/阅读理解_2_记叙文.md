# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Speech I Almost Ran From**

① When my English teacher, Ms. Chen, told me that I had been chosen to give the school's opening speech on Teachers' Day, I smiled on the outside and screamed on the inside. The hall held over five hundred students, parents and teachers. I was the shortest girl in Class 8 and had always been afraid of raising my hand even in small groups. I whispered "thank you," but walked home that afternoon as if my legs had become made of paper.

② For the first three days, I wrote nothing. I sat in front of a blank notebook and listened to my own heartbeat. Then, on Friday night, Mom placed a cup of hot milk beside me and said gently, "Don't think about the five hundred people. Write a letter to one teacher you really love." That night, I wrote three full pages about Ms. Chen — how she had sat with me for a whole lunch break when my cat had died, how she had turned my shaky English into a friendly friend.

③ Yet writing was only half of the battle. In front of the mirror, my voice was either too fast or too low. My hands kept touching my hair, my collar, my pocket — anything to hide my nervousness. My younger brother Tom, only eight years old, became my first audience. He sat on the sofa with a toy microphone and shouted, "Louder, sister, louder!" Every time I broke down, he gave me a small biscuit and said, "Try once more. I still can't hear the last word."

④ The big morning came. I waited backstage in a blue dress. My palms were wet, my throat was dry, and for one terrible second I thought of running out through the back door. Then Ms. Chen appeared in the doorway, smiled and said only seven words: "Just tell them what you told me." Her calm eyes felt like a hand gently placed on my shoulder.

⑤ I walked onto the stage. The lights were bright, the crowd was huge, but I found Ms. Chen's face in the third row and held on to it like a small lantern in the dark. I spoke slowly at first, then faster; my voice stopped shaking; my hands found a good place to rest. When I reached the last line — "Thank you, teachers, for every quiet hour you give us" — the hall became quiet for a second, then filled with warm clapping. I felt tears in my eyes, but for the first time, they were happy tears.

⑥ Later that afternoon, Tom asked me, "Were you really scared?" I answered honestly, "Very scared." He looked up at me in surprise. "Then why did you still go?" I thought for a while and said, "Because on the other side of fear, there is a person you have never met — a braver you." That evening, Ms. Chen gave me a small card. On it she had written only three words: *"Welcome, brave girl."*

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did the writer feel at first when she was chosen to give the speech?**

A. Very excited and proud.  
B. Secretly frightened.  
C. Totally uninterested.  
D. Angry with the teacher.

**2. What advice did the writer's mother give her?**

A. To refuse the speech.  
B. To write a letter to one teacher she loved.  
C. To read another student's speech.  
D. To ask the principal for help.

**3. What role did the writer's younger brother play?**

A. He wrote the whole speech for her.  
B. He was her first audience.  
C. He pushed her onto the stage.  
D. He recorded her voice for a website.

**4. What did Ms. Chen say to help the writer backstage?**

A. "Don't be afraid of anyone."  
B. "You are the best student here."  
C. "Just tell them what you told me."  
D. "Please speak in perfect English."

**5. What does the writer mainly want to tell us?**

A. Public speaking is only for confident people.  
B. On the other side of fear, there is a braver self.  
C. Teachers should always give students small gifts.  
D. Younger brothers are useless during important events.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "smiled on the outside and screamed on the inside"  
【解析】外表微笑内心恐惧。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Write a letter to one teacher you really love."  
【解析】妈妈建议写给一位敬爱的老师。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "my first audience"  
【解析】弟弟是她第一个观众。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "Just tell them what you told me."  
【解析】陈老师让她讲给她听过的话。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段 "on the other side of fear, there is a person you have never met — a braver you"  
【解析】主题是恐惧之外是更勇敢的自己。故选 B。
