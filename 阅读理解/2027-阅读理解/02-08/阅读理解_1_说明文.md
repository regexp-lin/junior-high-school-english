# 阅读理解（说明文） **From Bean to Bar: The Long Journey of Chocolate**

① A square of chocolate melting on your tongue may feel like the simplest of pleasures, yet the story behind it crosses oceans, years and the hands of thousands of workers. The journey begins far from any sweet shop — inside the **pods** of a tropical tree called **the cacao tree**, which grows mainly in West Africa, Central America and Southeast Asia.

② Every cacao pod is about the size of a small watermelon. Inside each pod, there are 30 to 50 white seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. Farmers cut the pods off the trees by hand, because the soft branches can easily break. Then the seeds are placed in wooden boxes and covered with banana leaves for five to seven days. During this time, the seeds **ferment**. They change colour, lose their bitter taste, and slowly develop the first hint of the rich chocolate flavour.

③ Next, the seeds are dried under the strong tropical sun for about a week. Workers must turn them often, or the seeds may become mouldy. Only after this careful drying are the beans ready to be shipped — often in heavy brown sacks — across the ocean to factories in Europe, America or Asia. A single bag may travel for a whole month by sea before it reaches a chocolate factory.

④ In the factory, the real magic begins. First, the beans are roasted in a big oven, in the same way coffee beans are. The heat releases hundreds of new flavours. After roasting, the beans are broken into small pieces called **nibs**. These nibs are ground for hours into a thick dark liquid called *chocolate liquor*. Although the name sounds strange, it contains no alcohol at all — only pure chocolate.

⑤ To make the chocolate bar we know, workers mix the liquor with sugar, a little milk powder, and sometimes extra cocoa butter. The mixture is then stirred gently in a large machine for up to three days. This long slow stirring is the real secret of smooth chocolate. Finally, the warm chocolate is poured into bar-shaped moulds, cooled, wrapped and boxed. From bean to bar, one square of chocolate may take almost **a whole year**.

⑥ Scientists even tell us that a little dark chocolate, eaten slowly, may be good for the heart. But next time you open a chocolate bar, perhaps the sweetest thing to remember is not the sugar, but the long patient journey — the farmers, the ships, and the hundreds of careful hours hidden in a single tiny square.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where does the journey of chocolate begin?**

A. In a sweet shop.  
B. In the pods of the cacao tree.  
C. In an ice cream factory.  
D. In a sugar field.

**2. What happens to the seeds during the five-to-seven days in wooden boxes?**

A. They are washed with hot water.  
B. They are ground into small pieces.  
C. They ferment and lose their bitter taste.  
D. They grow into small trees.

**3. Why must workers turn the drying seeds often?**

A. To keep them warm.  
B. To stop them from becoming mouldy.  
C. To make them taste sweeter.  
D. To change their colour.

**4. What is the "real secret" of smooth chocolate, according to Paragraph 5?**

A. Using the biggest oven.  
B. Adding lots of water.  
C. Slow stirring for up to three days.  
D. Cooling the chocolate with ice.

**5. What can we learn from this passage?**

A. Chocolate is made in only one day.  
B. Every square of chocolate is the result of a long patient journey.  
C. Cacao trees grow mainly in cold places.  
D. Chocolate contains a lot of alcohol.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "inside the pods of a tropical tree called the cacao tree"  
【解析】巧克力之旅始于可可树的豆荚。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "the seeds ferment... lose their bitter taste"  
【解析】种子发酵并失去苦味。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "or the seeds may become mouldy"  
【解析】需翻动以防发霉。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "long slow stirring is the real secret of smooth chocolate"  
【解析】长时间慢速搅拌是丝滑的秘诀。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段 "the long patient journey"  
【解析】全文说明巧克力背后的漫长旅程。A、C、D 均不符。故选 B。
