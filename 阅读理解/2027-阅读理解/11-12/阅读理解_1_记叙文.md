# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Afternoon They Laughed at My Name**

① My name is **Shu-wan**. It is an old family name from my grandmother's side of the house, two gentle sounds that, in our village in northern Fujian, mean "a quiet morning". I had been proud of it since Grade 1, because Grandma would say it in her soft voice at every bedtime. When I moved to our city's new middle school in September, however, my name suddenly became **a small stone in my shoe**.

② It happened on the first **name-calling PE warm-up**. Mr. Tang, our new PE teacher, stood at the front with his class register. He read the names fast: "Li Wei! Chen Hao! Wang Yuting!" When he came to me, he hesitated, looked down, and said, slowly and a bit wrongly: "Shoe… Wan?" The boy on my left laughed. A group in the back row laughed, too, and quickly added: "**Which shoe?**" "Right or left?" "Is there also **Sock-wan**?" Twenty children laughed together for maybe eight seconds. To me it felt like eight minutes. My ears turned red; I stood still; I did not look up; I whispered "Present."

③ I did not tell my parents that night. I simply closed my bedroom door, buried my face in my pillow, and listened to the late November rain on the window. I kept thinking: "*How can they laugh at a name Grandma chose?*" I wanted to change it. I wanted to call myself Lily. I wanted to be smaller than a drop of rain.

④ The next morning, I did something brave-and-silly: I stayed after PE and waited for Mr. Tang. My voice was thin, but I said what I had prepared: "Sir, my name is not Shoe. It is **Shu**, with a soft first tone, like the word for 'book' in Mandarin, 书. And the second sound, Wan, is the same 'Wan' as in Wanli, 湾, 'a quiet bay'. Together it means 'a quiet morning'." Mr. Tang listened. He repeated it back, slowly, three times. When he got it right, he smiled and said quietly: "Thank you, Shu-wan. I'm sorry about yesterday. Tomorrow's register will be better."

⑤ The next PE class, he began the register a little differently. Before the first name, he told the whole class, "Every name has a small story. If I say one wrongly, please correct me — **no laughing**. We are Chinese speakers, we should be the best at Chinese names." When he came to my row, he said my name carefully and correctly: "**Shu-wan**." The same back-row group did not laugh. One boy even said, "That's pretty." The small stone in my shoe, suddenly, began to dissolve.

⑥ That weekend I called Grandma on video. Her face filled the small screen, brown and warm under the yellow kitchen light. I told her everything — the laughter, the pillow, the PE teacher. She listened silently, and then said, "Shu-wan, do you know why I chose that name for you? On the morning you were born, the rain had just ended, and the village was **very quiet**. The fishermen were mending their nets on the soft water. I wanted you to carry that morning **wherever you went**." She paused. "A child who carries a quiet morning inside her does not need to become Lily."

⑦ On Monday I did a second brave-and-silly thing. In my Chinese class, when we were asked to introduce ourselves again, I stood up and said: "My name is Shu-wan. It means 'a quiet morning' in a small fishing village in Fujian. My grandmother gave it to me on the day I was born. Please help me protect it." Twenty-eight small faces looked up. No one laughed. Our form teacher, Ms. Yu, wrote three small Chinese characters on the blackboard — 舒湾 — and said to the class, "A good name deserves careful ears."

⑧ Three weeks have passed. Sometimes my classmates still forget the tone and say the name slightly wrong. They correct themselves at once. My new friend Lin Jia, who sits in front of me, has even learned the story of "a quiet morning" so well that she can tell it to **any new transfer student** before I do. I still blush a little when I hear my own name in a loud corridor. But these days I blush less like a stone in my shoe, and more like a soft morning bell. Grandma was right. **A name is a small home we carry with us. When we refuse to throw it away, the world, slowly, learns to say it properly.**

---

**注释：** register 点名册；tone 声调；blush 脸红；bay 海湾；protect 保护

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer's classmates laugh at her name on the first PE day?**

A. Because she spoke too loudly.  
B. Because Mr. Tang hesitated and pronounced her name "Shoe… Wan?", and the other students repeated jokes like "Which shoe?", "Right or left?", and "Is there also Sock-wan?"  
C. Because she was late for class.  
D. Because she had forgotten her sports shoes.

**2. What did the writer want to do after the laughter, at home that night?**

A. To beat the boys who laughed.  
B. To quit school.  
C. She wanted to change her name to "Lily" and be smaller than a drop of rain.  
D. To write an angry letter to the teacher.

**3. How did the writer explain her name to Mr. Tang?**

A. She said nothing, and only wrote her name on paper.  
B. She explained that her name is "Shu", with the soft first tone like 书 (book), and "Wan", like Wanli 湾 (a quiet bay), together meaning "a quiet morning".  
C. She told him to simply call her "Lily".  
D. She asked him to give her a new name.

**4. What did Grandma tell the writer on the video call?**

A. That the writer should ignore the laughter.  
B. That on the morning Shu-wan was born, the rain had just ended, the village was very quiet, and fishermen were mending their nets on the soft water; she wanted Shu-wan to carry that morning wherever she went, and a child who carries a quiet morning inside her does not need to become Lily.  
C. That the writer should move back to the village.  
D. That the writer should hide her name.

**5. What is the main message of the last paragraph?**

A. A student should laugh at others' names.  
B. A name is a small home we carry with us; when we refuse to throw it away, the world slowly learns to say it properly — standing up for your name is a form of gentle self-respect.  
C. Students should change names often.  
D. Names do not matter much.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段笑声描写 "Which shoe? ... Sock-wan"  
【解析】直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "I wanted to call myself Lily ... smaller than a drop of rain"  
【解析】情绪动念直引。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段解释句  
【解析】两字释义直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段奶奶话  
【解析】命名缘由直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑧段末段比喻与结论  
【解析】自尊与温柔并立。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。
