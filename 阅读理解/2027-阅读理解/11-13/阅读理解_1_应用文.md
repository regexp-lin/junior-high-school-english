# 阅读理解（应用文） **Winter Cross-Country Challenge — Lihua Middle School**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · PE Department + Student Union**  
> **2027 Winter Cross-Country Challenge — "Two Kilometres, Three Friends, One Morning"**  
> **Not about being fast. About being out there together.**

The cold season makes many of us stay indoors more than we should. To keep our breath full and our mornings bright, the PE Department and the Student Union are together organising the 2027 Winter Cross-Country Challenge. It is not a race in the usual sense: **every student who finishes earns the same finisher's card**, and the race is won together, by the whole school, mile by mile.

---

## 🗓 Key Dates at a Glance

| Date | What Happens |
|---|---|
| Mon 15 Nov – Sun 21 Nov | **Open sign-up** (outside Room 214 or on the school mini-app). |
| Sat 28 Nov, 09:00 | **Practice Run 1** — 1 km, slow group, whole school welcomes. |
| Sat 5 Dec, 09:00 | **Practice Run 2** — 1.5 km, pair-up run. |
| **Sat 12 Dec, 08:45** | **Main Challenge Day** — 2 km loop in the school park. |
| Tue 15 Dec, 16:00 | Finisher cards handed out in the sports hall. |

---

## 🛤 The 2-km Route

Start at the south gate → along the old plane-tree road → past the library → one lap around the small lake → return along the bamboo path → back to the south gate. Volunteers will stand every **200 metres** with water, small towels, and a clear route sign. The route is **flat and paved**; no steep slopes.

---

## 👟 Four Run Groups

**Group A — Slow Group (約 16:00 / km).** For students who rarely run. Walk-run-walk allowed. **This is the recommended group for most of us.**

**Group B — Steady Group (約 9:00 / km).** For students who already run once a week.

**Group C — Faster Group (約 7:00 / km).** For members of the school track-and-field team and regular runners.

**Group D — Pair Group.** Two students run together, holding a light ribbon, neither ahead of nor behind the other. All four grades welcome.

Every group has the **same finisher card**. There is no "first place", only "finishers".

---

## 🙌 How to Take Part

1. Fill in the one-page entry form (outside Room 214 or mini-app → PE → Cross-Country).  
2. Choose **one group** and one run date.  
3. Ask a parent or form teacher to sign the small health-note at the bottom ("My child has no recent fever, no heart illness, and will eat breakfast before the run").  
4. Return the form to Mr. Lin, PE office, by **Friday 21 November, 17:30**.

---

## 🎽 What to Wear / Bring

- **Soft running shoes** (no new shoes on race day).  
- **Three thin layers** — T-shirt + long-sleeve + light jacket — not one big coat.  
- A warm hat and a pair of gloves (we are running outside in December).  
- A small water bottle.  
- **Leave behind**: mobile phones, earphones, keys, cash — our volunteers cannot keep them safe while running.

---

## 🚫 When You Should NOT Run That Day

- You have had fever / cough in the last 3 days.  
- You did not eat breakfast.  
- Your form teacher has not signed your note.  
- The outside temperature drops **below -5 °C** (we will move the date one week).  
- You feel any chest pain or sharp knee pain during warm-up.

---

## 🤝 Volunteers Wanted (34 spots)

- **14 route guards** — stand every 200 m, cheer runners, hand out water.  
- **6 pacers** — trained by Mr. Lin to run with the Slow Group at a steady pace.  
- **4 first-aid helpers** — students who already completed the school's first-aid short course.  
- **4 photographers** — we will print a small paper called *Two Kilometres Together*.  
- **6 end-line helpers** — hand out the finisher cards, warm ginger water, a small towel.

Sign-up deadline: **Tue 25 November, 17:30**.

---

## 🏁 The Finisher Card

Every student who completes 2 km receives a card, the same size as a library card, printed with:

- the runner's name and class;  
- the finishing time (only if you ask for it — otherwise left blank);  
- one handwritten sentence from a PE teacher;  
- the same quiet line at the bottom for everyone:

> *"You breathed the cold morning in and out and came back. That is already the race."*

---

## 💡 Three Quiet Rules

1. **Cheer every classmate — especially the last five.** They are the heart of this race.  
2. **Do not push any classmate to finish faster than their own body wants.**  
3. **No running alone before 08:30 or after sunset.** Winter mornings look safer than they are.

> "A cross-country challenge is a school's way of promising itself that the **cold cannot win** the whole year. Even at 6 °C, even with our noses red, we will step outside together for two soft kilometres — and the year, for a few hours, will belong to our breath."  
> — *Mr. Lin, PE Department, 13 November 2027*
