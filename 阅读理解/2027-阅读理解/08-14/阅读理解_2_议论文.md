# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle Schools Still Give Homework Over the Summer Holiday?**

① Every July, as the last bell of the school year rings, middle-school students across our city carry home a familiar heavy bag: the **summer holiday homework pack**. There are exercise books for Chinese, Maths, English, Physics, and sometimes extra reading lists and online tasks. Recently, the city education office has asked a sharp question in a public meeting: **should middle schools still give homework over the whole summer holiday, or should the holiday be really free?** Parents, teachers and students give very different answers. I believe that summer homework should **not** be cancelled, but it must be **carefully limited**.

② Those who want to **cancel** summer homework have their reasons. First, they say, after a long and tiring school year, children need real rest. "A holiday that still feels like school is not a holiday," my cousin said last July. Second, too much written work stops students from exploring their own interests — reading novels, going hiking, helping grandparents in the countryside, or even simply sleeping enough. Third, some argue that a lot of summer homework is copied from the internet or from smart classmates, so it actually teaches students nothing except how to "hand something in".

③ These points are fair, and I agree with them partly. However, I still think **some** summer homework is needed for clear reasons.

④ The first reason is **memory**. Our brain is not a computer hard disk; knowledge that is not used for six or seven weeks can quickly fade. In September, many Grade 8 students already find it hard to remember basic English tenses or maths formulas they learned in June. A little regular practice in July and August — even twenty minutes a day — protects what the students worked hard for. Without such practice, the first month of the new term easily becomes a painful "re-learning" month.

⑤ The second reason is **habit**. Middle school is exactly the stage when study habits are being built. A completely homework-free summer of two months can break the rhythm of daily reading and thinking. When school starts again, students often feel tired and slow for several weeks. Short daily tasks — a book chapter, a short English diary, a small maths problem set — keep the mind gently active, like a bicycle kept quietly moving forward.

⑥ The third reason is **fairness**. Not every family can pay for summer courses or foreign camps. For many students, especially in smaller neighbourhoods, a well-designed homework pack from the school may be their **main learning support** during the summer. Cancelling all homework without giving anything in return may simply widen the gap between rich and poor families.

⑦ Having said this, I do **not** believe that today's summer homework is in the right shape. Many students complain that their holiday pack contains dozens of tests copied from last year's textbooks. Some homework is simply "for quantity", not for thinking. So my idea is not "keep everything as now", but "**less, smarter, and more personal**".

⑧ I would suggest three changes. First, **reduce the amount**: two to three short tasks a day, not six. Second, **offer choice**: for example, a student who loves history may write a mini-report on a local museum, while another who loves science may watch a short documentary and make notes. Third, **leave at least ten full "free days"** with no homework at all, so students can really travel, rest or help at home. In this way, the summer becomes both restful and growing.

⑨ So, should schools still give summer homework? My answer is: **yes, but less, smarter, and more personal.** A holiday should not be a second school term, but it should not be a long empty wall either. The summer can be a garden where students rest and grow at the same time — if the "watering" is done wisely.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main view?**

A. Summer homework should be completely cancelled.  
B. Summer homework should stay exactly the same as today.  
C. Summer homework should still exist, but must be less, smarter and more personal.  
D. Only Grade 9 students need summer homework.

**2. According to the passage, why is a little summer practice helpful for memory?**

A. Because our brain is a computer hard disk that never forgets.  
B. Because knowledge that is not used for six or seven weeks can fade quickly, and regular short practice protects what was learned.  
C. Because memory is not really important in modern life.  
D. Because teachers want to punish their students in the holidays.

**3. Why does the writer think cancelling all summer homework may be unfair?**

A. Because school teachers would lose their jobs.  
B. Because not every family can pay for summer courses, so school homework may be the main free learning support for many students.  
C. Because rich students enjoy doing homework more.  
D. Because the government needs more paper factories.

**4. Which idea is **NOT** one of the writer's three suggestions for improving summer homework?**

A. Reducing the amount to two or three short tasks a day.  
B. Offering choice so that students can follow their own interests.  
C. Leaving at least ten full free days with no homework.  
D. Adding a big final exam on the first day of September.

**5. What is the writer's attitude to people who want to cancel all summer homework?**

A. The writer completely agrees and wants the same.  
B. The writer thinks their points are fair in part, but still prefers keeping some carefully limited homework.  
C. The writer strongly looks down on them.  
D. The writer thinks they are just lazy students.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 & 第⑨段 "yes, but less, smarter, and more personal"  
【解析】作者立场清晰：保留暑假作业，但要更少、更聪明、更个性化。A、B、D 均与文中立场不符。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "knowledge that is not used for six or seven weeks can quickly fade ... A little regular practice ... protects what the students worked hard for"  
【解析】原文指出记忆需要维持，短期练习可保护所学。A 项与原文"not a computer hard disk"相反；C、D 属无中生有。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "Not every family can pay for summer courses ... school may be their main learning support"  
【解析】原文明确指出取消全部作业可能使贫富差距扩大。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑧段三条建议  
【解析】作者三条建议是减量、提供选择、留出自由天数，未提到"加大期初考试"。故选 D。A、B、C 均为原文建议。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】写作态度题  
【定位】第②段 "have their reasons" & 第③段 "These points are fair, and I agree with them partly"  
【解析】作者承认反对者的理由合理，但仍主张保留有限度的作业。A 项过度认同；C、D 与文中语气明显不符。故选 B。
