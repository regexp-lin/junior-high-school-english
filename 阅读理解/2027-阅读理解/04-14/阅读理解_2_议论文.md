# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Schools Require a Lunchtime Nap?**

① In many Chinese middle schools, when the lunch bell rings at 12:30, students quickly finish their rice and then **lie down on their desks** for a 30-minute nap. In other countries, the same time is filled with games, chatting, or a walk outside. Now, some schools are asking a clear question: **should a lunchtime nap be a rule, just like morning exercise?** Let us see the two sides.

② Supporters give strong reasons. First, they point to **science**. After a big lunch, blood flows more to the stomach than to the brain. Without a short rest, many students feel sleepy in the first afternoon class. A 20–30 minute nap, studies show, can make students feel **alert and happier** for the rest of the day. Some doctors even say that regular short naps are "a quiet gift to the growing brain".

③ There is also a fairness reason. In Chinese homes, some children sleep only **six or seven hours** at night because of homework and extra classes. A short daytime nap helps these tired students catch up. Without a fixed nap, students with noisy classmates nearby, or with too much to do at lunch, may never find a quiet time to close their eyes. A common rule helps **everyone rest equally**.

④ However, others worry that a forced nap may not work for all. Some students just **cannot fall asleep** with their heads on a hard desk. They lie silently for 30 minutes, bored, sore in the neck, and then wake up more tired than before. In this way, the nap becomes a small punishment rather than a rest. Forcing such students to lie quietly may also make them **hate school rules** more in general.

⑤ There is also the question of **other useful lunch activities**. Many schools have wonderful clubs — chess, art, choir, English corner — that naturally meet during the lunch break. If every student must stay at the desk for a nap, these clubs may shrink, and students who love creative things may lose a precious corner of their school life. A one-size-fits-all rule may actually steal some colour from the day.

⑥ Maybe the answer is a **soft rule instead of a hard rule**. Schools can still keep a quiet "rest period" of 20 minutes, but **not force every student to sleep**. Students can choose to sleep, read quietly, stretch gently, or join a soft-voice club activity. Only loud noise and running around should be stopped. In this way, those who need sleep get sleep, and those who need movement or chat get those too. The lunchtime becomes a **healing middle**, not a small battle between rule and feeling.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末问题  
【解析】讨论午休是否强制。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "alert and happier"  
【解析】更清醒愉快。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "only six or seven hours at night"  
【解析】补回睡眠不足。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "cannot fall asleep... more tired than before"  
【解析】难入睡者更疲。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥段 "soft rule instead of a hard rule"  
【解析】安静时段+自由选择。故选 B。
