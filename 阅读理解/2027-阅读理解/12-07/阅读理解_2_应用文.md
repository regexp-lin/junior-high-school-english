# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Winter Solstice Dumpling Day 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Home Science Department + Student Union**  
> **Winter Solstice Dumpling Day 2027** — *"One Short Day, One Warm Table, One Old Custom."*  
> **Date**: **Tuesday, 22 December 2027 (Winter Solstice — dōng zhì 冬至)**  
> **Venue**: **Teaching Block A — all 18 classrooms + Cafeteria North Wing**  
> **Hours**: **09:30 – 14:30** (preparation and eating together during lunch extended period)

---

At Lihua, the winter solstice has, since 2019, been one quiet shared meal in the middle of the cold term. On the **shortest day of the year** (sunset at **16:52**, giving us only 9 hours and 23 minutes of daylight), the whole school — Grade 7, Grade 8, Grade 9, plus teachers and kitchen staff — stops classes for two hours, folds dumplings together, eats them together, and, in the old northern saying, *"protects our ears from the cold of the winter."* This is **not a competition**. It is a small ceremony: the dough, the stuffing, the thin pink edges, and twenty-seven pairs of hands at the same long wooden table.

## 🥟 What to Bring (and What NOT to Bring)

**Please bring**:
- **One clean apron** from home (for hygiene).  
- **One small rolling pin** if you have one — otherwise we provide.  
- **Hair band / hair clip** for students with long hair.

**Please do NOT bring**:
- **Raw meat from home.** All meat is prepared by the school kitchen for food safety (temperature-controlled, rinsed and weighed in the morning).  
- **Any sharp kitchen knives.** Only school-issued blunt edge knives are used.  
- **Personal electric kettles or stoves.** Heating is entirely in the school kitchen.

---

## 🧑‍🍳 Menu & Filling Options

The kitchen has prepared **four fillings** this year, pre-weighed into stainless bowls per class of 27:

1. **Classic Pork & Chinese Chive** (*zhū ròu jiǔ cài* 猪肉韭菜) — 4.2 kg/class  
2. **Egg, Tofu & Three Mushrooms** (vegetarian) — 3.8 kg/class  
3. **Shrimp & Celery with a small ginger note** — 2.5 kg/class  
4. **Pure Vegetable Five-Colour** (carrot, celery, corn, mushroom, tofu) — 3.0 kg/class (for allergies & dietary needs)

Each class will prepare roughly **650 dumplings**. Each student will fold **about 24 dumplings** (≈ 8 per filling type of their choice). Side dish: a simple stir-fried bok choy per cafeteria row. Warm drink: ginger-honey tea (no caffeine).

## ⚠️ Food Safety & Allergy Rules

- Any student with a food allergy must **sign the "Allergy Card"** at the homeroom desk by **Mon 21 Dec, 15:00**. Cards are coloured: **green** (shrimp-free), **yellow** (gluten-alternative: we offer rice-flour wrappers), **red** (strict vegan). Labels are pinned to that student's table position during lunch.  
- No one eats food they have **themselves dropped** on the table or floor — even if the wrapper looks fine. A small bin is at each table.  
- All fillings are **cooked before serving**; raw tasting is forbidden.  
- A school-nurse station is set up in the **cafeteria hallway (12:00 – 13:00)** with trained staff.

I made one with no filling by mistake. It was funny."* The school will supply simple small kraft-paper envelopes.

## 🧾 Small Practical Notes

- **No class ranking or dumpling contest.** We do not compete on speed, shape, or number.  
- **Teachers will also fold.** In 2024, Mr Shen, our physics teacher, folded 34 dumplings in 90 min, a school record that has not been broken — but that is a joke, not a target.  
- **Kitchen-staff table**: one long table in the cafeteria is reserved for kitchen staff, who will **sit down and eat first** this year, before students. Please give them the seats.  
- **Take-home portion**: each student may take home **3 frozen dumplings** (with school-approved ice pack), in case they want to share with family in the evening.

## 💬 A Word from Ms Hu, Chinese Department Head

> "On the shortest day of the year, in one warm kitchen, twenty-seven pairs of hands make hundreds of small soft parcels of something old. A dumpling is, in some way, a cold-weather poem: thin at the edge, warm at the centre, rounded and closed. Tomorrow will be slightly longer; the day after, longer again. Spring is already, quietly, on its way. This small lunch is our way of saying: **we noticed the winter, we sat at the same table, we fed each other. We are not afraid of December.**"  
> — *Ms Hu, Chinese Department, 7 December 2027*
