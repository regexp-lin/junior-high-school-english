# 阅读理解（议论文） **Is Listening to Music While Studying a Good Idea?**

① On a quiet Saturday afternoon, walk into the study rooms of almost any middle-school student and you will hear a soft hum. Most of the time, it does not come from the TV or the phone ring — it comes from a pair of **earphones**. According to a recent survey in seven big Chinese cities, **more than 60%** of Grade 7–9 students say they sometimes or often listen to music while doing homework. But does music really help us study better?

② People who say yes often speak from daily feeling. Soft music, they argue, can **hide noisy neighbours**, humming fridges and street cars. A pleasant tune may also lift the mood, and a happy brain is usually a faster brain. One Grade 8 girl in Shanghai wrote, "When I turn on my favourite piano song, my maths sheet suddenly looks less scary." For many students, music feels like a warm cup of tea beside their books.

③ There is also some science on this side. A few small studies show that **repetitive, slow music with no words**, such as nature sounds or simple piano, can help the brain relax and stay focused for longer. Some hospitals even use such music to help tired patients rest. From this point of view, the right kind of background music may be a gentle helper for certain tasks — mostly simple practice, like copying English words.

④ However, most brain scientists ask us to look more carefully. The brain's "main road" for **listening to words** is the very same road it uses to **read or write words**. If the music has lyrics, the two kinds of words fight for the same space. Eye-tracking tests show that students with song lyrics in their ears read slower, miss more mistakes, and remember fewer new words. A song you love may be worse than a song you feel nothing about.

⑤ The problem grows when homework is difficult. When a student is trying to solve a hard maths problem, understand a long English passage, or plan a Chinese essay, the brain needs most of its energy to build clear steps. Music — even soft music — is an **extra visitor** in the room. Some of the brain's power must serve the visitor, and less is left for the homework. At that moment, silence is often the best "sound".

⑥ So, is music good or bad for study? The answer depends on **what** kind of music, and **what** kind of task. For easy review and repetitive practice, slow music with no lyrics can be a quiet friend. For reading, writing, and hard thinking, a quiet room and a clear mind often win. Before you press "play" next time, ask yourself one question: "Will this song help me, or is it just a pleasant shadow that takes my mind away from my book?"

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What does the recent survey in the passage show?**

A. Most students never listen to music at home.  
B. More than 60% of Grade 7–9 students sometimes or often listen to music while doing homework.  
C. All students prefer to study in loud cafés.  
D. Classical music is the only type students like.

**2. Why do supporters think music helps study?**

A. It gives students free energy drinks.  
B. It makes the Internet faster.  
C. It can hide noise and lift the mood.  
D. It replaces the need for new textbooks.

**3. According to Paragraph 3, which kind of music may help the brain relax?**

A. Loud rock music with strong drums.  
B. Slow music with no words, such as nature sounds or simple piano.  
C. Songs in a language the student does not understand but with fast beats.  
D. Songs about famous pop stars.

**4. Why do brain scientists say songs with lyrics may hurt study?**

A. Lyrics use the same "road" of the brain as reading and writing.  
B. Lyrics often make earphones too hot.  
C. Lyrics always contain rude words.  
D. Lyrics cannot be sung in Chinese.

**5. What is the writer's final suggestion?**

A. Play music at the highest volume for every subject.  
B. Choose music by what you study and what kind of music it is.  
C. Never listen to any music at home.  
D. Study only after 10 p.m., when the house is quiet.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末句 "more than 60% of Grade 7–9 students"  
【解析】调查显示超 60% 学生偶尔或经常写作业时听音乐。A、C、D 与原文不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "hide noisy neighbours... lift the mood"  
【解析】支持者理由是掩盖噪音+提升心情。A、B、D 均无依据。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "repetitive, slow music with no words, such as nature sounds or simple piano"  
【解析】缓慢无词音乐可帮助大脑放松。A、C、D 与原文不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段 "The brain's 'main road' for listening to words is the very same road it uses to read or write words"  
【解析】歌词与读写使用同一脑区，因而干扰。B、C、D 均无依据。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "depends on what kind of music, and what kind of task"  
【解析】作者建议因任务与音乐类型而定。A、C、D 为极端或偏离。故选 B。
