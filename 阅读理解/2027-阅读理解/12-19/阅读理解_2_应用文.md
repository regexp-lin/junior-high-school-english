① For the twelfth year in a row, Lihua Middle School's PE Department and Student Union open, on one quiet Saturday afternoon of December, a small door into a pleasure that many of our students have never tried: **standing upright on two thin blades of steel, on a large sheet of perfectly flat, cold, smooth ice.** This year, on **27 December 2027**, **three and a half hours** at the Beihua Indoor Rink are booked entirely for Lihua students in Grades 7–9. Two qualified coaches, eighteen student captains, warm lemon tea, and about **180 pairs of rental skates** in every size are waiting. **No student has ever skated?** Perfect. **You have skated five times before?** Also perfect. The afternoon is for everybody, gentle, optional, and one-day only.

## 🧭 Three Zones on the Rink

| Zone | Location | Who it is for | Speed rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Beginner Zone** | West half of the rink, marked by soft red cones | Students who have never skated or have skated only 1–2 times | Walking pace; no forward gliding faster than slow-walk speed |
| **Intermediate Lane** | Outer big oval, east–north side | Students comfortable gliding and stopping | Moderate pace; no sudden turns |
| **Show Corner (small)** | Small painted square at the south-east corner | Any student with coach's OK who wants to practise small spins / simple backward skate | Skater-at-your-own-risk; captain watching |

- **Do not cross from Beginner Zone into the Intermediate Lane without a captain's nod.**  
- **No chasing, no hand-holding chains** of more than three students (they cause large fall groups).  
- **Skate in one direction only** in the Intermediate Lane (counter-clockwise, the standard international skating direction).

---

## 🛡️ Safety Gear (what you MUST wear)

- **Helmet** (provided free; please do not bring your own bicycle helmet — they do not fit skating falls).  
- **Thin gloves** (a fall on the ice can cut a bare hand on a skate blade; even mittens are fine).  
- **Long trousers** (no short skirts, no bare knees — ice burn is quick).  
- **Thick socks** (one pair is enough; two pairs in rental skates often cause painful pressure).  
- **OPTIONAL but recommended**: knee pads + wrist guards (provided free at the sign-in desk — first 120 students).

---

## 🤕 Injury & Kindness Rules

- **Nurse Song is on rink-side from 13:40 to 16:55**, in a bright yellow vest, with a small red medical bag.  
- **A captain or coach MUST be informed** of any fall harder than a gentle sit.  
- **If a wrist, elbow or head hit the ice hard**, the student must STOP skating for the rest of the day and ice the area for 10 minutes. This is non-negotiable.  
- **Dizziness / nausea / fainting**: 1 captain + 1 teacher take the student to the foyer rest-bench immediately; call home by 16:00 at the latest.  
- **Girls on their period**: warm tea is free, rest-bench is always available, no pressure to keep skating; PE Department fully respects private reasons to rest.

---

## 🙋 Student Captain Roles (18 captains)

- **6 Beginner-Zone captains** (pair up new skaters, hold hands for the first lap only, count every student every 10 minutes).  
- **6 Intermediate-Lane captains** (watch speed, remind about counter-clockwise direction, ready to guide fallen skaters to the side).  
- **3 Foyer & Lockers captains** (help with skate fitting, lockers, lost items).  
- **3 Kindness-Table captains** (serve hot lemon tea, ginger biscuits, and warm-up towels; quietly watch for cold-shaky classmates).

Captains are recognised by **green armbands** on their left sleeves. If you are unsure of any rule, ask a green armband first, then a teacher.

---

## 🧣 Clothing & Warm-up Advice

- **Three thin layers** (base + fleece + windbreaker) — the rink is kept at **about 6–8 °C** indoors. The ice radiates a lot of cold upward.  
- **Ears covered** (a thin knitted hat is OK under the helmet).  
- **Long scarf NOT recommended** — loose scarf ends can catch in a skate blade.  
- **No long earrings, no long necklaces, no phone lanyards around the neck**: all can become painful in a fall.  
- **Tied-back hair** strongly recommended for girls with long hair.

---

## 💴 Money & Rentals

- **Skate rental**: **free** (sponsored by the school's Culture Fund for this one day only).  
- **Helmet rental**: **free**.  
- **Locker**: **free** (bring a ¥1 coin returned at end).  
- **Warm drinks and ginger biscuits**: **free** (one cup and one biscuit per student; seconds allowed after 16:00).  
- **Skate-sharpening**: **not needed** — the rink sharpens its own skates.

---

## 🚌 Bus Rules (very important)

- **The only way to arrive** is the school shuttle bus at 13:00; students may not make their own way there.  
- **The only way to return** is the shuttle at 17:00; students may not leave alone afterwards (except to be picked up by a parent signed-in at the rink foyer by 16:45).  
- **No phones with headphones on the bus** — the captain needs to count twice.  
- **No eating or drinking on the bus** (sudden turns cause spills).

---

## ❗ Important Gentle Reminders

- **This is not a race. It is not a show.** Nobody's speed is measured; nobody's photo is posted.  
- **If you fall, do not apologise** — every beginner falls at least three times. The captains fell four times each at their own first lesson.  
- **Take off your skates at the end and walk one careful lap on rubber mats** before leaving the foyer — ice legs are wobbly on hard ground.  
- **Phones** may be used only in the foyer, not on the ice; skating and scrolling is the fastest way to a broken wrist.

---

## 💬 A Small Word from the PE Department

> "The first time most of us stand on ice in our lives, we are quite scared — of the blades, of the cold, of falling in front of classmates. And yet, about twenty minutes in, a small private miracle happens: the body, almost without being asked, finds its balance; the ankles learn to listen; and the rink becomes a quiet, bright, cool room in which, for a little while, you are taller, slower, more careful, and more amazed by your own body than you have been in months. **Come on Saturday. Fall three times. Laugh about them on the shuttle home.** Then go back to your classrooms on Monday with three small bruises you earned honestly on an ice sheet of your own kind winter."  
> — *PE Department & Student Union, 19 December 2027*
