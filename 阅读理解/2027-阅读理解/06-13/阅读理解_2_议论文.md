# 阅读理解（议论文） **Is It Good for Middle School Students to Watch Short Videos Every Day?**

Short videos are everywhere. On the subway, in the dining hall, even on the school bus, I can see students pulling out their phones and watching one short clip after another. Some people say short videos are harmless, just a small way to relax. However, I believe that **watching too many short videos every day is not good for middle school students**.

The first problem is attention. Most short videos last only 15 to 60 seconds. Every clip is designed to "catch" our eyes quickly with loud music, bright colours and surprises. After hours of such clips, our brain gets used to fast changes. Then, when we open an English article or a maths problem, we feel bored after two minutes. Teachers in my school often say that students who spend more than one hour a day on short videos have more trouble finishing a long reading task. This is not because they are lazy — it is because their attention has become shorter.

The second problem is information. Short videos often give us only a "small piece" of a story, and the piece is not always true. A 30-second clip about history, a disease or a new law cannot explain all the important facts. If we watch many such clips without checking, we may believe something wrong. Worse, some videos are made just to frighten or anger us, because strong feelings get more clicks. We quietly take in these feelings without noticing.

Of course, I do not think we should give up short videos completely. There are many videos that teach us English songs, famous scientists or how to cook an egg. A short, well-made video can open a new door in ten minutes. The problem is not short videos themselves, but watching them **without limit**.

So what can we do? First, set a clear time limit — perhaps 20 minutes a day, not two hours. Second, turn off "auto-play" so the next video does not start by itself. Third, choose videos with a clear purpose: learning a new word, watching a sports highlight, or laughing at one funny family. And finally, read a long article or book at least four times a week, so our brain does not forget how to think slowly.

Short videos are like candy. A little bit sweetens the day. Too much and we lose our appetite for real food.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the author's main opinion?**

A. Short videos should be forbidden at school.  
B. Watching too many short videos every day is not good for middle school students.  
C. Short videos are perfect for students of all ages.  
D. Only older students should watch short videos.

**2. According to paragraph 2, why is attention a problem?**

A. Because short videos are too quiet to hear.  
B. Because students watch without eating.  
C. Because the brain gets used to fast changes and long tasks then feel boring.  
D. Because the phone screen is too bright in the sun.

**3. What does the author say about the information in short videos?**

A. It is always correct and complete.  
B. It is only used by teachers.  
C. It often gives only a small piece of a story and may not be fully true.  
D. It is free of strong feelings.

**4. Which of the following is NOT a suggestion from the author?**

A. Set a clear time limit like 20 minutes a day.  
B. Turn off the "auto-play" function.  
C. Stop reading all long articles and books.  
D. Choose videos with a clear purpose.

**5. What does the last paragraph mainly say?**

A. Short videos are exactly the same as candy.  
B. Students should never eat candy.  
C. A little is fine, but too much harms our ability to enjoy deeper things.  
D. Candy has more sugar than short videos.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第 1 段末句  
【解析】作者反对学生每日过量看短视频。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段 "our brain gets used to fast changes..."  
【解析】大脑习惯快速刺激，长任务难坚持。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段 "only a 'small piece' of a story..."  
【解析】信息片段化且可能不全真。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题（反向）  
【定位】第 5 段建议清单  
【解析】作者建议继续读长文。A/B/D 均为建议。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】末段 "sweetens the day. Too much and we lose our appetite..."  
【解析】比喻：少则甜蜜，多则伤胃口。A/B/D 均片面。故选 C。
