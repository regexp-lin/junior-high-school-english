# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Every Classroom Have a "Silent Reading Corner"?**

① In our Chinese teacher's classroom, there is a quiet corner. Two soft chairs, a small lamp, and a simple wooden shelf with about fifty books. Above the chairs hangs a small paper sign: "**Please read in silence. Words need space.**" Students may use the corner during the lunch break or after they finish class tasks. Since this small change last term, fewer students run wildly in the corridor at noon, and the teachers report quieter classes in the afternoon. **Should every middle school classroom build such a "silent reading corner"?** I believe they should.

② First, a silent reading corner gives students a **chance to read for themselves**, not only to pass tests. In normal lessons, we read paragraphs chosen by the textbook. In the quiet corner, a student may pick a comic about Chinese history, a short science article, or a short novel about a boy in the mountains. Free reading, done regularly and slowly, grows vocabulary, widens the imagination and deepens interest in school — all without anyone shouting "Be quiet!".

③ Second, such a corner offers a **soft rest** between busy classes. A 10-minute lunch break in a loud classroom is not a rest at all; it may even make students more tired. Sitting in a chair with a soft lamp and a book, by contrast, lowers a student's heart rate and gives the brain a little cool shade. In the afternoon, the student comes back to math or physics feeling refreshed instead of fried.

④ Third, a corner like this teaches **respect for others**. The rule "Please read in silence" is not only about books. It teaches students that the school is a shared space, and that our noise always touches other people. Students who have practised being quiet for their classmates for a year may become, in the future, adults who speak gently on trains, in hospitals and in crowded offices.

⑤ Some people may worry that a reading corner will take too much classroom space, or that the books may be damaged. These worries are reasonable, but small. Two chairs and a shelf can fit beside the back wall of almost any classroom. Damage rarely happens when students are the ones choosing, placing, and caring for their own books. A **student rota** — with each class member checking the corner for one week — solves the problem at almost no cost.

⑥ A classroom with a reading corner is not a strange invention. It is the return of something very simple and very old: a quiet place, a lamp, and a book. If every classroom in our school had one, perhaps the whole building would breathe a little more slowly, think a little more deeply, and feel a little more **like a school, and a little less like a factory**.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main idea of the passage?**

A. All classrooms should have more TVs.  
B. Every middle school classroom should build a silent reading corner.  
C. Students should not read during lunch breaks.  
D. Schools should build more football fields.

**2. What is NOT a benefit of a silent reading corner, according to the writer?**

A. Students can read for their own interest, not only for tests.  
B. It offers a soft rest between classes.  
C. It teaches respect for others.  
D. It makes students run faster in sports lessons.

**3. Why does the writer say a loud lunch break is not a real rest?**

A. Because students forget to eat.  
B. Because a loud break may make students even more tired.  
C. Because loud noise makes the wall dirty.  
D. Because students sleep too long.

**4. How does the writer suggest the school take care of the books?**

A. Only the head teacher reads them.  
B. A student rota — each class member checks the corner for one week — can solve the problem at almost no cost.  
C. The books are locked away every evening.  
D. Students should buy their own books.

**5. What does the writer mean by "more like a school, and a little less like a factory"?**

A. Schools should produce more goods.  
B. Schools should be quieter, deeper and more thoughtful places, not places that only rush.  
C. Factories should close.  
D. Schools should use bigger machines.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末及全文  
【解析】作者支持班班设阅读角。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题（反向）  
【定位】第②–④段三大好处  
【解析】A/B/C 均属文中好处；D 与跑步无关。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "may even make students more tired."  
【解析】喧闹休息反而更累。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "A student rota... solves the problem at almost no cost."  
【解析】值日制度。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题／主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段末  
【解析】学校应安静深刻，而非只是机械运作。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
