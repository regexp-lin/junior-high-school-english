# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle-School Students Keep a Daily Handwritten Diary?**

Walk into any middle-school dormitory or quiet corner of the library, and you will find two kinds of people: one tapping quickly on a phone, the other writing slowly in a small paper notebook. Both are recording their thoughts, but the second group is keeping a **handwritten diary**. Some classmates laugh at them: "Why waste time writing by hand in 2027?" In my view, **keeping a handwritten diary for ten minutes every evening is still one of the best habits for a middle-school student — even if it feels old-fashioned**.

My first reason is **memory**. Many studies in recent years have shown that writing by hand pushes our brain to work harder than typing. When we write, we must plan each sentence carefully, because deleting is slower than pressing the **Delete** key. Our eyes move along the letters; our fingers feel each shape; our heart chooses the best word. After a week, the words written by hand are still alive in our heads; the words we typed on a phone have often disappeared like smoke. For someone who spends seven hours in class every day, a diary is not just a book — it is a way to **keep today alive tomorrow**.

A second reason is **emotion**. Middle school is a time of strong feelings: anxious exam days, sudden fights with friends, big dreams, small fears. If we keep these feelings only in our head, they can grow into heavy clouds. If we speak them out, we may say too much, too fast, to the wrong person. A diary is a safe third space. Pen on paper is slow enough to calm us down, private enough to hold the truth. Many adults look back and say, "My diary was my first real friend." That friendship is still worth building today.

Of course, some classmates argue that a **typed diary** on a phone is just as good. They may say, "It is faster, it is saved in the cloud, and I can even add voice and photos." These are real advantages. But a phone is not only a diary; it is also a small noise factory. One message pops up; one app sends a notification; ten minutes later, the diary has become a scroll through short videos. Handwriting, by contrast, demands attention. When the pen is on paper, nothing else can happen at the same time. In a world that pulls our focus into many pieces, a diary becomes training for the mind.

Those who are still doubtful often make a final point: "I have nothing to write." This worry is common, but it misses the point. A handwritten diary does not need a great story. Three simple lines — **one good thing, one bad thing, one small plan for tomorrow** — are enough. Written every day for a month, they quietly build a map of who we are becoming.

In short, a handwritten diary is not a return to an old world. It is a modern tool for memory, calm, and focus. Ten minutes of pen and paper each evening will not change the news or the exam schedule. But it will change something far more important — it will change us.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Typing is always better than writing.  
B. Students should never use phones.  
C. Keeping a handwritten diary for ten minutes every evening is still one of the best habits for students today.  
D. Only adults should write diaries.

**2. Why does the writer say handwriting helps memory?**

A. Because paper is cheaper than phones.  
B. Because writing is slower and makes the brain work harder; the hand and eye also help memory.  
C. Because teachers can check it quickly.  
D. Because handwriting always uses red ink.

**3. Why can a diary be useful for strong emotions?**

A. It lets us share feelings publicly.  
B. It is noisier than talking.  
C. It is a safe and slow space, private enough to hold the truth.  
D. It changes our feelings into money.

**4. What problem does the writer mention about typing a diary on a phone?**

A. The phone has no memory.  
B. The phone can easily distract us with messages, apps and short videos.  
C. The phone cannot type Chinese characters.  
D. The phone is too heavy for a student.

**5. According to the writer, what is the easiest way to start a daily diary?**

A. Write a long story of 500 words every day.  
B. Write three lines: one good thing, one bad thing, one small plan for tomorrow.  
C. Only write when something amazing happens.  
D. Write a diary only on Sundays.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】首段尾句  
【解析】每晚十分钟手写日记是优良习惯。A/B/D 均偏离。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"writing by hand pushes our brain to work harder..."  
【解析】过程更深加工，强化记忆。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"a safe third space... slow... private..."  
【解析】安全、缓慢、私密。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"a small noise factory... notification... short videos"  
【解析】手机易被打扰。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"one good thing, one bad thing, one small plan..."  
【解析】三行日记法。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
