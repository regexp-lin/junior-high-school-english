# 阅读理解（议论文） **Is It Still Worth Keeping a Daily Diary in the Age of Short Videos?**

① Every year, at the beginning of a new term, teachers at our school hand out a small, thin notebook and say, "Please write a diary every evening." Most students groan quietly. In a world of short videos, voice messages and fast photos, keeping a daily diary can feel a little old-fashioned. A cousin of mine once said, "Why write ten lines tonight when I can send a three-second video right now?" So, **is it still worth keeping a daily diary in 2027?** I believe it is — perhaps more worth it than ever.

② Those who disagree have reasonable reasons. First, they say that a diary takes too much time: 10 to 20 minutes every night, sometimes more, after a long day of classes. Second, the modern phone already "remembers" everything for us — photos, chat history, short videos — so the diary seems to repeat what the phone has already done. Third, some feel that writing about feelings is **embarrassing**, especially if a parent or classmate might secretly read the notebook one day.

③ These worries deserve a calm answer. None of them, in my view, is strong enough to close the diary forever.

④ First, a diary does take time, but **slow writing is itself part of the benefit**. When we type a quick chat message, we do not really think; we just react. But when a pen moves slowly across paper, the brain is forced to **sort feelings into words**. "I was a little angry, but maybe I was more tired than angry." "I said something rude to Ming, and I think I should say sorry tomorrow." This slow sorting is called **reflection**, and it is very hard to do while scrolling through short videos. A few minutes a night, in exchange for a clearer mind, is not a bad price.

⑤ Second, a phone remembers **data**, not **meaning**. A photo of our dinner shows what we ate, but not how we felt about the quiet moment when grandfather put the last piece of fish onto my plate. Video is powerful, but it lives on the outside of a moment; a diary walks into the inside. Looking back at old diaries years later, students often say, "I remember that day clearly now." But they almost never say this when looking through old photo albums.

⑥ Third, the worry about being read can be answered by a few simple habits. Write in a notebook that only you use. Keep the key on a private keyring. If a parent reads your diary without permission, that is a family conversation to have, not a reason to stop writing. Many writers, from old Chinese teachers to modern scientists, say that a private diary is one of the first safe spaces where **young people can practise honesty** — and honesty, once practised, slowly becomes a habit.

⑦ There are also positive reasons that are often forgotten. Writing a diary improves our **language skills** — in Chinese and, if we try, in English. Students who keep a small English diary of just five lines a day often report much more natural writing by the end of a term. A diary is also a **calendar of small wins**: small things we have done, small people we have helped, small mistakes we have repaired. On a bad day, turning back ten pages is a quiet way to remember that our life is, in fact, slowly moving forward.

⑧ Of course, I am not saying every page must be long and deep. Some nights, three short lines are enough: "Today was rainy. I lost my new pen. I told the truth about the English homework." Some weekends, a longer page about a trip or a friendship feels right. Quality matters more than length. It is much better to write **three honest lines** every night than to write ten pages once every three months.

⑨ So, is it still worth keeping a daily diary? My answer is a firm yes. A phone records the day; a diary **understands** the day. In a world where everything is fast and loud, a small, slow notebook under the desk lamp is an easy, old, and still surprising way for a middle-school student to grow a quieter, clearer, kinder self.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Keeping a daily diary is out of date and should be replaced with short videos.  
B. Keeping a daily diary is still very much worth it in 2027.  
C. Only Grade 9 students should keep a diary.  
D. A student should keep a diary only on weekends.

**2. According to the passage, which is one of the reasons given by people who **oppose** keeping a diary?**

A. Diaries are too heavy to carry.  
B. Writing a diary takes too much time every evening.  
C. Diaries are not sold in shops any more.  
D. Parents never buy diaries for their children.

**3. How does the writer answer the worry that "my phone already remembers everything"?**

A. By saying that the phone is more reliable than the diary.  
B. By saying that a phone remembers data, but a diary holds **meaning** — how we felt inside a moment.  
C. By saying that the writer hates phones.  
D. By saying that photos are always false.

**4. What extra benefit of keeping a diary is mentioned in paragraph ⑦?**

A. It makes students stronger in sports.  
B. It improves language skills in Chinese and, if the student tries, in English.  
C. It helps families to save money.  
D. It keeps pets happy.

**5. Which sentence best shows the writer's final view?**

A. "A phone records the day; a diary understands the day."  
B. "Most students groan quietly."  
C. "Quality matters less than length."  
D. "The modern phone already remembers everything for us."

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "perhaps more worth it than ever" & 第⑨段 "a firm yes"  
【解析】作者立场鲜明：日记仍值得坚持。A、C、D 均与立场不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第一条 "a diary takes too much time: 10 to 20 minutes every night"  
【解析】原文直接给出理由。A、C、D 均无文中依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "a phone remembers data, not meaning ... Video is powerful, but it lives on the outside of a moment; a diary walks into the inside"  
【解析】作者区分 data/meaning，强调日记记录意义。A、C、D 均与作者立场不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "Writing a diary improves our language skills — in Chinese and, if we try, in English"  
【解析】原文明确列出该好处。A、C、D 均未在文中提及。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑨段 "A phone records the day; a diary understands the day"  
【解析】该句最凝练地表达全篇立场。B、D 属描述性或反面观点；C 与作者"质量重于长度"相反。故选 A。
