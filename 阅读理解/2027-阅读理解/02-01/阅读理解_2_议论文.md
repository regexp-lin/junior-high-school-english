# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Students Use Mobile Phones Between Classes?**

① In more and more middle schools, teachers and parents are asking the same question: should students be allowed to use their mobile phones between classes? Some people say yes, but many others strongly disagree. This question is not as simple as it looks.

② Those who agree often point out that phones are useful tools. Between classes, students can quickly check the weather, ask a parent to bring a coat, or find the meaning of a new English word. Some apps can even help students review math formulas in five minutes. In their eyes, a ten-minute break with a phone is no different from a ten-minute break with a book.

③ However, this idea has clear problems. First, ten minutes is never enough for a phone. Once a short video or a game starts, students often cannot stop. They may still be holding the phone when the bell rings. As a result, they enter the next class with a tired mind and find it hard to listen. A recent survey in twenty schools showed that students who used phones between classes scored **8 percent lower** in English and math than those who did not.

④ Second, phones reduce real communication between friends. During the ten-minute break, the healthiest choice is to walk around, drink some water, talk, and laugh with classmates. Eyes need to look at the far sky, and legs need to move. If everyone keeps their head down over a small screen, the playground becomes strangely quiet, and friendship becomes thinner and thinner.

⑤ Of course, banning phones completely is not the answer either. Some students take the bus home alone and need a way to contact their parents. A good rule should find a middle way: phones may be kept in the school but must stay in the school bag during class time **and** during short breaks; they can only be used before 8:00 a.m. and after 4:30 p.m., or with the teacher's clear permission in an emergency.

⑥ A school is a place to learn, to grow, and to build real friendships. The ten minutes between classes is short, yet precious. Let us use it not for a screen, but for real people, real air and a real rest. That is the wiser choice.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main topic of this passage?**

A. How to teach students to use phones correctly.  
B. Whether students should use phones between classes.  
C. How phones change students' eyesight.  
D. Why phones are better than books.

**2. According to Paragraph 3, what problem do phones often cause between classes?**

A. Students cannot stop and enter the next class tired.  
B. Phones run out of battery quickly.  
C. Students break the phone screens easily.  
D. Teachers must repeat the same lesson.

**3. What does the survey in twenty schools show?**

A. Students who use phones more are happier.  
B. English and math scores drop by 8 percent.  
C. Phones help students remember more words.  
D. Phones make classrooms quieter.

**4. What does the writer think is the best solution?**

A. To ban all phones from the school.  
B. To allow phones only during classes.  
C. To keep phones in the bag during class and short breaks.  
D. To give every student a free phone.

**5. What is the writer's attitude in this passage?**

A. The writer is against students using phones between classes.  
B. The writer fully supports students using phones freely.  
C. The writer has no clear opinion on the question.  
D. The writer thinks phones are useless in daily life.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段及全文  
【解析】全文围绕"课间是否可用手机"展开讨论。A 偏窄，C、D 非主旨。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "they enter the next class with a tired mind"  
【解析】学生玩手机停不下来，下节课疲惫。B、C、D 均无依据。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "scored 8 percent lower in English and math"  
【解析】课间用手机的学生英、数成绩低 8%。A、C、D 均非调查结论。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "phones... must stay in the school bag during class time and during short breaks"  
【解析】作者主张课间和上课手机放书包。A 过绝，B 相反，D 无依据。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "Let us use it not for a screen, but for real people"  
【解析】作者明确反对课间用手机。B、D 与文意相反，C 不符，作者态度鲜明。故选 A。
