# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Day I Bought the Wrong Train Ticket**

① It was the last day of the National Day holiday. My mother had asked me to buy train tickets online for our trip back to Shanghai: two tickets, for 13:40, from Suzhou to Shanghai. I had done this twice before, but this time, because I was half-watching a basketball highlight, my fingers tapped too quickly. I only realised my mistake at the station, fifteen minutes before the train, when Mother looked at my phone and her face suddenly changed colour. "Bao Er," she said softly, "these tickets are from **Shanghai** to **Suzhou**, not from Suzhou to Shanghai."

② My stomach went cold. I could feel the edges of my ears becoming hot. The direction was reversed. The train we had to catch was already standing on the platform, but our tickets did not belong to that train. "Mother, I am so sorry," I whispered, looking at the floor. Mother did not shout. She only said, "Let's think. Do not be afraid."

③ We walked, almost running, to the ticket service desk. The queue was long. I kept pulling the strap of my bag, my head down. When we reached the desk, Mother explained calmly to the young woman in a blue uniform. The woman first checked my phone, then her computer. "You have two choices," she said. "First, we can change your tickets to the 14:25 train; there are still seats, and the difference in price is about 20 yuan each. Second, you can ask for a refund and buy new tickets, but those are almost sold out today."

④ Mother looked at me. "What do you think?" she asked. My heart beat fast. I thought for a few seconds. "The first choice — change the tickets — so we are sure to go home tonight." Mother smiled and nodded. The young woman quickly changed the tickets, printed two new pieces of paper, and told us the new platform number. Mother paid the extra 40 yuan. As we walked away, she squeezed my shoulder gently.

⑤ On the platform, with almost an hour to spare, I apologised again. "Mother, I'm really sorry. I wasted your time, and 40 yuan." She sat down next to me and said, "Listen, Bao Er. Mistakes are not the worst thing. The worst thing is to hide a mistake, or to blame other people. You told me right away; you thought about the choices; you made a decision. That is already much better than many adults." She took a small notebook out of her bag. "But," she said, "from next time, please **read the tickets twice before paying**." She drew a little circle on a blank page and wrote the word "twice". Then she passed the notebook to me.

⑥ On the 14:25 train, I looked out of the window at the green fields of Jiangsu turning slowly into the bright buildings of Shanghai. In my pocket, the small notebook pressed against my leg. Since that afternoon, whenever I book any ticket — for a train, a bus, or even a cinema — I read the page twice, then a third time for luck. A 40-yuan mistake, I realised, had taught me a lesson worth much more than 40 yuan.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What mistake did the writer make when buying the train tickets?**

A. Buying tickets for the wrong day.  
B. Buying tickets in the wrong direction — from Shanghai to Suzhou instead of from Suzhou to Shanghai.  
C. Buying tickets for the wrong friend.  
D. Buying too many tickets.

**2. How did Mother react when she learned about the mistake?**

A. She shouted loudly at the writer.  
B. She said softly and calmly, "Let's think. Do not be afraid."  
C. She walked away angrily.  
D. She called the police.

**3. Which choice did the writer decide on at the ticket service desk?**

A. To refund the tickets and buy completely new ones.  
B. To change the tickets to the 14:25 train, paying about 40 yuan more in total.  
C. To take a taxi home.  
D. To stay in Suzhou for one more day.

**4. According to Mother, what is the worst thing, rather than making a mistake?**

A. Telling the truth.  
B. Hiding a mistake or blaming other people.  
C. Asking for help.  
D. Reading the tickets twice.

**5. What lesson did the writer learn at the end of the story?**

A. Train tickets are useless.  
B. A small mistake can teach a lesson worth much more than its price, and the writer now always reads any ticket at least twice before paying.  
C. Never trust service desks.  
D. Mothers should always pay for tickets.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "these tickets are from Shanghai to Suzhou, not from Suzhou to Shanghai."  
【解析】方向反了。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Let's think. Do not be afraid."  
【解析】母亲镇定。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "change your tickets to the 14:25 train..."  
【解析】改签为 14:25 班。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "The worst thing is to hide a mistake, or to blame other people."  
【解析】最糟是掩饰与推责。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题／推理判断题  
【定位】第⑥段  
【解析】错误教会他比代价更重要的道理。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
