# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Teaching Grandma to Use a Smartphone**

① Last winter, my **Grandma** came to live with us for the holidays. She is seventy-two, kind and funny, but her old phone could only make calls and send very short text messages. "I am fine with that," she said with a smile. "I do not need all those new tricks."

② Everything changed on a rainy Wednesday. Grandma wanted to buy fresh vegetables, but the small market by her old neighbourhood had closed down. Mum opened a delivery app on her own phone, and in 30 minutes, a driver arrived at our door with tomatoes, tofu and a kilo of greens. Grandma's eyes grew wide. "That little box can do this?" she asked quietly.

③ That weekend, I became her **private teacher**. I borrowed Dad's spare smartphone, wiped it clean, and wrote a list of **"Big-Button Basics"**: how to switch it on, how to open WeChat, how to read the weather, and most importantly, how to **video-call** my cousin in Kunming. I made every icon large and printed a colour card to stick on the fridge.

④ The first lesson was not easy. Grandma tapped the screen too hard, and the phone jumped from one app to another. "It is flying away from me!" she said, half laughing, half worried. I took her hand gently. "Softly, Grandma, like touching a butterfly." She tried again — lighter this time — and the weather page came up: **sunny, 5°C**. She laughed like a child.

⑤ By the end of the week, Grandma could do three things by herself: order vegetables, scan a QR code to pay at the bakery, and video-call her sister in Chengdu. On Friday night, she video-called my cousin and they talked for forty minutes, showing each other the dumplings they were making. Grandma's cheeks were pink with joy.

⑥ Later that night, Grandma hugged me and said, "You taught me something more than a phone. You taught me that **I can still learn something new at seventy-two**." I went back to my room, but I did not sleep for a long time. I had always thought Grandma was the one teaching me. On that quiet winter night, I realized learning can go both ways — and that is perhaps the warmest kind of family lesson.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why didn't Grandma want to use a smartphone at first?**

A. She didn't like the colour.  
B. She thought she didn't need many new tricks.  
C. Her children didn't allow her.  
D. Phones were too expensive.

**2. What changed Grandma's mind about smartphones?**

A. A free gift from a shop.  
B. Seeing a delivery app order vegetables quickly on a rainy day.  
C. A TV programme.  
D. A trip to Shanghai.

**3. What did the writer do to make learning easier for Grandma?**

A. Made icons large and printed a colour card for the fridge.  
B. Bought a new expensive phone.  
C. Let Grandma watch long videos.  
D. Asked Grandma to join an online class.

**4. Which of the following did Grandma learn by the end of the week?**

A. How to build a website.  
B. How to order vegetables, pay by QR code and video-call.  
C. How to write computer programs.  
D. How to fix a phone.

**5. What did the writer realize at the end?**

A. Grandma was always wrong about technology.  
B. Learning can go both ways between grandparents and grandchildren.  
C. Smartphones are the best gift for all elderly people.  
D. Winter is the best season to study.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末句  
【解析】觉得旧手机足够。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段  
【解析】下雨天看见外卖APP买菜。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段  
【解析】放大图标＋冰箱色卡。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段  
【解析】下单、扫码、视频通话。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑥段末句  
【解析】学习可以双向进行。故选 B。
