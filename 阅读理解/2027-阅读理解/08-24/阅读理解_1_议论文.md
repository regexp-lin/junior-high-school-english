# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Every Middle-School Student Learn Basic First-Aid?**

① Last spring, an unusual event happened on our city's Line 3 metro. An old man suddenly lost consciousness on a moving train. Around him, for almost two long minutes, the crowd simply stood still. Finally a girl, about fourteen years old, pushed through the people and began pressing his chest steadily with both hands. When the ambulance came, the doctor said the girl had saved his life. She had taken a **first-aid class** at her middle school a year before. This story was reported in many city newspapers, and it raised a question that I believe more schools should answer: **should every middle-school student learn basic first-aid?** My firm answer is yes.

② Those who think first-aid lessons are not necessary often give three reasons. First, they say that children will only "play around" with the practice dummies, and will not really learn anything. Second, they worry that untrained students may try to help in a real emergency and make the situation **worse**. Third, they argue that first-aid is a job for adults and for doctors — "our role as students is to study".

③ Each of these worries has a reasonable side, but none of them is strong enough to close the door.

④ The "just playing around" worry underestimates middle-school students. At age 13 or 14, we are not little children any more. In our science and biology classes, we have already learned the main parts of the body, the idea of **blood flow**, and the basics of **breathing**. A two-hour class on how to press the chest, how to call the emergency number, how to place a fainting person safely on the side, is very much within our understanding. Well-designed classes, taught by real paramedics, are taken seriously by the students I know.

⑤ The "may do harm" worry is also largely answered by **good teaching**. The whole point of a first-aid course is to teach exactly when to act and when to wait. Students are told clearly: call 120 first, stay calm, **do not move** an injured person's neck, do not give water to someone who is unconscious. A student who has done even four hours of practice is far less likely to make the wrong move than one who has never learned anything. The real danger is a bystander who freezes — as happened in the first painful minute on Line 3.

⑥ The third worry — "it's a job for doctors" — forgets a simple number: the average ambulance in our city takes about **8 to 12 minutes** to arrive after a call. In those minutes, a brain without oxygen begins to suffer. Doctors themselves say that **what happens in the first few minutes** often decides whether a person survives. The adult with the training, or the student with the training, is the only one who can use those minutes. Waiting for a doctor to appear at the scene is, in many cases, simply too late.

⑦ There are also positive reasons that go beyond emergencies. First-aid classes teach students how to **stay calm under pressure**. They teach us that a human body, although strong, has simple rules: clear airways, steady breathing, gentle bleeding control. They also build a quiet kind of courage: the courage to step forward instead of standing still. In a crowded, busy city, a generation of young people with this courage makes the whole city a little safer.

⑧ Of course, one class in 45 minutes is not enough. I would suggest three things. First, **short but regular lessons**: a 30-minute class every two weeks, not one long "safety day" once a year. Second, **real practice**: dummies, bandages, even a few visits to the hospital education centre. Third, **a simple school certificate** at the end of Grade 8, showing what each student can do — not as an exam, but as a life-long skill record.

⑨ So, should every middle-school student learn basic first-aid? Yes, and not as a strange "extra" subject, but as an ordinary part of growing up. Maths helps us count; Chinese helps us read; first-aid may one day help us **hold someone's life in our hands for three short minutes**, until the people with white coats arrive. That should be enough reason for every school to open this small, important door.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main view?**

A. First-aid should only be taught to medical students.  
B. Every middle-school student should learn basic first-aid as an ordinary part of growing up.  
C. First-aid should be an online class only; no real practice is needed.  
D. Middle-school students are too young to learn any first-aid skill.

**2. According to the passage, which is one of the worries from people who oppose first-aid in school?**

A. It is too expensive for the school.  
B. Students may try to help in a real emergency and make the situation worse.  
C. There are no doctors in this country.  
D. First-aid will make students too strong.

**3. How does the writer answer the "may do harm" worry?**

A. By saying nobody should ever try to help a sick person.  
B. By saying that good teaching is exactly about when to act and when not to, and well-trained students are far less likely to make wrong moves than those with no training at all.  
C. By saying that only doctors know the right thing to do.  
D. By saying students should not use first-aid until they are 18.

**4. Why does the writer think "waiting for a doctor to arrive" is not always a safe answer?**

A. Because city doctors are very lazy.  
B. Because ambulances usually take about 8 to 12 minutes, and what happens in those first few minutes often decides whether a person survives.  
C. Because doctors often refuse to come to emergencies.  
D. Because doctors always go to the wrong address.

**5. Which suggestion is **NOT** one of the writer's three practical ideas for first-aid in schools?**

A. Short but regular lessons, such as 30 minutes every two weeks.  
B. Real practice with dummies, bandages and possibly hospital visits.  
C. A simple school certificate at the end of Grade 8 as a life-long skill record.  
D. A large final exam worth 50% of the Maths mark.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "should every middle-school student learn basic first-aid? My firm answer is yes" & 第⑨段 "not as a strange 'extra' subject, but as an ordinary part of growing up"  
【解析】作者鲜明主张把急救纳入学生日常成长。A、C、D 均与立场不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第二条 "untrained students may try to help in a real emergency and make the situation worse"  
【解析】原文明确列举。A、C、D 均无文中依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "The whole point of a first-aid course is to teach exactly when to act and when to wait ... A student who has done even four hours of practice is far less likely to make the wrong move"  
【解析】作者强调训练本身即教"该不该动"。A、C、D 均与作者立场不符。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "about 8 to 12 minutes to arrive ... what happens in the first few minutes often decides whether a person survives"  
【解析】原文精确说明救护车到达时间及黄金时间重要性。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑧段 "three things"  
【解析】作者三项建议是 A、B、C；并非大型考试。故选 D。
