# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Schools Reduce Weekend Homework?**

① In many middle schools, the weekend is no longer a break. Students carry home thick bags of books on Friday evening, and when Sunday night finally arrives, they are still finishing the last page. This simple fact leads to a big question: **should schools clearly reduce weekend homework?**

② Those against the idea point out that learning is not a nine-to-five job. A language like English cannot be built only from Monday to Friday. In two full rest days, words already learned slip out of mind, math skills become slow, and students return to class on Monday as if they had been away for a month. From this view, weekend homework is a soft glue that keeps last week's lessons in place.

③ These worries are fair, but they miss an important point: **quality, not quantity**. A weekend of twenty pages of copying or thirty repeated math problems is not the same as a weekend of one good book and two careful exercises. When homework becomes a mountain, students only run through the pages. When it is a small, careful hill, students can climb slowly and deeply understand.

④ Too much weekend homework also hurts other kinds of learning. On Saturday morning, a student could help his grandmother make dumplings, visit a museum with his father, or read an English novel for pure joy. All of these grow the mind in ways that no exercise book can copy. When the entire weekend is eaten by paper tasks, these richer lessons are lost, one silent hour at a time.

⑤ Health is another serious concern. A survey in 2025 of 1,200 middle school students found that those who spent **more than 8 hours on weekend homework** slept over an hour less on Sunday night, and more than **40 percent** said they felt tired again before Monday class even began. A week that starts tired cannot end bright.

⑥ So what should schools do? The answer is not to cancel weekend homework, but to **shape** it. Teachers can give one small writing task, one interesting reading passage and a free activity — perhaps "take a photo of a family meal and describe it in five English sentences." Students should also have the right to say, "I have finished; may I rest now?" When rest is given a place in the timetable, learning on Monday morning will be lighter, not heavier.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What problem does the writer describe in Paragraph 1?**

A. Students have too little homework on weekends.  
B. Students carry heavy homework all weekend and finish only on Sunday night.  
C. Students do not like English at all.  
D. Students refuse to go to school on Monday.

**2. Why do some people support weekend homework?**

A. They think it makes teachers richer.  
B. They believe it keeps last week's lessons fresh in mind.  
C. They want students to skip family dinners.  
D. They believe homework should last forever.

**3. According to the writer, what matters most about weekend homework?**

A. How heavy the schoolbag is.  
B. How colourful the cover is.  
C. The quality, not the quantity.  
D. How many pages have red corrections.

**4. What did the 2025 survey find?**

A. Students with more than 8 hours of homework slept over an hour less on Sunday.  
B. Students always sleep better on weekends.  
C. Students have no homework at all.  
D. Students prefer Saturday over Sunday.

**5. What is the writer's final suggestion?**

A. Cancel all weekend homework.  
B. Double the weekend homework.  
C. Shape homework into small, meaningful tasks and protect rest time.  
D. Move all homework to the bus on Monday morning.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段描述  
【解析】周末仍被作业填满。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "soft glue that keeps last week's lessons in place"  
【解析】作业是巩固上周所学的"胶水"。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "quality, not quantity"  
【解析】关键在于质量。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "slept over an hour less on Sunday night"  
【解析】作业多者周日睡眠少 1 小时。故选 A。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "shape it... protect rest"  
【解析】作业应精而小，并保护休息时间。故选 C。
