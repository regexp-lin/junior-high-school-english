# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Teens Watch Short Videos Every Day?**

① Pick up any teenager's phone on a subway in China today, and the screen is almost certainly showing the same thing: **short videos**. Fifteen seconds of dancing, thirty seconds of cooking, a funny cat, a quick science trick. These little clips are fun, colourful and easy to swallow. But as more parents complain, a hot question keeps coming back: **should middle school students watch short videos every day?**

② Supporters say short videos are a fast, modern classroom. A two-minute clip can explain why the sky is blue, show the way a volcano erupts, or teach a new English word with a catchy song. Students who feel shy in class may learn more in these tiny lessons than in a long textbook chapter. For lonely teens, short videos can also be a window — they see friends laughing across the country and feel less alone.

③ But the worries are real. Most short-video apps are designed to **"catch" our attention**. A clever system chooses the next clip based on what you just liked, so you keep scrolling without noticing. One hour disappears. Then two. By bedtime, the student feels tired but not full, like eating snacks for dinner. Eyes hurt. Homework is only half done. Sleep arrives late, and morning class comes too soon.

④ There is also a **quality problem**. Many short videos are made just to shock, not to teach. Dangerous "challenges", fake news, and loud arguments can all slip into a child's screen. Even gentle clips often show only a small, perfect slice of life — a smiling face, a beautiful kitchen — which may quietly make viewers feel their own life is grey and boring.

⑤ In my view, the answer is not "never" but "wisely". A simple 30-30 rule can help. **Watch short videos for no more than 30 minutes a day, and after every 30 minutes of watching, look up and move around.** Turn off auto-play. Follow a few quality channels — a museum, a science teacher, a book club — instead of following whatever pops up. Let the phone serve our minds, not swallow them.

⑥ Short videos are not the enemy, and they are not the answer either. They are simply a new tool. The real question for every middle school student is not **"Am I allowed to watch?"** but **"Am I still the one choosing?"** If we can answer "yes", then even the tiniest clip can become a small window to a bigger world.

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第①段  
【解析】地铁场景说明短视频之普及。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义猜测题  
【定位】第③段

**4.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题

【解析】未提丢书包。故选 D。

【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑤-⑥段  
【解析】关键是保持自我选择权。故选 B。
