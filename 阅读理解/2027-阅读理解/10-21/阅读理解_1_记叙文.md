# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Five O'Clock at the Morning Market with Dad**

① Last Sunday, Dad shook my shoulder at **four forty-five** in the morning. "Xiao Hang, get up. You said you wanted to see where our vegetables come from." I groaned. It was still dark outside, and the heater felt warmer than any other friend I had. But I had asked him twice last week, and a promise was a promise. I put on my old grey hoodie and followed him down the stairs.

② The morning market was only fifteen minutes away by e-bike, but it felt like **another country**. Orange lamps lit up long rows of tents. Trucks, heavy with autumn, reversed slowly into the lanes. The air smelled of fresh earth, wet cabbage, and a thin, soft smoke from a noodle stall. It was five ten in the morning, and already five hundred people were working hard around us.

③ Dad walked straight to a corner where a short, thin man was stacking cardboard boxes of pears. "This is **Uncle Liu**," Dad whispered. "He has sold me vegetables for nine years. Watch his hands." I watched. Uncle Liu did not look at the pears. His fingers ran along the fruit the way my music teacher's fingers ran along the piano — quick, sure, gentle. The damaged pears he pulled aside into a small red basket. "For the soup kitchen down the road," he said, without looking up. "Children of migrant workers eat there. These pears are ugly, but still sweet."

④ Dad picked up two bags of vegetables and three kilograms of pears. "How much?" he asked. Uncle Liu shook his head. "**Not today.**" He pressed the pears into my arms. "Your son came with you at five in the morning. That already pays for them." Dad's face went red. He slipped a fifty-yuan note into Uncle Liu's pocket anyway and walked off quickly, pretending to look for something else.

⑤ We moved on. At a fish stall, a middle-aged woman was scaling a large carp with one hand, feeding a tiny baby in a pink blanket with the other. At a dumpling stand, a man's hands were **covered in flour up to his elbows**, and he laughed without cleaning them as he called out greetings to customers. A grandmother sat on a tiny stool, selling exactly **three kinds of greens**, all grown in her own courtyard. "They are few," she told me proudly, "but every leaf is mine."

⑥ Dad bought six eggs, some tofu, and a bunch of chives. At the last stall, he handed me a five-yuan note. "You choose something for breakfast," he said. I had not chosen food with real money for a long time. I stood there, shy, as if at a school exam. A smell of hot sesame pulled me to a small old cart: **freshly fried dough twists**, golden brown, three yuan each. I bought two.

⑦ On the way home, the sky turned from black, to grey, to a pale autumn pink. Dad and I sat on the roadside for two minutes to eat. The dough twist was so crisp that it cracked like ice. The city was slowly waking up, and the market behind us was already quieter — its noisiest hour, we had just lived through, was over. "In an hour," Dad said, "most people will only see the clean shelves of the supermarket. They will never meet Uncle Liu. But Uncle Liu feeds those shelves." He looked at me seriously. "Remember this morning. A lot of our easy life is paid for by people who get up in the dark."

⑧ At home, Mum was still asleep. I put the bags on the kitchen table and went back to the balcony. The sky was a soft gold now. I was tired, happy, and a little changed. **I would never eat a pear in the same way again.**

---

**注释：** market 集市；hoodie 连帽衫；scale (鱼) 刮鳞；dough twist 麻花；migrant worker 外来务工者

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did Xiao Hang agree to go to the market at five in the morning?**

A. Because school asked him to.  
B. Because he had asked his father twice last week, and he felt a promise should be kept.  
C. Because he wanted to buy a toy.  
D. Because the weather was warm.

**2. Why did Uncle Liu put some pears into a small red basket?**

A. Because they were poisonous.  
B. Because they would be thrown away.  
C. Because the damaged pears would be sent to a soup kitchen that feeds children of migrant workers.  
D. Because they would be frozen.

**3. What did Dad do when Uncle Liu refused to accept the money?**

A. He took all the pears back.  
B. He slipped a fifty-yuan note into Uncle Liu's pocket anyway and walked off quickly.  
C. He got angry.  
D. He called the police.

**4. What did the old grandmother say about her three kinds of greens?**

A. "I grew them in a big factory."  
B. "They are bought from abroad."  
C. "They are few, but every leaf is mine."  
D. "They are not for sale."

**5. What does Xiao Hang seem to understand by the end of the story?**

A. He prefers supermarkets to markets.  
B. Food is cheap only because of bad farmers.  
C. A lot of ordinary, easy life is paid for by people who get up in the dark, and he feels quiet respect for them.  
D. He wants to open his own market tomorrow.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第①段 "I had asked him twice last week, and a promise was a promise."  
【解析】源于自己守诺的心理。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "for the soup kitchen down the road ... Children of migrant workers"  
【解析】残果捐赠施粥处。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "slipped a fifty-yuan note into Uncle Liu's pocket anyway and walked off quickly"  
【解析】直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "They are few, but every leaf is mine."  
【解析】直引。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑦段 "A lot of our easy life is paid for by people who get up in the dark" + 第⑧段 "a little changed"  
【解析】顿悟与敬意。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。
