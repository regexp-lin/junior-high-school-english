# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Cooking Be a Required Class in Middle School?**

① A few years ago, cooking was often seen as a skill that parents would naturally teach at home. Today, however, many homes rarely cook in the traditional way. Ready-made food is delivered in thirty minutes, and many teenagers can boil instant noodles but cannot cut a tomato safely. This situation has led to a lively question: **should middle schools add a weekly cooking class, just like math or English?**

② Some people believe this is not necessary. They say that **school is for knowledge**, not for kitchens. Cooking, in their eyes, can be learned during the summer holiday, from a parent, an uncle, or a short video online. Adding a new subject also means more pressure for already busy students. "My son can hardly finish his homework before ten," one father said. "Please don't give him another task."

③ These worries make sense. Still, cooking is not a task in the same way that math is. A good cooking class does not give written homework. Students chop, boil, taste, and clean the table together. In forty minutes, tired minds find a quiet kind of rest. A school doctor in Chengdu noticed that students after a cooking class were **more cheerful and ate more fruit at lunch**, a small change that many teachers found surprising.

④ Beyond rest, cooking teaches **life skills**. A student who can make three simple dishes — tomato with eggs, cabbage and rice, a pot of pork soup — has a tool for the rest of his life. When he studies in another city after high school, when his parents fall ill for a week, or when a younger brother needs lunch during a holiday, these quiet hands will be ready. Knowledge in textbooks is important, but knowledge in the palm is just as precious.

⑤ Cooking also builds **values**. To chop an onion slowly for a grandmother, to try a soup and add one more grain of salt, to share a hot dish with a classmate who forgot lunch — these small moments teach patience, care and generosity. No math exercise can teach a child what a warm spoon of noodles to a sick friend can teach in five minutes.

⑥ Of course, safety first. Knives should be child-sized; stoves must be watched by a teacher; schools should choose simple and safe dishes. Under these rules, forty minutes once a week is a very small price. A mind that can think, a body that can move, and a hand that can cook — these three together make a young person truly ready for the road ahead.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What question is the writer trying to answer?**

A. Whether schools should teach ancient cooking recipes.  
B. Whether middle schools should add a weekly cooking class.  
C. Whether students should cook at home every day.  
D. Whether parents should stop cooking.

**2. Why do some people think a cooking class is not necessary?**

A. Because cooking is dangerous for everyone.  
B. Because students are already busy and cooking can be learned at home.  
C. Because schools have no kitchens.  
D. Because parents are bad cooks.

**3. What did a school doctor in Chengdu notice?**

A. Students slept more after cooking class.  
B. Students became more cheerful and ate more fruit.  
C. Students forgot how to read.  
D. Students refused to do any homework.

**4. According to Paragraph 4, why is cooking useful for later life?**

A. It helps students become famous chefs.  
B. It gives a tool for the rest of life, useful in many situations.  
C. It teaches students how to sell food.  
D. It makes students eat less at school.

**5. What does the writer finally suggest?**

A. Forty minutes of cooking per week is a small price to pay.  
B. Cooking classes should be taught every day.  
C. Cooking should replace math classes.  
D. Students should only cook on the weekend.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句  
【解析】全文讨论烹饪课是否应加入课程。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "school is for knowledge... more pressure"  
【解析】学生已忙，家中可学。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "more cheerful and ate more fruit at lunch"  
【解析】学生更开朗、吃水果更多。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "a tool for the rest of his life"  
【解析】终身受益的技能。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "forty minutes once a week is a very small price"  
【解析】每周 40 分钟代价小。故选 A。
