# 阅读理解（议论文） **Are Traditional Festivals Becoming Only Shopping Days?**

① Every year, as the Mid-Autumn Festival or the Dragon Boat Festival comes near, shopping websites light up with bright red banners: "Moon Cake Sale 50% Off!" "Super Rice Dumpling Week!" Young people send mooncakes by courier, order zongzi through a small screen, and spend the holiday afternoon in a shopping mall. This is convenient, and it is modern. Yet a worrying question is rising: **are our traditional festivals slowly becoming only shopping days?**

② Some people do not see any problem. In their view, shopping makes the festivals alive. "Without the big sales," one young writer said on the internet, "how would city children even know the festival is coming?" Gifts help parents stay close to their children in other cities, and modern products can make old festivals more interesting. In a sense, buying *is* a new form of celebrating.

③ However, there is a quiet cost. Once the focus moves to boxes, bills and brand names, the deeper meaning slips away. A mooncake ordered in three clicks is the same shape as a handmade one, but the moon above it feels different. Children who only receive the **gift** of a festival, without ever **making** anything together with their parents, may know the taste of mooncake but miss the taste of the festival itself.

④ Real festivals are not shopping lists; they are living memories. The fragrant smoke from making zongzi in May, the careful folding of reed leaves, the smell of mugwort at the door — all of these tell a story that money cannot fully buy. At the Spring Festival, sticking paper-cuttings on a window, writing a couplet with grandfather, sweeping the yard together — these small actions are the glue that holds a whole generation to the last. Once lost, they are hard to find again.

⑤ We also must not forget that festivals are a quiet teacher of **culture**. The Dragon Boat Festival reminds us of Qu Yuan and his love for his country. Qingming Festival teaches us to remember those who went before us and thank them. If children only remember discount codes and free delivery, these deeper lessons may be washed away like footprints at high tide.

⑥ So what should we do? We do not need to refuse online shopping — that would be silly. But we should ask for **more than shopping**. Families can make one small thing together each festival: one bowl of zongzi by hand, one lantern painted by the whole family, one dumpling folded with a grandma's help. Schools can invite grandparents to tell old stories to the students. A festival with only bills becomes small and cold; a festival with hands, voices and memories stays warm for a hundred years.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why does the writer worry about traditional festivals today?**

A. Because young people refuse to celebrate them.  
B. Because they are becoming only shopping days.  
C. Because shops sell too few mooncakes.  
D. Because there are no holidays in winter.

**2. What do some people think about online shopping during festivals?**

A. It makes the festivals dirty.  
B. It makes the festivals alive and helps families stay close.  
C. It should be banned by the government.  
D. It is only useful during summer.

**3. What does Paragraph 3 mainly say?**

A. Mooncakes ordered online taste exactly the same as handmade ones.  
B. The deeper meaning of the festival slips away when only shopping is left.  
C. Shopping should be the main way of celebrating.  
D. Children today prefer not to eat mooncakes.

**4. What example does the writer give of a "living memory" of a festival?**

A. Getting a big red envelope from the post office.  
B. Ordering zongzi in three clicks.  
C. Folding reed leaves and making zongzi at home.  
D. Eating out at a fancy restaurant.

**5. What is the writer's final suggestion?**

A. Stop shopping online completely.  
B. Cancel all traditional festivals.  
C. Keep festivals warm by making something together, not just shopping.  
D. Only celebrate Spring Festival and forget the others.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨/细节综合  
【定位】第①段末句  
【解析】作者担心传统节日沦为购物节。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "makes the festivals alive... stay close"  
【解析】部分人认为购物让节日鲜活。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】段落主旨题  
【定位】第③段 "deeper meaning slips away"  
【解析】只剩购物会丢失深意。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "making zongzi in May, the careful folding of reed leaves"  
【解析】亲手包粽子是生活化的记忆。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "make one small thing together each festival"  
【解析】建议与家人一起动手过节。故选 C。
