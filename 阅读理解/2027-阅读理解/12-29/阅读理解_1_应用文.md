# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School 2028 Winter Student Health Check-Up Notice**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · School Clinic (Ms Fang, Head Nurse) + Student Affairs Office**  
> **2028 Winter Student Health Check-Up Notice**  
> **Notice published**: Wednesday 29 December 2027 · **Reference**: WHC-2028-01  
> **Check-up dates**: **Monday 10 January 2028 — Friday 14 January 2028** (five school days)  
> **Venue**: **Main Hall (1F) + School Clinic (2F, Building A)**

---

① Below is the official notice of the **2028 Winter Student Health Check-Up** at Lihua Middle School, for all students in Grade 7, Grade 8, and Grade 9. This is the **second and last health check-up** of the 2027–2028 school year; the first (autumn) check-up took place on 12–16 September 2027. **Every student should attend**, unless a written parent note is submitted to Ms Fang by Friday 8 January 2028 explaining a medical reason (for example, a recent surgery, a current fever, or a long-term illness). The check-up is **fully free**. No payment is required. **Please read the whole notice slowly before 10 January.**

---

## 🗓️ Check-Up Schedule by Grade and Class

| Date | Morning (08:20 – 11:10) | Afternoon (13:30 – 16:20) |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon 10 Jan** | Grade 7 – Classes A, B, C | Grade 7 – Classes D, E |
| **Tue 11 Jan** | Grade 8 – Classes A, B | Grade 8 – Classes C, D |
| **Wed 12 Jan** | Grade 8 – Class E | Grade 9 – Classes A, B |
| **Thu 13 Jan** | Grade 9 – Classes C, D | Grade 9 – Class E |
| **Fri 14 Jan** | **Make-up sessions** (any student who was absent) | **Make-up sessions** |

- Please arrive at the Main Hall **10 minutes before** your class's scheduled time window, carrying your student ID card.  
- Please **do not** bring heavy schoolbags; a small shoulder bag is enough.  
- Please **do not** eat a heavy breakfast on the morning of your check-up; a small bowl of porridge and one egg is fine. **Water is encouraged.**  
- **Do not** arrive at the clinic during a class other than your own — the clinic cannot interrupt another class's flow.

---

## 🩺 The Nine Check-Up Items

| # | Item | Station | Roughly takes | Clothing / Preparation note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Height and weight | Station A (Main Hall) | 1 min | Take off heavy coat; keep uniform |
| 2 | Vision (both eyes) | Station B (Main Hall) | 2 min | No glasses in step 1; glasses worn in step 2 |
| 3 | Hearing (both ears) | Station C (Main Hall) | 2 min | Quiet booth; remove earphones |
| 4 | Heart rate + blood pressure | Station D (Main Hall) | 2 min | Sit quietly for 2 min before measurement |
| 5 | Spine and posture | Station E (Main Hall) | 2 min | Thin uniform layer only; **students may ask for a same-gender nurse** |
| 6 | Mouth and teeth | Station F (Main Hall) | 2 min | Brush teeth in morning; no chewing gum |
| 7 | General body check by school doctor | Station G (Clinic 2F) | 3 min | One student at a time; curtain closed; any student may ask a friend or homeroom teacher to wait outside |
| 8 | Optional blood pressure recheck | Station D2 (Clinic 2F) | 2 min | Only if Station D finds a small irregular number |
| 9 | **Optional brief mental wellness chat** | Station H (Clinic 2F, separate room) | 5 min | **Fully optional**; no teacher present; notes are private |

- **Total time** per student: roughly **20 – 25 minutes** (without Stations 8 and 9).  
- **If you feel unwell during any station**, please say so immediately; a nurse will help you sit down and drink warm water.  
- **If you do not wish to do any specific item**, you may politely skip it; no student is forced to take any measurement.

---

## 🫶 Privacy and Comfort Rules (please read)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| 1. **No public ranking** | Height, weight, vision, and any other number are **never posted publicly**, never written on the class board, and never announced in class. |
| 2. **Same-gender nurse if requested** | For Item 5 (spine/posture) and Item 7 (general body check), any student may ask for a nurse of the same gender. Please say so quietly at the Main Hall entrance. |
| 3. **Results go home privately** | A sealed paper envelope with your own results only is given to each student on **Friday 20 January**, to take home to a parent or guardian; teachers do not see it. |
| 4. **Mental wellness chat (Station H)** | Fully optional; a kind school doctor listens; no notes are shared with any teacher or parent without the student's written permission; the student may end the chat at any minute. |
| 5. **No photography inside the clinic or Main Hall** | Any student, teacher, parent, or visitor is asked not to take photos during check-up sessions; signs on every door remind them. |
| 6. **Weight is a number, not a judgement** | The clinic does not use the words "too thin" or "too fat"; every student is given only a private number, and a short calm note if something is slightly outside normal range. |

## ❓ Frequently Gently-Asked Questions

**Q1. What if I feel nervous about any item?**  
A1. Please tell the nurse quietly at the entrance. You may take the items in any order. You may skip any item without reason.

**Q2. What if my spine check (Station E) finds something small?**  
A2. Ms Fang will quietly write a short private note to your parent with a recommendation — usually a gentle referral to a local hospital for a second look. No classmate will know; no teacher will know.

**Q3. Can I bring a friend to wait with me during the general body check (Station G)?**  
A3. **Yes, one friend may wait outside the curtain** if it helps you feel calmer. The friend does not enter the curtain area.

**Q4. Can a student see her own results?**  
A4. **Yes, of course.** Your sealed envelope on 20 January is your own private paper. You open it; your parent reads it with you if you wish.

**Q5. What about the mental wellness chat — will my teacher know what I said?**  
A5. **No.** Station H is private. Only you decide whether to tell anyone what was said. Ms Fang's rule is simple: *"Your worries are yours; you own them completely."*

## 💬 A Small Word from Ms Fang

> "Dear Lihua students, the winter check-up is not a test. There is no pass; there is no fail. There is no mark. There is no comparison. There is only, for each of you, about twenty quiet minutes with people who want you to be healthy and safe. We will keep the lights low. We will speak softly. We will not hurry you. If you need two minutes to breathe, we will wait. If you do not want to do something, we will not push. Please trust us. And please, if you are worrying quietly about something bigger than height or weight — a feeling, a sleep problem, a fear — the kind door of Station H is open. Come quietly; leave quietly; tell nobody if you do not wish to. Winter is long. Your health is a small warm lantern. Let us help you keep it lit.  
> — *Ms Fang, Head Nurse, 29 December 2027*"
