# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Do We Cry? Three Kinds of Tears**

① When you cut an onion, your eyes fill with tears in less than a minute. When wind blows into your eyes on a cold winter day, they water at once. When you read a very sad page of a book at midnight, tears may slip down silently. All three of these are **tears**, but, surprisingly, they are **not the same thing**. Scientists divide human tears into **three clear kinds**, and each kind has a different job.

② **The first kind is called "basal tears"** (基础泪). These are the tears you never notice. Every single minute, tiny glands above your eyes quietly release a small amount of salty water, about the size of a drop of rain in an hour. This thin film **covers the whole eye**, and does three important things at the same time: it keeps the eye from drying out, it carries away small bits of dust, and it **provides food** — yes, food — to the surface of the eye, since the surface has no blood vessels of its own. Without basal tears, a human eye would hurt within minutes.

③ **The second kind is called "reflex tears"** (反射泪). These are the tears that run out when your eye is in sudden trouble. A piece of dust flies in; a sharp onion gas touches the eye; a bright light hits you from the side. Your brain sends a fast order, and the glands produce **a flood** of water, much greater than usual. The job is simple: **wash the eye clean**. Reflex tears are mostly water, with almost no special chemicals. That is why, after a good cry caused by an onion, you usually do **not** feel any lighter in mood — your feelings were never inside the onion in the first place.

④ **The third kind is called "emotional tears"** (情感泪). These are the tears you remember: the ones that fell when you lost your favourite dog, when your grandmother said goodbye at the train station, or when a whole school hall cheered for your classmate who finally won a race. Under a microscope, emotional tears look different from reflex tears. They carry more **proteins** and more **hormones** linked to **stress**. Many scientists now believe that crying from strong feelings is one way the body actually **removes** some stress chemicals from the blood — which may explain the old line, "I feel better after a good cry."

⑤ Of course, culture also shapes how we cry. In some places, crying in public is seen as weak; in others, it is seen as deeply human, even at weddings. But biology does not care about fashion. Whether you hide in a washroom or cry at the front of a classroom, your body is doing one of three honest jobs: **keeping the eye alive** (basal), **protecting the eye from danger** (reflex), or **moving a heavy feeling out of your chest** (emotional).

⑥ Babies cry loudly with no tears at first — their glands are not fully open yet — but after about one month, the tears arrive. From that day until the last day, a human being will cry maybe **twenty-five thousand times**. Some people cry often, some almost never. Both are normal. Scientists now say the old advice "**big boys don't cry**" is simply **wrong biology**: holding tears in does not help the body; it only hides the signal that something needs care.

⑦ So next time you see a friend wipe her eyes, remember: she may be cutting an onion, fighting wind, or quietly finishing a sad page of a book. In every case, her body is doing one of its oldest, cleanest jobs. **Tears are not weakness. Tears are the eye telling the truth.**

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main job of "basal tears"?**

A. To wash away dust after an onion is cut.  
B. To cover the eye with a thin film that stops drying, removes small dust, and feeds the surface.  
C. To carry stress chemicals out of the body.  
D. To look beautiful on the face.

**2. Why does the writer say you usually do NOT feel lighter in mood after crying from an onion?**

A. Because the onion tears are reflex tears — mostly water, with almost no feeling-related chemicals inside.  
B. Because the onion is still on the chopping board.  
C. Because your eyes are closed.  
D. Because you are hungry.

**3. What is special about "emotional tears" under a microscope?**

A. They are the same as reflex tears.  
B. They contain more proteins and more stress-linked hormones than reflex tears.  
C. They are colourful.  
D. They have no water in them.

**4. What does the writer say about the advice "big boys don't cry"?**

A. It is good medical advice.  
B. It is simply wrong biology — holding tears in hides the signal that something needs care.  
C. It is true in most countries.  
D. It should be taught in every school.

**5. What is the writer's final idea in the last paragraph?**

A. Tears are a sign of weakness.  
B. Only children should cry.  
C. Tears — whatever the reason — are the eye doing an honest job, and "tears are not weakness; tears are the eye telling the truth."  
D. Crying should be avoided in public.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "covers the whole eye ... keeps the eye from drying ... carries away small bits of dust ... provides food"  
【解析】三项功能同时并列。A、C、D 均偏离。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Reflex tears are mostly water, with almost no special chemicals ... your feelings were never inside the onion"  
【解析】化学成分差异导致主观感受不同。B、C、D 无依据。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "more proteins and more hormones linked to stress"  
【解析】直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "simply wrong biology ... hides the signal that something needs care"  
【解析】作者明确批评旧说。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑦段结尾 "Tears are not weakness. Tears are the eye telling the truth."  
【解析】全文点题。A、B、D 均与作者立场相反或片面。故选 C。
