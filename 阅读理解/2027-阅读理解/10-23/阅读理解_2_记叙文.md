# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Day We Lost the Class Flag**

① It was the afternoon of our **autumn sports meeting**, and Class 8 (3) had reached the relay final. Our blue class flag — sewn by hand in Grade 7 by our art monitor Lin Xi — had been waving on the side of the track since nine in the morning. On it, in white thread, were four words: "**RUN WELL, LOSE WELL**". We had won two silver medals and one bronze that day, and we only needed the flag and the first few shouts to get ready for the relay.

② At half past two, I went to collect the flag. The pole was still there, pressed into the soft earth, but the blue cloth was **gone**. I stared for three full seconds. Then I ran back to our tent. "The flag," I said, out of breath. "It is missing." Fourteen faces turned to me. For a moment, nobody moved.

③ Lin Xi did not cry, but her face went pale. She had spent three weekends making that flag. Our class monitor, Ge Lei, took a deep breath. "**Search first. Blame later.**" We split into four groups of four. I walked around the west end of the track, asking every student I passed, "Have you seen a blue flag, maybe as large as a table cloth, with white words?"

④ Ten minutes passed. Nothing. A Grade 9 boy even offered us his own black flag, but Lin Xi shook her head gently. "Thank you, but the words have to be ours."

⑤ Then, at the very corner near the toilets, I heard a tiny sound. Looking down, I saw two Grade 4 children — one in a red T-shirt, one in yellow — squatting behind a bush, trying to lift **something heavy**. I walked around. Our flag, dusty but whole, was wrapped around a small injured **stray dog**. Its front leg was bent the wrong way. The two children looked up, afraid. "Please, please don't take it back yet," the red-shirt boy said quickly. "The dog was shaking, and this was the warmest cloth I could find."

⑥ I did not know what to say. Behind me, Ge Lei had arrived, and behind him, Lin Xi. Lin Xi walked very slowly to the little group. She knelt down, looked at the dog, then looked at the two small boys. She was quiet for five seconds. Then she said, "Keep the flag for now. But come with me. We will find someone who can really help."

⑦ In the next twenty minutes, our class did two things at once. Half of the relay team warmed up without a flag, using only voices. The other half — Lin Xi, Ge Lei, me, and the two boys — carried the dog (still wrapped in our flag) to the school clinic. Nurse Gao called a local animal-rescue station, and fifteen minutes later, a young woman with a tidy white van came to take the dog to the clinic.

⑧ Back at the track, the race had already started. Our runners were in third place. With no flag, we cheered so loudly our throats felt thin. Our fourth runner, Wanlin, pushed ahead in the last fifty metres, and we crossed the line **first**. Silver became gold. The whole tent jumped at once, then fell into a strange quiet — no flag was there to wave above our heads.

⑨ Two weeks later, the animal-rescue woman sent a short video to our class. The small dog, now clean and with a bright leg-plaster, wagged his tail at the camera. Lin Xi watched it in class twice, then stood up. "We are making a new flag," she said. "Same blue, same words. But this time, four smaller words will be added at the bottom." That afternoon, we sewed: "**LOSE THE CLOTH, KEEP THE HEART**."

⑩ A flag is only a piece of cloth. But that autumn afternoon, fifteen of us learned something no relay can teach. **A good team does not only run well; sometimes, it stops running for a moment, and finds a reason that matters more.**

---

**注释：** class flag 班旗；relay 接力；stray dog 流浪狗；rescue 救助；plaster 石膏

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What was written on the original class flag?**

A. "WIN AT ALL COSTS".  
B. "CLASS 8 IS NUMBER ONE".  
C. "RUN WELL, LOSE WELL".  
D. "NEVER GIVE UP".

**2. When the writer told the class that the flag was missing, what did class monitor Ge Lei say?**

A. "Blame Lin Xi first."  
B. "Search first. Blame later."  
C. "Forget the flag."  
D. "Run back to the classroom."

**3. Why were the two Grade 4 boys hiding behind the bush with the class flag?**

A. They wanted to steal it.  
B. They were playing a game.  
C. They had wrapped an injured stray dog in the flag because it was the warmest cloth they could find.  
D. They were making a costume.

**4. How did the class react to the loss of the flag during the relay?**

A. They refused to run.  
B. They cried and went back to the classroom.  
C. They split into two groups: half warmed up and cheered only with voices; the other half carried the dog, wrapped in the flag, to the school clinic.  
D. They complained to the teachers loudly.

**5. What is the main idea of the whole story?**

A. Class flags are more important than people.  
B. A good team must always keep a flag flying.  
C. A good team does not only run well; sometimes, it stops running for a moment, and finds a reason (like helping an injured animal) that matters more.  
D. Stray dogs should not be brought to schools.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "RUN WELL, LOSE WELL"  
【解析】直引。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Search first. Blame later."  
【解析】直引。A、C、D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "dog was shaking ... warmest cloth I could find"  
【解析】目的是取暖救助。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑦段 "half of the relay team warmed up ... The other half ... carried the dog ... to the school clinic"  
【解析】双线并行。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑩段结尾  
【解析】作者直接点题。A、B、D 均错或过于字面。故选 C。
