# 阅读理解（议论文） **Is It a Good Idea to Take a "Digital Break" During Summer?**

The long summer holiday is here again, and a familiar picture appears in many families: a teenager sitting on the sofa, phone in one hand, snack in the other, eyes glued to a small, glowing screen for hours. Some parents and teachers now suggest a different plan — a "**digital break**", where students stay away from phones, tablets and computers for one whole week or longer. Is this really a good idea? In my view, **a carefully designed digital break can help us in clear and practical ways**.

The first reason is health. When we stare at a screen for hours, our eyes forget to blink. Muscles in the neck and back grow stiff from the same bent position. A recent survey in our city reported that **sixty-two percent** of middle-school students feel tired eyes or a sore neck during holidays — far more than during school days, when their time is already scheduled. A week without screens is like a short holiday for the body. Our eyes rest, our necks straighten, and our sleep often becomes deeper.

Some students reply, "But I will be bored!" This worry is real, yet it also proves the second reason: many of us have **forgotten how to play without a screen**. When we were small, we loved to paint, read, ride bikes, build castles with blocks, talk to friends face to face. A digital break pushes us gently back to these activities. Last July, my cousin and I tried a "phone-free" week. On day one, we felt strange; by day three, we had finished two storybooks and built a tiny kite. We were not bored — we were surprised.

Of course, a fair discussion must admit the **difficulties**. A complete digital break can also create problems. Students sometimes need the internet to find information for a summer project, or to video-call grandparents living far away. For this reason, "full stop" is not the best answer. A smarter plan is to allow **short and clear windows** — for example, thirty minutes each morning for email and news, and one hour in the evening for family video calls. Outside these windows, phones stay in a drawer.

Some people also argue that learning English or coding online counts as "good screen time". That is true. But even "good" screen time is still screen time; our eyes feel no difference. The purpose of the digital break is not to judge activities as good or bad, but to give the **eyes and mind** a short rest.

Summer is a rare chance. When we return to school in September, tests, homework, and online classes will all pull us back to screens. A one- or two-week digital break now is not an ancient habit we are forced to return to. It is a modern choice — a **small gift** we can give ourselves before the busy year begins.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. All screens must be thrown away during summer.  
B. A carefully designed digital break can help us in clear and practical ways.  
C. Teenagers should stay on their phones as long as they like.  
D. Phones cause no problem at all.

**2. What did the recent survey in the city find?**

A. Twelve percent of students feel tired eyes or a sore neck in holidays.  
B. Sixty-two percent of students feel tired eyes or a sore neck in holidays.  
C. Most students feel perfectly fine during holidays.  
D. Students' sleep becomes worse at school.

**3. What happened to the writer's cousin during the "phone-free" week?**

A. They felt lonely and sad the whole week.  
B. They felt strange at first but later finished two storybooks and built a kite.  
C. They spent all their money on new toys.  
D. They moved to a new school.

**4. Why does the writer not support a "full stop" of all digital use?**

A. Because phones are too expensive to throw away.  
B. Because students sometimes need the internet for summer projects or video calls with grandparents.  
C. Because parents would be angry.  
D. Because it is against the law.

**5. What does the writer mean by "a small gift we can give ourselves"?**

A. Buying a new phone for ourselves.  
B. Making free time for our eyes and mind before the busy year starts.  
C. Giving presents to classmates.  
D. Opening a paid online course.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】首段尾句  
【解析】适度设计的"电子戒断"有益。A/C/D 均偏离。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"sixty-two percent"  
【解析】62%学生假期感到眼疲劳/脖子酸。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"felt strange... finished two storybooks and built a tiny kite"  
【解析】先不适应后有成果。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】因果推理题  
【定位】"need the internet to find information for a summer project..."  
【解析】需要查资料、视频通话等。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】"give the eyes and mind a short rest"  
【解析】送自己一份休息时间。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
