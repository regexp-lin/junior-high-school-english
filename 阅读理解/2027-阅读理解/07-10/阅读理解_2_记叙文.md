# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Umbrella I Forgot to Open**

It was already raining hard when the last bell rang. The sky outside Classroom 8-2 was the colour of a wet stone, and the wind was throwing leaves against the window in small brown handfuls. I stood at the door with my red umbrella in one hand and my school bag on my shoulder, staring at the steps that were quickly becoming a shallow river. The bus home would arrive in twelve minutes, at the stop at the end of the street. Normally twelve minutes was more than enough. Today it would not be easy.

Ahead of me, at the school gate, I saw a small figure. It was **Tingting**, a girl from the year below me. She was only ten, very small, with two short **plaits** tied in pink. She had no umbrella. Her pink school bag was already half soaked. She was staring at the rain as if she expected it to stop just for her.

My red umbrella, I knew, was large enough for two. But I was tired. My feet hurt. I wanted to get home, eat, lie on my bed, and read a book. I told myself, "She lives near the school. She will be fine. Someone will pick her up." I pulled my umbrella tighter, opened it, and walked past her. I did not even look at her face.

Twenty steps later, I stopped. Not because I changed my mind. But because a small voice inside me kept asking, *What would Mum do?* My mother had never been a loud woman, but she had a habit: she always stopped and helped. Once she pushed a stranger's car when its battery died. Once she stayed an extra hour at the supermarket to help a blind man find the right brand of tea. "Kindness," she liked to say, "is not a big speech. It is a few extra steps in the same rain."

I turned around. Tingting was still at the gate. I walked back and said, "Share my umbrella?" She looked up, surprised, and nodded.

We stepped into the rain together. My umbrella was not big enough for two big people — but Tingting was small, and the way we squeezed actually worked. My left shoulder got wet, and my left arm was soon cold, but a strange warm feeling grew inside me, as if a lamp had been turned on in my chest.

"Where do you live?" I asked.

"The old yellow apartment, on the corner of North Street," she said in a tiny voice.

"That is on my way," I said, though it was not — it was in the opposite direction. "Come on, let's walk."

We talked little. She told me the name of her hamster. I told her the name of my new book. At her door, she smiled shyly and said, "Thank you, **jiejie**." (Big sister.) Then she disappeared up the stairs.

By the time I reached my bus stop, I had missed the 5:08. I had to wait sixteen extra minutes in the rain, at a stop with no shelter. My trousers were wet to the knees. My hair looked like a bird's nest. I did not care at all.

That night, I wrote in my diary: "Today I had an umbrella, and I almost forgot to open it for someone else. Next time, I hope I will open it a little faster." The rain on the window tapped quietly, as if it agreed.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why was the writer worried at the school gate?**

A. She had lost her school bag.  
B. The rain was very heavy and her bus would arrive in twelve minutes.  
C. A dog was chasing her.  
D. She had no homework to do.

**2. What did the writer do first when she saw Tingting?**

A. She shared her umbrella at once.  
B. She walked past Tingting without helping.  
C. She called Tingting's mother.  
D. She gave Tingting her own umbrella.

**3. Why did the writer turn back?**

A. She had forgotten her school bag.  
B. She remembered her mother's example of small kindness.  
C. Tingting shouted at her.  
D. A teacher forced her to go back.

**4. How did the writer feel while sharing the umbrella?**

A. She felt bored and angry.  
B. Part of her was cold, but a warm feeling grew inside her.  
C. She felt hungry and tired.  
D. She felt sleepy.

**5. What is the lesson of the story?**

A. Always take two umbrellas when it rains.  
B. Strangers are dangerous in a storm.  
C. Kindness is a few extra steps in the same rain — try to offer help a little faster.  
D. Walking home alone is the only way to be brave.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"rain hard... bus... twelve minutes"  
【解析】大雨+公交 12 分钟。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"walked past her..."  
【解析】先是没帮，走过去了。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】因果推理题  
【定位】"What would Mum do?"  
【解析】想起妈妈的榜样。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"left arm cold... warm feeling grew..."  
【解析】冷与内心温暖并存。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】末段/全文主旨  
【解析】善意=多走几步；动作要快一点。A/B/D 均偏离。故选 C。
