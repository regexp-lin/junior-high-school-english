# 阅读理解（说明文） **How Does a Smoke Alarm Save Lives?**

In the small white box on our kitchen **ceiling**, there is a quiet hero. It has no name; it does nothing most days. Once a month, we may see a tiny green light flash. Once a year, when someone burns toast, it suddenly screams. This hero is a **smoke alarm**, and although it looks simple, the science inside is clever — and has saved millions of lives.

A smoke alarm has one main job: to warn us as early as possible when a fire starts. Why is early warning so important? Because the most dangerous part of a house fire is not usually the flame itself. It is **smoke**. Smoke contains tiny **particles** and poisonous gases. When people sleep through the smell, they may lose awareness long before the fire reaches their bedroom. A smoke alarm, however, never sleeps. Its job is to notice smoke when the amount is still small.

There are two common types of smoke alarms. The first is called an **ionisation** alarm. Inside it is a very small amount of a safe radioactive material. It gently **pushes** electrons through the air between two metal plates. The moving electrons create a tiny, steady electric current. When smoke enters the chamber, the particles interrupt this flow of electrons, and the current drops. The alarm's circuit notices the change and sounds a loud alarm. Ionisation alarms are quick to react to fast-burning fires with small particles — for example, a paper bin on fire or a dry Christmas tree.

The second is called a **photoelectric** alarm. Inside is a small light-emitting diode and a light sensor, set at an angle. Normally, light from the LED shoots straight across the chamber and misses the sensor. When smoke enters, the tiny smoke particles **scatter** the light. Some of the scattered light hits the sensor, which then triggers the alarm. Photoelectric alarms are especially good at noticing slow, smouldering fires — for example, a sofa catching fire from a forgotten cigarette.

Modern alarms often combine both methods in one box, giving early warning for fast and slow fires alike. Many new models also connect to a home's Wi-Fi. If a fire starts while the family is out, a message is sent to a parent's phone, and an automatic alert may even be sent to the fire department.

Even the best alarm is useless if it is not maintained. Safety experts recommend four simple habits. First, test the alarm once a month by pressing the small button; a short beep means the alarm is awake. Second, **replace** the battery at least once a year. Third, dust the alarm gently every three months — a heavy layer of dust can trick the photoelectric sensor. Fourth, **replace the whole alarm every ten years**, because small electronic parts wear out with time.

So, next time you walk past that quiet white box on the ceiling, think for a second. Inside it, tiny light beams and invisible electron streams never stop watching over you. In an emergency, you may have as little as two minutes to leave a burning room. A smoke alarm gives you those two minutes — and sometimes, those two minutes are the difference between life and death.

**1. According to the passage, why is early warning of fire so important?**

A. Because flames can kill quickly, and people always see them clearly.  
B. Because smoke contains tiny particles and poisonous gases; people can lose awareness before the flames reach them.

D. Because houses are usually empty during fires.

**2. How does an ionisation alarm notice smoke?**

A. It measures the temperature of the room.  
B. It listens for the sound of burning wood.

A. Because slow fires produce large smoke particles that scatter light onto the sensor.

A. Test it by pressing the button once a month.  
B. Replace the battery at least once a year.  
C. Paint the alarm a new colour every week.  
D. Replace the whole alarm every ten years.

**5. What is the main idea of the passage?**

A. Smoke alarms are beautiful and should be placed as decoration.

C. Fires only happen in tall buildings.  
D. Scientists cannot yet build smoke alarms that work well.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"smoke contains tiny particles and poisonous gases..."  
【解析】有毒烟雾让人失去意识。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】"small radioactive material... interrupt this flow..."  
【解析】放射源+烟干扰电流。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】因果推理题  
【定位】"smoke particles scatter the light"  
【解析】缓燃大颗粒散射光到传感器。B/C/D 均错。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题（反向）  
【定位】"four simple habits"  
【解析】文中并未建议每周涂色。A/B/D 均提及。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】全文  
【解析】科学+维护=救命。A/C/D 均偏离。故选 B。
