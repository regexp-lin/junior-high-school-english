# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Street Musician Who Changed My Saturday**

① Last Saturday morning, my mother gave me a short list and a blue cloth bag. "Bread, eggs, three apples, and a bottle of soy sauce," she said. "Straight there, straight back." I left the flat in a bad mood. It was raining a little, and I had planned to watch a basketball match on TV. Instead, I had to walk to the corner supermarket ten minutes away.

② Just outside the supermarket, I stopped. Under a grey sky, a thin man was playing a simple violin. He wore old clothes but clean shoes. In front of him lay an open hat with a few coins and a handwritten sign: "**Music for my mother's hospital bills. Thank you for listening.**" At first I felt a bit uncomfortable. Most shoppers walked past quickly. I told myself that I was in a hurry, too.

③ Inside the supermarket, I filled my basket. The soy sauce was heavy, the eggs were fragile, and the wet bag kept slipping down my shoulder. As I stood in the queue, I could still hear the violin through the glass doors. It was playing a soft, slow song, a little like the lullabies my grandmother used to sing. Without knowing why, I suddenly felt sorry that I had not even smiled at the man.

④ At the checkout, I paid, but I kept five yuan in my pocket on purpose. I had been planning to spend it on a cold drink later. Outside, the rain had stopped, and the sky was still grey. The man was now playing a different song — faster, brighter, almost like the wind through a spring field. I stood for a moment. Then I walked over, bent a little, and dropped the five-yuan note into the hat. "Thank you for the music," I said quietly. "It sounds like spring."

⑤ The man stopped playing. He looked up with surprised eyes, as if no one had spoken to him all morning. "Thank you, young friend," he said softly. "Your words are a bigger gift than the money." Then he smiled a small, tired smile and started a new song, just for me. Two other shoppers stopped, too, and a little girl began to dance on the wet ground. For about one minute, the corner became a small free concert.

⑥ Walking home, the bag felt lighter, though nothing inside had changed. My mother asked why I was smiling. I told her the whole story. She listened, then said gently, "Sometimes five yuan is really a thousand yuan, if you give it with open eyes." That evening, I missed the basketball match. But, for the first time in a long while, I did not feel I had lost anything.

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer go out on Saturday morning?**

A. To watch a basketball match.  
B. To meet the street musician.  
C. To buy a few things from the supermarket for his mother.  
D. To visit his grandmother in hospital.

**2. What did the sign next to the street musician say?**

A. "Please give me a new violin."  
B. "Music for my mother's hospital bills. Thank you for listening."  
C. "Dancing lessons only."  
D. "No coins, please."

**3. Why did the writer save the five-yuan note?**

A. He originally planned to buy a cold drink later.  
B. His mother had told him to give it to the musician.  
C. He wanted to buy another bottle of soy sauce.  
D. It was his school fee.

**4. How did the musician react when the writer spoke to him?**

A. He became angry and walked away.  
B. He was surprised, and said that the writer's words were a bigger gift than the money.  
C. He refused to play any more songs.  
D. He asked the writer to dance, too.

**5. What does the writer MOST probably want to say through this story?**

A. Shopping is always boring on Saturdays.  
B. Street musicians should not play in the rain.  
C. A small act of kindness, given with open eyes, can feel much bigger than it looks.  
D. Five yuan is never enough to help anyone.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段第1-2句 "my mother gave me a short list... Bread, eggs, three apples..."  
【解析】作者是被派去超市买东西。A 看球赛是他原来的计划，B/D 均与原文不符。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第3句 "Music for my mother's hospital bills..."  
【解析】告示原话是此内容。A/C/D 均无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段第2句 "I had been planning to spend it on a cold drink later."  
【解析】五元原来要买冷饮。B/C/D 均与原文不符。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段第2-3句 "'Your words are a bigger gift than the money.'"  
【解析】街头音乐人感到惊讶并说话语是更大的礼物。A 生气、C 停止演奏、D 邀作者起舞均与原文相反。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳/写作意图题  
【定位】第⑥段末句 "I did not feel I had lost anything."  
【解析】作者想表达小小善意若用心给出，会让人觉得收获更多。A/B/D 均不符主题。故选 C。
