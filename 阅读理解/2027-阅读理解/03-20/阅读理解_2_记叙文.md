# 阅读理解（记叙文） **A Surprise Dinner for Mum's Birthday**

① Mum's birthday always falls on a Thursday this year. Dad had a business trip to Xi'an, and my little brother, Dabao, was only seven. I was in Grade 8 — old enough, I thought, to do something real. Two weeks before, I whispered my plan to Dabao: "Let's cook a small dinner by ourselves, and buy her nothing from the shop. Just a home-made Happy Birthday." His eyes became as round as two coins.

② From that day on, our kitchen became a secret school. After homework, I watched short cooking videos with the sound very low. I chose three simple dishes: **scrambled eggs with tomato**, **boiled dumplings**, and **a cold cucumber salad**. Mum had taught me the egg dish once last winter, but on my first practice, the eggs stuck to the pan like **yellow glue**, and the smoke set off the kitchen alarm. Grandma ran in from her room, shook her head and then, laughing, showed me a tiny trick — "wait until the oil just smiles at you, not until it shouts."

③ On Thursday morning, Dabao and I held an emergency meeting in the washroom. Our money could only buy one cake, not a big one, so we chose a **small mango cake from the corner bakery** and paid with his New Year money, 38 yuan in a tiny red envelope. At school, I could hardly pay attention in class. I kept thinking about the **temperature of the pan**, the **five dumplings per minute**, and the **candle I had hidden in my schoolbag**.

④ After school, we rushed home. Dabao's job was to fold 24 frozen dumplings onto the plate, wash the cucumber, and set the table with Grandma's old flower cloth. My job was the hot part. I cracked four eggs, stirred them, and dropped them in the pan. This time, they slid softly into yellow clouds around red tomato pieces. I could not believe my own eyes; a warm feeling went up to my cheeks.

⑤ Mum came home at 18:30. When she pushed open the door, she saw the small cake in the middle of the table, two candles dancing, a child-made card saying "**Happy Birthday, Our Hero**". For a second, she did not say anything. Then she set down her bag and her office folder, walked to me, and held my hand, which still smelled like soap and tomato juice. "You made all this?" she asked quietly. Dabao jumped in front of her, "The dumplings are mine! I folded them! Twenty-four!"

⑥ That night, the three of us sang softly in the small living room. The eggs were a little salty, the cucumber salad too sour, and the cake almost fell when Dabao blew the candles too hard. But Mum said it was the **best birthday dinner** she had ever had. Before she slept, she sent me a short message: "Today I saw that my son has quietly grown up. Thank you for the dinner, and for making my heart warm." I read it under my blanket twice, and then three times, before I could finally close my eyes.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer decide to cook a dinner by himself?**

A. Mum asked him to practise cooking for an exam.  
B. Dad was not at home, and he wanted to do something real for Mum's birthday.  
C. The family had no money for a restaurant.  
D. His school had a cooking homework.

**2. What did Grandma teach the writer during his first practice?**

A. A song for Mum's birthday.  
B. How to buy cheap vegetables.  
C. A small trick about when to put food into the oil.  
D. How to write a birthday card.

**3. What did the writer and Dabao use to pay for the mango cake?**

A. A bank card from Dad.  
B. Money that Mum left on the table.  
C. Dabao's New Year money in a red envelope.  
D. A small loan from the school classmates.

**4. How did Mum react when she saw the dinner and the card?**

A. She was angry and worried about the fire.  
B. She said nothing for a while, then held the writer's hand in a warm way.  
C. She quickly called Dad to complain.  
D. She refused to eat the home-made food.

**5. What does the last paragraph mainly show?**

A. The dinner was perfect in every way.  
B. Mum felt that her son had quietly grown up, which moved them both.  
C. The writer did not like Mum's message and was a little sad.  
D. The family decided never to eat at home again.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "Dad had a business trip... something real... home-made Happy Birthday"  
【解析】爸爸出差，作者想做真正有意义的事。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段末 "wait until the oil just smiles at you, not until it shouts"  
【解析】奶奶教下油的时机。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "paid with his New Year money, 38 yuan in a tiny red envelope"  
【解析】用 Dabao 的压岁钱支付。A、B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "she did not say anything... walked to me, and held my hand"  
【解析】先沉默后温柔地握手。A、C、D 与原文情感相反。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨推断题  
【定位】第⑥段末 "my son has quietly grown up... my heart warm"  
【解析】表现母亲感到孩子成长、彼此感动。A、C、D 与文意不符。故选 B。

- **cucumber** [n.] 黄瓜  
- **candle** [n.] 蜡烛
