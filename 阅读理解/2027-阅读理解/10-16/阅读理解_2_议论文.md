# 阅读理解（议论文） **Is Keeping a Daily Diary Still Worth It for Middle-School Students?**

① Every year, around this time, our school hands out thin blue notebooks and asks each student to keep a **daily diary** for the whole term. Some of us cheer; most of us sigh. In the age of short videos and quick messages, many people ask: is spending ten minutes a day on a paper diary really **worth the time**? I think the honest answer is **yes** — but only when the diary is kept in a wise way.

② First, **a diary is a slow mirror**. Short videos show us only one bright second; a diary records the full hour around that second — how we got there, how we felt before, and what we thought afterwards. Over a year, reading back through the pages, we begin to see our own **patterns**: which days we are brave, which days we are lazy, which friendships grow stronger, which habits quietly drag us down. No app can give a student this kind of self-portrait.

③ Second, a diary is a **quiet writing coach**. Essays in class come once a week, and the topics are given by the teacher. A diary comes every evening, chosen by us. When we write about the soup Mum cooked, the lonely classmate at the basketball court, or the moon above the library roof, we slowly learn to turn ordinary life into sentences. Many Chinese writers — from Lu Xun to the young writers of today — have said that **their first reader was their own diary**.

④ Of course, critics are not wrong. They warn that a forced diary can turn into a **tired habit**: students copy last week's entry, invent a fake "good deed" to please the teacher, or simply write "today was normal, nothing special" for sixty days in a row. They also worry about **privacy**: a diary should hold honest feelings, but if the teacher will read it for marks, students may never write what they really think. A diary like that is not a mirror; it is a **mask**.

⑤ These worries are real, but they speak against **a certain kind** of diary, not against the habit itself. A better plan is **a half-open, half-private diary**. Each student keeps two notebooks side by side. The **open notebook** is for what you are happy to share: a book you liked, a walk after dinner, a question about class. The **private notebook**, locked with a simple key, is for what only you need to know. Teachers mark the open book for completion, not for content, and never read the private one.

⑥ Some people also ask, "Can't I just keep a digital diary on my phone?" You can, and many adults do. But for teenagers, **pen and paper still have a special gift**: they slow us down. Typing is fast; handwriting is thoughtful. While your hand moves slowly across the page, your mind has time to catch up, and true feelings — which are often quiet — finally have space to speak.

⑦ Ten minutes a night is not much. Over three years of middle school, it adds up to **about 180 hours** — roughly one average summer holiday. Spent on videos, those hours disappear without a trace. Spent on a diary, they turn into a small library about the person you are becoming. That library may not win any prize, but one day, perhaps after your own child is born, you will open it and find **yourself at thirteen**, still waiting on the page.

---

**注释：** diary 日记；self-portrait 自画像；forced 强迫的；privacy 隐私；handwriting 手写

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. According to Paragraph ②, what is the main benefit of a diary described as a "slow mirror"?**

A. It helps students win writing prizes.  
B. It helps students see their own patterns — moods, habits, and changes — over time.  
C. It helps students become more popular online.  
D. It can be read by teachers every day.

**2. In Paragraph ③, what does the writer compare a diary to?**

A. A noisy classroom.  
B. A quiet writing coach that trains students to turn ordinary life into sentences.  
C. A homework machine.  
D. A book of rules.

**3. What problem do critics of daily diaries worry about, according to Paragraph ④?**

A. Diaries may be too expensive.  
B. Students may turn diary-writing into a tired, fake habit, or feel unable to write honestly because the teacher will read it.  
C. Diaries take too much money to print.  
D. Students will forget English words.

**4. What solution does the writer suggest in Paragraph ⑤?**

A. Ban diaries completely.  
B. Let the teacher read everything for a mark.  
C. Use a "half-open, half-private" system: an open notebook marked only for completion, and a private notebook never read by teachers.  
D. Write only on phones.

**5. What is the writer's main opinion about keeping a diary?**

A. Diaries are a waste of time in the age of short videos.  
B. Diaries only help professional writers.  
C. Keeping a wise diary (honest, partly private, written by hand) is worth the time and becomes a small library of the person a student is becoming.  
D. Diaries should be as short as possible.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "see our own patterns ... which days we are brave ... which habits quietly drag us down"  
【解析】"慢镜子"作用在于看清长期的自我。A、C、D 均非核心益处。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第③段 "a diary is a quiet writing coach ... turn ordinary life into sentences"  
【解析】暗喻日记为安静的写作教练。A、C、D 偏离。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "tired habit ... invent a fake 'good deed' ... never write what they really think"  
【解析】反对派双重担忧：流于形式 + 隐私顾虑。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "half-open, half-private diary ... open notebook ... private notebook"  
【解析】两本并行，分而治之。A、B、D 均错。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段立论 + 第⑦段总结 "a small library about the person you are becoming"  
【解析】以"明智地"为条件肯定日记长期价值。A、B、D 均偏离。故选 C。
