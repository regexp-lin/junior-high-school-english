# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Teenagers Write Handwritten Letters to Their Grandparents?**

① My grandmother still keeps a small wooden box under her bed. Inside it, she has thirty-two letters that my father and my aunt wrote to her between 1998 and 2005, when they were studying in faraway cities. The paper has turned a soft yellow, but Grandmother says she can still "hear" their voices when she reads them. Today, when everyone uses voice messages and stickers, should teenagers still write **handwritten letters** to their grandparents from time to time? I strongly believe we should.

② First, a handwritten letter is a gift of **time**. A voice message takes ten seconds to send, but a letter takes twenty or thirty minutes to write. Those minutes say something that emojis cannot: "I thought about you for half an hour today." Grandparents, who often wait a whole week for a short phone call, feel this time-gift deeply. In a world that races past them, a paper letter stops the clock for a moment.

③ Second, a letter gives us space to **organize our thoughts**. When we talk on a video call, we often jump from one topic to another. A letter, however, forces us to sit down, choose the most important news, and arrange it clearly. "Last week I joined the running club. The first training was hard, but my legs feel stronger now." In three sentences, a teenager gives their grandparent something to imagine, picture and remember.

④ Third, handwriting itself carries **feelings** that machines cannot copy. Our Chinese teacher often says that characters have a shape like a person's face. A quick, messy letter shows that we are excited; a neat one shows that we are calm and thoughtful. Grandparents, who grew up reading real letters, can "see" our mood just from the way we hold the pen. No keyboard can send that kind of information.

⑤ Of course, some students say, "My grandparents also use smartphones now — why still bother with paper?" It is true that many older people now send voice messages. But messages disappear after a scroll; a letter can be read again in ten years, when grandparents may no longer be with us. A paper letter is not only a message for today, but a small, quiet **memory** for tomorrow.

⑥ I am not asking teenagers to write letters every week. Even **one or two letters a year** — maybe before Spring Festival and before Mid-Autumn — would be enough. Send the letter to your grandparent's address, and imagine them opening it slowly, perhaps with their reading glasses at the end of their nose. In that moment, you are not just a busy student with lots of homework. You are a small, warm piece of their day — and a gentle link to a future that remembers the past.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What does the writer believe?**

A. Teenagers should never write paper letters.  
B. Teenagers should write handwritten letters to their grandparents from time to time.  
C. Grandparents should stop using phones completely.  
D. Only adults should write letters.

**2. According to paragraph ②, why is a letter "a gift of time"?**

A. Because letters arrive faster than voice messages.  
B. Because a letter takes much longer to write than a voice message, showing that the writer thought about the reader for a long time.  
C. Because letters are cheaper.  
D. Because letters have beautiful stamps.

**3. Why does the writer say letters give us space to organize our thoughts?**

A. Because letters cannot contain any news.  
B. Because a letter forces us to sit down, choose the most important news, and arrange it clearly.  
C. Because we can copy directly from a textbook.  
D. Because we must use red ink.

**4. What, according to the writer, does handwriting show that a keyboard cannot?**

A. Our handwriting shows our age.  
B. Our handwriting carries feelings; a quick, messy letter shows excitement and a neat one shows calm, thoughtful mood.  
C. Our handwriting tells the weather.  
D. Our handwriting speaks foreign languages.

**5. How does the writer answer people who say "grandparents also use smartphones now"?**

A. Voice messages are always better than letters.  
B. Messages disappear after a scroll, but a paper letter can be read again in ten years.  
C. Grandparents should not use phones at all.  
D. Teenagers should throw away all phones.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末及全文  
【解析】作者主张偶尔给祖辈手写信。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "A voice message takes ten seconds... a letter takes twenty or thirty minutes..."  
【解析】花时间写信即是把时间作为礼物。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "A letter, however, forces us to sit down, choose the most important news, and arrange it clearly."  
【解析】写信帮助整理思路。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "A quick, messy letter shows that we are excited; a neat one shows that we are calm and thoughtful."  
【解析】字迹能传情。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题／推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "messages disappear after a scroll; a letter can be read again in ten years..."  
【解析】纸质信件是长久的记忆。A/C/D 均与原文相反。故选 B。
