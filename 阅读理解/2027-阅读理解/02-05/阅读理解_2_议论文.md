# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Parents Read with Their Children Every Night?**

① At the end of a long day, many parents sit down tired on the sofa and hand their middle school children a book. "Read on your own," they say. "You are not a baby anymore." Yet more and more teachers and reading experts are asking a gentle question: *Should parents continue to read with their children every night, even in the teenage years?*

② Some parents feel the answer is clearly "no." They believe that reading together belongs to early childhood. Once a child can read alone, parents should let him be independent. Besides, parents themselves are often busy. Between work, cooking and cleaning, spending another twenty minutes on a book feels like too much. "My son is fourteen," one father said, "He can read far more difficult books than I can."

③ However, these reasons only tell half of the story. Reading together does not mean reading *the same words* together; it means sharing the **same book and the same thoughts**. Even if the child reads silently and the parent listens, or the parent reads aloud a short news article at breakfast, the quiet moment builds a bridge between two minds that are often apart during the day.

④ Reading together also helps students understand difficult topics. A teenage reader may rush through a chapter and miss the writer's careful feelings. A parent, with more life experience, may stop and ask, "Why do you think the hero refused the money?" or "Have you ever felt the same?" Such simple questions can turn a forty-page story into a deep conversation about courage, honesty, or even first love.

⑤ Another important reason is **silent support**. Teenagers often say that they do not need their parents, yet they still want to know that their parents are nearby. When a father sits on the sofa with his own newspaper while his daughter reads her novel in the same room, a soft feeling of safety fills the air. Research from a reading group in Shanghai in 2024 showed that students whose parents read in the same room for at least 20 minutes a day were more willing to talk about their worries — even if no word was spoken during the reading itself.

⑥ Of course, parents should never use nightly reading as a second exam. Pushing, testing or writing long notes after each page will kill the joy quickly. The secret is simple: pick a book both sides like, read at the same time, close the book at the same time, and chat softly about only one or two things. Under the warm light of a table lamp, a family grows — not louder, not richer, but quietly wiser and closer.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the question discussed in this passage?**

A. Whether middle school students should read e-books.  
B. Whether parents should read with their teenage children every night.  
C. Whether schools should give reading homework every night.  
D. Whether children should read alone in the early years.

**2. Why do some parents think "reading together" is not needed anymore?**

A. They think their children are too young.  
B. They worry about the cost of books.  
C. They believe their children are independent and they themselves are busy.  
D. They hate reading books.

**3. What does "reading together" truly mean, according to the writer?**

A. Reading the same words at the same speed.  
B. Sharing the same book and the same thoughts.  
C. Buying two copies of every book.  
D. Reading in the same library every weekend.

**4. What did the 2024 reading group in Shanghai find?**

A. Students who read alone became more independent.  
B. Students whose parents read in the same room were more willing to share worries.  
C. Parents who read novels sleep better.  
D. Students without parents at home read faster.

**5. What mistake should parents avoid during family reading?**

A. Buying easy books.  
B. Turning reading into a new exam.  
C. Reading silently with the child.  
D. Asking one simple question about the book.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句  
【解析】全文围绕"父母是否应每晚与中学生一起阅读"展开。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "parents themselves are often busy... He can read far more difficult books"  
【解析】家长认为孩子能自读、且自身太忙。A 相反，B、D 无依据。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第③段 "it means sharing the same book and the same thoughts"  
【解析】真正的同读是分享同一本书和想法。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "students whose parents read in the same room... more willing to talk about their worries"  
【解析】父母同室阅读的学生更愿倾诉烦恼。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "Pushing, testing... will kill the joy"  
【解析】不要把共读变成考试。故选 B。
