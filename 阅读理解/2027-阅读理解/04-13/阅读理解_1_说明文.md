# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Is the Sky Blue, and Why Are Sunsets Red?**

① On a clear spring day, we see a sky as blue as a deep lake. In the evening, the same sky often turns into a warm painting of **orange, pink, and red**. Many children think the sky is simply painted by an artist living among the clouds. In fact, the answer is much more interesting: the colours above us come from a quiet conversation between **sunlight** and tiny bits of **air**.

② Sunlight looks white to our eyes, but it is actually a **mix of all the colours** of the rainbow — red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet. When sunlight enters the Earth's atmosphere, it meets millions of tiny gas molecules, mostly **nitrogen and oxygen**. These tiny molecules are much smaller than the wavelengths of light. As sunlight passes through them, something very special happens — **scattering**.

③ Not all colours are scattered equally. **Short-wavelength colours** like **blue and violet** are scattered much more strongly than long-wavelength colours like red and orange. Imagine throwing a ball through a forest. A small, quick ball (blue light) hits many branches and bounces in every direction, while a big, slow ball (red light) passes through more easily. Because of this, blue light is thrown all over the sky, and our eyes catch it wherever we look. That is why the sky **looks blue** to us during the day.

④ "But why not violet?" is a good question. Violet light is scattered even more than blue. However, our eyes are **less sensitive** to violet, and some violet light is absorbed higher up in the atmosphere. So the blue we see is the strongest one our eyes can clearly pick up. A tiny amount of the violet is still there; it just hides quietly behind the brighter blue.

⑤ Now think about the sunset. When the sun is low, its light travels a **much longer path** through the atmosphere. Along this long trip, most of the blue and violet light is scattered away long before it reaches our eyes. What is left is mostly the **red, orange, and pink** light. These colours come straight to us and paint the clouds and the horizon. The dustier or wetter the air, the redder the sunset may look — which is why people often say, "**Red sky at night, sailor's delight.**"

⑥ So the blue of noon and the red of sunset are not tricks of painting; they are a natural show of **physics and air**. The sky is a great mixer of light, and our eyes are the lucky audience. The next time you lie on the grass in a spring afternoon, remember: above you, billions of small gas molecules are bouncing blue light into your eyes, just to make an ordinary day a little more beautiful.

**1. What does the article mainly explain?**

A. How rainbows are made in the shower.  
B. Why the sky is blue during the day and red at sunset.  
C. How artists paint clouds.  
D. Why grass grows green in spring.

**2. Sunlight is actually made up of _______.**

A. only blue and red light  
B. only white light with no colours  
C. a mix of all the colours of the rainbow  
D. only violet light

**3. Why do we see the sky as blue during the day?**

A. Blue paint is sprayed by airplanes.  
B. Short-wavelength blue light is scattered more strongly by tiny gas molecules.  
C. The ocean reflects blue onto the sky.  
D. The sun is blue underneath.

**4. Why do we not see a violet sky, even though violet is scattered more than blue?**

A. Violet is too small to be seen.  
B. Our eyes are less sensitive to violet, and some violet is absorbed higher up.  
C. The moon eats up all the violet light.  
D. Violet only appears at night.

**5. Why do sunsets often look red or orange?**

A. The sun burns stronger in the evening.  
B. Red paint is added to the clouds by wind.  
C. Sunlight travels a longer path, and most blue light is scattered away before reaching our eyes.  
D. Farmers burn wood at sunset.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句  
【解析】全文解释天蓝与日落红色。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "a mix of all the colours of the rainbow"  
【解析】阳光是彩虹色混合。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "blue and violet... scattered much more strongly"  
【解析】短波长蓝光更强散射。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "our eyes are less sensitive to violet..."  
【解析】眼睛对紫不敏感。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "longer path... blue and violet light is scattered away"  
【解析】日落时蓝光已散尽。故选 C。
