# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Lost Wallet on the Number 5 Bus**

① Last Wednesday after school, I took the **Number 5 Bus** home, as usual. My head was full of a geography test I had just finished, and my ears were full of a new song from my headphones. I sat near the back, next to the window, watching the spring city slide by. Three stops before mine, an old man in a grey coat got off in a hurry, leaving behind a small, **brown leather wallet** on his seat.

② I noticed the wallet only after he had already stepped off the bus. At first, I felt very lucky, and then immediately very silly. "Maybe someone else will take it," a small voice whispered inside me. "You are only 14; this is not your business." But then I thought of my own grandpa — how **he always worried about losing his things**, how he would panic for hours. My hand reached out before my brain fully agreed. I picked up the wallet and held it tight against my bag.

③ At my stop, I did not go home. Instead, I walked two streets to the **small neighbourhood police station**. Officer Yang was at the front desk, drinking a paper cup of hot water. He looked tired but smiled when I walked in. "Uncle," I said, my voice a little thin, "I found this on the Number 5 Bus. I did not open it. I think it belongs to an old man in a grey coat." Officer Yang's eyes became warm. "Thank you, kid. You did the right thing — calmly and quickly."

④ He asked me to write a short **"handover note"**, then gently opened the wallet in front of me. Inside, there were 1,200 yuan in cash, an old photo of a little girl holding a balloon, and a small paper with **one phone number**, written in careful, round handwriting. "This little girl must be his granddaughter," Officer Yang said softly. "And this phone number is likely his own." He picked up the desk phone and dialed.

⑤ Twenty minutes later, the old man rushed in. His face was red from running. When he saw the wallet, tears began to shine in his old eyes. "I thought I had lost everything," he said, holding the photo in a shaking hand. "This is **my wife's last photo of our granddaughter**. The cash is money I just took for her school trip. If I had lost the photo… I do not know." Officer Yang looked at me. "This young student brought it in himself."

⑥ The old man took my hand in both of his. His hands were warm and full of thin lines. "What is your name, child?" he asked. "Tell me your school; tell me your teacher's name." I felt shy and tried to pull my hand back. "Please," I said, "do not make it a big thing. I only did what my grandpa would have wanted me to do." He smiled through his tears and said, "Then your grandpa must be a very lucky man."

⑦ On the way home, the sunset was a soft pink, and the clouds looked like cotton candy. I kept thinking about the photo with the balloon girl. In our busy city, it is easy to look the other way, to pretend we did not see a wallet, a bag, or a person in trouble. But that day, I learned something new. **The smallest kindness can hold the biggest hope for a stranger.** I opened my headphones again, but this time, I did not turn on any music. The city itself, with its buses, its lights, and its people, was already a song worth listening to.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. How did the writer first feel when he saw the wallet left on the seat?**

A. Very worried and sad.  
B. Lucky at first, then a little silly and confused.  
C. Angry at the old man.  
D. Too sleepy to care.

**2. What made the writer decide to pick up the wallet?**

A. He needed extra money for school.  
B. He remembered how his own grandpa always worried about losing things.  
C. He wanted to show his headphones were fancy.  
D. A teacher on the bus told him to.

**3. What did the writer do after getting off the bus?**

A. He went straight home and told his mother.  
B. He walked to a small neighbourhood police station and gave in the wallet.  
C. He hid the wallet in his bag and kept it a secret.  
D. He gave the wallet to a stranger in the street.

**4. What was the MOST important thing inside the wallet for the old man?**

A. The 1,200 yuan in cash.  
B. The phone number on the paper.  
C. His wife's last photo of their granddaughter.  
D. His bank card.

**5. What lesson did the writer learn from this story?**

A. The smallest kindness can hold the biggest hope for a stranger.  
B. Buses are the best place to sleep.  
C. Police officers are always too busy to help.  
D. Only adults should help lost people.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "very lucky, and then immediately very silly"  
【解析】先庆幸再犹豫。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "I thought of my own grandpa..."  
【解析】想到自己爷爷会担心。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "walked two streets to the small neighbourhood police station"  
【解析】去派出所。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "my wife's last photo of our granddaughter"  
【解析】照片最重要。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦段 "smallest kindness can hold the biggest hope"  
【解析】微小善意意义重大。故选 A。
