# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle-School Students Learn Simple Programming?**

① A few years ago, writing computer code was seen as something only university students or engineers needed to do. Today, many primary schools already teach block-based programming, and parents often ask a new question: **should middle-school students also spend time learning simple programming?** Both sides of this question have strong voices.

② Those who say "no" often worry about time. Middle-school students already have Chinese, maths, English, physics, and P.E., plus piles of homework every evening. "My son sleeps at 11 p.m.," one father said on a school notice board. "Where can he fit in another subject?" Others worry that children will stare at screens even longer, which is bad for their eyes and their sleep.

③ However, the people who support programming believe these worries miss the point. First, learning to program is really **learning how to think clearly**. When a student writes a small game, she must break a big problem into small steps, check her mistakes, and try again. These thinking habits help with maths, writing, and even daily life — for example, planning a family trip or solving a puzzle with friends.

④ Second, simple programming is more **creative** than many people imagine. With block-based tools like Scratch, a student can build a talking cat, a guessing game, or an animation about her grandmother's hometown. She is not just "using" a phone; she is making something new from an empty screen. This kind of activity turns screen time from passive watching into active creating.

⑤ Third, in a world full of AI chat tools and smart robots, the student who **understands even a little code** can see more clearly how these tools work and where they can go wrong. She knows why an AI sometimes gives a strange answer, and she can talk about the rules that guide it. Without this basic understanding, she will always be a user but never a confident maker.

⑥ Of course, no one is saying that every teenager must become a programmer. Two or three hours a week, guided by a good teacher, can already open the door. Whether a student ends up loving art, medicine, or business, the habit of thinking step by step will stay with her for life. For this reason, I believe middle-school students should learn at least a **simple level** of programming — not to add more pressure, but to add more **clear thinking** to their future.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What question does the passage mainly discuss?**

A. How to write a popular mobile game.  
B. Whether middle-school students should learn simple programming.

**2. What do the parents in Paragraph 2 worry about?**

A. Programming is too easy for middle-school students.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句 + 全文讨论  
【解析】全文围绕"中学生是否应学习简单编程"展开。A、C、D 偏题或无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段第 3 句 + 末句 "Where can he fit in another subject?... stare at screens even longer"  
【解析】家长担心时间不够+长时间盯屏。A 项与"too easy"相反；C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段第 1 句 "learning to program is really learning how to think clearly"  
【解析】作者明确指出最大好处是"学会清晰思考"。A、B、D 均非主要论点。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "understands even a little code... can see more clearly how these tools work and where they can go wrong"  
【解析】作者提 AI 工具是为说明懂一点代码的人能看清其工作原理与错误。A 项"永远正确"、B 项"应禁止"均与文意相反；D 项偏离论点。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段末句 "middle-school students should learn at least a simple level of programming"  
【解析】作者最终结论是中学生至少应学习简单水平的编程。A 项过度推断；B、D 无依据。故选 C。
