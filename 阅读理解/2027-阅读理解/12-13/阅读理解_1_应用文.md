# 阅读理解（应用文） **Lihua Middle School Winter Handicraft Fair 2027**

---

> **Lihua Middle School · Student Union + Art Department**  
> **Winter Handicraft Fair 2027** — *"Small Hands, Warm Gifts, Kind Winter."*  
> **Date**: **Saturday, 20 December 2027**  
> **Time**: **09:00 – 15:30** (six and a half hours, one-day event)  
> **Venue**: **Lihua Middle School Main Hall (1F) + Outdoor Plaza**  
> **Beneficiary**: All money raised goes to **Sunshine Elderly Home, 4.2 km east of school** (for warm blankets and winter fruit)

---

① The short December days are cold, but our school has warm lights and 812 pairs of careful student hands. For the **twelfth year in a row**, Lihua Middle School's Student Union and Art Department are holding the **Winter Handicraft Fair**: one Saturday, one main hall, hundreds of small handmade gifts — priced between **¥3 and ¥30** — sold by students to students, parents, and neighbours. Every single yuan raised is walked, on the same afternoon, to Sunshine Elderly Home, where Ms Xu (the kind director) will convert it, before 25 December, into **warm blankets, winter pears, and oranges** for the 86 elderly residents. Nothing is kept by the school. Nothing is kept by any student.

---

---

## 💴 Simple Money Rules

- **Small cash (1, 5, 10, 20 yuan notes)** is preferred; please bring from home.  
- **WeChat/Alipay QR codes** are allowed at each stall; each stall has **ONE official QR code** tied to the Student Union public account (not to any student's private account).  
- **No haggling** — prices are fair and final; all prices have been reviewed by the Art Department.  
- **No credit, no "I owe you"** — every item is paid for in full on the spot.  
- **Each student stall-holder** keeps a small paper ledger; Student Union treasurer Zhou Xin counts the total at 15:00.

---

## 🏷️ Stall-Holder Rules (for the 46 student sellers)

1. Bring at least **15 handmade items** by 08:30.  
2. Items must be **truly handmade by you** — no factory-bought goods, no re-sale of family items.  
3. Each item must have a **small paper price tag** (name, maker, price).  
4. **Dangerous materials** (sharp pins, very small parts under 3 cm, glass) are **not allowed**; the school's 3-year-old preschool children may visit.  
5. If an item does not sell by 15:00, you may **keep it** or **donate it** to Sunshine Elderly Home (they especially welcome knitted scarves and bookmarks).  
6. All stall-holders receive a **small green "Kind Hands" sticker** on their student-ID holder.

---

## 👐 Visitor Kindness Rules

- **One gift, many hands**: if you are in Grade 9, please leave one item for Grades 7 and 8 to buy as well.  
- **Touch gently**: paper-cuts and small clay figures break easily.  
- **Say a small thank-you** to the maker when you buy; the maker is often standing right there, shy and happy.  
- **Do not** ask for a lower price; the price already supports the elderly home.  
- **Leave the stage area clear** during the two live demos (10:30 paper-cut, 14:00 calligraphy).

---

## ❗ Quiet Reminders

- **This is not a fundraising competition.** No stall is ranked by sales.  
- **No one's name is read out on stage** for raising the most money.  
- **If a stall sells out early**, the student may sit at another stall's table and help out for the rest of the day.  
- **Every maker is equal** — the student who sold three bookmarks matters as much as the student who sold thirty.  
- **Phones**: students selling at stalls may use phones only for payment QR codes; no short-video scrolling while customers wait.

## 💬 A Small Word from the Student Union

> "A handmade gift carries two small warmths: the warmth of the person who made it, and the warmth of the person who will receive it. This Saturday, **812 pairs of student hands** will quietly turn small paper, wool, and clay into warm blankets and winter pears for 86 grandparents across the park. We will not shout. We will not rank. We will simply make, sell, count, and walk. **Come and stand with us, for six and a half hours, inside the small warm light of a winter Saturday hall.** Your ¥5 and our kind hands, together, become one blanket for one grandmother we have never met. That is the whole point of the day."
