# 阅读理解（说明文） **The Great Wall: A Long Stone River Across the Land**

① When people talk about China, they often think of the Great Wall first. This huge wall runs across northern China like a long stone river. It is one of the greatest buildings ever made by hand, and every year millions of visitors come to walk on it.

② The earliest parts of the Great Wall were built more than two thousand years ago. Different states built their own walls to protect their people and fields from attacks. Later, the First Emperor of the Qin Dynasty decided to connect these walls into one long line. After him, many dynasties, such as the Han and the Ming, repaired and made it even longer and stronger.

③ The parts that most visitors see today were built in the Ming Dynasty, about 500 to 600 years ago. Workers used stones, bricks and heavy soil. In many places, the wall runs up steep mountains and across deep valleys. Strong towers were built every few hundred metres. Soldiers used them to watch the land, to rest at night, and to send warning signals using fires and smoke during the day.

④ The Great Wall is not just one single line. It is a system of walls, towers, gates and small roads. Some experts say that, if all these parts were put together in a straight line, the total length would be more than 20,000 kilometres. That is almost half the distance around the earth.

⑤ Today, the wall no longer keeps away soldiers, but it keeps bringing people together. Visitors from all over the world climb its stone steps, take pictures, and listen to stories about the past. To protect this great treasure, we should not draw on the stones, throw rubbish, or step on small plants. The Great Wall belongs not only to China, but also to all of us.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did different states start to build walls long ago?**

A. To welcome visitors from the south.  
B. To protect their people and fields from attacks.  
C. To keep animals out of the cities.  
D. To make money by selling tickets.

**2. Who first decided to connect the walls into one long line?**

A. The First Emperor of the Qin Dynasty.  
B. A general from the Han Dynasty.  
C. A famous artist of the Ming Dynasty.  
D. A modern mountain worker.

**3. What did soldiers use the towers for?**

A. Growing vegetables.  
B. Selling food to travellers.  
C. Watching the land and sending warning signals.  
D. Teaching English to children.

**4. According to Paragraph ④, how long would the Great Wall be in a straight line?**

A. About 2,000 kilometres.  
B. More than 20,000 kilometres.  
C. The same as the Yangtze River.  
D. Half as long as the coast of China.

**5. What is the writer's main advice in the last paragraph?**

A. We should build more new walls.  
B. We should protect the Great Wall when we visit it.  
C. We should only visit the wall in winter.  
D. We should buy stones from the Great Wall.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Different states built their own walls to protect their people and fields from attacks."  
【解析】各州筑墙是为了防御侵袭。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "the First Emperor of the Qin Dynasty decided to connect these walls into one long line."  
【解析】秦始皇首次将长城连接成一整条。B、C、D 与原文不符。故选 A。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Soldiers used them to watch the land... to send warning signals using fires and smoke."  
【解析】士兵利用烽火台观察并发信号。其他选项均无依据。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "the total length would be more than 20,000 kilometres."  
【解析】全长超 2 万公里。A、C、D 与原文不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意/写作意图题  
【定位】末段 "we should not draw on the stones, throw rubbish, or step on small plants."  
【解析】作者希望人们爱护长城。A、C、D 与文意不符。故选 B。
