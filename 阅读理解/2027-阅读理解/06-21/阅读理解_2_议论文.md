# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle School Students Be Allowed to Use AI for Homework?**

In the past two years, more and more middle school students have been using AI chat tools for their homework. Some finish an English essay in five minutes. Some ask the AI to "explain this physics problem step by step". Are these tools good or bad for students? I believe middle school students **should** be allowed to use AI for homework, but only in a **careful**, **limited** way.

First, we must be honest: AI is not going away. In five years, when today's Grade 8 students become young workers, AI will be on every desk and every phone. If we do not learn to use it wisely now, we will lose a very important skill. Therefore, a total ban in schools does not prepare students for the real world. A better answer is to teach students how to use AI as a tool — just as we once learned to use calculators and search engines.

Second, AI can be a patient "teacher". A good student usually has 3 – 5 maths questions that he does not fully understand, but he is too shy to ask the teacher again and again. At home, he can ask an AI: "Please explain this problem with a simple example." The AI will not become tired or impatient. It will try five different ways to help him. This kind of one-on-one support is very difficult to get in a class of 45 students.

However, there are clear dangers. If students use AI to **replace** their own thinking — for example, copy an AI essay, change two words, and hand it in — then they are only fooling themselves. Over time, their own writing will become weak, and in a real exam without AI, they will have no ideas at all. Studies in several cities already show that some students who copy AI answers for a long time find it harder to start writing on their own.

So what should schools do? First, make rules clear. Students may use AI to **check** their work, **explain** a hard point, or **brainstorm** ideas. They should **not** ask AI to write the full homework for them. Second, teach students to write an "AI note" at the end of their homework — for example, "I used an AI tool to check my grammar, but the ideas and examples are mine." This is like saying where you found information in a book. Third, teachers should still hold short face-to-face talks with students; these quickly show who truly understands and who is just copying.

In short, AI is neither an angel nor a monster. It is a new kind of pen. A pen in wise hands can write a great story. In lazy hands, it can only copy. Our duty, both as students and teachers, is to choose the right hand.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the author's main opinion?**

A. Middle school students should be completely banned from using AI for homework.  
B. Middle school students should use AI for everything, without rules.  
C. Middle school students should be allowed to use AI for homework, but only in a careful, limited way.  
D. Only senior high school students can use AI.

**2. Why does the author say a total ban is not a good answer?**

A. Because AI is cheap and easy to get.  
B. Because AI will be everywhere in the future, and students need to learn how to use it wisely for their real lives.  
C. Because students will be angry with their teachers.  
D. Because AI is smarter than all teachers.

**3. Why is AI described as a "patient teacher" in paragraph 3?**

A. Because it never makes mistakes.  
B. Because it can give one-on-one help in different ways, without getting tired or impatient.  
C. Because it sits beside the student all day.  
D. Because it gives every student a prize.

**4. What is the danger of using AI the wrong way?**

A. Students may sleep less.  
B. Students may copy AI essays and slowly lose their own ability to think and write.  
C. Students may forget how to ride a bicycle.  
D. Students may buy the wrong kind of calculator.

**5. What is the "AI note" suggested by the author?**

A. A paper that students write to praise AI.  
B. A short note at the end of the homework saying how AI was used — for example, only to check grammar.  
C. A note that AI writes automatically every week.  
D. A note from the parents approving AI use.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第 1 段末句  
【解析】有限、谨慎地允许。A/B/D 均错。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 2 段  
【解析】AI 是未来不可避免的工具。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 3 段  
【解析】AI 能反复解释、一对一帮助。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 4 段  
【解析】依赖抄袭使写作能力衰退。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第 5 段 "write an 'AI note'..."  
【解析】附注声明 AI 使用方式。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
