# 阅读理解（说明文） **Why Does Ice Float on Water?**

① Take a cold can of cola out of the freezer. Open it, drop in an ice cube, and watch. The ice does not sink; it **floats on top**, just like a small boat. This simple scene happens in every kitchen, every lake, and every polar sea. Yet few of us stop to ask a strange question: **almost all solids sink in their own liquids, so why does ice choose to float?**

② The answer lies in the **shape** and **behaviour** of water molecules. Every drop of water is made of countless tiny **H₂O molecules**. When water is warm, these molecules move fast and pack tightly, like a busy market square with no empty space. When water gets colder, the molecules slow down and, just as we might expect, they pack even more tightly — up to a special point.

③ That special point is **4 ℃**. At 4 ℃, water reaches its most tightly packed, heaviest form. If water keeps cooling below 4 ℃, something surprising happens: the molecules form a **six-sided crystal** called **ice**. In this crystal, molecules hold hands at **fixed angles**, leaving tiny open spaces between them. So ice is, in fact, **less crowded** than liquid water. This is why ice is **lighter per cubic centimetre**, and why it floats.

④ This small fact has huge results for life on Earth. When a lake freezes in winter, the ice layer forms **on the top**, not at the bottom. Below the ice, fish, plants, and insects can still live in water just a few degrees above zero. If ice sank, it would pile up on the lake floor, and many lakes could freeze from the bottom up. Many water creatures, which our planet depends on, might not survive a single cold winter.

⑤ We can also see this idea in big polar scenes. The great white **icebergs** floating in the Arctic and Antarctic oceans are actually huge blocks of fresh-water ice, with about **90 percent under the surface** and only 10 percent above. They drift like silent giants, but the science behind them is the same as the one in your cola: ice is a little lighter than water.

⑥ So the next time you hear the small "**clink**" of an ice cube in a summer glass, remember: it is not just a cold sound. It is a quiet performance of physics, a reminder that our whole living world — from tiny fish in a winter lake to huge whales under the ice shelf — owes something wonderful to this little, floating miracle.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main question the article tries to answer?**

A. How ice cubes are made at home.  
B. Why ice floats on water while most solids sink.  
C. How cola cans are produced.  
D. How to fish under thick ice.

**2. At what temperature is water MOST tightly packed?**

A. 0 ℃.  
B. 4 ℃.  
C. 10 ℃.  
D. 100 ℃.

**3. What happens to water molecules when water freezes into ice?**

A. They form a tight, flat shape.  
B. They run around faster than in warm water.  
C. They form a six-sided crystal with tiny open spaces.  
D. They disappear completely.

**4. Why is it important that ice forms on TOP of lakes in winter?**

A. It looks more beautiful in photos.  
B. It makes the lake smell better.  
C. Fish and other creatures can still live in the water below.  
D. It helps ships move faster on the lake.

**5. About how much of an iceberg is hidden under the ocean surface?**

A. About 10 percent.  
B. About 50 percent.  
C. About 70 percent.  
D. About 90 percent.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末问题  
【解析】讨论冰为何浮在水面。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "4 ℃... most tightly packed, heaviest form"  
【解析】4℃时水密度最大。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "six-sided crystal... leaving tiny open spaces"  
【解析】六边形晶体含间隙。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "fish, plants, and insects can still live"  
【解析】冰盖在上使水下生命存活。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "90 percent under the surface"  
【解析】冰山约90%藏于水下。故选 D。
