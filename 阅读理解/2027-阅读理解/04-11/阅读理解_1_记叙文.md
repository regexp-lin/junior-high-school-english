# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Quiet New Boy Who Became My Best Friend**

① On the first Monday of March, our class door opened and a thin boy walked in, holding a blue schoolbag too big for his shoulders. "This is **Wen'an**," our head teacher said. "He has just moved here from Chongqing. Please help him." Most of us smiled and said nothing more. Wen'an sat beside me, silent as a small brown stone. I waved. He gave me the tiniest nod and then looked down at his book.

② For two weeks, Wen'an almost never spoke. He answered questions with a short "yes" or a softer "mm". During break, while the rest of us played ping-pong and shouted jokes, he sat by the window and folded **paper cranes**. Little white birds slowly grew on his desk. One day I asked, "Why cranes?" He said, "My **grandfather** is in hospital. One crane, one day, one wish." Then he turned pink and looked away.

③ The next afternoon, during P.E., I forgot my water bottle at home. My throat was dry, and the sun was strong. Suddenly, a plastic bottle appeared beside me. Wen'an had taken the long corridor trip to the fountain and filled it with cold water. He didn't say anything — he just put the bottle on the bench and walked back to his corner. Something in me warmed. I promised myself: **I would not let this new boy disappear in our noise.**

④ On a rainy Wednesday, I asked Wen'an if he wanted to walk home together. He paused, as if surprised that anyone had asked him, then nodded. We shared my red umbrella. It was too small for two people; half of his left shoulder was wet by the time we reached my gate, and half of my right shoulder was wet too. But neither of us moved away. We walked slowly through the puddles, talking about **video games**, **Chongqing hotpot**, and the strange taste of our school's cabbage soup.

⑤ On the weekend, Wen'an invited me to the hospital to meet his grandfather. The old man was thin and pale, but his eyes smiled when he saw us. "So this is the friend my grandson told me about?" he asked in a kind voice. He held my hand for a second, and I felt his hand was very cold, though the sun was warm. Wen'an placed his newest paper crane on the white bedside table. "Ye Ye, today's crane is for you," he whispered. I felt something quiet and deep, bigger than any classroom rule.

⑥ In April, Wen'an began to talk more. He read our class poems in a calm, gentle voice; he learned ping-pong at lunch; he even made up a small joke about our strict maths teacher that made five classmates laugh out loud. Colour slowly returned to his cheeks. When his grandfather passed away in late April, Wen'an cried at his desk for a whole break, and no one teased him. Instead, three girls from the back row placed **three paper cranes** on his desk — they had learned to fold them, secretly, for him.

⑦ I used to think friendship was something loud: shouting names across the playground, running together in a big group. Now I understand that friendship can also be **a shared umbrella on a rainy day, a bottle of cool water, and a quiet boy who folds a crane for a wish**. A new classmate is never "just a new classmate". Behind every silent face, there is a full world, waiting for a patient friend to step inside.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Where did Wen'an move from before joining the new class?**

A. Shanghai.  
B. Beijing.  
C. Chongqing.  
D. Hong Kong.

**2. Why did Wen'an fold paper cranes every day?**

A. He was going to sell them at the market.  
B. He wished to win a paper craft competition.  
C. Each crane was a wish for his sick grandfather.  
D. He needed them for a school decoration.

**3. What did Wen'an do when the writer was thirsty in P.E.?**

A. He gave the writer his own bottle with a smile.  
B. He walked to the fountain and filled a bottle with cold water.  
C. He told the teacher to stop the class.  
D. He laughed at the writer for forgetting the bottle.

**4. What did the three back-row girls do after Wen'an's grandfather passed away?**

A. They laughed at him quietly.  
B. They placed three paper cranes on his desk.  
C. They sent him an email with jokes.  
D. They borrowed his homework book.

**5. What does the writer finally understand about friendship?**

A. Friendship must always be loud and shouting.  
B. Friendship can be small, quiet acts like sharing an umbrella.  
C. Friendship needs large parties every weekend.  
D. Friendship is a waste of time for busy students.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "just moved here from Chongqing"  
【解析】从重庆转来。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "One crane, one day, one wish"  
【解析】每只纸鹤为爷爷许愿。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "taken the long corridor trip to the fountain..."  
【解析】去饮水机打水。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "placed three paper cranes on his desk"  
【解析】留下三只纸鹤。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑦段 "a shared umbrella... a bottle of cool water..."  
【解析】友谊也可以安静而微小。故选 B。

- **fountain** [n.] 饮水机/泉  
- **corridor** [n.] 走廊
