# 阅读理解（说明文） **Sorting Rubbish: A Small Habit with Big Power**

① Every day, each person in a city throws away about one kilogram of rubbish. When all this rubbish is mixed together, it is hard to clean, and it may pollute the earth, the water and the air. In recent years, cities in China and many other countries have started to ask people to sort rubbish into different groups before throwing it away.

② There are usually four main groups. The first is **recyclable rubbish**, such as paper, clean bottles and old clothes. They can be sent to factories and made into new products. The second is **kitchen rubbish** — leftover food, fruit skins and vegetable leaves. It can be turned into natural fertilizer for plants. The third is **harmful rubbish**, including used batteries, old medicines and broken light bulbs. They must be collected carefully because they may hurt the soil and water. The last group is **other rubbish**, which usually goes to a safe burning centre or a landfill.

③ Sorting rubbish helps us in several ways. It saves raw materials like wood and oil because more things can be recycled. It also saves land, because less rubbish will be buried. For schools, it is a chance to teach students about science and social responsibility. Some students even invent small boxes or apps that tell their parents where each kind of rubbish should go.

④ To make sorting easy at home, a family can put three or four small bins in the kitchen, each in a different color. Children can wash plastic bottles quickly with water before they go into the "recyclable" bin. Dry batteries should be kept in a small jar until there are enough to send to a collection point. It only takes a few extra seconds each day.

⑤ Our planet is our common home. A small habit like sorting rubbish may look simple, but millions of hands together can make a real difference. The next time you finish a bottle of water, think for two seconds before you throw it away.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why do more and more cities ask people to sort rubbish?**

A. Because rubbish is very cheap to sell.  
B. Because mixed rubbish may pollute the environment.  
C. Because workers are too lazy to collect it.  
D. Because people want to buy more new bins.

**2. Which of the following is NOT a recyclable item?**

A. A clean plastic bottle.  
B. Used paper.  
C. Old clothes.  
D. A fruit skin.

**3. What is kitchen rubbish often turned into?**

A. Glass for new windows.  
B. Natural fertilizer for plants.  
C. Metal for cars.  
D. Cotton for clothes.

**4. According to Paragraph ③, sorting rubbish can help schools ______.**

A. sell more school uniforms  
B. earn large amounts of money  
C. teach students about science and social responsibility  
D. remove all teachers from the classroom

**5. What is the writer's main hope in the last paragraph?**

A. People should stop drinking water from bottles.  
B. Every family should buy a big new house.  
C. Many small actions together can protect our planet.  
D. Only government officials can solve the rubbish problem.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末句 "it may pollute the earth, the water and the air."  
【解析】混合垃圾会污染环境，因此需要分类。A、C、D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "kitchen rubbish — leftover food, fruit skins..."  
【解析】果皮属于厨余垃圾，不是可回收物。A、B、C 均在可回收之列。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "It can be turned into natural fertilizer for plants."  
【解析】厨余可转化为天然肥料。A、C、D 均与原文无关。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "For schools, it is a chance to teach students about science and social responsibility."  
【解析】垃圾分类帮助学校开展科学和社会责任教育。A、B、D 均无依据。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】末段 "millions of hands together can make a real difference."  
【解析】作者希望人人都行动起来一起保护地球。A 过度推断；B 与主旨无关；D 把责任仅归政府与文意相反。故选 C。
