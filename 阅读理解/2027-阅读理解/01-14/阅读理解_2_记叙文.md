# 阅读理解（记叙文） **My Shaking Knees on the Stage**

① When Miss Lin asked our class to choose a student for the city English speaking competition, everyone looked at me. I got good marks in English tests, but I was the quietest girl in the class. "Anna, would you like to try?" Miss Lin smiled. I wanted to say no, but somehow I nodded.

② During the following three weeks, I practised every evening. I stood in front of a long mirror in my bedroom, tried different smiles, and read my speech again and again. My father helped me check my pronunciation, and my little brother pretended to be the judge. Slowly I could repeat the whole speech without looking at the paper, but my heart still beat fast whenever I thought of the real stage.

③ The big day finally came. Before my turn, I sat backstage and watched other students speak with great confidence. Their voices were strong and clear. My hands became cold, and my knees began to shake. I wanted to run away. Then Miss Lin walked over and put her warm hand on my shoulder. "Anna, just tell them your true story. Forget about the prize."

④ When my name was called, I walked up the stage step by step. The lights were bright, and I could not see the faces below. I took a long breath and started. My voice was a little shaky at first, but I thought of my family, my mirror, my bedroom — and the words came back like old friends. Little by little, I relaxed. I even smiled at the judges.

⑤ I did not win the first prize, but I got a "Most Improved" award. More important than the award, I felt a quiet power inside me. If I could stand on that stage and finish my speech, many other hard things in life would feel less scary. From that day on, I knew that a shy girl could also have a brave heart.

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why was Anna chosen to enter the competition?**

A. Because she always talked more than anyone else.  
B. Because she was good at English though she was quiet.  
C. Because she really wanted to win a first prize.  
D. Because her brother asked Miss Lin to choose her.

**2. How did Anna prepare for the speech at home?**

A. She asked a famous actor to teach her.  
B. She only read the speech silently in her heart.  
C. She practised in front of a mirror and with her family.  
D. She memorized songs instead of her speech.

**3. What did Miss Lin say to Anna backstage?**

A. "Remember, you must win the top prize."  
B. "Just tell them your true story. Forget about the prize."  
C. "Read your paper and do not look up at all."  
D. "Do not say anything about yourself."

**4. What does the writer mean by "the words came back like old friends"?**

A. Her classmates arrived to cheer her up on stage.  
B. She suddenly remembered some old English songs.  
C. The familiar sentences returned easily because she had practised so much.  
D. A friend gave her a paper with the speech on it.

**5. What is the main lesson Anna learned from the competition?**

A. Shy students should never try English speaking contests.  
B. The first prize is the only thing that really matters.  
C. A shy person can also be brave and gain inner strength.  
D. Parents should always stop children from public speaking.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "I got good marks in English tests, but I was the quietest girl in the class."  
【解析】Anna 英语好但性格安静。A 与"最安静"矛盾；C 文中未提她想要一等奖；D 弟弟只是帮助练习。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "I stood in front of a long mirror... My father helped me check... my little brother pretended to be the judge."  
【解析】Anna 在镜前练习并让家人帮忙。A、B、D 均不符原文。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 Miss Lin 的原话 "Anna, just tell them your true story. Forget about the prize."  
【解析】老师鼓励她讲真实故事，别在意奖项。A、C、D 均与原话相反。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】词义/句义猜测题  
【定位】第④段 "my mirror, my bedroom — and the words came back like old friends."  
【解析】上文讲述她在家反复练习，所以台上熟悉的句子像老朋友般回来。A 同学未到场；B 与英语歌无关；D 无人递稿。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】末段 "I knew that a shy girl could also have a brave heart."  
【解析】作者的核心感悟是内向的人同样可以勇敢。A 否定演讲与文意相反；B 片面强调奖项；D 阻止演讲与家人支持矛盾。故选 C。

- **judge** [n.] 评委
