# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle School Students Read the News Every Day?**

① At the start of this term, Mr. Zhou, our politics teacher, gave us a small challenge. "From next Monday," he said, "each class will have a **three-minute news corner** at the beginning of every morning. One student will share one piece of news — local, national, or from the big world outside." Most of us clapped, but a few friends frowned. A question followed us into the hallway: **should middle school students really read the news every day?**

② Those who **say yes** offer strong reasons. First, the world is big, and we are not living on an island. Whether we talk about a new rocket sent into space, a sports game on the other side of the earth, or a kind stranger who returned a lost wallet, **news helps us know what is happening** around us. Second, reading news **trains thinking skills**. Two reports on the same event may use different words, different pictures, even different numbers. A student who reads regularly slowly learns to ask, "Who wrote this? Is there another side?" This habit will be more valuable than many exam scores ten years later. Third, news also brings **small but warm stories**: a village teacher who stayed 30 years in the mountains, a young doctor who flew to help after an earthquake. These stories quietly shape the kind of adult we hope to become.

③ However, there are worries too. Some parents point out that a **middle school day is already tight** — six or seven classes, homework, evening study, maybe a hobby class. Adding a daily news task may feel like "one more ball to juggle". Other voices say that not all news is suitable for teenagers. Many online news feeds push shocking pictures, bad accidents, or endless arguments. Without a good filter, young readers may feel worried, angry, or just tired. **"More news" does not always mean "better thinking".**

④ So, is there a middle road? I believe yes. Students can read **a small amount, but regularly**. Ten minutes in the morning is enough — perhaps a single children's newspaper, the school news board, or the family news app chosen by parents. Teachers can also turn each week's **Friday lesson** into a short news discussion, where classmates share one piece of news and say what they learned and what they still doubt. In this way, we read less but think more; we meet the world without being overwhelmed by it.

⑤ A student who never reads the news grows up inside a small cup. A student who follows every shocking headline may drown in a cold river. The wiser way is to stand on a friendly bridge — to look at the world carefully, share thoughts with friends, and come back home with a warmer heart and a clearer mind.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the main question the writer tries to answer?**

A. Whether teenagers should learn to make short films.  
B. Whether middle school students should read the news every day.  
C. Why Mr. Zhou became a politics teacher.  
D. How news can be faster than radio.

**2. Which is NOT a reason given by supporters of daily news reading?**

A. News helps students learn what is happening in the world.  
B. Reading news can train students' thinking skills.  
C. News often tells warm stories that shape character.  
D. News reading will quickly raise English exam scores.

**3. According to paragraph ③, why do some parents worry?**

A. Students already have a tight daily schedule.  
B. News reports are too easy for teenagers.  
C. News always makes students cleverer.  
D. News only comes in foreign languages.

**4. What does the writer suggest in paragraph ④?**

A. Read as much news as possible, day and night.  
B. Never let students touch news before high school.  
C. Read a small amount regularly and discuss news with classmates.  
D. Let parents read news for children and tell them everything.

**5. What does the last paragraph compare news reading with?**

A. Climbing a tall mountain.  
B. Sitting inside a small cup or drowning in a cold river.  
C. Driving a fast train.  
D. Running in a long race.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "should middle school students really read the news every day?"  
【解析】核心议题为中学生是否每日读新闻。A、C、D 非主题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节否定题  
【定位】第②段三点理由：认识世界、训练思维、温暖故事  
【解析】文中未提"迅速提高英语分数"。A、B、C 均是原文理由。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "middle school day is already tight... one more ball to juggle"  
【解析】家长担心日程已紧。B、C、D 与原文相反或无依据。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "a small amount, but regularly... Friday lesson... short news discussion"  
【解析】少量规律 + 课堂讨论。A、B、D 与作者建议不符。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】修辞分析题  
【定位】第⑤段 "small cup... drown in a cold river... friendly bridge"  
【解析】用"小杯/冷河/友好桥"比喻。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**词汇注释（超纲词）**  
- **juggle** [v.] 同时应对多件事  
- **filter** [n.] 过滤器（此处比喻筛选）  
- **overwhelmed** [adj.] 不堪重负的
