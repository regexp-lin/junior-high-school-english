# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Evening Homework Time Be Limited for Middle School Students?**

① A quiet walk past any residential building at 10 p.m. on a school night will show you a familiar sight: many windows are still bright, and behind some of those windows, a young student sits at a desk, finishing another sheet of maths or a third English essay. In recent years, more and more parents, teachers and doctors have asked a serious question: **should a firm time limit be set on evening homework for middle school students?** I believe the answer is yes, and the earlier the better.

② First, research on teenage sleep is very clear. Students aged 12–15 need about **nine hours** of sleep each night to grow well, think clearly, and stay in a good mood. Yet students who are still writing homework at 11 p.m. can hardly sleep before midnight. A tired brain the next morning absorbs less in class, remembers less, and answers more slowly in tests. Cutting homework time by one hour, in many cases, does not hurt learning — it may protect it.

③ Second, endless homework pushes out other important parts of growing up. A teenager also needs time to **exercise**, to read stories that have nothing to do with exams, to help at home, to chat with family, and to sit quietly with his or her own thoughts. Without these hours, a student may have high test scores for a while, but lose the balance that makes a healthy, kind and confident adult.

④ Third, a fair homework limit helps make **learning more efficient**. When students know they have exactly 90 minutes, not "until everything is done", they learn to focus, to plan their time, and to ask their teachers clear questions when stuck. Teachers also feel pressure to design better, sharper homework — 10 good questions can teach much more than 30 routine ones. In this way, a time limit pushes both sides to improve.

⑤ Some parents worry that less homework time will hurt their child's competitive edge in exams. This fear is understandable in our busy city. However, many top countries have seen similar results: students with shorter but clearer homework do not fall behind. What really matters is **focus per minute**, not minutes per evening. A clear limit, together with good teaching, is a smarter path than long, tired nights.

⑥ In conclusion, the evening is a precious short window that a young person uses for sleep, family, rest and thought. Homework is important, but it is only one part of a much richer life. I strongly believe our city should set a clear rule — for example, no homework written **after 9:30 p.m.** for Grade 7, and **10:00 p.m.** for Grade 9. Such a rule gives young people back something they are quietly losing: **the right to rest**.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's central question?**

A. Whether homework should be given at all.  
B. Whether a firm time limit should be set on evening homework for middle school students.  
C. Whether teachers should be paid more.  
D. Whether students should attend extra weekend classes.

**2. According to paragraph ②, why is sleep important for students aged 12–15?**

A. Sleep makes them taller than all adults.  
B. Students need about nine hours of sleep to grow, think clearly and stay in a good mood.  
C. Sleep helps them stop eating breakfast.  
D. Sleep is only important during weekends.

**3. What does the writer say a fair homework limit encourages students to do?**

A. Write slowly to use up the whole evening.  
B. Focus, plan time, and ask clearer questions.  
C. Copy classmates' answers.  
D. Skip homework altogether.

**4. How does the writer answer parents who worry about competitive edge?**

A. He agrees completely that more homework always wins.  
B. He says that what really matters is focus per minute, not minutes per evening.  
C. He says students should give up exams.  
D. He says parents should do the homework for their children.

**5. What specific rule does the writer propose in the last paragraph?**

A. Homework should be banned in all schools.  
B. No homework may be written after 9:30 p.m. for Grade 7 and 10:00 p.m. for Grade 9.  
C. Homework must be done only at 6 a.m.  
D. Homework can be done only on weekends.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末 "should a firm time limit be set on evening homework..."  
【解析】核心讨论是否应对夜间作业设定时间限制。A/C/D 均非中心议题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "Students aged 12–15 need about nine hours of sleep each night..."  
【解析】12–15 岁每晚需约九小时睡眠。A/C/D 均与原文不符。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "they learn to focus, to plan their time, and to ask their teachers clear questions..."  
【解析】有时间限制能让学生更专注、更会规划并主动提问。A/C/D 均与原文相反。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "What really matters is focus per minute, not minutes per evening."  
【解析】作者强调每分钟的专注更重要。A/C/D 均不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解/主旨题  
【定位】第⑥段末 "no homework written after 9:30 p.m. for Grade 7, and 10:00 p.m. for Grade 9."  
【解析】作者提出具体时限建议。A/C/D 均与原文不符。故选 B。
