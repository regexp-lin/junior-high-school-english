# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Middle School Students Still Celebrate Children's Day?**

① Every 1 June, kindergartens and primary schools in China hold colourful parties. Small children wear bright clothes, sing songs, and come home with paper flowers in their bags. In most middle schools, however, this day is just another quiet school day — a math quiz here, an English dictation there. Some students begin to ask their parents a shy question: "We are teenagers now. **Are we still allowed to celebrate Children's Day?**" I strongly believe the answer should be **yes**.

② First, the international name of the day itself includes us. The United Nations defines a "child" as any person **under 18**. Most middle school students are between 12 and 15. This means a ninth-grade student, standing almost as tall as a teacher, is still legally a child. If the day is "International Children's Day", then by name and by law, it is our day too.

③ Second, middle school students often need a special day more than smaller children. Teenagers face heavy homework, many exams and a lot of stress. A half-day party does not slow down learning; instead, it gives everyone a short, warm pause. Even twenty minutes of group singing, a short drama performance, or a little basketball game between classes can lower stress levels and make the long summer term feel lighter.

④ Third, a well-designed Children's Day celebration can **reconnect** teenagers with their younger selves. During the party, students may be invited to write a short letter to the "little me" — the seven-year-old they used to be. What would they say? "I still love drawing," or "I am trying to be brave." Such a simple activity reminds us that growing up is not about throwing our past away; it is about carrying our younger heart gently into a bigger world.

⑤ Some parents worry that a big Children's Day party will make teenagers "stay young" and not get ready for real life. This worry, in my opinion, misunderstands children. Young hearts are not the opposite of strong minds. Most adults we admire — scientists, doctors, good teachers — are people who kept some of the wonder and playfulness of childhood all their lives. A school that celebrates Children's Day for its middle school students is simply saying: *we know you are growing up, and that is exactly why your inner child needs to be loved, not hidden.*

⑥ Children's Day in middle school does not have to be a silly party. It can be a short afternoon with a mini-concert, a paper letter to the "little me", a slice of cake and a group photo. Such an afternoon costs almost nothing, but it gives every student one small, warm memory for the busy years ahead. On 1 June this year, I hope more middle schools will stop a little, look at their students, and gently say: **"You are still a child, and that is wonderful."**

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What is the writer's main opinion?**

A. Middle school students should not celebrate Children's Day any more.  
B. Middle school students should still celebrate Children's Day.  
C. Only primary school pupils need Children's Day.  
D. Only parents should celebrate Children's Day.

**2. According to paragraph ②, who does the United Nations define as a "child"?**

A. Any person under 12.  
B. Any person under 18.  
C. Any person under 6.  
D. Only babies.

**3. What benefit of a middle-school Children's Day is mentioned in paragraph ③?**

A. It replaces summer holidays completely.  
B. Short activities such as group singing, a drama performance or a basketball game can lower stress and make the term feel lighter.  
C. It forces students to study more math.  
D. It makes all students become singers.

D. Students write to the school principal.

A. Young hearts are the enemy of strong minds.

D. Parents should ban Children's Day in every school.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末及全文  
【解析】作者支持中学生依然庆祝。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "any person under 18."  
【解析】18 岁以下皆为儿童。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段  
【解析】A/C/D 均不符；B 为原文所述益处。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义猜测题／细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "the seven-year-old they used to be..."  
【解析】写给自己的小时候。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑤段 "Young hearts are not the opposite of strong minds..."  
【解析】童心与成熟可共存。A/C/D 均错。故选 B。
