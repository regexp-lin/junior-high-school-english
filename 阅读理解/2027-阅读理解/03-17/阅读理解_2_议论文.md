# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Calligraphy Be a Required Class in Middle School?**

① Every Tuesday afternoon at our school, Grade 8 students take a special class — **Chinese calligraphy**. They sit quietly, hold a brush, and copy old characters on soft rice paper. Some students love it; others think it is "a slow thing in a fast world". Recently, our city asked all middle schools to make calligraphy a **required subject** for two years. The plan has started a warm, and sometimes hot, discussion.

② Those who **agree** with the new rule give three main reasons. First, calligraphy is part of Chinese culture. Each character carries history — the way a "water" stroke flows, the way "mountain" stands tall. If teenagers never hold a brush, they may one day read ancient poems on a phone screen but feel nothing behind the words. Second, **calligraphy trains patience**. A good character may need the student to write it twenty times. In an age of short videos and fast games, learning to sit still for twenty minutes is itself a rare gift. Third, several studies from Beijing Normal University show that students who practise calligraphy weekly often **write Chinese characters more neatly** in exams — which helps scores in language and history.

③ Others **do not agree**. They point out that middle-school students already have **seven or eight subjects**, plus homework, plus sport. Adding another required class may mean less time for sleep or family dinner. Some parents also argue that **not every child is good at brush work**; forcing a boy who prefers football to sit with a brush may cause stress, not love. A third point is practical: good **brushes, ink and special paper** are not cheap. In smaller towns, some schools may not have enough money for good materials, so the lessons might become "brush-waving" rather than real learning.

④ I think the city rule is **basically right, but it needs soft edges**. Calligraphy is indeed a treasure — hiding it away in museums is a pity. Yet the class should **not** become a heavy test subject. Maybe schools can keep it **once a week, 40 minutes**, with no written exam, only a small exhibition each term. Students with no interest may simply enjoy the quiet; students who fall in love with the brush can join a club after school. In this way, culture is kept alive, and young hearts are not tired.

⑤ After all, education is **not about filling a bottle**, but about lighting a lamp. A single brush-stroke in spring may become, many years later, a warm memory that says: this is where I come from.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why does the writer mention Grade 8 students on Tuesday afternoons at the beginning?**

A. To prove calligraphy is the most popular class in school.  
B. To show the writer is against the new rule.  
C. To introduce the topic of the article naturally.  
D. To advertise a calligraphy competition.

**2. According to paragraph ②, which is NOT a reason given by those who agree?**

A. Calligraphy helps pass on Chinese culture.  
B. Practising calligraphy may improve exam handwriting.  
C. Calligraphy trains students' patience.  
D. Calligraphy makes students run faster on the playground.

**3. What is the meaning of the underlined phrase "brush-waving" in paragraph ③?**

A. Real, careful practice of calligraphy.  
B. Only moving the brush in the air without serious learning.  
C. Learning to paint beautiful flowers.  
D. A famous style of Chinese painting.

**4. What is the writer's own opinion about the new city rule?**

A. The rule should be cancelled at once.  
B. The rule is good in direction but needs to be gentle in practice.  
C. The rule should be made even stricter with written exams.  
D. Only top students should be allowed to take calligraphy.

**5. Which saying best matches the last paragraph?**

A. Practice makes perfect.  
B. Time is money.  
C. Education is the lighting of a fire, not the filling of a vessel.  
D. Seeing is believing.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】写作意图题  
【定位】第①段第 1 句开始举出周二下午书法课的场景，并引出全文话题  
【解析】通过具体场景自然引出议题。A 过度；B 与下文立场不符；D 无依据。故选 C。

**2.**  
【答案】D  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段三个理由：文化、耐心、书写整洁  
【解析】文中支持方未提及"使学生跑得更快"。A、B、C 均为原文理由。故选 D。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义猜测题  
【定位】第③段末 "materials... so the lessons might become 'brush-waving' rather than real learning"  
【解析】与 "real learning" 对比，意为只挥笔不真正学习。A 与之相反；C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第④段首句 "basically right, but it needs soft edges"  
【解析】作者认同方向但主张温和实施。A、C、D 均不符合作者观点。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "education is not about filling a bottle, but about lighting a lamp"  
【解析】与"教育是点燃火焰，而非装满容器"最吻合。A、B、D 与主旨不符。故选 C。
