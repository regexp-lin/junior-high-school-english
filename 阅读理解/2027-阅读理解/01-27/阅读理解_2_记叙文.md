# 阅读理解（记叙文） **A Busy Day at the Zoo**

① Last weekend, I joined a student volunteer day at the city zoo. I had visited the zoo before, but that day was totally different — I was not a visitor any more, but a helper with a green cap and a small name tag.

② In the morning, a keeper named Uncle Li took five of us to the rabbit area. He taught us how to wash the water bowls and put fresh vegetables in the small feeders. Rabbits are shy, so we needed to move slowly and speak softly. One tiny white rabbit ran to my shoes and started to smell them. I wanted to touch it, but Uncle Li reminded me to wait until the rabbit came closer. "Trust needs time," he said with a smile.

③ After lunch, we helped at the visitor entrance. Many families arrived with young children. Our job was to hand out small maps of the zoo and answer simple questions. A little boy held his mother's hand and asked me in a low voice, "Excuse me, where can I see the elephants?" I checked the map and led them to the right path. When the boy waved goodbye, my whole afternoon suddenly felt brighter.

④ Not every moment was easy. In a corner of the bird house, a teenage visitor was tapping loudly on the glass. I walked over and politely asked him to stop. "It may scare the birds and make them sick," I explained. At first, he looked a little annoyed, but then he nodded and said sorry. I realized that being a volunteer also meant being brave enough to speak up in a friendly way.

⑤ Before we left, Uncle Li gave each of us a small paper certificate and a photo with the rabbits. On the bus home, I looked out of the window and thought about the day. The zoo is not just a place to see animals; it is also a place to learn care, patience and quiet courage. I am going back next month, and I hope more classmates will join me.

---

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why was that day at the zoo different for the writer?**

A. Because the zoo had many new animals.  
B. Because he went as a volunteer, not a visitor.  
C. Because the weather was very hot.  
D. Because his classmates all came with him.

**2. What did Uncle Li teach the volunteers to do in the rabbit area?**

A. To feed tigers with fresh meat.  
B. To wash water bowls and put fresh vegetables in the feeders.  
C. To sing songs for the rabbits.  
D. To catch the rabbits with their hands.

**3. What did the writer do in the afternoon at the entrance?**

A. He sold cold drinks to tourists.  
B. He cleaned the zoo parking lot.  
C. He handed out maps and answered simple questions.  
D. He took photos for a local newspaper.

**4. What did the writer do when a visitor tapped the glass in the bird house?**

A. He ignored the behaviour.  
B. He asked the visitor politely to stop.  
C. He called the police at once.  
D. He closed the bird house door.

**5. What is the main lesson the writer learned?**

A. Animals are always afraid of children.  
B. Volunteers should never say a word to visitors.  
C. Being a volunteer teaches care, patience and quiet courage.  
D. The zoo needs more expensive equipment.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "I was not a visitor any more, but a helper with a green cap and a small name tag."  
【解析】不同之处在于他是志愿者。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "He taught us how to wash the water bowls and put fresh vegetables in the small feeders."  
【解析】清洗水碗和放新鲜蔬菜。A、C、D 均不符。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Our job was to hand out small maps of the zoo and answer simple questions."  
【解析】发放地图并回答问题。A、B、D 均无依据。故选 C。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "I walked over and politely asked him to stop."  
【解析】作者礼貌地请求游客停止。A、C、D 均不符。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】末段 "a place to learn care, patience and quiet courage."  
【解析】志愿者教会他关心、耐心与勇气。A 片面；B 与他的提醒相反；D 无依据。故选 C。
