# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Summer We Built a Treehouse**

① The summer I turned twelve was the year my cousin Peter came to stay in our village for six long weeks. His parents were working in a distant city, and mine promised them, "We will keep him happy and safe." On the very first evening, Peter stood in our back garden, looked at the tall old pear tree, and said with shining eyes, "I know how we will keep me happy. We are going to build a treehouse."

② I had never built anything bigger than a paper plane. Peter, two years older than me, had once built a bookshelf with his father. I was worried, but he had a plan. He drew it on a thin paper napkin: four wooden posts, a small square floor, a short ladder, and a roof made of old tiles. "Only simple things," he said. "We are not building a palace." My father laughed, checked the tree for safety, and told us, "You may use the old wood by the shed — but I must approve every step."

③ For the next three weekends, Peter and I worked in the garden under a hot sun. We measured and sawed. We hammered our own fingers more than once. We argued about the angle of the ladder. We fought over the last biscuit at teatime. But every night we slept like stones, and every morning we were back in the garden with a little more confidence than the day before. My grandmother brought us cold tea and said we looked like two small carpenters.

④ One afternoon, a sudden storm blew over the unfinished frame. We came out after the rain to find our wooden floor soaking wet and one post knocked down. Peter looked close to tears. I felt angry at the whole sky. My father sat on the garden wall and said quietly, "Every builder learns to rebuild. This is not the end — it is a real day of work." We dried the wood, fixed the post, and started again. Strangely, that rainy failure made us closer.

⑤ On the fifth Saturday, we finally climbed up the short ladder together. From the floor of our treehouse, we could see over the garden wall to the bending green of the river. We ate a simple picnic up there — cold rice balls, a small bottle of lemon tea, two sweet peaches. Peter wrote on a board inside: "**Peter & Tao, July 2027. We built this with our hands.**" My grandmother took a photo from the ground. The wind moved the pear leaves as if they were clapping.

⑥ When Peter went home at the end of August, he hugged me tightly and whispered, "Treehouses are great, but cousins are better." I smiled and waved until his bus disappeared. The treehouse is still there today, a little older, a little greener with moss. Some summers, I climb up alone, listen to the leaves, and remember that small tired, happy boy who learned that summer how to measure twice, to cut once, and to start again after the rain.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did Peter come to stay in the writer's village?**

A. He wanted to open a bookshop.  
B. His parents were working in a distant city.  
C. His school sent him for punishment.  
D. He was ill and needed rest.

**2. What did Peter and the writer plan to build?**

A. A bookshelf.  
B. A long wooden fence.  
C. A treehouse in the old pear tree.  
D. A boat for the river.

**3. What happened one afternoon after a sudden storm?**

A. They found a stray dog in the garden.  
B. The wooden floor got soaking wet and one post was knocked down.  
C. A tree was struck by lightning.  
D. Their grandmother disappeared.

**4. What did the writer's father say after the storm?**

A. "Stop building at once, it is dangerous."  
B. "Every builder learns to rebuild. This is not the end — it is a real day of work."  
C. "Let's buy a finished treehouse instead."  
D. "You are too young for such a project."

**5. What is the MAIN lesson the writer learned that summer?**

A. Paper planes are more fun than treehouses.  
B. It is safer to never start any big project.  
C. With careful planning, teamwork, and the courage to start again after failure, small hands can build real things.  
D. Grandmothers should not take photographs.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段第2句 "His parents were working in a distant city..."  
【解析】父母在外地工作，故表弟来作者家住。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段末 "We are going to build a treehouse."  
【解析】他们要在老梨树上建树屋。A/B/D 均不符。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "the wooden floor soaking wet and one post knocked down."  
【解析】暴雨后木地板湿透、一柱倒下。A/C/D 均无据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "Every builder learns to rebuild. This is not the end..."  
【解析】父亲原话即此。A/C/D 均不符原意。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨归纳题  
【定位】第⑥段末 "measure twice, to cut once, and to start again after the rain."  
【解析】作者领悟到计划、合作与失败后重来的重要。A/B/D 均与主题相反。故选 C。
