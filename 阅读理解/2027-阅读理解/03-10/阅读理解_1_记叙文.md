# 阅读理解（记叙文） **Racing the Rain to Save Our Laundry**

① Last Sunday started with sunshine so bright that Mum and I decided to wash almost everything in the house — sheets, pillowcases, school uniforms, towels, even the old blue curtains. By noon, three long silver lines of wet clothes were dancing in the wind on our balcony. "Look at them! They'll be dry by dinner," Mum said proudly, and went inside to make lunch.

② I sat on the sofa with a cup of iced tea and my English workbook. Just as I finished my second paragraph, I looked up. The sky was no longer blue. A thick grey cloud, like a huge dirty blanket, was rolling over the buildings in front of our flat. Before I could say a word, a big drop hit the window — *tap!* Then came two, three, ten. Rain was sliding down the glass in silver lines.

③ "Mum! The clothes!" I shouted. Mum rushed out of the kitchen, her hands still white with flour. We ran to the balcony. The wind was strong, and the sheets were already swinging like angry ghosts. Mum grabbed one end of the line; I grabbed the other. The clothes pins kept jumping off like tiny grey frogs. A small pillowcase almost flew into the neighbour's balcony, but I caught it by one corner just in time.

④ Our little brother Tao, only seven, heard the noise and ran out too. He wanted to help but was too short to reach the line. So he held a big yellow basket in both hands and shouted, "Put them here! I am the basket boy!" Mum and I laughed — even in the middle of flying socks and wet hair, we laughed. We dropped shirt after shirt into Tao's basket. He staggered inside, half-hidden by a mountain of cloth, and came back for more.

⑤ In about six minutes, the balcony was empty, the rain was a shower, and our living room looked like a laundry shop. Mum's hair was stuck to her face. My slippers were floating in a small puddle. Tao was sitting on the sofa, wearing a towel on his head like a king's crown. "Well," Mum said, wiping her cheek, "sunshine brings clean clothes, but rain brings a good team."

⑥ That night, the clothes took forever to dry on the indoor racks, and the flat smelled like wet towels for two days. But every time I thought of Tao and his "basket boy" face, I started smiling. The storm had stolen our easy Sunday, and left us with something better — a small family story that even a bigger storm, years later, could never wash away.

**根据短文内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did Mum and the writer decide to do so much washing on Sunday morning?**

A. The washing machine was broken for a month.  
B. The sunshine was very bright, so they expected the clothes to dry quickly.  
C. They wanted to sell some old clothes.  
D. Grandma was coming to visit that evening.

**2. What made the writer look up from his English workbook?**

A. Mum's loud shout.  
B. Tao throwing a toy at him.  
C. Rain drops tapping the window.  
D. A phone call from a classmate.

**3. How did Tao help his family save the clothes?**

A. He held a big yellow basket and received clothes from the balcony.  
B. He climbed up to the line and pulled clothes down.  
C. He phoned the neighbours to close their windows.  
D. He cleaned the wet floor with a small towel.

**4. What does the writer mean by "the living room looked like a laundry shop"?**

A. The family opened a new shop.  
B. The room was full of wet clothes piled everywhere.  
C. The neighbours came to pick up their clothes.  
D. The walls of the room turned white.

**5. What is the main feeling the writer wants to share in the last paragraph?**

A. Regret about wasting a sunny Sunday.  
B. Worry that the clothes were not clean enough.  
C. Happiness about a warm family memory born during the storm.  
D. Anger at the weather forecast on TV.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段第 1 句 "sunshine so bright... decided to wash almost everything"  
【解析】因为阳光灿烂，估计衣物能很快晒干。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段末 "a big drop hit the window — tap!"  
【解析】作者发现雨滴敲窗才抬头。A、B、D 均未发生于此刻。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】A  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "held a big yellow basket... Put them here! I am the basket boy!"  
【解析】弟弟用大黄篮子接收衣物。B、C、D 均无依据。故选 A。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】词义/推理判断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "our living room looked like a laundry shop"  
【解析】指客厅里堆满湿衣服。A、C、D 不符原意。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】主旨/情感题  
【定位】第⑥段末句 "a small family story that even a bigger storm, years later, could never wash away"  
【解析】作者强调暴雨带来了珍贵的家庭回忆。A、B、D 与情感相反。故选 C。
