# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Our School Have a "No-Phone Day" Once a Month?**

① Last term, the students' union of Bright Future Middle School put a new idea on the wall: "Let us try a **No-Phone Day** on the last Friday of every month — from 7 a.m. to 9 p.m., no one, not even teachers, uses a mobile phone inside the school gate." Since then, the idea has been talked about in every classroom, at lunch tables, and even on the parents' chat group.

② Those who **support** this idea have several reasons. First, a whole day **without a phone** pushes students to use their eyes and voices again. They will look up at trees, at classmates' faces, at the old clock on the wall, instead of always looking down at a small screen. Second, study research shows that teenagers in China spend on average **more than four hours a day** on phones. A phone-free day is only one day in thirty, but it gives our necks, eyes, and minds a short but real holiday. Third, the day can build **healthier habits**. Students may find that reading a paper book, playing table tennis, or just talking with a friend can also be fun — and they can take these simple pleasures home, not only keep them inside school walls.

③ However, some students and parents **worry** about the plan. On one side, phones are useful tools, not just toys. Many students use phone apps to **take class notes**, scan QR codes for after-class exercises, or call parents after school. A whole day without a phone might make it harder to get home safely or ask a busy parent for help. On the other side, some parents feel that "forbidden" usually leads to **stronger desire**. A student who loses the phone for a day may spend twice as much screen time over the weekend, which does not really help.

④ Is there a way in the middle? I believe there is. The school can keep the **No-Phone Day**, but allow one "family-call window" from 12:10 to 12:30, when students may use the office phone or a school tablet to contact their parents. Teachers can also prepare **rich offline activities** that day — Go chess, Rubik's cube, handwriting, dance, chorus, or a school yard run. Phones then lose their charm not because they are banned, but because something else is already more interesting.

⑤ A phone is a clever tool, but a clever student should be its master, not its servant. One quiet day each month will not damage our studies; it may, instead, help us rediscover the busy, colourful, wonderful real world right beside our desks.

---

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. What does the writer mainly want to discuss?**

A. Why Chinese students need mobile phones every day.  
B. Whether the school should hold a No-Phone Day every month.  
C. How to buy a cheaper smartphone for middle-school students.  
D. How mobile phones changed parent-child communication.

**2. Which is NOT a reason given by the supporters?**

A. Students will look up and notice the world around them.  
B. Teenagers spend too many hours on phones every day.  
C. The school can save money on electricity with no-phone days.  
D. Students may build healthier daily habits.

**3. According to paragraph ③, why are some parents worried?**

A. They are afraid children may lose their phones.  
B. They think forbidden things can make the desire even stronger.  
C. They believe phones make children study harder.  
D. They want their children to have better phones.

**4. What does the writer suggest as a middle way in paragraph ④?**

A. Cancel the No-Phone Day right away.  
B. Give every student a second phone for emergencies.  
C. Keep the No-Phone Day, with a short calling window and many offline activities.  
D. Ban phones for a whole week, not only a day.

**5. What does the last paragraph mainly mean?**

A. Phones are bad tools and should be thrown away.  
B. Students should become masters of phones, not servants.  
C. Schools should buy more phones for students.  
D. Parents must stay away from phones, too.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段引出"每月无手机日"话题，全文围绕支持/反对/折中进行讨论  
【解析】讨论是否应实行月度无手机日。A、C、D 均非主题。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】细节辨析题  
【定位】第②段支持方三条理由：抬头看世界、四小时屏幕使用、建立健康习惯  
【解析】"节省电费"文中未提。A、B、D 均为原文理由。故选 C。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "'forbidden' usually leads to stronger desire"  
【解析】家长担心禁止反而刺激欲望。A、C、D 无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】C  
【题型】推理判断题  
【定位】第④段 "keep the No-Phone Day, but allow one family-call window... prepare rich offline activities"  
【解析】保留无手机日，加短通话窗口和丰富线下活动。A、B、D 与原文相反或无依据。故选 C。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨推断题  
【定位】第⑤段 "a clever student should be its master, not its servant"  
【解析】学生应做手机的主人。A、C、D 均不符合作者立场。故选 B。

---

**词汇注释（超纲词）**  
- **forbidden** [adj.] 被禁止的  
- **servant** [n.] 仆人；这里指被手机驱使的人  
- **chorus** [n.] 合唱
