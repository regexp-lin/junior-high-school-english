# 阅读理解（议论文） **Should Students Keep a Daily Journal?**

① In many English classes, teachers ask students to keep a **daily journal**: a small notebook where the student writes two or three lines about the day. Some students love it; others see it as extra homework. So it is fair to ask: is it really useful for middle school students to write a journal every day?

② Those who say "no" often give practical reasons. Middle school students already face many tests and long nights of homework. "Another writing task," some students say, "is the last drop that makes the cup spill." They also worry about their private thoughts being read. After all, a good journal should tell the truth, but truth shared with the wrong eyes may bring trouble at home or in the classroom.

③ These worries make sense, yet they show a misunderstanding of what a daily journal really is. A journal does not need to be long. Two lines — "*The sky was grey. My mother made egg soup.*" — are enough. Such small sentences take less than five minutes, far less than scrolling through videos. They do not steal time from real study; they often save time, because a clear mind writes faster during exams.

④ More importantly, a journal is a quiet friend. During the busy teenage years, many feelings rise and fall like a tide — worry, joy, anger, loneliness. If these feelings never leave the heart, they can grow heavy and dark. But once the hand writes them down, the weight becomes much lighter. Research from a school in Hangzhou in 2025 found that students who wrote three lines each night before bed reported **less anxiety** and slept almost **twenty minutes** longer.

⑤ A journal also trains language. When a student tries to describe a red sunset or an angry classmate, he reaches for better words, new expressions, longer sentences. Over a year, this small daily effort can improve English almost without the student noticing. Teachers often see that students who keep a journal write clearer exam compositions, with less of the stiff "textbook tone" and more of a natural voice.

⑥ Of course, a journal must remain **private** to be useful. Teachers should not read it word by word, and parents should never open it without permission. Schools can simply count the pages, trust the student, and collect a short summary only if the student is willing. Under these fair rules, five minutes a night may be one of the smallest yet most valuable habits a teenager can build — a tiny daily gift to the future self.

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第①段末句  
【解析】全文围绕"学生是否应每日写日记"展开。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第②段 "another writing task... worry about their private thoughts being read"  
【解析】既觉多一项作业，又担心隐私。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Two lines... are enough"  
【解析】两三行即可。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "reported less anxiety and slept almost twenty minutes longer"  
【解析】研究显示写日记者焦虑更少、睡得更久。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】观点态度题  
【定位】第⑥段 "Under these fair rules... most valuable habits a teenager can build"  
【解析】作者明确支持，前提是公平规则。故选 B。
