# 阅读理解（记叙文） **The Clock That Stopped on a Winter Afternoon**

① My grandfather died last winter, on a Sunday afternoon when the city outside was covered in quiet white snow. A week later, my father drove our family back to the small town where grandfather used to live, to help grandmother clean his study. Grandfather had been a middle-school history teacher for almost forty years, and his study always smelled of paper, dust, and a little old wood. Nothing in that room ever seemed to change — nothing, except one thing on the wall.

② Hanging above his dark wooden desk was a large **wall clock** with a round face and a slow, heavy **pendulum**. It was not a fancy clock; its white face was a little yellow, and a small crack ran from the number 8 to the number 11. But for my whole childhood, every time I visited grandfather's study, that clock was ticking: **tick—tock—tick—tock**, slow and steady, like the heart of the old room.

③ On that cold afternoon after his funeral, I stepped into the study and put my hand on grandfather's empty chair. The room was very quiet — too quiet. It took me several seconds to understand why. The clock on the wall was not ticking any more. Its **pendulum** had stopped at a strange angle; its long black hand was frozen between three and four. Somehow, in just one week, the old clock had died too.

④ I told my grandmother. She came in slowly, wiped her hands on her apron, and looked up at the clock for a long time. Then she said softly, "That clock is older than you. Your grandfather wound it up every Sunday morning for more than thirty years, right after breakfast. When no one turns the key, the clock quietly stops." She turned to me. "If you like, why don't you take it home?"

⑤ I hesitated. "But I don't know how to wind it."  
"I'll show you," she said.

⑥ That afternoon, grandmother taught me how to open the small glass door, how to put the shiny brass key into the hole, and how to turn it slowly, one click at a time, until the spring inside felt tight. "Do not turn too fast," she whispered. "Listen to it. When it does not want to turn any more, stop. A clock is like a person — you must be patient with it."

⑦ I wrapped the clock carefully in an old wool blanket and took it home. That night, I hung it on the wall above my desk. I placed the pendulum again, pushed it gently, and listened. **Tick—tock—tick—tock.** It was a sound I had heard a thousand times in grandfather's study. Now, for the first time, it was ticking in my own room.

⑧ These days, every Sunday morning right after breakfast, before I open my Maths exercise book, I open the small glass door of the old clock, turn the brass key slowly, and feel the spring get tight under my fingers. It only takes two minutes. In those two minutes, I seem to see my grandfather at his old desk, quietly smiling at me through the soft light of a winter morning. And I understand something I did not understand before: **some people leave us, but the rhythm they have taught us can still be kept alive by our own hands**, one quiet Sunday at a time.

**根据文章内容，选择最佳答案。**

**1. Why did the writer's family go back to the small town after grandfather died?**

A. To sell grandfather's house to strangers.  
B. To help grandmother clean grandfather's study.  
C. To open a new bookshop in the town.  
D. To enter the local middle school.

**2. What was strange about the old clock when the writer entered the study after the funeral?**

A. It had turned completely gold in one week.  
B. The clock had stopped; its pendulum had stopped at a strange angle and the long hand was frozen between three and four.  
C. It was now playing loud music.  
D. It had completely disappeared from the wall.

**3. According to grandmother, why did the clock stop?**

A. Because the town had a power cut that week.  
B. Because grandfather had wound it up every Sunday morning for over thirty years, and with no one to turn the key, the clock naturally stopped.  
C. Because the writer had dropped it when he was small.  
D. Because a strong wind broke the glass door.

**4. What did grandmother teach the writer to do?**

A. To write a new history book.  
B. To open the small glass door, put the brass key into the hole and turn it slowly, one click at a time.  
C. To repaint the clock's white face.  
D. To replace the clock with a modern electric one.

**5. What does the writer most likely want readers to understand from this story?**

A. Old clocks always work better than new clocks.  
B. Some people we love leave us, but the rhythms and habits they have taught us can still be kept alive by our own hands.  
C. Middle-school students should stop studying Maths on Sunday mornings.  
D. Grandparents should never have any personal belongings.

---

## 答案与解析

**1.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第①段 "to help grandmother clean his study"  
【解析】原文明确说明是回去帮奶奶清理爷爷书房。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**2.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第③段 "Its pendulum had stopped at a strange angle; its long black hand was frozen between three and four"  
【解析】原文精确描述了钟停的状况。A、C、D 均无文中依据。故选 B。

**3.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第④段 "Your grandfather wound it up every Sunday morning for more than thirty years ... When no one turns the key, the clock quietly stops"  
【解析】奶奶的原话直接回答了原因。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**4.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】细节理解题  
【定位】第⑥段 "how to open the small glass door ... turn it slowly, one click at a time"  
【解析】原文直接描述奶奶所教的动作步骤。A、C、D 均无依据。故选 B。

**5.**  
【答案】B  
【题型】主旨大意题  
【定位】第⑧段末 "some people leave us, but the rhythm they have taught us can still be kept alive by our own hands"  
【解析】作者结尾升华主题：亲人离去但他们留下的节奏可以被我们延续。A、C、D 均与作者立场不符。故选 B。
