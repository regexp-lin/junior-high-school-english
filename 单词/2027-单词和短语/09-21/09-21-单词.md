## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **plausible** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：貌似合理的；花言巧语的；可信的（表面）
- **英文释义**：seeming reasonable or probable; appearing worthy of belief
- **例句**：
  - She offered a plausible explanation for being late, though no one could verify it.
  - 她为迟到给出了一个貌似合理的解释，尽管无人能核实。
  - The detective found several plausible suspects but lacked solid evidence.
  - 侦探找到了几个貌似可疑的人，但缺乏确凿证据。
- **助记**：plaus（鼓掌）+ ible → 听起来值得鼓掌 → 貌似可信

### 2. **revere** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：崇敬；敬畏；尊崇
- **英文释义**：to feel deep respect or admiration for someone or something
- **例句**：
  - Many students revere their teachers who have guided them for years.
  - 许多学生崇敬多年来指导他们的老师。
  - The community continues to revere the hero who saved lives during the flood.
  - 社区仍然敬仰在洪水中救人的那位英雄。
- **助记**：re（一再）+ vere（敬畏，同 awe）→ 一再敬畏 → 崇敬

### 3. **tacit** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：心照不宣的；默示的；未明说的
- **英文释义**：understood or implied without being stated openly
- **例句**：
  - They reached a tacit agreement not to discuss politics at family dinners.
  - 他们达成了心照不宣的约定：家庭聚餐时不谈政治。
  - His silence was taken as tacit approval of the plan.
  - 他的沉默被视为对计划的默许。
- **助记**：tac（安静，如 taciturn）+ it → 安静不说出来 → 默示的

### 4. **vehement** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：激烈的；猛烈的；（情感）强烈的
- **英文释义**：showing strong feeling; forceful, intense, or emotional
- **例句**：
  - She expressed vehement opposition to the proposal at the meeting.
  - 她在会上对提案表示强烈反对。
  - The debate became vehement as both sides refused to compromise.
  - 由于双方都不肯妥协，辩论变得异常激烈。
- **助记**：veh（携带力量）+ ement → 带着强烈力量 → 激烈的

### 5. **wane** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：减弱；衰落；（月）亏
- **英文释义**：to decrease gradually in strength, size, or intensity
- **例句**：
  - Public interest in the scandal began to wane after a few weeks.
  - 几周后，公众对这桩丑闻的兴趣开始减弱。
  - The moon wanes from full to new over about two weeks.
  - 月亮大约在两周内从满月亏到新月。
- **助记**：联想 wax and wane（月盈月亏）→ wane 是“亏、衰”

### 6. **bolster** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：支持；增强；用垫枕支撑
- **英文释义**：to support or strengthen; to prop up
- **例句**：
  - New evidence helped bolster the lawyer’s argument in court.
  - 新证据有助于加强律师在法庭上的论点。
  - The government promised measures to bolster the economy.
  - 政府承诺采取措施提振经济。
- **助记**：bolster 原义长枕 → 垫在下面托起 → 支持、增强

### 7. **concede** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：承认；让步；让给
- **英文释义**：to admit that something is true or valid; to yield
- **例句**：
  - He had to concede that his opponent’s idea was more practical.
  - 他不得不承认对手的想法更切实可行。
  - After losing the vote, she conceded defeat gracefully.
  - 投票失利后，她大方地承认失败。
- **助记**：con（完全）+ cede（走、让）→ 完全让出 → 让步/承认

### 8. **dubious** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：可疑的；不确定的；犹豫的
- **英文释义**：hesitating or doubting; not to be relied upon; suspicious
- **例句**：
  - The police found his alibi dubious and decided to investigate further.
  - 警方认为他的不在场证明可疑，决定进一步调查。
  - I’m dubious about whether the plan will work in practice.
  - 我怀疑这计划在实践中是否可行。
- **助记**：dub（二、双）+ ious → 在两者间犹豫 → 可疑的

### 9. **elicit** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：引出；套出；诱出（反应、信息等）
- **英文释义**：to draw forth or bring out a response, answer, or fact
- **例句**：
  - The teacher asked open questions to elicit students’ opinions.
  - 老师用开放式问题引导学生说出观点。
  - The interview was designed to elicit honest feedback from employees.
  - 这次访谈旨在引出员工的坦诚反馈。
- **助记**：e（出）+ licit（引诱）→ 诱出 → 引出

### 10. **forgo** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：放弃；弃绝；不享用
- **英文释义**：to give up or do without something desirable
- **例句**：
  - She decided to forgo dessert to stay healthy.
  - 为了保持健康，她决定不吃甜点。
  - They had to forgo their vacation plans because of the emergency.
  - 由于突发情况，他们不得不放弃度假计划。
- **助记**：for（离开）+ go → 让它走开 → 放弃

### 高一词汇

### 11. **exacerbate** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：使恶化；使加剧；激怒
- **英文释义**：to make a problem, bad situation, or negative feeling worse
- **例句**：
  - Loud arguments only exacerbated the tension between the two families.
  - 大声争吵只会加剧两个家庭之间的紧张关系。
  - Cutting funds for healthcare could exacerbate inequality in society.
  - 削减医疗经费可能加剧社会不平等。
- **助记**：ex（向外）+ acerb（尖酸）+ ate → 使更尖锐 → 加剧

### 12. **juxtaposition** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：并置；并列对照
- **英文释义**：the fact of placing two things close together for contrast or comparison
- **例句**：
  - The film’s power lies in the juxtaposition of humor and tragedy.
  - 这部影片的力量在于幽默与悲剧的并置。
  - The artist used juxtaposition of light and dark colors to create drama.
  - 艺术家用明暗色彩的并置来营造戏剧性。
- **助记**：juxta（靠近）+ position → 放在相邻位置 → 并置

### 13. **obfuscate** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：使模糊；使困惑；混淆视听
- **英文释义**：to make something obscure, unclear, or unintelligible
- **例句**：
  - The spokesperson seemed to obfuscate rather than answer directly.
  - 发言人似乎在含糊其辞，而非直接回答。
  - Technical jargon can obfuscate simple ideas for ordinary readers.
  - 专业术语可能让普通读者把简单概念也弄糊涂。
- **助记**：ob（对着）+ fusc（黑暗）+ ate → 使之变暗 → 混淆

### 14. **pragmatic** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：务实的；实用主义的
- **英文释义**：dealing with things sensibly and realistically in a practical way
- **例句**：
  - We need a pragmatic solution that works within our budget.
  - 我们需要一个在预算内可行的务实方案。
  - She is known for her pragmatic approach to classroom management.
  - 她以管理课堂的务实方式著称。
- **助记**：pragma（行为、事务）+ tic → 重实际行为的 → 务实的

### 15. **veracity** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：真实性；诚实；准确
- **英文释义**：conformity to facts; habitual truthfulness
- **例句**：
  - Journalists must check the veracity of every claim before publishing.
  - 记者在发表前必须核实每条说法的真实性。
  - His veracity was never questioned during the trial.
  - 在审判中，他的诚实从未受到质疑。
- **助记**：ver（真实，如 verify）+ acity → 真实这一性质 → 真实性
