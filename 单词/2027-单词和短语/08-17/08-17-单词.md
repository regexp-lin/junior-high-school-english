## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **platitude** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：陈词滥调；老生常谈；平庸乏味的言论
- **英文释义**：a remark or statement, especially one with an moral content, that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful
- **例句**：
  - The speech was full of platitudes about teamwork and perseverance.
  - 演讲里净是关于团队合作和坚持的陈词滥调。
  - She avoided platitudes and instead offered concrete advice.
  - 她避免空话，而是给出了具体建议。
- **助记**：plat（平）+ itude → 平淡无奇的话 → 陈词滥调

### 2. **polemic** `初三` n./adj.

- **中文释义**：激烈争论；攻击性的文章；好辩的
- **英文释义**：a strong verbal or written attack on someone or something; disputatious
- **例句**：
  - The book is a fierce polemic against corporate greed.
  - 这本书是对企业贪婪的猛烈抨击。
  - His polemic style made him popular on talk radio but unpopular in academia.
  - 他好辩的风格在脱口秀上受欢迎，在学术界却不太讨喜。
- **助记**：polem（战争）+ ic → 文字上的战争 → 论战

### 3. **portentous** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：预兆的；不祥的；自命不凡的（贬）
- **英文释义**：of momentous or ominous significance; done in a pompously solemn manner
- **例句**：
  - Dark clouds gathered in a portentous sky before the storm.
  - 暴风雨前，乌云在阴沉不祥的天空中聚集。
  - He made a portentous speech about the future of civilization.
  - 他就人类文明的未来发表了一番煞有介事的演讲。
- **助记**：portent（预兆）+ ous → 充满预兆的 → 不祥的/自大的

### 4. **precipitate** `初三` v./adj.

- **中文释义**：促成；使突然发生；仓促的；沉淀
- **英文释义**：to cause an event to happen suddenly or sooner than expected; hasty
- **例句**：
  - The scandal may precipitate the minister's resignation.
  - 丑闻可能促使部长辞职。
  - A precipitate decision without data cost the firm millions.
  - 没有数据就仓促做决定，让公司损失了数百万。
- **助记**：pre（前）+ cip（头）+ itate → 头朝前冲 → 促成/仓促

### 5. **prevaricate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：支吾；搪塞；说谎敷衍
- **英文释义**：to speak or act in an evasive way; to quibble
- **例句**：
  - When questioned by police, the suspect continued to prevaricate.
  - 被警方讯问时，嫌疑人继续支吾其词。
  - Stop prevaricating and tell us whether you signed the contract.
  - 别再搪塞了，告诉我们你有没有签合同。
- **助记**：pre（在前）+ vari（变化）+ cate → 把话变来变去 → 搪塞

### 6. **probity** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：正直；诚实；廉正
- **英文释义**：the quality of having strong moral principles; honesty and decency
- **例句**：
  - Judges are expected to demonstrate absolute probity in financial matters.
  - 法官在财务问题上应表现出绝对廉洁。
  - His reputation for probity made him the ideal choice for auditor.
  - 他以正直闻名，是审计员的理想人选。
- **助记**：prob（证明/正直）+ ity → 可经考验的品格 → 正直

### 7. **profligate** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：挥霍的；放荡的；极度浪费的
- **英文释义**：recklessly extravagant or wasteful in the use of resources; dissolute
- **例句**：
  - The profligate spending of public funds triggered a government inquiry.
  - 公款的挥霍引发了政府调查。
  - He led a profligate lifestyle until he went bankrupt.
  - 他过着放荡的生活，直到破产。
- **助记**：pro（向前）+ flig（打）→ 把钱打出去 → 挥霍

### 8. **propitiate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：抚慰；劝解；使息怒
- **英文释义**：to win or regain the favor of a god, spirit, or person by doing something that pleases them
- **例句**：
  - Ancient tribes offered sacrifices to propitiate angry gods.
  - 古代部落献祭以平息神的怒气。
  - He brought flowers, hoping to propitiate his partner after the argument.
  - 吵架后他带了花，希望哄伴侣消气。
- **助记**：pro（向前）+ pit（同情）+ iate → 求得同情 → 抚慰

### 9. **prosaic** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：平淡无奇的；散文体的；乏味的
- **英文释义**：lacking poetic beauty; commonplace; dull
- **例句**：
  - After years of adventure, he found prosaic office work strangely comforting.
  - 多年冒险之后，他觉得平淡的办公室工作反而令人安心。
  - The novel's prosaic language disappointed readers who expected lush descriptions.
  - 小说平淡的文笔让期待华丽描写的读者失望。
- **助记**：prosa（散文）+ ic → 像散文一样平实 → 乏味的

### 10. **prurient** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：好色的；淫秽的；有窥淫欲的
- **英文释义**：having or encouraging an excessive interest in sexual matters
- **例句**：
  - The tabloid was accused of appealing to prurient curiosity about celebrities' private lives.
  - 小报被指责迎合公众对名人私生活的好色好奇。
  - Prurient content was removed from the website to comply with regulations.
  - 为符合规定，网站删除了色情内容。
- **助记**：prur（痒）+ ient → 心痒难耐 → 好色

### 高一词汇

### 11. **raconteur** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：善于讲故事的人；健谈的说书者
- **英文释义**：a person who tells anecdotes in a skillful and amusing way
- **例句**：
  - My uncle is a gifted raconteur who keeps dinner guests laughing for hours.
  - 我叔叔特别会讲故事，能让晚宴客人笑上几个小时。
  - The author appeared on talk shows as a witty raconteur promoting her memoir.
  - 作者以机智的讲故事者身份上脱口秀推销回忆录。
- **助记**：法语 raconter（讲述）→ 讲故事的人

### 12. **recant** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：公开撤回（声明、信仰）；宣布放弃
- **英文释义**：to withdraw and disavow a previously held belief or statement formally
- **例句**：
  - Under pressure, the witness recanted her earlier testimony.
  - 在压力下，证人撤回了先前的证词。
  - Galileo was forced to recant his scientific views before the Inquisition.
  - 伽利略被迫在宗教法庭前宣布放弃自己的科学观点。
- **助记**：re（回）+ cant（唱/说）→ 把说过的话唱回去 → 撤回

### 13. **redolent** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：芬芳的；使人联想起……的；充满……气息的
- **英文释义**：strongly suggestive or evocative of something; fragrant
- **例句**：
  - The kitchen was redolent of garlic and fresh herbs.
  - 厨房里弥漫着大蒜和新鲜香草的香味。
  - The old photographs were redolent of a simpler time.
  - 这些老照片让人想起更单纯的年代。
- **助记**：re（加强）+ ol（闻）+ ent → 可闻到的 → 芬芳/联想

### 14. **refractory** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：倔强的；难治疗的；耐火的（材料）
- **英文释义**：stubborn or unmanageable; resistant to treatment or heat
- **例句**：
  - The refractory teenager refused every suggestion from his parents.
  - 倔强的少年拒绝父母的一切建议。
  - Refractory bricks line the inside of the steel furnace.
  - 炼钢炉内壁砌有耐火砖。
- **助记**：re（回）+ fract（打破）+ ory → 打不破的 → 顽固/耐火

### 15. **remonstrate** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：抗议；规劝；告诫
- **英文释义**：to make a forcefully reproachful protest
- **例句**：
  - Teachers remonstrated with students who cheated on the exam.
  - 老师对考试作弊的学生进行了告诫。
  - The ambassador remonstrated against the new trade restrictions.
  - 大使对新的贸易限制作出抗议。
- **助记**：re（加强）+ monstr（显示）+ ate → 摆出理由反对 → 抗议
