## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **meticulous** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：一丝不苟的；极为仔细的
- **英文释义**：showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
- **例句**：
  - The scientist kept meticulous records of every experiment.
  - 科学家对每次实验都做了极为详细的记录。
  - Meticulous proofreading catches errors that spell-check misses.
  - 仔细校对能发现拼写检查漏掉的错误。
- **助记**：meta（害怕）+ culos（惩罚）→ 怕出错受罚 → 一丝不苟

### 2. **negligent** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：疏忽的；失职的；粗心大意的
- **英文释义**：failing to take proper care in doing something; careless
- **例句**：
  - The landlord was found negligent after the fire inspection failed.
  - 消防检查不合格后，房东被判失职。
  - Negligent driving causes thousands of accidents each year.
  - 疏忽驾驶每年造成数千起事故。
- **助记**：neg（不）+ lig（选择）+ ent → 不尽责 → 疏忽

### 3. **onerous** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：繁重的；艰巨的；令人负担沉重的
- **英文释义**：involving great effort and difficulty; burdensome
- **例句**：
  - The new regulations placed onerous reporting duties on small shops.
  - 新规定给小商店增加了繁重的申报义务。
  - He found the commute onerous and moved closer to work.
  - 他觉得通勤太累，搬到了离工作更近的地方。
- **助记**：oner（负担）+ ous → 沉重的

### 4. **palpable** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：可感知的；明显的；摸得着的
- **英文释义**：able to be touched or felt; so intense as to be almost tangible
- **例句**：
  - Tension in the room was palpable before the announcement.
  - 宣布前房间里的紧张气氛显而易见。
  - There was palpable relief when the rescue team arrived.
  - 救援队到达时，人们明显松了一口气。
- **助记**：palp（摸）+ able → 可摸到的 → 明显的

### 5. **quibble** `初三` v./n.

- **中文释义**：吹毛求疵；为琐事争辩；遁词
- **英文释义**：to argue or raise objections about petty matters; a minor complaint
- **例句**：
  - Let's not quibble over a few dollars and close the deal.
  - 别为几块钱斤斤计较，成交吧。
  - His speech was full of quibbles rather than clear answers.
  - 他的发言尽是遁词，没有明确回答。
- **助记**：quib（谁）→ 纠缠谁对谁错 → 诡辩

### 6. **ravenous** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：极饿的；贪婪的；狼吞虎咽的
- **英文释义**：extremely hungry; voracious
- **例句**：
  - After the hike, the group was ravenous and ate every dish.
  - 徒步后一行人饿极了，把每道菜都吃光。
  - The stray dog gave a ravenous look at the meat in the shop window.
  - 流浪狗贪婪地盯着橱窗里的肉。
- **助记**：raven（掠夺）+ ous → 如掠夺般饥饿 → 极饿

### 7. **salient** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：显著的；突出的；最重要的
- **英文释义**：most noticeable or important; prominent; jutting out
- **例句**：
  - The salient points of the report fit on one page.
  - 报告的要点一页纸就能写下。
  - Her salient feature in the crowd was bright red hair.
  - 她在人群中最显眼的是一头红发。
- **助记**：sali（跳）+ ent → 跳出来 → 显著

### 8. **tacit** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：心照不宣的；默示的；不言而喻的
- **英文释义**：understood or implied without being stated openly
- **例句**：
  - They had a tacit agreement not to discuss politics at dinner.
  - 他们心照不宣地约定晚餐不谈政治。
  - Silence can be tacit approval in some cultures.
  - 在某些文化中沉默可以表示默许。
- **助记**：tac（安静）+ it → 安静不说 → 默示的

### 9. **untenable** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：站不住脚的；难以防守的；不能维持的
- **英文释义**：indefensible; not able to be maintained or defended
- **例句**：
  - Once new evidence appeared, his theory became untenable.
  - 新证据出现后，他的理论便站不住脚了。
  - The old fort was untenable against modern artillery.
  - 这座旧堡垒在现代炮火下难以防守。
- **助记**：un（不）+ ten（持有）+ able → 守不住 → 站不住脚

### 10. **vapid** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：乏味的；无生气的；平淡无奇的
- **英文释义**：offering nothing stimulating or challenging; dull and flat
- **例句**：
  - The lecture was so vapid that half the audience fell asleep.
  - 讲座乏味至极，一半听众睡着了。
  - She avoided vapid small talk at parties by asking deeper questions.
  - 她在聚会上用更深层的问题避免空洞寒暄。
- **助记**：vap（蒸汽）+ id → 像蒸汽一样空 → 乏味

### 高一词汇

### 11. **aberrant** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：异常的；偏离常规的；畸变的
- **英文释义**：departing from an accepted standard; abnormal
- **例句**：
  - Aberrant behavior in animals can signal illness or stress.
  - 动物的异常行为可能预示疾病或压力。
  - The scientist studied aberrant cells under the microscope.
  - 科学家在显微镜下研究异常细胞。
- **助记**：ab（离开）+ err（走）+ ant → 走偏 → 异常

### 12. **boon** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：恩惠；裨益；及时的帮助
- **英文释义**：a helpful or beneficial thing; a blessing
- **例句**：
  - The mild winter was a boon to farmers worried about frost.
  - 暖冬对担心霜冻的农民来说是福音。
  - Free public Wi-Fi has been a boon to students without home internet.
  - 免费公共 Wi-Fi 对没有家庭网络的学生大有裨益。
- **助记**：boon 联想 bonus（奖金）→ 好处 → 恩惠

### 13. **contentious** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：有争议的；爱争论的；可能引起争端的
- **英文释义**：causing or likely to cause disagreement; quarrelsome
- **例句**：
  - Immigration remains a contentious issue in many countries.
  - 移民在许多国家仍是有争议的话题。
  - He avoided contentious topics at the family reunion.
  - 家庭聚会上他避开易起争执的话题。
- **助记**：contend（争论）+ tious → 好争论的

### 14. **deference** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：尊重；顺从；敬意
- **英文释义**：humble submission and respect; courteous regard for judgment
- **例句**：
  - Junior staff spoke with deference to the experienced director.
  - 初级职员对资深主任说话十分恭敬。
  - The court showed deference to earlier rulings on similar cases.
  - 法院对类似案件的早期判决表示尊重。
- **助记**：defer（推迟/听从）+ ence → 听从的态度 → 尊重

### 15. **winsome** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：迷人的；楚楚动人的；讨人喜欢的
- **英文释义**：attractive or appealing in a fresh, innocent way; charming
- **例句**：
  - The child gave a winsome smile that melted the stranger's heart.
  - 孩子迷人一笑，融化了陌生人的心。
  - Her winsome manners made her popular at school events.
  - 她可爱的举止在校活动中很受欢迎。
- **助记**：win（赢得）+ some → 赢得好感的 → 迷人
