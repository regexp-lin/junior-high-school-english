## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **feasible** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：可行的；行得通的；做得到的
- **英文释义**：possible to do easily or conveniently; likely to succeed
- **例句**：
  - The committee concluded that the plan was financially feasible.
  - 委员会认为该计划在财务上可行。
  - It is not feasible to finish the report in one day without extra help.
  - 没有额外帮助，一天内完成报告是不现实的。
- **助记**：feas（做，如 feat）+ ible → 能做出来的 → 可行的

### 2. **garish** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：俗艳的；过于花哨的；刺眼的
- **英文释义**：obtrusively bright and showy; lurid
- **例句**：
  - She disliked the garish neon signs along the tourist street.
  - 她不喜欢旅游街上那些俗艳的霓虹灯招牌。
  - The room was painted in garish colors that hurt the eyes.
  - 房间漆成刺眼的颜色，很伤眼睛。
- **助记**：联想 glare（刺眼的强光）→ garish 刺眼、过于亮

### 3. **harbinger** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：预兆；先驱；前兆
- **英文释义**：a person or thing that announces or signals the approach of another
- **例句**：
  - The first robin is often seen as a harbinger of spring.
  - 人们常把第一只知更鸟视为春天的预兆。
  - Rising prices can be a harbinger of inflation.
  - 物价上涨可能是通货膨胀的前兆。
- **助记**：harbor（庇护）+ ger（带来）→ 带来消息者 → 预兆

### 4. **impeccable** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：无可挑剔的；无瑕疵的；无错误的
- **英文释义**：in accordance with the highest standards; faultless
- **例句**：
  - His English pronunciation is impeccable after years of practice.
  - 多年练习后，他的英语发音无可挑剔。
  - The hotel is known for its impeccable service.
  - 这家酒店以无可挑剔的服务著称。
- **助记**：im（不）+ pecc（过错）+ able → 无过错的 → 完美无瑕

### 5. **mitigate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：减轻；缓和；使温和
- **英文释义**：to make something less severe, serious, or painful
- **例句**：
  - Trees can help mitigate the effects of urban heat islands.
  - 树木有助于减轻城市热岛效应。
  - The government took steps to mitigate the damage from the flood.
  - 政府采取措施减轻洪灾造成的损失。
- **助记**：miti（温和）+ gate → 使变温和 → 缓和

### 6. **nominal** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：名义上的；象征性的；微不足道的（费用）
- **英文释义**：existing in name only; very small in comparison with expectations
- **例句**：
  - He remained the nominal head of the company after retirement.
  - 退休后他仍是公司的名义负责人。
  - They charged a nominal fee for the community workshop.
  - 他们对社区工作坊只收取象征性费用。
- **助记**：nomin（名字）+ al → 只在名字上 → 名义上的

### 7. **ostensible** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：表面上的；貌似真实的；宣称的
- **英文释义**：stated or appearing to be true, but not necessarily so
- **例句**：
  - The ostensible reason for the visit was to see relatives.
  - 访问的表面理由是探亲。
  - Her ostensible confidence hid deep anxiety.
  - 她表面上的自信掩盖了深深的焦虑。
- **助记**：ostens（展示）+ ible → 展示给人看的 → 表面的

### 8. **palliate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：减轻（痛苦）；掩饰（罪行）；辩解
- **英文释义**：to make a disease or its symptoms less severe without addressing the cause
- **例句**：
  - Medicine can only palliate the symptoms of this chronic illness.
  - 药物只能减轻这种慢性病的症状。
  - He tried to palliate his rude behavior with a weak apology.
  - 他用敷衍的道歉来掩饰粗鲁行为。
- **助记**：palli（斗篷）+ ate → 像斗篷一样盖住 → 减轻、掩饰

### 9. **quell** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：镇压；平息；消除（恐惧）
- **英文释义**：to put an end to, typically by the use of force; to suppress
- **例句**：
  - Police were called in to quell the riot in the city center.
  - 警方被召来平息市中心的骚乱。
  - She took deep breaths to quell her nervousness before the speech.
  - 演讲前她深呼吸以平复紧张。
- **助记**：quell 音近 kill 压制 → 强力平息

### 10. **replete** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：充满的；饱足的；供应充足的
- **英文释义**：filled or well-supplied with something
- **例句**：
  - The essay is replete with examples from history.
  - 这篇文章充满了历史例证。
  - After the feast, everyone felt replete and sleepy.
  - 宴会后大家都吃得饱足发困。
- **助记**：re（再）+ plet（填满）→ 再次填满 → 充满的

### 高一词汇

### 11. **quotidian** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：日常的；平凡的；每日发生的
- **英文释义**：of or occurring every day; mundane or everyday
- **例句**：
  - She found beauty even in quotidian tasks like washing dishes.
  - 她甚至在洗碗这样的日常琐事中也发现美。
  - The novel describes the quotidian life of a small town.
  - 这部小说描写了一座小镇的日常生活。
- **助记**：quot（每天）+ idian → 每日的 → 平凡的

### 12. **recalcitrant** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：桀骜不驯的；顽抗的；难管束的
- **英文释义**：having an obstinately uncooperative attitude toward authority
- **例句**：
  - The recalcitrant student refused to follow any classroom rules.
  - 这名桀骜不驯的学生拒绝遵守任何课堂规则。
  - Recalcitrant employees made the reform difficult to implement.
  - 顽抗的员工使改革难以推行。
- **助记**：re（反）+ calc（脚跟）→ 踢回去 → 不服管束

### 13. **sanguine** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：乐观的；满怀希望的；面色红润的
- **英文释义**：optimistic or positive, especially in a difficult situation
- **例句**：
  - Despite the setbacks, she remained sanguine about the project’s future.
  - 尽管遭遇挫折，她仍对项目前景保持乐观。
  - He gave a sanguine assessment of the team’s chances.
  - 他对球队的胜算给出了乐观的评估。
- **助记**：sanguin（血）→ 血气旺盛 → 乐观、红润

### 14. **truncate** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：截短；删去（一部分）
- **英文释义**：to shorten something by cutting off the top or end
- **例句**：
  - The article was truncated to fit the magazine page.
  - 文章被截短以适合杂志版面。
  - Budget cuts forced them to truncate the training program.
  - 预算削减迫使他们缩短培训项目。
- **助记**：trunc（树干）+ ate → 砍去一截 → 截短

### 15. **vacillate** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：犹豫；摇摆不定；踌躇
- **英文释义**：to waver between different opinions or actions; be indecisive
- **例句**：
  - He vacillated between two career paths for months.
  - 他在两条职业道路之间犹豫了好几个月。
  - Leaders should not vacillate when making urgent decisions.
  - 做紧急决定时领导者不应摇摆不定。
- **助记**：vacill（摇摆）→ 像钟摆 → 犹豫
