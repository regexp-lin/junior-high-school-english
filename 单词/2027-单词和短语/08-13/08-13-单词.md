## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **aspire** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：渴望；追求；立志
- **英文释义**：to direct one's hopes or ambitions towards achieving something
- **例句**：
  - Many young athletes aspire to compete in the Olympic Games one day.
  - 许多年轻运动员渴望有朝一日参加奥运会。
  - She aspires to become a world-renowned surgeon when she finishes medical school.
  - 她立志在医学院毕业后成为一名世界著名的外科医生。
- **助记**：a（朝向）+ spire（呼吸/精神）→ 朝着精神目标前进 → 渴望/立志

### 2. **compel** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：强迫；迫使；使不得不
- **英文释义**：to force or oblige someone to do something
- **例句**：
  - The evidence compelled the jury to reach a guilty verdict.
  - 证据迫使陪审团做出有罪判决。
  - Bad weather compelled the hikers to turn back before reaching the summit.
  - 恶劣天气迫使登山者在到达山顶前折返。
- **助记**：com（一起）+ pel（推/驱动）→ 一起推动 → 强迫/迫使

### 3. **deviate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：偏离；偏差；背离
- **英文释义**：to depart from an established course or from usual standards
- **例句**：
  - The pilot had to deviate from the planned flight path to avoid a severe thunderstorm.
  - 飞行员不得不偏离计划的飞行路线以避开严重的雷暴。
  - His behavior deviated significantly from the social norms expected in the community.
  - 他的行为严重偏离了社区所期望的社会规范。
- **助记**：de（偏离）+ via（道路）+ te → 偏离道路 → 偏离/背离

### 4. **exacerbate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：使恶化；加重；使加剧
- **英文释义**：to make a problem, bad situation, or negative feeling worse
- **例句**：
  - The drought was exacerbated by record-high temperatures this summer.
  - 今年夏天创纪录的高温加剧了干旱。
  - Arguing with him will only exacerbate the situation, not resolve it.
  - 和他争吵只会使情况恶化，而不会解决问题。
- **助记**：ex（加强）+ acerb（苦/尖锐）+ ate → 使更加苦涩 → 使恶化

### 5. **forge** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：锻造；建立；伪造
- **英文释义**：to make or shape metal by heating and hammering; to create by means of much effort; to produce a fraudulent copy
- **例句**：
  - The two countries forged a strong alliance to combat climate change.
  - 两国建立了强大的联盟来应对气候变化。
  - The criminal forged the millionaire's signature on the check.
  - 罪犯在支票上伪造了百万富翁的签名。
- **助记**：forge 本义是铁匠铺 → 在铁匠铺中锻造 → 引申为建立/伪造

### 6. **impair** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：损害；削弱；减少
- **英文释义**：to weaken or damage, especially gradually
- **例句**：
  - Prolonged exposure to loud noise can impair your hearing permanently.
  - 长时间暴露在巨大噪音中会永久性地损害你的听力。
  - Lack of sleep can impair your ability to concentrate and make good decisions.
  - 睡眠不足会削弱你集中注意力和做出正确决定的能力。
- **助记**：im（使）+ pair（更差/损坏）→ 使变差 → 损害/削弱

### 7. **linger** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：逗留；徘徊；迟迟不消退
- **英文释义**：to stay in a place longer than necessary; to be slow to disappear
- **例句**：
  - The tourists lingered at the scenic viewpoint, reluctant to leave the breathtaking view.
  - 游客们在景观台逗留，不愿离开那令人叹为观止的景色。
  - The smell of smoke lingered in the room for days after the fire.
  - 火灾后，烟味在房间里持续了好几天。
- **助记**：linger 联想 longer（更长）→ 待得更长时间 → 逗留/徘徊

### 8. **notable** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：值得注意的；显著的；著名的
- **英文释义**：worthy of attention or notice; remarkable
- **例句**：
  - There has been a notable improvement in air quality since the new regulations were introduced.
  - 自从引入新法规以来，空气质量有了显著改善。
  - Several notable scientists attended the international conference on climate change.
  - 几位著名科学家参加了关于气候变化的国际会议。
- **助记**：not（标记）+ able → 值得被标记的 → 值得注意的/显著的

### 9. **petition** `初三` n./v.

- **中文释义**：请愿书；请求；向……请愿
- **英文释义**：a formal written request signed by many people; to make a formal request
- **例句**：
  - The residents signed a petition to stop the construction of the factory near their homes.
  - 居民们签署了请愿书，反对在他们住所附近建造工厂。
  - The group petitioned the government to provide more funding for public schools.
  - 该团体向政府请愿，要求为公立学校提供更多资金。
- **助记**：pet（追求/寻求）+ ition → 寻求帮助 → 请愿

### 10. **subjective** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：主观的；个人的；偏颇的
- **英文释义**：based on or influenced by personal feelings, tastes, or opinions
- **例句**：
  - Beauty is subjective — what one person finds attractive, another may not.
  - 美是主观的——一个人认为有吸引力的，另一个人可能不这样认为。
  - The critic's review was highly subjective and did not consider the film's technical merits.
  - 评论家的评价高度主观，没有考虑到电影的技术优点。
- **助记**：sub（在下面）+ ject（投射）+ ive → 投射自己内心的看法 → 主观的

### 高一词汇

### 11. **clandestine** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：秘密的；暗中的；偷偷摸摸的
- **英文释义**：kept secret or done secretively, especially because illicit
- **例句**：
  - The spies held a clandestine meeting in a remote cabin in the mountains.
  - 间谍们在山上一个偏僻的小屋里秘密会面。
  - The journalist uncovered a clandestine operation to smuggle weapons across the border.
  - 记者揭露了一项秘密走私武器越境的行动。
- **助记**：clan（关门）+ destine → 关上门偷偷做的 → 秘密的

### 12. **equivocal** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：模棱两可的；含糊的；意义不明确的
- **英文释义**：open to more than one interpretation; ambiguous
- **例句**：
  - The politician gave an equivocal answer that satisfied neither side of the debate.
  - 这位政治家给出了一个模棱两可的回答，辩论双方都不满意。
  - The results of the study were equivocal, making it difficult to draw clear conclusions.
  - 研究结果模棱两可，难以得出明确的结论。
- **助记**：equi（相等）+ vocal（声音）→ 两种声音一样大 → 模棱两可的

### 13. **imperious** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：专横的；傲慢的；迫切的
- **英文释义**：assuming power or authority without justification; domineering
- **例句**：
  - Her imperious manner of speaking alienated many of her colleagues.
  - 她专横的说话方式疏远了许多同事。
  - The manager issued imperious demands without considering the team's workload.
  - 经理发出了专横的要求，没有考虑到团队的工作量。
- **助记**：imper（命令/统治，如 emperor）+ ious → 像皇帝一样的 → 专横的

### 14. **parsimonious** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：吝啬的；过分节俭的；小气的
- **英文释义**：unwilling to spend money or use resources; stingy
- **例句**：
  - The parsimonious landlord refused to spend money on necessary repairs to the building.
  - 吝啬的房东拒绝花钱对建筑进行必要的维修。
  - Despite being wealthy, he was known for his parsimonious habits.
  - 尽管富有，他以吝啬的习惯闻名。
- **助记**：parsimoni（节省）+ ous → 过度节省的 → 吝啬的

### 15. **voracious** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：贪婪的；如饥似渴的；贪吃的
- **英文释义**：wanting or devouring great quantities of food or knowledge
- **例句**：
  - She is a voracious reader who finishes at least three books a week.
  - 她是一个如饥似渴的读者，每周至少读完三本书。
  - The voracious appetite of teenagers is well known to every parent.
  - 每个父母都知道青少年贪吃的胃口。
- **助记**：vor（吞噬）+ acious → 不断吞噬的 → 贪婪的/如饥似渴的
