## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **abet** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：教唆；怂恿；协助（作恶）
- **英文释义**：to encourage or assist someone to commit a crime or wrongdoing
- **例句**：
  - He was accused of abetting the theft by providing keys.
  - 他被控因提供钥匙而教唆盗窃。
  - Silence can abet bullying if witnesses say nothing.
  - 若目击者沉默，沉默会纵容霸凌。
- **助记**：a + bet（打赌）→ 怂恿别人去赌 → 教唆（法律常用 aid and abet）

### 2. **brazen** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：厚颜无耻的；黄铜制的；公然挑衅的
- **英文释义**：bold and without shame; made of brass
- **例句**：
  - It was brazen of him to deny what everyone had seen.
  - 他厚颜否认大家都看到的事。
  - The thief gave a brazen smile when caught.
  - 小偷被抓时还厚颜无耻地笑。
- **助记**：braze（黄铜）→ 脸皮像铜墙 → 厚颜

### 3. **cajole** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：哄骗；劝诱；用甜言蜜语说服
- **英文释义**：to persuade someone to do something by sustained coaxing
- **例句**：
  - She cajoled her brother into helping with homework.
  - 她哄弟弟帮忙做作业。
  - Advertisers cajole consumers with promises of discounts.
  - 广告商用折扣承诺劝诱消费者。
- **助记**：联想 gentle 哄 → cajole 哄劝

### 4. **debase** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：贬低；降低（品质、价值）；使贬值
- **英文释义**：to reduce in quality or value; to degrade
- **例句**：
  - Inflation debased the currency’s purchasing power.
  - 通货膨胀削弱了货币购买力。
  - Insults debased the tone of public debate.
  - 侮辱性言辞降低了公共辩论的水准。
- **助记**：de（向下）+ base（基底）→ 使变低劣 → 贬低

### 5. **efface** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：抹去；擦掉；使不被觉察
- **英文释义**：to erase or remove completely; to make inconspicuous
- **例句**：
  - Time had effaced the inscription on the stone.
  - 岁月磨去了石头上的铭文。
  - She effaced herself in group discussions, speaking little.
  - 她在小组讨论中刻意不引人注目，很少发言。
- **助记**：ef（出）+ face（面）→ 从表面抹去 → 抹去

### 6. **forbear** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：忍耐；克制；避免（做某事）
- **英文释义**：to refrain from doing something one has a right or urge to do
- **例句**：
  - He forbore to mention her mistake in public.
  - 他克制住没有在当众提及她的错误。
  - Please forbear from interrupting the speaker.
  - 请勿打断发言者。
- **助记**：for（前）+ bear（承受）→ 事先承受住 → 克制

### 7. **grapple** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：扭打；努力解决；紧紧抓住
- **英文释义**：to engage in a close fight; to wrestle with a problem
- **例句**：
  - The officer grappled with the suspect on the ground.
  - 警察在地上与嫌疑人扭打。
  - The team grappled with budget cuts all month.
  - 整个月团队都在艰难应对预算削减。
- **助记**：grap（抓）→ 扭打、抓住问题

### 8. **hovel** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：简陋小屋；茅舍；肮脏拥挤的住处
- **英文释义**：a small, squalid, or simply constructed dwelling
- **例句**：
  - The family lived in a hovel with leaking roofs.
  - 这家人住在漏雨的简陋小屋里。
  - Refugees were housed in temporary hovels near the border.
  - 难民被安置在边境附近的临时陋屋中。
- **助记**：hovel 音近“耗屋”→ 破旧耗损的屋子

### 9. **inane** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：空洞的；愚蠢的；无意义的
- **英文释义**：silly; senseless; lacking substance
- **例句**：
  - The meeting was full of inane small talk.
  - 会议充满空洞的闲聊。
  - Don’t post inane comments on serious news threads.
  - 不要在严肃新闻下发表无聊评论。
- **助记**：in（无）+ an（心灵）→ 无心智 → 空洞愚蠢

### 10. **jostle** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：推挤；争抢；挤过
- **英文释义**：to push or bump against roughly in a crowd; to compete for
- **例句**：
  - Commuters jostled for space on the crowded train.
  - 通勤者在拥挤的火车上争抢空间。
  - Photographers jostled to get a better angle.
  - 摄影师推挤着寻找更好角度。
- **助记**：jost（冲撞）→ 推挤

### 高一词汇

### 11. **knavery** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：欺骗；无赖行为；奸诈
- **英文释义**：roguish or dishonest behavior; trickery
- **例句**：
  - The play’s humor depended on knavery and mistaken identity.
  - 这部剧的笑料来自欺骗与认错人。
  - He was known for political knavery rather than honest debate.
  - 他以政治上的奸诈而非诚实辩论著称。
- **助记**：knav（无赖）+ ery → 无赖行径 → 奸诈

### 12. **labyrinthine** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：迷宫般的；错综复杂的；曲折难懂的
- **英文释义**：like a labyrinth; intricate and confusing
- **例句**：
  - The old palace had labyrinthine corridors that confused tourists.
  - 古老宫殿里有迷宫般的走廊，让游客迷路。
  - Tax law is labyrinthine and requires expert help.
  - 税法错综复杂，需要专家协助。
- **助记**：labyrinth（迷宫）+ ine → 如迷宫般

### 13. **maelstrom** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：大漩涡；极度混乱的局面
- **英文释义**：a powerful whirlpool; a situation of confused movement or violent turmoil
- **例句**：
  - The ship was caught in a maelstrom near the rocks.
  - 船在礁石附近陷入大漩涡。
  - She was lost in a maelstrom of rumors and gossip.
  - 她陷入流言蜚语的漩涡中。
- **助记**：mal（磨）+ strom（流）→ 猛烈旋转水流 → 漩涡、混乱

### 14. **nihilism** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：虚无主义；否定一切传统与权威
- **英文释义**：the rejection of religious and moral principles; belief in nothing
- **例句**：
  - The novel explores moral nihilism after the war.
  - 这部小说探讨战后的道德虚无主义。
  - Critics warned against nihilism in youth culture.
  - 批评者警告青年文化中的虚无主义。
- **助记**：nihil（无）→ 一切皆无意义 → 虚无主义

### 15. **obviate** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：排除；使不必要；预先防止
- **英文释义**：to remove a need or difficulty; to make unnecessary
- **例句**：
  - Better planning could obviate many common errors.
  - 更好的规划可以消除许多常见错误。
  - The new bridge obviates the need for a long detour.
  - 新桥消除了绕远路的必要。
- **助记**：ob（对抗）+ vi（路）→ 挡开障碍 → 消除需要
