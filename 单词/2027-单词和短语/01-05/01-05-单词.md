## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **bargain** /ˈbɑːɡɪn/ `初三`

- **词性**：n./v.
- **中文释义**：便宜货；讨价还价
- **英文释义**：something bought cheaply; to negotiate the terms of a deal
- **例句**：
  - This coat was a real bargain — only 50 yuan!
  - 这件大衣真便宜——只要50元！
  - She bargained with the shopkeeper for a lower price.
  - 她和店主讨价还价要求降价。
- **助记**：bar（酒吧）+ gain（获得）→ 在酒吧以低价获得好东西 → 便宜货

### 2. **barrier** /ˈbæriə(r)/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：障碍；屏障
- **英文释义**：a fence or wall that prevents people from moving forward; something that prevents progress
- **例句**：
  - Language is a major barrier to communication.
  - 语言是沟通的一大障碍。
  - The police set up barriers to control the crowd.
  - 警察设置了路障来控制人群。
- **助记**：bar（栏杆）+ -rier → 像栏杆一样挡住 → 障碍

### 3. **beneath** /bɪˈniːθ/ `初三`

- **词性**：prep.
- **中文释义**：在……下面；低于
- **英文释义**：in or to a lower position than; not good enough for
- **例句**：
  - The treasure was buried beneath the old tree.
  - 宝藏埋在老树下面。
  - He thinks such work is beneath him.
  - 他觉得这种工作配不上他。
- **助记**：be-（在）+ neath（下面）→ 在下面

### 4. **beneficial** /ˌbenɪˈfɪʃl/ `初三`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：有益的；有利的
- **英文释义**：having a good or helpful effect
- **例句**：
  - Regular exercise is beneficial to your health.
  - 经常锻炼对健康有益。
  - The new policy will be beneficial for small businesses.
  - 新政策将对小企业有利。
- **助记**：bene-（好）+ fic（做）+ -ial → 做好事的 → 有益的

### 5. **betray** /bɪˈtreɪ/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：背叛；泄露
- **英文释义**：to be disloyal to someone who trusts you; to reveal something unintentionally
- **例句**：
  - He betrayed his best friend's secret.
  - 他出卖了好朋友的秘密。
  - Her voice betrayed her nervousness.
  - 她的声音泄露了她的紧张。
- **助记**：be-（使）+ tray（交出）→ 把信任交出去 → 背叛

### 6. **bias** /ˈbaɪəs/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：偏见；偏向
- **英文释义**：an unfair preference for or against something
- **例句**：
  - The article shows a clear bias against women.
  - 这篇文章明显对女性有偏见。
  - We should try to avoid bias when making decisions.
  - 做决定时我们应该避免偏见。
- **助记**：bi-（两个）+ as → 只看到两面中的一面 → 偏见

### 7. **bind** /baɪnd/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：绑；束缚
- **英文释义**：to tie or fasten tightly; to create a connection between people
- **例句**：
  - She bound the wound with a bandage.
  - 她用绷带包扎了伤口。
  - The contract binds both parties to the agreement.
  - 合同约束双方遵守协议。
- **助记**：bind → band（带子）→ 用带子绑起来 → 绑；束缚

### 8. **bounce** /baʊns/ `初三`

- **词性**：v.
- **中文释义**：弹跳；反弹
- **英文释义**：to move quickly up or away from a surface after hitting it
- **例句**：
  - The ball bounced off the wall.
  - 球从墙上弹了回来。
  - The children were bouncing on the bed.
  - 孩子们在床上蹦跳。
- **助记**：bounce → "蹦磁"（谐音）→ 弹跳

### 9. **breakthrough** /ˈbreɪkθruː/ `初三`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：突破；重大进展
- **英文释义**：an important development or achievement
- **例句**：
  - Scientists made a major breakthrough in cancer research.
  - 科学家在癌症研究方面取得了重大突破。
  - The peace talks led to a breakthrough in the negotiations.
  - 和平谈判使谈判取得了突破。
- **助记**：break（打破）+ through（穿过）→ 打破并穿过障碍 → 突破

### 10. **burden** /ˈbɜːdn/ `初三`

- **词性**：n./v.
- **中文释义**：负担；使负重
- **英文释义**：a heavy load; something that causes worry or difficulty
- **例句**：
  - The financial burden on the family is very heavy.
  - 家庭的经济负担很重。
  - I don't want to burden you with my problems.
  - 我不想让你替我的问题操心。
- **助记**：burd-（承载）+ -en → 需要承载的东西 → 负担

### 高一词汇

### 11. **cacophony** /kəˈkɒfəni/ `高一`

- **词性**：n.
- **中文释义**：刺耳的声音；噪音
- **英文释义**：a harsh, unpleasant mixture of sounds
- **例句**：
  - The cacophony of car horns made it impossible to concentrate.
  - 汽车喇叭的噪音让人无法集中注意力。
  - A cacophony of voices filled the crowded market.
  - 拥挤的市场里充斥着嘈杂的声音。
- **助记**：caco-（坏的）+ phon（声音）+ -y → 坏的声音 → 噪音

### 12. **candid** /ˈkændɪd/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：坦率的；直言不讳的
- **英文释义**：truthful and straightforward; frank
- **例句**：
  - She gave a candid interview about her struggles.
  - 她坦率地接受采访，谈了自己的困境。
  - To be candid, I don't think your plan will work.
  - 坦率地说，我不认为你的计划会奏效。
- **助记**：cand-（白/发光）+ -id → 像白光一样透明 → 坦率的

### 13. **capricious** /kəˈprɪʃəs/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：反复无常的；善变的
- **英文释义**：changing mood or behaviour suddenly and unexpectedly
- **例句**：
  - The capricious weather made it hard to plan outdoor activities.
  - 反复无常的天气让户外活动难以计划。
  - She has a capricious temperament that confuses everyone.
  - 她善变的性格让所有人困惑。
- **助记**：capri-（山羊）+ -cious → 像山羊一样跳来跳去 → 反复无常的

### 14. **celestial** /sɪˈlestiəl/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：天上的；天体的
- **英文释义**：relating to the sky or outer space; heavenly
- **例句**：
  - The astronomer studied celestial bodies through the telescope.
  - 天文学家通过望远镜研究天体。
  - The music had an almost celestial beauty.
  - 这音乐有一种近乎天上的美。
- **助记**：celest-（天空）+ -ial → 天上的

### 15. **clandestine** /klænˈdestɪn/ `高一`

- **词性**：adj.
- **中文释义**：秘密的；暗中的
- **英文释义**：kept secret or done secretly, especially because it is illegal
- **例句**：
  - They held a clandestine meeting in the basement.
  - 他们在地下室举行了一次秘密会议。
  - The spy carried out clandestine operations abroad.
  - 间谍在海外执行秘密行动。
- **助记**：clam（关闭）+ dest（隐藏）+ -ine → 关上门隐藏的 → 秘密的
