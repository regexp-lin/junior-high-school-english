## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **lamentable** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：令人惋惜的；可悲的；痛心的
- **英文释义**：deplorably bad or unsatisfactory; regrettable
- **例句**：
  - The team’s lamentable performance disappointed thousands of fans.
  - 球队令人失望的表现让成千上万的球迷失望。
  - It is lamentable that so many children still lack basic education.
  - 如此多的儿童仍缺乏基础教育，令人痛心。
- **助记**：lament（悲叹）+ able → 值得悲叹的 → 可悲的

### 2. **magnate** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：大亨；巨头；权贵
- **英文释义**：a wealthy and influential person, especially in business
- **例句**：
  - A media magnate owned several newspapers and TV stations.
  - 一位媒体大亨拥有多家报社和电视台。
  - Oil magnates gathered at the international energy forum.
  - 石油巨头齐聚国际能源论坛。
- **助记**：magn（大）+ ate → 大人物 → 大亨

### 3. **nadir** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：最低点；最糟时刻；（天）天底
- **英文释义**：the lowest or most unsuccessful point in a situation
- **例句**：
  - Morale reached its nadir after the third defeat in a row.
  - 连续第三次失败后，士气跌到谷底。
  - His career hit its nadir before the comeback season.
  - 在复出赛季之前，他的事业跌入最低点。
- **助记**：与 zenith（顶点）相对 → nadir 为最低点

### 4. **obloquy** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：公开指责；耻辱；恶名
- **英文释义**：strong public criticism or verbal abuse; disgrace
- **例句**：
  - The official resigned under public obloquy.
  - 这位官员在公众谴责下辞职。
  - She faced obloquy for a decision that was widely misunderstood.
  - 她因一个被广泛误解的决定而蒙受骂名。
- **助记**：ob（对着）+ loqu（说）→ 对着说坏话 → 辱骂、恶名

### 5. **pariah** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：被排斥者；贱民；局外人
- **英文释义**：an outcast; a person despised or rejected by society
- **例句**：
  - After the scandal, he became a social pariah.
  - 丑闻之后，他成了社交场合的被排斥者。
  - Whistleblowers are sometimes treated as pariahs.
  - 举报者有时被当作异类对待。
- **助记**：来自泰米尔语“鼓手”种姓 → 被排斥阶层 → 贱民

### 6. **quixotic** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：堂吉诃德式的；不切实际的；空想的
- **英文释义**：exceedingly idealistic; unrealistic and impractical
- **例句**：
  - His quixotic plan to save every stray animal touched but worried friends.
  - 他拯救每只流浪动物的不切实际的计划令人感动又担忧。
  - Quixotic dreams rarely survive contact with reality.
  - 不切实际的梦想很少经得起现实考验。
- **助记**：来自 Don Quixote → 空想侠义 → 不切实际

### 7. **redolent** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：芬芳的；充满……气息的；使人联想起
- **英文释义**：strongly suggestive or evocative of something; fragrant
- **例句**：
  - The kitchen was redolent of fresh bread and herbs.
  - 厨房里弥漫着新鲜面包和香草的香味。
  - The poem is redolent of classical Chinese imagery.
  - 这首诗充满中国古典意象的韵味。
- **助记**：re（再）+ ol（闻）→ 一再可闻 → 芬芳、意味

### 8. **sagacious** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：睿智的；有洞察力的；精明的
- **英文释义**：having or showing keen mental discernment and good judgment
- **例句**：
  - The sagacious judge saw through the false testimony.
  - 睿智的法官识破了虚假证词。
  - Sagacious investors avoided the bubble market in time.
  - 精明的投资者及时避开了泡沫市场。
- **助记**：sagac（敏锐）+ ious → 敏锐智慧的 → 睿智

### 9. **trenchant** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：（言辞）犀利的；尖锐的；鲜明的
- **英文释义**：vigorous or incisive in expression or style; forceful
- **例句**：
  - The article offered a trenchant critique of government policy.
  - 文章对政府政策进行了犀利批评。
  - Her trenchant remarks silenced the room.
  - 她尖锐的发言让全场安静下来。
- **助记**：trench（沟）→ 如刀切沟 → 犀利

### 10. **urbane** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：温文尔雅的；都市化的；有礼貌的
- **英文释义**：courteous and refined in manner; suave
- **例句**：
  - The host’s urbane conversation put guests at ease.
  - 主人温文尔雅的谈吐让客人放松。
  - Urbane manners are valued in diplomatic circles.
  - 外交圈重视温文尔雅的举止。
- **助记**：urban（城市）→ 城市绅士风度 → 文雅

### 高一词汇

### 11. **languid** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：慵懒的；没精打采的；迟缓的
- **英文释义**：slow and relaxed; displaying little energy or interest
- **例句**：
  - On hot afternoons, the cat lay in a languid pose on the sofa.
  - 炎热午后，猫慵懒地趴在沙发上。
  - His languid voice made the story sound even sadder.
  - 他无精打采的声音让故事听起来更悲伤。
- **助记**：langu（虚弱）+ id → 软弱无力的 → 慵懒

### 12. **misanthrope** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：厌恶人类者；愤世嫉俗者
- **英文释义**：a person who dislikes humankind and avoids human society
- **例句**：
  - The character was portrayed as a bitter misanthrope living alone.
  - 这个角色被塑造成独居的愤世嫉俗者。
  - Not every introvert is a misanthrope.
  - 并非每个内向者都厌恶人类。
- **助记**：misan（厌恶）+ thrope（人）→ 厌人者

### 13. **nondescript** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：毫无特色的；平庸的；难以形容的
- **英文释义**：lacking distinctive or interesting features; dull
- **例句**：
  - The suspect wore a nondescript gray jacket and jeans.
  - 嫌疑人穿着毫无特色的灰夹克和牛仔裤。
  - The building was nondescript and easy to miss.
  - 那座建筑平平无奇，很容易被忽略。
- **助记**：non（无）+ descript（描述）→ 无法描述特点 → 平庸

### 14. **ossify** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：僵化；骨化；使变死板
- **英文释义**：to become rigid or fixed in attitude or behavior; to turn into bone
- **例句**：
  - Bureaucracy can ossify innovation in large organizations.
  - 官僚作风会使大机构的创新僵化。
  - Some traditions ossify into empty rituals over time.
  - 有些传统久而久之僵化成空洞仪式。
- **助记**：oss（骨）+ ify → 变成骨 → 僵化

### 15. **propitiate** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：安抚；劝解；使息怒
- **英文释义**：to win or regain the favor of a god, spirit, or person by doing something that pleases them
- **例句**：
  - Ancient people offered gifts to propitiate the gods.
  - 古人献祭以安抚神灵。
  - He brought flowers to propitiate his angry neighbor.
  - 他带花去安抚生气的邻居。
- **助记**：pro（向前）+ pit（仁慈）+ iate → 求得仁慈 → 安抚
