## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **torpid** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：迟钝的；麻木的；冬眠的；不活泼的
- **英文释义**：mentally or physically inactive; lethargic; dormant
- **例句**：
  - After a heavy lunch, he felt torpid and unproductive all afternoon.
  - 午饭后他整个下午都昏昏沉沉，效率低下。
  - Torpid snakes lay motionless on the sun-warmed rocks.
  - 冬眠的蛇一动不动地趴在晒热的岩石上。
- **助记**：torp（麻木）+ id → 像冬眠一样迟钝

### 2. **tractable** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：易驾驭的；温顺的；易处理的
- **英文释义**：easy to deal with or control; docile
- **例句**：
  - The tractable puppy learned basic commands within a week.
  - 那只温顺的小狗一周内就学会了基本指令。
  - The problem proved more tractable once we split it into smaller tasks.
  - 把问题拆成小任务后，证明更容易处理。
- **助记**：tract（拉）+ able → 可被拉动的 → 易驾驭的

### 3. **transitory** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：短暂的；转瞬即逝的
- **英文释义**：not permanent; transient; fleeting
- **例句**：
  - Youthful enthusiasm is often transitory but powerful while it lasts.
  - 青春的激情往往短暂，但在持续时很有力量。
  - The transitory nature of online fame worries many content creators.
  - 网络名气的转瞬即逝让许多内容创作者担忧。
- **助记**：trans（穿过）+ it + ory → 穿过即走 → 短暂的

### 4. **turpitude** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：卑鄙；堕落；（尤指）道德败坏
- **英文释义**：depravity; wickedness; vile or shameful behavior
- **例句**：
  - The official was charged with moral turpitude for accepting bribes.
  - 该官员因受贿被控道德败坏。
  - Historical records documented the turpitude of the corrupt regime.
  - 历史记录记载了腐败政权的种种恶行。
- **助记**：turp（肮脏）+ itude → 卑鄙

### 5. **umbrage** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：不快；愤怒；（古）树荫
- **英文释义**：offense or annoyance; a feeling of being slighted
- **例句**：
  - She took umbrage at his suggestion that she was unprepared.
  - 他暗示她准备不足，她感到不快。
  - The critic's harsh review caused the author to take umbrage on social media.
  - 评论家的刻薄评论让作者在社交媒体上感到恼火。
- **助记**：umbra（阴影）→ 心里投下阴影 → 不快

### 6. **unfettered** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：不受束缚的；自由的；无拘束的
- **英文释义**：not confined or restricted; unrestrained
- **例句**：
  - Artists need unfettered imagination to create breakthrough works.
  - 艺术家需要不受限制的想象力才能创作突破之作。
  - The dictator demanded unfettered power over the military.
  - 独裁者要求对军队拥有不受限制的权力。
- **助记**：un（不）+ fetter（脚镣）+ ed → 无镣铐 → 自由的

### 7. **vacuous** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：空洞的；无思想的；愚蠢的
- **英文释义**：having or showing a lack of thought or intelligence; mindless
- **例句**：
  - The interview showed him to be handsome but vacuous.
  - 采访显示他英俊但空洞。
  - Vacuous celebrity gossip fills the tabloids every week.
  - 空洞的名人八卦每周填满了小报。
- **助记**：vacu（空）+ ous → 空空如也 → 空洞的

### 8. **vaunted** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：被吹嘘的；被夸大的
- **英文释义**：praised or boasted about, especially excessively
- **例句**：
  - The vaunted security system failed during the first cyberattack.
  - 被夸得天花乱坠的安保系统在第一次网络攻击时就失败了。
  - The vaunted benefits of the new drug were not supported by data.
  - 新药被夸大的疗效并没有数据支持。
- **助记**：vaunt（吹嘘）+ ed → 被吹出来的

### 9. **venal** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：贪污的；唯利是图的；可收买的
- **英文释义**：showing or motivated by susceptibility to bribery; corrupt
- **例句**：
  - Venal officials sold state secrets to foreign agents.
  - 贪官向外国特工出售国家机密。
  - The novel exposes the venal politics of the capital city.
  - 小说揭露了首都唯利是图的政治。
- **助记**：ven（卖，如 vendor）+ al → 可收买的

### 10. **vilify** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：诽谤；中伤；污蔑
- **英文释义**：to speak or write about in an abusively disparaging manner
- **例句**：
  - Opponents tried to vilify the candidate with false stories.
  - 对手试图用虚假故事诽谤候选人。
  - She was vilified online for a comment taken out of context.
  - 她因一句被断章取义的话在网上遭到谩骂。
- **助记**：vil（卑鄙）+ ify → 使显得卑鄙 → 诽谤

### 高一词汇

### 11. **vituperate** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：谩骂；痛斥；责骂
- **英文释义**：to blame or insult someone in strong or violent language
- **例句**：
  - The editorial vituperated against the proposed tax cuts.
  - 社论对拟议的减税政策大加谩骂。
  - He vituperated his former partner in an angry blog post.
  - 他在愤怒的博文中痛斥前合伙人。
- **助记**：vituper（责骂）+ ate → 厉声责骂

### 12. **wan** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：（面色）苍白憔悴的；无血色的；暗淡的
- **英文释义**：pale and giving the impression of illness or unhappiness
- **例句**：
  - After weeks of illness, she looked wan and thin.
  - 病了几周后，她看起来苍白消瘦。
  - A wan smile was all he could manage after the bad news.
  - 听到坏消息后，他只能勉强挤出一丝苦笑。
- **助记**：联想 wane（月亏）→ 失去光彩 → 苍白

### 13. **abstruse** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：深奥难懂的；玄妙的
- **英文释义**：difficult to understand; obscure
- **例句**：
  - The philosopher's abstruse arguments confused first-year students.
  - 哲学家深奥的论证让一年级学生困惑。
  - Legal contracts are often written in abstruse language on purpose.
  - 合同法律语言往往故意写得晦涩难懂。
- **助记**：abs（离开）+ trus（推）→ 推到远处 → 深奥

### 14. **adamantine** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：坚硬无比的；坚不可摧的；坚决不动摇的
- **英文释义**：unbreakable; impossible to persuade; like a diamond in hardness
- **例句**：
  - She maintained an adamantine refusal to compromise on safety standards.
  - 她在安全标准上坚决拒绝妥协。
  - The myth described an adamantine shield that could not be pierced.
  - 神话描述了一面坚不可摧的盾牌。
- **助记**：adamant（坚硬）+ ine → 如金刚石般硬

### 15. **anodyne** `高一` adj./n.

- **中文释义**：平淡乏味的；不得罪人的；止痛剂
- **英文释义**：not likely to offend or upset anyone; inoffensive; a painkilling drug
- **例句**：
  - The politician's speech was anodyne and avoided every controversial topic.
  - 那位政客的演讲平淡圆滑，避开了所有争议话题。
  - Doctors prescribed an anodyne for the patient's chronic pain.
  - 医生为病人的慢性疼痛开了止痛药。
- **助记**：an（无）+ odyne（痛）→ 无痛 → 止痛/平淡
