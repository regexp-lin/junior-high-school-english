## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **ransom** `初三` n./v.

- **中文释义**：赎金；赎回；勒赎
- **英文释义**：a sum demanded for the release of a captive; to obtain release by payment
- **例句**：
  - The kidnappers demanded a ransom of one million dollars.
  - 绑匪索要一百万美元赎金。
  - The hostages were ransomed after weeks of negotiation.
  - 经过数周谈判，人质被赎出。
- **助记**：ransom 联想 random 无关 → 赎（金）固定词

### 2. **scoff** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：嘲笑；嘲弄；狼吞虎咽（与 at/down 搭配）
- **英文释义**：to speak to someone or about something in a scornfully derisive manner
- **例句**：
  - Critics scoffed at the idea that the plan would work.
  - 批评者嘲笑该计划会奏效的想法。
  - He scoffed when she said she could finish in an hour.
  - 当她说她一小时能做完时，他嗤之以鼻。
- **助记**：scoff 联想 cough 嘲笑时嗤之以鼻 → 嘲笑

### 3. **tether** `初三` v./n.

- **中文释义**：用绳拴住；限制；系绳；牵连
- **英文释义**：to tie an animal with a rope; to restrict someone's freedom; a rope or chain
- **例句**：
  - The goat was tethered to a post in the yard.
  - 山羊被拴在院子的柱子上。
  - Budget cuts tethered the department's ability to hire new staff.
  - 预算削减限制了该部门招聘新员工的能力。
- **助记**：tether 联想 together 用绳系在一起 → 拴住

### 4. **ulterior** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：隐秘的；别有用心的；较远的
- **英文释义**：existing beyond what is obvious or admitted; hidden motives
- **例句**：
  - She suspected he had an ulterior motive for offering help.
  - 她怀疑他主动帮忙别有用心。
  - The donation seemed generous, but ulterior goals emerged later.
  - 捐款看似慷慨，后来却暴露出隐秘目的。
- **助记**：ulter（那边）→ 藏在更远处的 → 隐秘的

### 5. **vagrant** `初三` n./adj.

- **中文释义**：流浪者；漂泊的；无业游民
- **英文释义**：a person without a settled home or regular work; wandering or drifting
- **例句**：
  - The shelter provides meals for vagrants in winter.
  - 收容所为冬季的流浪者提供餐食。
  - He led a vagrant life after losing his job and apartment.
  - 失业又失去公寓后他过着漂泊生活。
- **助记**：vag（漫游，如 vague）+ rant → 四处游荡的人 → 流浪者

### 6. **winsome** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：迷人的；可爱的；令人愉悦的
- **英文释义**：attractive or appealing in a fresh, innocent way; charming
- **例句**：
  - The child gave a winsome smile that melted everyone's heart.
  - 孩子露出迷人的微笑，融化了所有人的心。
  - Her winsome personality made her popular in the community.
  - 她可爱的性格使她在社区很受欢迎。
- **助记**：win（赢得）+ some → 赢得好感的 → 迷人的

### 7. **yonder** `初三` adv.

- **中文释义**：在那边；远处（古语/方言色彩）
- **英文释义**：at some distance in the direction indicated; over there
- **例句**：
  - The hills yonder turn gold at sunset.
  - 远处山峦在日落时变成金色。
  - Walk toward the barn yonder and turn left at the oak.
  - 朝那边的谷仓走，在橡树处左转。
- **助记**：yond（那边）+ er → 在那边

### 8. **zestful** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：充满热情的；兴致勃勃的
- **英文释义**：full of enthusiasm, energy, and enjoyment
- **例句**：
  - The zestful volunteers painted the school in one weekend.
  - 热情的志愿者一个周末就刷好了学校。
  - He approached every challenge with a zestful attitude.
  - 他以兴致勃勃的态度面对每个挑战。
- **助记**：zest（热情、柠檬皮提味）+ ful → 有滋有味有劲头 → 热情的

### 9. **abashed** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：窘迫的；羞愧的；局促不安的
- **英文释义**：embarrassed, disconcerted, or ashamed
- **例句**：
  - She was abashed when she tripped on stage during the play.
  - 演出时在台上绊倒，她十分窘迫。
  - He looked abashed after his rude comment was pointed out.
  - 粗鲁言论被指出后他显得羞愧。
- **助记**：a（加强）+ bash（猛撞、使尴尬）→ 被撞懵 → 窘迫的

### 10. **bemoan** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：哀叹；抱怨；为……惋惜
- **英文释义**：to express discontent or sorrow over something
- **例句**：
  - He bemoaned the lack of funding for public libraries.
  - 他为公共图书馆经费不足而哀叹。
  - Travelers bemoaned the delay but could do nothing.
  - 旅客抱怨延误却无可奈何。
- **助记**：be（使）+ moan（呻吟）→ 哀叹

### 高一词汇

### 11. **cajole** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：哄骗；劝诱；用好话游说
- **英文释义**：to persuade someone to do something by sustained coaxing or flattery
- **例句**：
  - She cajoled her parents into letting her study abroad.
  - 她哄劝父母同意她出国留学。
  - The salesman tried to cajole customers with free samples.
  - 推销员试图用免费样品劝诱顾客。
- **助记**：caj（笼，法语）→ 把人笼络住 → 哄劝

### 12. **debacle** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：溃败；惨败；崩溃
- **英文释义**：a sudden and ignominious failure; a fiasco
- **例句**：
  - The product launch was a debacle when the demo crashed live.
  - 演示现场崩溃，产品发布成了一场惨败。
  - Investors fled after the financial debacle.
  - 金融崩盘后投资者纷纷逃离。
- **助记**：de（去掉）+ bac（棒）→ 棍棒折断 → 溃败

### 13. **effusive** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：热情洋溢的；感情溢于言表的；过多夸奖的
- **英文释义**：expressing feelings of gratitude, pleasure, or approval in an unrestrained way
- **例句**：
  - She was effusive in her thanks after the rescue.
  - 获救后她感激之情溢于言表。
  - His effusive praise made the shy student uncomfortable.
  - 他热情洋溢的表扬让害羞的学生不自在。
- **助记**：ef（出）+ fus（流）+ ive → 情感外流 → 热情洋溢的

### 14. **fiasco** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：惨败；可笑的失败；一团糟
- **英文释义**：a complete failure, especially a ludicrous or humiliating one
- **例句**：
  - The picnic was a fiasco when it rained and the food spoiled.
  - 下雨食物变质，野餐成了惨败。
  - The first version of the app was a fiasco full of bugs.
  - 该应用第一版漏洞百出，彻底失败。
- **助记**：意大利语 fiasco（瓶子）→ 演砸 → 惨败

### 15. **grotesque** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：怪诞的；丑陋奇异的；荒唐的
- **英文释义**：comically or repulsively ugly or distorted; incongruous
- **例句**：
  - The statue combined human and animal forms in a grotesque design.
  - 雕像以怪诞设计把人与兽形结合。
  - Pay inequality on this scale is grotesque.
  - 如此悬殊的薪酬差距是荒唐的。
- **助记**：grotto（洞穴壁画）→ 奇异变形 → 怪诞的
