## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **preclude** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：阻止；排除；妨碍
- **英文释义**：to prevent from happening; to make impossible
- **例句**：
  - The heavy snowfall precluded any possibility of traveling by road.
  - 大雪阻止了任何公路出行的可能性。
  - His lack of experience precludes him from applying for the senior position.
  - 他缺乏经验使他无法申请高级职位。
- **助记**：pre（预先）+ clude（关闭）→ 预先关上门 → 阻止

### 2. **candid** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：坦诚的；直率的；率真的
- **英文释义**：truthful and straightforward; frank
- **例句**：
  - I appreciate your candid feedback, even though it was hard to hear.
  - 我感谢你坦诚的反馈，即使它很难听。
  - The politician gave a candid interview about the challenges facing the country.
  - 这位政治家就国家面临的挑战进行了坦率的采访。
- **助记**：cand（白/明亮）+ id → 光明磊落 → 坦诚的

### 3. **fabricate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：编造；捏造；伪造
- **英文释义**：to invent or concoct something, typically in order to deceive
- **例句**：
  - The suspect fabricated an alibi to avoid suspicion from the police.
  - 嫌疑人编造了不在场证明以避免警察的怀疑。
  - The journalist was fired for fabricating stories that were published as facts.
  - 那位记者因编造被当作事实发表的故事而被解雇。
- **助记**：fabric（织物）+ ate → 像织布一样编织 → 编造

### 4. **arbitrary** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：武断的；任意的；随心所欲的
- **英文释义**：based on random choice or personal whim, rather than any reason or system
- **例句**：
  - The teacher's grading system seemed arbitrary and unfair to the students.
  - 老师的评分体系在学生看来似乎武断且不公平。
  - The government's arbitrary restrictions angered many citizens.
  - 政府武断的限制措施激怒了许多公民。
- **助记**：arbitr（裁判/判断）+ ary → 个人判断的 → 武断的

### 5. **placate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：安抚；抚慰；平息
- **英文释义**：to make someone less angry or hostile
- **例句**：
  - The manager offered a full refund to placate the angry customer.
  - 经理提出全额退款以安抚愤怒的客户。
  - Nothing could placate the protesters, who demanded immediate reform.
  - 没有什么能平息抗议者的愤怒，他们要求立即改革。
- **助记**：plac（平静）+ ate → 使平静 → 安抚

### 6. **ubiquitous** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：无处不在的；普遍存在的
- **英文释义**：present, appearing, or found everywhere
- **例句**：
  - Smartphones have become ubiquitous in modern society.
  - 智能手机在现代社会已无处不在。
  - Coffee shops are ubiquitous in urban areas, appearing on nearly every street corner.
  - 咖啡店在城市地区无处不在，几乎每个街角都有。
- **助记**：ubique（到处）+ ous → 到处都有的 → 无处不在的

### 7. **emancipate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：解放；使自由；解除束缚
- **英文释义**：to set free from legal, social, or political restrictions
- **例句**：
  - The civil rights movement fought to emancipate people from racial discrimination.
  - 民权运动为把人们从种族歧视中解放出来而斗争。
  - Education can emancipate individuals from the cycle of poverty.
  - 教育能把个人从贫困循环中解放出来。
- **助记**：e（出）+ mancip（手/控制）+ ate → 从手中释放 → 解放

### 8. **laborious** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：费力的；艰辛的；辛苦的
- **英文释义**：requiring considerable effort and time
- **例句**：
  - Translating the ancient manuscript was a laborious task that took years to complete.
  - 翻译这份古代手稿是一项费力的任务，花了好几年才完成。
  - The laborious process of data entry left the interns exhausted.
  - 费力的数据录入过程让实习生们精疲力竭。
- **助记**：labor（劳动）+ ious → 需要大量劳动的 → 费力的

### 9. **mitigate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：减轻；缓和；缓解
- **英文释义**：to make less severe, serious, or painful
- **例句**：
  - Planting trees can help mitigate the effects of climate change.
  - 植树可以帮助减轻气候变化的影响。
  - The government introduced policies to mitigate the economic impact of the pandemic.
  - 政府推出了缓解疫情经济影响的政策。
- **助记**：mit（温和）+ igate → 使变温和 → 减轻

### 10. **resilient** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：有韧性的；恢复力强的；坚强的
- **英文释义**：able to withstand or recover quickly from difficult conditions
- **例句**：
  - Children are remarkably resilient and can adapt to new environments quickly.
  - 儿童非常有韧性，能快速适应新环境。
  - The resilient community rebuilt their homes after the devastating earthquake.
  - 坚强的社区在毁灭性地震后重建了他们的家园。
- **助记**：re（回）+ sil（跳）+ ient → 弹回来 → 有韧性的

### 高一词汇

### 11. **antediluvian** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：极古老的；过时的；上古的
- **英文释义**：of or belonging to the time before the biblical Flood; ridiculously old-fashioned
- **例句**：
  - His antediluvian views on gender roles shocked the younger generation.
  - 他关于性别角色的过时观点让年轻一代感到震惊。
  - The museum displayed antediluvian fossils dating back millions of years.
  - 博物馆展出了追溯到数百万年前的远古化石。
- **助记**：ante（之前）+ diluvian（洪水）→ 大洪水之前的 → 极古老的

### 12. **pulchritudinous** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：美丽的；漂亮的
- **英文释义**：having great physical beauty
- **例句**：
  - The pulchritudinous sunset over the ocean captivated every observer on the beach.
  - 海面上美丽的日落迷住了海滩上的每一个观者。
  - Despite its pulchritudinous sound, the word is rarely used in everyday conversation.
  - 尽管这个词听起来很华丽，但在日常对话中很少使用。
- **助记**：pulchritude（美丽）+ inous → 美丽的（本身就是一个"丑"拼写的"美"词）

### 13. **verisimilitude** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：逼真；真实感；似真性
- **英文释义**：the appearance of being true or real
- **例句**：
  - The film's verisimilitude impressed critics, who praised its realistic depiction of wartime life.
  - 这部电影的逼真性给评论家留下了深刻印象，他们赞扬了其对战时生活的真实描绘。
  - Good historical fiction requires a high degree of verisimilitude to be convincing.
  - 好的历史小说需要很高的真实感才能令人信服。
- **助记**：veri（真实）+ simil（相似）+ tude → 像真的一样 → 逼真

### 14. **recalcitrant** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：顽抗的；不服从的；桀骜不驯的
- **英文释义**：having an obstinately uncooperative attitude toward authority or discipline
- **例句**：
  - The recalcitrant student refused to follow the school's dress code despite repeated warnings.
  - 这个桀骜不驯的学生尽管被多次警告，仍然拒绝遵守学校的着装规定。
  - Dealing with recalcitrant suppliers delayed the project by several months.
  - 应对不合作的供应商使项目延迟了好几个月。
- **助记**：re（反）+ calcitrant（踢）→ 往回踢 → 顽抗的

### 15. **obsequious** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：谄媚的；奉承的；恭顺的
- **英文释义**：obedient or attentive to an excessive or servile degree
- **例句**：
  - The obsequious assistant agreed with every decision the boss made, no matter how questionable.
  - 那个谄媚的助手对老板做的每个决定都表示同意，不管多么可疑。
  - His obsequious behavior toward the wealthy client made his colleagues uncomfortable.
  - 他对富有客户的奉承行为让同事们感到不自在。
- **助记**：ob（朝向）+ sequ（跟随）+ ious → 一直跟在后面 → 谄媚的
