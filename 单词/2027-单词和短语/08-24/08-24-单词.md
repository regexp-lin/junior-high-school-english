## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **agile** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：敏捷的；灵活的；机敏的
- **英文释义**：able to move quickly and easily; able to think quickly and clearly
- **例句**：
  - The agile cat leaped onto the high fence in one bound.
  - 敏捷的猫一跃跳上高栅栏。
  - Startups need an agile approach to respond to market changes.
  - 初创公司需要灵活的方法以应对市场变化。
- **助记**：ag（做、驱动）+ ile → 动作利索 → 敏捷的

### 2. **cynical** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：愤世嫉俗的；冷嘲热讽的；怀疑人善意的
- **英文释义**：believing that people are motivated purely by self-interest; distrustful of sincerity
- **例句**：
  - He gave a cynical laugh when told politicians would keep their promises.
  - 听说政客会信守承诺时，他冷笑了一声。
  - Cynical voters stayed home on election day.
  - 愤世嫉俗的选民在选举日待在家里未投票。
- **助记**：cynic（犬儒学派）→ 对人性不信任 → 愤世嫉俗的

### 3. **erudite** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：博学的；有学问的；学术性的
- **英文释义**：having or showing great knowledge; learned and scholarly
- **例句**：
  - The professor delivered an erudite lecture on ancient philosophy.
  - 教授就古代哲学做了一场博学的演讲。
  - Her essays are erudite yet accessible to general readers.
  - 她的文章博学而普通读者也能读懂。
- **助记**：e（出）+ rud（原始）+ ite → 从无知中走出 → 博学的

### 4. **fervent** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：热烈的；热情的；强烈的
- **英文释义**：displaying or characterized by intense passion or conviction
- **例句**：
  - She is a fervent advocate for children's education rights.
  - 她是儿童教育权的热烈倡导者。
  - He spoke with fervent hope that peace would return.
  - 他带着强烈的希望发言，期盼和平归来。
- **助记**：ferv（沸腾，如 fever）+ ent → 内心沸腾 → 热烈的

### 5. **gullible** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：轻信的；易受骗的
- **英文释义**：easily persuaded to believe something; naive and trusting
- **例句**：
  - Scammers often target gullible elderly people by phone.
  - 骗子经常通过电话瞄准轻信的老人。
  - Don't be so gullible — check the facts before you share the story.
  - 别这么轻信——分享前先核实事实。
- **助记**：gull（鸥）联想轻易被诱捕 → 易受骗的

### 6. **hapless** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：倒霉的；不幸的；运气不好的
- **英文释义**：unfortunate; having no luck
- **例句**：
  - The hapless traveler missed three connections in one day.
  - 这位倒霉的旅客一天错过了三次转机。
  - Even the hapless team finally won a game last week.
  - 就连那支倒霉的球队上周也终于赢了一场。
- **助记**：hap（运气）+ less → 没有运气的 → 不幸的

### 7. **idiosyncratic** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：独特的；怪癖的；个人特有的
- **英文释义**：peculiar or individual; distinctive to a particular person or thing
- **例句**：
  - The director's idiosyncratic style makes his films easy to recognize.
  - 这位导演独特的风格使他的电影易于辨认。
  - She has an idiosyncratic way of organizing her desk.
  - 她整理书桌的方式很独特。
- **助记**：idio（个人的）+ syncratic → 个人特有的 → 独特的

### 8. **judicious** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：明智的；审慎的；判断得当的
- **英文释义**：having, showing, or done with good judgment; sensible and careful
- **例句**：
  - A judicious investment at the right time doubled his savings.
  - 在恰当时机做的明智投资使他的储蓄翻了一番。
  - The editor made judicious cuts to shorten the article.
  - 编辑审慎删节以缩短文章。
- **助记**：judic（判断）+ ious → 善于判断的 → 明智的

### 9. **knell** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：丧钟；不祥之兆；（事物）终结的信号
- **英文释义**：the sound of a bell, especially when rung solemnly after a death; a sign of failure or ending
- **例句**：
  - The distant knell echoed through the village at the funeral.
  - 葬礼上远处的丧钟在村庄回荡。
  - The bankruptcy sounded the knell for the century-old factory.
  - 破产宣告了这家百年老厂的终结。
- **助记**：knell 联想 bell（钟）→ 钟声尤指丧钟

### 10. **listless** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：无精打采的；懒洋洋的；缺乏兴趣的
- **英文释义**：lacking energy or enthusiasm; lethargic and indifferent
- **例句**：
  - The hot weather made everyone feel listless in the afternoon.
  - 炎热天气让大家下午都提不起精神。
  - She seemed listless after hearing the bad news.
  - 听到坏消息后她显得无精打采。
- **助记**：list（渴望）+ less → 没有渴望、没劲头 → 无精打采的

### 高一词汇

### 11. **orthodox** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：正统的；传统的；普遍接受的
- **英文释义**：conforming to what is generally or traditionally accepted; conventional
- **例句**：
  - He follows orthodox medical treatment rather than alternative cures.
  - 他接受正统医学治疗，不用另类疗法。
  - Orthodox economic theory did not predict the crisis.
  - 正统经济理论未能预见这场危机。
- **助记**：ortho（正、直）+ dox（观点）→ 正统观点 → 正统的

### 12. **paradigm** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：范式；典范；思维模式
- **英文释义**：a typical example or pattern of something; a model or framework
- **例句**：
  - The discovery shifted the paradigm in modern physics.
  - 这一发现改变了现代物理学的范式。
  - Her success became a paradigm for young entrepreneurs.
  - 她的成功成为年轻创业者的典范。
- **助记**：para（旁边）+ digm（展示）→ 展示供仿效的样板 → 范式

### 13. **quixotic** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：堂吉诃德式的；不切实际的；空想的
- **英文释义**：exceedingly idealistic; unrealistic and impractical
- **例句**：
  - His quixotic quest to save every stray animal exhausted his savings.
  - 他不切实际地要救助每只流浪动物，耗尽了积蓄。
  - The plan was dismissed as quixotic by the board.
  - 董事会认为该计划不切实际而不予考虑。
- **助记**：来自 Don Quixote（堂吉诃德）→ 空想侠义 → 不切实际的

### 14. **replete** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：充满的；饱足的；供应充足的
- **英文释义**：filled or well-supplied with something; gorged or stuffed
- **例句**：
  - The essay is replete with historical references.
  - 这篇文章充满历史典故。
  - After the feast, we felt replete and sleepy.
  - 盛宴之后我们感到饱足困倦。
- **助记**：re（再）+ plere（填满）→ 再填满 → 充满的

### 15. **scintillating** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：才华横溢的；妙趣横生的；闪烁的
- **英文释义**：sparkling or shining brightly; brilliantly clever and lively
- **例句**：
  - The dinner conversation was scintillating and lasted past midnight.
  - 席间谈话妙趣横生，一直持续到午夜后。
  - Stars were scintillating in the clear desert sky.
  - 星星在清澈的沙漠夜空中闪烁。
- **助记**：scintilla（火花）→ 迸发火花 → 才华横溢的
