## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **bewilder** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：使困惑；使迷惑；使不知所措
- **英文释义**：to cause someone to become perplexed and confused
- **例句**：
  - The complex rules of the game bewildered the new players.
  - 游戏复杂的规则让新玩家们感到困惑。
- **助记**：be（使）+ wilder（更迷茫）→ 使更迷茫 → 使困惑

### 2. **curtail** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：缩减；削减；限制
- **英文释义**：to reduce in extent or quantity; to impose a restriction on
- **例句**：
  - The government curtailed public spending to reduce the national debt.
  - 政府削减公共开支以减少国债。
- **助记**：cur（短）+ tail（尾巴）→ 剪短尾巴 → 缩减/削减

### 3. **disclose** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：揭露；公开；透露
- **英文释义**：to make new or secret information known
- **例句**：
  - The company refused to disclose the details of the merger until the deal was finalized.
  - 在交易最终确定之前，公司拒绝透露合并的细节。
- **助记**：dis（去除）+ close（关闭）→ 打开关闭的东西 → 揭露/透露

### 4. **expedite** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：加快；促进；加速处理
- **英文释义**：to make an action or process happen sooner or be accomplished more quickly
- **例句**：
  - We hired additional staff to expedite the delivery of orders during the holiday season.
  - 我们增聘了员工以加快假期期间的订单交付。
- **助记**：ex（出）+ ped（脚）+ ite → 把脚伸出来快走 → 加快

### 5. **flaunt** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：炫耀；夸示；招摇
- **英文释义**：to display something ostentatiously in order to provoke envy or admiration
- **例句**：
  - He likes to flaunt his expensive watches and designer clothes.
  - 他喜欢炫耀他昂贵的手表和名牌衣服。
- **助记**：flaunt → 联想"飞扬"→ 到处飞扬地展示 → 炫耀

### 6. **impoverish** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：使贫穷；使枯竭；使贫瘠
- **英文释义**：to make a person or area poor; to exhaust the strength or vitality of
- **例句**：
  - Years of drought and conflict have impoverished the entire region.
  - 多年的干旱和冲突使整个地区陷入贫困。
- **助记**：im（使）+ pover（贫穷）+ ish → 使变贫穷 → 使贫穷

### 7. **obstinate** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：固执的；顽固的；执拗的
- **英文释义**：stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action
- **例句**：
  - The obstinate child refused to eat his vegetables no matter what his parents said.
  - 这个固执的孩子无论父母怎么说都不肯吃蔬菜。
- **助记**：ob（对着）+ stin（站立）+ ate → 对着站立不让步 → 固执的

### 8. **rectify** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：纠正；矫正；改正
- **英文释义**：to put right; to correct
- **例句**：
  - The school took immediate steps to rectify the errors in the exam results.
  - 学校立即采取措施纠正了考试成绩中的错误。
- **助记**：rect（正/直）+ ify → 使变正 → 纠正

### 9. **sporadic** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：零星的；偶发的；不规律的
- **英文释义**：occurring at irregular intervals or only in a few places; scattered
- **例句**：
  - There were sporadic outbreaks of violence in the city during the protests.
  - 抗议期间，城市中零星爆发了暴力事件。
- **助记**：spor（种子）+ adic → 像种子一样散落各处 → 零星的

### 10. **waive** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：放弃（权利）；免除；搁置
- **英文释义**：to refrain from insisting on or using a right or claim
- **例句**：
  - The university agreed to waive the application fee for students from low-income families.
  - 大学同意免除低收入家庭学生的申请费。
- **助记**：waive → 联想"wave（挥手）"→ 挥手让它走 → 放弃/免除

### 高一词汇

### 11. **belligerent** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：好战的；好斗的；交战中的
- **英文释义**：hostile and aggressive; engaged in a war or conflict
- **例句**：
  - The belligerent tone of his speech alarmed the diplomatic community.
  - 他演讲中好战的语气令外交界感到不安。
- **助记**：bell（战争）+ iger（携带）+ ent → 携带战争的 → 好战的

### 12. **enigma** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：谜；难以理解的人或事；不可思议的事物
- **英文释义**：a person or thing that is mysterious, puzzling, or difficult to understand
- **例句**：
  - The disappearance of the ancient civilization remains an enigma to this day.
  - 这个古代文明的消失至今仍是一个谜。
- **助记**：enigma → 源自希腊语"说谜语"→ 谜/难以理解的事物

### 13. **incorrigible** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：无可救药的；积习难改的；不可纠正的
- **英文释义**：not able to be corrected, improved, or reformed
- **例句**：
  - Despite numerous warnings, the incorrigible student continued to disrupt the class.
  - 尽管多次警告，这个不可救药的学生仍然继续扰乱课堂。
- **助记**：in（不）+ corrig（纠正）+ ible → 不能纠正的 → 无可救药的

### 14. **prolific** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：多产的；丰富的；大量的
- **英文释义**：producing much fruit, foliage, or offspring; present in large numbers or quantities
- **例句**：
  - Shakespeare was one of the most prolific writers in the history of English literature.
  - 莎士比亚是英国文学史上最多产的作家之一。
- **助记**：pro（向前）+ lif（生命）+ ic → 不断向前产生生命 → 多产的

### 15. **supercilious** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：傲慢的；目中无人的；高高在上的
- **英文释义**：behaving or looking as though one thinks one is superior to others
- **例句**：
  - The supercilious waiter made the diners feel unwelcome with his dismissive attitude.
  - 那位傲慢的服务员用他不屑的态度让用餐者感到不受欢迎。
- **助记**：super（上面）+ cili（眼睫毛）+ ous → 抬起眉毛看人 → 傲慢的
