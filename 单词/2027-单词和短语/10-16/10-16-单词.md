## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **abrupt** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：突然的；意外的；唐突的
- **英文释义**：sudden and unexpected; brief to the point of rudeness
- **例句**：
  - The meeting came to an abrupt end when the fire alarm rang.
  - 火警响起时会议突然结束。
  - His abrupt manner of speaking offended some colleagues.
  - 他唐突的说话方式冒犯了一些同事。
- **助记**：ab（离开）+ rupt（断裂）→ 突然断裂 → 突然的

### 2. **brisk** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：轻快的；活跃的；凛冽的
- **英文释义**：active, fast, and energetic; (of weather) cold but fresh
- **例句**：
  - A brisk walk in the morning helps wake you up.
  - 早上轻快地散步有助于你清醒。
  - Business has been brisk at the new restaurant since it opened.
  - 新餐厅开业以来生意一直很红火。
- **助记**：brisk 联想 brick（砖）→ 像砖头般结实有力 → 活跃

### 3. **curb** `初三` v./n.

- **中文释义**：抑制；控制；路缘石
- **英文释义**：to restrain or keep in check; the edge of a pavement
- **例句**：
  - The new law aims to curb excessive use of plastic bags.
  - 新法律旨在抑制塑料袋的过度使用。
  - Parents should help children curb their screen time.
  - 父母应帮助孩子控制使用屏幕的时间。
- **助记**：curb → 像路缘石一样限制 → 抑制

### 4. **disrupt** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：扰乱；打断；使中断
- **英文释义**：to interrupt the normal course or unity of; to cause disorder
- **例句**：
  - The heavy snowstorm disrupted train services across the country.
  - 大暴雪扰乱了全国的火车服务。
  - New technologies often disrupt traditional business models.
  - 新技术常常颠覆传统商业模式。
- **助记**：dis（分开）+ rupt（断）→ 使断裂 → 扰乱

### 5. **erratic** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：不稳定的；反复无常的；古怪的
- **英文释义**：not even or regular in pattern; unpredictable
- **例句**：
  - The erratic bus schedule made commuting frustrating.
  - 不规律的公交时刻表让通勤令人沮丧。
  - His erratic behavior worried his family and friends.
  - 他反复无常的行为让家人和朋友担忧。
- **助记**：err（错误/偏离）+ atic → 偏离正常 → 不稳定的

### 6. **flaw** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：缺陷；瑕疵；弱点
- **英文释义**：a mark, fault, or other imperfection that mars a substance or quality
- **例句**：
  - The diamond was almost perfect except for a tiny flaw inside.
  - 这颗钻石几乎完美，只是内部有一个小瑕疵。
  - A fatal flaw in the plan led to the project's failure.
  - 计划中的一个致命缺陷导致了项目失败。
- **助记**：flaw 联想 flow（流动）→ 流出的裂缝 → 缺陷

### 7. **garnish** `初三` v./n.

- **中文释义**：装饰（菜肴）；点缀；配菜
- **英文释义**：to decorate food; an ornamental addition to food
- **例句**：
  - The chef garnished the soup with fresh herbs and a drizzle of cream.
  - 厨师用新鲜香草和少许奶油装饰了汤。
  - A lemon garnish adds color and freshness to the dish.
  - 柠檬装饰为菜肴增添了色彩和清新感。
- **助记**：garnish 联想 garment（衣服）→ 给食物穿上衣服 → 装饰

### 8. **hostile** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：敌对的；怀敌意的；不友好的
- **英文释义**：unfriendly; antagonistic; showing strong dislike
- **例句**：
  - The hostile crowd booed the visiting team as they entered the field.
  - 充满敌意的观众在客队入场时发出嘘声。
  - A hostile work environment can damage employee mental health.
  - 充满敌意的工作环境会损害员工心理健康。
- **助记**：host（主人）+ ile → 主人变敌人 → 敌对的

### 9. **impair** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：损害；削弱；减损
- **英文释义**：to weaken or damage something, especially an ability or function
- **例句**：
  - Loud music can impair your hearing over time.
  - 长期听大声音乐会损害你的听力。
  - Lack of sleep impairs concentration and decision-making.
  - 睡眠不足会削弱注意力和决策能力。
- **助记**：im（进入）+ pair（变差）→ 变得更差 → 损害

### 10. **lavish** `初三` adj./v.

- **中文释义**：奢华的；丰富的；慷慨给予
- **英文释义**：sumptuously rich or elaborate; to bestow generously
- **例句**：
  - The wedding reception featured a lavish buffet with dozens of dishes.
  - 婚宴有一个摆满数十道菜的奢华自助餐。
  - Grandparents lavished gifts on the children every holiday.
  - 祖父母每个假期都慷慨地给孩子们送礼物。
- **助记**：lavish 联想 lava（岩浆）→ 像岩浆般喷涌而出 → 慷慨/奢华

### 高一词汇

### 11. **malediction** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：诅咒；咒骂；恶语
- **英文释义**：a magical word or phrase uttered to bring about evil; a curse
- **例句**：
  - In the fairy tale, the witch placed a malediction on the entire village.
  - 在童话中，女巫对整个村庄施了诅咒。
  - His parting words sounded more like a malediction than a farewell.
  - 他的临别之言听起来更像诅咒而非告别。
- **助记**：male（坏）+ dict（说）+ ion → 说坏话 → 诅咒

### 12. **nebulous** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：模糊的；朦胧的；不明确的
- **英文释义**：in the form of a cloud or haze; vague; unclear
- **例句**：
  - The company's future plans remain nebulous and unconvincing.
  - 公司未来的计划仍然模糊且缺乏说服力。
  - His nebulous explanation left the audience more confused than before.
  - 他含糊的解释让听众比之前更困惑。
- **助记**：nebul（云/雾）+ ous → 如云雾般 → 模糊的

### 13. **opaque** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：不透明的；晦涩的；难理解的
- **英文释义**：not able to be seen through; hard to understand
- **例句**：
  - The opaque glass prevented anyone from looking into the room.
  - 不透明的玻璃阻止任何人窥视房间。
  - The legal document was so opaque that even lawyers found it confusing.
  - 这份法律文件晦涩到连律师都觉得难以理解。
- **助记**：o（不）+ paque（透明）→ 不透明的

### 14. **pernicious** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：有害的；恶性的；致命的
- **英文释义**：having a harmful effect, especially in a gradual or subtle way
- **例句**：
  - The pernicious effects of smoking are well documented by science.
  - 吸烟的有害影响已被科学充分记录。
  - Pernicious rumors can spread quickly through social media.
  - 恶意谣言可以通过社交媒体迅速传播。
- **助记**：per（彻底）+ nic（杀）+ ious → 彻底杀伤 → 有害的

### 15. **recalcitrant** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：不服从的；顽抗的；桀骜不驯的
- **英文释义**：having an obstinately uncooperative attitude toward authority
- **例句**：
  - The recalcitrant student repeatedly refused to follow classroom rules.
  - 桀骜不驯的学生反复拒绝遵守课堂纪律。
  - Dealing with recalcitrant employees requires patience and firmness.
  - 应对不服从的员工需要耐心和坚定。
- **助记**：re（回）+ calc（踢）+ itrant → 往回踢 → 顽抗
