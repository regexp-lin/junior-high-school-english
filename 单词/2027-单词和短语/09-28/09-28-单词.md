## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **aberrant** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：异常的；偏离常规的；变态的
- **英文释义**：departing from an accepted standard; abnormal
- **例句**：
  - Aberrant behavior in class led to a meeting with parents.
  - 课堂上的异常行为导致与家长面谈。
  - Scientists studied aberrant cells under the microscope.
  - 科学家在显微镜下研究异常细胞。
- **助记**：ab（离开）+ err（漫游）→ 偏离正道 → 异常的

### 2. **burnish** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：擦亮；磨光；使生辉
- **英文释义**：to polish; to enhance a reputation or image
- **例句**：
  - He burnished the brass until it shone.
  - 他把黄铜擦得锃亮。
  - The award burnished her reputation worldwide.
  - 该奖项使她享誉全球。
- **助记**：burn 联想摩擦生热 → 擦亮

### 3. **capricious** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：反复无常的；任性的；善变的
- **英文释义**：given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior
- **例句**：
  - Capricious weather ruined several outdoor events.
  - 变幻莫测的天气搞砸了几场户外活动。
  - The boss was capricious, praising and criticizing the same work.
  - 老板喜怒无常，同一份工作既夸又批。
- **助记**：capr（山羊）→ 山羊跳跃无常 → 反复无常

### 4. **deleterious** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：有害的；损伤的
- **英文释义**：causing harm or damage in a subtle or unexpected way
- **例句**：
  - Lack of sleep has deleterious effects on memory.
  - 睡眠不足对记忆有害。
  - The chemical is deleterious to aquatic life.
  - 这种化学物质对水生生物有害。
- **助记**：delet（破坏）+ erious → 破坏性的 → 有害的

### 5. **ebullient** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：热情洋溢的；奔放的；（液体）沸腾的
- **英文释义**：cheerful and full of energy; bubbling with enthusiasm
- **例句**：
  - The ebullient host welcomed every guest personally.
  - 热情的主人亲自欢迎每位客人。
  - Her ebullient personality lit up the office.
  - 她热情洋溢的性格点亮了办公室。
- **助记**：e（出）+ bull（沸泡）→ 冒泡 → 热情洋溢

### 6. **facetious** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：乱开玩笑的；滑稽的；不严肃的
- **英文释义**：treating serious issues with deliberately inappropriate humor
- **例句**：
  - His facetious remarks were ill-timed at the funeral.
  - 在葬礼上乱开玩笑不合时宜。
  - She answered with a facetious comment that eased the tension.
  - 她用一句玩笑话缓解了紧张。
- **助记**：facet（面）→ 表面玩笑 → 滑稽不严肃

### 7. **galvanize** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：激励；刺激；电镀
- **英文释义**：to shock or excite someone into taking action; to coat with zinc
- **例句**：
  - The tragedy galvanized the community into volunteering.
  - 悲剧激励社区投入志愿服务。
  - His speech galvanized young voters to register.
  - 他的演讲激励年轻选民登记投票。
- **助记**：来自 Galvani 电流实验 → 电击般激励

### 8. **heinous** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：令人发指的；邪恶的；极恶的
- **英文释义**：utterly odious or wicked; atrocious
- **例句**：
  - The court condemned the heinous crime in strong terms.
  - 法庭强烈谴责这桩令人发指的罪行。
  - Heinous acts have no place in a civilized society.
  - 文明社会不容令人发指的行径。
- **助记**：hain（恨，古英语）→ 可恨之极 → 邪恶

### 9. **implicate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：牵连；暗示；涉及
- **英文释义**：to show that someone is involved in a crime; to imply
- **例句**：
  - New evidence implicated two more suspects.
  - 新证据牵连出另外两名嫌疑人。
  - The report did not implicate the minister directly.
  - 报告并未直接牵连到部长。
- **助记**：im（进入）+ plic（折叠）→ 折进去 → 牵连

### 10. **juncture** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：关键时刻；接合点；关头
- **英文释义**：a particular point in events or time; a critical occasion
- **例句**：
  - At this juncture, we must decide whether to continue.
  - 在此关头，我们必须决定是否继续。
  - The peace talks reached a critical juncture.
  - 和谈到了关键时刻。
- **助记**：junct（连接）+ ure → 连接点 → 关头

### 高一词汇

### 11. **knell** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：丧钟；凶兆；终结之声
- **英文释义**：the sound of a bell, especially when rung solemnly after a death or at a funeral
- **例句**：
  - The final policy shift sounded the knell for the old factory.
  - 最后的政策转向敲响了老工厂的丧钟。
  - Many heard the knell of traditional retail in the digital age.
  - 许多人认为数字时代传统零售的丧钟已敲响。
- **助记**：knell 音近“丧钟”联想 → 终结预兆

### 12. **laconic** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：简洁的；寡言的；精练的
- **英文释义**：using very few words; brief and concise
- **例句**：
  - His laconic reply left room for doubt.
  - 他简短的回答留下了疑问空间。
  - Spartan warriors were famous for laconic wit.
  - 斯巴达战士以言简意赅著称。
- **助记**：来自 Laconia（斯巴达）→ 说话简短

### 13. **mendacious** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：虚假的；撒谎的；捏造的
- **英文释义**：not telling the truth; lying
- **例句**：
  - The article was full of mendacious claims about the candidate.
  - 这篇文章充满对候选人的虚假说法。
  - Mendacious advertising misled many consumers.
  - 虚假广告误导了许多消费者。
- **助记**：mendax（拉丁“说谎”）→ 与 mend（修补）无关 → 撒谎的

### 14. **obsequious** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：谄媚的；奉承的；巴结的
- **英文释义**：obedient or attentive to an excessive or servile degree
- **例句**：
  - The obsequious assistant agreed with every word the boss said.
  - 谄媚的助理对老板说的每句话都附和。
  - Obsequious flattery annoyed honest colleagues.
  - 阿谀奉承让正直的同事反感。
- **助记**：ob（朝向）+ sequ（跟随）→ 紧跟拍马 → 谄媚

### 15. **prodigal** `高一` adj./n.

- **中文释义**：挥霍的；浪子；（喻）慷慨的（大量给予）
- **英文释义**：spending money or resources freely and recklessly; wastefully extravagant
- **例句**：
  - The prodigal son returned home after wasting his inheritance.
  - 挥霍家产的浪子回到了家。
  - Nature was prodigal with sunlight that summer.
  - 那年夏天阳光格外慷慨。
- **助记**：prod（浪费）+ igal → 挥霍的浪子
