## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **approbation** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：赞许；批准；认可
- **英文释义**：approval or praise; official approval
- **例句**：
  - The design won the approbation of the architectural committee.
  - 该设计赢得了建筑委员会的赞许。
  - Parental approbation meant more to her than any prize.
  - 对她来说，父母的认可比任何奖项都重要。
- **助记**：ap（加强）+ prob（证明/好）+ ation → 证明为好 → 赞许

### 2. **arrogate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：僭取；冒称；霸占（权利等）
- **英文释义**：to take or claim something without justification; to appropriate
- **例句**：
  - The dictator arrogated all judicial power to himself.
  - 独裁者把所有司法权据为己有。
  - She arrogated the credit for a project she barely contributed to.
  - 她几乎没参与项目却冒领功劳。
- **助记**：ar（朝向）+ rog（询问/要求）+ ate → 强行要求 → 僭取

### 3. **ascetic** `初三` adj./n.

- **中文释义**：苦行的；禁欲的；苦行者
- **英文释义**：characterized by severe self-discipline and abstention from indulgence; a person who practices such discipline
- **例句**：
  - He led an ascetic life, eating only rice and vegetables.
  - 他过着苦行生活，只吃米饭和蔬菜。
  - The desert monks were famous ascetics who slept on stone floors.
  - 沙漠修士以睡石地板的禁欲者闻名。
- **助记**：希腊 ask（练习/苦修）→ 苦行

### 4. **attenuate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：减弱；使稀释；使变细
- **英文释义**：to reduce the force, effect, or value of; to weaken
- **例句**：
  - Vaccines attenuate viruses so they cannot cause serious disease.
  - 疫苗使病毒弱化，使其无法引发严重疾病。
  - Distance attenuated the sound of the alarm until it was barely audible.
  - 距离使警报声渐渐减弱，几乎听不见。
- **助记**：at（向）+ tenu（薄）+ ate → 变薄 → 减弱

### 5. **augury** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：预兆；占卜；吉凶之兆
- **英文释义**：a sign of what will happen in the future; an omen; divination
- **例句**：
  - Investors saw the merger as a positive augury for the industry.
  - 投资者将合并视为行业的积极预兆。
  - Ancient Romans consulted augury by reading the flight of birds.
  - 古罗马人通过观鸟占卜吉凶。
- **助记**：aug（增加/吉兆）+ ury → 吉兆

### 6. **baleful** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：凶兆的；有害的；恶意的
- **英文释义**：threatening harm; menacing; having harmful intent
- **例句**：
  - The villain shot a baleful glance at the hero across the room.
  - 反派在房间另一头向英雄投去凶恶的一瞥。
  - Baleful economic trends worried investors worldwide.
  - 不祥的经济趋势让全球投资者担忧。
- **助记**：bale（祸害）+ ful → 有害的

### 7. **beatific** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：极乐的；幸福的；圣徒般的安详
- **英文释义**：blissfully happy; imparting holy bliss; serene
- **例句**：
  - The grandmother wore a beatific smile as she watched the children play.
  - 祖母看着孩子们玩耍，脸上露出安详幸福的微笑。
  - Renaissance paintings often show saints with beatific expressions.
  - 文艺复兴时期的绘画常把圣徒画得安详极乐。
- **助记**：beatify（行宣福礼）+ ic → 蒙福的

### 8. **bilious** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：胆汁的；坏脾气的；颜色难看的
- **英文释义**：affected by or associated with nausea or bile; peevish
- **例句**：
  - He felt bilious after eating greasy food at midnight.
  - 半夜吃了油腻食物后他感到恶心。
  - Her bilious tone made the meeting unpleasant for everyone.
  - 她暴躁的口气让会议对每个人都很不愉快。
- **助记**：bili（胆汁）+ ous → 胆汁的/暴躁

### 9. **bowdlerize** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：删改（书籍）以去除敏感内容；过度删节
- **英文释义**：to remove material that is considered indecent or objectionable from a text
- **例句**：
  - The publisher bowdlerized the classic novel for school editions.
  - 出版社为校本删改了经典小说中的敏感内容。
  - Critics accused the network of bowdlerizing the director's original film.
  - 批评者指责电视台对导演原片进行了过度删节。
- **助记**：来自人名 Bowdler（删节莎剧者）→ 删节净化

### 10. **burnish** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：擦亮；磨光；使光彩夺目
- **英文释义**：to polish; to enhance or perfect something
- **例句**：
  - He burnished the brass until it shone like gold.
  - 他把黄铜擦得如金般闪亮。
  - The politician tried to burnish his image with charity donations.
  - 那位政客试图用慈善捐款来美化自己的形象。
- **助记**：burn（燃烧）→ 摩擦生热 → 擦亮

### 高一词汇

### 11. **cachet** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：声望；威信；（药）封蜡；标记
- **英文释义**：prestige; respectability; an official seal or stamp
- **例句**：
  - Winning the award gave the young artist instant cachet.
  - 获奖使这位年轻艺术家立刻获得声望。
  - The old university has a certain cachet among employers worldwide.
  - 这所古老大学在全球雇主中有一定声望。
- **助记**：法语“印章”→ 有印记 → 声望

### 12. **cadge** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：乞讨；白要；蹭
- **英文释义**：to ask for or obtain something without paying for it
- **例句**：
  - He cadged cigarettes from strangers outside the bar.
  - 他在酒吧外向陌生人蹭烟。
  - The interns cadged rides home from coworkers every Friday.
  - 实习生们每周五都蹭同事的车回家。
- **助记**：联想 beggar（乞丐）→ 乞讨

### 13. **calumniate** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：诽谤；中伤；诬蔑
- **英文释义**：to make false and defamatory statements about someone
- **例句**：
  - The article calumniated a scientist without checking the facts.
  - 文章未核实事实就诽谤了一位科学家。
  - He sued the newspaper for calumniating his reputation.
  - 他起诉诽谤他名誉的报纸。
- **助记**：calumn（诽谤）+ iate → 诽谤

### 14. **captious** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：吹毛求疵的；强词夺理的
- **英文释义**：tending to find fault or raise petty objections
- **例句**：
  - The captious reviewer criticized every comma in the manuscript.
  - 吹毛求疵的审稿人挑剔手稿中的每一个逗号。
  - Captious questions during the debate frustrated the speaker.
  - 辩论中刁钻的问题让发言者感到恼火。
- **助记**：capt（抓）+ ious → 到处抓小错 → 挑剔

### 15. **cavil** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：无端挑剔；挑刺；吹毛求疵
- **英文释义**：to make petty objections; to quibble
- **例句**：
  - Critics caviled at the film's length, ignoring its artistic merits.
  - 批评者挑剔影片长度，却忽视其艺术价值。
  - Stop caviling and focus on the main proposal.
  - 别挑刺了，关注主要提案。
- **助记**：联想 trivial（琐碎）→ 挑剔琐事
