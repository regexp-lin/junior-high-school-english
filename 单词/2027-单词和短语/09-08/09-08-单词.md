## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **recalcitrant** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：桀骜不驯的；顽抗的；难管束的
- **英文释义**：having an obstinately uncooperative attitude toward authority
- **例句**：
  - The recalcitrant student refused to follow any classroom rules.
  - 这名桀骜不驯的学生拒绝遵守任何课堂规则。
  - Recalcitrant nations faced sanctions for violating the treaty.
  - 违反条约的顽抗国家面临制裁。
- **助记**：re（再）+ calcit（踢）→ 反复踢开 → 顽抗

### 2. **salient** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：显著的；突出的；最重要的
- **英文释义**：most noticeable or important; prominent
- **例句**：
  - The salient feature of the new phone is its week-long battery life.
  - 新手机最突出的特点是长达一周的续航。
  - She summarized the salient points of the report in two minutes.
  - 她在两分钟内概括了报告的要点。
- **助记**：sali（跳）+ ent → 跳出来 → 显著的

### 3. **truncate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：截短；删节；削减
- **英文释义**：to shorten by cutting off the top or end
- **例句**：
  - The editor truncated the article to fit the newspaper column.
  - 编辑删节文章以适合报纸版面。
  - Budget cuts truncated several planned public works projects.
  - 预算削减使多项计划中的公共工程缩水。
- **助记**：trunc（树干）+ ate → 砍去一截 → 截短

### 4. **usurp** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：篡夺；夺取；侵占
- **英文释义**：to seize power or a position illegally or by force
- **例句**：
  - The general usurped the throne after assassinating the king.
  - 将军刺杀国王后篡夺王位。
  - Critics accused the board of usurping shareholders' voting rights.
  - 批评者指责董事会侵占股东投票权。
- **助记**：us（使用）+urp → 非法取用 → 篡夺

### 5. **venerate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：崇敬；敬重
- **英文释义**：to regard with great respect; revere
- **例句**：
  - Many cultures venerate ancestors with annual memorial rituals.
  - 许多文化以年度祭祀仪式崇敬祖先。
  - The saint was venerated for her lifelong charity work.
  - 这位圣徒因毕生慈善工作而受敬仰。
- **助记**：ven（爱敬）+ erate → 崇敬

### 6. **wrangle** `初三` v./n.

- **中文释义**：争吵；争辩；放牧
- **英文释义**：to argue noisily; a dispute; to round up livestock
- **例句**：
  - The siblings wrangled over who would inherit the family house.
  - 兄弟姐妹为谁来继承祖宅争吵不休。
  - Cowboys wrangled the cattle into the pen before the storm.
  - 暴风雨前牛仔把牛赶进围栏。
- **助记**：wrang（扭曲）→ 纠缠争执

### 7. **yearn** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：渴望；思念；向往
- **英文释义**：to have an intense feeling of longing for something
- **例句**：
  - Exiles yearn for the day they can return home safely.
  - 流亡者渴望能安全回家的那一天。
  - She yearned to study abroad despite her parents' objections.
  - 尽管父母反对，她仍渴望出国留学。
- **助记**：yearn 与 yarn（故事）无关联，联想 year + n → 长年渴望

### 8. **zany** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：滑稽古怪的；小丑式的；荒诞的
- **英文释义**：amusingly unconventional and idiosyncratic; clownish
- **例句**：
  - The comedian's zany costumes made children laugh uncontrollably.
  - 喜剧演员滑稽古怪的服装让孩子们笑个不停。
  - His zany ideas sometimes solved problems traditional methods could not.
  - 他荒诞的主意有时能解决传统方法解决不了的问题。
- **助记**：来自意大利喜剧中的 zanni（小丑）→ 滑稽古怪

### 9. **abrogate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：废除；取消（法律、协议）
- **英文释义**：to repeal or abolish an agreement, law, or custom formally
- **例句**：
  - The new government abrogated treaties signed by the previous regime.
  - 新政府废除了前政权签署的条约。
  - The court cannot abrogate constitutional rights without due process.
  - 未经正当程序，法院不得废除宪法权利。
- **助记**：ab（离开）+ rog（询问/规则）→ 撤掉规则 → 废除

### 10. **burnish** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：擦亮；磨光；使光彩夺目
- **英文释义**：to polish; to enhance a reputation or image
- **例句**：
  - The craftsman burnished the bronze statue until it gleamed.
  - 工匠把铜像擦得锃亮。
  - The CEO tried to burnish the company's image with green initiatives.
  - 首席执行官试图用环保举措提升公司形象。
- **助记**：burn（摩擦发热）+ ish → 摩擦抛光 → 擦亮

### 高一词汇

### 11. **cajole** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：哄劝；用甜言蜜语诱骗
- **英文释义**：to persuade someone to do something by sustained coaxing or flattery
- **例句**：
  - He cajoled his sister into lending him her car for the weekend.
  - 他哄劝妹妹周末把车借给他。
  - Lobbyists cajoled lawmakers with dinners and campaign donations.
  - 游说者用晚宴和竞选捐款拉拢议员。
- **助记**：caj（笼）+ ole → 把人笼络住 → 哄劝

### 12. **desultory** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：散漫的；无条理的；东拉西扯的
- **英文释义**：lacking a plan or purpose; disconnected; random
- **例句**：
  - The meeting was desultory and ended without any decisions.
  - 会议散漫无章，结束时没有任何决议。
  - He flipped through the book in a desultory way, reading only a few pages.
  - 他随意翻书，只读了几页。
- **助记**：de（离开）+ sul（跳）→ 跳来跳去 → 散漫

### 13. **ebullient** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：热情洋溢的；奔放的；沸腾的
- **英文释义**：cheerful and full of energy; bubbling with enthusiasm
- **例句**：
  - The ebullient host welcomed every guest with hugs and laughter.
  - 热情洋溢的主人拥抱欢笑迎接每位客人。
  - Her ebullient mood infected the whole office on Monday morning.
  - 周一早晨她奔放的情绪感染了全办公室。
- **助记**：e（出）+ bull（沸）→ 沸腾溢出 → 热情洋溢

### 14. **fatuous** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：愚昧自满的；愚蠢的；昏头的
- **英文释义**：foolish and silly; complacent in a silly way
- **例句**：
  - The politician's fatuous remarks drew mockery on social media.
  - 政客愚蠢的言论在社交媒体上遭到嘲笑。
  - Only a fatuous optimist would ignore all warning signs.
  - 只有昏头的乐观主义者会忽视所有警示。
- **助记**：fat（饱/蠢）+ uous → 自满而蠢 → 愚昧的

### 15. **gainsay** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：否认；反驳；反对
- **英文释义**：to deny or contradict; to speak against
- **例句**：
  - The evidence is so strong that few historians can gainsay the conclusion.
  - 证据如此确凿，很少有历史学家能否认这一结论。
  - Nobody could gainsay her right to choose her own career path.
  - 无人能否认她选择自己职业道路的权利。
- **助记**：gain（反对）+ say → 反着说 → 否认
