## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **wan** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：苍白无力的；病态的；暗淡的
- **英文释义**：pale and giving the impression of illness or unhappiness
- **例句**：
  - After a week of fever, she looked wan and tired.
  - 发烧一周后，她面色苍白、疲惫不堪。
  - The wan winter sun barely warmed the frozen fields.
  - 冬日惨淡的阳光几乎无法温暖冰冻的田野。
- **助记**：联想“瘦弱无力”→ wan

### 2. **wanton** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：肆意的；蛮横的；淫乱的
- **英文释义**：deliberate and unprovoked; reckless; sexually unrestrained
- **例句**：
  - The invaders committed wanton destruction of cultural heritage sites.
  - 侵略者肆意破坏文化遗产。
  - Wanton disregard for safety rules led to the factory explosion.
  - 对安全规程的肆意漠视导致工厂爆炸。
- **助记**：want（缺乏控制）+ on → 放纵无拘 → 肆意

### 3. **yaw** `初三` v./n.

- **中文释义**：（船、飞机）偏航；左右摆；偏航角
- **英文释义**：to twist or oscillate about a vertical axis; deviation from a straight course
- **例句**：
  - The ship yawed violently in the crosswind as it approached the harbor.
  - 进港时船在侧风中剧烈偏航。
  - Pilots train to correct yaw during turbulent flights.
  - 飞行员训练在颠簸飞行中修正偏航。
- **助记**：航海术语 → 船头左右摆 → 偏航

### 4. **zealot** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：狂热者；极端分子
- **英文释义**：a person who is fanatical and uncompromising in pursuit of their ideals
- **例句**：
  - Political zealots refuse to listen to any opposing viewpoint.
  - 政治狂热者拒绝听取任何反对意见。
  - The reformer was called a zealot by those who resisted change.
  - 抵制变革的人称这位改革者为狂热分子。
- **助记**：zeal（热情）+ ot → 过度热情 → 狂热者

### 5. **abjure** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：发誓放弃；公开放弃
- **英文释义**：to solemnly renounce a belief, cause, or claim
- **例句**：
  - The prisoner was forced to abjure his faith under threat of execution.
  - 囚犯在处死威胁下被迫发誓放弃信仰。
  - She abjured violence and devoted herself to peaceful protest.
  - 她发誓弃绝暴力，投身和平抗议。
- **助记**：ab（离开）+ jure（发誓）→ 发誓离开（旧信念）→ 公开放弃

### 6. **bereft** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：失去……的；丧亲的；凄凉的
- **英文释义**：deprived of or lacking something; sad and lonely
- **例句**：
  - Bereft of hope, the refugees waited in endless lines at the border.
  - 难民失去希望，在边境无尽排队。
  - He was bereft when his lifelong companion passed away.
  - 终身伴侣去世时，他悲痛欲绝。
- **助记**：be + reft（夺走）→ 被夺走 → 失去

### 7. **chicanery** `初三` n.

- **中文释义**：诡计；狡辩；欺骗手段
- **英文释义**：the use of trickery to achieve a political, financial, or legal purpose
- **例句**：
  - The contract was full of legal chicanery designed to trap unwary clients.
  - 合同充满旨在坑害粗心客户的法律花招。
  - Voters were tired of political chicanery and demanded transparency.
  - 选民厌倦政治诡计，要求透明。
- **助记**：来自法语 chicaner（刁钻）→ 狡辩诡计

### 8. **dissemble** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：掩饰；伪装；假装
- **英文释义**：to conceal one's true motives or feelings
- **例句**：
  - The spy learned to dissemble his fear during interrogations.
  - 间谍学会在审讯中掩饰恐惧。
  - She dissembled indifference though she cared deeply about the outcome.
  - 她假装漠不关心，其实非常在意结果。
- **助记**：dis（分开）+ semble（相同）→ 使表里不一 → 掩饰

### 9. **eclectic** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：兼收并蓄的；博采众长的
- **英文释义**：deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad range of sources
- **例句**：
  - Her eclectic taste in music ranges from jazz to heavy metal.
  - 她在音乐上兼收并蓄，从爵士到重金属都听。
  - The architect's eclectic designs blended Eastern and Western elements.
  - 这位建筑师兼收并蓄的设计融合了东西方元素。
- **助记**：ec（出）+ lect（选）→ 从各处选出 → 兼收并蓄

### 10. **fervent** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：热烈的；强烈的；热情的
- **英文释义**：displaying a passionate intensity; burning hot
- **例句**：
  - She was a fervent supporter of animal rights throughout her life.
  - 她一生都是动物权利的热烈支持者。
  - The crowd responded with fervent applause after the speech.
  - 演讲结束后人群报以热烈掌声。
- **助记**：ferv（沸腾）→ 热血沸腾 → 热烈的

### 高一词汇

### 11. **garrulous** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：喋喋不休的；絮叨的
- **英文释义**：excessively talkative, especially on trivial matters
- **例句**：
  - The garrulous taxi driver narrated his entire life story during the ride.
  - 喋喋不休的出租车司机一路上讲完了自己的人生。
  - Garrulous students received lower grades on timed essays.
  - 啰嗦的学生在限时作文中得分较低。
- **助记**：garr（聊天）+ ulous → 话多的

### 12. **harbinger** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：预兆；先驱；前兆
- **英文释义**：a person or thing that announces or signals the approach of another
- **例句**：
  - The first robin is a harbinger of spring in many regions.
  - 在许多地区，第一只知更鸟是春天的预兆。
  - Rising sea levels are a harbinger of more severe climate impacts.
  - 海平面上升是更严重气候影响的前兆。
- **助记**：harb（军队营地）+ inger → 先行探路者 → 预兆

### 13. **ignoble** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：卑鄙的；可耻的；出身卑微的
- **英文释义**：not honorable in character or purpose; of humble origin
- **例句**：
  - Betraying a friend for money is an ignoble act.
  - 为钱出卖朋友是可耻行径。
  - He rose from ignoble beginnings to lead the nation.
  - 他出身寒微，后来领导了国家。
- **助记**：ig（不）+ noble（高贵）→ 不高尚 → 卑鄙

### 14. **juncture** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：关键时刻；接合点；关头
- **英文释义**：a particular point in events or time; a joint or connection
- **例句**：
  - At this critical juncture, we cannot afford to hesitate.
  - 在这关键时刻，我们不能犹豫。
  - The peace talks arrived at a delicate juncture when both sides threatened to quit.
  - 和谈到了双方都威胁退出的微妙关头。
- **助记**：junct（连接）→ 事情连接的关键点 → 关头

### 15. **knell** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：丧钟；不祥之兆
- **英文释义**：the sound of a bell, especially when rung solemnly after a death
- **例句**：
  - The final bell tolled the knell of the old factory's hundred-year history.
  - 最后一声钟响宣告了这座百年老厂的终结。
  - For many retailers, e-commerce sounded the knell of brick-and-mortar dominance.
  - 对许多零售商而言，电商敲响了实体店主导时代的丧钟。
- **助记**：knell 与 knoll（小丘）区分 → 钟声 → 丧钟
