## 📖 单词详解

### 初三词汇

### 1. **alleviate** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：减轻；缓解；缓和
- **英文释义**：to make suffering, deficiency, or a problem less severe
- **例句**：
  - The new medicine can alleviate the symptoms of chronic pain within hours.
  - 这种新药能在数小时内缓解慢性疼痛的症状。
  - Community volunteers worked to alleviate food insecurity in rural areas.
  - 社区志愿者努力缓解农村地区的粮食不安全问题。
- **助记**：al（向）+ levi（轻）+ ate → 使变轻 → 减轻/缓解

### 2. **bolster** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：支持；加强；巩固
- **英文释义**：to support or strengthen; prop up
- **例句**：
  - Fresh evidence bolstered the prosecution's case at the trial.
  - 新证据在庭审中加强了控方的论据。
  - She used pillows to bolster her back while reading in bed.
  - 她在床上看书时用枕头垫着背部以支撑。
- **助记**：bolster 原指长枕 → 用枕头支撑 → 引申为加强/支持

### 3. **candid** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：坦率的；直言不讳的；公正的
- **英文释义**：truthful and straightforward; frank
- **例句**：
  - The manager gave a candid assessment of the team's weaknesses.
  - 经理对团队的弱点做了坦率的评价。
  - Candid photos often capture more natural expressions than posed ones.
  - 抓拍照片往往比摆拍更能捕捉自然的表情。
- **助记**：cand（白/光，如 candle）→ 心里透亮 → 坦率的

### 4. **diligent** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：勤奋的；勤勉的；细致的
- **英文释义**：showing care and effort in one's work or duties
- **例句**：
  - Diligent students review their notes every evening after class.
  - 勤奋的学生每天课后都会复习笔记。
  - Her diligent research uncovered errors that others had missed.
  - 她细致的研究发现了别人遗漏的错误。
- **助记**：dilig（选择/珍爱）+ ent → 对学业珍而重之 → 勤奋的

### 5. **elicit** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：引出；诱出；套出
- **英文释义**：to draw out a response, fact, or reaction from someone
- **例句**：
  - The interviewer skillfully elicited honest opinions from the guests.
  - 采访者巧妙地引导嘉宾说出真实想法。
  - The teacher's questions elicited lively discussion among the students.
  - 老师的问题引发了学生之间热烈的讨论。
- **助记**：e（出）+ licit（引诱）→ 引出 → 引出/诱出

### 6. **feasible** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：可行的；行得通的；合理的
- **英文释义**：possible to do easily or conveniently; likely to succeed
- **例句**：
  - The committee concluded that the plan was financially feasible.
  - 委员会认为该计划在财务上是可行的。
  - It is not feasible to finish the entire project in one week without extra staff.
  - 没有额外人手的话，一周内完成整个项目是不可行的。
- **助记**：feas（做/制作）+ ible → 能做出来的 → 可行的

### 7. **harness** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：利用；控制；给……上挽具
- **英文释义**：to control and use the natural force or power of something
- **例句**：
  - Engineers designed turbines to harness wind energy along the coast.
  - 工程师设计涡轮机以利用沿海的风能。
  - She learned to harness her anxiety and turn it into motivation before exams.
  - 她学会在考试前驾驭焦虑，把它转化为动力。
- **助记**：harness 本义马具 → 驾驭马匹 → 引申为利用/控制

### 8. **inevitable** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：不可避免的；必然发生的
- **英文释义**：certain to happen; unavoidable
- **例句**：
  - Conflict was inevitable once both sides refused to compromise.
  - 双方拒绝妥协后，冲突就不可避免了。
  - Aging is an inevitable part of life that everyone must face.
  - 衰老是每个人都要面对的人生必然阶段。
- **助记**：in（不）+ evit（避免）+ able → 无法避免的 → 不可避免的

### 9. **jeopardize** `初三` v.

- **中文释义**：危及；危害；使陷入危险
- **英文释义**：to put someone or something into a situation in which there is a risk of loss or harm
- **例句**：
  - One careless mistake could jeopardize the entire rescue mission.
  - 一次粗心大意就可能危及整个救援任务。
  - He refused to jeopardize his reputation by signing the fraudulent contract.
  - 他拒绝签署欺诈合同以免损害自己的名誉。
- **助记**：jeopard（危险，如 jeopardy）+ ize → 使处于危险 → 危及

### 10. **meticulous** `初三` adj.

- **中文释义**：一丝不苟的；极为仔细的；谨小慎微的
- **英文释义**：showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
- **例句**：
  - The surgeon is known for her meticulous technique in delicate operations.
  - 这位外科医生以在精细手术中一丝不苟的技术著称。
  - He kept meticulous records of every expense during the trip.
  - 他对旅途中的每一笔开支都做了极为仔细的记录。
- **助记**：metic（恐惧/小心）+ ulous → 因害怕出错而极度仔细 → 一丝不苟的

### 高一词汇

### 11. **acrimonious** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：尖刻的；激烈的；充满怨气的
- **英文释义**：angry and bitter in manner or tone
- **例句**：
  - The divorce led to an acrimonious dispute over property and custody.
  - 离婚引发了关于财产和监护权的激烈争执。
  - Acrimonious debates dominated the final weeks of the election campaign.
  - 尖刻的辩论主导了竞选活动的最后几周。
- **助记**：acri（尖锐/酸）+ monious → 言语尖锐带刺 → 尖刻的

### 12. **burgeon** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：迅速增长；蓬勃发展；萌芽
- **英文释义**：to begin to grow or increase rapidly; flourish
- **例句**：
  - Start-up companies burgeoned in the tech hub over the past decade.
  - 过去十年里，科技中心涌现出大量初创公司并迅速发展。
  - Interest in environmental science has burgeoned among high school students.
  - 高中生对环境科学的兴趣迅速增长。
- **助记**：bourgeon（法语嫩芽）→ 像新芽猛长 → 蓬勃发展

### 13. **corroborate** `高一` v.

- **中文释义**：证实；佐证；支持
- **英文释义**：to confirm or give support to a statement, theory, or finding
- **例句**：
  - Witness testimony corroborated the defendant's alibi.
  - 证人证词证实了被告的不在场证明。
  - New DNA evidence corroborated the original investigation's conclusions.
  - 新的 DNA 证据印证了最初调查的结论。
- **助记**：cor（一起）+ robor（力量）→ 一起使更有力 → 证实/佐证

### 14. **dichotomy** `高一` n.

- **中文释义**：二分法；对立；截然划分
- **英文释义**：a division or contrast between two things that are opposed or entirely different
- **例句**：
  - The essay explores the dichotomy between tradition and modernity in urban design.
  - 文章探讨了城市设计中传统与现代的二元对立。
  - Critics noted a dichotomy between the politician's public speeches and private actions.
  - 批评者指出这位政治家的公开言论与私下行为存在鲜明对立。
- **助记**：dicho（一分为二）+ tomy（切分）→ 一分为二 → 二分法

### 15. **ephemeral** `高一` adj.

- **中文释义**：短暂的；朝生暮死的；转瞬即逝的
- **英文释义**：lasting for a very short time; transitory
- **例句**：
  - Cherry blossoms are beautiful but ephemeral, lasting only a few weeks each spring.
  - 樱花很美却花期短暂，每年春天只开几周。
  - Social media fame can be ephemeral if not backed by real talent.
  - 若没有真才实学，社交媒体上的名气可能转瞬即逝。
- **助记**：ephemer（一日）+ al → 只活一天的 → 短暂的
