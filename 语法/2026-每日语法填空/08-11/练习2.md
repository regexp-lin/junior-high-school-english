# 08-11 中考英语语法填空 练习二

主题：编程入门 (Introduction to Coding)

## 一、题目

阅读下面短文，在空白处填入一个适当的单词，或填入括号中所给单词的正确形式。

Coding has become an important skill in the modern world. Many schools in China have started offering programming classes to students as young (1) ______ ten years old. I took my first coding class last summer and found it both (2) ______ (challenge) and exciting. The teacher began by (3) ______ (teach) us a language called Python. At first, the screen was full of strange symbols that made no sense to me. But after a few lessons, I began to understand the (4) ______ (base) logic behind the code. My proudest moment was when I wrote a simple programme that could (5) ______ (calculate) the area of any triangle. I showed it to my maths teacher, and she was (6) ______ (impress) by what I had done. Coding teaches you to think (7) ______ (logical) and solve problems step by step. If one line of code does not work, you have to find the mistake and fix it (8) ______ (patient). This process, called "debugging," is actually good training for the brain. Some people worry that coding is too (9) ______ (difficulty) for young students. But I disagree. With the right guidance, anyone can learn the basics. I plan to continue learning and hope to build (10) ______ (I) own app one day.

---

## 二、答案与解析

1. **答案：as**
   - **考点**：连词。`as young as ten years old` 为同级比较结构，表示"年仅十岁"。

2. **答案：challenging**
   - **考点**：词性转换。与 `exciting` 并列，`challenge` 变为 `challenging`（有挑战性的）。

3. **答案：teaching**
   - **考点**：动名词。`by` 为介词，后接动名词。

4. **答案：basic**
   - **考点**：词性转换。修饰名词 `logic` 需要形容词，`base` 变为 `basic`（基本的）。

5. **答案：calculate**
   - **考点**：动词原形。`could` 后接动词原形。

6. **答案：impressed**
   - **考点**：词性转换。`be impressed by` 表示"对……印象深刻"，`impress` 变为 `impressed`。

7. **答案：logically**
   - **考点**：词性转换。修饰动词 `think` 需要副词，`logical` 变为 `logically`。

8. **答案：patiently**
   - **考点**：词性转换。修饰动词 `fix` 需要副词，`patient` 变为 `patiently`。

9. **答案：difficult**
   - **考点**：词性转换。`too + 形容词` 结构，`difficulty` 变为 `difficult`。

10. **答案：my**
    - **考点**：形容词性物主代词。修饰名词 `own app`，用 `my`。
