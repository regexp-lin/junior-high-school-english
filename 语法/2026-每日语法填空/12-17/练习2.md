# 12-17 中考英语语法填空 练习二

主题：日常生活中的发明 (Inventions in Daily Life)

## 一、题目

阅读下面短文，在空白处填入一个适当的单词，或填入括号中所给单词的正确形式。

We are surrounded by inventions that make our daily lives easier, yet we rarely stop (1) ______ (think) about how they came about. Take the zip, for example. Before it was invented, people used buttons and hooks to fasten their clothes, (2) ______ was slow and inconvenient. The zip was patented in 1917 and quickly became one of the most (3) ______ (wide) used fastening devices in the world. Another everyday invention is the sticky note. It was created (4) ______ accident in 1968 when a scientist at 3M developed a weak adhesive that could stick to surfaces without (5) ______ (damage) them. At first, nobody knew what to do with it. It was not (6) ______ 1977 that another employee came up with the idea of using it to mark pages in his hymn book. Today, sticky notes can (7) ______ (find) in offices and schools everywhere. The microwave oven is yet another invention born from an accident. In 1945, an engineer (8) ______ (name) Percy Spencer noticed that a chocolate bar in his pocket had melted while he was working near a radar machine. He realised that the radar waves could heat food, and the first microwave oven was built shortly (9) ______. These stories remind us that great inventions do not always come from careful planning. Sometimes, the most (10) ______ (use) things are discovered by chance.

---

## 二、答案与解析

1. **答案：to think**
   - **考点**：不定式。`stop to think` 表示"停下来去思考"。

2. **答案：which**
   - **考点**：关系代词。引导非限制性定语从句，指代使用纽扣和钩子这件事。

3. **答案：widely**
   - **考点**：词性转换。修饰形容词 `used` 需要副词。

4. **答案：by**
   - **考点**：介词。`by accident` 表示"偶然地"。

5. **答案：damaging**
   - **考点**：动名词。介词 `without` 后接动名词。

6. **答案：until**
   - **考点**：连词。`It was not until...that...` 为强调句型。

7. **答案：be found**
   - **考点**：被动语态。`can be found`，情态动词后接被动。

8. **答案：named**
   - **考点**：过去分词作定语。`an engineer named Percy Spencer`。

9. **答案：after / afterwards**
   - **考点**：副词。`shortly after/afterwards` 表示"不久之后"。

10. **答案：useful**
    - **考点**：词性转换。`the most useful things`，修饰名词需要形容词。
